# Competitor Analysis — MetalForge

*Know your competition. Find your edge.*

---

## Direct Competitors

### 1. DrummerWorld.com
- **Focus:** All genres, famous drummers
- **Strengths:** Huge database, established authority
- **Weaknesses:** Outdated design, no gear focus, no prices
- **Our Edge:** Metal niche, gear-focused, modern UX

### 2. ModernDrummer.com
- **Focus:** Magazine-style, interviews, news
- **Strengths:** Industry reputation, exclusive content
- **Weaknesses:** Subscription model, not gear-database focused
- **Our Edge:** Free access, pure gear focus

### 3. MusicRadar Drummer Section
- **Focus:** News, reviews, tutorials
- **Strengths:** High traffic, broad appeal
- **Weaknesses:** Not drummer-profile focused
- **Our Edge:** Dedicated profiles with complete setups

---

## Indirect Competitors

### 4. Wikipedia
- **Threat Level:** High (ranks for drummer names)
- **Gap:** No gear details, no videos, boring format
- **Our Edge:** Rich media, complete gear lists

### 5. YouTube
- **Threat Level:** Medium (drum content hub)
- **Gap:** No structured data, hard to find specific gear
- **Our Edge:** Curated videos + structured gear info

### 6. Gear Retailer Sites (Thomann, Sweetwater)
- **Threat Level:** Low (different intent)
- **Opportunity:** Affiliate partnerships

## SEO Keyword Gaps

Keywords we should target that competitors miss:

1. "[drummer name] drum kit"
2. "[drummer name] cymbals"
3. "[drummer name] gear 2024"
4. "best metal drum setup"
5. "metal drummer equipment list"
6. "[drummer name] setup cost"
7. "[drummer] vs [drummer] gear"

## Opportunities

1. **Niche Down:** Own "metal drummer gear" completely
2. **Price Info:** Nobody shows setup costs — we should
3. **Comparison:** No good side-by-side tools exist
4. **Visual Design:** Most drummer sites look like 2005
