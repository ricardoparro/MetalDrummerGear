# MetalDrumGear — Engagement Strategy

## 🎯 Goal
Grow from 0 → 5,000 followers organically through consistent, valuable engagement.

---

## 📅 Posting Schedule

### Frequency
- **2-3 posts/day** (optimal for growth without spam)
- **5-10 replies/day** to other accounts (key for visibility!)

### Best Times (Metal/Music Community)
- **Morning:** 8-10 AM (coffee & scroll)
- **Lunch:** 12-2 PM (break time)
- **Evening:** 7-10 PM (peak engagement for music fans)
- **Weekend:** More flexible, evenings still best

### Weekly Content Mix
| Day | Content Type |
|-----|-------------|
| Mon | Did You Know? fact + engagement question |
| Tue | Gear Spotlight (specific drummer's kit) |
| Wed | Comparison/Poll ("Lars vs Lombardo?") |
| Thu | Quiz promo or site feature |
| Fri | Weekend jam vibes / throwback content |
| Sat | Engage heavily with others' posts |
| Sun | Recap/best of week or chill content |

---

## 💬 Reply Strategy (MOST IMPORTANT!)

### Who to Reply To

**High Priority (reply within 1h if possible):**
1. **Drummers we follow** posting about gear, shows, or achievements
2. **Gear brands** (Vic Firth, Paiste, Meinl, Pearl, TAMA)
3. **Metal news accounts** (Loudwire, Metal Hammer, Revolver)
4. **Anyone discussing metal drummers**

**Medium Priority:**
- Band accounts posting about tours/albums
- Drum YouTubers/educators
- Anyone asking drummer questions

### Reply Style Guide

✅ **Good replies add value:**
```
"The snare tone on that track is incredible — he uses a 14x6.5 
Ludwig Black Beauty if anyone's curious 🥁"

"Gene Hoglan is criminally underrated. That footwork on 
'Symbolic' changed death metal drumming forever"

"Great pick! If you like his style, check out Tomas Haake — 
similar polyrhythmic approach but different tone philosophy"
```

❌ **Bad replies (avoid):**
```
"Check out our site!" (spam)
"Nice!" (low effort)
"🔥🔥🔥" (empty)
"Follow us for more!" (desperate)
```

### Reply Templates

**When someone posts drum gear:**
> "That [gear] is sick! [Drummer name] uses the same/similar setup. The [specific detail] really shapes their sound 🥁"

**When someone asks "who's the best drummer?":**
> "[Opinion with reasoning]. But it depends what you value — speed, groove, creativity? We actually built a quiz for this: [link]"

**When a drummer posts achievement:**
> "Congrats! Your work on [specific song/album] was [specific praise]. The [technical detail] in that track is insane"

**When gear brand posts new product:**
> "This would pair perfectly with [specific setup]. Can already imagine [drummer] using this for [style]"

## 🔄 Engagement Tactics

### Quote Tweets (1-2/day max)
Add genuine insight when quote tweeting:
```
"This is why [drummer] is a legend. Fun fact: his kit on this 
tour was actually [detail from our data]"
```

### Retweets (3-5/day)
Retweet quality content from:
- Drummers sharing performances
- Gear demos
- Metal news/reviews
- Drum education content

### Polls (1-2/week)
```
"Greatest metal double-bass drummer?"
□ Dave Lombardo
□ Gene Hoglan  
□ George Kollias
□ Pete Sandoval
```

### Threads (1/week)
Deep-dive threads perform well:
```
🧵 "The evolution of Lars Ulrich's kit (1983-2024)"

1/7: In 1983, Lars started with a basic Camco kit...
[continue with gear evolution + images if possible]
```

## 🚫 Things to Avoid

1. **Don't spam links** — only link when genuinely relevant
2. **Don't argue** — metal fans are passionate, stay positive
3. **Don't post too much** — quality > quantity
4. **Don't ignore replies** — always respond to engagement
5. **Don't be generic** — specificity shows expertise
6. **Don't chase trends blindly** — stay on-brand

---

## 📱 Daily Routine (15-20 min)

### Morning (5 min)
- Check notifications, reply to any overnight engagement
- Quick scroll of followed accounts

### Afternoon (5 min)
- Post main content of the day
- Reply to 2-3 relevant posts

### Evening (10 min)
- Second post if planned
- Heavy engagement time — reply to 5+ posts
- Retweet good content
- Check for trending topics

---

## 🎸 Voice & Tone

**Be:**
- Knowledgeable but not pretentious
- Enthusiastic but not over-the-top
- Specific and factual
- Part of the community, not above it

**Sound like:**
> "A drummer friend who knows their gear and loves talking shop"

**Not like:**
> "A brand account trying to sell something"
