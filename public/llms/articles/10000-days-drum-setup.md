# 10,000 Days Drum Setup: Danny Carey's Polyrhythmic Pinnacle (2006)

> Complete breakdown of Danny Carey's drum gear on Tool's 10,000 Days — Grammy Best Recording Package winner (2007). Explore his Sonor SQ2 kit, Paiste 2002 cymbals, Roland SPD-S integration, and the odd-time mastery behind Vicarious and Jambi.

## Overview

Released on May 2, 2006, Tool's "10,000 Days" arrived five years after "Lateralus" and thirteen years before "Fear Inoculum," occupying a pivotal place in Danny Carey's evolution as a drummer. Debuting at #1 on the Billboard 200 and reaching the top spot in nine countries, the album captured Tool at the height of their commercial power while pushing their sonic ambitions further than ever before.

The album's title honors Maynard James Keenan's mother, Judith Marie, who spent approximately 10,000 days in a wheelchair following a stroke — lending a raw emotional weight to the record. For Danny, that emotional depth demanded drumming that was simultaneously technically complex and deeply human.

"10,000 Days" won the Grammy Award for Best Recording Package at the 49th Grammy Awards in 2007, recognizing the album's extraordinary visual and physical presentation. The gatefold package included lenticular 3D glasses — an artistic statement entirely in keeping with Tool's reputation for immersive, concept-driven releases.

Danny's drumming on this album represents the zenith of his Lateralus-era setup, refined over five years of touring and studio exploration. Tracks like "Vicarious" (7/8), "Jambi" (complex polyrhythm over a sustained drone), and the epic title suite "10,000 Days (Wings Pt. 1 & 2)" pushed his odd-time mastery to new heights. This article breaks down every piece of gear Danny used to create this landmark recording.

### The Evolved Sonor: Danny's SQ2 Custom Configuration

By 2006, Danny Carey's relationship with Sonor had matured into one of the most productive artist-manufacturer partnerships in modern drumming. The kit he brought to the 10,000 Days sessions was a refined evolution of the setup heard on Lateralus — deeper in tone, more precisely configured, and better suited to the longer, more complex compositions Tool had written.

The 24" x 18" bass drum remained the anchor of the setup. Its cavernous depth delivered the subsonic weight that underpins songs like "Vicarious" and "The Pot," providing the physical presence that Tool's music demands. Danny continued his single-pedal approach, demonstrating that technical velocity and musical expression don't require a double kit.

The tom array expanded toward the full configuration seen on later tours. Seven toms spanning 8" to 18" gave Danny a melodic range that rivals a chromatic percussion instrument. On "10,000 Days (Wings Pt. 2)," he treats these toms as a melodic voice, weaving around Adam Jones's guitar with fills that sing rather than simply punctuate.

The Heavy Beech shells had been fully broken in by five years of touring behind Lateralus. This seasoning — the gradual settling of shell wood and hardware — contributed to the warmer, slightly more compressed tone audible on the record compared to Lateralus. The shells responded beautifully to both gentle ghost notes and full-power crashes.

### The Signature Bronze: Fully Developed

By the time "10,000 Days" went into production, the Sonor Danny Carey Signature snare had moved well beyond prototype status. The 14" x 8" cast bronze drum was now a fully specified production instrument, though Danny's personal unit featured custom touches that distinguished it from the retail version.

The bronze shell's sonic character is central to the album's drum sound. On "Vicarious," the snare's dark attack cuts through the 7/8 groove with authority — each backbeat landing with a combination of warmth and crack that a steel or aluminum shell simply can't replicate. The overtones sing in a way that complements rather than fights Adam Jones's guitar tones.

The 8" depth continued to be the key advantage over standard metal snares. On the instrumental passages in "Jambi" and the sprawling "10,000 Days (Wings Pt. 1 & 2)," Danny needed a snare that could function both as a rhythmic anchor and a tonal color. The deep bronze shell fulfilled both roles with authority.

Danny tuned the drum to his characteristic medium-high tension during the sessions — enough crack for the climactic moments, enough sensitivity for the delicate ghost notes that pepper his playing throughout the record. The die-cast hoops added the rim-shot definition needed for Tool's complex arrangements.

### Paiste 2002 and Signature: Power and Complexity

For "10,000 Days," Danny drew on both the Paiste Signature series (his mainstay for nuanced, musical playing) and the older Paiste 2002 series for raw power and aggression. This combination gave him the best of both worlds: the sophisticated harmonic complexity of the Signature line for intricate passages, and the raw, uncompromising attack of the 2002 series for the album's heavier moments.

The Paiste 2002 series, one of the most iconic cymbal lines in rock history, had been in production since the 1970s. Its bright, powerful character and exceptional durability made it a favorite for studio work — the attack translates clearly on tape, and the sustain doesn't clutter complex arrangements.

The 15" Sound Edge hi-hats remained central to Danny's sound. On "Vicarious," his hi-hat work in 7/8 is a masterclass in rhythmic displacement — the hats provide a steady pulse while his snare and kick create the polyrhythmic tension that defines the song's groove. The Sound Edge serration keeps the pattern articulate even at tempo.

The 22" Dry Heavy Ride handled the long, sustained passages in "Jambi" and the extended suite "10,000 Days (Wings Pt. 1 & 2)." Its controlled wash allowed Danny to play rhythmic patterns that remained clear in the mix without drowning in overtones.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drum kit did Danny Carey use on 10,000 Days?**

A: Danny Carey recorded Tool's 10,000 Days in 2006 on a Sonor SQ2 custom configuration with Heavy Beech shells. The setup included a single 24x18 inch bass drum, five rack toms at 8, 10, 12, 13, and 14 inches, and floor toms at 16 and 18 inches. By this point his Sonor partnership was fully established, and the Heavy Beech shells had been refined over five years of touring behind Lateralus to deliver the warm, powerful tone heard throughout the album.

**Q: What cymbals did Danny Carey use on 10,000 Days?**

A: Danny Carey used a combination of Paiste Signature and Paiste 2002 series cymbals on 10,000 Days. His setup featured Paiste Signature 15 inch Sound Edge Hi-Hats, a mix of Signature crashes (17 and 19 inch) and 2002 crashes (18 and 20 inch), a Signature 22 inch Dry Heavy Ride, a Signature 18 inch Thin China, and a 2002 20 inch China. The Signature series provided musical nuance while the 2002 series added raw attack for heavier passages.

**Q: Did Danny Carey use electronic drums on 10,000 Days?**

A: 10,000 Days marked Danny Carey's first substantive use of electronic percussion in Tool's studio recordings, integrating a Roland SPD-S sampling pad alongside his acoustic Sonor SQ2 kit. The SPD-S allowed him to trigger ambient samples and textural elements — most notably on the title suite "10,000 Days (Wings Pt. 1 & 2)." This was an early step toward the deeper Mandala pad integration that would define the Fear Inoculum sessions thirteen years later.
