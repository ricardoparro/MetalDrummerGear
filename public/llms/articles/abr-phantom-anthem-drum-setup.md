# August Burns Red 'Phantom Anthem' Drum Setup (Matt Greiner, 2017)

## Overview

Released on September 15, 2017 on Fearless Records, August Burns Red's *Phantom Anthem* earned a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance for the track "King of Sorrow" — placing the band among the most recognized acts in metal and confirming what the metalcore world had long known: Matt Greiner is the genre's most compositionally sophisticated drummer.

*Phantom Anthem* arrived two years after *Found in Far Away Places* (2015), closing the gap between what would become the middle chapter of ABR's discography before *Beacon* (2023). The album leaned into urgency — tighter production from Carson Slovak and Grant McFarland at Atrium Audio in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, more direct songwriting, and a drum sound that cut through the mix with controlled precision.

Greiner recorded *Phantom Anthem* with his Pearl Reference Pure kit — the maple/mahogany hybrid shell setup that defined his mature gear era. The Zildjian A Custom and Sabian AAX cymbals provided bright, fast response suited to the album's direct character. On tracks like "King of Sorrow," "Lifeline," and "Bloodletter," Greiner's lever-driven double bass patterns and ghost note integration function as compositional architecture — the rhythmic logic that holds the album's most demanding passages together.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure (maple/mahogany hybrid shells)
- **Snare:** Pearl Matt Greiner Signature, 14" x 6" steel
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom (hi-hats, crashes, ride) + Sabian AAX (crash, Chinese)
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal; Pearl H-2050 Eliminator Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Nitro Throne; Vic Firth Matt Greiner Signature sticks
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 (bass drums), Remo Ambassador Coated (toms/snare batter), Remo Ambassador Snare Side (resonant)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high tension — crack and projection with ghost note sensitivity

### Pearl Reference Pure: The Mature Setup

By the time ABR entered Atrium Audio for *Phantom Anthem*, Matt Greiner had settled on the Pearl Reference Pure as his defining instrument. The hybrid maple/mahogany shells deliver a character distinct from the Ludwig Classic Maple era of *Leveler* (2011): tighter attack, more articulate transient definition, and a focused punch that suits *Phantom Anthem*'s direct production. The two 22" bass drums gave Greiner the symmetric, independent response his lever-driven technique demands.

### Zildjian A Custom / Sabian AAX Cymbals

The A Custom / AAX combination brought brighter, faster-responding character to *Phantom Anthem* compared to Greiner's earlier Paiste 2002 era. Zildjian A Custom hi-hats provided precise 16th-note articulation through complex rhythmic passages; the AAX crashes delivered cutting accent weight for section climaxes; the A Custom ride articulated bell and bow patterns in the album's mid-tempo sections.

### Pearl Demon Drive: Direct-Drive Precision

Pearl's Demon Drive direct-drive double pedal anchored Greiner's kick technique across the album's sustained double-bass passages. The direct mechanism's consistent pedal feel at both feet is central to the symmetry audible in tracks like "Bloodletter" and "Lifeline."

## Grammy Context: "King of Sorrow"

"King of Sorrow" — Phantom Anthem's lead single — received a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance at the 60th Grammy Awards (January 28, 2018). The nomination placed August Burns Red among the highest-profile metal acts recognized by the Recording Academy and established *Phantom Anthem* as ABR's most mainstream-facing studio statement.

## FAQ

**What cymbals does Matt Greiner use on Phantom Anthem?**
On *Phantom Anthem* (2017), Matt Greiner used Zildjian A Custom and Sabian AAX cymbals — Zildjian A Custom 14" hi-hats, 16" crash, and 20" ride; Sabian AAX 18" crash and 18" Chinese. The A Custom / AAX combination delivers bright, cutting character suited to *Phantom Anthem*'s direct metalcore production.

**Was Phantom Anthem Grammy-nominated?**
Yes. "King of Sorrow" from *Phantom Anthem* (2017) received a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance at the 60th Grammy Awards (2018), placing August Burns Red among the top metal acts recognized by the Recording Academy.

**What tuning does Matt Greiner use on Phantom Anthem?**
On *Phantom Anthem* (2017), Matt Greiner tuned his Pearl Reference Pure kit for attack and projection: medium-high snare tension on the Pearl Signature steel snare, Remo Powerstroke 3 bass drum heads for controlled resonance, and Ambassador Coated tom heads at mid-range tension for balanced sustain and definition in the dense mix.

**What album did August Burns Red release after Found in Far Away Places?**
August Burns Red released *Phantom Anthem* in 2017 as the follow-up to *Found in Far Away Places* (2015), then *Beacon* in 2023. *Phantom Anthem* was released September 15, 2017 on Fearless Records and earned a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance for "King of Sorrow" (60th Grammy Awards, 2018).
