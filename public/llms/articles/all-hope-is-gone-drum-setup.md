# Joey Jordison's Drum Setup on Slipknot's All Hope Is Gone (2008)

> Complete breakdown of Joey Jordison's drum setup on Slipknot's All Hope Is Gone (2008). Tama Starclassic Bubinga 9-piece, Zildjian K Custom Dark cymbals — the final studio album recorded with Joey before his 2013 departure.

## Overview

Released on August 26, 2008, Slipknot's fourth studio album *All Hope Is Gone* arrived at a pivotal moment in the band's career. Debuting at #1 on the Billboard 200 — the first Slipknot album to do so — it demonstrated the band's commercial ascent while simultaneously dividing the fanbase with its more expansive, melodic production approach. At the kit was Joey Jordison, five years removed from the bludgeoning intensity of *Iowa* and pushing his drumming in new directions.

*All Hope Is Gone* represents the final studio document of Jordison's tenure with Slipknot. He would be dismissed from the band in December 2013 under circumstances that remained publicly unaddressed for years — Joey revealing in 2021 that he had been battling transverse myelitis, a spinal cord inflammation that had severely affected his ability to play. The 2008 sessions, then, are the last time audiences hear Jordison at full power within the Slipknot framework.

Produced by Dave Fortman at Henson Recording Studios in Hollywood, the album took a deliberately more varied approach than its predecessors. Where *Iowa* was an assault without pause and *Vol. 3* experimented with melody under Rick Rubin's direction, *All Hope Is Gone* aimed for both extremes simultaneously — the blast-beat violence of "Psychosocial" alongside the orchestrated melancholy of "Dead Memories." Joey's drumming tracked every shift, expanding his already considerable palette with a larger kit configuration and a revised cymbal setup tuned for the album's wider dynamic range.

### The Bubinga Beast: Tama Starclassic 9-Piece

For *All Hope Is Gone*, Joey Jordison ran a Tama Starclassic Bubinga configuration — a notable departure from the Pearl Masterworks that defined the *Iowa* era. The Starclassic Bubinga is built from one of the densest commercially available drumshell woods, delivering a low-frequency weight and sustained fundamental tone that maple or birch configurations cannot match.

The 9-piece expanded layout added a third floor tom (20" x 18") to the standard six-tom configuration Jordison had used since Slipknot's early years. This gave him an enormous tonal range at the low end: fills descending from the 8" rack tom through all three floor toms produced a cascading, orchestral sweep rarely heard at extreme metal tempos. On "All Hope Is Gone" and "Sulfur," these extended tom voicings are audible in the fills between sections.

The double 22" x 18" bass drums remained unchanged — Jordison's foot technique was so well-established by 2008 that altering the kick drum dimensions would have introduced unnecessary variables.

**Kit configuration:**
- Bass Drums: 22" x 18" (x2)
- Rack Toms: 8" x 7", 10" x 8", 12" x 10", 13" x 11"
- Floor Toms: 16" x 16", 18" x 16", 20" x 18"

### Zildjian K Custom Dark: A New Palette

The *All Hope Is Gone* era saw Joey Jordison move to Zildjian K Custom Dark cymbals — a clear sonic statement matching the album's darker tonal ambitions. The K Custom Dark series produces a complex, dry, dark sound with prominent overtones and a quick, controlled decay. Where the Paiste RUDE cymbals of the *Iowa* era delivered raw, aggressive sustain, the K Custom Dark offered a more nuanced, layered character suited to an album that demanded drums function across a wider dynamic range.

**Cymbal setup:**
- Hi-Hats: Zildjian K Custom Dark 14"
- Crashes: 16", 18", and 19" K Custom Dark
- Ride: Zildjian K Custom Dark 20"
- China: Zildjian K Custom Dark 18"
- Splash: Zildjian K 10"

### Track Highlights

**"Psychosocial"** (142 BPM) — Grammy-nominated for Best Metal Performance. Machine-gun double bass in the verse drives relentless forward momentum. One of Jordison's most widely cited recorded grooves.

**"All Hope Is Gone"** (195+ BPM) — The album's most extreme track. Blast beats throughout, extended tom fills showcasing the 9-piece range, closest to Iowa-era aggression.

**"Sulfur"** (120 BPM) — Mid-tempo groove track. Shows Jordison's pocket and restraint. K Custom Dark ride is particularly audible.

**"Dead Memories"** (108 BPM) — Radio single with restrained drumming. Ghost notes on snare, full-kit crashes landing with maximum impact by contrast with quiet verses.

**"Snuff"** — Acoustic ballad. Every drum hit is deliberate; demonstrates Jordison's dynamic range beyond extreme tempos.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What drums did Joey Jordison use on All Hope Is Gone?**
Joey Jordison recorded *All Hope Is Gone* in 2008 using a Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit in a 9-piece expanded configuration. The setup included double 22" x 18" bass drums, four rack toms (8", 10", 12", 13"), and three floor toms (16", 18", and 20"). Bubinga shells deliver a heavier fundamental tone and longer sustain than maple, suited to Dave Fortman's expansive production.

**How many drums did Joey Jordison play on All Hope Is Gone?**
Joey Jordison used a 9-piece drum kit: two 22" x 18" bass drums, four rack toms (8", 10", 12", and 13"), and three floor toms (16", 18", and 20"). The third floor tom at 20" was a notable expansion from his earlier Slipknot setups.

**What cymbals did Joey Jordison use on All Hope Is Gone?**
Joey Jordison used Zildjian K Custom Dark cymbals — a significant shift from the Paiste RUDE and 2002 series of the Iowa era. The setup included 14" hi-hats, crashes in 16", 18", and 19" sizes, a 20" ride, an 18" China, and a 10" splash.

**Is All Hope Is Gone Joey Jordison's last album with Slipknot?**
Yes. *All Hope Is Gone* (2008) is the final studio album Joey Jordison recorded with Slipknot. He was dismissed from the band in December 2013. In 2021, Jordison revealed he had been battling transverse myelitis, which severely affected his ability to play during that period. Jay Weinberg joined Slipknot for the 2014 album *5: The Gray Chapter*.
