# Angel of Retribution Drum Setup: Scott Travis's Halford Reunion Kit

> Complete breakdown of Scott Travis's drum gear on Judas Priest's Angel of Retribution (2005). Discover the Pearl Reference kit, Pearl Free-Floating snare, and how Travis re-captured Painkiller intensity after Rob Halford's 14-year absence.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [Scott Travis](/llms/drummers/scott-travis.md)
**Band / Album:** Judas Priest — *Angel of Retribution* (2005)
**Genre:** Heavy Metal

## Overview

When Rob Halford rejoined Judas Priest in 2003 after a 14-year split, the entire metal world held its breath. The question wasn't just whether Halford could still hit those impossible high notes — it was whether the reunited Priest could recapture the fire of their greatest era. *Angel of Retribution* (2005) answered that question with a resounding yes.

At the heart of that answer was Scott Travis, whose drumming on this album stands as one of the most significant performances of his career. Between the Tama-powered fury of *Painkiller* (1990) and the modern confidence of *Firepower* (2018), Angel of Retribution is the bridge — the album where Travis switched to a Pearl Reference kit and had to prove he could match the Painkiller standard after a decade-plus away from Rob Halford's voice and range.

The album debuted at #13 on the US Billboard 200 and #5 in the UK — remarkable chart performance for a traditional heavy metal album in 2005. Critics and fans alike hailed it as a triumphant return. Scott Travis's drum work was central to that triumph, delivering both the thunderous moments ("Judas Rising," "Deal with the Devil") and the more measured, groove-driven passages that showed his evolution as a player.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series (Black Ice finish)
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass Shell Snare, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian — A Custom / A Series
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator P-2002B Double Pedal; Pearl H-2050 Hi-Hat Stand; Pearl D-3000 Roadster Throne; Vic Firth 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Coated Ambassador (snare batter), Remo Emperor Clear (toms), Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium tension — balancing sustain with attack, warmer than Painkiller era

### Bass Drum Configuration

The 22" x 18" bass drum is deeper than Painkiller's 22" x 16" — more resonance and fundamental tone for a 2005 mix. Travis uses single or double pedal on a per-track basis. "Judas Rising," "Deal with the Devil," and "Hellrider" use double bass at full intensity. "Revolution" and "Worth Fighting For" use single pedal for an open, melodic groove feel.

### Pearl Reference Kit: Warmer Than Painkiller

The Pearl Reference hybrid shell (maple/falkata with reinforcement rings) produces a rounder, warmer tone than the Tama Artstar II birch of the Painkiller era. Birch delivers aggressive high-frequency attack; the Pearl hybrid rounds that attack with more low-mid body. The result is a drum sound that sits differently in a 2005 mix — broader and warmer, less razor-sharp than 1990.

### Pearl Free-Floating Snare: A Different Crack

Pearl's Free-Floating system isolates the shell from the throw-off mounting hardware, allowing the brass shell to vibrate freely. The 14" x 6.5" brass shell delivers longer sustain and fuller tone than a steel snare. On "Revolution" and "Loch Ness," the snare's tonal depth adds dimension; on "Judas Rising" and "Deal with the Devil," it delivers a powerful, musical crack through the dual-guitar wall.

### Zildjian Cymbals: The Sound of the Reunion

The switch from Paiste (Painkiller era) to Zildjian A Custom represents a clear sonic shift. Travis's setup: Zildjian A Custom 14" hi-hats, Zildjian A 16" Medium Thin Crash, Zildjian A Custom 17" and 18" Projection Crashes, Zildjian A 20" Medium Ride, Zildjian A 18" China. The A Custom Projection crashes cut through the combined volume of Glenn Tipton and K.K. Downing's guitar stacks. The China cymbal is used selectively — each hit a statement, not background texture.

## Key Facts

- Pearl Reference kit replaced Painkiller-era Tama Artstar II birch — warmer, rounder tone
- Pearl Free-Floating brass snare offers fuller sustain than the steel snare of 1990
- Cymbals switched from Paiste to Zildjian A Custom series
- Double bass used selectively per track — not constant as on Painkiller
- Album debuted #13 US Billboard 200, #5 UK Albums Chart
- Produced by Roy Z at Pie in the Sky Studios (Tampa) and Metalworks (Toronto)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What kit did Scott Travis use on Angel of Retribution?**
On Angel of Retribution (2005), Scott Travis played a Pearl Reference series kit in Black Ice finish. The Pearl Reference features a hybrid shell construction combining maple and falkata with reinforcement rings, producing a warmer tone than the Tama Artstar II birch kit from Painkiller (1990). His configuration included a 22" x 18" bass drum, 10" and 12" rack toms, and 14" and 16" floor toms.

**How does the Angel of Retribution drum sound compare to Painkiller?**
The Painkiller kit was Tama Artstar II birch — aggressive, high-frequency attack. The Pearl Reference on Angel of Retribution is warmer, with more low-mid body. Snare changed from Tama steel to Pearl Free-Floating brass — warmer sustain, fuller tone. Cymbals switched from Paiste to Zildjian A Custom. Travis also uses double bass selectively on Angel of Retribution rather than constantly. See the [Painkiller drum setup article](/llms/articles/painkiller-drum-setup.md) for direct comparison.

**Did Scott Travis use double bass on Angel of Retribution?**
Yes, on selected tracks. His Pearl Eliminator P-2002B double pedal appears on "Judas Rising," "Deal with the Devil," and "Hellrider." On tracks like "Revolution" and "Worth Fighting For," Travis plays single pedal. The selective approach was a conscious production decision with Roy Z: by reserving double bass for the most intense moments, each double-kick passage hits harder by contrast.

**What cymbals did Scott Travis use on Angel of Retribution?**
Zildjian cymbals — a departure from Paiste on Painkiller. Setup: Zildjian A Custom 14" hi-hats, Zildjian A 16" Medium Thin Crash, Zildjian A Custom 17" and 18" Projection Crashes, Zildjian A 20" Medium Ride, Zildjian A 18" China.
