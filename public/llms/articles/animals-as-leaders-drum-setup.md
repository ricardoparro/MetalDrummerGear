## Overview

When Matt Garstka joined Animals as Leaders in 2012, replacing Navene Koperweis, he brought something the band had never had: a Berklee-educated jazz-fusion drummer applying polyrhythmic academic rigor to the djent and progressive metal context that Tosin Abasi and Javier Reyes had pioneered. His first album with the band, *The Joy of Motion* (2014), immediately established the new Animals as Leaders sonic identity — and established Garstka as one of the most significant drummers of his generation.

*The Joy of Motion* expanded the band's compositional scope beyond the earlier albums. Garstka's arrival brought dynamic complexity: ghost notes threading through passages that a less-trained drummer would leave empty, traditional grip in a genre dominated by matched, polyrhythmic hi-hat patterns that created independent rhythmic layers over the guitar texture, and an overall sensitivity that made the band's dense arrangements breathe.

For the *Joy of Motion* sessions and the subsequent *The Madness of Many* (2016), Garstka played his Pearl Masterworks kit — a compact four-piece configuration that prioritized dynamic range and articulation over the massive setups typical of extreme metal. Meinl Byzance cymbals provided the dark, complex warmth his jazz-influenced playing demanded. Together, this setup produced the drum sound that defined djent's progressive evolution in the mid-2010s.

Note: The earlier Animals as Leaders albums — the self-titled debut (2009) and *Weightless* (2011) — featured Navene Koperweis on drums. Garstka's Animals as Leaders work begins with *The Joy of Motion* (2014).

### Pearl Masterworks: Compact Power for Complex Music

Matt Garstka's Pearl Masterworks setup for Animals as Leaders is defined by what it lacks as much as what it includes: a single 20-inch bass drum, two rack toms, and one floor tom in a configuration deliberately smaller than the massive kits that dominate extreme metal. This minimalism is a musical philosophy statement — where most djent drummers pile up cymbals and drums as visual declarations of technical ambition, Garstka chose restraint and responsiveness.

The 20-inch bass drum is the most immediately surprising element. At a size more common in jazz than metal, it produces a focused, articulate kick tone with faster attack and decay than 22" or 24" alternatives. For the complex bass drum patterns in Animals as Leaders' music — intricate single-bass ostinatos that complement the band's complex riffing rather than duplicating it — this quick-speaking character is essential. Every note registers distinctly, even in the fastest passages.

Pearl Masterworks shells are all-maple custom construction, built at Pearl's USA Custom Shop. The maple's warmth and resonance produced a full-bodied tom sound that gave the four-piece layout more tonal range than its size might suggest. MasterCast die-cast hoops provided additional attack and focus on each drum — sharper fundamental definition that suited Garstka's articulate, precision-oriented technique.

### Pearl Garstka Signature Snare: Sensitivity Meets Crack

The Pearl Matt Garstka Signature snare was developed collaboratively with Pearl for the specific demands of his playing style: a drum that could whisper in ghost note passages and crack in backbeats without any adjustment between modes. At 14" x 5" — shallower than most metal snares — it was designed for sensitivity and quick response rather than maximum power.

The maple shell's warmth served Garstka's dynamic approach throughout Animals as Leaders recordings. Tracks with extended quiet passages featured Garstka threading ghost notes through the arrangement with the Signature snare's barely-audible response. When the arrangement demanded full backbeat authority, the same drum cracked with authority, the maple warmth preventing harshness at high volume.

The unique bearing edge calibrated for this model was engineered for dual-mode playing: sensitive enough for jazz-quality ghost notes at three-inch stick heights, powerful enough for rimshots at full force.

### Meinl Byzance: Dark Complexity Over Aggressive Brightness

Meinl Byzance cymbals are the defining element of Garstka's sound on Animals as Leaders recordings. Where most progressive metal drummers choose Zildjian or Sabian for bright, articulate attack, Garstka's Byzance selection brought a darker, more harmonically complex character to *The Joy of Motion* — a choice that matched the band's compositional sophistication.

The Byzance series' hand-hammered B20 bronze produces sustained, complex overtones that interact with the arrangement rather than simply punctuating it. In Animals as Leaders' music — where guitar textures are deliberately layered and tonally complex — brighter cymbals can create frequency conflict. The Byzance's darker overtones occupy a different harmonic space, allowing guitar and drum tones to coexist with minimal compression needed in the mix.

The 15" Dual Hi-Hats are central to Garstka's technical signature — the "Dual" construction combining brilliant and raw finishes for complex overtones that respond differently at different dynamics. The 22" Sand Ride's dry, articulate bow attack and defined bell tone have made it one of the most-copied cymbal choices in modern progressive metal. Its restrained character provides groove section texture without cumulative wash, essential in arrangements where the ride must complement complex guitar texture rather than dominating it.

## FAQ

**What drums does Matt Garstka use for Animals as Leaders?**
Matt Garstka plays a Pearl Masterworks Maple kit for Animals as Leaders — a compact, USA Custom Shop all-maple configuration centered on a 20" x 16" bass drum, 10" x 7" and 12" x 8" rack toms, and a 14" x 14" floor tom. His snare is the Pearl Matt Garstka Signature model at 14" x 5" (maple shell, shallower than typical metal snares for sensitivity and quick response). Cymbals are Meinl Byzance series throughout, and his bass drum pedal is a Pearl Demon Drive single. He joined Animals as Leaders in 2012, and his first album with the band was The Joy of Motion (2014). For complete details, see [Matt Garstka's full kit breakdown](/articles/whats-in-matt-garstkas-kit).

**When did Matt Garstka join Animals as Leaders?**
Matt Garstka joined Animals as Leaders in 2012, replacing Navene Koperweis (now known as Navene-K). The two earlier Animals as Leaders albums — the self-titled debut (2009) and Weightless (2011) — featured Koperweis on drums. Garstka's first Animals as Leaders album was The Joy of Motion, released in 2014. His second was The Madness of Many (2016), followed by Parrhesia (2022). His arrival transformed the band's drum identity, introducing polyrhythmic complexity and dynamic sensitivity that became central to their evolving sound.

**Why does Matt Garstka use a 20-inch bass drum in a metal band?**
Matt Garstka uses a 20" bass drum — notably smaller than the 22" and 24" bass drums typical in metal — because Animals as Leaders' music prioritizes articulation and dynamic complexity over raw power. The 20" size produces a faster attack and quicker decay than larger alternatives, which is essential for the intricate, independent bass drum patterns that characterize AAL's compositions. This small-kit philosophy reflects Garstka's jazz-influenced priority: musical precision over visual scale.

**What cymbals does Matt Garstka use for Animals as Leaders?**
Matt Garstka uses Meinl Byzance cymbals throughout his Animals as Leaders setup. His core setup includes Meinl Byzance 15" Dual Hi-Hats, 18" Extra Dry Thin Crash, 19" and 20" Dual Crashes, a 22" Sand Ride, a stacked crash combination, and an 18" Vintage Trash Hat China. The Byzance series' B20 bronze construction and hand-hammering produce sustained, warm overtones that complement AAL's complex guitar textures without frequency conflict.
