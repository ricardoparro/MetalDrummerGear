## Overview

Released on September 26, 2005, *Are You Dead Yet?* is Children of Bodom's fifth full-length album and their most commercially successful record — debuting at number 56 on the Finnish charts and marking the point where the band's sound evolved toward broader accessibility without abandoning the technical foundation that defined their earlier work. Recorded at Astia Studio in Imatra, Finland with Anssi Kippo, the album documented a deliberate shift in approach: more mid-paced grooves, shorter song structures, and a streamlined production that brought CoB's sound to a wider audience.

At the center of this evolution was Jaska Raatikainen's gear transition — the move from the Tama Starclassic era of *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003) to a Pearl Masters Premium Maple setup with Zildjian A Custom cymbals and Pearl Eliminator double pedals. This was the beginning of the Pearl endorsement relationship that would define Raatikainen's setup for the remainder of Children of Bodom's career, and the combination produced a different tonal character: Pearl's more focused attack and Zildjian A Custom's multi-stage lathing bringing a brighter, more defined sound than the Tama/Paiste combination.

*Are You Dead Yet?* sits at the mid-point of the Jaska Raatikainen discovery arc: it fills the gear transition gap between the Tama Starclassic era (*Hate Crew Deathroll*, 2003) and the established Pearl Masters years (*Blooddrunk*, 2008). This article breaks down every component of Raatikainen's setup on *Are You Dead Yet?* — the Pearl Masters Premium Maple shells, the Zildjian A Custom cymbal selection, the Pearl Eliminator double pedal, and the adjusted stylistic approach behind CoB's most commercially successful record.

### Pearl Masters Premium Maple: The Beginning of the Pearl Era

For *Are You Dead Yet?*, Jaska Raatikainen transitioned to the Pearl Masters Premium Maple — Pearl's professional-grade production shell line and the beginning of an endorsement relationship that would carry through the remainder of Children of Bodom's career. The shift from Tama Starclassic Maple brought a different construction philosophy and a noticeably different tonal character to CoB's drum sound.

Pearl's Masters Premium Maple shells are built around a six-ply all-maple construction with tighter manufacturing tolerances than the Tama Starclassic's approach. The result is a more focused, immediate attack with less sustain resonance — a sound well-suited to the more mid-paced, groove-oriented arrangements that *Are You Dead Yet?* favoured compared to the sustained-velocity extremity of *Follow the Reaper* and *Hate Crew Deathroll*.

The double bass drum configuration — twin 22" x 18" kick drums — remained central to Raatikainen's setup, now paired with the Pearl Eliminator double pedal. Even with *Are You Dead Yet?*'s shift toward more accessible tempos, Children of Bodom's arrangements still required the double-kick foundation as a structural element: tracks like "In Your Face" and "Are You Dead Yet?" deploy double-bass patterns not as continuous velocity showcases but as dynamic punctuation within groove-centred arrangements.

The compact tom spread — 10" and 12" rack toms, 14" and 16" floor toms — continued from the Tama era, maintaining the tonal range for CoB's melodic fill language in a new shell construction context. The Pearl Masters' more focused attack meant that the melody-following fill work registered with a different character than the warmer, more open Starclassic sound.

### Pearl Masters 14" x 5.5" Maple Snare

Raatikainen's snare on *Are You Dead Yet?* was a Pearl Masters maple model — matched to the kit's shell series and consistent with the overall Pearl endorsement transition. The 14" x 5.5" dimensions maintained the dimensions of his Tama Starclassic snare while the Pearl construction brought a subtly different character: tighter maple construction producing a slightly more focused crack with less shell resonance than the Starclassic model.

The Pearl Masters snare's character suited the more groove-oriented arrangements on *Are You Dead Yet?* well. Where the *Hate Crew Deathroll* snare sound — recorded at Finnvox Studios with Mikko Karmila's production — emphasized the aggressive leading edge, Anssi Kippo's Astia Studio approach with the Pearl snare produced a sound that balanced attack with body, fitting the album's more mid-paced, accessible arrangements.

Ghost notes and dynamic snare work are more prominent on *Are You Dead Yet?* than on any previous CoB album — reflecting both the more groove-centred song structures and the Pearl snare's natural dynamics across a wider dynamic range than blast-and-fill-dominated playing requires.

### Zildjian A Custom: The Transition from Paiste to Zildjian's Precision Series

Jaska Raatikainen's cymbal setup on *Are You Dead Yet?* marked the second major cymbal transition of his Children of Bodom career — from the Paiste 2002 / Sound Edge combination of the Tama era to Zildjian A Custom and K Custom, a combination that would define his sound through the remainder of CoB's catalog.

The Zildjian A Custom series uses a multi-stage lathing process that produces a brighter, more defined sound than the Paiste 2002's character — more cutting in the upper frequencies, with a faster decay that keeps the cymbal mix clean in dense arrangements. Where the Paiste 2002's CuSn8 alloy produced warmth alongside brightness, the A Custom's B20 bronze with brilliant finish delivers precision and immediacy. This transition is part of what gives *Are You Dead Yet?* its different sonic character compared to the Tama-era albums.

The A Custom 14" hi-hats provided the articulation foundation for *Are You Dead Yet?*'s more groove-centred rhythmic approach. The multi-stage lathing gave a faster, more defined chick sound than the Paiste Sound Edge — clean and present at the tempos the album favoured without becoming harsh.

The Zildjian K Custom 20" ride brought a darker, more complex character into the setup — its warmer bell and body sound creating a different textural dimension from the Paiste 2002 ride's brighter character. This combination of A Custom crashes and K Custom ride is a classic Zildjian pairing that balances cut and warmth across different cymbal functions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drums did Jaska Raatikainen use on Are You Dead Yet?**

A: On *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005), Jaska Raatikainen transitioned to Pearl Masters Premium Maple drums — his first Pearl album, marking the beginning of an endorsement relationship that would continue through Children of Bodom's career. The setup centered on twin 22" x 18" bass drums, rack toms (10" and 12"), and floor toms (14" and 16"), all in Pearl's six-ply maple construction. This was the beginning of the Pearl Masters era that replaced the Tama Starclassic Maple of *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003). For Raatikainen's complete gear history, see the [Jaska Raatikainen drummer profile](/drummer/jaska-raatikainen).

**Q: What pedals did Jaska Raatikainen use on Are You Dead Yet?**

A: Jaska Raatikainen used the Pearl Eliminator double pedal on *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005) — his transition from the Tama Iron Cobra of the Starclassic era. The Eliminator's interchangeable cam system allowed him to dial in a specific response feel suited to *Are You Dead Yet?*'s more groove-oriented arrangements: controlled beater follow-through for mid-paced patterns, with the capability for full double-kick velocity when tracks like "In Your Face" demanded it. The Pearl Eliminator would remain his pedal choice through the remainder of CoB's career. See the [Jaska Raatikainen full kit guide](/articles/jaska-raatikainen-drum-setup) for his complete pedal history.

**Q: What cymbals did Jaska Raatikainen use on Are You Dead Yet?**

This marked the transition from the Paiste 2002 / Sound Edge combination of the *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003) sessions. The A Custom's multi-stage lathing brought a brighter, more defined character than the Paiste 2002's CuSn8 alloy. For more context on how this setup evolved, see the [Blooddrunk drum setup article](/articles/blooddrunk-drum-setup).

**Q: Why is Are You Dead Yet? different from earlier CoB albums?**

A: *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005) represented a deliberate shift in Children of Bodom's approach toward more accessible, groove-centred arrangements — shorter song structures, more mid-paced tempos, and a streamlined production that brought their sound to a wider audience. The album's #56 Finnish chart debut was their commercial peak at the time. For Jaska Raatikainen, this meant adapting his drumming style to serve groove-oriented tracks alongside the high-velocity passages that defined *Follow the Reaper* (2000) and *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003). The Pearl Masters and Pearl Eliminator setup suited this more varied approach — different tonal character and adjustable cam response compared to the Tama era.
