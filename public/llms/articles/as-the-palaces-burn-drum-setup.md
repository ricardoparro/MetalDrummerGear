# As the Palaces Burn Drum Setup: Chris Adler's Lamb of God Breakthrough Kit (2003)

> Complete breakdown of Chris Adler's drum setup on Lamb of God's As the Palaces Burn (2003). Mapex Pro M kit, Zildjian A Custom cymbals, and the groove metal technique that launched LoG internationally.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [Chris Adler](/llms/drummers/chris-adler.md)
**Band / Album:** Lamb of God — *As the Palaces Burn* (2003)
**Genre:** Groove Metal / New Wave of American Heavy Metal

## Overview

Released on April 15, 2003 on Prosthetic Records, Lamb of God's "As the Palaces Burn" was the record that transformed the Richmond, Virginia metalheads from respected underground acts into bona fide international metal forces. Where "New American Gospel" (2000) had established the template, "As the Palaces Burn" refined and amplified it — tighter riffs, more dynamic songwriting, and Chris Adler's groove metal drumming at a level that grabbed metal audiences worldwide.

The album arrived at a pivotal moment in heavy music. Nu-metal was fading, metalcore was still finding its footing, and the metal underground was hungry for something that bridged the aggression of extreme metal with the accessibility and groove that could fill venues. "As the Palaces Burn" was exactly that record — and "Omerta," the album's iconic instrumental, gave the drumming world a showcase piece that metal drummers still study decades later.

For Chris Adler, this was the album where his Mapex Pro M setup became truly battle-tested. The dual 22" kick drums, three rack toms (10", 12", 13"), and two floor toms represented a broader configuration than he would later refine for "Ashes of the Wake" — a sign of a drummer still exploring the full reach of his setup. His cymbal configuration featured Zildjian A Custom cymbals, in the period immediately preceding the Meinl Byzance endorsement partnership that would define his sound from "Ashes of the Wake" (2004) onward.

Producer Machine (Mark Lewis) — who would guide Lamb of God through "Ashes of the Wake," "Sacrament," and "Wrath" — captured Adler's drumming with the groove-first philosophy that would characterize their entire collaboration.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Mapex Pro M Series (all-maple shells, 22"×18" dual kick, 10"/12"/13" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms)
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther Maple, 14" x 5.5" (die-cast hoops)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Pedals:** Mapex P400 Double Pedal (used as two singles on dual kick)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A American Classic (pre-signature era)
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke P3 Clear (kick batter), Remo Emperor Clear (tom batter), Remo Controlled Sound Coated (snare batter)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high for crack and ghost note response

### The Groove Metal Foundation: Adler's Mapex Pro M Configuration

For "As the Palaces Burn," Chris Adler's Mapex Pro M Series kit represented the professional-grade foundation that would carry him through the breakthrough years. The all-maple shells provided the fundamental combination of attack and warmth that groove metal demands — punchy enough to cut through Lamb of God's guitar assault, resonant enough to groove with undeniable rhythmic swagger.

The three-rack-tom configuration — 10", 12", and 13" — gave Adler broader melodic reach for fills than the two-rack setup he would later standardize for "Ashes of the Wake." The dual 22"×18" bass drums were already non-negotiable by 2003: two independent kick drums meant independent tonal character per foot, visual presence matching Lamb of God's intensity, and flexibility to tune each drum independently.

This same Pro M Series kit would power "Ashes of the Wake" (2004), providing tonal continuity between the two albums even as the cymbal setup changed dramatically with the Meinl Byzance endorsement.

### The Pre-Meinl Arsenal: Zildjian A Custom

"As the Palaces Burn" captures Chris Adler's cymbal sound in transition — the Zildjian A Custom setup was the last configuration before the Meinl Byzance endorsement partnership that began with "Ashes of the Wake" (2004).

The Zildjian A Custom series delivers bright, cutting, bell-forward clarity. In "As the Palaces Burn"'s mix, the A Custom cymbals sit with presence and sparkle, cutting through the guitar density. The 14" hi-hats are notably larger than the 13" Meinl Byzance hats Adler would adopt for "Ashes of the Wake" — moving to 13" gave him faster left-foot response for his developing "pivot technique."

The China cymbal is already present and already essential. Even in the Zildjian A Custom configuration, Adler's China accent placement — trashy, explosive punctuations against riff entrances and structural transitions — is the signature element that defines Lamb of God's rhythmic identity.

### Key Track: Omerta

Omerta is significant for drummers because it demonstrates Chris Adler's dynamic control and groove-first philosophy as an instrumental showcase with nowhere to hide. The track builds from near silence to full-kit assault, with Adler's drumming serving the arrangement's drama rather than filling space. The slower tempo exposes the full tonal range of his Mapex Pro M kit, the precision of his Zildjian A Custom China placements, and the foundation of his pivot technique hi-hat independence work.

## As the Palaces Burn vs Ashes of the Wake: The Key Gear Differences

## Key Facts

- Released April 15, 2003 on Prosthetic Records — Lamb of God's international breakthrough
- Mapex Pro M Series with three-rack-tom configuration (streamlined to two for Ashes)
- Zildjian A Custom cymbals — last album before Meinl Byzance endorsement partnership
- "Omerta" is the most-cited Lamb of God track in drumming study communities
- Produced by Machine (Mark Lewis) — first of five LoG albums with this producer
- Fills the discography gap: New American Gospel (2000) → AtPB (2003) → Ashes of the Wake (2004)
- Estimated kit value: $2,000-3,000 (2003 Pro M configuration)
- Estimated cymbal value: $1,200-1,800 (2003 Zildjian A Custom setup)
