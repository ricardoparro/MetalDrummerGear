> Discover Adrian Erlandsson's drum setup on At the Gates' 2014 reunion album At War with Reality — the Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit, Sabian AAX/HHX cymbals, and Studio Fredman sessions that brought the band's drummer back after a 19-year studio gap.

## Overview

At War with Reality, released on Century Media Records in late October 2014, is the album fans of At the Gates never expected to get: the band's first new studio material since Slaughter of the Soul in 1995. At the Gates had disbanded in 1996 at the height of their influence, reunited for live shows from 2007 onward, and finally committed to writing and recording new music together in 2013 — closing a 19-year gap that had, by that point, seen the Gothenburg melodic death metal sound they helped invent become one of the most widely imitated templates in extreme music.

Adrian Erlandsson was back behind the kit for the sessions, recorded at the band's old home base, Studio Fredman in Gothenburg, between June 24 and July 18, 2014. Fredrik Nordström — the producer whose direct, dry drum sound had defined Slaughter of the Soul two decades earlier — returned as engineer, this time working alongside Henrik Udd, with Jens Bogren handling mixing and mastering at Fascination Street Studios. The pairing of Nordström's studio and Bogren's modern mix sensibility gave the album a sound that honored the band's 90s foundation while sitting comfortably next to 2014's heavier, more polished metal productions.

Erlandsson's drumming setup had changed dramatically in the years between the two albums. Where Slaughter of the Soul was tracked on unglamorous, undocumented studio house gear, At War with Reality captured him on the Tama Starclassic Bubinga rig he had developed across a career that took him through The Haunted, Cradle of Filth, and Paradise Lost — a mature, professional setup built for a drummer who by 2014 had spent nearly two decades touring and recording internationally at the highest level of extreme metal.

The album itself is a loose concept record inspired by magical realism and the short fiction of Jorge Luis Borges — track titles like "The Circular Ruins" and "The Book of Sand" draw directly from Borges' work. Musically, it picks up the Gothenburg thread where Slaughter of the Soul left it: tremolo-picked melodic riffing over relentless D-beat and blast-beat drumming, delivered with the same discipline that made the band's 90s work so influential. Commercially, it was a triumph, debuting at number three in Sweden and reaching number 53 on the Billboard 200 — remarkable chart positions for a band returning after nearly two decades away.

This article breaks down Adrian Erlandsson's drum setup for At War with Reality — the Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit, Sabian cymbal configuration, and the Studio Fredman/Fascination Street production chain that brought At the Gates' drummer back into the studio for the first time since 1995.

- **Drums:** Tama Tama Starclassic Bubinga (Transparent black finish over Bubinga shells finish)

- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high tension for articulation with added body from the deeper shell

### Tama Starclassic Bubinga: The Rig Built Across Two Decades

By the time At the Gates reconvened to write and record At War with Reality, Adrian Erlandsson had spent the better part of two decades building a professional touring and recording career away from the band — with The Haunted (which he co-founded in 1996), Cradle of Filth (1999–2006), and Paradise Lost (2009–2016). The kit he brought to those 2014 Studio Fredman sessions reflects that development: a Tama Starclassic Bubinga in a transparent black finish, the same rig documented from his Paradise Lost years and carried forward, essentially unchanged, into every At the Gates and Haunted recording since.

The four-tom, double-bass configuration — twin 22" x 18" kick drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, 16" and 18" floor toms — mirrors the double-kick approach he used on Slaughter of the Soul, but on dramatically more capable hardware. Bubinga is a dense African hardwood that Tama has used since the 1990s for its Starclassic series specifically because of its low-end focus and strong projection, qualities suited to a drummer who needs his kick drums to cut cleanly through layered, tremolo-picked guitar tracks.

Where the Slaughter of the Soul kit was studio house gear chosen out of necessity, the Starclassic Bubinga is a rig Erlandsson selected and refined across years of touring — the difference between a budget death metal band's constraints in 1994 and an established professional's considered setup twenty years later.

### Tama Starclassic Bubinga Snare: Depth and Cut

Erlandsson's snare for At War with Reality was the matching Bubinga-shell snare from his Starclassic setup — a 14" x 6.5" drum considerably deeper and more resonant than the studio snare used on Slaughter of the Soul nearly twenty years earlier. Where the 1995 sessions produced a dry, tight crack through minimal-ambience close-miking, the At War with Reality snare carries more body and depth, in keeping with Jens Bogren's fuller, more contemporary mix.

The Bubinga shell's dense, hardwood construction produces a full-bodied crack with strong low-mid presence, giving the snare enough weight to sit prominently against Studio Fredman's still-direct recording approach while retaining the articulation needed for the album's blast-beat passages. Fredrik Nordström's engineering kept the drum sound honest and largely unprocessed at the tracking stage, with the final tonal shaping happening at Fascination Street during Bogren's mix.

Compared to the thin, high-tuned crack of the Slaughter of the Soul snare, the Starclassic Bubinga's extra shell depth gives Erlandsson a fuller sound better suited to 2014's denser, more layered guitar production — a clear marker of how both his gear and the album's overall sonic ambition had matured since 1995.

### Sabian AAX/HHX: A Mature Cymbal Voice

Erlandsson's cymbal setup for At War with Reality moved him firmly into Sabian's professional AAX and HHX lines — a step up in both consistency and tonal control from the Zildjian A-Series cymbals documented on Slaughter of the Soul. The 15" HHX Xcelerator hi-hats deliver a bright, controlled sound built for the fast, articulate patterns his D-beat and blast-beat vocabulary demands, while offering more consistent response across dynamic levels than the budget hi-hats available to him in 1994.

The AAX Iso 20" crash and AAX Paragon 19" China give the record its accent character — the China cymbal in particular carries forward the same aggressive, trashy role it played on Slaughter of the Soul, marking riff transitions throughout tracks like "Death and the Labyrinth" and "The Head of the Hydra" with the same rhythmic function, now delivered through more refined, professionally engineered metal.

This Sabian AAX/HHX combination is the setup Erlandsson carried over from his Paradise Lost years and has kept largely unchanged since — a stable, mature cymbal voice built over nearly two decades of professional recording and touring, in contrast to the studio-availability cymbal choices of the Slaughter of the Soul sessions.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who played drums on At the Gates' At War with Reality?**

A: Adrian Erlandsson played drums on At War with Reality (2014), his first studio recording with At the Gates since Slaughter of the Soul in 1995. He remained the band's drummer through the 2007 reunion and its subsequent full-time reformation from 2008 onward, and continued on all of At the Gates' reunion-era studio albums, including To Drink from the Night Itself (2018). For his original 1995 gear breakdown, see the [Slaughter of the Soul drum setup](/articles/slaughter-of-the-soul-drum-setup).

**Q: What drum kit did Adrian Erlandsson use on At War with Reality?**

A: Erlandsson recorded At War with Reality on a Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit in a transparent black finish — twin 22" x 18" bass drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, and 16" and 18" floor toms, paired with a matching 14" x 6.5" Bubinga snare. This was the rig he had developed and refined during his years with Paradise Lost and carried forward into the At the Gates reunion, a considerable upgrade from the undocumented studio house kit used on Slaughter of the Soul in 1995.

**Q: Where was At War with Reality recorded?**

A: At War with Reality was recorded at Studio Fredman in Gothenburg, Sweden between June 24 and July 18, 2014 — the same studio where Slaughter of the Soul was tracked in 1994 — with spoken narration for the opening track recorded separately at HVR Studios in Suffolk, UK. The band self-produced the album, with Fredrik Nordström (who also engineered Slaughter of the Soul) and Henrik Udd handling engineering. Jens Bogren mixed and mastered the record at Fascination Street Studios.

**Q: Was At War with Reality At the Gates' first album since Slaughter of the Soul?**

A: Yes. At the Gates disbanded in 1996, the year after releasing Slaughter of the Soul, and reunited for live performances starting in 2007 before committing to a full-time reformation. At War with Reality, released in October 2014 on Century Media Records, was the band's first new studio album in the 19 years since Slaughter of the Soul — making it one of the most anticipated comeback records in melodic death metal history. It was followed by To Drink from the Night Itself in 2018.
