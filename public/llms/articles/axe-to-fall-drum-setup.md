# Converge Axe to Fall Drum Setup: Ben Koller's 2009 Gear Breakdown

## Overview

Released in 2009 on Epitaph Records, Converge's "Axe to Fall" represents the band at their most musically ambitious — a record that expanded the sonic vocabulary established by "Jane Doe," "You Fail Me," and "No Heroes" while incorporating an unprecedented range of collaborative voices. Featuring guest appearances from Scott Kelly of Neurosis, Stephen Brodsky of Cave In, Hamilton Jordan of Genghis Tron, and others, "Axe to Fall" is Converge's genre-spanning masterpiece: a record that moves from pure hardcore aggression to doom-influenced heaviness to delicate post-rock texture within a single album.

Ben Koller's drumming on "Axe to Fall" is a masterclass in versatility. By 2009, he had spent eight years refining the approach developed on "Jane Doe" — his kit had evolved toward the Tama Starclassic Performer B/B configuration, his cymbal setup was solidifying around Zildjian's K Custom and A Custom lines, and his understanding of what Converge's music demanded had deepened into something intuitive and automatic. The result is a performance that matches the album's scope: technically demanding where the songs demand it, patient and atmospheric where the collaborative passages require restraint.

Recorded and produced by Kurt Ballou at Godcity Studio in Salem, Massachusetts, "Axe to Fall" carries the same direct, unprocessed character that has defined Converge's recordings throughout their career. Ballou's philosophy — capture the performance, let the instruments speak naturally, resist the urge to replace or trigger drums — meant that Koller's playing needed to be exactly right at every moment. The album's range of dynamics, from whisper-quiet passages to full-chaos hardcore assaults, demanded a drummer who could modulate his intensity with surgical precision.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Performer or Starclassic Maple (2009) — 22" bass drum, 10"/12" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Tama S.L.P. or steel snare, 14" x 5.5"–6.5", tuned medium-bright
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom Dark Hi-Hats 14", A Custom Crashes 18"/19", K Custom Ride, A Custom China 18", K Splash 10"
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra Double Pedal; Vic Firth 5B sticks
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Coated (toms), Evans EMAD (bass), Evans G1 Coated (snare)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-bright for versatility across hardcore and atmospheric passages

### Ben's 2009 Kit: The Tama Starclassic Evolution

By the time Converge entered Godcity Studio to record "Axe to Fall" in 2009, Ben Koller's kit had evolved toward the Tama Starclassic range — the professional-grade series that would become his long-term platform. This represented a meaningful upgrade from the earlier touring kits used on albums like "Jane Doe," with the Starclassic's shell construction providing improved resonance, projection, and response for both studio recording and increasingly demanding live performance.

The Starclassic's birch or maple shell construction offered Koller a more refined tonal palette than the earlier touring configurations. In Ballou's Godcity recording environment — close-miked, acoustically treated, designed to capture the natural character of each instrument — the Starclassic's improved shell quality translated directly into a richer, more dimensional drum sound. The toms on "Axe to Fall" have warmth and sustain that differs from the more raw, immediate character of "Jane Doe."

Koller maintained the compact configuration he had used throughout his Converge career: 10", 12", and 16" toms around a 22" bass drum. This layout was appropriate for "Axe to Fall"'s demands — the album moves through hardcore, doom, post-metal, and experimental passages, all of which this setup handles efficiently. The compact arrangement kept the kit manageable for the physical intensity of Converge's performance style while providing the tonal range the album's varied material required.

The doom and post-metal influenced sections of "Axe to Fall" — particularly tracks like "Worms Will Feed / Rats Will Feast" and "Cruel Bloom" — demanded a different approach from the blast-beat intensity of the album's harder moments. Koller's ability to adapt his touch and timing to these slower, more spacious passages demonstrates the musical maturity that eight years of Converge had developed in him.

### The Snare: Authority Across Styles

The snare on "Axe to Fall" carries a different character than "Jane Doe" — still authoritative and cutting, but with more body and dimension. This reflects both Koller's maturing snare setup in the Starclassic era and the sonic choices Ballou made for an album with significantly more dynamic range than Converge's earlier work.

"Axe to Fall" requires a snare that works across contexts that would challenge any drummer. In the hardcore sections — the crushing aggression of "Dark Horse" or the relentless intensity of "Wishing Well" — the snare needs to cut with the same authority as the "Jane Doe" recordings. In the doom-influenced passages, the snare must integrate into a slower, more spacious sonic environment without losing presence. In the collaborative sections with Scott Kelly and Stephen Brodsky, the drum must support voices and guitar textures from very different sonic worlds than Converge's own.

Koller's snare setup for this period was moving toward the Tama S.L.P. series that would define his later work. The 6.5" depth provides enough body for the doom sections while maintaining the cutting authority for the hardcore passages. Ballou's continued close-mic approach captured the snare with direct clarity — the "Axe to Fall" production has a slightly more developed quality than "Jane Doe," with more definition in the low frequencies, but the fundamental philosophy remained the same.

### Cymbal Setup: K Custom and A Custom Solidifying

By 2009, Ben Koller's cymbal setup had solidified toward the K Custom/A Custom hybrid configuration that defines his current work. The "Axe to Fall" sessions represent the point in his career where this setup became a mature, deliberate choice rather than an evolving preference — the K Custom Dark Hi-Hats for controlled, complex rhythm driving, the A Custom crashes for explosive accents, the K Custom Ride for tonal complexity in varied passages.

The "Axe to Fall" material tested this cymbal configuration across an unusually wide range of sonic contexts. The collaboration with Scott Kelly (Neurosis) required Koller to integrate his cymbal work into Neurosis's characteristic doom and post-metal textures — styles built on different rhythmic foundations than Converge's hardcore. The A Custom crashes, with their fast response and bright explosion, work in both environments: they serve hardcore aggression and they speak clearly in slower, heavier passages where each crash lands with isolated weight.

The K Custom Dark Hi-Hats drove many of "Axe to Fall"'s faster sections with the controlled, slightly darker response that distinguishes them from Zildjian's brighter A Series options. In the album's most furious moments — the blast-beat sections, the rapid 16th-note driving passages — the Dark Hi-Hats' controlled wash prevents the cymbal from overwhelming the mix, keeping the rhythmic information clear even at extreme tempos.

Converge's collaboration with Stephen Brodsky (Cave In) and Hamilton Jordan (Genghis Tron) brought additional sonic textures that pushed Koller's cymbal vocabulary further. The K Custom Ride's complex, washy character served these more experimental passages — a sound that bridges Converge's hardcore aggression and the progressive, textural qualities that Cave In and Genghis Tron brought to the collaboration.

## FAQ

**What drum setup did Ben Koller use on Axe to Fall?**
Ben Koller recorded Axe to Fall in 2009 using a Tama Starclassic-era kit — the professional setup he was developing toward his current Starclassic Performer B/B configuration. His cymbal setup featured Zildjian K Custom Dark Hi-Hats 14 inch, A Custom Crashes 18 and 19 inch, a K Custom Ride, and an A Custom China 18 inch. He used Tama Iron Cobra double pedals and Evans drumheads including G2 Coated on toms and EMAD on the bass drum. Sessions took place at Godcity Studio in Salem, Massachusetts, produced by Kurt Ballou.

**Who are the guest musicians on Axe to Fall?**
Axe to Fall features an extraordinary range of guest collaborators from across the extreme music world: Scott Kelly of Neurosis, Stephen Brodsky of Cave In, Hamilton Jordan of Genghis Tron, Nate Newton and Aaron Dalbec of Bane, Bruce Lamont of Yakuza, and others. This collaboration required Ben Koller to adapt his drumming to very different sonic contexts — Neurosis's doom and post-metal textures, Cave In's progressive approach, and Genghis Tron's electronic-influenced metalcore all demanded different things from his playing.

**How does Axe to Fall differ from Jane Doe in terms of drumming?**
Jane Doe (2001) is characterized by raw hardcore and mathcore aggression, with Koller's drumming pushing technical and physical limits at every moment. Axe to Fall (2009) demonstrates Koller's expanded range eight years later: the hardcore intensity is still present in tracks like "Dark Horse" and "Wishing Well," but the album also features doom-influenced passages, atmospheric sections, and collaborative moments requiring patience, dynamic control, and the ability to serve very different musical contexts. Axe to Fall shows a drummer who has mastered everything on Jane Doe and then learned what to do with that mastery.

**Who produced Axe to Fall and where was it recorded?**
Axe to Fall was produced by Kurt Ballou — Converge's guitarist — at Godcity Studio in Salem, Massachusetts, the same studio used for the majority of Converge's catalog. Ballou's recording philosophy centers on natural acoustic drum sounds with close miking and minimal processing. This approach gave Koller's 2009 performance the same direct, uncompromising character as the earlier Converge recordings while benefiting from Godcity's evolution as a studio over the decade between Jane Doe and Axe to Fall.

**What label released Axe to Fall and why is it significant?**
Axe to Fall was released on Epitaph Records — Converge's first major independent label release after years on Equal Vision and Deathwish (Converge's own label). Epitaph's distribution network gave the album significantly wider reach than previous Converge releases, exposing Ben Koller's drumming and Converge's music to a larger audience. The album debuted at number 106 on the Billboard 200, unprecedented for a Converge release, and earned widespread critical acclaim.
