# Blood Mountain Drum Setup: Brann Dailor's Gear on Mastodon's Breakthrough

> Discover the exact drum kit, cymbals, and gear Brann Dailor used to record Mastodon's mainstream breakthrough Blood Mountain (2006). Full breakdown of the DW Collector's Series kit, Zildjian cymbals, DW 9000 double pedal, and the fills-as-lead-instrument approach behind one of progressive metal's most inventive drummers.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [Brann Dailor](/llms/drummers/brann-dailor.md)
**Band / Album:** Mastodon — *Blood Mountain* (2006)
**Genre:** Progressive Sludge Metal

## Overview

Released on October 3, 2006, Mastodon's *Blood Mountain* was the album that introduced [Brann Dailor](/llms/drummers/brann-dailor.md)'s melodic drumming philosophy to a mainstream audience. The band's first record for Atlantic Records debuted at #28 on the US Billboard 200 — a commercial breakthrough for a band that had built its reputation on the underground metal circuit. The album arrived as a creative step forward from the rawer, more abrasive *Leviathan* (2004), with Dailor's fills becoming more prominent, more melodic, and more obviously central to Mastodon's compositional identity.

*Blood Mountain* is a concept album built around creature mythology — a man's journey up a mountain, encountering fantastical beings at each stage. The narrative ambition matched the musical ambition: Brann's drumming functions not as rhythmic support but as a melodic narrator, his fills weaving through the music like an additional voice. This approach — fills as lead instrument — would reach its peak refinement on [Crack the Skye](/llms/articles/crack-the-skye-drum-setup.md) three years later, but *Blood Mountain* is where the philosophy became unmistakable.

The album was produced by Matt Bayles, who had previously produced *Leviathan* (2004), giving him deep familiarity with Dailor's playing style. Bayles and Mastodon used *Blood Mountain* to bring melodic clarity to the drum sound — allowing Dailor's tom melodies and complex fills to sit higher in the mix than on any previous Mastodon record.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series (maple shells, natural/custom finish)
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series Steel, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A and K Series
- **Hardware / Pedals:** DW 9000 Double Bass Drum Pedal; DW 9000 Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Nitro Throne; Vic Firth 5A American Classic
- **Heads:** Evans UV1 Coated (batter), Evans Hazy 300 (snare resonant), Evans EMAD Clear (kick batter)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium tension — balanced attack and sensitivity for ghost note work

### The DW Collector's Series: Dailor's Fill Canvas

Brann Dailor used a DW Collector's Series kit with maple shells for the *Blood Mountain* sessions — the same premium shell line he would carry into the [Crack the Skye](/llms/articles/crack-the-skye-drum-setup.md) sessions three years later. DW's Collector's Series represents the company's flagship custom offering, built with the tight tolerances and bearing-edge geometry that produce consistent, musical pitches across all drums.

Maple shell construction is central to what makes *Blood Mountain*'s drum sound distinctive. Maple resonates with warmth and fundamental tone — producing pitches that register as musical notes, not just percussive noise. This property is essential for Dailor's compositional approach: he uses toms as melodic instruments, cascading through fills that function as melodic phrases rather than rhythmic interjections. On tracks like "The Wolf Is Loose" and "Capillarian Crest," those tom cascades are melodies with harmonic intention.

The three-rack-tom configuration (10", 12", 13") gives Dailor the dense melodic resolution his playing demands — three adjacent pitches close enough to form melodic shapes without large intervallic jumps. Combined with the 16" floor tom, he has a four-voice melodic palette spanning a full musical range.

### Zildjian: The Bright, Cutting Voice of Blood Mountain

Brann Dailor's Zildjian A and K Series cymbals on *Blood Mountain* reflect the sonic priorities of this specific era. Where later Mastodon records would see him move toward the darker tones of Meinl Byzance — a change most audible on [Crack the Skye](/llms/articles/crack-the-skye-drum-setup.md) — the Zildjian setup on *Blood Mountain* brings brightness, attack, and cutting power to a record that needed to assert itself in the mainstream rock market.

The Zildjian A Series hi-hats provide a clear, responsive top-end that cuts through Mastodon's dense, saturated guitar arrangements. The crashes are fast and articulate — medium-thin A Series cymbals respond quickly and decay cleanly, suiting the album's tempo and the band's tendency to crash on strong beats. The 22" A Medium Ride provides defined bell response for riding passages.

This Zildjian setup represents Dailor's pre-Meinl era. The eventual transition to Meinl Byzance's darker, more atmospheric character would parallel Mastodon's compositional evolution toward the richer, more layered sound of *Crack the Skye*.

### The DW 9000 Double Pedal

The DW 9000 double bass drum pedal anchored Dailor's *Blood Mountain* setup, providing smooth, consistent action for the album's rhythmically demanding bass drum patterns. The DW 9000 series offers adjustable cam drive, multiple footboard angle options, and robust construction that withstands aggressive playing without losing responsiveness. Dailor later moved to a single bass drum setup on subsequent Mastodon albums, but the double-kick foundation of *Blood Mountain* represents an important chapter of his rhythmic vocabulary.

## Key Facts

- Recorded at Doppler Studios, Atlanta, Georgia; produced by Matt Bayles
- Released October 3, 2006 on Atlantic Records (US debut #28 Billboard 200 — Mastodon's mainstream breakthrough)
- DW Collector's Series maple shells — warm resonance for fills-as-melody approach
- Zildjian A and K Series cymbals: 14" hi-hats, 18" Thin Crash, 20" Medium Crash, 22" Ride, 18" China
- DW 9000 double pedal — double bass drum configuration
- Vic Firth 5A American Classic sticks
- Evans UV1 batter heads

## FAQs

**What drums does Brann Dailor use on Blood Mountain?**
DW Collector's Series with maple shells. Double bass drum setup (two 22" kicks), rack toms at 10", 12", 13", and 16" floor tom.

**What cymbals does Brann Dailor use on Blood Mountain?**
Zildjian A and K Series: 14" hi-hats, 18" A Medium Thin Crash, 20" A Medium Crash, 22" A Medium Ride, 18" A China. Bright, cutting character — Dailor's pre-Meinl era.

**What makes his style unique on this album?**
The "fills as lead instrument" approach — tom patterns function as melodic phrases with harmonic intention, not just rhythmic transitions. Blood Mountain was the mainstream breakthrough that made this compositional philosophy undeniable.

**What double pedal does he use?**
DW 9000 double bass drum pedal. Chain drive with adjustable cam. Dailor later returned to single kick on subsequent Mastodon albums.

**Where was Blood Mountain recorded?**
Doppler Studios, Atlanta, Georgia. Produced by Matt Bayles. Released October 3, 2006 on Atlantic Records.

**Who guests on Blood Mountain?**
Scott Kelly of Neurosis guests on "Colony of Birchmen." Cedric Bixler-Zavala of The Mars Volta guests on "Siberian Divide."
