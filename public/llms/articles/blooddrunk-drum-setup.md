## Overview

Released on April 2, 2008, *Blooddrunk* is Children of Bodom's sixth full-length album and a deliberate return to the aggressive, velocity-driven approach that defined their earlier work after the more commercially accessible *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005). Recorded at Sonic Pump Studios in Helsinki, Finland — a departure from the Astia Studio environment that had defined CoB's recording history — *Blooddrunk* marked the first time Anssi Kippo worked with the band in a different studio context, producing a drum sound with a new character while maintaining the musical intelligence of Raatikainen's playing.

For Jaska Raatikainen, *Blooddrunk* represented the Pearl Masters era fully established. The Pearl Masters Premium Maple shells, Zildjian A Custom and K Custom cymbals, and Pearl Eliminator double pedal that had debuted on *Are You Dead Yet?* were now a settled, familiar setup — the same gear foundation deployed across the CoB discography from this point forward. The result is a drum sound with the focused attack of Pearl's maple construction, the bright precision of Zildjian A Custom cymbals, and the aggressive double-kick capability of the Eliminator.

Tracks like "Hellhounds on My Trail," "Blooddrunk," and "LoBodomy" demonstrated the return to higher velocities and more demanding double-kick work compared to *Are You Dead Yet?*, while "One Day You Will Cry" showed the dynamic range the Pearl era setup could deliver across softer, more melodic passages. This article breaks down every component of Raatikainen's setup on *Blooddrunk* — the Pearl Masters shells, the Zildjian cymbal selection, the Pearl Eliminator, and the stylistic approach behind CoB's sixth studio record.

### Pearl Masters Premium Maple: The Established Pearl Era

For *Blooddrunk*, Jaska Raatikainen played the established Pearl Masters Premium Maple setup — the same core kit configuration that had debuted on *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005), now captured for the first time at Sonic Pump Studios in Helsinki rather than the Astia Studio environment that had defined all previous CoB studio recordings.

The Pearl Masters' six-ply maple construction was a settled, familiar setup by 2008: the shell construction's focused attack and controlled resonance suited both the high-velocity passages that dominated *Blooddrunk*'s more aggressive tracklist and the dynamic range required for the album's more varied arrangements. Raatikainen had three years of live and studio experience with the Pearl Masters by the time the *Blooddrunk* sessions began, and that familiarity is audible in the assurance of the performances.

The double bass drum configuration — twin 22" x 18" kick drums — remained central to the *Blooddrunk* approach, with the return to higher-velocity double-kick emphasis reflecting the album's intention to reassert the aggressive character of early CoB after the commercial pivot of *Are You Dead Yet?*. Tracks like "Hellhounds on My Trail" and "LoBodomy" deploy sustained double-bass patterns at tempo ranges closer to *Follow the Reaper* and *Hate Crew Deathroll* than to the more measured approach of *Are You Dead Yet?*

The compact tom spread — 10" and 12" rack toms, 14" and 16" floor toms — maintained Raatikainen's established fill vocabulary in the Pearl era: melody-following tom phrases that track Janne Wirman's keyboard lines and Alexi Laiho's guitar runs with the Pearl shells' characteristically focused, immediate attack.

### Pearl Masters 14" x 5.5" Maple Snare

Raatikainen's snare on *Blooddrunk* was a Pearl Masters maple model — the same shell series as his *Are You Dead Yet?* snare, now captured in Sonic Pump Studios' different acoustic environment. The 14" x 5.5" dimensions delivered the focused crack and attack appropriate for *Blooddrunk*'s return to aggressive tempos, with the Pearl maple construction providing the controlled body that kept the snare sound precise rather than boomy in Anssi Kippo's mix.

*Blooddrunk*'s more aggressive character compared to *Are You Dead Yet?* changed the snare's role in the arrangements: higher velocities and more blast beat deployment required the Pearl maple snare to serve the same high-impact function that the Tama Starclassic snare had on *Follow the Reaper* and *Hate Crew Deathroll*. The Pearl snare's focused attack delivered this at Sonic Pump's different acoustic profile.

Kippo's adaptation to Sonic Pump Studios — handling the room's different reflection character compared to Astia Studio — meant the snare's close-miked signal was treated with greater care to preserve the natural Pearl character against the unfamiliar acoustic environment. The result is a snare sound on *Blooddrunk* with a distinctive clarity that differs slightly from the Astia Studio snare recordings of earlier albums.

### Zildjian A Custom and K Custom: Established Precision for the Aggressive Return

Jaska Raatikainen's Zildjian A Custom and K Custom cymbal setup on *Blooddrunk* was the same configuration established on *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005), now fully settled and deployed in a more aggressive musical context that suited the A Custom's bright, cutting character particularly well.

The Zildjian A Custom series' multi-stage lathing and brilliant finish produce a brighter, more immediately cutting sound than the Paiste 2002 of the Tama era — qualities that are an advantage in *Blooddrunk*'s more aggressive arrangements. The A Custom's fast attack and bright upper frequency presence allowed cymbal work to cut through Janne Wirman's keyboard-dense arrangements naturally, without post-processing, even at the higher velocities that *Blooddrunk*'s return to aggression demanded.

The A Custom 14" hi-hats' articulation through *Blooddrunk*'s faster passages — particularly on "Hellhounds on My Trail" and "LoBodomy" — demonstrates the setup's suitability for high-velocity playing. The multi-stage lathing delivers consistent stick response across the full surface at tempos where hi-hat articulation must register as distinct events rather than blurring into continuous texture.

The combination of A Custom crashes and K Custom ride continued to serve its function: the crashes providing bright, fast accent work at structurally precise moments; the K Custom ride offering a darker, warmer textural alternative for sustained patterns and melodic keyboard-driven passages. The China cymbal's deployment was more prominent on *Blooddrunk* than on *Are You Dead Yet?*, reflecting the album's more aggressive character and the structural moments where the China's upward-cutting attack serves best.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drums did Jaska Raatikainen use on Blooddrunk?**

A: On *Blooddrunk* (2008), Jaska Raatikainen used Pearl Masters Premium Maple drums — the same shell configuration established on *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005), now fully settled after three years of touring and studio use. The setup centered on twin 22" x 18" bass drums, rack toms (10" and 12"), and floor toms (14" and 16") in Pearl's six-ply maple construction. **Q: What pedals did Jaska Raatikainen use on Blooddrunk?**

A: Jaska Raatikainen used the Pearl Eliminator double pedal on *Blooddrunk* (2008) — the same pedal that had debuted on *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005), now fully established. Three years of touring and studio experience with the Eliminator's cam geometry gave Raatikainen complete confidence in the mechanism across *Blooddrunk*'s more demanding double-kick passages, particularly on "Hellhounds on My Trail" and "LoBodomy." **Q: What cymbals did Jaska Raatikainen use on Blooddrunk?**

A: On *Blooddrunk* (2008), Jaska Raatikainen used the established Zildjian A Custom and K Custom configuration — A Custom 14" hi-hats, A Custom crashes (17" and 18"), a K Custom 20" ride, and an A Custom 18" China. This was the same setup as *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005), now deployed in a more aggressive musical context where the A Custom's bright, cutting character suited *Blooddrunk*'s return to higher-velocity playing. For context on the Paiste 2002 era this setup replaced, see the [Hate Crew Deathroll drum setup article](/articles/hate-crew-deathroll-drum-setup).

**Q: How does Blooddrunk differ from Are You Dead Yet? in drum approach?**

A: *Blooddrunk* (2008) represents a deliberate return to the aggressive, high-velocity approach of *Follow the Reaper* (2000) and *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003) after the more commercially accessible, groove-oriented *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005). Double-kick patterns are more sustained and at higher tempos on *Blooddrunk*; blast beats are more prominent; China cymbal deployment is more frequent. The Pearl Masters Premium Maple / Pearl Eliminator setup is identical across both albums — the difference is entirely in how Raatikainen deployed it. For the full arc, see the [Are You Dead Yet? drum setup article](/articles/are-you-dead-yet-drum-setup) and the [Jaska Raatikainen drummer profile](/drummer/jaska-raatikainen).
