# City Drum Setup: Gene Hoglan's Industrial Machine for Strapping Young Lad

> Complete breakdown of Gene Hoglan's drum gear on Strapping Young Lad's City (1997). Tama Artstar II shells, Roland SPD-20 electronic pad integration, Pearl Free-Floating snare, and the industrial metal production approach that made City a blueprint for djent and progressive extreme metal.

## Overview

Released on February 17, 1997, Strapping Young Lad's "City" arrived as one of the most extreme and forward-looking metal records of the decade. Producer and bandleader Devin Townsend brought Gene Hoglan — fresh from his celebrated run on Death's "Symbolic" (1995) — into an industrial production framework that demanded something genuinely new: integrate electronic percussion with acoustic extreme metal drumming as a core compositional element, not an afterthought.

The result was a drum setup and production approach that influenced a generation of extreme metal drummers. "City" is widely cited as a direct precursor to djent — its rhythmic precision, electronic integration, and industrial production density established templates that Meshuggah, Periphery, and the broader progressive extreme metal community would develop through the 2000s.

This article covers the complete gear Hoglan used on City: his Tama Artstar II acoustic kit, Roland SPD-20 Total Percussion Pad, Pearl Free-Floating snare, and Zildjian A Custom cymbal setup. For Hoglan's earlier acoustic work see [individual-thought-patterns-drum-setup](https://metalforge.io/articles/individual-thought-patterns-drum-setup) (Death, 1993) and [symbolic-drum-setup](https://metalforge.io/articles/symbolic-drum-setup) (Death, 1995). For his complete career gear overview see [whats-in-gene-hoglans-kit](https://metalforge.io/articles/whats-in-gene-hoglans-kit).

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Tama Artstar II (birch shells with maple reinforcement rings)
- **Configuration:** Two 22" x 16" bass drums (independent double kick), 10" x 10" and 12" x 12" rack toms, 14" x 14" and 16" x 16" floor toms
- **Electronic:** Roland SPD-20 Total Percussion Pad — integrated for industrial sample triggering
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — 14" Mastersound Hi-Hats, 16"/18"/19" Medium Crashes, 20" Ride, 18" China
- **Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide (individual single pedals, one per bass drum)
- **Sticks:** Pro-Mark 5B Wood Tip
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated (snare batter), Remo Emperor Clear (tom batter), Remo Powerstroke 3 (bass drum)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high tension for projection through industrial production processing
- **Estimated kit value:** $2,500–3,500 (1997)

### Tama Artstar II: Acoustic Foundation

Gene Hoglan used the Tama Artstar II — the same platform as his Death Symbolic sessions — for City's acoustic drum foundation. Birch shells with maple reinforcement rings delivered the focused, bright attack that Devin Townsend's industrial production required: precise transients that survived heavy compression and saturation treatment. The double bass drum setup (two 22" x 16" kicks with individual Iron Cobra pedals) provided the independent double-kick foundation for City's most extreme passages. Unlike Symbolic's acoustic warmth, City demanded birch's inherently brighter and more attack-forward character — better suited to a production built around electronic density.

### Roland SPD-20: The Industrial Innovation

The Roland SPD-20 Total Percussion Pad was the defining element that set the City setup apart from any previous Gene Hoglan configuration. Mounted alongside his acoustic kit, the SPD-20 allowed Hoglan to trigger industrial samples and electronic sounds programmed by Townsend into the production. This integration — a world-class acoustic drummer incorporating electronic percussion as a compositional voice, not a live performance shortcut — was genuinely pioneering in extreme metal in 1997 and helped establish templates for electronic integration in industrial and djent-influenced metal throughout the following decade.

### Pearl Free-Floating Snare

The Pearl Free-Floating Steel snare (14" x 6.5") returned from the Individual Thought Patterns era. Pearl's Free-Floating design decouples the shell from its mounting hardware, allowing freer resonance and an open, explosive crack. The steel shell projected through Townsend's dense production layers with bright authority. Medium-high tuning maximized articulation across the album's extreme tempos while maintaining sensitivity for dynamic passages like "Underneath the Waves."

### Zildjian A Custom Cymbals

For City, Hoglan transitioned from the Paiste 2002/RUDE setup of Symbolic to Zildjian A Custom — a brighter, more modern series with quick response and controlled sustain suited to high-definition industrial production. The 14" Mastersound hi-hats (wavy-holed bottom cymbal for reduced air lock) enabled fast, responsive footwork at extreme tempos. Three graduated crashes (16", 18", 19") provided dynamic range from sharp accent to explosive section marker. The 18" A Custom China added industrial-edge texture that complemented Townsend's production aesthetic.

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Gene Hoglan use on Strapping Young Lad City?**
A: Gene Hoglan recorded Strapping Young Lad's City in 1997 using a Tama Artstar II kit with birch shells — the same platform as Death's Symbolic (1995). Configuration included two 22" x 16" bass drums (independent double kick), 10 and 12 inch power rack toms, and 14 and 16 inch floor toms. A Roland SPD-20 Total Percussion Pad was integrated for industrial sample triggering — marking one of the first times a world-class extreme metal drummer incorporated electronic percussion as a core studio element.

**Q: Did Gene Hoglan use electronic drums on Strapping Young Lad City?**
A: Yes — the Roland SPD-20 Total Percussion Pad was a defining element of the City recording setup. Mounted alongside his acoustic Tama Artstar II kit, the SPD-20 allowed Hoglan to trigger industrial samples and electronic sounds that producer Devin Townsend integrated into City's production. This hybrid acoustic-electronic approach was pioneering for extreme metal in 1997 and helped establish templates for electronic integration in industrial and djent-influenced metal throughout the following decade.

**Q: How did Strapping Young Lad get that drum sound on City?**
A: The City drum sound resulted from Devin Townsend's industrial production philosophy applied to Gene Hoglan's hybrid acoustic-electronic setup. Townsend used significant compression and saturation on Hoglan's Tama Artstar II acoustic drums, creating a mechanical, industrial character. The Roland SPD-20's electronic signal was blended with the processed acoustic drums. Hoglan's metronomic precision was essential — Townsend's heavy processing amplified inconsistencies as readily as strengths, and only a drummer of Hoglan's accuracy could produce a coherent result under that processing chain.

**Q: What cymbals did Gene Hoglan use on Strapping Young Lad City?**
A: Gene Hoglan used Zildjian A Custom cymbals on City — transitioning from the Paiste 2002 and RUDE series of Symbolic (1995). The A Custom lineup included 14" Mastersound hi-hats, 16", 18", and 19" Medium Crashes, a 20" Ride, and an 18" China. The A Custom series offered brighter, more modern character than the Paistes, with quick response well-suited to Devin Townsend's high-definition industrial production approach.
