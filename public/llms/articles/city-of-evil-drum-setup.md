# City of Evil Drum Setup — The Rev's Pearl Masters MCX (Avenged Sevenfold, 2005)

> Complete breakdown of The Rev's drum kit on Avenged Sevenfold's City of Evil (2005). Pearl Masters MCX, Zildjian A Custom cymbals — the gear behind A7X's mainstream breakthrough and Grammy-nominated 'Beast and the Harlot'.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup

**Band / Album:** Avenged Sevenfold — *City of Evil* (2005)
**Genre:** Hard Rock / Heavy Metal

## Overview

Released on June 7, 2005 on Warner Bros. Records, *City of Evil* is Avenged Sevenfold's major-label debut and the album that launched them from metalcore cult act to mainstream hard rock prominence. It peaked at #30 on the US Billboard 200, eventually achieved Platinum certification, and earned the band a Grammy nomination for Best Hard Rock Performance in 2007 for "Beast and the Harlot." The album remains the defining record of The Rev's career and the most important document of his drumming.

Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan (August 10, 1981 – December 28, 2009) was A7X's founding drummer and creative co-architect. He co-wrote the majority of *City of Evil*'s tracks, sang backing vocals throughout, and delivered one of the most technically demanding drum performances in mainstream hard rock of the decade. His parts blend jazz-influenced cymbal orchestration, progressive rock complexity, and raw rock power — a combination unique in the genre at the time.

*City of Evil* marked A7X's deliberate shift away from metalcore toward hard rock and heavy metal, with Guns N' Roses, Iron Maiden, and classic Metallica as acknowledged touchstones. The Rev's drumming evolved with that shift: wider dynamic ranges, melodic tom work, intricate fills that function as compositional statements rather than transitional passages.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters MCX (six-ply maple/birch hybrid shells, ISS tom mounting) — double 22"x18" bass drums, four rack toms (10"–14"), two floor toms (16", 18")
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass 14" x 6.5" — bright, projecting crack for dense guitar mix clarity
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/19" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China, 10" splash
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal (one per kick drum); Pearl Heavy H-2050 hi-hat stand; Roc-N-Soc Nitro throne; Vic Firth 5B Wood Tip sticks
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear batter kick / Powerstroke 3 Ebony front; Remo Ambassador Clear batter toms / Ambassador Ebony resonant; Remo Ambassador Coated snare batter

### Pearl Masters MCX: The Rev's Breakthrough Kit

The Pearl Masters MCX was The Rev's primary kit for *City of Evil* and the touring cycle that followed. Pearl's professional flagship production line uses a six-ply maple/birch hybrid shell — maple's warmth and sustain combined with birch's added attack and upper-frequency brightness — that balanced the dual sonic demands of the album: melodic resonance for The Rev's compositional tom work, and punch for the hard rock and metal grooves.

The double 22"x18" bass drum configuration was essential. Songs like "Beast and the Harlot," "Burn It Down," and "Bat Country" deploy double-kick patterns ranging from straight-ahead rock propulsion to technically complex metalcore-influenced figures. Two separate kick drums provide the physical mass and low-frequency authority that double bass pedal on a single drum cannot replicate at high volume.

Four rack toms (10" through 14") and two floor toms (16" and 18") gave The Rev the tonal range his melodic tom work required. The Rev treated the toms as melodic instruments, not purely percussive punctuation. His fills throughout *City of Evil* are harmonically motivated — responding to guitar and vocal melodies rather than simply occupying rhythmic space. Pearl's ISS (Independent Suspension System) mounting preserved each shell's resonance by isolating it from the hardware, essential for this approach.

### Zildjian A Custom: Brightness for the Hard Rock Context

The A Custom series — Zildjian's computer-guided lathed B20 bronze line — delivered the bright, consistent projection that *City of Evil*'s dense hard rock mix required. Against layered guitar arrangements and full production, the A Custom's cutting attack ensured cymbal clarity at every dynamic level without aggressive processing.

The 14" A Custom hi-hats drove *City of Evil*'s rhythmic identity. The Rev's hi-hat technique was more varied than typical metal drumming — open, half-open, and closed positions used compositionally throughout songs to create tonal texture. The A Custom delivered the response and consistency that approach demanded. Multiple crash configurations (16", 18", 19") provided the dynamic toolkit for the album's range: quick accents, section endings, and the maximum-intensity climaxes of "Beast and the Harlot" and "Bat Country."

## Track Highlights

**"Beast and the Harlot"** (Grammy-nominated) — Driving double-kick at 196 BPM anchors the main riff. The A Custom 19" crash delivers climactic accents. Ghost notes and snare orchestration in the verse demonstrate compositional nuance above the groove.

**"Bat Country"** — A7X's highest-charting single. The A Custom hi-hats provide crisp quarter-note articulation for the verse groove. The MCX floor toms' warmth gives the pre-chorus transition fill a musical resonance. The Rev's tom fill before the final chorus is melodically motivated.

**"Seize the Day"** — Ballad dynamic requiring maximum sensitivity from the Pearl Free-Floating snare at low tension. Ride bell work adds melodic texture. Drum swell into the final chorus is a compositional build matching the vocal arc.

**"Burn It Down"** — Double-kick intensity at hard rock tempo. China cymbal punctuates the riff structure. The snare's brass shell brightness cuts through dense guitar production.

## FAQ

**Q: Who played drums on Avenged Sevenfold's City of Evil?**
Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan played drums on Avenged Sevenfold's City of Evil (2005). The Rev (August 10, 1981 – December 28, 2009) was A7X's founding drummer and creative co-architect, co-writing the majority of the album's tracks and contributing backing vocals throughout. His performance on City of Evil is considered one of the most technically demanding in mainstream hard rock of the decade. City of Evil is the last A7X studio album The Rev played drums on before his death on December 28, 2009, at age 28.

**Q: What drum kit did The Rev use on City of Evil?**
The Rev used a Pearl Masters MCX drum kit on City of Evil (2005). Configuration: double 22"x18" bass drums, four rack toms (10"–14"), and two floor toms (16", 18"). His snare was a Pearl Free-Floating Brass 14"x6.5". For cymbals, The Rev used the Zildjian A Custom series: 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/19" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China, and 10" splash. The Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal (one per kick drum) handled the double-kick demands throughout the album.

**Q: What did The Rev play drums on?**
The Rev (Jimmy Sullivan) played drums on all Avenged Sevenfold studio albums from the band's 1999 founding through his death: Sounding the Seventh Trumpet (2001), Waking the Fallen (2003), City of Evil (2005), and Avenged Sevenfold (self-titled, 2007). He also composed the drum parts for Nightmare (2010), which were performed by Mike Portnoy as a tribute. City of Evil (2005) is his most commercially significant recording and the album most associated with his mainstream breakthrough.

**Q: Was City of Evil Grammy nominated?**
Yes. "Beast and the Harlot" — the opening track of City of Evil — was nominated for Best Hard Rock Performance at the 2007 Grammy Awards. It was Avenged Sevenfold's first major Grammy nomination. City of Evil charted #30 on the US Billboard 200 and eventually achieved Platinum certification as it grew through touring and radio exposure.

**Q: What is A7X's breakthrough album?**
City of Evil (2005) is widely considered Avenged Sevenfold's mainstream breakthrough album. It was their first major-label release (Warner Bros. Records), charted #30 on the US Billboard 200, and produced the Grammy-nominated single "Beast and the Harlot." While Waking the Fallen (2003) established A7X within the metalcore scene, City of Evil reached a significantly broader hard rock and heavy metal audience. Nightmare (2010) later achieved A7X's first US #1 debut.

**Q: Who was The Rev?**
Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan (1981–2009) was the founding drummer and one of the principal songwriters of Avenged Sevenfold. He co-founded the band in Huntington Beach, California in 1999 and remained their drummer until his death. Beyond drumming, The Rev sang backing vocals on most A7X albums and was a central creative force in the band's musical direction. He died on December 28, 2009, at age 28, from acute polydrug toxicity. Mike Portnoy subsequently performed The Rev's composed drum parts for Nightmare (2010) as a tribute.
