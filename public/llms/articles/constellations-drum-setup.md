# August Burns Red 'Constellations' Drum Setup — Matt Greiner's 2009 Kit Breakdown

## Overview

August Burns Red's *Constellations* (June 2, 2009, Metal Blade Records) is the album that crystallized Matt Greiner's reputation as metalcore's most musical drummer. Recorded at Audiohammer Studios with producer Jason Suecof, *Constellations* pushed the technical and dynamic ceiling of the genre. At the time, Greiner was working with early Pearl endorsement gear before the flagship Reference Pure kit he plays today.

## Drum Kit

- **Drums:** Pearl Export / Pearl Vision (early endorsement era, pre-Reference Pure)
- **Bass Drums:** 22" x 18" (x2 double kick)
- **Toms:** 10", 12" rack toms; 14" and 16" floor toms
- **Shells:** Poplar/maple hybrid (Pearl Export/Vision construction)
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating 14" x 6.5" steel (primary)
- **Estimated Kit Value:** $1,200–2,200 (shell pack, 2009 pricing)

## Cymbals

- **Brand:** Meinl Byzance (developing endorsement)
- **Hi-Hats:** Meinl Byzance 14" Hi-Hats
- **Crashes:** Meinl Byzance 18" and 19" Crash
- **Ride:** Meinl Byzance 20"/21" Ride
- **China:** Meinl Byzance 18" China
- **Splash:** Meinl Byzance 10" Splash
- **Estimated Cymbal Value:** $1,500–2,500

## Key Facts

- Recorded at Audiohammer Studios, DeLand, Florida with producer Jason Suecof
- Released June 2, 2009 on Metal Blade Records
- Pearl production kit (Export/Vision) — early endorsement era before Reference Pure
- Pearl Free-Floating snare for bright, cutting crack through dense ABR guitar mixes

## FAQ

**Q: What drums does Matt Greiner use on Constellations?**
A: On *Constellations* (2009), Matt Greiner played a Pearl production kit in the Export/Vision tier — the early-endorsement Pearl configuration before his current Pearl Reference Pure. His setup included double 22" bass drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, and 14" and 16" floor toms. The recording at Audiohammer Studios with Jason Suecof shaped the Pearl shells into the tight, punchy drum sound the album is known for.

**Q: What is Matt Greiner's Constellations-era setup worth?**
A: Matt Greiner's Constellations-era Pearl production kit (Export/Vision tier) retailed for approximately $1,200 to $2,200 as a shell pack in 2009. Adding a Pearl Free-Floating snare ($300–500), developing Meinl Byzance cymbals ($1,500–2,500), and Pearl Eliminator double pedal ($350–500) brings the total rig to roughly $3,500 to $5,700 — significantly less than his current Reference Pure setup, which runs $6,000 to $12,000 fully equipped.

**Q: How does Matt Greiner's 2009 Constellations setup differ from his current kit?**
A: The most significant differences are the drum shells and snare. On Constellations, Greiner played Pearl Export/Vision production kits with poplar/maple shells at Pearl's mid-tier level. His current setup is the Pearl Reference Pure in Piano Black — flagship maple/mahogany shells with free-floating architecture. His snare evolved from Pearl Free-Floating models to his Pearl Matt Greiner Signature 14"x6" steel shell. His Meinl Byzance relationship deepened from a developing endorsement into the full Extra Dry and Dual series configuration. His pedal moved from Pearl Eliminator to Pearl Demon Drive direct-drive system.
