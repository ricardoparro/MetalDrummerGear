## Overview

Released on February 13, 2009 through Relapse Records, Obscura's *Cosmogenesis* is one of the albums that defined what progressive technical death metal could be in the post-*Epitaph* era — a record that combined Necrophagist's technical precision with broader compositional ambitions drawn from jazz, progressive rock, and classical music. At the center of this synthesis was Hannes Grossmann, co-founder and drummer of Obscura, producing and performing his most compositionally sophisticated recording to date.

Where *Epitaph* (2004) established Grossmann as technically elite, *Cosmogenesis* demonstrated the full range of what he could do with compositional freedom. He and guitarist Steffen Kummerer built an album drawing on polyrhythmic counterpoint, jazz-inflected dynamic shaping, and classical voice-leading thinking in drum fills. Tracks like "Anticosmic Overload," "Orbital Elements," and "A Valediction" contain drum performances that reward analytical listening — each limb operating with its own internal logic, converging into music of genuine compositional depth.

The album was self-produced by Grossmann at his facility in Regensburg, Germany — giving him complete control over the drum sound specification from microphone placement through mix integration.

### Pearl Masters Custom: Responsive Foundation for Compositional Drumming

For *Cosmogenesis*, Grossmann used a Pearl Masters Custom kit — Pearl's flagship production series of the era, built with all-maple shells designed for consistent, responsive studio and live performance. The Pearl Masters' maple construction delivers warm midrange and articulate stick response: precise enough for technical passages, resonant enough for the melodic sections where tone matters as much as speed.

His configuration: two 22" x 18" bass drums (deeper shells than the Epitaph era for fuller low-end in progressive arrangements), 10" and 12" rack toms, and 14" and 16" floor toms. The four-tom spread covers the melodic range his compositional approach requires — his fills on *Cosmogenesis* function as counterlines within harmonically complex arrangements, and the consistent Pearl shell construction makes melodic tom tuning reliable across sessions.

The transition from Tama Starclassic Performer (*Epitaph*) to Pearl Masters represents a deliberate shift toward warmer, more resonant character — appropriate for a record whose ambitions extended beyond pure technical speed into the broader sonic territory of progressive death metal.

### Meinl Byzance: Warm Complexity for Progressive Death Metal

Grossmann's shift to Meinl Byzance cymbals for *Cosmogenesis* — from Pure Alloy on *Epitaph* — reflects the album's expanded musical ambitions. Where Pure Alloy's brightness served Necrophagist's pure precision context, Byzance's warmer B20 bronze character integrates naturally within *Cosmogenesis*'s more complex arrangements: fretless bass, string textures, and progressive guitar writing across a broader frequency range.

His setup: 14" Byzance Traditional hi-hats (right hand, open-handed technique), 18" and 19" Byzance Brilliant crash cymbals, a 21" Byzance Traditional Ride, and a 10" Byzance Splash for quick compositional accents.

The 21" Byzance Traditional Ride is particularly important on *Cosmogenesis*. Tracks in mixed meters require a ride that provides metric clarity without the aggressive brightness of an extreme metal ride — the Byzance Traditional's dark, complex character guides meter without fighting the harmonic density of the arrangements.

### Open-Handed Technique on Cosmogenesis

Grossmann's open-handed technique — right hand on hi-hat, left hand on snare — is central to *Cosmogenesis*'s drum performances. This eliminates hand-crossing and enables limb combinations that crossed-hands technique cannot achieve, particularly audible on "Anticosmic Overload" and "A Valediction." The Byzance Traditional hi-hats' even response to both sticks supports this approach.

## FAQ

**What drum kit did Hannes Grossmann use on Obscura Cosmogenesis?**
Hannes Grossmann recorded Obscura's Cosmogenesis (2009) using a Pearl Masters Custom kit with two 22" x 18" bass drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, and 14" and 16" floor toms. Cymbals were Meinl Byzance series. Pedals were DW 9000 series. The album was self-produced by Grossmann at his own facility in Regensburg, Germany.

**Who produced Obscura Cosmogenesis?**
Obscura's Cosmogenesis (2009) was produced by Hannes Grossmann at Grossmann Recordings in Regensburg, Germany. As the album's drummer and Obscura co-founder alongside guitarist Steffen Kummerer, Grossmann's production role gave him complete control over the drum sound — from microphone placement through mix integration. The self-produced approach is audible in the precision of the drum recording, where every dynamic nuance is captured with unusual clarity.

**How does Hannes Grossmann use open-handed technique on Cosmogenesis?**
Grossmann plays open-handed — right hand on hi-hat, left hand on snare, no crossing — enabling limb combinations that crossed-hands technique cannot achieve. This is particularly audible on "Anticosmic Overload" and "A Valediction." The Meinl Byzance Traditional hi-hats support this by responding evenly to both sticks.

**What makes Cosmogenesis different from Necrophagist Epitaph drumming-wise?**
While Epitaph (2004) prioritized extreme technical precision in service of Suiçmez's arrangements, Cosmogenesis adds compositional breadth: jazz-inflected dynamic shaping, classical counterpoint thinking in fills, complex odd-meter navigation, and a warmer sonic palette. Pearl Masters and Meinl Byzance replaced the Tama and Meinl Pure Alloy of the Epitaph era, reflecting Grossmann's evolution from sideman (Necrophagist) to co-composer (Obscura).
