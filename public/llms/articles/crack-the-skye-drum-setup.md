# Crack the Skye Drum Setup: Brann Dailor's Mastodon 2009 Masterpiece

> Complete breakdown of Brann Dailor's drum setup on Mastodon's Grammy-nominated Crack the Skye (2009). DW Collector's Series kit, Meinl Byzance cymbals, DW 9000 pedal — the full gear guide for progressive metal's most melodic drummer at his career peak.

## Overview

Released on March 24, 2009, Mastodon's *Crack the Skye* is one of the most critically acclaimed metal albums of the 21st century — a Grammy-nominated progressive concept album built around time loops, astral projection, and Rasputin that Rolling Stone placed among the top albums of the 2000s. At its rhythmic foundation was Brann Dailor, delivering what many consider the most melodically sophisticated drumming ever recorded on a heavy metal album.

Dailor's approach challenged every assumption about what metal drums are supposed to do. Where most metal drummers serve as the rhythmic backbone, Dailor plays melodies on the kit — toms tuned in musical intervals deployed as counter-melodic voices that interweave with Bill Kelliher and Brent Hinds's guitar lines rather than simply supporting them. On tracks like "The Czar" and "The Last Baron," the drum parts function as independent compositional statements.

The album was recorded at Doppler Studios in Atlanta, Georgia with producer Brendan O'Brien — known for Pearl Jam, Bruce Springsteen, AC/DC, and Soundgarden. O'Brien captured organic dynamics and natural room sound suited to Mastodon's layered, atmospheric approach. The drum sound is warm, resonant, and three-dimensional — no heavy triggering or sample replacement.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series (maple shells, natural finish)
- **Bass Drum:** 22" x 18" single kick (no double bass)
- **Toms:** 10", 12", 13" rack toms; 16" floor tom — tuned in musical intervals
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series 14" x 6.5" steel/maple
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Traditional series (B20 hand-hammered Turkish bronze)
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 Series (single pedal — single kick only)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A (early career, pre-Vater endorsement)
- **Heads:** Evans UV1 Coated (batter), Evans G1 Clear (resonant toms)
- **Estimated Kit Value:** $4,000–6,500 (2009)

### DW Collector's Series: The Melodic Foundation

Brann Dailor's DW Collector's Series drums were chosen for warmth and musical resonance. Hand-selected North American maple with thin natural lacquer finish — allowing the shells to vibrate freely and produce the sustained, tonal sound his melodic playing demands. Each tom tuned to a specific musical pitch, deployed as a compositional voice throughout the arrangements.

The single 22" bass drum is Dailor's most distinctive choice in the metal world. Where almost all metal drummers use double kick, Dailor's single-kick approach focuses on precise groove placement — the kick as musical punctuation, not sustained velocity. His single-pedal patterns on *Crack the Skye* interact with tom melodies and guitar figures in ways that double-bass saturation would obscure.

### Meinl Byzance Traditional Cymbals

Meinl Byzance Traditional series — hand-hammered B20 bronze in the Turkish tradition. Complex overtones, warm fundamental tones, natural decay that develops over time. In *Crack the Skye*'s atmospheric production, the Byzance cymbals function as textural instruments rather than pure accent markers.

**Setup:**
- Hi-Hats: Meinl Byzance 14" Traditional Hi-Hats
- Crashes: Meinl Byzance 18" and 20" Traditional Crashes
- Ride: Meinl Byzance 22" Traditional Ride
- China: Meinl Byzance 18" China

Brendan O'Brien's Neumann U87 overheads in spaced configuration captured the Byzance's full tonal range — the complex overtone structure that close-miked, compressed production would eliminate.

## Track Highlights

**"Oblivion"** — Opening track, Dailor sings lead vocals while drumming. Jazz ghost note density, melodic fill work. Most accessible showcase of single-kick groove philosophy.

**"Crack the Skye" (title track)** — Contains the album's definitive polyrhythmic section: 7-over-4 patterns with kick holding 4/4 while hands phrase in 7. Benchmark for advanced polyrhythm study.

**"The Czar"** — 10-minute, four-movement progressive epic ("Usurper / Escape / Martyr / Spiral"). Toms carry melodic weight through the "Martyr" section. Complete takes required due to interlocking rhythmic relationships.

**"The Last Baron"** — 13+ minutes, covers Dailor's complete vocabulary from restrained groove to explosive fills. Extended melodic tom phrases in the central section among his most compositionally sophisticated work.

## FAQ

**Q: What drums does Brann Dailor use on Crack the Skye?**
A: On *Crack the Skye* (2009), Brann Dailor used a DW Collector's Series drum kit with maple shells in a natural finish — a single 22" x 18" bass drum, three rack toms (10", 12", 13"), and a 16" floor tom. DW's flagship shell line provided the warm, resonant tone his melodic playing demands. Each tom was tuned in musical intervals and deployed as a counter-melodic voice throughout the arrangements.

**Q: What cymbals does Brann Dailor use on Crack the Skye?**
A: Brann Dailor used Meinl Byzance Traditional series cymbals on *Crack the Skye* — 14" Traditional Hi-Hats, 18" and 20" Traditional Crashes, a 22" Traditional Ride, and an 18" China cymbal. Meinl Byzance cymbals are hand-hammered B20 bronze in the Turkish tradition, producing complex overtones and warm fundamental tones suited to the album's atmospheric production.

**Q: What makes Brann Dailor's drumming unique on Crack the Skye?**
A: Three interconnected qualities: (1) Melodic tom integration — toms tuned in musical intervals and deployed as counter-melodic compositional voices, not just rhythmic fills. (2) Single-kick philosophy — a single 22" bass drum with a single pedal, placing precise groove over sustained velocity. (3) Polyrhythmic sophistication — most notably the 7-over-4 patterns on the title track. All executed while simultaneously singing lead and harmony vocal parts.

**Q: What pedal does Brann Dailor use on Crack the Skye?**
A: Brann Dailor used a DW 9000 Series pedal during the *Crack the Skye* sessions, employed as a single-kick pedal. He plays a single bass drum throughout — a defining choice that focuses kick work on precise groove placement rather than sustained double-bass velocity.

**Q: Does Brann Dailor use double bass on Crack the Skye?**
A: No. Brann Dailor plays a single bass drum with a single pedal throughout *Crack the Skye* and across Mastodon's entire catalog. This is one of the most distinctive choices in progressive metal drumming — a deliberate rejection of double-kick convention in favor of melodic, groove-focused single-kick playing.

**Q: Who produced the drums on Crack the Skye?**
A: Brendan O'Brien produced *Crack the Skye* at Doppler Studios in Atlanta, Georgia. O'Brien — known for Pearl Jam, AC/DC, Soundgarden, and Bruce Springsteen — captured natural drum sounds without heavy triggering or sample replacement. Neumann U87 overheads in spaced configuration captured the full tonal range of the Meinl Byzance cymbals and DW Collector's Series shells.
