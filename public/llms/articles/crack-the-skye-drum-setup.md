> Complete breakdown of Brann Dailor's drum setup on Mastodon's Crack the Skye (2009). DW Collector's Series maple shells, Meinl Byzance cymbals, DW 9000 pedal, Evans UV1 heads — and the polyrhythmic techniques behind the title track's legendary 7-over-4 patterns.

## Overview

Released on March 24, 2009, Mastodon's *Crack the Skye* is the band's most critically acclaimed album — a Grammy-nominated concept record about astral projection, time loops, and the Russian mystic Rasputin. At its centre is Brann Dailor's drumming, which treats the drum kit as a melodic instrument equal to the guitars, not merely a rhythmic engine beneath them.

Recorded at Doppler Studios in Atlanta with producer Brendan O'Brien (Pearl Jam's *Vs.*, Springsteen's *The Rising*), the album captures Dailor's fills as melodic phrases shaped by pitch and arc, his polyrhythmic patterns as narrative devices mirroring the album's themes of temporal dislocation, and his ability to sing lead and harmony vocals while executing the most technically demanding drum parts of his career.

*Crack the Skye* was Grammy-nominated for Best Metal Performance ("Oblivion") and appeared on Rolling Stone's list of the 100 best albums of the 2000s. It remains the definitive entry point for understanding Dailor's drumming philosophy and the most studied Mastodon album in music education contexts.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series (100% North American Hard Rock Maple, VLT construction, natural finish)
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series Maple Snare, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Traditional Series — hand-hammered Turkish B20 bronze
- **Hardware / Pedals:** DW 9000 Double Pedal; DW 9000 Series Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Nitro Throne; Vic Firth 5A/5B American Classic sticks
- **Heads:** Evans UV1 Coated (tom batters, snare batter); Evans EMAD2 Clear (bass drum batter); Evans 300 Snare Side (resonant)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium tension — balancing ghost note sensitivity with backbeat projection

### DW Collector's Series: The Crack the Skye Studio Kit

For the *Crack the Skye* sessions, Dailor played a DW Collector's Series kit — DW's flagship professional line with 100% North American Hard Rock Maple and VLT (Vertical Low Timbre) shell construction. VLT orients maple staves vertically rather than in traditional horizontal ply layering, reducing internal shell stress and allowing more natural wood resonance.

Configuration: single 22"x18" bass drum, three rack toms (10"x8", 12"x9", 13"x10"), one 16"x16" floor tom. Natural finish preserved full shell resonance — no wrap damping. The three rack toms tuned in close musical intervals gave Dailor a dense melodic palette for the cascading fills that define the album's sound. A single bass drum reflects his groove-centred approach: precise placement over speed.

### Meinl Byzance: Turkish Bronze for a Melodic Drummer

Meinl Byzance cymbals — hand-hammered Turkish B20 bronze from Meinl's Gülbahçe, Turkey facility — produce warm, dark, overtone-rich sounds that develop tonally over their full decay rather than cutting off sharply. Setup: 14" Traditional hi-hats, 18" and 20" Traditional crashes, 22" Traditional ride, 18" China.

For a drummer whose cymbal work is about colour and atmosphere as much as rhythmic punctuation, Byzance's character is logically essential. The 14" hi-hats register subtle open-close variations and fully-open passages with equal musical quality. The 18" crash speaks quickly for abrupt accents; the 20" handles fuller structural section endings. The 22" ride's washy body adds atmospheric colour to the album's more progressive passages. Meinl Byzance has remained Dailor's primary cymbal endorsement through all subsequent Mastodon albums.

### Evans UV1 Heads: Sensitivity and Durability

Evans UV1 Coated heads on tom batters and snare batter provided the warm attack and ghost-note sensitivity Dailor's playing demands. A single-ply coated head tuned at medium tension registers soft strokes with tone rather than disappearing into noise — critical for the continuous ghost-note work woven through every groove on the record.

## FAQs

**What drums does Brann Dailor use on Crack the Skye?**
DW Collector's Series with 100% North American Hard Rock Maple shells in natural finish. Configuration: single 22"x18" bass drum, 10"x8", 12"x9", 13"x10" rack toms, 16"x16" floor tom.

**What cymbals did Brann Dailor use on Crack the Skye?**
Meinl Byzance Traditional Series: 14" hi-hats, 18" and 20" crashes, 22" ride, 18" China. Hand-hammered Turkish B20 bronze.

**What makes Brann Dailor's drumming style unique?**
He treats the drum kit as a melodic instrument: fills are pitch-contoured melodic phrases responding to the harmonic content around them, not rhythmic interruptions. He also applies polyrhythmic displacement (7-over-4 in the title track) and sings simultaneously while executing these patterns.

**What double pedal does Brann Dailor use on Crack the Skye?**
DW 9000 double pedal, matching the DW Collector's Series kit. Groove-centred use — each stroke placed purposefully, not for continuous high-speed patterns.

**What heads does Brann Dailor use on Crack the Skye?**
Evans UV1 Coated on tom batters and snare batter; Evans EMAD2 Clear on bass drum; Evans 300 Snare Side.

**Where was Crack the Skye recorded?**
Doppler Studios, Atlanta, Georgia. Produced by Brendan O'Brien, engineered by Vance Powell. Room microphones captured the studio's natural ambience throughout.
