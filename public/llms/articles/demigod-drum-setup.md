# Demigod Drum Setup — Inferno's Behemoth Breakthrough Kit (2004)

> Complete breakdown of Inferno's drum setup for Behemoth's Demigod (2004). Discover the Pearl Masters BRX kit, Paiste Signature Dark Energy cymbals, Pearl Eliminator double pedal, and the 240+ BPM blast beats that defined blackened death metal and put Behemoth on the US charts for the first time.

## Overview

Released on November 9, 2004 through Regain Records, *Demigod* is Behemoth's sixth studio album and their commercial breakthrough. It was the **first Behemoth album recorded at Hertz Studio** in Białystok, Poland — the facility run by the Wiesławski brothers that became their studio home for every subsequent record including *Evangelion* (2009) and *The Satanist* (2014). It was also the **first Behemoth album to chart in the United States**, reaching #16 on the Billboard Heatseekers chart and establishing the band's international commercial trajectory.

At the rhythmic core of *Demigod* is Inferno — Zbigniew Robert Promiński — delivering what is widely cited as his definitive pre-*Evangelion* studio performance. The album's blast beat passages push into **240+ BPM territory**, among the fastest documented for a major blackened death metal release of the period. Tracks like "Conquer All," "Slaves Shall Serve," and the title track "Demigod" cemented Inferno's status as one of extreme metal's most technically capable drummers.

*Demigod* also marked **Inferno's first studio album on the Pearl Masters BRX** — a maple/birch hybrid shell series providing sharper attack and faster decay than pure maple, suited to the album's extreme tempo requirements. The Paiste Signature Dark Energy cymbal selection complemented his playing with fast-decaying, controlled tonality. The Pearl Eliminator double pedal drove the album's double-kick architecture with the precision 240+ BPM demands.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters BRX (maple/birch hybrid shells), double 22" bass drums, 10" rack tom, 13" rack tom, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass Snare, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Paiste Signature Dark Energy — 14" hi-hats, 16" crash, 18" crash, 20" ride
- **Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator Double Pedal (adjustable cam, chain-drive)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Coated Ambassador (snare batter), Remo Powerstroke P3 (kick batter), Remo Ambassador (resonant)

### Pearl Masters BRX Kit

The BRX designation refers to Pearl's maple/birch hybrid shell construction. Birch adds sharper attack transients and faster decay than pure maple — at 240+ BPM blast beat tempos, that difference keeps each drum stroke clearly articulated rather than bleeding into sustained fundamental resonance. The compact configuration (10" and 13" rack toms, single 16" floor tom, double 22" bass drums) matched *Demigod*'s arrangement priorities: forward momentum over tom-fill complexity.

This was Inferno's first studio album on the BRX. The Wiesławski brothers at Hertz Studio — recording Behemoth for the first time — worked with the BRX's defined transient character in the mix, placing Inferno's kit with the clarity that would become a signature of the Behemoth studio sound going forward.

### Pearl Free-Floating Snare

The Pearl Free-Floating system decouples the snare wire frame from the drum's shell and hoops, allowing the shell to vibrate without constraint. The result is a more open, resonant tone with faster, freer decay than conventional snare construction. The brass shell adds cutting projection and focused attack — essential for articulation through *Demigod*'s dense, downtuned guitar layers at extreme blast tempos. Tuned to high tension for maximum clarity.

### Paiste Signature Dark Energy Cymbals

Inferno's pre-Meinl era cymbal choice applied the same core philosophy he would carry forward: darkness and controlled fast decay over brightness and sustain. The Paiste Signature Dark Energy series uses B8 alloy with specific finishing that produces quick-decaying, dark-toned cymbals suited to extreme metal environments. At 240 BPM, any cymbal with prolonged sustain blurs individual strokes into undifferentiated wash — the Dark Energy's fast-decaying character kept each hi-hat closure and crash accent discrete and rhythmically positioned. After *Demigod*, Inferno transitioned to Meinl (Byzance Dark on *Evangelion*, Classics Custom Dark on *The Satanist*) — but the doctrine remained identical.

### Pearl Eliminator Double Pedal

The Pearl Eliminator was Pearl's flagship performance pedal in the mid-2000s. Its adjustable cam profiles allowed drummers to customize the power delivery curve for their specific technique — critical at 240+ BPM where consistent power transfer on every stroke determines rhythmic clarity. This preceded Inferno's later adoption of the Pearl Demon Drive.

## Recording: Hertz Studio, Białystok (First Session)

*Demigod* marked the beginning of Behemoth's long-running collaboration with Wojtek and Sławek Wiesławski at Hertz Studio in Białystok. Self-produced by Behemoth with Sławek Wiesławski engineering, the album's drum production placed Inferno's kit with clarity and impact that set the template for *Evangelion* and *The Satanist* sessions in the same room. The BRX's transient definition and the Dark Energy's controlled decay gave the Wiesławski brothers precision sonic material to work with in a dense, downtuned extreme metal mix.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drums does Inferno use on Demigod?**
A: Inferno recorded Demigod (2004) on a Pearl Masters BRX kit — his first studio album on this platform. Configuration: double 22" bass drums, 10" and 13" rack toms, 16" floor tom. The BRX's maple/birch hybrid construction provides sharper attack and faster decay than pure maple, maintaining rhythmic clarity at 240+ BPM blast beat tempos. For his current setup, see the [What's In Inferno's Kit breakdown](/articles/whats-in-infernos-kit).

**Q: What cymbals did Inferno use on Demigod?**
A: Paiste Signature Dark Energy series — 14" hi-hats, 16" and 18" crashes, 20" ride. The Dark Energy line provides fast-decaying, dark-toned character suited to 240+ BPM blast tempos where sustained cymbal wash destroys rhythmic definition. This was Inferno's pre-Meinl cymbal era; he later transitioned to Meinl Byzance Dark (Evangelion, 2009) and Meinl Classics Custom Dark (The Satanist, 2014), applying the same darkness-and-control philosophy through a different manufacturer.

**Q: How fast does Inferno play on Demigod?**
A: Inferno delivers blast beats at 240+ BPM on Demigod — one of the fastest velocities documented for a major-label blackened death metal release of the period. "Conquer All" and "Slaves Shall Serve" sustain these tempos across extended passages. The Pearl Eliminator's adjustable cam system, the Paiste Dark Energy hi-hats' fast decay, and the Pearl Free-Floating snare's precise articulation all contributed to maintaining rhythmic clarity at this velocity.

**Q: What snare did Inferno use on Demigod?**
A: Pearl Free-Floating Brass Snare, 14" x 6.5". The Free-Floating system decouples the snare wire frame from the shell, producing a more open, resonant tone with faster free decay than conventional construction. The brass shell adds cutting projection and focused attack, essential for articulation during 240 BPM blast passages. This differs from the Tama and Pearl Masterworks snares used on later Behemoth recordings.

**Q: Why is Demigod significant for Behemoth?**
A: Demigod (2004) was Behemoth's commercial breakthrough: first album recorded at Hertz Studio (their home for every subsequent record), first to chart in the US (#16 Billboard Heatseekers), and the record that defined the blackened death metal sound they became internationally known for. For Inferno, it was the first major international document of his drumming at full technical capacity — 240+ BPM blast beats on a widely distributed, professionally recorded album.

**Q: How does Demigod fit in Behemoth's arc?**
A: Demigod fills the critical arc gap between the transitional Zos Kia Cultus (2002) and the genre-defining Evangelion (2009). It crystallized the blackened death metal blueprint — first Hertz Studio session, first US chart entry, Pearl Masters BRX era — that all subsequent Behemoth studio albums built on. The Wiesławski brothers / Hertz Studio relationship that produced The Satanist (2014), Behemoth's AOTY, began here.
