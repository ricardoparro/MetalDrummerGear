## Overview

*Demigod* (2004) was Behemoth's commercial breakthrough — the album that transformed the Polish band from a cult underground act into an internationally recognized extreme metal force. Released on October 4, 2004 through Regain Records, it reached #16 on the US Billboard Heatseekers chart, Behemoth's first American chart entry.

The album was also Behemoth's first recording at Hertz Studio in Białystok, Poland, produced by Behemoth alongside brothers Wojtek and Sławek Wiesławski. This partnership would continue through *Evangelion* (2009) and *The Satanist* (2014), defining the band's studio sound for the next decade.

For Inferno, *Demigod* marked a pivotal gear transition: his first album using Pearl Masters BRX maple/birch hybrid shells, paired with a Paiste Signature Dark Energy cymbal setup and Pearl Eliminator double pedal. Blast beats sustained at 240+ BPM across tracks like "Conquer All" and "Slaves Shall Serve" rank among the fastest documented performances on a major extreme metal release of the 2004 era.

The album anchors the essential Behemoth discography arc: *Thelema.6* (2000) → *Zos Kia Cultus* (2002) → **Demigod** (2004) → *The Apostasy* (2007) → *Evangelion* (2009) → *The Satanist* (2014).

## Gear Details

### Pearl Masters BRX: Inferno's Breakthrough Kit

The Pearl Masters BRX represented Inferno's first departure from the setups he had used on earlier Behemoth recordings. The BRX designation refers to Pearl's maple/birch hybrid shell construction: maple outer and inner plies for warmth and sustain, birch middle plies for focused attack and projection. This hybrid approach served *Demigod*'s extreme metal context by providing tonal definition (from the birch) without sacrificing body and resonance (from the maple).

The double 22" bass drum configuration — two independent drums rather than a double pedal on a single drum — had been a constant in Inferno's setup and remained so throughout the *Demigod* era. Two separate shells provide symmetrical mechanical response on each foot, avoiding the tonal inconsistency that can arise when a single resonant head handles double-pedal stress at extreme tempos.

The compact three-tom spread — 10" and 13" rack toms, 16" floor tom — matched *Demigod*'s arrangements: relentless forward momentum rather than melodic tom fill vocabulary. The Wiesławski brothers' approach at Hertz Studio captured the BRX shells with clarity and presence, the birch attack prominent in the kick articulation and the maple warmth audible in the tom tones.

### Pearl Free-Floating 14" x 6.5" Snare

Pearl's Free-Floating system suspends the snare shell independently of both hoops, allowing unrestricted resonance. At 240+ BPM blast tempos, this translates to maximum head sensitivity across the full surface — every stroke registers clearly, whether struck at center or toward the edge.

The 14" x 6.5" steel shell added a cutting attack that cut through Behemoth's dense, down-tuned guitar arrangements without losing definition. Tuned to medium-high tension with Remo Coated Ambassador batter head, the snare delivered the crack and clarity that drives *Demigod*'s rhythmic architecture on its most aggressive tracks.

### Paiste Signature Dark Energy Cymbals

Inferno's cymbal setup for *Demigod* used the Paiste Signature Dark Energy series — a premium handcrafted line built from Paiste's proprietary 2002 bronze alloy, processed to produce darker, faster-decaying tonal characteristics than the standard 2002 or PST series.

| Type | Model | Size | Notes |
|------|-------|------|-------|
| Hi-Hats | Paiste Signature Dark Energy | 14" | Fast decay — controlled articulation at 240+ BPM |
| Crash | Paiste Signature Dark Energy | 16" | Quick accent cymbal |
| Crash | Paiste Signature Dark Energy | 18" | Primary crash — dark, focused character |
| Ride | Paiste Signature Dark Energy | 20" | Controlled definition, minimal wash |
| China | Paiste Signature Dark Energy | 18" | Trashy accent for riff transitions |

At 240+ BPM blast beat tempos, cymbal wash is the enemy of rhythmic clarity — each hi-hat closure and crash accent must register as a discrete event rather than merging into sustained noise. The Dark Energy's fast-decay character was the practical solution: controlled, dark, and quick to settle within *Demigod*'s dense arrangements.

Inferno later transitioned to Meinl Byzance Dark cymbals by the *Evangelion* sessions (2009) — a different manufacturer's approach to the same performance philosophy of controlled dark tone at extreme tempos.

### Pearl Eliminator Double Pedal

The Pearl Eliminator's chain-drive mechanism with adjustable cam system gave Inferno control over the mechanical relationship between footboard travel and beater movement — essential for optimizing response at the sustained high-velocity double bass passages that characterize *Demigod*'s most demanding tracks. The dual-chain drive delivered the smooth, responsive feel suited to high-velocity work.

## Recording Context

Hertz Studio in Białystok, Poland — run by brothers Wojtek and Sławek Wiesławski — became Behemoth's permanent studio home beginning with *Demigod*. The Wiesławski brothers' approach prioritized rhythmic articulation: Inferno's drum tracks are mixed prominently across the album, with snare and kick attacks clearly defined within the dense guitar arrangements.

This recording philosophy contrasts with the more compressed, rhythmically buried drum sounds common in contemporaneous extreme metal production. On *Demigod*, you can hear the Pearl Masters BRX's shell character and the Paiste Dark Energy's cymbal voice as distinct elements in the mix — a characteristic of every subsequent Behemoth/Wiesławski collaboration through *The Satanist*.

## Track Highlights

### Conquer All
One of *Demigod*'s most widely cited tracks and a live set staple since 2004. Full-speed blast at 240 BPM from the opening bar — the Paiste Dark Energy hi-hats' controlled fast-decay character is essential to maintaining articulation at maximum velocity. Became the Behemoth crowd anthem from this era.

### Slaves Shall Serve
Combines peak blast intensity with mid-tempo groove passages — demonstrating Inferno's ability to shift dynamically while maintaining rhythmic authority. The Pearl Free-Floating snare's sensitivity across tempo variations is audible in the contrast between the track's full-blast and controlled sections. One of Behemoth's longest-running setlist entries.

### Demigod (title track)
The title track's mid-paced opening builds to full blast intensity — the widest dynamic range on the album and the clearest showcase of the Pearl Masters BRX kit's full tonal palette. Tom passages during transitional sections reveal the BRX's maple warmth in contrast to the birch-driven attack of the blast sections.

## FAQs

**What drums did Inferno use on Demigod?**
Inferno recorded *Demigod* (2004) on a Pearl Masters BRX kit — his first album on this maple/birch hybrid shell series. Configuration: double 22" x 18" bass drums, 10" and 13" rack toms, 16" floor tom. The BRX's hybrid construction delivered balanced warmth and attack at Hertz Studio in Białystok, and this was also Behemoth's first recording at what would become their permanent studio home.

**What cymbals did Inferno use on Demigod?**
Inferno used the Paiste Signature Dark Energy series — 14" Hi-Hats, 16" and 18" Crashes, a 20" Ride, and an 18" China. The Dark Energy's fast-decay character was critical for maintaining rhythmic clarity during *Demigod*'s 240+ BPM blast beat passages. He later transitioned to Meinl Byzance Dark cymbals by the *Evangelion* (2009) sessions.

**How fast does Inferno play blast beats on Demigod?**
Inferno sustains blast beats at 240+ BPM across extended sections of *Demigod*, particularly on "Conquer All" and "Slaves Shall Serve" — among the fastest blast beat performances documented on a major extreme metal release in 2004. The Pearl Eliminator double pedal's chain-drive precision and the Paiste Dark Energy hi-hats' fast-decay character were both essential to sustaining this velocity with rhythmic clarity.

**What snare did Inferno use on Demigod?**
Inferno used a Pearl Free-Floating 14" x 6.5" steel snare on *Demigod*. Pearl's Free-Floating system suspends the shell independently of both hoops, maximizing sensitivity across the full head surface. The steel shell delivered a cutting attack that cut through Behemoth's dense, down-tuned guitar arrangements. Tuned to medium-high tension with a Remo Coated Ambassador batter head.

**What was significant about Demigod for Behemoth?**
*Demigod* (2004) was Behemoth's international commercial breakthrough: first album to chart in the US (#16 Billboard Heatseekers), first recording at Hertz Studio in Białystok, and the album that firmly established their 'blackened death metal' identity worldwide. For Inferno specifically, it was his first album on Pearl Masters BRX shells — the kit that defined his studio sound throughout the mid-2000s breakthrough era.

**How does Demigod differ from Evangelion in drum gear?**
On *Demigod* (2004): Pearl Masters BRX (maple/birch), Paiste Signature Dark Energy cymbals, Pearl Eliminator double pedal — a setup built around Inferno's Pearl endorsement of the period. On *Evangelion* (2009): Tama Starclassic Maple, Meinl Byzance Dark cymbals, Axis A Longboard double pedal — a different manufacturer profile reflecting gear evolution over the five-year gap. Both setups prioritize fast-decay cymbal character and precise kick mechanism for extreme blast beat performance, but the specific instruments differ significantly.
