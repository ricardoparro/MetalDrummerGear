## Overview

Released on May 4, 2010, *Diamond Eyes* stands as one of the most emotionally complex albums in modern heavy music. Written and recorded in the shadow of bassist Chi Cheng's catastrophic car accident, the album transformed grief, uncertainty, and loyalty into some of the most powerful songs Deftones had ever committed to tape. For Abe Cunningham, the sessions at Studio 606 in Northridge, California were unlike anything he had experienced in 15 years of drumming for the band.

Where *White Pony* demanded atmospheric restraint and *Saturday Night Wrist* explored fragmentation, *Diamond Eyes* pushed Cunningham toward something harder to name: a kind of energized focus that was simultaneously heavy and cathartic, punishing and luminous. The record opens with "Diamond Eyes" itself — a wall of layered guitars over a driving, almost triumphant groove — and immediately signals that this is a band channeling real emotion rather than calculated sound design.

Produced by Nick Raskulinecz at Dave Grohl's Studio 606 in Northridge, the album was recorded with a preference for live, dynamic performances over heavily edited digital precision, capturing the band's raw energy while preserving the nuance that defines their best work. This production philosophy pushed Cunningham to lock in tightly while leaving room for the subtle variations in feel that separate human drumming from machine-perfect grids.

The album earned a Grammy nomination for Best Hard Rock Album, becoming the band's second Grammy-nominated record after *White Pony*. For Abe Cunningham, *Diamond Eyes* marked the maturation of his Tama Starclassic Maple/Bubinga setup and his Zildjian cymbal palette into their most refined configuration yet.

## Recording Context

Recorded at Studio 606 in Northridge, California — Dave Grohl's private recording facility — with producer Nick Raskulinecz. The album was completed rapidly after the band shelved a previous project (*Eros*) following bassist Chi Cheng's 2008 car accident. Raskulinecz's live, dynamic production approach preserved Cunningham's natural feel variations rather than quantizing performances to a digital grid.

## Signature Tracks

**"Diamond Eyes"** — The album opener features a driving hi-hat pattern and ascending tom fills in the bridge that build anticipation before the final chorus release.

**"Rocket Skates"** — The lead single features Cunningham's most aggressive performance on the record: a syncopated verse pocket with kick-guitar interplay and quick hi-hat open/close dynamics.

**"You've Seen the Butcher"** — Demonstrates Abe's ghost note vocabulary and ability to disappear into a song's texture, transitioning from whispered ghost notes to full-force chorus in a single bar.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Abe Cunningham use on Diamond Eyes?**
A: Abe Cunningham recorded Diamond Eyes using a Tama Starclassic Maple/Bubinga hybrid kit at Studio 606. The setup included a 22x18" bass drum, 10 and 12" rack toms, and 14 and 16" floor toms. The Maple/Bubinga shell combination provided warm projection with focused low-end punch, suited to Nick Raskulinecz's preference for dynamic, unprocessed drum tones.

**Q: What snare did Abe Cunningham use on Diamond Eyes?**
A: Abe Cunningham used a Tama S.L.P. Big Black Steel snare at 14" x 8". The deep steel shell delivered authoritative punch that cut through the album's dense, down-tuned guitar arrangements without excessive processing.

**Q: Who produced Diamond Eyes, and where was it recorded?**
A: Diamond Eyes was produced by Nick Raskulinecz and recorded at Studio 606 in Northridge, California. Raskulinecz favored capturing live performances with natural room ambience rather than heavy digital processing, a philosophy that suited Abe Cunningham's dynamic, feel-based drumming style.

**Q: Was Diamond Eyes Grammy nominated?**
A: Yes. Diamond Eyes was nominated for Best Hard Rock Album at the 2011 Grammy Awards — Deftones' second Grammy-nominated album after White Pony (which won Best Metal Performance for "Elite" in 2001).
