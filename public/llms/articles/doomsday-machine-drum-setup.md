## Overview

Released on October 14, 2005 through Century Media Records, Arch Enemy's *Doomsday Machine* is the album that gave the world "Nemesis" — one of melodic death metal's most recognizable songs and a track that has defined the genre for twenty years. At the rhythmic center of *Doomsday Machine* was Daniel Erlandsson, the co-founding drummer who had been driving Arch Enemy since 1995 and whose approach to melodic death metal drumming reached its most fully realized expression on this record.

By 2005, Erlandsson had developed the drumming philosophy that distinguishes him from other extreme metal players: blast beats serve melody rather than overwhelm it; fills anticipate chord changes rather than occupy rhythmic cycles; dynamics — the contrast between thundering half-time grooves and explosive double-bass sections — create the emotional architecture that makes heavy music feel genuinely powerful rather than uniformly loud.

*Doomsday Machine* is the fullest demonstration of these principles. On "Nemesis," Erlandsson navigates tempo shifts and intensity builds with controlled precision — the blast sections feel like a release of tension rather than an attack, because he has spent the preceding verses building that tension with deliberate restraint. On "Carry the Black Flame" and "My Apocalypse," the same dynamic intelligence organizes every rhythmic decision.

The album was recorded at Mix Studio in Jona, Sweden and produced by guitarist Michael Amott.

### Pearl Reference: Projection and Warmth for Melodic Death Metal

For *Doomsday Machine*, Erlandsson used a Pearl Reference kit — Pearl's flagship production shell line with maple/mahogany hybrid construction delivering the combination of attack clarity and warm low-end that Arch Enemy's melodic arrangements require.

His configuration: two 22" x 18" bass drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, and 14" and 16" floor toms. The double kick drums provide the low-frequency foundation for "Nemesis" and "My Apocalypse" — double-bass sections that sustain across complete song sections while maintaining rhythmic clarity. The compact four-tom spread (10", 12", 14", 16") supports the melodic fill work that distinguishes Erlandsson's playing: fills tracking guitar phrase shapes, not simply occupying rhythmic space.

### Paiste RUDE: Maximum Projection Through Dense Production

Erlandsson's Paiste RUDE cymbal setup is built for one purpose: cutting through Michael Amott's dense multilayered guitar arrangements at *Doomsday Machine*'s production scale. The RUDE series — Paiste's loudest, most aggressively projected cymbal line — delivers extreme cut and raw character that maintains presence in a dense mix without post-production boosting.

His setup: 14" RUDE hi-hats, 18" and 19" RUDE Crash Rides, a 22" RUDE Power Ride for defined bell and bow in melodic passages, and a Paiste 2002 18" China for the album's most aggressive accent moments.

The RUDE hi-hats (14") drive the rhythmic grid in the fast passages — their extreme brightness allows the hi-hat patterns to register clearly even within Amott's multilayered wall of guitars.

### Pearl Eliminator Double Pedal

Erlandsson used Pearl Eliminator double bass pedals for *Doomsday Machine*. The Eliminator's interchangeable cam system allows feel-curve tuning for specific performance demands — from the driving 16th-note double-bass runs in "Nemesis" to the explosive blast-beat kick work in "Carry the Black Flame." Consistent spring action maintained even response across the full recording session and subsequent world tour.

## FAQ

**What drum kit did Daniel Erlandsson use on Arch Enemy Doomsday Machine?**
Daniel Erlandsson recorded Arch Enemy's Doomsday Machine (2005) using a Pearl Reference kit with two 22" x 18" bass drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, and 14" and 16" floor toms. Cymbals were Paiste RUDE series. Pedals were Pearl Eliminator double bass. Recorded at Mix Studio, Jona, Sweden and produced by Michael Amott.

**What is the drum setup on Nemesis by Arch Enemy?**
"Nemesis" features Daniel Erlandsson on Pearl Reference drums with Paiste RUDE cymbals and Pearl Eliminator pedals. The track showcases his dynamic philosophy at its most effective: a deliberate half-time groove in the verses builds tension while the chorus double-bass 16th-note runs and blast sections release it. RUDE Crash Rides mark the song's biggest structural moments. "Nemesis" is considered one of melodic death metal's definitive drum performances.

**What cymbals does Daniel Erlandsson use on Doomsday Machine?**
Erlandsson used Paiste RUDE cymbals on Doomsday Machine (2005) — Paiste's loudest series, chosen to cut through Michael Amott's dense multilayered guitars. His setup included 14" RUDE hi-hats, 18" and 19" RUDE Crash Rides, a 22" RUDE Power Ride, and a Paiste 2002 18" China for the album's most aggressive accent work.

**Who produced Arch Enemy Doomsday Machine?**
Arch Enemy's Doomsday Machine (2005) was produced by Michael Amott — guitarist and Arch Enemy co-founder — at Mix Studio in Jona, Sweden, with mixing by Rickard Bengtsson. Amott's production balances melodic guitar clarity with aggressive drum presence, making Doomsday Machine one of melodic death metal's best-produced albums.
