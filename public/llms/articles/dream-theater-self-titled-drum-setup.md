# Mike Mangini's Kit on 'Dream Theater' (Dream Theater, 2013)

> Mike Mangini's second Dream Theater studio album marked a pivotal gear shift to the Ludwig Keystone X maple kit. Full breakdown of the drums, Meinl Byzance cymbals, and DW 9002 double pedals behind the self-titled's progressive metal sound.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup

**Band / Album:** Dream Theater — *Dream Theater* (2013)
**Genre:** Progressive Metal

## Overview

By the time Dream Theater entered the studio to record their eleventh album in early 2013, Mike Mangini had spent two years as the band's drummer — touring the world behind "A Dramatic Turn of Events," learning the full catalog from the inside, and developing a stage chemistry with John Petrucci, John Myung, Jordan Rudess, and James LaBrie that no audition process could have generated.

That experience translated directly into the recording. Where "A Dramatic Turn of Events" was a debut — a world-record holder and Berklee professor proving he could handle the most technically demanding material in progressive metal — the self-titled "Dream Theater" (released September 24, 2013, on Roadrunner Records) was something different: an album where Mangini shaped the writing from the ground up alongside the rest of the band. Songs were not delivered to him to be learned; they were built around the full band's contributions in a room, with Mangini's scientific approach to rhythm woven into the compositional DNA.

The self-titled album debuted at number eight on the Billboard 200, number one in Germany, and number one in Greece — chart performance that reflected the critical acceptance of the Mangini era. The album's eight tracks range from the driving hard rock of "The Enemy Inside" (the lead single) to the 22-minute "Illumination Theory" that closes the record.

Central to this album's story is a significant gear change: Mangini moved to the Ludwig Keystone X maple kit — a departure from the Pearl Masterworks maple/birch hybrid configuration of "A Dramatic Turn of Events." Pure maple construction gives the Ludwig Keystone X a warmer, more resonant fundamental tone than the hybrid shells, and the difference is audible in the recording.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Ludwig Keystone X Maple (pure maple construction with reinforcement rings)
- **Snare:** Ludwig Supraphonic LM402, 14" x 5", aluminum shell
- **Cymbals:** Meinl — Meinl Byzance Traditional series
- **Hardware / Pedals:** DW 9002 Double Pedal; DW 9000 Series Hi-Hat Stand; DW 9000 Series Straight & Boom Stands; DW 9120M Drum Throne; Vic Firth MM (Mike Mangini) Signature Sticks
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Clear (toms), Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick), Remo Coated Ambassador (snare)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium batter tension; light snare wire tension for ghost note sensitivity and dynamic range

### Ludwig Keystone X Maple: A New Sonic Foundation

The most significant gear story on Dream Theater's self-titled album is Mike Mangini's move to the Ludwig Keystone X Maple kit. Coming from the Pearl Masterworks maple/birch hybrid shells used on "A Dramatic Turn of Events," the switch to pure maple construction was a deliberate tonal decision: Ludwig's Keystone X delivers warmer, more resonant fundamentals with a slower, singing decay that suits the harmonic density of Dream Theater's progressive arrangements.

Mangini's configuration mirrors the broad architecture of his A Dramatic Turn setup: a single 22" x 18" bass drum driven by a double pedal, four rack toms (8", 10", 12", 13"), and two floor toms (14", 16"). This six-voice tom array is used actively throughout the record — the melodic tom passages in "Illumination Theory," the cascading fills across the full kit in "Enigma Machine," and the driving floor tom accents of "The Enemy Inside" all reflect a drummer whose playing vocabulary is inseparable from his instrument's physical range.

The single bass drum configuration reflects Mangini's philosophical preference for single-drum efficiency over two-kick power. The Keystone X's deep 18" shell gives the kick warmth and weight without requiring a second drum to generate low-end impact.

### Meinl Byzance: Continuity Across the Mangini Arc

Mike Mangini's continued use of Meinl Byzance cymbals on the self-titled album provides sonic continuity that links this record to "A Dramatic Turn of Events" — even as the drum kit underneath shifted from Pearl to Ludwig. The Byzance Traditional series, hand-hammered in Turkey using B20 bronze alloy, produces the warm, complex, harmonically rich tones that have become Mangini's cymbal signature with Dream Theater.

The setup included 14" Traditional Medium hi-hats, three crashes (16" thin, 18" medium, 20" medium), a 22" Traditional Medium ride, an 18" Traditional China, and a 10" Traditional splash. The Byzance Traditional series' warm character integrates with Dream Theater's layered keyboard and guitar arrangements rather than competing with them.

The 22" Byzance ride's defined bell is central to the album's more pattern-driven sections, delivering clarity through the densest Jordan Rudess keyboard textures. Multiple Byzance crashes give Mangini dynamic range in accent work — the 16" thin crash responds fast enough for rapid single-note accents; the 20" medium provides the broad sustain for the epic transitions in "Illumination Theory."

### DW 9002 Double Pedal: Controlled Precision

The DW 9002 chain-drive double pedal remains Mangini's footwork platform on the self-titled. The chain drive delivers direct, predictable beater response suited to Mangini's controlled velocity technique. On "Enigma Machine," where kick patterns shift between driving grooves and syncopated 16th-note bursts, the pedal's consistency keeps every hit articulate in the recording.

## Key Facts

- Released September 24, 2013, on Roadrunner Records
- Produced by John Petrucci — Mangini's first DT album as performer only, not co-producer
- Second Mangini studio album — first where he participated in songwriting from the ground up
- Debuted at #8 Billboard 200, #1 Germany, #1 Greece
- Ludwig Keystone X maple kit — significant gear shift from A Dramatic Turn's Pearl Masterworks maple/birch hybrid
- Meinl Byzance Traditional cymbals continued from A Dramatic Turn
- DW 9002 double pedal drives both feet from single 22" bass drum
- Eight tracks including lead single "The Enemy Inside," instrumental "Enigma Machine," and 22-minute closer "Illumination Theory"
- Estimated kit value: $6,000-10,000 (Ludwig Keystone X configuration)

## Track Highlights

**The Enemy Inside** — lead single; driving verse groove with Byzance hi-hat 16th-note pattern; Supraphonic aluminum snare crack cuts through layered guitars.

**Enigma Machine** — pure instrumental showcase; odd-meter phrases, six-tom orchestrations, and DW 9002 double-bass precision; the most direct display of Mangini's technical vocabulary on the album.

**Illumination Theory** — 22-minute closer; spans enormous dynamic range; Byzance 20" crash marks major climactic transitions; all six Ludwig toms feature in extended melodic passages.
