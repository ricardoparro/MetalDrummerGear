# Emperor of Sand Drum Setup: Brann Dailor's Grammy-Winning Kit on Mastodon's 2017 Masterpiece

> Discover the exact drum kit, cymbals, and gear Brann Dailor used to record Mastodon's Grammy-winning Emperor of Sand (2017). Full breakdown of the Tama Starclassic Maple kit, Meinl Byzance cymbals, Iron Cobra single pedal, and the melodic single-kick approach behind progressive metal's most emotionally resonant album.

## Overview

Released on March 31, 2017, Mastodon's *Emperor of Sand* is a concept album about mortality and loss — a wanderer sentenced to death, traversing a desert and encountering forces that mirror the band's own experiences with cancer diagnoses affecting family members and loved ones. The album won **Best Metal Performance at the 2018 Grammy Awards** for "Sultan's Curse," Mastodon's first Grammy win, confirming the album's status as one of the most emotionally significant heavy releases of the decade.

For Brann Dailor, *Emperor of Sand* represents a compositional turning point. After the double-kick complexity of *Crack the Skye* (2009) and the expansive prog architecture of earlier records, Dailor deliberately returned to single bass drum — redirecting that creative energy into melodic, groove-centred playing and expanded vocal responsibilities. On tracks like "Show Yourself" and "Ancient Kingdom," he anchors both the rhythm section and the vocal harmonies simultaneously, demonstrating a discipline and musicality that elevates the album's emotional impact.

The gear Dailor used reflects this philosophical shift: the Tama Starclassic Maple kit, warm and melodically capable, combined with an expanded Meinl Byzance cymbal palette and a Tama Iron Cobra single pedal to produce one of Mastodon's most groove-forward, emotionally resonant drum performances. The album fills the critical arc between *The Hunter* (2011) and *Hushed and Grim* (2021) and represents the peak of Dailor's mature compositional voice.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple (single kick configuration)
- **Snare:** Tama Starphonic or Artstar, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance (Traditional, Foundry Reserve, and Brilliant series)
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra Single Bass Drum Pedal; Tama Iron Cobra Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Nitro Throne; Meinl Stick & Brush
- **Heads:** Evans UV1 Coated (batter), Evans Hazy 300 (snare resonant), Evans EMAD Clear (kick batter)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high tension — tuned for crack and projection with ghost note sensitivity

### The Tama Starclassic Maple: Single-Kick Melodic Canvas

For the *Emperor of Sand* sessions, Brann Dailor anchored his setup around a Tama Starclassic Maple kit in a single bass drum configuration — a deliberate shift from the double-kick architecture of *Crack the Skye* (2009). The Starclassic Maple is Tama's flagship maple-shell series, featuring 100% pure maple shells with Tama's Star-Cast mounting system that allows shells to resonate freely without lug restriction.

The single 22" bass drum, driven by the Tama Iron Cobra pedal, produces a full, round fundamental that anchors the album in groove rather than texture. The three-rack-tom configuration (10", 12", 13") plus 16" floor tom provides the dense melodic resolution that Dailor's playing demands — three closely spaced toms for melodic shapes without large intervallic jumps, with the floor tom as the lower anchor.

The maple shell construction suits the album's accessible character. *Emperor of Sand* is Mastodon's most melodically open record — concept album ambition married to radio-ready song architecture — and the Starclassic's warm, fundamental-rich tone allows the kit to sing through the mix without the aggressive punch of a birch or poplar shell.

### Meinl Byzance: Expanded Palette for the Desert Concept

Building on the Meinl Byzance foundation established on *Crack the Skye*, Dailor's *Emperor of Sand* cymbal setup expanded to include Traditional, Foundry Reserve, and Brilliant series — giving him a wider emotional and tonal range. The Foundry Reserve crashes feature a slower bloom and softer attack than the Traditional series, functioning as atmospheric textures rather than aggressive punctuation on the album's more reflective passages.

The Byzance series' B20 bronze (80% copper, 20% tin), hand-hammered using Turkish techniques, produces dark, warm, complex overtones that suited the album's vast emotional scope. Each cymbal accent on *Emperor of Sand* is placed with compositional intent — restraint as a skill, not a limitation.

### The Tama Iron Cobra Single Pedal

The Tama Iron Cobra single pedal was central to the *Emperor of Sand* sound. Its chain drive mechanism and linear cam provided consistent, responsive action for the groove-forward playing that defines the album — particularly on "Show Yourself" and "Sultan's Curse," where the single kick's clean, round fundamental drives the song's infectious momentum without the mechanical doubling effect of double kick.

## FAQs

**What drums does Brann Dailor use on Emperor of Sand?**
Tama Starclassic Maple with a single 22" bass drum configuration. Three rack toms at 10", 12", 13" and a 16" floor tom. This represents a return to single kick after the double-kick setup used on Crack the Skye (2009).

**What kit did Brann Dailor use in 2017?**
Tama Starclassic Maple kit with single 22" bass drum driven by a Tama Iron Cobra single pedal. Meinl Byzance cymbals across Traditional, Foundry Reserve, and Brilliant series. Meinl Stick & Brush sticks. Evans UV1 Coated batter heads.

**What cymbals does Brann Dailor use on Emperor of Sand?**
Meinl Byzance across multiple series: 14" Traditional hi-hats, 18" Foundry Reserve crash, 20" Traditional crash, 22" Traditional ride, 18" China, 10" Brilliant splash. B20 bronze, hand-hammered, dark and atmospheric.

**Did Mastodon win a Grammy for Emperor of Sand?**
Yes. Mastodon won Best Metal Performance at the 60th Grammy Awards (2018) for "Sultan's Curse" from Emperor of Sand. This was Mastodon's first Grammy win.

**How does Emperor of Sand differ from earlier Mastodon albums in terms of drumming?**
The most fundamental change is the return to single bass drum — Crack the Skye (2009) featured extensive double-kick playing, while Emperor of Sand commits to a single Iron Cobra pedal throughout. The playing is more restrained and groove-forward, with greater emphasis on feel and emotional storytelling over technical complexity.

**What pedal does Brann Dailor use on Emperor of Sand?**
Tama Iron Cobra single bass drum pedal. Chain drive with linear cam. Matches the Tama Starclassic kit and reflects the deliberate single-kick approach that defines the Emperor of Sand era.
