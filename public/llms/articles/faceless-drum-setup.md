## Overview

Released on April 8, 2003, *Faceless* is the commercial summit of Godsmack's career — a hard rock album that debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 in its first week and eventually certified 4× Platinum in the United States. At the center of that achievement was Shannon Larkin, recording his first full studio album with Godsmack less than a year after joining the band in 2002.

Larkin replaced founding drummer Tommy Stewart, bringing a resume that included thrash metal work with Wrathchild America and the mainstream crossover success of Ugly Kid Joe's "Everything About You" (1992). On *Faceless*, he used his Tama Starclassic Maple kit and Sabian AAX cymbal setup — the gear he had refined through years of professional touring — and delivered the most commercially significant hard rock drum performance of the decade's first half.

The album's lead single, "I Stand Alone" — written for the 2004 film *The Chronicles of Riddick* — became one of the most-played rock songs of the 2000s on American radio, driven by Larkin's relentless, pounding groove. "Straight Out of Line" demonstrated a heavier, more aggressive approach. "Changes" showcased his dynamic range. Together they established Larkin as one of hard rock's most powerful drummers almost immediately upon joining one of its biggest bands.

## Gear Details

### Tama Starclassic Maple: The Kit That Topped the Billboard 200

Shannon Larkin's early Godsmack era was defined by his Tama Starclassic Maple kit — one of the most respected professional drum kits available in the early 2000s. The Starclassic Maple's all-maple shell construction delivers a warm, full-bodied tone with a focused attack, characteristics that translate exceptionally well in both studio environments and the arena stages Godsmack was beginning to headline.

The double 22" kick configuration was central to Larkin's approach on *Faceless*. The 22" Starclassic kicks delivered punchy, defined low-end that locked with bassist Robbie Merrill's foundation throughout the album's 13 tracks. The Tama shells' natural tonal character sits beautifully in hard rock mixes — dense enough to feel powerful but open enough to breathe within arrangements that required dynamic range.

The streamlined four-tom setup — two rack toms (10", 12") and two floor toms (14", 16") — gave Larkin the range for creative fills without overcomplicating his configuration. Godsmack's music demands power and momentum above technical complexity, and Larkin's Tama setup was calibrated precisely for those priorities.

Larkin later transitioned to Ddrum Reflex endorsement, but *Faceless* remains the primary document of his Tama Starclassic Maple era.

### Steel Snare Authority: The Crack Behind the #1 Album

The snare voice on *Faceless* is one of the most immediately identifiable elements of the album's drum sound: a cutting, authoritative crack that drives through Godsmack's wall-of-guitar production with complete clarity. The 14" x 6.5" steel snare produced a high-frequency attack and projection that defined Godsmack's hard rock backbeat across every track.

Steel shell construction delivers the bright, cutting attack that hard rock production demands at this level. In Godsmack's sonic context — heavy guitars, prominent bass, and Sully Erna's powerful vocal delivery — the snare needed to establish its own sonic territory with authority. Steel shells' bright upper-midrange character claims that space naturally.

The 6.5" depth provided the body and sustain that separates a powerful snare sound from a thin one. Larkin tuned the snare at medium-high tension for cut and definition, a technique choice as much as a tuning choice.

### Sabian AAX: Bright and Explosive for Hard Rock's Biggest Stage

Larkin's Sabian AAX setup on *Faceless* was chosen for its aggressive, bright character — a cymbal voice that cuts through dense hard rock production without disappearing into the guitar wall. The AAX series, machine-hammered from brilliant brass, delivers a fast-speaking, high-frequency profile ideally suited to Godsmack's sonic environment.

The 14" AAX Stage Hi-Hats drove eighth-note patterns on "I Stand Alone," the relentless pulse of "Straight Out of Line," and the more nuanced quarter-note work on "Changes." The crash configuration — 18" and 19" AAX X-Plosion Crashes — provided two distinct accent voices: the 18" speaks fast for transitional fills, the 19" delivers a larger resonant crash for the album's biggest moments.

This pure Sabian AAX configuration represented Larkin's cymbal choice at the *Faceless* sessions. He later expanded to a dual AAX/HHX setup in subsequent Godsmack albums, adding HHX series cymbals for greater tonal complexity.

### DW 9000: The Mechanical Foundation for Double-Kick Grooves

The DW 9000 double bass pedal was the foundation for Faceless's driving double-kick patterns. Its chain-drive mechanism delivers consistent, reliable feel suited to Godsmack's groove-first approach — the kick patterns on "I Stand Alone," "Straight Out of Line," and "Dead and Broken" are built on deliberate placement and power. The adjustable cam let Larkin dial in the exact power curve and return speed his playing demanded. Notably, the DW 9000 remains Larkin's pedal of choice in his current Godsmack setup — unchanged from his Faceless-era configuration.

## Track Analysis

### I Stand Alone
Godsmack's most commercially successful track — driven by Larkin's pounding, relentless groove at approximately 136 BPM. Quarter-note kick pattern with authoritative snare on 2 and 4 — the definition of hard rock pocket drumming. Hi-hat work is consistent and aggressive. Originally written for the film *The Chronicles of Riddick* (2004); became one of the most-played rock songs of the 2000s.

### Straight Out of Line
One of *Faceless*'s heavier tracks at approximately 148 BPM — more aggressive tempo with driving double-kick foundation. Double-bass patterns underpin the verse sections. The steel snare cuts through with maximum authority. Crash accent placement is deliberate: every hit marks a genuine musical climax.

### Changes
*Faceless*'s most dynamically versatile track. Restrained verse sections with softer hi-hat work escalate into full-power choruses — demonstrating the dynamic range Larkin brought from his Ugly Kid Joe and Wrathchild America experience. A track that required genuine dynamic sensitivity, and Larkin delivers it without losing hard rock identity.

## FAQs

**What drums does Shannon Larkin use?**
Shannon Larkin currently plays Ddrum Reflex Series drums, but on *Faceless* (2003) — his debut studio album with Godsmack — he recorded with his Tama Starclassic Maple kit: double 22" bass drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, 14" and 16" floor toms. He later transitioned to Ddrum endorsement. For his complete current gear, see the [Shannon Larkin drum setup guide](/articles/shannon-larkin-drum-setup).

**What was Shannon Larkin's setup on Faceless?**
On Godsmack's *Faceless* (2003), Shannon Larkin used a Tama Starclassic Maple kit with double 22" bass drums, Sabian AAX cymbals including 14" Stage Hi-Hats and 18"/19" X-Plosion Crashes, a 14" x 6.5" steel snare tuned at medium-high tension, and DW 9000 double bass pedals. He played Vater Hickory 5B sticks (pre-signature era). It was his first studio album with Godsmack after joining the band in 2002 to replace founding drummer Tommy Stewart.

**What cymbals does Shannon Larkin use?**
Shannon Larkin is a Sabian endorsee. On *Faceless* (2003) he used the Sabian AAX series — 14" Stage Hi-Hats, 18" and 19" X-Plosion Crashes, a 21" Stage Ride, and an 18" Chinese cymbal. His current setup uses a dual AAX/HHX configuration with the HHX Raw Bell Dry Ride added for tonal complexity and the Sabian AAX X-Plosion Crashes and Stage Hi-Hats retained from the Faceless era. See the [Shannon Larkin drum setup](/articles/shannon-larkin-drum-setup) for his complete current cymbal configuration.

**Why did Faceless debut at #1 on the Billboard 200?**
Godsmack's *Faceless* (2003) debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 on the strength of "I Stand Alone" — one of the most-played rock songs of the 2000s on American radio — combined with Godsmack's established mainstream following built over two previous platinum albums. Shannon Larkin's arrival strengthened the live and studio unit: his powerful groove-first approach suited Godsmack's sound perfectly. The album ultimately certified 4× Platinum in the US.

**When did Shannon Larkin join Godsmack?**
Shannon Larkin joined Godsmack in 2002, replacing founding drummer Tommy Stewart. *Faceless* (2003) was his first full studio album with the band. He has remained Godsmack's drummer through all subsequent releases: *IV* (2006), *The Oracle* (2010), *1000hp* (2014), *When Legends Rise* (2018), and *Lighting Up the Sky* (2023).
