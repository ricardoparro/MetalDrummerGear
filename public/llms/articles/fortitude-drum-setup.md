## Overview

Released on April 30, 2021, Gojira's seventh studio album *Fortitude* arrived as the culmination of a decade-long artistic evolution — the final chapter in a story that began with the raw, exploratory *Magma* and ended with one of the most polished, ambitious records of the band's career. Where *Magma* stripped everything back, *Fortitude* builds outward with confidence: richer arrangements, broader sonic palette, and a production philosophy that elevated every element of the band's sound.

At the center of this evolution sits Mario Duplantier's most refined setup to date. For *Fortitude*, Mario committed to a custom Mapex drum kit — the culmination of his long-running relationship with the brand. The result is a drum sound that combines the punch and clarity he developed through the Pearl Reference Pure years with a new dimensional richness: deeper resonance, more complex overtones, and a physical presence that commands the record's ambitious arrangements.

The album's Grammy credential is "Amazonia" — nominated for Best Metal Performance at the 65th Grammy Awards (2023). The track is a thunderous environmental anthem, built around one of Mario's most powerful drum performances: a driving, intensely physical pattern that never sacrifices musicality for aggression. That a Grammy nomination came from such an uncompromising track is a testament to how completely Gojira had made their vision accessible without diluting it.

*Fortitude* debuted on international charts, continuing the commercial trajectory that *Magma* had established. The album was self-produced by Joe Duplantier at Silver Cord Studio in New York — but with a crucial new element: mixing by Andy Wallace, the legendary engineer behind Nirvana's *Nevermind*, Rage Against the Machine's debut, and System of a Down's *Toxicity*. Wallace's touch elevated the drum sound to a new standard of clarity and power, giving Mario's Mapex kit the full-frequency impact it deserved.

This article breaks down every component of Mario Duplantier's setup on *Fortitude*, explains the gear evolution from *Magma*, and examines what the Grammy nomination for "Amazonia" reveals about his place at the summit of progressive metal drumming.

### The Custom Mapex Era: Dimensional Richness

Mario Duplantier's transition to a custom Mapex drum kit for *Fortitude* marks the final gear evolution in his recorded catalog — at least through 2021 — and arguably his most deliberate. After the Pearl Reference Pure's maple/mahogany hybrid served *Magma*'s leaner production, the custom Mapex configuration offered a new dimension: greater shell resonance, a broader tonal palette, and a physical presence suited to *Fortitude*'s more ambitious arrangements.

Mapex drums are known for their engineering precision and tonal consistency. The custom configuration Mario employed retained his fundamental approach — dual 22" x 18" bass drums, four-tom setup — but introduced a new character to the shell tone. Where the Pearl Reference Pure emphasized warmth and punch, the Mapex shells deliver a more complex resonance: present, three-dimensional, and capable of greater dynamic range.

Mario's dual bass drum configuration remained the constant anchor across every gear change. Two independent 22" kick drums — each played with a single pedal, not a double pedal — define Gojira's rhythmic identity as much as any compositional choice. The bass drum tone on *Fortitude* is particularly notable: fuller and more commanding than on *Magma*, with Andy Wallace's mixing ensuring the kick translates with physical impact across every playback system.

The four-tom configuration persisted as Mario's melodic canvas. His approach to toms — tribal patterns, pitched melodic figures, rhythmic countermelodies — demands shells that respond consistently at any tuning. The custom Mapex configuration delivers this across the full range from tight rack toms to deep, resonant floor toms. On tracks like "Born for One Thing" and "Amazonia," the toms are not fill vehicles but melodic statements — extensions of the composition itself.

### Precision and Authority on 'Amazonia'

Mario's snare on *Fortitude* is a custom Mapex snare, matched to the kit configuration. The 14" x 6.5" dimensions align with his long-standing preference — a size that delivers authority in the heaviest passages while maintaining sensitivity at the dynamic lower end.

The custom maple construction provides the clarity and definition that *Fortitude*'s polished production demands. Where the Pearl Reference Pure snare on *Magma* was warm and slightly dark, the Mapex snare delivers a cleaner, more direct attack — suited to a mix where Andy Wallace's engineering maximizes the punch of each stroke without adding artificial sheen.

On "Amazonia" — the Grammy-nominated track — the snare is the rhythmic spine of one of Mario's most driving performances. The backbeat lands with authority throughout the track's relentless central groove, every rim shot translating as a physical statement rather than a technical choice. The custom Mapex snare's response time enables the complexity Mario deploys: ghost notes beneath the groove, sudden explosive accents at phrase peaks, and the ability to shift from restraint to full power within a single bar.

*Fortitude*'s production, courtesy of Joe Duplantier and Andy Wallace's mixing, gives the snare remarkable clarity. The drum sits in the center of the mix with presence and definition — not buried, not harsh, but exactly present enough to anchor every rhythmic figure across twelve tracks.

### Paiste Endorsement Continues: Evolved Signature Setup

Mario Duplantier's Paiste endorsement continued into the *Fortitude* era, maintaining the tonal philosophy he established on *Magma* while serving a more ambitious and polished production. The Paiste Signature series — made from Paiste's proprietary 2002 Bronze alloy — remained his cymbal of choice: full, musical, and present without harshness.

For *Fortitude*, the cymbal setup's primary function shifted slightly from *Magma*'s direct, stripped-back production. Where *Magma* used the Signature cymbals' warmth to fill a leaner mix, *Fortitude*'s more layered arrangements demand cymbals that project clearly across denser textures. The Paiste Signature's balanced, full-bodied character — present and projecting without dominating — proved equally suited to this context.

The 14" Signature Medium hi-hats provide the controlled, musical foundation for Mario's groove work. On "Born for One Thing" — the album's relentless opening statement — the hi-hats drive the main groove with authority, each stroke clearly articulated beneath the central guitar riff. The Signature hi-hats' weight and character maintain definition at high tempos without becoming harsh.

The crash configuration — 17", 19", and 20" Signature crashes — gives Mario the full range of attack and sustain profiles his dynamic approach requires. The 17" Fast Crash provides immediate, decaying accents for tight transitions; the 20" Medium Crash sustains for the musical resolution of longer phrases. Paiste Signature crashes sit in a mix rather than dominating it — serving the track rather than advertising the instrument.

Andy Wallace's mixing treated the Paiste Signature cymbals with the same care applied to every other element of the drum sound. The result is a cymbal presentation that is natural, dimensional, and perfectly balanced across the full frequency spectrum — a professional benchmark for modern metal production.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drum kit did Mario Duplantier use on Fortitude?**

A: On Fortitude (2021), Mario Duplantier played a custom Mapex drum kit, marking his transition from the Pearl Reference Pure drums used on Magma (2016). His configuration maintained the signature Gojira approach: two independent 22" x 18" bass drums (single pedals, not a double pedal), 10" and 12" rack toms, and 14" and 16" floor toms. The custom Mapex setup paired with Paiste Signature cymbals and DW hardware. Andy Wallace's mixing on Fortitude gave the Mapex kit its full dimensional impact on record — the most sonically complete drum sound of Mario's recorded career to date.

**Q: Was "Amazonia" from Fortitude nominated for a Grammy?**

A: Yes. "Amazonia" by Gojira, from the album Fortitude (2021), was nominated for Best Metal Performance at the 65th Grammy Awards (2023). The track is an environmental anthem and features one of Mario Duplantier's most physically powerful recorded drum performances — a relentless, anthemic groove that drives the track's message with uncompromising intensity. The nomination followed Gojira's first Grammy nomination for "Silvera" from Magma at the 59th Grammy Awards (2017), establishing the band as consistent Grammy contenders in the metal performance category.

**Q: How does the Fortitude drum setup compare to the Magma era?**

A: The Fortitude drum setup represents a significant evolution from Magma. The primary change was from Pearl Reference Pure drums (Magma) to a custom Mapex kit (Fortitude) — a shift that introduced greater dimensional resonance and tonal complexity suited to Fortitude's more ambitious arrangements. Cymbals remained Paiste Signature (continuity from Magma). Hardware evolved from Pearl Eliminator pedals to DW 9000 series. The most transformative addition, however, was Andy Wallace as mixing engineer — his work on Fortitude elevated the drum sound to a new professional standard compared to Magma's Joe Duplantier-mixed approach, giving every element of the custom Mapex setup maximum sonic impact.

**Q: Who produced and mixed the drums on Fortitude?**

A: Fortitude was self-produced by Joe Duplantier at Silver Cord Studio in New York — continuing Gojira's tradition of complete creative control. The key difference from previous albums was the addition of Andy Wallace as mixing engineer. Wallace, whose credits include Nirvana's Nevermind, Rage Against the Machine's debut album, System of a Down's Toxicity, and Jeff Buckley's Grace, brought world-class mixing expertise that gave Mario's custom Mapex kit its maximum sonic potential. The combination of Joe's intimate production knowledge and Wallace's mix engineering produced the most polished and powerful drum sound in Gojira's catalog.
