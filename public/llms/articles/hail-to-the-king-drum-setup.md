## Overview

Released on August 27, 2013, *Hail to the King* debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 — Avenged Sevenfold's commercial peak and the only full studio album recorded by Arin Ilejay as the band's official drummer. Ilejay joined A7X in 2011 following the death of founding drummer Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan in December 2009 and Dream Theater's Mike Portnoy serving as a temporary stand-in for the Nightmare album (2010).

Ilejay's response to the impossible task of replacing The Rev was disciplined and strategic. Rather than attempt to replicate The Rev's technically extravagant, jazz-influenced approach, he and the band leaned into a deliberate classic heavy metal aesthetic — Black Sabbath, Metallica, and Iron Maiden as touchstones. Producer Mike Elizondo captured a punchy, direct drum sound that matched this vision. The album debuted at #1 in multiple countries and went 2× Platinum in the United States.

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Arin Ilejay use on Hail to the King?**
A: Arin Ilejay recorded Hail to the King (2013) using a DW Collector's Series drum kit in Tobacco Burst finish. The configuration included double 22" x 18" bass drums, three rack toms (10", 12", 14"), and two floor toms (16", 18"). The North American Hard Rock Maple shells provided the attack and projection required for A7X's arena-scale performances. DW's Collector's Series is the company's flagship production lineup, used by professional drummers across mainstream metal.

**Q: Why did Arin Ilejay leave Avenged Sevenfold?**
A: Arin Ilejay departed Avenged Sevenfold in late 2014. The band announced the split without a detailed public explanation at the time. Ilejay later stated in interviews that he was let go rather than choosing to leave. Brooks Wackerman (then of Bad Religion) replaced him and has remained A7X's drummer since. Hail to the King (2013) is the only full studio album Ilejay recorded as the band's official drummer.

**Q: What is the drum setup on Hail to the King?**
A: The drum setup on Avenged Sevenfold's Hail to the King features a DW Collector's Series kit in Tobacco Burst, a DW 14" x 5.5" Collector's Maple/Mahogany snare, Zildjian A Custom cymbals (14" hi-hats, 16", 18", and 19" crashes, 20" ride, and a China cymbal), and DW 9000 Series double bass pedals. Drummer Arin Ilejay used Vic Firth American Classic 5A sticks. Producer Mike Elizondo pursued a direct, punchy drum sound matching the album's classic heavy metal aesthetic.

**Q: How does Arin Ilejay's kit on Hail to the King compare to The Rev's setup?**
A: The Rev (Jimmy Sullivan) played Pearl drums and Zildjian cymbals with a technically extravagant, jazz-influenced approach on earlier A7X albums including City of Evil (2005) and Avenged Sevenfold (2007). Arin Ilejay's DW Collector's setup on Hail to the King reflects a fundamentally different philosophy — power and authority in service of the album's classic metal aesthetic, rather than technical showmanship. Both setups are comparable in quality tier, but the playing approach and production context are distinctly different. Ilejay deliberately did not attempt to replicate The Rev's unique style, focusing instead on groove, power, and consistency.
