# Hardwired...to Self-Destruct Drum Setup: Lars Ulrich's 2016 Pearl Reference Kit

> Complete gear breakdown for Metallica's Hardwired...to Self-Destruct (2016). Discover Lars Ulrich's Pearl Reference Pure kit, his Ludwig Black Beauty snare, Zildjian A Custom cymbals, DW 9000 pedals, and the WorldWired tour setup behind Metallica's fastest album since ...And Justice for All.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [Lars Ulrich](/llms/drummers/lars-ulrich.md)
**Band / Album:** Metallica — *Hardwired...to Self-Destruct* (2016)
**Genre:** Thrash Metal / Heavy Metal

## Overview

Released on November 18, 2016, Hardwired...to Self-Destruct was Metallica's first album in eight years and their most aggressive record since ...And Justice for All in 1988. Debuting at #1 in 18 countries — including Metallica's first-ever #1 on the US Billboard 200 — the double album marked a decisive return to thrash roots. Lars Ulrich's drum performance is his most focused and tempo-demanding since the Justice era, anchored by a new Pearl Reference Pure kit and the Ludwig Black Beauty snare that became his WorldWired-era trademark.

Recorded at Metallica's own HQ Studios in San Rafael, California, with producer Greg Fidelman (who had also engineered Death Magnetic), Hardwired's drum sound prioritizes clarity and dynamic range over the compressed density of the 2008 record. The Pearl Reference Pure's single-ply maple shells, the Black Beauty's brass-shell warmth, and Fidelman's reduced-compression approach give the drums more space than any Metallica album since the early 90s.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure (custom Metallica matte black finish) — 22"×18" kick, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Ludwig Black Beauty LB417, 14"×6.5", seamless brass shell with black nickel finish
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/19" crashes, 18" China, 20" ride
- **Hardware / Pedals:** DW 9000 Double Pedal; DW 9000 Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Original throne; Ahead Lars Ulrich Signature sticks
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD2 Clear (kick batter), Evans EQ3 Resonant (kick front), Evans EC2 Clear (toms), Evans HD Dry (snare batter), Evans 300 Snare Side (resonant)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high tension — tighter than the Black Album prototype, looser than Justice-era steel snare

### Lars's Studio Kit: Pearl Reference Pure

The Pearl Reference Pure represents Lars's most significant gear shift in decades — leaving his long-standing Tama endorsement for Pearl's flagship single-ply maple shell line. Where Tama Starclassic used multi-ply construction for controlled attack, the Reference Pure's single-ply maple delivers a wider, more resonant decay suited to Fidelman's clarity-focused production.

The 22"×18" bass drum (deeper than any previous Lars kick) provides the low-end foundation for tracks approaching 225 BPM. The four-tom layout — 10", 12", 14", 16" — matches Lars's standard configuration across the Black Album and Justice eras, but the shell character delivers more open sustain, audible in the breakdown sections of "Atlas, Rise!" and the groove passages of "Dream No More."

The same Reference Pure configuration was used throughout the WorldWired tour (2016–2019), making Hardwired Lars's most consistent live-to-studio gear period since the early 90s.

### Ludwig Black Beauty LB417: The WorldWired Trademark

Lars returned to Ludwig for the first time since the Supraphonic he used on Master of Puppets (1986). The Black Beauty LB417 — 14"×6.5" with seamless brass shell and black nickel plating — delivers a warmer, fatter crack than the aluminum or steel snares of Lars's thrash and Black Album eras.

Tuned at medium-high tension with Evans HD Dry batter and Evans 300 snare side, the Black Beauty cracks with authority on the fastest tracks ("Hardwired," "Spit Out the Bone") while maintaining body and warmth on mid-tempo material. It became visually synonymous with the WorldWired era — recognizable in every live drum-cam video from 2016 onward.

### Zildjian A Custom: Brightness for the Return to Thrash

The A Custom series — brighter and faster-responding than the traditional A series — suits Hardwired's tempo demands. The 14" hi-hats speak cleanly through fast 8th-note patterns; the three-crash array (16"/18"/19") provides dynamic range from quick accents to massive climactic crashes; the 18" China marks riff transitions throughout the album.

### DW 9000 Pedals: Direct Drive at 225 BPM

Lars switched from Tama Iron Cobra (cam-driven) to DW 9000 (direct drive) double pedals for the Hardwired era. At "Spit Out the Bone"'s approximately 225 BPM, the direct drive mechanism's lower inertia provides more consistent double-bass performance than the cam-based Iron Cobra — a practical gear decision driven by the album's tempo demands.

## Production: Greg Fidelman and the Hardwired Sound

Recorded at Metallica's HQ Studios in San Rafael, California. Key production decisions:

- **Reduced compression** vs. Death Magnetic — transient definition and dynamic range restored
- **Room mics** used more than Death Magnetic, less prominently than the Black Album — a middle path
- **Complete-take performance philosophy** — Lars recorded songs start-to-finish to preserve energy and feel
- **Double album structure** — Disc 1 opens with fastest material (213–225 BPM); Disc 2 explores more dynamic range
- Microphone setup: Shure Beta 52A (kick inside), AKG D112 (kick outside), Sennheiser MD421 (toms), Shure SM57 (snare top), AKG C451 (hi-hat), Neumann U87 (overheads)

## FAQ

**What snare does Lars Ulrich use on Hardwired...to Self-Destruct?**
Lars used a Ludwig Black Beauty LB417 (14"×6.5") — seamless brass shell with black nickel finish, tuned at medium-high tension. The batter head is an Evans HD Dry; the resonant side uses an Evans 300 Snare Side. The brass shell delivers a warmer, fatter crack than the aluminum Supraphonic from the Master of Puppets era or the steel snares Lars used through Justice and the Black Album. The Black Beauty became Lars's long-term WorldWired-era trademark and is now his most recognized instrument.

**Is Hardwired Lars Ulrich's best drumming?**
Hardwired represents Lars's most focused thrash drumming since ...And Justice for All. It is intentionally economical — Lars plays to serve the song's aggression rather than showcase technical complexity. "Spit Out the Bone" (~225 BPM) tests double-bass endurance; "Moth Into Flame" and "Atlas, Rise!" demonstrate groove and dynamic range. Whether it's his "best" depends on what you value: Justice (1988) peaks for technical complexity, the Black Album (1991) for groove feel, Hardwired for focused thrash aggression in a modern production context. Most drummers consider the Justice era Lars's technical ceiling.

**What kit did Metallica use on the WorldWired tour?**
Lars used Pearl Reference Pure throughout the WorldWired tour (2016–2019): 22"×18" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms in custom matte black finish. Snare: Ludwig Black Beauty LB417 (14"×6.5"). Cymbals: Zildjian A Custom — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/19" crashes, 18" China, 20" ride. Hardware: DW 9000 double pedal and stands. Heads: Evans throughout (EMAD2 kick, EC2 toms, HD Dry snare). The WorldWired live setup is essentially identical to the Hardwired studio configuration — one of the most consistent live-to-studio transitions of Lars's career.

**What is different about Hardwired drums compared to Death Magnetic?**
Three major differences: (1) Compression — Death Magnetic was infamously brickwalled; Hardwired producer Greg Fidelman reduced compression significantly, restoring transient definition and dynamic range. (2) Tempo — Hardwired is Metallica's fastest album since Justice, with "Spit Out the Bone" approaching 225 BPM, a context Death Magnetic rarely reached. (3) Gear — Lars moved from Tama Starclassic Maple and Iron Cobra pedals to Pearl Reference Pure shells and DW 9000 double pedals, changing the attack character of the kick drum substantially.
