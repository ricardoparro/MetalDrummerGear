# Children of Bodom 'Hate Crew Deathroll' Drum Setup — Jaska Raatikainen's 2003 Gear

> Discover Jaska Raatikainen's drum setup on Children of Bodom's commercial and critical peak, Hate Crew Deathroll (2003) — Tama Starclassic Maple kit, Paiste 2002 cymbals, Tama Iron Cobra double pedal, and the melodic death metal technique behind one of Finland's defining metal albums.

**Genre:** Melodic Death Metal

## Overview

Released on January 27, 2003 on Nuclear Blast Records, Children of Bodom's fourth studio album Hate Crew Deathroll represents the band at the height of their powers. Recorded at Tico Tico Studio in Kemi, Finland and produced by Hiili Hiilesmaa, the album distilled everything that made the Espoo quintet essential: neoclassical guitar leads, keyboard countermelodies, and a rhythm section that refused to treat heavy metal as a backdrop.

Co-founding the band alongside the late Alexi Laiho in 1993, Jaska Raatikainen spent a decade developing a drumming approach precise enough to complement Laiho's neoclassical picking runs while aggressive enough to anchor the band's death metal identity. By 2003 that synthesis was fully realised. Hate Crew Deathroll gave Raatikainen the canvas to demonstrate everything his style could do — from the opening double-bass velocity of "Needled 24/7" to the mid-paced, groove-driven backbone of the title track.

For the Hate Crew Deathroll sessions, Raatikainen used a Tama Starclassic Maple shell pack — reflecting the practical reality that studio and live setups don't always match. The Paiste 2002 cymbal series provided the attack and brightness the album's dense arrangements demanded. The Tama Iron Cobra double pedal delivered the mechanical consistency his sustained double-kick patterns required.

### Drum Kit

The Tama Starclassic Maple is Tama's professional flagship line — all-maple shells with Tama's Star-Cast mounting system that isolates the shell from hardware contact, allowing each drum to resonate freely. Maple shells speak quickly: each stroke produces a defined onset with controlled sustain, giving double-bass patterns and tom fills the note separation needed to register individually in dense arrangements where neoclassical guitar runs and keyboard lines compete for the same frequency space. The double 22" kick drum configuration underpins sustained double-bass patterns that run through entire songs at tempos ranging from 160 to 210 BPM. The compact tom spread (10", 12", 14", 16") supports the fast melodic fill sequences that follow Laiho's guitar runs and Wirman's keyboard lines throughout the album.

### Snare

The snare sound on Hate Crew Deathroll is dry and direct — a tight, cracking attack that cuts through the guitar and keyboard arrangements without wash. The Tama Starclassic Maple snare (14" x 6") provides body depth for projection while the maple shell character delivers warmth at the attack. Tuned medium to medium-high with tight snare wires, the drum delivers the sharp crack that Children of Bodom's recordings are known for.

### Cymbals

The Paiste 2002 series is built for projection and clarity. The brilliant NS12 bronze alloy finish produces cymbals that speak through any mix without post-production enhancement. The Paiste Sound Edge 14" hi-hats feature a wavy edge on the top cymbal, creating a distinct half-open chick that remains cutting at Children of Bodom's high tempos. The 18" China provides the aggressive, upward-cutting trashy character that marks riff transitions and blast-beat entries — used structurally throughout "Needled 24/7," "You're Better Off Dead," and the title track.

### Pedals

The Tama Iron Cobra double bass pedal anchors Raatikainen's sustained double-kick work. Its interchangeable cam design (Rolling Glide or Power Glide) and dual-chain drive deliver consistent stroke response across extended playing — essential when double-bass patterns sustain through full songs at 185–210 BPM tempos.

## Recording Context: Tico Tico Studio

Tico Tico Studio in Kemi, Finland was the recording home for several Children of Bodom albums. Hiili Hiilesmaa's production philosophy emphasised clarity and definition: kick drums register with direct punchy response, the snare sits at the front of the mix with dry crack, toms decay fast enough for clean fill articulation, and the Paiste 2002 brightness complements the recording without frequency conflict. The result is one of the cleaner-sounding extreme metal drum productions of the 2003 period — aggressive in character but detailed in execution.
