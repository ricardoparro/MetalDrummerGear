## Overview

Released on February 1, 1999, *Hatebreeder* is Children of Bodom's second full-length album and the record that established their signature neo-classical death metal sound. Recorded at Astia Studio in Imatra, Finland with producer Anssi Kippo — who would become a key figure in Finnish metal production — it was Jaska Raatikainen's first major professional studio work, completed at age 19.

At the center of *Hatebreeder*'s relentless momentum was Raatikainen's early Tama configuration, paired with Zildjian A Series cymbals and DW 5000 double pedals. This pre-endorsement setup delivered both the velocity required for the album's most aggressive passages and the musical sensitivity needed to track Janne Wirman's neo-classical keyboard countermelodies and Alexi Laiho's guitar runs. The album is the entry point for any Jaska Raatikainen discovery cluster — the origin of the drumming language that *Follow the Reaper* (2000) and *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003) would develop and refine.

- **Drums:** Tama custom early configuration (maple shells, 6-piece)
- **Bass Drums:** 22" x 18" (x2 — double kick configuration)
- **Toms:** 10", 12" rack toms; 14", 16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Tama 14" x 5.5" Maple
- **Hi-Hats:** Zildjian A 14" Hi-Hats
- **Crashes:** Zildjian A 16" Crash, Zildjian A 18" Crash
- **Ride:** Zildjian A 20" Ride

- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A

- **Heads (Bass):** Remo Powerstroke 3 (batter and resonant)

## Gear Details

### Early Tama Configuration: The Foundation of a Career

For *Hatebreeder*, Jaska Raatikainen played an early Tama configuration — maple shells in a 6-piece double-kick setup that predates the Tama Starclassic Maple era documented on *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003). Tama's professional construction philosophy delivered the attack definition and tonal body that Children of Bodom's neo-classical death metal arrangements required at Astia Studio.

The double bass drum configuration — twin 22" x 18" kick drums — was central to Raatikainen's approach from the start. Tracks like "Towards Dead End" and "Children of Decadence" deploy continuous double-bass patterns across extended passages, supporting the neo-classical guitar and keyboard lines without competing with them. The 22" diameter gave the kick drums the low-frequency presence that death metal production requires; the 18" depth added the punch that cut through Anssi Kippo's dense mix.

The compact tom spread (10", 12" rack toms; 14", 16" floor toms) gave Raatikainen the tonal range for Children of Bodom's characteristically melodic fill work. His instinct, even at 19, was to follow melodic contours with tom phrases that echoed Alexi Laiho's guitar runs and Janne Wirman's keyboard lines — a defining characteristic of CoB's drumming approach that was fully present on this debut.

### Zildjian A Series: Brightness and Cut for the Neo-Classical Debut

Raatikainen's cymbal setup on *Hatebreeder* centered on Zildjian's A Series — a pre-endorsement selection that preceded the Paiste 2002 combination on *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003) and the later Zildjian A Custom / K Custom pairing of his Pearl era. The Zildjian A Series' B20 bronze alloy produces a bright, immediately responsive sound with clear attack — qualities essential in dense, keyboard-heavy arrangements where cymbals must cut through the mix naturally.

The 14" A hi-hats anchored the rhythmic foundation at extreme tempos. At the BPM ranges Children of Bodom deployed on *Hatebreeder*, each sixteenth note needed to register distinctly in the mix. Two A Series crashes (16" and 18") provided tonal variety for accent work: the 16" for fast accents in tight melodic passages; the 18" for section boundaries and climactic builds. The 18" A China added the aggressive, upward-cutting attack essential to the album's most aggressive riff transitions.

### DW 5000: The Double Kick Engine of the CoB Debut

The DW 5000 double pedal was Jaska Raatikainen's foot foundation on *Hatebreeder* — one of the most respected chain-drive double pedals in the late 1990s. DW's 5000 series chain-drive mechanism delivers consistent mechanical response across high-tempo double-bass patterns: each stroke drives through with the same force and returns to the same resting position, supporting the pattern consistency that Children of Bodom's technical arrangements demanded. On tracks like "Towards Dead End" and "Children of Decadence," where double-bass patterns run for complete song sections, pedal consistency directly determined whether each stroke registered as a distinct musical event.

## Track Analysis

### Warheart
Album opener — immediate statement of the CoB neo-classical death metal template. Sustained double-bass patterns from the first bar establish the DW 5000's performance profile. Blast beat sections alternate with groove passages, demonstrating dynamic control from track one. Zildjian A hi-hat articulation at extreme tempo establishes the A Series' clarity in the keyboard-dense mix.

### Children of Decadence
Complex fill work showcasing the earliest expression of Raatikainen's neo-classical fill language. Tom phrases follow Janne Wirman's keyboard lines — early demonstration of the melody-following approach that defines CoB's drumming identity. Double-kick pattern shifts support rhythmic interplay with keyboard and guitar counterpoint. Zildjian A crashes deployed on structurally significant beats, deliberate rather than decorative.

### Downfall
Album closer and Raatikainen's most compositionally varied performance on the record. Multiple dynamic transitions from blast sections to groove passages demonstrate full musical range. Ride cymbal deployed for textural variation — A Series 20" ride bell providing deliberate accent work in the contrasting sections. Snare ghost notes audible in verse passages demonstrate the maple shell's dynamic range from whisper to full force.

### Towards Dead End
Sustained double-bass showcase — extended patterns across the full track without simplification. The DW 5000's chain-drive consistency is most audible here: each stroke registers as a distinct musical event rather than blur. China cymbal deployed for riff-accent work at structural transitions, not as continuous texture.

## FAQs

**What drums did Jaska Raatikainen use on Hatebreeder?**
On *Hatebreeder* (1999), Jaska Raatikainen played an early Tama maple configuration — a 6-piece double-kick setup with twin 22" x 18" bass drums, rack toms (10" and 12"), and floor toms (14" and 16"). This was the pre-Starclassic era, distinct from the Tama Starclassic Maple setup documented on *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003) and the Pearl Masters endorsement that followed. Recorded at Astia Studio in Imatra with Anssi Kippo producing. For Raatikainen's complete gear history across CoB's career, see the [Jaska Raatikainen drummer profile](/drummer/jaska-raatikainen).

**What pedals did Jaska Raatikainen use on Hatebreeder?**
Jaska Raatikainen used the DW 5000 double pedal on *Hatebreeder* (1999). The DW 5000's chain-drive mechanism delivered the consistent response essential to sustained double-kick patterns at extreme tempos — tracks like "Towards Dead End" and "Children of Decadence" deploy continuous double-bass passages where pedal consistency determined whether each stroke registered as a distinct musical event. See the [Jaska Raatikainen full kit guide](/articles/jaska-raatikainen-drum-setup) for his pedal evolution across CoB's career.

**What cymbals did Jaska Raatikainen use on Hatebreeder?**
Jaska Raatikainen used Zildjian A Series cymbals on *Hatebreeder* (1999) — 14" A hi-hats, A crashes (16" and 18"), a 20" A ride, and an 18" A China. This predates the Paiste 2002 setup on *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003) and his later Zildjian A Custom endorsement era. The A Series' B20 bronze and traditional lathing provided the natural cut through keyboard-heavy arrangements that Astia Studio's mix required.

**What is the drumming style on Hatebreeder?**
*Hatebreeder* (1999) documents the first fully-realized expression of Jaska Raatikainen's neo-classical death metal drumming language: sustained double-bass velocity at extreme tempos combined with melodically aware fill work that tracks Janne Wirman's keyboard lines and Alexi Laiho's guitar runs. At 19, Raatikainen was already deploying the compositional intelligence that defines CoB's drumming — tom fills following melodic contours, blast beats as structural intensity peaks rather than constant texture, ghost notes for dynamic range. The album established the template that *Hate Crew Deathroll* would refine.

**How does the Hatebreeder setup compare to Hate Crew Deathroll?**
The *Hatebreeder* setup is the origin point: early Tama maple configuration, Zildjian A Series, DW 5000 double pedal. By *Hate Crew Deathroll* (2003), Raatikainen had moved to the Tama Starclassic Maple, Paiste 2002 cymbals, and Tama Iron Cobra pedals — a more refined combination that produced the polished drum sound of CoB's commercial peak. The drumming language is consistent across both albums; the gear evolved around it. See the [Hate Crew Deathroll drum setup](/articles/hate-crew-deathroll-drum-setup) for the full comparison.
