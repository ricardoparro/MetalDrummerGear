# Immutable Drum Setup: Tomas Haake's 2022 Meshuggah Kit Breakdown

> Complete breakdown of Tomas Haake's drum gear on Meshuggah's Immutable (2022). Discover the Grammy-nominated Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit, Zildjian A Custom and K series cymbals, Axis longboard pedals, and the hardware refinements behind Meshuggah's most recent studio album.

## Overview

Released on April 1, 2022, *Immutable* is Meshuggah's ninth studio album and their most recent release. Recorded at Sweetspot Studios in Halmstad, Sweden after a COVID-forced delay from *The Violent Sleep of Reason* (2016), the album represents Tomas Haake's most refined Tama Starclassic Bubinga configuration to date. The album earned a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance — the band's first Grammy recognition since the *obZen* era. For drummers and researchers, *Immutable* is the definitive present-day source for "tomas haake drum kit" and "meshuggah current setup" queries.

The extra time created by the COVID delay gave Haake the opportunity to work through hardware refinements without recording pressure — subtle changes to pedal response, snare tuning, and kit positioning that collectively produce the most polished version of his post-obZen rig.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga Custom (Meshuggah-spec), double 22"x18" bass drums, 10"x8" and 12"x9" rack toms, 14"x14" and 16"x16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Tama Starphonic Steel, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom 14" Hi-Hats, A Custom 18" and 19" Crashes, A Custom 21" Ping Ride; Zildjian K 19" China, K 18" Dark Thin Crash
- **Pedals:** Axis A Longboard (configured as two independent single pedals — no connecting drive shaft)
- **Hi-Hat Stand:** Tama Iron Cobra
- **Throne:** Porter & Davies BC2 (vibration-transmitting)
- **Sticks:** Wincent Tomas Haake Signature
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke P3 Clear (kick batter), Remo Emperor Clear (toms), Remo Ambassador Coated (snare batter)

### Drums: Tama Starclassic Bubinga

Haake's Tama Starclassic Bubinga custom spec is the present-day answer to any "what does Tomas Haake play?" query. Bubinga is a dense African hardwood that produces more attack and faster decay than maple, with a tight low-end response suited to Meshuggah's downtuned (F standard) harmonic environment. The compact configuration — four toms over double 22"x18" kick drums — reflects Meshuggah's approach: the bass drums and snare carry the rhythmic architecture; toms serve specific, deliberate roles.

The two-single-pedal configuration is Haake's signature hardware choice, unchanged across his career. Both feet operate with complete mechanical independence — no drive shaft, no crosstalk. This independence is structurally necessary for the polyrhythmic bass drum patterns that define the band's sound. The COVID delay allowed Haake to refine the Bubinga configuration further before committing to recording.

### Snare: Tama Starphonic Steel 14"x6.5"

The Starphonic Steel delivers a bright, defined crack that cuts through Meshuggah's dense guitar layers. At 6.5" depth, it provides body without the sustain that would blur Haake's precisely placed snare hits. Tuned medium-high with tight snare wires, the snare prioritises attack and note separation — every placement is a deliberate rhythmic event.

### Cymbals: Zildjian A Custom + K Series

The shift from Sabian (obZen era) to Zildjian (Immutable era) reflects a palette expansion. Zildjian A Custom provides the brightness and precision for rapid hi-hat patterns and defined crash accents. The K series additions — 19" China and 18" Dark Thin Crash — introduce darker, warmer overtones that create tonal contrast for specific musical moments. This dual-character setup allows Haake to match cymbal voice to musical context within Meshuggah's varied arrangements.

### Pedals: Axis A Longboard

The Axis A Longboard replaced the Sonor Perfect Balance pedals of the obZen era. The longboard footboard and Axis cam-and-linkage mechanism provide smooth, consistent response with minimal mechanical resistance — essential for the extreme endurance and pattern complexity of Meshuggah's music. Configured as two fully independent single pedals, the Axis units deliver the foot autonomy that is non-negotiable for Haake's polyrhythmic bass patterns.
