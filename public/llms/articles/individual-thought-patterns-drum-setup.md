# Individual Thought Patterns Drum Setup: Gene Hoglan's Death Debut (1993)

## Overview

Released on June 22, 1993, Death's "Individual Thought Patterns" is the pivotal transitional album between Sean Reinert's jazz-infused masterwork on *Human* (1991) and Gene Hoglan's universally acclaimed peak on *Symbolic* (1995). This is the record where Chuck Schuldiner first unlocked Gene Hoglan's polyrhythmic potential — writing compositions specifically around the precision that earned Gene the nickname "The Atomic Clock."

When Sean Reinert left Death after *Human* to focus on Cynic, Schuldiner didn't look for a stylistic clone. He found something different in Gene Hoglan, then known for his crushing work in Dark Angel. Hoglan brought mathematical precision where Reinert had brought jazz swing, and *Individual Thought Patterns* is the record where those qualities first met Schuldiner's increasingly progressive compositional vision.

Recorded at Morrisound Recording in Tampa with legendary producer Scott Burns, the album featured guitarist Andy LaRocque (King Diamond), Sean Murphy on guitar, and returning bassist Steve DiGiorgio. At its rhythmic heart was Gene Hoglan, making his Death debut and announcing himself as one of extreme metal's essential forces.

This is the "missing link" in Death's rhythmic evolution — and among drummers, its polyrhythmic complexity and Hoglan's performance are highly regarded.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series, 6-piece (Natural Lacquer finish)
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel or Sensitone Steel, 14" x 6.5" (transitional period before full DW endorsement)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series
- **Hardware / Pedals:** DW 5000 Series (two single pedals, one per bass drum); DW 5000 Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Standard throne; Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Clear (toms batter), Remo Ambassador Clear (toms resonant); Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick batter); Remo Ambassador Coated (snare batter)

### DW Collector's Series: Precision in Six Pieces

Gene Hoglan used a DW Collector's Series kit for the Individual Thought Patterns sessions — a six-piece configuration with twin 22" x 18" bass drums. DW's North American maple shells delivered focused attack and warm projection, differentiating Hoglan's ITP sound from the drier tones of his later Tama work on Symbolic.

The twin 22" bass drums were driven by two individual DW 5000 pedals — true double bass, not a slave-chain double pedal. This gave each foot independent response and feel, critical for the even, sustained double bass patterns throughout the album. The rack toms (10" and 12") provided articulate attack for polyrhythmic fill vocabulary, while the floor toms (14" and 16") completed a setup designed for melodic movement rather than sheer aggression.

### The Pearl Transitional Snare

At this stage in 1993, Hoglan had not yet committed fully to DW hardware. The snare was a Pearl steel drum — bright, cutting, and projecting — that suited Scott Burns's Morrisound production approach perfectly. Tuned medium-high for maximum articulation, it delivered the crack needed for death metal intensity while remaining sensitive enough for ghost notes and dynamic passages.

### Zildjian A-Series: Clarity for Polyrhythmic Complexity

Gene's cymbal setup was built around Zildjian A-series — providing clarity, projection, and predictable response across the wide dynamic range Death's compositions demanded. The 14" New Beat hi-hats allowed intricate patterns at any tempo. Multiple crashes (16", 18", 19") covered the album's dynamic range. The 20" A Medium Ride incorporated ride-pattern work in a way that Chuck Schuldiner specifically appreciated.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What kit did Gene Hoglan use on Individual Thought Patterns?**
Gene Hoglan used a DW Collector's Series kit for the ITP sessions in 1993 — a six-piece configuration with twin 22" bass drums. His snare was a Pearl steel drum (a transitional choice before full DW endorsement), and cymbals were Zildjian A-series including 14" New Beat hi-hats and a 20" A Medium Ride. Recorded at Morrisound Recording in Tampa with producer Scott Burns.

**Q: Why did Sean Reinert leave Death after Human?**
Sean Reinert left Death after *Human* (1991) primarily to focus on Cynic, the progressive metal band he co-founded with guitarist Paul Masvidal. Reinert and Masvidal had been developing Cynic's unique fusion of death metal, jazz, and electronic elements and chose to commit fully to that project. The departure was professional and amicable.

**Q: How does Individual Thought Patterns' drumming differ from Symbolic?**
Both albums feature Gene Hoglan, but they reflect different stages of his Death collaboration. On ITP, Gene was establishing his vocabulary within Chuck's compositions — powerful, precise, and polyrhythmic, but slightly more direct. By Symbolic, two years of working with Schuldiner had deepened the compositional interplay: fills are more musical, dynamics more sophisticated, performances more refined. ITP is where the partnership began; Symbolic is where it peaked.

**Q: Who played guitar on Individual Thought Patterns?**
The guitar lineup featured Chuck Schuldiner alongside Andy LaRocque (known as lead guitarist in King Diamond) and Sean Murphy. Steve DiGiorgio, who had also played on *Human*, returned on fretless bass — maintaining rhythmic continuity with the previous album despite the drummer change.

**Q: Is Individual Thought Patterns considered a classic Death album?**
It is widely respected but often overshadowed by *Human* and *Symbolic*. Among drummers and technically-minded metal fans, the album's polyrhythmic complexity and Hoglan's performance are highly regarded. It is the essential "missing link" in Death's rhythmic evolution.
