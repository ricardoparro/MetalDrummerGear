> Complete drum gear breakdown for Cavalera Conspiracy's debut album Inflikted. Discover Igor Cavalera's 2008 kit — the Tama Starclassic that powered the Cavalera brothers' reunion album after twelve years apart, plus Paiste RUDE cymbals and Tama Speed Cobra pedals.

## Overview

Released in April 2008 through Roadrunner Records, "Inflikted" is one of the most emotionally loaded debut albums in metal history — because it was not really a debut at all. It was a homecoming. When Max Cavalera left Sepultura in early 1997 after a decade as its frontman, the split fractured one of metal's most beloved brother partnerships. For ten years, fans wondered if Max and Igor Cavalera would ever make music together again. "Inflikted" answered that question with a detonation of thrash and death metal aggression that debuted at number 37 on the US Billboard 200.

For Igor Cavalera, the reunion with Max was personal before it was musical. After Max's departure, Igor remained in Sepultura for another decade, recording four more studio albums. He finally left in 2006, closing the chapter on a band he had co-founded at age fourteen. Two years later, Cavalera Conspiracy was born — not as a nostalgia act or cash-in on the brothers' name, but as a genuine creative partnership reinvigorated by years apart.

The music on "Inflikted" sounds exactly like what it is: two brothers who grew up playing metal together, stripped of the institutional compromises that inevitably accumulate in a long-running band. There are no tribal experiments here, no industrial tangents, no attempts to evolve into a different kind of band. Cavalera Conspiracy on "Inflikted" plays aggressive, forward-driving thrash and death metal with the confidence of people who invented a version of this genre. Igor's drumming matches that confidence — hard-hitting, groove-anchored, technically precise without being clinical.

This article breaks down the drum gear Igor Cavalera used to record "Inflikted," the Tama Starclassic kit that powered the brothers' return, and what the recording reveals about Igor's playing at this point in his career.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Tama Tama Starclassic Maple (Dark stain / black wrap finish)
- **Snare:** Tama Tama Steel Snare, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Paiste — Paiste RUDE Series
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Tama Speed Cobra HP910LSW; Tama Iron Cobra Hi-Hat Stand HH905N; Roc-N-Soc Nitro; Promark 2B Wood Tip
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated (batter), Remo Ambassador Snare Side (resonant)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high tension — bright crack with sufficient body for metal mix

### Igor's 2008 Tama Starclassic: Power Rebuilt

For Cavalera Conspiracy's debut album, Igor Cavalera moved to a Tama Starclassic Maple kit — the flagship of Tama's acoustic drum line and one of the most respected professional kits in heavy music. The Starclassic's maple shells provide a full-bodied warmth combined with punchy attack, making it equally suitable for mid-paced groove sections and flat-out thrash.

The choice of Tama after years on Pearl equipment represented a natural evolution for Igor. The Starclassic's Star-Cast mounting system allows drums to resonate freely without the shell dampening caused by traditional lug placement. On "Inflikted," the toms sound open and responsive — they cut through the dense guitar mix without sounding choked or compressed.

The single 22" bass drum was a deliberate choice consistent with Igor's groove-first philosophy. Double bass setups dominate contemporary thrash and death metal, but Igor's signature has always been exceptional single-pedal technique with strategic double-bass deployment. His kick patterns on "Inflikted" are powerful precisely because they leave space — every hit counts.

With two rack toms and two floor toms, Igor had the fill vocabulary needed for the album's dynamic range. The "Inflikted" title track and "Hunt You Down" showcase his tom work — not flashy cascades, but purposeful accents that serve the riff and drive the song forward.

The Tama Starclassic is still in production today, making this an accessible reference point for drummers looking to replicate Igor's Cavalera Conspiracy-era sound. The maple construction remains the defining character of the kit.

### Tama Steel: The Crack of the Conspiracy

Igor's snare on "Inflikted" is a 14" x 6.5" Tama steel snare — a workhorse choice that prioritizes cutting power and projection over tonal complexity. Steel shells deliver the bright, focused crack that penetrates dense guitar mixes without requiring aggressive EQ or heavy compression.

For Cavalera Conspiracy's aggressive thrash and death metal direction on "Inflikted," steel was the right call. The snare sound on the album is tight and authoritative — every backbeat lands like a statement. Logan Mader's production gave the snare space in the mix, letting Igor's natural attack speak rather than papering it over with heavy processing.

The 6.5" depth provides enough body so the snare doesn't sound thin or brittle, while remaining bright enough to cut at the velocities Igor plays. Tuned medium-high for the sessions, the drum responds quickly to Igor's broad strokes and delivers maximum impact on ghost notes and rimshots alike.

This snare choice reflects Igor's overall approach on the album: direct, powerful, and unambiguous. There is no subtlety needed when you are recording the reunion album that two generations of metal fans have been waiting for since 1997.

### Paiste RUDE: Uncompromising Volume and Character

Igor Cavalera has been a Paiste artist for the bulk of his career, and the Paiste RUDE series has been his primary cymbal choice since the Roots era of the mid-1990s. For "Inflikted," he returned to the full RUDE setup — a range known for its aggressive character, raw surface texture, and ability to project at extreme volumes without becoming shrill.

The RUDE series was designed for heavy playing, and it shows. Unlike thinner, more delicate cymbal lines that reward a lighter touch, the RUDE cymbals are built to be punished. Igor's hard-hitting style, developed over two decades of metal drumming, works naturally with their mass and construction. The cymbals project without forcing the drummer to baby them.

For "Inflikted," the 14" RUDE hi-hats provide the cutting definition required for Igor's groove patterns. The heavier weight of these hi-hats gives each chick and open note a physical presence in the mix. The multiple crash options — 16", 18", and 19" — give Igor choices for different sections, from quick accent crashes to full explosive washes.

The China cymbal is a Cavalera Conspiracy signature sound. The trashy, cutting explosions of a RUDE China punctuate the album's heaviest riff transitions, adding aggression that a standard crash cannot deliver.

Returning to these cymbals after years of recording was part of Igor's deliberate reset with Cavalera Conspiracy — familiar tools in service of a familiar-but-reinvented direction.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What band did Max and Igor Cavalera form together after Sepultura?**

A: Max and Igor Cavalera formed Cavalera Conspiracy in 2007, releasing their debut album Inflikted in 2008 on Roadrunner Records. Max had left Sepultura in early 1997 following a dispute over management, and Igor left in 2006 after a decade of continuing the band without his brother. Cavalera Conspiracy reunited the founding Cavalera brothers in a project focused on aggressive thrash and death metal, stepping away from both Sepultura's later industrial direction and Max's world-music-influenced Soulfly output.

**Q: What drum kit did Igor Cavalera use on Inflikted?**

A: On Cavalera Conspiracy's Inflikted recorded in 2007 and 2008, Igor Cavalera played a Tama Starclassic Maple kit. The setup featured a single 22x18 inch bass drum, rack toms in 10 and 12 inches, and floor toms in 14 and 16 inches. The Tama Starclassic's maple shells provided the full-bodied warmth and punchy attack suited to the album's aggressive thrash and death metal direction, and the Star-Cast mounting system allowed free shell resonance throughout the recording.

**Q: What cymbals did Igor Cavalera use on Inflikted?**

A: On Inflikted in 2008, Igor Cavalera used the Paiste RUDE series — the same cymbal line he had used throughout much of the Sepultura era, particularly from Roots onward. His Cavalera Conspiracy setup included Paiste RUDE 14 inch hi-hats, RUDE 16 and 18 inch crashes, a RUDE 19 inch crash, a RUDE 20 inch ride, and a RUDE 18 inch China. The RUDE series is designed for high-volume, aggressive playing, matching Igor's hard-hitting approach on the album.

**Q: How does Igor's drumming on Inflikted compare to his Sepultura work?**

A: Igor Cavalera's drumming on Inflikted is a deliberate return to the aggressive thrash and death metal approach of early Sepultura — particularly the Arise era — rather than the groove-metal and tribal fusion of Chaos A.D. and Roots. With Cavalera Conspiracy, Igor stripped back the tribal elements and world music influences that defined Sepultura's 1993 to 1996 period, focusing on power, precision, and forward-driving groove. The result is Igor sounding like a veteran who has internalized every phase of his development and chosen to emphasize the most direct and heavy elements.

**Q: When did the Cavalera brothers reunite, and why did they form Cavalera Conspiracy?**

A: The Cavalera brothers — Max and Igor — reunited musically in 2007, recording Inflikted together for release in 2008. Max had departed Sepultura in January 1997 following a management dispute involving his wife Gloria, who had been acting as the band's manager. Igor remained in Sepultura for nearly a decade before leaving in 2006. The brothers eventually reconciled privately before deciding to record together again. Cavalera Conspiracy was named after the two brothers rather than any particular geographic or genre allegiance, signaling that the band's identity was rooted in their shared creative bond.
