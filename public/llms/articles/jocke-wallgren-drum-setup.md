## Overview

Jocke Wallgren (born September 19, 1981, in Sundbyberg, Sweden) has been the drummer for Amon Amarth since 2015, joining the Swedish melodic death metal titans just before the recording of Jomsviking (2016). He replaced Fredrik Andersson and quickly became an essential part of the band's Viking-themed epic metal assault.

What Wallgren brings to Amon Amarth is a combination of crushing power, stamina, and melodic awareness that suits the band's cinematic approach to metal. The band's music demands massive kick patterns that underpin melodic guitar harmonies and sweeping arrangements — a context where raw aggression without musicality would flatten everything. Wallgren navigates this balance with impressive control.

His setup centers on the Tama Starclassic Performer Birch/Bubinga — a shell choice that emphasizes low-end punch and sustain, ideal for the grand, epic tone that Amon Amarth cultivates. Meinl Byzance cymbals provide a warm, organic character that avoids the brittle brightness that would clash with the band's orchestral production style. DW 9002 pedals provide the reliable dual-chain double-kick mechanism required to sustain the rhythmic foundation across two-hour sets.

Amon Amarth's live production is among the most elaborate in extreme metal — pyro, longships, and theatrical staging that demands a drummer who can hold the anchor while the spectacle unfolds around him. Wallgren handles this with the poise of a seasoned professional.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Jocke Wallgren use with Amon Amarth?**
A: Jocke Wallgren plays a Tama Starclassic Performer Birch/Bubinga kit with Amon Amarth. The birch/bubinga shell combination provides the warm, low-end-forward tone ideal for Amon Amarth's epic, orchestral metal production style. He runs a double 22" bass drum configuration for the sustained melodic death metal patterns the band requires.

**Q: What cymbals does Jocke Wallgren use?**
A: Jocke Wallgren plays Meinl Byzance cymbals. The Byzance series' warm, B20 alloy character complements Amon Amarth's orchestral production style without the harsh brightness that clashes with violin and choir arrangements.

**Q: What double bass pedal does Jocke Wallgren use?**
A: Jocke Wallgren uses DW 9002 double bass drum pedals. The 9002 features dual-chain drive providing reliable power transfer over extended sets — an important consideration for Amon Amarth's lengthy, high-intensity live performances.

**Q: When did Jocke Wallgren join Amon Amarth?**
A: Jocke Wallgren joined Amon Amarth in 2015, replacing founding drummer Fredrik Andersson. His first album with the band was Jomsviking (2016), Amon Amarth's first concept album. He has since recorded Berserker (2019) and The Great Heathen Army (2022) with the band.
