> Five years after Joey Jordison's death on July 26, 2021, Slipknot's 2026 album and world tour carry tributes to their founding drummer. A look at the gear — Pearl snare, Paiste RUDE cymbals, ProMark sticks — and the sound that still defines metal drumming.

## Overview

July 26, 2026 marks five years since Joey Jordison died at age 46. As Slipknot's founding drummer from 1995 to 2013, he built the rhythmic architecture — the blast beats, the rotating drum riser, the sheer physical extremity — that defined a generation of metal drumming, on a kit and cymbal setup that drummers still study and buy today.

The anniversary carries extra weight in 2026. Slipknot's new album and world tour both fold in tributes to their former drummer: percussionist Chris Fehn and turntablist Sid Wilson have separately spoken about Jordison's continuing influence on the band, memories that resurfaced as the touring cycle brought Slipknot back to the songs he helped write (Loudersound, 2026; sources below).

This tribute takes MetalForge's own angle: not another oral history, but the gear. Every piece of Jordison's rig — snare, sticks, cymbals — is still documented, still linked, and in most cases still in production. Five years on, you can still buy the sound.

### The Kit Behind the Riser

Jordison's Pearl kits were built to survive the rotating, invert-capable drum riser he made famous — double 22" bass drums, four rack toms, and 20-ply Reference shells chosen for the attack and low end needed to cut through Slipknot's nine-piece wall of sound. The full era-by-era breakdown, including the Iowa-era Masterworks purple/black fade, lives on his drummer profile.

### The Signature Snare, Still in Production

The Pearl JJ1365 — a 13" steel-shell snare, smaller and higher-pitched than a standard 14" — was the snare Jordison actually played live throughout his Slipknot years, chosen for the tight, cutting crack needed to punch through layered guitars and samples. It remains in production today, one of the most direct ways for a drummer to plug into his sound.

### Paiste RUDE & 2002: Cymbals Built to Survive Him

Jordison's cymbal setup ran on Paiste's RUDE and 2002 series — 14" Wild Hi-Hats, four Power Crashes from 16" to 19", twin Wild Chinas (20" and 22"), and a 22" Power Ride. The RUDE line's raw, unlathed bronze was built to survive exactly the kind of punishment his playing delivered night after night, and the lineup is largely unchanged today.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: When did Joey Jordison die?**

A: Joey Jordison died on July 26, 2021, at age 46.

**Q: What was Joey Jordison's illness?**

A: In 2016, Jordison disclosed that he had been diagnosed with acute transverse myelitis, an inflammatory condition affecting the spinal cord that temporarily left him unable to walk. He had been living with it privately for years before his December 2013 departure from Slipknot.

**Q: What drums and sticks did Joey Jordison use?**

A: Jordison played a Pearl Reference Series / Masterworks kit with his signature Pearl Joey Jordison 13"x6.5" snare, Paiste RUDE & 2002 series cymbals, and ProMark TX515W signature sticks. The full breakdown of his kit, snare, cymbal setup, and sticks — including where each is still sold today — is linked throughout this article.

**Q: Is Slipknot honoring Joey Jordison in 2026?**

A: Yes. Slipknot's 2026 album cycle and world tour both include tributes to their founding drummer, with bandmates Chris Fehn and Sid Wilson separately speaking about his lasting influence on the band.

**Q: How long was Joey Jordison in Slipknot?**

A: Jordison was Slipknot's drummer from 1995, when the band formed, until December 2013 — eighteen years, across four studio albums.
