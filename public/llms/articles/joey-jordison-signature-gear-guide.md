## Overview

Joey Jordison (April 26, 1975 – July 26, 2021) left behind one of metal's most distinctive drum setups. His Pearl Masterworks kit, Paiste RUDE cymbals, and signature snare defined the sound of Slipknot's Iowa era and influenced a generation of extreme metal drummers. Since his passing, interest in his signature items has only grown — both from players who want to capture his sound and from collectors seeking a piece of metal history.

This guide covers exactly what's still available from Joey's rig, what has been discontinued, and where to find everything from signature production pieces to vintage Iowa-era gear.

## Signature Items: In Production vs Discontinued

### Pearl Joey Jordison Signature Snare (JJ1365) — IN PRODUCTION
- **Size:** 13" x 6.5" steel shell, 1.0mm beaded construction
- **Price:** $250–350 new
- **Availability:** Pearl authorized dealers, Guitar Center, Sweetwater, Musician's Friend
- **Notes:** Same design Joey played throughout his Slipknot career. Never discontinued. Most popular and accessible Joey Jordison signature item.

### Promark TX515W Joey Jordison Signature Sticks — IN PRODUCTION
- **Price:** $12–15 per pair
- **Availability:** All major music retailers
- **Notes:** Longer than a standard 5B for reach across his massive kit; wood tip for cymbal articulation at speed.

### Paiste RUDE Series Cymbals — IN PRODUCTION
- **Models (Iowa setup):** 14" Wild Hi-Hats, 16"–19" Power Crashes, 20" + 22" Wild Chinas, 22" Power Ride
- **Price:** $150–400 per cymbal; $2,000–3,000 for full Iowa configuration
- **Availability:** Paiste authorized dealers
- **Notes:** Same exact models Joey used. Unlathed bells, raw finish — designed for aggressive heavy-metal playing.

### Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal — IN PRODUCTION (Updated)
- **Price:** $500–650 new (current model); $150–300 used (Iowa-era units)
- **Availability:** Pearl dealers new; Reverb.com for Iowa-era vintage
- **Notes:** Current model updated since Iowa era but maintains direct-drive concept.

## Collector's Corner: Iowa-Era Authentic Gear

**Pearl Masterworks (Iowa Era, 2001–2004)**
Custom purple/black fade finish, maple/African mahogany hybrid shells. Identifiable by pre-2005 Pearl Masterworks badging. Prices: $3,000–6,000 complete configuration. Source: Reverb.com, eBay (verify serial numbers).

**Pearl JJ1365 Snare — Original vs Current**
The JJ1365 design has not changed significantly since the early 2000s. Do not pay a premium for "vintage" JJ1365 snares without documented tour provenance — the current production model is essentially identical.

**What to Watch For**
- Pearl Masterworks Custom shells, maple/mahogany, 2000–2008 vintage
- Pearl Icon Rack System (Iowa mounting system)
- Paiste RUDE cymbals with early 2000s production markings
- Pearl Demon Drive pedals, Iowa-era (2000–2005)

**Red Flags**
- "Original Joey Jordison" claims without documentation
- Post-2010 Pearl Masterworks badging sold as "Iowa era"
- Counterfeit signature sticks — buy from established retailers only

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Joey Jordison's Pearl signature snare still available?**
Yes. The Pearl JJ1365 remains in production as of 2026. It's a 13" x 6.5" steel shell — the same design Joey played throughout his Slipknot career. Street price $250–350 at Pearl authorized dealers. See [Iowa drum setup](/articles/iowa-drum-setup) for studio context.

**What drum kit did Joey Jordison use for the Iowa tour?**
A Pearl Masterworks Custom in a custom purple/black fade finish with six toms (8", 10", 12", 14" rack toms, 16" and 18" floor toms) and double 22" x 18" bass drums on a Pearl Icon Rack System. Cymbals: Paiste RUDE and 2002 series. Full breakdown at [Iowa drum setup](/articles/iowa-drum-setup). Career timeline at [Joey Jordison drummer profile](/drummer/joey-jordison).

**How do I build a setup that sounds like Joey Jordison?**
Start with three signature items: Pearl JJ1365 snare ($280), Promark TX515W sticks ($13/pair), and at least one Paiste RUDE Wild China ($250–320). All still in production. Add Paiste RUDE Wild Hi-Hats and Power Crashes. For the kit: any Pearl maple-shell drum works; a used Reference or Masterworks is closest to Iowa. Full guide at [Joey Jordison complete gear breakdown](/articles/whats-in-joey-jordisons-kit).

**What happened to Joey Jordison signature items after he passed away?**
Pearl and Promark have continued producing their respective signature items since Joey's passing on July 26, 2021. The JJ1365 and TX515W remain active catalog items. Paiste RUDE cymbals (not signature-branded but the exact models Joey used) remain in full production. No discontinuation announced.

**Where can I find Joey Jordison Pearl Masterworks drums for sale?**
Reverb.com — search "Pearl Masterworks joey jordison" or "Pearl Masterworks custom purple black". eBay as secondary source. Vintage drum specialists (Vintage Drum Center, Classic Drum Shop) occasionally source authenticated Iowa-era Pearl pieces. Budget alternative: used Pearl Reference Series at $2,000–4,000.
