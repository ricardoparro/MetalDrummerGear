## Overview

John Otto is the founding drummer of Limp Bizkit and one of the most recognizable groove drummers of the nu-metal era. Born in Jacksonville, Florida, alongside bandmates Fred Durst, Wes Borland, Sam Rivers, and DJ Lethal, Otto was there from the band's earliest days in the mid-1990s and remained the rhythmic anchor throughout Limp Bizkit's commercial peak.

Limp Bizkit's music made demands unlike most metal: Otto needed to lock with DJ Lethal's turntable work and Fred Durst's rap cadences as much as Wes Borland's guitar. This created a distinctive drumming context where fat, fat groove — not pure power or speed — was the primary requirement. Otto delivered this with authority.

His drumming on Three Dollar Bill, Y'all (1997), Significant Other (1999), and Chocolate Starfish and the Hot Dog Flavored Water (2000) helped make Limp Bizkit one of the best-selling acts of the late 1990s. "Break Stuff," "Nookie," and "My Generation" showcase an approach where the kick drum and snare create a hip-hop-informed foundation that met Borland's guitar aggression head-on.

Otto plays OCDP (Orange County Drum and Percussion) custom drums — a brand associated with aggressive, heavy-duty build quality — complemented by Zildjian cymbals and Gibraltar hardware.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does John Otto use?**
A: John Otto plays OCDP (Orange County Drum and Percussion) custom drums. OCDP is a California-based custom drum brand known for heavy-duty construction. His configuration centers on maple shells in a conventional tom spread, optimized for the fat, punchy groove that defines Limp Bizkit's rhythmic foundation.

**Q: What cymbals does John Otto play?**
A: John Otto plays Zildjian cymbals — primarily the Zildjian A series. His setup includes standard configuration hi-hats, crash cymbals in the 16"–18" range, a 20" ride, and a China cymbal. The A series' versatile character suits Limp Bizkit's crossing of metal and hip-hop influences.

**Q: What is John Otto's drumming style?**
A: John Otto's drumming style is heavily groove-oriented, drawing equally from hip-hop and heavy metal. He prioritizes pocket, fat kick-and-snare grooves that lock with DJ Lethal's turntable work and Fred Durst's rap patterns. This approach distinguished Limp Bizkit from purely metal bands and was central to the band's commercial crossover success.

**Q: What drumheads does John Otto use?**
A: John Otto uses Remo drumheads across his OCDP custom kit — coated Emperors on toms for extra attack and warmth, and Powerstroke 3 or Felt Tone on the bass drums for controlled thud without excessive ring.
