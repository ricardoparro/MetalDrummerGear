## Overview

Released on June 26, 2012, *L'Enfant Sauvage* is the album where Gojira stopped being a cult metal band and became a mainstream force. Signed to Roadrunner Records, the Duplantier brothers brought their progressive death metal vision to a global audience — and a Grammy nomination followed.

At the drum kit, Mario Duplantier made a decisive gear shift. After years as a Tama and Zildjian player, he switched to DW Collector's Series drums and Meinl Byzance Brilliant cymbals. The DW maple shells gave him a warmer, more resonant low end. The Meinl Byzance Brilliant cymbals added shimmering clarity to the aggressive playing that Gojira demands.

The album was recorded at Silvercord Studio in New York — a significant shift from the band's French recording history. The title track earned a nomination for Best Hard Rock/Metal Performance at the 55th Grammy Awards in 2013. Guitar World put the band on their cover. It was Gojira's mainstream breakthrough moment.

Mario's drumming on this record builds directly on the foundation of *From Mars to Sirius* (2005) and *The Way of All Flesh* (2008), but the DW/Meinl combination adds dimension that his Tama/Zildjian setup lacked.

### DW Collector's Series: The New Foundation

For *L'Enfant Sauvage*, Mario Duplantier switched from Tama to DW Collector's Series drums — and the difference is audible from the first notes of the title track. DW's North American maple shells are warmer and more resonant than the birch/bubinga combination of *From Mars to Sirius* and the bubinga Starclassics of *The Way of All Flesh*. The result is a drum sound with more natural sustain and a richer fundamental tone.

Mario retained his hallmark setup: dual 22" x 18" bass drums rather than a single kick with double pedal. This visual signature is as much part of Gojira's identity as Joe Duplantier's angular guitar tone. The 18" depth (deeper than standard 16") provides the subsonic rumble that anchors Gojira's heaviest passages.

The Natural Satin Oil finish strips away lacquer for a more open, resonant tone — aligning with Gojira's organic, unprocessed aesthetic.

### Meinl Byzance Brilliant: A New Sonic Identity

The switch from Zildjian to Meinl Byzance Brilliant cymbals is the most sonically distinctive change Mario made for this album. Zildjian's K Custom and K series (used on previous Gojira records) favor darkness and complex overtones. Meinl Byzance Brilliant cymbals are brighter, clearer, with a more defined attack and shimmering sustain.

Setup included:
- **Hi-hats:** Meinl Byzance Brilliant 14"
- **Crashes:** Meinl Byzance Brilliant 17", 19", 20"
- **Ride:** Meinl Byzance Brilliant 22"
- **China:** Meinl Byzance Brilliant 18"

The Brilliant finish — a buffed, polished surface treatment — enhances natural brightness. This clarity suited Silvercord Studio's more refined, modern recording environment.

### DW 9000 Pedals

Mario uses two DW 9000 single pedals, one for each bass drum. The ball-bearing mechanism and adjustable cam provide immediate, consistent response — critical for Gojira's syncopated kick patterns that require exact timing. This was a departure from the Tama Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide pedals he used on previous albums.

### Recording at Silvercord Studio

Silvercord Studio, New York provided superior acoustic flexibility compared to previous Gojira recording locations (a converted swimming pool in Bayonne, France for *From Mars to Sirius* and Studio Château de Beaulieu for *The Way of All Flesh*). Joe Duplantier handled engineering and production, maintaining Gojira's self-produced philosophy on their major-label debut.

The drum sound on *L'Enfant Sauvage* is drier and tighter than previous albums — less natural reverb, more controlled. Mario tuned the DW kit slightly higher than the dark tones of *The Way of All Flesh*, producing a more singing, defined tone that the Byzance Brilliant cymbals complement.

## Grammy Nomination Context

The title track "L'Enfant Sauvage" was nominated for Best Hard Rock/Metal Performance at the 55th Grammy Awards in February 2013. This was Gojira's first Grammy nomination — a major milestone that reflected the album's mainstream breakthrough. The nomination validated years of underground critical acclaim and confirmed Mario Duplantier's status as one of the most important drummers in contemporary metal.

## Key Tracks

- **"L'Enfant Sauvage"** — Title track and Grammy-nominated single. 180 BPM main groove driven by DW kick precision and Byzance Brilliant crash accents.
- **"Explosia"** — Album opener with iconic tribal tom intro. DW floor toms sing with warmth in the melodic intro pattern.
- **"The Axe"** — Showcases Mario's syncopated kick technique with Byzance Brilliant hi-hat nuance.
- **"Planned Obsolescence"** — Atmospheric restraint demonstrating Mario's compositional discipline.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Mario Duplantier use on L'Enfant Sauvage?**
A: Mario Duplantier used a DW Collector's Series maple drum kit on L'Enfant Sauvage (2012). This was a significant switch from his previous Tama Starclassic Bubinga setup. The DW Collector's Series features North American maple shells in a Natural Satin Oil finish, with dual 22" x 18" bass drums.

**Q: When did Mario Duplantier switch to Meinl cymbals?**
A: Mario Duplantier switched from Zildjian to Meinl Byzance Brilliant cymbals around the time of L'Enfant Sauvage (2012). He had used Zildjian K Custom and A Custom cymbals on From Mars to Sirius (2005) and The Way of All Flesh (2008). The Byzance Brilliant series gave him a brighter, more defined sound suited to the cleaner production of this era.

**Q: Was L'Enfant Sauvage nominated for a Grammy?**
A: Yes. The title track was nominated for Best Hard Rock/Metal Performance at the 55th Grammy Awards in 2013. This was Gojira's first Grammy nomination and reflected the album's mainstream breakthrough on Roadrunner Records.

**Q: What pedals does Mario Duplantier use for L'Enfant Sauvage?**
A: Mario used DW 9000 Series single pedals — two of them, one for each bass drum. Earlier on From Mars to Sirius and The Way of All Flesh he used Tama Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide single pedals. The switch to DW 9000 pedals accompanied his broader move to DW hardware for this era.

**Q: Where was L'Enfant Sauvage recorded?**
A: L'Enfant Sauvage was recorded at Silvercord Studio in New York, NY. This marked a departure from Gojira's previous recording locations in France. The album was self-produced by Joe Duplantier.
