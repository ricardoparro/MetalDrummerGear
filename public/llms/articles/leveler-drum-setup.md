## Overview

Released on June 28, 2011 on Metal Blade Records, August Burns Red's fourth studio album *Leveler* represents the peak of the band's Grammy-nominated run and a landmark in progressive metalcore. At the center of that performance was Matt Greiner — the Lancaster, Pennsylvania native who has been the rhythmic foundation of August Burns Red since the band's formation in 2003.

The *Leveler* era captures Greiner at a pivotal moment in his career: still on the Ludwig Classic Maple kit that defined his early ABR sound, but pushing into genuinely progressive territory with odd-meter patterns, polyrhythmic layering, and a lever-driven double bass technique that set new precision standards for metalcore drumming.

The Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance that followed the album's release confirmed what the genre already knew: Greiner was one of the most musically sophisticated drummers working in heavy music — combining technical command with the jazz-influenced dynamic range that distinguishes his playing from drummers who prioritize aggression above all else.

## Technique Notes

**Lever-Driven Double Bass:** Greiner's double bass technique initiates each stroke mechanically at the lever point — generating clean separation between hits even at high velocity. The DW 9000's chain-drive mechanism complements this approach by delivering consistent resistance at each stroke initiation point.

**Odd-Meter Fluency:** Throughout Leveler, Greiner navigates between 4/4, 5/4, 7/8, and polyrhythmic patterns without losing the groove feel that distinguishes his playing from purely technical metalcore drumming. He treats complex time signatures as vocabulary rather than technical demonstrations.

**Dynamic Range:** Where many metalcore records operate at near-constant intensity, Leveler contains moments of genuine dynamic contrast — ghost notes, controlled fills, and space — all enabled by the Ludwig Classic Maple's natural acoustic responsiveness.

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Matt Greiner use on Leveler?**
A: Matt Greiner recorded August Burns Red's *Leveler* (2011) on a Ludwig Classic Maple kit with double 22" bass drums and a DW 9000 double pedal. The Classic Maple's 6-ply maple shells produced a warm, musical tone contrasting with the more synthetic drum sounds common in 2011 metalcore production. He has since transitioned to Pearl Reference Pure drums for his current setup.

**Q: What is Matt Greiner known for technically?**
A: Matt Greiner is best known for his lever-driven double bass technique — a precise mechanical approach generating clean, separated strokes at high velocity. He is also recognized for odd-meter fluency (treating 5/4 and 7/8 patterns as natural vocabulary), and jazz-influenced dynamics including ghost notes and cymbal orchestration that give his playing musical depth beyond aggression alone.

**Q: What makes the drumming on Leveler unique?**
A: The drumming on *Leveler* stands out for three combined qualities: progressive metric vocabulary that feels natural rather than forced; genuine dynamic range in a genre that often sacrifices dynamics for intensity; and intricate cymbal orchestration using the Paiste 2002's harmonic richness as a musical layer. The Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance recognized exactly this combination.

**Q: What cymbals did Matt Greiner use on Leveler?**
A: Greiner used the Paiste 2002 series for Leveler — 14" Sound Edge hi-hats, 16" and 18" crashes, a 20" ride, and an 18" China. The 2002's CuSn8 alloy (nickel-silver) produces fuller, more harmonically complex sound than B20 bronze, matching Greiner's approach of treating cymbals as melodic components rather than pure accent tools. He has since switched to Meinl Byzance.

**Q: What double bass pedal did Matt Greiner use on Leveler?**
A: Matt Greiner used the DW 9000 Double Bass Drum Pedal for the Leveler sessions. The 9000's chain-drive mechanism provided the consistent, precise feel his lever-driven double bass technique requires. He later transitioned to the Pearl Demon Drive (direct-drive) for his current setup with August Burns Red.
