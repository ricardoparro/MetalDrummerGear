# Mastodon Leviathan Drum Setup: Brann Dailor's 2004 Gear Breakdown

**Band / Album:** Mastodon — *Leviathan* (2004)
**Genre:** Progressive Sludge Metal

## Overview

Released on September 7, 2004 on Relapse Records, Mastodon's *Leviathan* is the album that transformed the Atlanta quartet from an underground metal cult act into one of their generation's most important bands. A full-length concept record based on Herman Melville's *Moby-Dick*, *Leviathan* matched the novel's oceanic ambition with music of extraordinary density and sophistication — tracks that move from sludge-metal brutality to progressive complexity within the same song, bound together by a narrative ambition that no metal band of the period was attempting at the same scale.

At the centre of *Leviathan*'s sound is [Brann Dailor](/drummer/brann-dailor)'s drumming: relentless, melodic, and compositionally integrated in ways that redefined what a drummer's role could be in extreme metal. Where most sludge and heavy metal drummers anchor the song from beneath, Dailor weaves through Mastodon's arrangements as an equal voice — his fills constructing melodic phrases, his polyrhythmic patterns creating cross-rhythms against the guitars that generate the music's signature tension and release.

*Leviathan* earned a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance for "Blood and Thunder" — the first major industry recognition for Mastodon and confirmation that Dailor's approach to the instrument was registering beyond underground metal circles.

The album was produced by Matt Bayles at Rancho de la Luna in Joshua Tree, California, and Doppler Studios in Atlanta. The drum sound on *Leviathan* is dense and powerful, the production placing Dailor's performance in the context of a wall-of-sound recording that suits the album's oceanic concept.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series (Natural maple)
- **Snare:** DW Craviotto Solid Shell, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian — A Series
- **Hardware / Pedals:** DW 9000 Double Pedal; DW 9000 Series Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Nitro Throne; Vic Firth 5A American Classic
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD Clear kick (front: Evans Resonant Bass); Evans UV1 Coated toms; Remo Ambassador Coated snare
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high tension for crack and projection through dense guitar mix

### The DW Collector's Series: Foundation of the Leviathan Sound

For the *Leviathan* sessions, Brann Dailor anchored his setup around a DW Collector's Series drum kit — the same premium maple shell line he would carry through the [Blood Mountain](/articles/blood-mountain-drum-setup) and [Crack the Skye](/articles/crack-the-skye-drum-setup) eras. DW's Collector's Series is built at their Oxnard, California facility to specifications that produce consistent, musical pitches from shell to shell.

The maple shell construction is the key to what makes *Leviathan*'s drum sound work within the album's dense arrangements. Mastodon's guitar tunings on this record are extremely low — drop tuning that produces a thick, saturated low-mid frequency mass. In that sonic environment, drums that produced noise rather than pitch would disappear into the guitar texture. Dailor's DW maple shells cut through precisely because they produce real pitches: the toms register as notes, not thuds, and the fills cascade through harmonic intervals that the ear can follow even through the guitar density.

The three-rack-tom configuration (10", 12", 13") provides the dense melodic resolution that Dailor's fills require. Three adjacent pitches in close intervals give him the ability to construct melodic phrases without the large intervallic jumps that would make the fills sound like random percussive punctuation. Combined with the 16" floor tom as a fourth voice at a lower register, he has a four-note melodic palette that spans enough range to build real melodic content.

### The Snare: Crack and Power for Blood and Thunder

The DW Craviotto Solid Shell snare at 14" x 6.5" delivers crack and body — solid maple construction for warmth and fundamental tone, 6.5" depth for projection, and a bearing edge geometry that gives the drum fast response for intricate work.

On "Blood and Thunder," the snare's aggressive crack punctuates the main groove with authority. On the progressive sections of "Hearts Alive," the same drum shows its sensitivity: Dailor uses it for ghost notes and subtle dynamic shadings that would disappear on a less responsive drum.

### Zildjian: The Cutting Voice of Leviathan

Brann Dailor's Zildjian A Series setup on *Leviathan* reflects the sonic demands of the album's production: dense, aggressive, and built to cut through the walls of down-tuned guitars. The Zildjian A Series' brightness and attack are the right cymbal palette for this environment.

The 14" New Beat hi-hats give Dailor's intricate hi-hat patterns a presence in the mix that heavier, darker cymbals would not provide at *Leviathan*'s densities. The crash selections are fast and articulate — responding quickly and decaying cleanly, suiting Mastodon's tendency to accent transitions with hard, short crashes. The 20" A Medium Ride provides a defined bell for the album's more sustained grooves, particularly in the longer progressive passages of "Hearts Alive" and "Aqua Dementia."

This Zildjian setup represents Dailor's pre-Meinl era — the cymbal palette that defined *Leviathan* and [Blood Mountain](/articles/blood-mountain-drum-setup) before his transition to the darker Meinl Byzance tones of [Crack the Skye](/articles/crack-the-skye-drum-setup).

## FAQ

**Q: What drums does Brann Dailor use on Leviathan?**
A: Brann Dailor recorded Mastodon's Leviathan (2004) using a DW Collector's Series drum kit with maple shells. The configuration featured a double bass drum setup with two 22" kick drums, three rack toms (10", 12", and 13"), and a 16" floor tom. The maple shells provide the warm, resonant tone that allows Dailor's fill sequences to function as melodic phrases.

**Q: What snare does Brann Dailor use on Leviathan?**
A: On Leviathan (2004), Brann Dailor used a DW Craviotto Solid Shell snare in 14" x 6.5". The solid maple construction gives exceptional resonance and a pure fundamental note, with the 6.5" depth providing body and projection essential for cutting through Mastodon's dense guitar arrangements.

**Q: What cymbals does Brann Dailor use on Leviathan?**
A: On Leviathan (2004), Brann Dailor used Zildjian A Series cymbals including 14" New Beat hi-hats, a 16" Medium Thin Crash, an 18" Medium Crash, a 20" Medium Ride, and an 18" China Boy. The Zildjian A Series' bright, cutting character defined this era before his later transition to Meinl Byzance.

**Q: Was Leviathan Grammy-nominated?**
A: Yes. Mastodon's "Blood and Thunder" from Leviathan received a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance — Mastodon's first Grammy nomination and confirmation that Brann Dailor's melodic drumming approach was registering beyond underground metal circles.

**Q: Where was Leviathan recorded?**
A: Leviathan was recorded at Rancho de la Luna in Joshua Tree, California and Doppler Studios in Atlanta, Georgia, produced by Matt Bayles. Released September 7, 2004 on Relapse Records.
