## Overview

Martin Lopez (born February 8, 1979, in Lund, Sweden) is one of the most complete drummers ever to play in a heavy band. During his tenure with Opeth (2000–2006), Lopez helped shape the sound of progressive death metal's peak artistic period — a run that produced Blackwater Park (2001), Deliverance (2002), Damnation (2003), and Ghost Reveries (2005).

What distinguished Lopez from his contemporaries wasn't blast speed or technical showmanship — it was musicality. He brought a jazz drummer's sensitivity to a death metal context: listening, supporting, leaving space, varying texture, and treating the drum kit as an ensemble instrument rather than a battering ram. On Blackwater Park, his shuffles and jazz-influenced patterns turned songs like "The Drapery Falls" into profound emotional experiences.

After leaving Opeth in 2006 due to health issues, Lopez co-founded Soen, a progressive metal band where his approach found perhaps its purest expression — songs built around the interaction between complex rhythms and deeply melodic songwriting. His Sonor SQ2 custom kit, Zildjian cymbals, and DW 9002 pedals give him the tone and control his musical approach demands.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Martin Lopez use?**
A: Martin Lopez plays Sonor SQ2 Custom drums — Sonor's hand-built, fully customizable flagship line manufactured in Bad Berleburg, Germany. The SQ2 allows shell material, thickness, and finish to be specified per drum, giving Lopez the precise tonal control his musical approach demands.

**Q: What cymbals does Martin Lopez play?**
A: Martin Lopez plays Zildjian cymbals across A and K series. For hats and crashes he favors the A series' responsive, versatile character. His ride choice — the Zildjian K Custom Dry Ride — reflects his jazz background: the dry finish reduces overtones and provides the clear, dark stick definition necessary for intricate progressive patterns.

**Q: Why is Martin Lopez considered special as a drummer?**
A: Martin Lopez is notable for bringing jazz-ensemble sensitivity to a metal context. He prioritizes musical interaction over technical display, varying his dynamics, leaving silence where other drummers would fill, and making his choices serve the composition. His work on Opeth's Blackwater Park is studied by drummers and music students as an example of advanced listening in a heavy band.

**Q: What sticks does Martin Lopez use?**
A: Martin Lopez plays Vic Firth American Classic 5A sticks with a nylon tip. The 5A is a versatile, balanced stick appropriate for both heavy accents and sensitive brush-adjacent work, consistent with his range from death metal extremity to delicate progressive passages.

**Q: Why did Martin Lopez leave Opeth?**
A: Martin Lopez departed Opeth in 2006 due to health issues. He was replaced by Martin Axenrot, who has remained the band's drummer since. Lopez went on to co-found Soen, a band whose music is in some ways the fullest realization of his artistic vision.
