## Overview

Nick Augusto was the drummer for Trivium from 2010 to 2014, appearing on two studio albums — In Waves (2011) and Vengeance Falls (2013) — during a period that saw the band sharpen their commercial metal focus and push into more accessible sonic territory while retaining their technical credibility.

Augusto joined Trivium after the departure of Nick Bellone, slotting into a band whose music requires a precise combination of metalcore agility, classic metal power, and commercial polish. Trivium's dual-vocal approach, combining screaming and clean singing within single songs, demands a drummer who can match the dynamic range — from aggressive, blast-adjacent passages to melodic verses that support clean harmonies.

His setup centered on the Tama Starclassic Performer Birch/Bubinga — Tama's workhorse professional series — complemented by Meinl cymbals and Tama Iron Cobra double bass drum pedals. The Iron Cobra's chain-drive mechanism and PowerGlide cam provide the consistent, smooth response required for the precise double-kick patterns that run throughout Trivium's catalog.

While his tenure with Trivium was relatively brief, Augusto's drumming on In Waves and Vengeance Falls represents two of the band's most consistent and commercially successful records, and his contributions to Trivium's sound during those years remain well-regarded within the metal community.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Nick Augusto use with Trivium?**
A: Nick Augusto played Tama Starclassic Performer Birch/Bubinga drums during his time with Trivium. The Performer Birch/Bubinga's combination of focused, punchy attack from the birch shells and warm low-end from the bubinga reinforcement suited Trivium's dual-purpose sound: aggressive enough for metalcore passages, musical enough for melodic clean-vocal sections.

**Q: What cymbals did Nick Augusto play?**
A: Nick Augusto played Meinl cymbals throughout his tenure with Trivium. His setup included Meinl 14" hi-hats, crash cymbals ranging from 16" to 18", a 20" ride, and a China cymbal.

**Q: What double bass pedals did Nick Augusto use?**
A: Nick Augusto used Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide double bass pedals. The Iron Cobra is one of the most widely used professional double pedals in metal — its Power Glide cam provides a smooth, consistent acceleration curve from footboard motion to beater strike.

**Q: What albums did Nick Augusto record with Trivium?**
A: Nick Augusto recorded two studio albums with Trivium: In Waves (2011) and Vengeance Falls (2013). In Waves debuted at #17 on the Billboard 200. Vengeance Falls was produced by David Draiman of Disturbed. Augusto departed Trivium in 2014 and was replaced by Paul Wandtke, then Mat Madiro, before Alex Bent became the permanent drummer.
