## Overview

Nick Menza (July 23, 1964 – May 21, 2016) was the drummer for Megadeth from 1989 to 1998 and briefly in 2004, appearing on four of the band's most celebrated and commercially successful albums. His era with Megadeth coincided with the band's transition from pure underground thrash to a more accessible — yet still aggressive — form of heavy metal.

Menza's drumming on Rust in Peace (1990) remains definitive: technically demanding thrash drumming that locked with Dave Mustaine and Marty Friedman's complex, interlocking guitar arrangements. The title track, "Holy Wars...The Punishment Due," and "Tornado of Souls" showcase his ability to support guitar-driven compositions with precision and aggression without overwhelming the arrangements.

Countdown to Extinction (1992) and Youthanasia (1994) saw Menza adapting his approach to the band's commercial shift — more groove, more groove dynamics, but still hitting hard when the songs required. These albums sold millions of copies, introducing Megadeth to an enormous mainstream audience.

His primary kit for the Countdown to Extinction era was a Pearl Masters Custom maple — Menza configured it with a wide tom spread and Pearl Free-Floating snare. For Rust in Peace, he also used a Tama Artstar II birch kit. His Zildjian A cymbals and Tama Iron Cobra pedals were constants across both eras.

Menza passed away on May 21, 2016, while performing on stage with OHM, a Los Angeles-based jazz fusion band.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Nick Menza use with Megadeth?**
A: Nick Menza used two primary kits during his Megadeth tenure. For the Rust in Peace album and tour, he played a Tama Artstar II birch kit. For Countdown to Extinction, Youthanasia, and Cryptic Writings, he primarily used a Pearl Masters Custom maple kit. Both configurations featured a large tom spread and double bass drum setup.

**Q: What cymbals did Nick Menza use?**
A: Nick Menza was a Zildjian A series player throughout his Megadeth years. His setup included Zildjian A 14" New Beat Hi-Hats, 17" and 19" crash cymbals, a 20" Medium Ride, and a Zildjian A 18" China Boy High for the aggressive accent accents heard throughout the thrash catalog.

**Q: What double bass pedal did Nick Menza use?**
A: Nick Menza used Tama Iron Cobra double bass pedals throughout his Megadeth tenure. The Iron Cobra's Power Glide cam and durable chain-drive mechanism gave him the consistent feel required for Megadeth's demanding tempo-based thrash drumming.

**Q: How did Nick Menza approach Megadeth's thrash drumming?**
A: Nick Menza's approach to Megadeth's music was primarily supportive — he served Mustaine and Friedman's complex guitar arrangements by providing a locked, precise rhythmic foundation rather than competing with the guitarists. On tracks like "Tornado of Souls," his drumming creates forward momentum without overplaying in the guitar-driven sections.

**Q: When did Nick Menza pass away?**
A: Nick Menza passed away on May 21, 2016, while performing on stage with OHM at the Baked Potato club in Studio City, California. He was 51 years old. He died of a cardiac arrest during the performance.
