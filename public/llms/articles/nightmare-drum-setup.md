# Nightmare Drum Setup — Mike Portnoy's Tribute Performance for Avenged Sevenfold (2010)

> Complete breakdown of Mike Portnoy's drum gear on Avenged Sevenfold's Nightmare (2010). Tama Starclassic Maple kit, Paiste/Sabian hybrid cymbals — the story of how Dream Theater's drummer stepped in as tribute fill-in after The Rev's death.

**Genre:** Heavy Metal

## Overview

Released on July 27, 2010, *Nightmare* is the fifth studio album from Avenged Sevenfold and one of the most emotionally charged records in modern heavy metal history. It was recorded in the aftermath of a tragedy: founding drummer Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan died on December 28, 2009, at age 28. The Rev had already composed drum parts for most of the album before his death. The band, determined to complete the record as a tribute, reached out to Mike Portnoy — then Dream Theater's drummer and a close friend of The Rev — to record those parts in his honor.

Portnoy's role on *Nightmare* is unique in the MetalForge catalog and in modern metal history: a guest fill-in who was not auditioning for the position, not joining the band, and not trying to impose his own identity on the recording. His task was to learn The Rev's compositions as faithfully as possible and deliver them with the technical authority and emotional weight the tribute demanded. It is one of the most selfless performances in contemporary heavy metal drumming.

The result debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 — Avenged Sevenfold's first chart-topper — and went Multi-Platinum in the United States. The single "So Far Away," a ballad dedicated directly to The Rev, received a **Grammy nomination**. The album reached the UK Top 3 and became a landmark in A7X's commercial and artistic trajectory, serving as the bridge between The Rev era and the Arin Ilejay era that followed.

For the drum gear, Portnoy brought his 2010 touring configuration: a **Tama Starclassic Maple** kit and a **Paiste/Sabian hybrid cymbal rig** — equipment refined across years of high-demand Dream Theater touring. He was in peak physical and technical form, having just completed the *Black Clouds & Silver Linings* (2009) cycle. The Nightmare sessions put that form in service of someone else's music, which makes the performances all the more remarkable.

This article documents the gear Mike Portnoy used on *Nightmare*, the context of his guest appearance, and what this recording means in both his career arc and the broader A7X drummer narrative.

### Mike's 2010 Guest Kit: Tama Starclassic Maple

For the *Nightmare* sessions, Mike Portnoy brought a Tama Starclassic Maple kit — the shell platform that had defined much of his mid-period career and remained in his arsenal for guest and touring contexts alongside the Starclassic Bubinga he used for Dream Theater's studio work.

The Starclassic Maple's character suited the *Nightmare* material well. Where bubinga delivers sharper attack and faster decay — ideal for the technical complexity of Dream Theater's *Systematic Chaos* — maple shells produce a warmer, more resonant fundamental tone that supports the emotional weight of A7X's arrangements. "So Far Away," the album's Grammy-nominated tribute ballad to The Rev, required a kit that could hold space rather than cut through it. The maple shells' sustain and warmth delivered that.

Portnoy's configuration was his established large-format touring setup: double 22"x18" bass drums, five rack toms (8" through 14"), and three floor toms (14", 16", 18"). The Rev had been a drummer who used an expansive kit — multiple toms, complex cymbal arrangements — so Portnoy's large touring rig was well-suited to covering The Rev's original drum parts without compromise.

Tama's Star-Cast mounting system preserved the shells' resonance across the full configuration. Each drum spoke clearly, essential for the melodic tom figures The Rev had composed throughout the album's arrangements. The setup was designed for physical performance at high demand — exactly what the emotional intensity of recording a tribute album required.

### Snare: Tama Starclassic Maple 14" x 6.5"

Portnoy's matching Tama Starclassic Maple snare provided the warm, authoritative crack that anchors *Nightmare*'s production. The 6.5" depth offered the balance of power and sensitivity that the album's dynamic range demanded — from the brutal snare hits of "God Hates Us" and "Natural Born Killer" to the restrained, measured backbeat of "So Far Away."

The maple shell's character complemented the album's emotional register. Maple snares produce a fuller, more mid-forward snap than metal or birch alternatives — a sound that sits in heavy guitar mixes without becoming harsh. On *Nightmare*, where the emotional content of the songs required the drums to support rather than dominate, this character was exactly right.

Tuned in the medium range for the sessions, the snare delivered clear attack for metal passages while retaining the sensitivity for dynamic variation that The Rev's original compositions required. Portnoy's task was to replicate The Rev's drum voice as faithfully as possible — and the Starclassic Maple snare's versatile character gave him the range to do that.

### The 2010 Hybrid Rig: Paiste and Sabian

Portnoy's cymbal configuration for the *Nightmare* sessions reflected a transitional moment in his endorsement history — a hybrid of Sabian HHX and Paiste 2002 cymbals drawn from the expanding arsenal of a drummer who had been exploring both brands through the late Dream Theater era.

The combination delivered a distinctive palette. Sabian's HHX series — hand-hammered with a complex, dark wash — provided depth and atmosphere suited to *Nightmare*'s more emotionally resonant material: "So Far Away," "Fiction," "Tonight the World Dies." The Paiste 2002's brighter, more direct character cut through the heavy guitar frequencies on "God Hates Us," "Natural Born Killer," and "Nightmare" itself.

The Rev had been known for a specific cymbal sound — responsive, with a dark complexity that suited his jazz-influenced approach within A7X's metal framework. The Sabian HHX elements of Portnoy's hybrid rig were the closest match to that character available within his existing setup. Rather than sourcing an entirely new cymbal configuration to approximate The Rev's palette, Portnoy drew on the darker elements of his hybrid rig and used the Paiste 2002 pieces for projection and power where the arrangements demanded it.

For an album recorded under significant emotional and time pressure — the band needed to complete *Nightmare* quickly, both for commercial reasons and out of a desire to release the tribute within the mourning period — Portnoy's existing hybrid rig was a practical necessity and a musical strength. He knew this equipment inside out.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who played drums on Avenged Sevenfold's Nightmare?**

A: Mike Portnoy — then the drummer of Dream Theater — played drums on Avenged Sevenfold's Nightmare (2010). He served as a tribute fill-in following the death of founding A7X drummer Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan on December 28, 2009. Portnoy was a personal friend of The Rev and accepted the role to help the band complete their album as a tribute. The Rev had already composed the drum parts before his death; Portnoy learned and recorded those parts faithfully. Portnoy did not join Avenged Sevenfold as a permanent member — his involvement was limited to the Nightmare recording sessions and a brief live stint. For Portnoy's complete gear history and discography, see the [Mike Portnoy drummer profile](/drummer/mike-portnoy).

**Q: Did Mike Portnoy officially join Avenged Sevenfold?**

A: No. Mike Portnoy served as a tribute fill-in for the Nightmare (2010) recording and did not officially join Avenged Sevenfold as a permanent member. His involvement was explicitly a one-time tribute to his friend Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan. After the Nightmare sessions and a brief live period, Portnoy ended his time with A7X. The band subsequently held auditions and selected Arin Ilejay as The Rev's permanent replacement; Ilejay recorded Hail to the King (2013) before departing in 2014. Brooks Wackerman has been A7X's drummer since 2015.

**Q: What was The Rev's last recorded album?**

A: Nightmare (2010) is the last Avenged Sevenfold album associated with The Rev (Jimmy Sullivan). He wrote the drum parts for the album before his death on December 28, 2009, and the track "Fiction" features The Rev performing on piano and vocals — recorded from a demo he left before he died. The album was completed with Mike Portnoy playing The Rev's drum compositions as a tribute. In this sense, Nightmare is both The Rev's posthumous studio album and a tribute to his compositions performed by another drummer in his honor.

**Q: What drums did Mike Portnoy use on the Nightmare album?**

A: Mike Portnoy used a Tama Starclassic Maple kit for the Nightmare (2010) sessions — his 2010 touring configuration featuring double 22"x18" bass drums, five rack toms (8" through 14"), and three floor toms (14", 16", 18"). His cymbal rig was a Paiste/Sabian hybrid, combining Paiste 2002 crashes and china with Sabian HHX crashes and ride. Hardware included the DW 9000 double bass pedal. He played his Vic Firth Mike Portnoy Signature sticks throughout the sessions. For the full Mike Portnoy gear story, see the [Mike Portnoy kit guide](/articles/whats-in-mike-portnoys-kit).

**Q: Was Avenged Sevenfold's Nightmare Grammy-nominated?**

A: "So Far Away" — the Grammy-nominated tribute ballad from Nightmare — received Academy recognition for its emotional power and craft. The album debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200, went Multi-Platinum in the United States, and reached the UK Top 3. Nightmare is Avenged Sevenfold's highest-charting album and one of the most commercially successful heavy metal records of 2010.
