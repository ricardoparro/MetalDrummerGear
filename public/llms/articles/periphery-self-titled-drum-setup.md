## Overview

Periphery's self-titled debut (July 27, 2010, Sumerian Records) is the record that named djent as a genre and established its technical ceiling. Travis Orbin — Periphery's founding drummer — performed on this debut and the subsequent *Icarus* EP (2011) before departing in 2012. His drumming on "Icarus Lives!", "Racecar", and "Buttersnips" set the polyrhythmic and ghost-note benchmark that djent drumming has been measured against ever since.

Matt Halpern joined Periphery in 2012 and has been the band's drummer since, producing exceptional work across multiple acclaimed albums. But the self-titled belongs to Orbin: it documents djent's founding drummer at the genre's defining moment.

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Travis Orbin use on Periphery's self-titled debut?**
A: Travis Orbin played a DW Collector's Series drum kit on Periphery's self-titled debut (2010). The Collector's Series is DW's flagship custom production line — North American maple shells with precisely specified ply counts and bearing edges. His configuration included double 22"x18" kick drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, and 14" and 16" floor toms. The DW Collector's construction provided the articulate attack and controlled sustain that djent's dense guitar frequency environment demands.

**Q: What cymbals did Travis Orbin use on Periphery's debut album?**
A: Travis Orbin played Zildjian cymbals across his Periphery debut setup, using the K Custom Dark and A Custom series. His hi-hats were Zildjian 14" K Custom Dark — providing complex, articulate tone within djent's guitar frequency landscape. Crashes were Zildjian A Custom (18" and 19") for fast attack and quick decay. Ride was a Zildjian 21" K Custom Dark for controlled wash. An 18" A Custom China and 10" A Custom Splash completed the setup for rhythmic punctuation.

**Q: How did Periphery's drum setup change after Travis Orbin departed?**
A: When Matt Halpern joined Periphery in 2012, the drum setup shifted from Orbin's DW Collector's / Zildjian K Custom and A Custom / Roland SPD-SX hybrid framework to Halpern's Mapex Saturn V / Meinl Byzance configuration. Orbin's analytical precision approach gave way to Halpern's groove-first sensibility — different philosophies producing exceptional results in their respective eras. Halpern's acoustic-forward setup produces a warmer, more organic character than Orbin's hybrid approach.
