## Overview

Released on February 7, 2020, Sepultura's "Quadra" stands as Eloy Casagrande's most technically ambitious work with the band — and his final studio album before the most high-profile career move in modern metal drumming. Within three years of this recording, he would be recruited to join Metallica's M72 World Tour and ultimately become Slipknot's new drummer. But first came "Quadra": a sprawling double concept album divided into four thematic quadrants, spanning orchestral passages, thrash assaults, groove metal, and introspective progressive sections.

Recorded at Fascination Street Studios in Örebro, Sweden — the facility behind records by Opeth, Amon Amarth, and Arch Enemy — with producer Jens Bogren, "Quadra" pushed Eloy to demonstrate every dimension of his playing. At 28 years old, nine years into his Sepultura tenure, he delivered a performance that confirmed what anyone who had watched him on stage already knew: this was one of the most complete drummers in metal.

The album concept drew on ancient Roman mythology, the four classical elements, and the four stages of life. For Eloy, that conceptual ambition translated into drum parts that move between explosive double-bass thrash, carefully measured orchestral grooves, polyrhythmic progressive passages, and the relentless tribal aggression that had always been Sepultura's signature.

This article breaks down the precise gear Eloy used for "Quadra" — the Pearl Master Maple Reserve shells, Mapex Black Panther snare, and Meinl Byzance cymbals that powered his definitive Sepultura statement — and why this recording represents the peak of his Sepultura era before the Metallica and Slipknot chapters began.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Pearl Master Maple Reserve (Gloss Black finish)
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance (Traditional and Extra Dry series)
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Tama Speed Cobra 910 Twin; Tama Iron Cobra Hi-Hat Stand; Promark Eloy Casagrande Signature TX5BW
- **Heads:** Remo Coated Ambassador batter / Remo Ambassador Snare Side resonant (snare); Remo Emperor Coated / Remo Ambassador Clear (toms); Remo Powerstroke 3 Coated (bass drums)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium to medium-high for attack clarity across dynamic range

### Eloy's Pearl Master Maple Reserve — Sepultura Studio Workhorse

For "Quadra," Eloy Casagrande played Pearl's Master Maple Reserve — the company's flagship acoustic drum line, built from 6-ply Select Maple with Pearl's proprietary SST shell technology. The Master Maple Reserve delivers the balanced tone profile that a double concept album demands: warm low-end for orchestral sections, punchy midrange for thrash passages, and clear high-end articulation for the progressive fills spread throughout the album's four quadrants.

The dual 22"x18" bass drums were central to the "Quadra" drum sound. At 18 inches deep, they produce the extended low-end sustain that Jens Bogren's production captured so effectively — a thunderous bottom that anchors the album's heaviest moments without sacrificing the definition needed for the complex double-bass patterns in tracks like "Isolation" and "Means to an End."

Eloy's tom configuration — three rack toms plus a 16" floor tom — gave him the melodic voice the "Quadra" material required. The album's progressive sections call for flowing tom patterns that connect rhythmic ideas across bars in a way that pure thrash metal rarely demands. The Master Maple Reserve's warm maple tone made those melodic tom passages sing without sacrificing the aggression needed elsewhere.

Jens Bogren's recording approach at Fascination Street Studios maximized the kit's acoustic properties. Known for his work with Opeth and Amon Amarth, Bogren positioned the drums as the foundation of the mix — present, clear, and dynamic, responding to Eloy's wide range from whisper-quiet ghost notes to full-power thrash explosions.

### Mapex Black Panther: The Sepultura Crack

Eloy's snare choice for the "Quadra" era was the Mapex Black Panther — a drum with a well-earned reputation among metal drummers for its aggressive projection and tight, cracking character. The Black Panther's maple shell construction produced the focused attack that "Quadra" required: bright enough to cut through Sepultura's dense guitar arrangements, with enough warmth to serve the album's quieter orchestral passages.

The 6.5" depth strikes the balance Eloy's playing demands. Deep enough for body and authority on the big backbeats throughout the album's thrash sections; not so deep that the drum loses its snap during the ghost-note patterns that appear in the progressive quadrants. The Black Panther's reinforcement rings added resonance control and projection — a natural choice for a studio recording where the snare needed to sit clearly in the mix across a wide dynamic range.

Jens Bogren's approach to snare recording complemented the Black Panther's character: close-mic'd with attention to attack transients, letting the maple shell's natural warmth round out the initial crack into a full, satisfying pop. The result is the clearest, most controlled snare sound of Eloy's Sepultura discography.

### Meinl Byzance: Sepultura Warmth Meets Metal Aggression

Eloy's Meinl Byzance setup for "Quadra" was carefully chosen for the album's wide dynamic range. The Byzance series — hand-hammered B20 bronze — delivers a warmth and complexity that raw, unlathed aggressive cymbals cannot match, and "Quadra" demanded both tonal beauty in the orchestral passages and raw aggression in the thrash sections.

His setup included Byzance Traditional 14" hi-hats, a 16" Traditional Thin Crash, an 18" Extra Dry Thin Crash, a 20" Extra Dry Crash, a 22" Jazz Thin Ride, and an 18" Byzance China. The Byzance Traditional series provided the foundation: warm, rich overtones that work equally well for the album's reflective progressive passages and its explosive thrash moments. The Extra Dry models — with their muted, controlled character — gave Eloy a contrasting voice for sections requiring definition without excessive wash.

The 22" Jazz Thin Ride represents a sophisticated choice for a thrash album. Its complex, washy overtones appear in "Quadra"'s progressive passages — sections where Eloy plays with jazz-influenced ride patterns that contrast sharply with the album's heavier moments. Few extreme metal drummers would reach for a jazz ride; the fact that Eloy did reveals the musical range that made him the natural choice for Metallica's M72 collaboration years later.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**What drum kit did Eloy Casagrande use on Sepultura's Quadra?**
On Sepultura's Quadra (2020), Eloy Casagrande played a Pearl Master Maple Reserve kit with dual 22x18 inch bass drums, three rack toms (10, 12, and 14 inches), and a 16 inch floor tom. The Master Maple Reserve's 6-ply Select Maple shells delivered the balanced tonal profile needed for Quadra's wide dynamic range — from orchestral passages to full thrash assault. The album was recorded at Fascination Street Studios in Sweden with producer Jens Bogren.

**What snare did Eloy Casagrande use on Quadra?**
Eloy Casagrande used a Mapex Black Panther snare (14" x 6.5") on Sepultura's Quadra. The Black Panther's maple shell construction provides the aggressive, cracking attack that Sepultura's rhythmic style demands, with enough warmth to serve the album's more restrained orchestral sections. It was tuned medium-to-high for attack clarity across the album's wide dynamic range.

**What cymbals did Eloy Casagrande use on Quadra?**
Eloy Casagrande used Meinl Byzance cymbals on Sepultura's Quadra. His setup included Byzance Traditional 14 inch hi-hats, a 16 inch Traditional Thin Crash, an 18 inch Extra Dry Thin Crash, a 20 inch Extra Dry Crash, a 22 inch Jazz Thin Ride, and an 18 inch Byzance China. The Byzance series' warm, hand-hammered B20 bronze character suited Quadra's mix of progressive musical passages and aggressive thrash sections.

**Was Quadra Eloy Casagrande's last Sepultura album?**
Yes, Quadra (2020) was Eloy Casagrande's final full studio album with Sepultura. He joined Metallica's M72 World Tour as a co-drummer in 2023 while still with Sepultura, then departed in 2024 to join Slipknot as their new drummer. Quadra stands as the peak of his Sepultura studio work — a double concept album that showcased every dimension of his playing before the most high-profile career move in modern metal drumming.

**How does Eloy Casagrande's Quadra kit compare to his Metallica M72 setup?**
The gear changed significantly between Quadra and the M72 World Tour. Shells shifted from Pearl Master Maple Reserve (warm, studio-balanced) to Pearl Reference Pure (brighter, stadium-projecting). Snare moved from Mapex Black Panther maple to Pearl Free-Floating Steel. Cymbals switched from Meinl Byzance (warm, complex) to Zildjian A Custom (bright, fast). Pedals went from Tama Speed Cobra (cam-drive) to Pearl Demon Drive (direct-drive). Only his Promark Eloy Casagrande Signature sticks remained unchanged across both configurations.
