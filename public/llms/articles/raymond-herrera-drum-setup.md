## Overview

Raymond Herrera (born April 18, 1972, in Los Angeles, California) was the founding drummer of Fear Factory, one of the most influential bands at the intersection of death metal, industrial music, and alternative metal. From 1990 to 2002, Herrera's drumming was the mechanical, precise heartbeat of a band whose concept — the machine versus humanity — was expressed as much through the rhythmic logic of the music as through the lyrics.

Fear Factory's approach required a drumming style unlike conventional metal: electronic triggers, sequencer-aligned patterns, and the disciplined precision of a live drummer performing in sync with programmed industrial elements. Herrera delivered this with extraordinary consistency. On Demanufacture (1995) and Obsolete (1998) — the band's artistic peaks — his drumming bridged organic aggression and mechanical precision in a way that felt genuinely threatening rather than clinical.

His setup during Fear Factory's key years centered on a Pearl Reference kit augmented by extensive ddrum and Roland electronic trigger integration on the bass drums and snare. Zildjian Z Custom cymbals provided the bright, aggressive projection needed to cut through Fear Factory's dense, downtuned guitar wall and industrial processing. Pearl Eliminator pedals sustained the sustained double-kick patterns that are one of Herrera's rhythmic signatures.

Herrera departed Fear Factory in 2002 and pursued various production and session projects before reuniting briefly with the band.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Raymond Herrera use with Fear Factory?**
A: Raymond Herrera played Pearl Reference drums during Fear Factory's key years. However, unlike standard acoustic setups, Herrera used heavy electronic trigger integration — ddrum and Roland acoustic triggers on both bass drums and the snare, routing these signals through a drum module to blend sampled and acoustic sounds in sync with Fear Factory's industrial programming.

**Q: What cymbals did Raymond Herrera play?**
A: Raymond Herrera used Zildjian Z Custom cymbals. The Z Custom series are among Zildjian's most aggressive-sounding models — cast from B20 alloy with heavy hammering and bright-voiced characteristics. Their cutting, fast-attacking character suited Fear Factory's industrial metal production sound.

**Q: Why did Raymond Herrera use electronic triggers?**
A: Fear Factory's music is built on the conceptual tension between organic humanity and industrial machines. Musically, this meant live drumming had to interact precisely with programmed industrial elements and sequences. Electronic triggers allowed Herrera to trigger consistent, machine-perfect attack transients from his acoustic playing while maintaining the feel of a live drummer rather than pure sequencing.

**Q: What pedals did Raymond Herrera use?**
A: Raymond Herrera used Pearl Eliminator double bass drum pedals. The Eliminator's interchangeable cam system and smooth chain-drive mechanism provided the consistent, powerful response needed for Fear Factory's sustained double-kick industrial patterns.

**Q: What albums did Raymond Herrera record with Fear Factory?**
A: Raymond Herrera recorded Soul of a New Machine (1992), Demanufacture (1995), Obsolete (1998), and Digimortal (2001) with Fear Factory. Demanufacture and Obsolete are widely considered the band's artistic peaks and are foundational documents of industrial metal.
