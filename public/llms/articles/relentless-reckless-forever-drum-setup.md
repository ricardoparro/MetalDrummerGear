## Overview

Released on February 11, 2011, *Relentless Reckless Forever* is Children of Bodom's seventh full-length album and the record that completed the band's commercial Nuclear Blast era with a signature blend of melodic aggression and keyboard-driven neoclassical songwriting. Recorded at Sonic Pump Studios in Helsinki with producer Anssi Kippo — the same environment that had defined *Blooddrunk* (2008) — the album delivered one of the most focused and confident drum performances of Jaska Raatikainen's studio career.

For Jaska Raatikainen, *Relentless Reckless Forever* represented the Pearl Masters era in its most refined state. The Pearl Masters MCX shells — an evolution from the Premium Maple configuration that had debuted on *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005) and been consolidated through *Blooddrunk* — brought a refined shell construction to the settled Zildjian A Custom and K Custom cymbal setup and the familiar Pearl Eliminator double pedal. The result is the most polished and controlled Pearl-era drum sound in CoB's catalog: every component understood, every performance deliberate.

Tracks like "Shovel Knockout" demonstrate the double-kick authority that Raatikainen had developed across six years of the Pearl endorsement, while "Was It Worth It?" showcases the dynamic intelligence behind the technical ferocity — melodic fill work that tracks the keyboard countermelodies with the precision that defines CoB's compositional approach. This article breaks down every component of Raatikainen's setup on *Relentless Reckless Forever* — the Pearl Masters MCX shells, the Zildjian cymbal configuration, the Pearl Eliminator, and the studio context that shaped the seventh CoB album.

### Pearl Masters MCX: The Pearl Era at Peak Refinement

For *Relentless Reckless Forever*, Jaska Raatikainen played the Pearl Masters MCX — the evolved Pearl Masters shell construction that refined the six-ply maple configuration of the earlier Premium Maple era into a more precisely voiced instrument. Captured at Sonic Pump Studios with the accumulated studio knowledge of three CoB albums in that environment, the MCX kit produced the most focused and consistent Pearl-era drum sound in CoB's catalog.

The Pearl Masters MCX shell construction brought improved resonance characteristics compared to the earlier Premium Maple: enhanced shell stiffness and more uniform maple ply selection produced a more immediate attack transient and a tighter, more controlled sustain. In Sonic Pump Studios' acoustic environment — which Anssi Kippo had learned thoroughly across *Blooddrunk* — the MCX shells' attack-forward character translated directly to the mix with minimal post-processing.

The double bass drum configuration — twin 22" x 18" kick drums — remained the cornerstone of Raatikainen's approach, deployed across *Relentless Reckless Forever*'s varied tempo landscape. "Shovel Knockout" drives sustained double-kick patterns through the verse-to-chorus architecture; the slower, more melodic tracks allowed the MCX shells' controlled sustain to serve the arrangements without the kick-focused high-velocity context.

The compact tom spread — 10" and 12" rack toms, 14" and 16" floor toms — maintained the CoB fill vocabulary: Janne Wirman's keyboard lines tracked by melodic tom phrases that serve as compositional countermelody rather than pure rhythmic punctuation. In the MCX's more precisely voiced character, these fill passages had a clarity and definition that suited *Relentless Reckless Forever*'s refined production.

### Pearl Masters 14" x 5.5" MCX Snare

Raatikainen's snare on *Relentless Reckless Forever* was a Pearl Masters MCX model — the evolved shell construction that brought the same refined character to the snare as to the kit's tom and bass drum shells. The 14" x 5.5" dimensions delivered focused crack and attack suited to the album's varied tempo demands, with the MCX construction's tighter sustain providing cleaner articulation in Anssi Kippo's dense keyboard-layered mix.

The album's dynamic range — from the sustained aggression of "Shovel Knockout" to the more measured architectural approach of slower tracks — required the snare to serve multiple roles: high-impact backbeat work at full velocity, ghost note passages in verse arrangements, and precise accent placement across fill phrases. The MCX snare's focused attack served all these roles with the confidence of a deeply familiar instrument.

Kippo's Sonic Pump Studios treatment of the MCX snare reflected six years of accumulated knowledge with Pearl Masters shells in this environment: close-miked signal treated to preserve the natural MCX attack character, with compression and EQ dialed to Raatikainen's playing dynamics and the album's production priorities.

### Zildjian A Custom and K Custom: The Settled Cymbal Configuration

Jaska Raatikainen's Zildjian A Custom and K Custom cymbal setup on *Relentless Reckless Forever* was the settled, fully understood configuration of the Pearl endorsement peak — the same arrangement that had debuted on *Are You Dead Yet?* (2005) and been refined through *Blooddrunk* (2008), now deployed with six years of accumulated familiarity.

The Zildjian A Custom series' multi-stage lathing and brilliant finish remained the right choice for CoB's keyboard-dense production approach: the A Custom's bright, cutting character allowed cymbal work to register clearly in Janne Wirman's synth-heavy arrangements without requiring significant EQ assistance in the mix. The fast attack and bright upper frequency presence of the A Custom 14" hi-hats maintained articulation clarity across *Relentless Reckless Forever*'s varied tempo demands.

By *Relentless Reckless Forever*, the A Custom / K Custom combination had been in Raatikainen's hands for six years of touring and recording. The familiarity is audible in the placement precision: crashes land at structurally significant moments with the deliberateness of a drummer who knows exactly what each cymbal sounds like in every dynamic context, rather than a player still learning the instrument's character.

The K Custom 20" ride's darker bell and body character continued to serve its role as textural counterpoint to the brighter A Custom crashes — warmth for sustained melodic passages and keyboard-driven sections, bright cut from the A Custom for high-velocity transitions and intensity peaks. The China cymbal deployment on *Relentless Reckless Forever* maintained the prominent role established on *Blooddrunk*, serving the structural articulation moments of "Shovel Knockout" and similar tracks.
