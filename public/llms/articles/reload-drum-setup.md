# Lars Ulrich's Drum Setup on Metallica's Reload (1997)

> Complete gear breakdown for Metallica's Reload (1997). Discover Lars Ulrich's Tama Artstar II ES kit, Zildjian A Custom cymbals, and the Bob Rock production that powered 'Fuel,' 'The Memory Remains,' and 'Fixxxer.' 5× Platinum certified.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [Lars Ulrich](/llms/drummers/lars-ulrich.md)
**Band / Album:** Metallica — *Reload* (1997)
**Genre:** Heavy Metal
**Certification:** 5× Platinum US

## Overview

Released on November 18, 1997, Metallica's "Reload" was the second volume of what the band conceived as a two-part project with "Load" (1996). Together, Load and Reload represented a conscious departure from the thrash metal blueprint — longer grooves, bluesier riffs, and a more deliberate, feel-oriented approach to drumming that Bob Rock had begun cultivating on the Black Album.

Reload was recorded using many of the same sessions and the same gear configuration as Load, with Lars Ulrich working at The Plant Studios in Sausalito and HQ Studios in Tiburon, California. The album contains some of Metallica's most radio-friendly material: "Fuel" is an up-tempo, adrenaline-driven standout, "The Memory Remains" (featuring Marianne Faithfull) became a major single, and "Fixxxer" closes the album with an eight-minute epic that showcases Lars's dynamic range.

The drum setup Lars used across Reload carried over and evolved from Load. By 1997 he was settled into a Tama Artstar II ES (Extended Shell) configuration — a birch/maple hybrid that delivered warmer low-end than his thrash-era Artstar II birch setup while retaining the attack and projection Metallica demanded. Zildjian A Custom cymbals, which Lars had adopted for the mid-1990s era, completed the sonic picture: brighter and more modern than the traditional A Series of the early catalog.

Reload is certified 5× Platinum in the United States and completes the Load/Reload double-album era — a critical period documenting how Lars Ulrich adapted his drumming to a more groove-centric context while maintaining the physicality that made his reputation.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Tama Artstar II ES (birch/maple hybrid, Black with chrome hardware)
- **Snare:** Tama Lars Ulrich Prototype Snare (LU1465 precursor), 14" x 6.5", steel shell
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom series
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra Double Pedal; Tama Titan Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Original throne; Ahead Lars Ulrich Signature sticks
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador Coated (batter snare), Remo Ambassador Clear (batter toms), Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium tension — brighter than Black Album, more versatile than thrash era

### Lars's Studio Kit: Tama Artstar II ES

The Tama Artstar II ES (Extended Shell) that Lars Ulrich used during the Load/Reload period represented a midpoint between his thrash-era birch Artstar II and the full maple Artist kit of the Black Album. The birch/maple hybrid construction — birch core with maple outer ply — gave Lars a drum that could serve the Load/Reload material's range of tempos and dynamics.

On an up-tempo track like "Fuel," the birch's tight, punchy attack gave the kick and snare the definition needed at high speed. On slower, blues-inflected tracks like "Low Man's Lyric" or the atmospheric closer "Fixxxer," the maple component provided warmth and sustain that the pure birch of the Justice era couldn't deliver.

The 22" x 18" bass drum continued the deep-shell trend Bob Rock had established on the Black Album, retaining significant low-end presence. Lars used his Tama Iron Cobra double pedal for the handful of demanding passages — "Fuel" in particular features some of the album's most aggressive double-bass work — while groove-focused tracks relied on a single-pedal approach with the left foot managing the hi-hat.

Tom configuration remained the four-tom setup Lars had used since the Black Album (10", 12", 14", 16"), tuned for clarity and attack rather than maximum resonance.

### The LU1465 Prototype in Its Mid-Career

By the Reload sessions, Lars Ulrich had been playing his Tama Lars Ulrich prototype snare — the instrument that would become the production LU1465 — for several years. The steel shell with Star hardware had settled into its definitive character: a bright, focused crack with enough body to cut through Metallica's dense guitar arrangements without sounding thin.

Compared to the Black Album snare tuning — where Bob Rock pushed Lars to loosen the batter head far below his comfort zone — the Reload sessions used a more versatile medium-tension approach. The result sits between the boomy Black Album sound and the high-tension crack of the Master of Puppets era.

### Zildjian A Custom — The Modern Standard

By the time Metallica recorded Reload, Lars Ulrich had firmly established the Zildjian A Custom series as his standard — a significant evolution from the traditional A Series cymbals of the Master of Puppets, Justice, and early Black Album era.

Cymbal setup:
- **Hi-Hats:** Zildjian A Custom 14" — bright, fast response
- **Crash (left):** Zildjian A Custom 16" — quick accents and transitions
- **Crash (primary):** Zildjian A Custom 18" — section endings and big moments
- **Crash (far right):** Zildjian A Custom 19" — expanded palette versus earlier setups
- **Ride:** Zildjian A Custom 20" — clear bell for mid-paced riding passages
- **China:** Zildjian A Custom 18" — aggressive accents on riff-punctuation moments like "Fuel"

## Recording Context

Bob Rock used The Plant Studios in Sausalito and HQ Studios in Tiburon for both Load and Reload — the sessions were largely continuous. The drum sound is deliberately consistent across both albums: more present and controlled than the heavily reverbed Black Album sound, with room mics blended conservatively to give the kit a punchy, radio-friendly character.

Microphone approach: Shure Beta 52A inside kick / AKG D112 outside, Shure SM57 top snare / AKG C451 bottom, Sennheiser MD421 on each tom, AKG C451 on hi-hat, Neumann U87 overheads in spaced pair. Room mics (Neumann U47s) at lower blend than Black Album.

## Key Facts

- Released November 18, 1997 — companion album to Load (1996)
- 5× Platinum US certified
- Recorded at The Plant Studios, Sausalito and HQ Studios, Tiburon, CA
- Producer: Bob Rock & Metallica — continuous with Load sessions
- Tama Artstar II ES (birch/maple hybrid) — midpoint between thrash-era birch and Black Album maple

- Iron Cobra double pedal consistent with Black Album era
