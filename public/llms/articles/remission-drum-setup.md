# Remission Drum Setup: Brann Dailor's Gear on Mastodon's Debut (2002)

> Discover the exact drum kit, cymbals, and gear Brann Dailor used to record Mastodon's debut full-length Remission (2002) on Relapse Records. Full breakdown of the DW Collector's Series kit, Zildjian A series cymbals, Pearl Eliminator double pedal, and the raw Southern sludge metal drum approach behind one of heavy music's most important debuts.

## Overview

Released on June 25, 2002, Mastodon's *Remission* is the debut full-length that launched the band's career and established [Brann Dailor](/llms/drummers/brann-dailor.md) as one of heavy music's most distinctive drum voices. Recorded for Relapse Records — the Philadelphia-based label that had become the definitive home of extreme American metal — *Remission* introduced Mastodon's Southern sludge metal identity to an international underground audience and placed Dailor's jazz-schooled fluidity in the most demanding possible context: massive downtuned guitars, slow-to-mid tempos, and the relentless physical aggression that characterised the post-Melvins sludge tradition.

The album occupies a unique position in the Mastodon discography. Where *Remission* is the origin point — raw, direct, sludge-rooted — later albums like [Blood Mountain](/llms/articles/blood-mountain-drum-setup.md) (2006) and [Crack the Skye](/llms/articles/crack-the-skye-drum-setup.md) (2009) would expand the band's progressive ambitions into mainstream critical acclaim. *Remission* is Mastodon before refinement: the debut that established the foundational drum vocabulary Dailor would evolve across the following two decades.

Producer Matt Bayles — who would return for [Blood Mountain](/llms/articles/blood-mountain-drum-setup.md) four years later — captured the album with a close-miked, direct approach that emphasised the physicality of Dailor's playing rather than the atmospheric ambience of later productions. The result is a drum sound that hits the listener with immediate force: raw, present, and perfectly calibrated to the album's sludge metal demands.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series (maple shells, natural finish), 22"/10"/12"/14"/16" — pre-Tama endorsement era
- **Snare:** DW 14x6.5 Black Nickel over Steel
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" New Beat hi-hats, 18" Medium Thin Crash, 20" Medium Crash, 22" Medium Ride, 18" China
- **Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator double bass drum pedal (early period, before DW hardware transition)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A American Classic
- **Heads:** Evans UV1 Coated (batter), Evans Hazy 300 (snare resonant), Evans EMAD Clear (kick batter)

### The DW Collector's Series: Before the Tama Era

Brann Dailor used a DW Collector's Series drum kit for the *Remission* sessions — the premium American custom line he would carry through the early Mastodon recording years before transitioning to Tama Starclassic Maple for the later progressive era. DW's Collector's Series builds shells to spec in California from premium hardwoods, producing drums with consistent, musical tuning ranges across the entire configuration.

The choice of maple shells was foundational for Dailor's sound even on this debut record. Maple resonates with warmth and fundamental tone — producing pitched notes rather than pure percussive noise — and even within *Remission*'s dense, crushing sludge context, Dailor's toms ring with melodic presence. This is not accidental: Dailor was already approaching the kit as a melodic instrument at the beginning of his career, tuning his drums to produce musical intervals that could function as melodic content within the fills.

The double bass drum configuration on *Remission* reflects the album's sludge metal demands. Heavy, driving bass drum patterns anchor the massive downtuned guitar work, and the double-kick setup gives Dailor the rhythmic flexibility and physical authority the music requires.

### Zildjian A Series: Early Career Brightness

Dailor's Zildjian A series setup on *Remission* represents his early-career cymbal language — bright, cutting, and aggressive in a way that suits the album's sludge metal context. The Zildjian A series is the flagship American-made cymbal line, using B8 bronze alloy to produce a clear, penetrating sound with consistent attack and defined decay.

On *Remission*, that brightness serves the music. The album's massive downtuned guitars occupy the low-mid register with extreme density, requiring cymbals with genuine high-frequency presence to cut through the mix. The A series New Beat hi-hats deliver a crisp, responsive top-end that asserts itself in the arrangement, and the crashes attack quickly and decay cleanly — suited to Mastodon's structural accent patterns.

This Zildjian setup is markedly different from the darker Meinl Byzance palette Dailor would adopt for [Crack the Skye](/llms/articles/crack-the-skye-drum-setup.md) and beyond. The Byzance's dark, complex, Turkish-forged character would suit the atmospheric and melodically sophisticated albums of Mastodon's progressive era. On *Remission*, the Zildjian A's brightness is the correct tool for a sludge metal debut.

### The Pearl Eliminator Double Pedal

The Pearl Eliminator double bass drum pedal was Dailor's early-period choice for these sessions, providing the double-kick foundation for *Remission*'s sludge metal rhythmic demands. The Pearl Eliminator is a highly regarded professional double pedal known for versatility and robust construction, with multiple cam drive settings that allow customisation of feel and resistance. On *Remission*, the double-kick configuration drives the grinding bass drum patterns that anchor the album's foundation.

## FAQs

**What kit did Brann Dailor use on Remission?**
A DW Collector's Series kit with maple shells — the pre-Tama endorsement configuration. Double bass drum setup with a 22" kick, rack toms at 10", 12", 14", and 16" floor tom.

**Is Remission Mastodon's first album?**
Yes — Remission (2002) is Mastodon's debut full-length, released on Relapse Records on June 25, 2002. Before Remission, the band released the Lifesblood EP (2001). Remission is the origin point of the Mastodon discography.

**How does Brann Dailor's drumming on Remission differ from later albums?**
Remission has a rawer, more direct sound compared to Blood Mountain (2006) and Crack the Skye (2009). The drum patterns are more groove-oriented and less polyrhythmically complex, reflecting the sludge metal context. The production is close-miked and physical rather than room-ambient and atmospheric.

**What cymbals does Brann Dailor use on Remission?**
Zildjian A series — 14" New Beat hi-hats, 18" and 20" crashes, 22" medium ride, 18" China. The bright Zildjian A character suited the sludge metal density before his later Meinl Byzance transition.

**What double pedal does he use?**
Pearl Eliminator double bass drum pedal. Early career choice before transitioning to DW hardware and, eventually, the single-kick approach of his later Mastodon work.

**Who produced Remission?**
Matt Bayles, who also produced Blood Mountain (2006). Bayles' close-miked drum approach gave the album its raw, physical character.
