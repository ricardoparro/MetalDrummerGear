## Overview

Released on June 25, 2002, Mastodon's *Remission* is the origin point of the entire Mastodon discography — the debut full-length that introduced the world to one of heavy music's most distinctive drumming voices. Brann Dailor arrived on *Remission* as a fully formed player: jazz-schooled, technically fluid, and completely committed to serving the song's raw sludge-metal energy rather than showcasing individual technique.

*Remission* represents a specific moment in Mastodon's creative arc that no subsequent record duplicates. Where *Leviathan* (2004) and *Blood Mountain* (2006) introduced progressive complexity, and *Crack the Skye* (2009) elevated the band to Grammy-nominated heights, *Remission* is the undiluted origin: Southern sludge metal in the tradition of Neurosis and Melvins, with Dailor's jazz background providing the rhythmic intelligence underneath the heaviness.

The drum sound on *Remission* is rawer and more direct than anything on the band's later Reprise Records output. Produced by Matt Bayles, the album captures a drum performance that is powerful without polish. For the debut sessions, Dailor used a DW Collector's Series kit — the pre-Tama endorsement configuration — with Zildjian A series cymbals and a Pearl Eliminator double pedal.

### The DW Collector's Series: Brann's Pre-Tama Debut Kit

For *Remission*, Brann Dailor used a DW Collector's Series kit before Mastodon's later Tama endorsement era. The kit configuration — 22" kick, rack toms at 10", 12", and 14", with a 16" floor tom — gave Dailor the spread of pitches needed for the album's tom patterns without the dense three-rack configuration he would adopt for *Crack the Skye*.

The wider intervallic gaps between toms (10", 12", 14" rather than 10", 12", 13") produced the more dramatically assertive fills that suit *Remission*'s sludge metal character. What is immediately apparent even on this debut is Dailor's jazz-influenced approach: tom patterns on tracks like "March of the Fire Ants" and "Mother Puncher" have a compositional logic that reflects his background in jazz rather than purely in metal.

### DW Black Nickel over Steel Snare

The DW 14x6.5 Black Nickel over Steel snare provides the bite and projection that *Remission*'s production demands. Steel shell construction gives a harder, more focused attack than wood alternatives — important on an album where the drums cut through heavily down-tuned guitars without extensive post-production processing. At 6.5" depth, the shell produces more body and low-end thud than standard-depth snares while maintaining the attack that anchors the groove.

Matt Bayles's production gave the snare a raw, present quality that differs sharply from the more refined snare sounds on later Mastodon records.

### Zildjian A Series: Bright Attack for the Sludge Debut

On *Remission*, Dailor used Zildjian A series cymbals — a deliberate contrast to the darker, more atmospheric Meinl Byzance setup he would choose for *Crack the Skye* seven years later. The Zildjian A series is Zildjian's foundational professional line: B20 bronze, bright in character, projecting, with fast attack response. For a 2002 sludge metal debut with raw production values, the A series provided the attack and presence to cut through heavily distorted, down-tuned guitar arrangements.

The comparison between the Zildjian A setup on *Remission* and the Meinl Byzance setup on *Crack the Skye* is instructive: same player, different record, completely different cymbal philosophy. *Remission* needed brightness and attack. *Crack the Skye* needed atmospheric depth. The gear choices follow the musical requirements.

### Pearl Eliminator Double Pedal

The Pearl Eliminator double bass drum pedal was Dailor's early-period hardware choice before adopting DW hardware for later Mastodon records. The Pearl Eliminator offers adjustable cam drive and footboard settings. For *Remission*'s demanding bass drum patterns, the Eliminator provided reliable double kick action through the recording sessions.

## FAQs

**What kit did Brann Dailor use on Remission?**
A DW Collector's Series drum kit, before Mastodon's later Tama endorsement. Configuration: 22" bass drum, rack toms at 10", 12", and 14", and a 16" floor tom.

**Is Remission Mastodon's first album?**
Yes — Remission (released June 25, 2002) is Mastodon's debut full-length album, released on Relapse Records. It followed the debut EP Lifesblood (2001) and established the Southern sludge metal foundation that later albums developed.

**How does Brann Dailor's drumming on Remission differ from later albums?**
Remission has a rawer production and more direct sludge metal approach. The polyrhythmic complexity of Crack the Skye is present in early form but less elaborate. Zildjian A series cymbals give an aggressive attack vs. the dark atmospheric warmth of the Meinl Byzance setup on Crack the Skye.

**What cymbals did Brann Dailor use on Remission?**
Zildjian A series — bright, cutting, fast attack response suited to raw sludge metal production. This contrasts with the darker Meinl Byzance cymbals adopted for Mastodon's progressive-era records.

**What double pedal did Brann Dailor use on Remission?**
Pearl Eliminator double bass drum pedal — early-period hardware before DW adoption on later Mastodon records.
