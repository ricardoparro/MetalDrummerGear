## Overview

Ryan Van Poederooyen is the Canadian drummer who has anchored Devin Townsend's musical universe since 1999 — one of the longest-running drummer-artist partnerships in progressive metal. His Pearl Reference series kit, Sabian AAX and HHX cymbals, and Tama Iron Cobra or DW 9000 double bass pedals form the foundation of a setup optimized for the extreme dynamic range that Devin Townsend's catalogue demands.

Van Poederooyen's versatility is his defining quality: from the brutal extreme metal of *Deconstruction* (2011) to the near-inaudible ambience of *Ghost* (2011), his setup and technique serve every direction Townsend's music travels. His double bass approach prioritizes forward rhythmic drive over maximum speed — the goal is physical impact and groove, not blast-beat tempos.

### Pearl Reference Setup: Built for Prog-Metal Versatility

Pearl's Reference series uses a hybrid maple and mahogany shell construction that delivers the versatility Van Poederooyen needs for Devin Townsend's genre-spanning catalogue. Maple provides immediate attack and clear high-frequency transient response; mahogany adds warmth and low-end body. The result is a kit that performs equally well in the controlled studio environments Townsend's layered productions require and the large-venue touring that follows.

Van Poederooyen's double bass configuration — two 22" x 18" Pearl Reference kick drums — provides the sub-bass mass for the double kick patterns on heavier DTP material. At 22" diameter and 18" depth, each kick generates both attack speed and low-frequency resonance: felt in the chest on *Deconstruction*'s crushing sections, articulate and controlled on the groove-focused passages of *Epicloud* and *Transcendence*.

The tom configuration (10", 12" rack toms; 16", 18" floor toms) spans the range DTP's compositional style requires — sufficient spread for transitional fills and melodic tom work without overcomplicating the physical setup.

### Sabian AAX/HHX: Musical Character for Prog-Metal

Van Poederooyen's Sabian cymbal setup bridges the tonal extremes of Devin Townsend's music. The AAX line — machine-hammered B20 bronze — produces focused, bright instruments with fast attack and controlled decay: the right tool for articulation in dense metal arrangements. The HHX series adds darker, more complex tonal character suited to the atmospheric and progressive dimensions of Townsend's catalogue.

**Cymbal configuration:**
- 14" AAX Hi-Hats — fast, versatile response for both driving metal pulse and delicate ambient work
- 16" AAX Crash — quick decay for accent work in DTP's densely arranged passages
- 18" HHX Crash — darker, fuller tone for section transitions and climactic moments
- 20" HHX Ride — complex wash and defined bell for extended progressive passages
- 18" AAX China — aggressive trash accent for DTP's heaviest sections

### Double Bass Configuration

Van Poederooyen uses a true double bass drum setup — two independent 22" x 18" Pearl Reference kick drums rather than a single drum with a double pedal. His double pedal of choice is either the Tama Iron Cobra (power cam system for driving, consistent stroke) or the DW 9000 (smooth direct-drive feel for controlled acceleration at sustained tempos).

His double bass approach is powerful and groove-focused rather than speed-maximizing. The double kick provides the forward momentum and physical presence that DTP's heavier material requires — "Kingdom," "Decimator," and *Deconstruction*'s extreme passages — without sacrificing the musical control needed when the same setlist turns ambient and textural.

## Career Overview

Ryan Van Poederooyen has been Devin Townsend's drummer since 1999 — over 25 years of collaboration spanning:

- **Devin Townsend Band era (1999–2008):** Physicist, Terria, Accelerated Evolution, Ziltoid the Omniscient
- **Devin Townsend Project (2009–2018):** Ki, Addicted, Deconstruction, Ghost, Epicloud, Z², Transcendence
- **Solo era (2019–present):** Empath, Lightwork, global touring

*Deconstruction* (2011) is widely cited among prog-metal drummers as one of the most demanding drum performances in the genre. The simultaneous release of *Deconstruction* and *Ghost* on the same day — one a brutal extreme metal opus, the other an ambient near-acoustic record — documents Van Poederooyen's range more completely than any single album could.
