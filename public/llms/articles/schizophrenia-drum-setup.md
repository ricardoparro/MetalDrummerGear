# Igor Cavalera's Drum Setup on Sepultura's Schizophrenia (1987)

**Genre:** Thrash Metal / Death Metal
**Label:** Cogumelo Records (pre-Roadrunner era)

## Overview

Before Roadrunner Records. Before Morrisound. Before the world had heard of Sepultura. There was *Schizophrenia* — a ferocious 1987 record recorded by four young men from Belo Horizonte, Brazil on a shoestring budget, released on the tiny Cogumelo Records label, and destined to open the Sepultura arc that would eventually conquer global metal.

Igor Cavalera was 17 years old when Sepultura recorded *Schizophrenia*. There were no endorsements, no major studio budgets, no international distribution network. What there was: raw talent, relentless rehearsal, and a hunger to prove that South American metal deserved its place in the conversation. The drum gear was whatever Igor could source locally in Brazil — no Pearl representative was knocking on the door, no Paiste box arrived in the mail. This was pure, unsponsored, pre-endorsement drumming at its most honest.

*Schizophrenia* sits at the hinge point of Sepultura's early evolution. Their debut *Morbid Visions* (1986) was rawer and more death metal in character. *Schizophrenia* sharpened that attack into something with hooks, dynamics, and a thrash precision that anticipated the international breakthrough of *Beneath the Remains* (1989). Songs like "From the Past Comes the Storms," "Inquisition Symphony," and "R.I.P. (Rest in Pain)" revealed a band — and a drummer — developing at extraordinary speed.

The album was co-produced by Jairo T., the band's original second guitarist, reflecting Sepultura's DIY ethos of the period. Recorded at a local São Paulo studio with limited equipment, *Schizophrenia* sounds exactly like what it is: teenagers building something new from the ground up, with nothing but conviction and capability.

- **Heads:** Remo or equivalent budget batter heads
- **Estimated kit value:** $300–500 (1987 Brazilian market)

### The Pre-Endorsement Kit

The drum kit Igor Cavalera used on *Schizophrenia* was never formally documented — no sponsorship paperwork, no press kits, no endorsement announcement. What we know comes from period photos, interviews, and the sonic evidence of the recording itself: a Pearl Export or equivalent budget kit sourced in Brazil during the late 1980s, when professional drum gear was scarce and expensive in South America.

The Pearl Export was the most accessible quality kit available to aspiring drummers in Brazil during this period. Its poplar/basswood shells produced a punchy, mid-forward tone that suited the aggressive playing style Igor was developing. Without the tuning refinements and professional engineering that Scott Burns would later apply at Morrisound, the shells had to do more heavy lifting — and they delivered.

### Unbranded Budget Cymbals

On *Schizophrenia*, Igor played budget, unbranded cymbals — a direct consequence of recording in Brazil in 1987 with no sponsorship deals and limited access to premium cymbal brands. Paiste, Zildjian, and Sabian products were expensive imports; local, unbranded cymbals were the practical choice.

The hi-hats are already purposeful, already driving thrash patterns with commitment. The crashes are placed compositionally — announcing section changes, not filling space. The pre-endorsement cymbal constraint is historically significant: it documents Igor at his most unfiltered, building vocabulary he'd deploy with professional Paiste 2002 tools two years later on [Beneath the Remains](/articles/beneath-the-remains-drum-setup).

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Igor Cavalera play on Schizophrenia?**
A: Igor Cavalera used a Pearl Export kit with unbranded cymbals on Sepultura's Schizophrenia (1987) — recorded in Brazil before any major endorsement deals. The gear was sourced locally in Brazil, where professional drum equipment was expensive and scarce. The Pearl Export's poplar and basswood shells delivered a punchy, mid-forward tone suited to the aggressive thrash and death metal style Sepultura was developing.

**Q: When did Sepultura record Schizophrenia?**
A: Sepultura recorded Schizophrenia in 1987, releasing it on Cogumelo Records in Brazil. This was the band's second full-length album, following Morbid Visions (1986), and preceded their international breakthrough Beneath the Remains (1989) on Roadrunner Records. The album was co-produced by Jairo T., the band's original second guitarist, making it a fully self-directed production.

**Q: How old was Igor Cavalera when Schizophrenia was recorded?**
A: Igor Cavalera was 17 years old when Sepultura recorded Schizophrenia in 1987. Born September 4, 1970, he was among the youngest drummers to record a major extreme metal album of that era. The technical competence already present — double-bass bursts, purposeful fills, authoritative backbeat — makes this one of the most remarkable teenage performances in metal history.

**Q: What label released Schizophrenia?**
A: Schizophrenia was released in 1987 on Cogumelo Records, a Brazilian independent label based in Belo Horizonte. Cogumelo was the incubator for the entire Brazilian extreme metal scene. Sepultura later signed with Roadrunner Records for Beneath the Remains (1989), which gave them international distribution and launched their global career.

**Q: How does Schizophrenia compare to Beneath the Remains in terms of drum performance?**
A: Schizophrenia (1987) captures Igor Cavalera actively developing the technique he would perfect on Beneath the Remains (1989). The core approach — powerful snare, economical fills, double-bass bursts — is already fully formed at 17. What changed between the two albums: sustained double-bass stamina, professional recording (Morrisound, Scott Burns), upgraded cymbals (Paiste 2002), and the physical conditioning of two more years of intense practice and touring. Schizophrenia documents potential; Beneath the Remains documents arrival.
