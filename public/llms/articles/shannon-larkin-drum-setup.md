## Overview

Shannon Larkin (born April 24, 1967, in New London, Connecticut) has been the drummer for Godsmack since 2002, replacing original drummer Tommy Stewart and immediately becoming one of hard rock's most recognizable figures. Where Godsmack's music could have settled into generic post-grunge commercial formula, Larkin's powerhouse drumming elevates every track — his fills are creative, his timing is locked, and his physical presence behind the kit communicates the relentless energy Godsmack concerts are known for.

Before Godsmack, Larkin built a substantial resume: Wrathchild America (later Souls at Zero), Ugly Kid Joe, and various touring positions. He came to Godsmack already a road-tested professional with the technical foundation to handle any configuration the band required.

His drumming on IV (2006), The Oracle (2010), 1000hp (2014), When Legends Rise (2018), and Lighting Up the Sky (2023) documents an evolution without loss of identity — he's added sophistication while retaining the explosive groove that defines his style. His famous drum battles with Sully Erna have been a cornerstone of Godsmack's live show for two decades.

Larkin's setup centers on Ddrum Reflex drums — a line designed for heavy-duty live performance — Sabian AAX and HHX cymbals, and DW 9000 double bass pedals.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Shannon Larkin use?**
A: Shannon Larkin plays Ddrum Reflex drums with Godsmack. The Ddrum Reflex series is designed for live durability and powerful acoustic projection. His configuration includes double 22" bass drums and a five-tom setup spread for the creative, wide-ranging fills he's known for.

**Q: What cymbals does Shannon Larkin play?**
A: Shannon Larkin is a Sabian endorsee using both AAX and HHX series cymbals. His setup blends the bright, cutting character of Sabian AAX for hi-hats and ride with the darker, more sophisticated HHX Evolution crashes for accent work. The HHX X-Treme China provides aggressive stabs during heavy passages.

**Q: What sticks does Shannon Larkin use?**
A: Shannon Larkin plays Vater Shannon Larkin Signature drumsticks — custom-designed to handle both the explosive power passages and the controlled ghost-note work in Godsmack's live show. The signature model reflects his specific balance between grip, weight, and rebound.

**Q: What is the Shannon Larkin vs. Sully Erna drum battle?**
A: Godsmack's live shows famously feature an extended drum duet between Shannon Larkin and vocalist Sully Erna — himself a skilled drummer. The two have alternated back-and-forth drum battles on tour for over 15 years. This segment, often called "The Battle" among fans, showcases both musicians' technique and has become one of the most anticipated moments at Godsmack concerts.

**Q: When did Shannon Larkin join Godsmack?**
A: Shannon Larkin joined Godsmack in 2002, replacing founding drummer Tommy Stewart. His first studio album with the band was Faceless (2003). He has remained the band's drummer through all subsequent releases.
