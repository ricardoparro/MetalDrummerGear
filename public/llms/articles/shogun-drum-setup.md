# Shogun Drum Setup: Nick Augusto's Gear on Trivium's Magnum Opus (2008)

> Discover the exact drum kit, cymbals, and gear Nick Augusto used to record Trivium's progressive thrash masterpiece Shogun (2008). Full breakdown of the DW Performance Series kit, Meinl Byzance cymbals, DW 9000 pedals, and the technical demands behind one of modern metal's most ambitious drum performances.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup

**Genre:** Progressive Thrash Metal

## Overview

Released on September 30, 2008, Trivium's *Shogun* is widely regarded as the band's defining artistic statement — a 70-minute progressive thrash epic that topped metal publication year-end lists globally and cemented Trivium's position among the genre's elite acts. Nick Augusto joined Trivium just before recording began, and *Shogun* stands as his technical peak with the band: the album that demonstrates the full range of his capabilities as a drummer.

*Shogun* features the most technically demanding drum work of Trivium's entire catalog. Odd-time signatures weave through aggressive thrash passages, 180+ BPM blast sections erupt without warning, and extended jazz-influenced sequences unfold across epic tracks like "The Calamity" (9:55) and the 11:39 title track — the longest song Trivium has ever recorded. The album was produced by Jason Suecof at Audiohammer Studio in Sanford, Florida.

The record marks Trivium's first major pairing with Meinl — Nick Augusto's Byzance Traditional series cymbals brought a darker, more complex top-end to the production, distinct from both the band's earlier cymbal choices and the Meinl Classics Custom period that followed.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** DW Performance Series in Natural Lacquer — 22" kick, 10"/12"/14" rack toms, 16"/18" floor toms
- **Snare:** DW Edge 14×6.5 (power snare for Shogun's dense production)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Traditional series
- **Hi-hats:** 14" Meinl Byzance Traditional
- **Crashes:** 16" and 18" Meinl Byzance Medium
- **Ride:** 20" Meinl Byzance Traditional
- **China:** 18" Meinl Byzance
- **Pedals:** DW 9000 Series Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A American Classic
- **Heads:** Evans UV1 Coated (batter), Evans EMAD Clear (kick batter)

### The DW Performance Series: Augusto's Shogun Foundation

Nick Augusto used a DW Performance Series kit in Natural Lacquer for the *Shogun* sessions — the company's professional mid-range offering using maple/poplar hybrid shell construction. The five-tom configuration (10", 12", 14" rack; 16", 18" floor) gave Augusto the full sonic range that *Shogun*'s wide-ranging writing demanded, from tight thrash rack tom fills to deep floor tom work in the album's atmospheric passages.

The Natural Lacquer finish maintains consistent resonance and tuning stability — essential for extended recording sessions capturing technically demanding performances across tracks like the 11:39 title song.

### Meinl Byzance: The Band's First Major Meinl Pairing

The *Shogun* sessions marked Trivium's first major endorsement pairing with Meinl. The Byzance Traditional series' hand-hammered B20 bronze produces a dark, warm, complex cymbal voice that served *Shogun*'s multi-genre character — aggressive thrash passages, death metal blasts, and jazz-influenced progressive sequences all benefit from a cymbal voice that is versatile rather than purely bright and cutting. The 14" Byzance Traditional hi-hats deliver complex, slightly dark top-end that sits alongside Trivium's dense guitar arrangements rather than competing with them.

### DW 9000 Double Pedal

The DW 9000 Series double pedal powered Augusto's *Shogun* double-kick architecture — one of the most technically demanding bass drum performances on any Trivium record. The DW 9000's consistent primary-to-secondary response was critical for the album's 180+ BPM passages and the extended double-kick sequences throughout the 11:39 title track.

## FAQs

**What drums did Nick Augusto use on Shogun?**
DW Performance Series in Natural Lacquer. Single 22" kick with DW 9000 double pedal; rack toms at 10", 12", 14"; floor toms at 16" and 18".

**Is Shogun the hardest Trivium album to play on drums?**
Yes — widely regarded as the most demanding Trivium record for drums. 180+ BPM blast sections, odd-time signatures, jazz-influenced ghost note sequences, and the 9:55 and 11:39 extended tracks require sustained technical precision that no other Trivium album matches.

**What time signature is the Shogun title track?**
Multiple — sections in 4/4 alternate with passages in 7/8, 5/4, and compound meters across the 11:39 running time. The metric complexity is central to the album's progressive thrash identity.

**Did Nick Augusto play all drums on the Shogun album?**
Yes. All drum parts across Shogun's ten tracks were performed by Nick Augusto at Audiohammer Studio with producer Jason Suecof.

**What cymbals did Nick Augusto use on Shogun?**
Meinl Byzance Traditional series — Trivium's first major Meinl endorsement. 14" Byzance Traditional hi-hats, 16"/18" Byzance Medium crashes, 20" Byzance Traditional ride, 18" Byzance China. Distinct from the Meinl Classics Custom period that followed.
