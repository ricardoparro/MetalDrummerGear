# Shogun Drum Setup: Nick Augusto's Progressive Thrash Masterpiece (Trivium, 2008)

> Complete breakdown of Nick Augusto's drum gear on Trivium's Shogun. DW Performance Series kit, Meinl Byzance cymbals, DW 9000 double pedal, and the techniques behind Trivium's magnum opus.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [Nick Augusto](/llms/drummers/nick-augusto.md)
**Band / Album:** Trivium — *Shogun* (2008)
**Genre:** Progressive Thrash Metal / Metalcore

## Overview

Released on September 29, 2008, Trivium's fourth studio album "Shogun" is widely regarded as the band's magnum opus — a 70-minute progressive thrash record that topped metal publication year-end lists globally. The album's scope (including the 11:39 title track and 9:55 "The Calamity") pushed Trivium far beyond metalcore into progressive thrash territory.

Nick Augusto joined Trivium in 2007, just before Shogun entered production, and his work on the album represents the technical peak of his career with the band. Recorded at Morrisound Recording in Tampa, Florida with producer Nick Raskulinecz, the sessions captured Augusto navigating odd time signatures, blast sections above 180 BPM, and extended jazz-influenced passages — all within single compositions.

Shogun marks Trivium's first major Meinl Byzance pairing, distinct from the later Meinl Classics Custom period that defined the "In Waves" era.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Kit:** DW Performance Series in Natural Lacquer — 22" kick, 10"/12"/14" rack toms, 16"/18" floor toms
- **Snare:** DW Edge 14×6.5" (power snare configuration for dense production)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance series
- **Hi-hats:** 14" Meinl Byzance Traditional
- **Crash:** 16" and 18" Meinl Byzance Medium
- **Ride:** 20" Meinl Byzance Traditional Medium
- **China:** 18" Meinl Byzance China (above floor tom)
- **Pedals:** DW 9000 Series Double Bass Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A American Classic
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD2 (bass drum batter), Evans EC2 Coated (tom batter)

### DW Performance Series: Five-Tom Progressive Metal Configuration

Augusto's DW Performance Series kit in Natural Lacquer used a notably expanded configuration for Shogun: three rack toms (10", 12", 14") and two floor toms (16", 18") provided the melodic range required for Shogun's sophisticated arrangements. The 22" bass drum delivered authority across tempos from slow progressive passages to full blast intensity.

DW Performance Series uses North American maple shells — the same shell material as the flagship Collector's Series — delivering fast attack with controlled sustain. The Natural Lacquer finish preserves the maple's natural resonance without adding shell mass.

### DW Edge 14×6.5" Snare

The 6.5" power snare depth suited Shogun's orchestrated production. The steel shell contributes bright crack that cuts through the album's dense, harmonically complex guitar arrangements. Augusto tuned medium-high: bright rimshots for aggressive sections, enough body for dynamic drops in progressive passages. This is a deeper configuration than his later 14×6" Tama S.L.P. work on "In Waves" — the larger body matched Shogun's bigger-sounding production.

### Meinl Byzance: Trivium's First Meinl Chapter

The Byzance series, handcrafted in Turkey from B20 bronze, delivers dark, complex overtones that complement Shogun's lower guitar tunings and multi-layered arrangements. The 14" Traditional hi-hats have a darker voice than the Classics Custom hats Augusto used on later albums — appropriate for Shogun's denser, more orchestrated production context. The 16" and 18" Medium Crashes cover fast accent work through full section-ending wash respectively. The 20" Traditional Medium Ride provides dark bow tone for progressive passages and cutting bell definition for metronomic sections.

### DW 9000 Series Double Bass Pedal

The DW 9000 is a direct-drive flagship pedal — eliminating chain-drive mechanical noise while providing the feel and response needed for sustained high-tempo kick work. For the Shogun title track alone (11:39), Augusto needed a pedal that could sustain extended double-kick patterns through multiple tempo sections and dynamic ranges. The adjustable cam system enabled feel tuning between the album's varied tempos. Dual-chain drive option provided power transfer with minimal mechanical play.

## Key Technical Highlights

- **Shogun title track (11:39):** Longest Trivium song — extended double-kick patterns, multiple time signatures, wide dynamic range across the full runtime
- **"The Calamity" (9:55):** Multiple time signatures (7/8, odd passages), jazz-influenced sections alongside 180+ BPM blast work — Augusto's most technically diverse performance
- **"Kirisute Gomen":** Progressive thrash landmark — multiple tempo shifts, precise double-kick underpinning complex guitar lines, Byzance ride bell work through solo
- **"Into the Mouth of Hell We March":** Album opener at ~185 BPM — sets the technical benchmark immediately with sustained double-kick 16th-note patterns

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Nick Augusto use on Shogun?**
A: DW Performance Series in Natural Lacquer — 22" bass drum, three rack toms (10"/12"/14"), two floor toms (16"/18"). Five-tom configuration for progressive arrangements.

**Q: Is Shogun the hardest Trivium album to play on drums?**
A: Yes — extended compositions, multiple time signatures within single songs, 180+ BPM blast sections, and jazz-influenced passages combine to make it Trivium's most technically demanding record.

**Q: What time signature is the Shogun title track?**
A: Multiple time signatures across 11:39, including 4/4 for direct sections, 7/8 and additional odd-time figures through progressive passages.

**Q: Did Nick Augusto play all drums on Shogun?**
A: Yes — all drums recorded at Morrisound Recording, Tampa, FL, with producer Nick Raskulinecz. Shogun was Augusto's first studio album with Trivium.
