# Dave Lombardo's Drum Setup on Slayer's Show No Mercy (1983)

> Dave Lombardo's debut Slayer recording — a raw Ludwig 5-piece setup at 220+ BPM before endorsement deals changed everything.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [Dave Lombardo](/llms/drummers/dave-lombardo.md)
**Band / Album:** Slayer — *Show No Mercy* (1983)
**Genre:** Thrash Metal / Speed Metal

## Overview

Released on December 3, 1983 on Metal Blade Records, *Show No Mercy* is Slayer's debut album and the first professional studio recording of Dave Lombardo, then 19 years old. The album was recorded at Track Studios in Los Angeles with a budget of approximately $4,000, without a click track, and without a traditional producer — Metal Blade founder Brian Slagel served as executive producer while the band directed their own sessions.

Lombardo played a 5-piece Ludwig drum kit — his pre-endorsement setup before transitioning to Tama — with Zildjian A cymbals. The double bass configuration (two separate 22" bass drums, not a double pedal) was already established. Tempos on the fastest tracks reach 220 to 240 BPM, performed without metronomic reference in a budget studio environment.

*Show No Mercy* predates the Tama endorsement, the Paiste endorsement, and Rick Rubin's production guidance that shaped *Reign in Blood* (1986). It documents Lombardo's raw ability before professional infrastructure arrived — a historically distinct recording that contrasts sharply with the polished extremity of later Slayer albums.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Ludwig 5-piece (Standard / Rocker series), maple shells, Black finish
- **Bass Drums:** Two 22" x 14" bass drums (double kick — separate drums, not double pedal)
- **Toms:** 12" x 8" rack tom, 13" x 9" rack tom, 16" x 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Ludwig Acrolite / Supraphonic, 14" x 5", aluminum/steel shell
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series
  - Hi-Hats: Zildjian A 14"
  - Crash (1): Zildjian A 16"
  - Crash (2): Zildjian A 18"
  - Ride: Zildjian A 20"
  - China: Zildjian A 18"
- **Pedals:** Ludwig Speed King / standard chain-drive (pre-Iron Cobra era)
- **Sticks:** Pro-Mark 2B Wood Tip
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador Coated (snare batter), Remo Ambassador Clear (toms)

## Key Facts

- Recorded at Track Studios, Los Angeles, CA — budget approximately $4,000
- Released December 3, 1983 on Metal Blade Records
- Dave Lombardo was born February 16, 1964 — age 19 at time of recording
- No click track: tempo fluctuations are an integral part of the album's character
- Pre-endorsement era: Ludwig kit and Zildjian A cymbals before Tama and Paiste deals
- Double bass = two separate 22" kick drums (not a double pedal)
- Fastest tracks reach 220–240 BPM
- Produced by Brian Slagel (executive) / Slayer (self-directed sessions)
- Contrast with *Reign in Blood* (1986): Tama Artstar II birch shells, Paiste RUDE/2002 cymbals, Rick Rubin production at Eldorado Studios

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Dave Lombardo use on Show No Mercy?**
A: Dave Lombardo used a 5-piece Ludwig drum kit on *Show No Mercy* (1983) — his pre-endorsement setup with two 22" bass drums (separate double kick), two rack toms (12", 13"), and a 16" floor tom. Ludwig maple shells. He had not yet secured the Tama endorsement he would use on *Reign in Blood* (1986).

**Q: How old was Dave Lombardo when Slayer recorded Show No Mercy?**
A: Dave Lombardo was 19 years old. Born February 16, 1964 in Havana, Cuba, the recording in 1983 was his first professional studio album. His performance at 220–240 BPM without a click track was an extraordinary demonstration of raw natural ability at that age.

**Q: What cymbals did Dave Lombardo use on Show No Mercy?**
A: Zildjian A series cymbals — 14" hi-hats, 16" and 18" crashes, 20" ride, and 18" China. This was his pre-endorsement choice before transitioning to Paiste RUDE and 2002 cymbals for *Reign in Blood* (1986).

**Q: How does the Show No Mercy drum sound compare to Reign in Blood?**
A: *Show No Mercy* (Ludwig maple / Zildjian A / budget studio / no click track) sounds warmer, rawer, and more organically variable in tempo. *Reign in Blood* (Tama birch / Paiste RUDE / Eldorado Studios / Rick Rubin) is punchier, drier, and more precise. The double bass approach was consistent across both — two separate 22" kick drums. The biggest differences are shell material (maple vs. birch), cymbal character (Zildjian bright vs. Paiste aggressive), and production environment.

**Q: Was Show No Mercy recorded with a click track?**
A: No. The album was self-directed in a budget studio without metronomic reference. This produces audible tempo fluctuations — particularly on the fastest tracks — that are part of the album's raw, dangerous character.
