## Overview

Released on June 29, 1999, Slipknot's self-titled debut introduced the world to nine masked musicians from Des Moines, Iowa — and to Joey Jordison, one of the most ferocious drummers in metal history. Joey was 24 years old, playing a Tama Rockstar kit, with no major endorsement deals. What he had was a once-in-a-generation combination of speed, precision, and musical intelligence that would make him a household name in metal drumming within two years.

The debut's drum sound is rawer and more aggressive than anything that followed. Producer Ross Robinson captured the band at Indigo Ranch Studios in Malibu with the urgency of a band that had everything to prove. The album went 5× Platinum in the United States, certified the band as a genuine phenomenon, and established Joey's kit — a mid-range Tama setup, not yet the Pearl Masterworks that would define the Iowa era — as the foundation of one of metal's most recognizable drum sounds.

The debut is the origin document of Joey Jordison's career. Before Pearl, before Paiste, before Iowa — there was this recording, and it was already fully formed.

### The Pre-Endorsement Rig: Tama Rockstar

Joey Jordison recorded Slipknot's 1999 debut on a Tama Rockstar kit — a mid-range poplar shell kit representing his gear before the Pearl endorsement deal that would define the Iowa era. The poplar shells provided a punchier, brighter tone than the maple-heavy setups Joey would later use, contributing to the debut's raw, cutting drum sound.

The double 22" bass drum configuration established from the debut the twin-kick foundation synonymous with Joey's sound. The pre-endorsement context matters: Joey was playing the best kit he had access to, not a custom-spec'd artist setup. The performances speak to Jordison's ability as a player rather than his access to premium gear.

### Zildjian A Custom: The Pre-Paiste Era

Joey's cymbal setup for the 1999 debut was built around Zildjian's A Custom series — a distinct contrast to the Paiste RUDE and 2002 series he would adopt for the Iowa era. The A Custom series, introduced in 1996, delivered bright, cutting tones with fast response. Where Paiste RUDE cymbals prioritize raw aggression and unlathed character, the Zildjian A Customs provided a more articulate, studio-friendly sound that sits clearly in Robinson's dense production.

### Tama Iron Cobra: Built for Speed

The Tama Iron Cobra double pedal was Joey's primary bass drum tool for the debut sessions. The Iron Cobra's cam-driven mechanism and spring-loaded action enabled the sustained double-bass patterns that Slipknot's debut demanded — before the transition to Pearl Demon Drive hardware for the Iowa era.

## FAQ

**What drums did Joey Jordison use on the Slipknot debut?**
Joey Jordison recorded Slipknot's 1999 self-titled debut on a Tama Rockstar kit with poplar shells, in the pre-Pearl endorsement era. The configuration included double 22-inch bass drums and a standard tom spread with rack toms at 10, 12, and 14 inches plus a 16-inch floor tom. This was a mid-range Tama setup rather than a custom artist kit. Joey would transition to Pearl Masterworks for the Iowa sessions in 2001.

**How did the debut kit differ from the Iowa kit?**
The key differences between Joey Jordison's debut (1999) and Iowa (2001) setups were the drum brand, cymbal brand, and pedals. On the debut: Tama Rockstar with poplar shells, Zildjian A Custom cymbals, Tama Iron Cobra double pedal. For Iowa: Pearl Masterworks Custom with maple/mahogany shells, Paiste RUDE and 2002 series cymbals, Pearl Demon Drive pedals. Iowa also featured a more extensive six-tom configuration vs the debut's four-tom spread.

**What was Joey Jordison's first endorsement deal?**
Joey Jordison's first major endorsements came through the commercial success of Slipknot's 1999 debut. The debut was recorded with pre-endorsement gear — Tama Rockstar kit, Zildjian A Custom cymbals, and a Tama Iron Cobra double pedal. After the album went Platinum and Slipknot became a mainstream phenomenon, Joey secured endorsement relationships with Pearl drums, Paiste cymbals, and Promark sticks. By Iowa (2001), he was fully endorsed by Pearl and Paiste.

**What cymbals did Joey Jordison use on the Slipknot self-titled album?**
Joey Jordison used Zildjian A Custom cymbals on Slipknot's 1999 debut — the pre-Paiste era of his career. The A Custom setup included 14-inch hi-hats, crashes in 16 and 18 inches, a 20-inch ride, an 18-inch China, and a 10-inch splash. The transition from Zildjian to Paiste coincided with his broader gear shift from Tama to Pearl hardware for the Iowa era.
