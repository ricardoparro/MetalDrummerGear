# Systematic Chaos Drum Setup — Mike Portnoy's Grammy-Nominated 2007 Dream Theater Kit

> Complete breakdown of Mike Portnoy's drum gear on Dream Theater's Systematic Chaos (2007). Discover the Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit, Sabian HHX Evolution cymbals, DW 9000 pedals, and the Grammy-nominated technique behind 'The Dark Eternal Night'.

## Overview

Released on June 5, 2007, *Systematic Chaos* is Dream Theater's ninth studio album and the heaviest, most prog-ambitious record of Mike Portnoy's final phase with the band. At 77 minutes across eight tracks — including the 16-minute bookend epic "In the Presence of Enemies – Part II" — it received a **Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance** for "The Dark Eternal Night" at the 50th Grammy Awards in February 2008, the band's first Academy recognition.

The album sits at the midpoint between *Train of Thought* (2003) and *Black Clouds & Silver Linings* (2009, Portnoy's final Dream Theater album), synthesizing both heaviness and progressive depth without compromise. "Constant Motion" and "The Dark Eternal Night" are among the most aggressively demanding tracks of his career; "In the Presence of Enemies" (Part I opens, Part II closes) creates a 25-minute narrative arc that bookends an album's worth of contrasting material.

For Mike Portnoy, *Systematic Chaos* marked a decisive gear evolution: Tama Starclassic **Bubinga** shells replaced the Maple platform he had used since the late 1990s; Sabian **HHX Evolution** cymbals replaced the AAX series of the *Train of Thought* era; and DW **9000** double pedals marked a return to DW after the Pearl Eliminator chapter. The kit was again recorded at Avatar Studios (The Power Station), New York, self-produced by Portnoy and John Petrucci.

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga (~13 drums), double 22"x18" bass drums, rack toms 8"/10"/12"/13"/14", floor toms 14"/16"/18"
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga Snare, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX Evolution — 14" hi-hats, 16"/17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride; Sabian HHX 18" Chinese, 10" Splash
- **Pedals:** DW 9000 Double Pedal (roller-bearing cam, smooth action)
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Hi-Hat Stand, Tama Power Tower rack
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Mike Portnoy Signature
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador Coated (snare batter), Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick batter), Remo Ambassador Coated (toms)

### Tama Starclassic Bubinga Kit

The defining gear change on *Systematic Chaos* was Portnoy's shift from Starclassic **Maple** to Starclassic **Bubinga**. Bubinga is a dense African hardwood significantly heavier than maple — it produces sharper attack, tighter decay, and more articulate transients. Where maple provides warmth and sustain (suited to the emotional dynamics of *Scenes from a Memory*), bubinga delivers precision and clarity suited to the album's dual demands: brutal metal passages and intricate prog architecture within the same 77-minute statement.

The configuration maintained the large 13-drum setup: double 22"x18" bass drums, five rack toms (8" through 14"), and three floor toms (14", 16", 18"). Same architectural scale as *Train of Thought*, fundamentally different shell character.

### Sabian HHX Evolution Cymbals

The shift from Sabian AAX to Sabian HHX Evolution is one of the most consequential cymbal changes of Portnoy's career. AAX is machine-hammered with a brilliant finish — bright, fast, aggressive. HHX Evolution is hand-hammered with a raw finish — darker, more complex, richer wash. For an album that needed to serve both "The Dark Eternal Night" and the atmospheric passages of "Repentance," the HHX Evolution cymbals provided the dynamic range and tonal complexity the AAX line could not.

The HHX Evolution 21" ride is particularly notable: its complex bell and hammered bow are central to the prog sections of "In the Presence of Enemies – Part II," providing shimmering sustain across the 16-minute arc.

### DW 9000 Double Pedals

After the Pearl Eliminator (used on *Train of Thought*), Portnoy returned to DW with the 9000 series — widely regarded as one of the smoothest and most responsive double pedals ever manufactured. The roller-bearing cam system and extended footboard provide finer dynamic gradation than the power-focused Eliminator, critical for an album that demanded full-velocity assault on some tracks and measured precision on others.

## Recording: Avatar Studios (The Power Station)

*Systematic Chaos* was recorded at Avatar Studios, New York — the same facility as *Train of Thought* — self-produced by Petrucci and Portnoy. The production philosophy aimed for a drum sound that could support the album's full dynamic range: close-mic'd enough for the metal passages, with moderate room capture (more than the dry *Train of Thought* sessions, less than the ambient *Scenes from a Memory* sessions). The bubinga shells' sharper transients gave the production team more low-mid clarity to work with in the mix.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drums does Mike Portnoy use on Systematic Chaos?**
A: Mike Portnoy recorded Systematic Chaos (2007) on a Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit — a significant upgrade from the Starclassic Maple he played since the Scenes from a Memory era. The configuration included approximately 13 drums: double 22"x18" bass drums, five rack toms (8", 10", 12", 13", 14"), and three floor toms (14", 16", 18"). Bubinga is denser and harder-attacking than maple, producing sharper transients and tighter decay. For the complete gear story, see the [Mike Portnoy drummer page](/drummer/mike-portnoy).

**Q: How does the Systematic Chaos kit differ from Scenes from a Memory?**
A: Key differences: (1) Shell material — Tama Starclassic Bubinga (Systematic Chaos) vs. Starclassic Maple (Scenes from a Memory); bubinga is denser and sharper-attacking, maple is warmer and more sustained. (2) Cymbals — Sabian HHX Evolution (dark, complex, hand-hammered) vs. Sabian Artisan (also dark, but more vintage-voiced). (3) Pedals — DW 9000 (roller-bearing cam, smooth) vs. DW 5000 (chain-drive). (4) Kit size — ~13 drums vs. 8 drums. (5) Playing context — prog-metal versatility vs. narrative concept-album dynamics. See the [Scenes from a Memory drum setup article](/articles/scenes-from-a-memory-drum-setup) for the full comparison.

**Q: What makes In the Presence of Enemies hard to drum?**
A: "In the Presence of Enemies" presents multiple simultaneous challenges: (1) Odd-time fluency at high intensity across Parts I and II; (2) Extended form — nearly 25 minutes total, requiring sustained physical endurance and compositional focus; (3) Dynamic range within the epic, moving from atmospheric restraint to full-velocity prog metal; (4) Structural responsibility as bookend pieces — Part II must feel like a genuine payoff to Part I despite an album's worth of separation. The piece tests technique, endurance, odd-time mastery, and musical storytelling simultaneously.

**Q: What cymbals did Mike Portnoy use on Systematic Chaos?**
A: Portnoy used Sabian HHX Evolution cymbals for Systematic Chaos — a significant shift from the Sabian AAX series of the Train of Thought era. HHX Evolution is hand-hammered with a raw finish, producing a darker, more complex sound than the bright machine-hammered AAX line. His setup included HHX Evolution 14" hi-hats, crashes at 16", 17", 18", and 19", a 21" Evolution Ride, and an 18" HHX Chinese cymbal. The darker character suited the album's blend of prog complexity and metal aggression.

**Q: Was Systematic Chaos nominated for a Grammy?**
A: Yes — Dream Theater received their first Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance at the 50th Grammy Awards (February 2008) for "The Dark Eternal Night" from Systematic Chaos. The track features some of Portnoy's most demanding drumming: relentless double-bass patterns beneath technically complex odd-time riffing. The Grammy nomination is a key driver of search interest in "dream theater systematic chaos drums" and "mike portnoy 2007 kit" queries.
