## Overview

Released in June 2003, "The Link" is Gojira's second full-length album and the record that transformed the Bayonne, France quintet from a regional underground act into a band earning spots on major European festival bills. Coming two years after the raw, self-released "Terra Incognita," The Link shows Mario Duplantier in a crucial transitional phase — moving toward the fully realized sound that would explode on "From Mars to Sirius" (2005) while still working within the constraints of self-financed, DIY production.

The album was recorded at Windferno Studio, Gojira's own primitive recording space in Bayonne. The limitations of that environment forced creative solutions: Mario's drums had to be powerful enough to project through a mix recorded without professional acoustics, with gear chosen for durability and impact rather than studio refinement. The result is a drum sound that is raw, dense, and ferociously energetic — capturing Mario at 22 years old, playing with everything he had.

What makes The Link historically significant in Mario Duplantier's development isn't just the music — it's what it represents. The kit and cymbal choices here (Mapex Black Panther, Paiste 2002) reflect the in-between period: no longer the bare-bones setup of Terra Incognita, not yet the full professional endorsement rig of the From Mars to Sirius era. This is Mario building toward his signature sound.

### Mario's 2003 Transitional Rig: The Mapex Black Panther

For The Link, Mario Duplantier used a Mapex Black Panther kit — a clear step up from the bare-bones setup that defined Terra Incognita, and a practical professional choice before the Tama endorsement deals of the mid-2000s. The Mapex Black Panther was well suited to the early 2000s underground metal scene: professional quality at accessible cost, tonally suited to the dense, heavy sound Gojira was developing.

The Black Panther's birch shells provided the attack and projection essential for cutting through Joe Duplantier's notoriously heavy guitar tone. Birch is naturally bright and punchy — qualities Mario needed when recording in Windferno Studio, where the acoustic environment offered none of the natural room resonance of the converted swimming pool that would define From Mars to Sirius's drum sound.

The dual 22" x 18" bass drum configuration was already locked in — Mario's commitment to two independent kick drums rather than a double pedal was established well before The Link, and it shows. The visual symmetry and the independent resonance of two separate shells were non-negotiable elements of his approach from the earliest days.

The tom configuration followed his characteristic minimalist philosophy: two rack toms and two floor toms, providing the full tonal range his tribal patterns required without excessive complexity.

### Classic Metal Tones: Paiste 2002

Mario's cymbal choice for The Link was the Paiste 2002 series — one of the most storied lines in metal cymbal history. The 2002s are known for their bright, cutting, and powerful character: more aggressive and focused than the darker Zildjian K Custom setup Mario would adopt for [From Mars to Sirius](/llms/articles/from-mars-to-sirius-drum-setup.md). At this stage of Gojira's development, the 2002's direct and powerful voice was well matched to the album's raw, urgent energy.

The 14" 2002 hi-hats are bright and crisp, providing clear articulation through The Link's dense arrangements. The crash selection — 16" and 18" — reflects both budget realities and musical needs: fast, sharp accents and a primary crash for heavy section punctuation. The 2002 ride provided defined bell presence for rhythmic phrasing during sustained passages.

### The Gear Transition: From The Link to From Mars to Sirius

The shift to Tama drums and Zildjian K Custom cymbals for [From Mars to Sirius](/llms/articles/from-mars-to-sirius-drum-setup.md) (2005) reflects two years of touring, growing budgets, and professional endorsement opportunities. Comparing The Link's drum sound to [The Way of All Flesh](/llms/articles/the-way-of-all-flesh-drum-setup.md) (2008) reveals the full extent of Mario's development — from raw, direct energy to a highly nuanced dynamic range with sophisticated snare technique and ghost note embellishments that require both developed skill and a production budget to capture properly.

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Mario Duplantier use on The Link?**
A: On Gojira's The Link (2003), Mario Duplantier used a Mapex Black Panther drum kit with dual 22x18 inch bass drums, birch shells, and a minimalist tom configuration of two rack toms and two floor toms. This was a transitional kit between his early Terra Incognita setup and the Tama Starclassic gear he adopted for From Mars to Sirius.

**Q: How did Mario's setup differ between The Link and From Mars to Sirius?**
A: Between The Link (2003) and From Mars to Sirius (2005), Mario switched from a Mapex Black Panther kit with Paiste 2002 cymbals to a Tama Starclassic Performer with Zildjian K Custom cymbals. The recording environment also changed from Windferno Studio's DIY setup to the band's converted swimming pool studio in Bayonne, which gave From Mars to Sirius its legendary room ambience.

**Q: What cymbals did Mario Duplantier use on The Link?**
A: On The Link (2003), Mario Duplantier used Paiste 2002 cymbals — a bright, powerful, and cutting series well suited to the album's raw, self-produced character. His setup included 14" hi-hats, 16" and 18" crashes, a 20" ride, and an 18" China. He later transitioned to Zildjian K Custom for From Mars to Sirius and eventually to Meinl Byzance for his current setup.
