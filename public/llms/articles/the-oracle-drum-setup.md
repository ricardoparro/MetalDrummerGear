# The Oracle Drum Setup: Shannon Larkin's Gear on Godsmack's #1 Billboard 200 Album (2010)

> Complete gear breakdown for Godsmack's The Oracle (2010). Discover the Pearl Reference kit, Zildjian A Custom + Paiste Signature cymbal blend, Pearl Eliminator pedals, and the hard rock drumming that powered 'Love-Hate-Sex-Pain' — a then-record 35 consecutive weeks in the Top 25 mainstream rock airplay.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup

**Genre:** Hard Rock / Alternative Metal

## Overview

Released on April 20, 2010, *The Oracle* debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 — a commercial peak for Godsmack built on the driving groove of 'Love-Hate-Sex-Pain,' which spent 35 consecutive weeks in the Top 25 of mainstream rock airplay (a then-record). At the center of that achievement was Shannon Larkin, by 2010 eight years into his Godsmack tenure and at full command of his role as one of American hard rock's most authoritative drummers.

Larkin's gear had evolved significantly since *Faceless* (2003). Where his 2003 setup centered on a Tama Starclassic Maple kit with pure Sabian AAX cymbals, the *Oracle* era brought Pearl Reference/Masters Premium MCX drums, a blended Zildjian A Custom and Paiste Signature cymbal configuration, Pearl Eliminator double bass pedals, and Vic Firth custom sticks. This more refined, tonally varied setup matched a playing approach deepened by nearly a decade of headline touring with Godsmack.

The album was produced by David Fortman at Studio 4 Recording in Conshohocken, PA — a production environment that captured the Pearl Reference shells' focused, defined attack in a clean, punchy hard rock context. The drum sound on *The Oracle* is more controlled and precise than earlier Godsmack records, reflecting both the gear evolution and Larkin's accumulated professional refinement.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference / Masters Premium MCX (maple/birch hybrid shells)
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel Snare, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom (hi-hats, crashes) + Paiste Signature (ride, China)
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator Double Bass Pedal (interchangeable cam system); Pearl H-2050 Heavy Hi-Hat Stand
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated (batter), Remo Powerstroke 3 (bass drum batter), Remo Ambassador Snare Side (resonant)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Custom (5B profile)

### Pearl Reference: The Maple/Birch Hybrid Behind Godsmack's #1 Album

Shannon Larkin's Pearl Reference/Masters Premium MCX kit brought a new tonal character to Godsmack's drum sound on *The Oracle*. The Reference series' maple/birch hybrid shell construction combines the warmth and resonance of maple with the attack and projection of birch — a sound profile well suited to both studio recording and hard rock arena performance.

The double 22" bass drum configuration remained constant from Larkin's earlier Godsmack work. The Pearl Reference shells' focused, defined attack gave the low-end foundation of *The Oracle* its characteristic punch — clean enough to articulate double-kick patterns, powerful enough to anchor the heavy riff sections that define Godsmack's live show.

Pearl's free-floating shell design added another dimension of tonal control. By allowing the shell to vibrate freely without hardware contact, the Reference series delivered a natural, open resonance that recording engineers could capture with minimal processing — giving David Fortman's production a drum sound that felt authentic and physical in the hard rock context.

### Pearl Free-Floating Steel Snare: The Crack Behind The Oracle

Larkin's snare voice on *The Oracle* continues the steel-shell philosophy of his earlier Godsmack work. The Pearl Free-Floating steel snare's high-frequency attack penetrated the album's wall-of-guitar production with absolute clarity — heard most directly on 'Love-Hate-Sex-Pain' and 'Cryin' Like a Bitch!!'

Pearl's free-floating strainer system suspended the snare wires independently from the shell, eliminating tension interference and producing a clean, natural resonance that required minimal processing in the mix. The 14" x 6.5" dimensions provided the body and sustain that separates a powerful snare sound from a thin one — each backbeat arriving with three-dimensional authority.

### Zildjian A Custom + Paiste Signature: The Cymbal Evolution

The blended cymbal configuration on *The Oracle* represents meaningful evolution from Larkin's earlier pure-Sabian AAX approach. Zildjian A Custom hi-hats (14") and crashes (17", 19") delivered machine-lathed brightness and fast stick response for attack-focused applications. The Paiste Signature ride (20" Full Ride) and China (18") provided fuller, more complex voicing for sustained tone and accent work.

This dual-brand approach gave Larkin tonal variety unavailable from a single-series setup: Zildjian A Custom brightness cutting through the guitar production for hi-hats and crashes, Paiste Signature warmth providing musical depth in the ride passages. The contrast between A Custom's brilliant-finish articulation and Paiste Signature's darker, more complex character is audible in the album's more dynamically varied tracks.

### Pearl Eliminator: Customized Double-Kick Power

The Pearl Eliminator's defining feature — its interchangeable cam system — allowed Larkin to select from four cam profiles to dial in precisely the power curve and rebound speed his playing required. For Godsmack's groove-first approach, the mechanical consistency and adjustability of the Eliminator suited the deliberate, authoritative kick placement central to tracks like 'Love-Hate-Sex-Pain' and 'Oracle.'

The Eliminator's dual-chain drive delivered the power of chain mechanism with the responsiveness of direct-drive feel — a hybrid matching Larkin's playing style at this stage of his career.
