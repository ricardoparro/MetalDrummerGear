# The Parallax II: Future Sequence Drum Setup: Blake Richardson's Progressive Metal Peak

> Blake Richardson used a refined DW Collector's Series kit, Meinl Byzance Dark and Extra Dry cymbals, and a DW 9002 double bass pedal to record Between the Buried and Me's The Parallax II: Future Sequence (2012) — the 74-minute science fiction concept album that earned Grammy consideration, Prog Magazine's Album of the Decade, and stands as Richardson's most compositionally developed recorded performance.

## Overview

Released on October 9, 2012 through Metal Blade Records, *The Parallax II: Future Sequence* is Between the Buried and Me's most ambitious record — a 74-minute science fiction concept album following two parallel narrative threads across nine extended tracks. *Prog Magazine* named it Album of the Decade. It earned Grammy consideration and reached new commercial territory for a band that had never compromised for accessibility. For Blake Richardson, it is the album that showcases his drumming at its peak compositional development.

Every element of Richardson's technique appears across *The Parallax II*: metric modulations that shift tempo perception across genre boundaries, jazz-influenced ghost note architecture that builds complex rhythmic textures beneath the guitars, compositional double-bass deployment that varies kick density across each track's emotional arc, and anticipatory fill work that prepares listeners for the album's constant genre transitions before they arrive. Songs like "Telos" and "Lay Your Ghosts to Rest" are among the most-studied drum performances in progressive metal.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series (refined configuration after seven years of BTBAM touring), double 22" bass drums, 10"/12"/13" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series Maple 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Dark + Extra Dry — 14" Dark Hi-Hats, 17" Extra Dry Thin Crash, 18" Dark Crash, 20" Extra Dry Ride, 10" Dark Splash, 18" Byzance China
- **Pedals:** DW 9002 Double Bass Pedal (chain-drive, adjustable cam)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Coated (snare batter), Remo Emperor Coated / Ambassador Coated (toms), Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick)

### DW Collector's Series (Refined): Seven Years of BTBAM Evolution

By the time of *The Parallax II*, Richardson's DW Collector's Series kit had been refined through seven years of BTBAM touring and recording — including *Alaska*, *Colors*, and *The Great Misdirect*. The kit configuration remained consistent, but Richardson's relationship with his gear had deepened into the intimate familiarity that allows complete attention on the music rather than the instrument.

The DW Collector's maple shells continued to provide the warm fundamental that anchors *The Parallax II*'s massive dynamic range. The album's concept — a science fiction narrative spanning multiple realities — requires musical vocabulary that can move from intimate, quiet passages to full orchestral-scale intensity within minutes. The three rack toms (10", 12", 13") proved essential for the fill architecture that connects the album's narrative sections: Richardson's fills throughout "Telos" and "Lay Your Ghosts to Rest" are compositional statements that mark structural transitions and prepare listeners for harmonic shifts.

### Meinl Byzance Dark and Extra Dry: Expanded Tonal Vocabulary

The expansion from the single-series Extra Dry setup of *Alaska* to the two-series Dark and Extra Dry configuration of *The Parallax II* reflects the album's greater dynamic and emotional range. The Dark sub-series added warmer, more complex voices for restrained sections; the Extra Dry series continued to provide controlled articulation for precise rhythmic work.

The 14" Byzance Dark Hi-Hats anchor the setup with a darker, more complex voice than the Extra Dry hats of the *Alaska* era. *The Parallax II*'s hi-hat work is more varied and harmonically complex — the album's concept-driven architecture requires the hi-hat to serve as everything from a precise rhythmic layer in fast technical passages to a soft shimmer in the album's most restrained moments.

Both crash cymbals serve different accent functions: the 17" Extra Dry Thin for fast precision during technical passages; the 18" Dark for fuller explosions at emotional peaks. The Extra Dry 20" Ride maintains clean articulation across *The Parallax II*'s most complex passages — "Telos" features multiple meter changes and metric modulations where a washy ride would blur the rhythmic detail.

### Producer Jamie King at Basement Studios

By 2012, Jamie King had recorded five BTBAM albums with Richardson — developing an intimate production knowledge that allowed him to capture Richardson's nuanced performances with complete understanding of the sonic space the kit occupies in BTBAM's music. The drum sound on *The Parallax II* is warm and present without dominating the dense orchestration.

## Key Track Analysis

- **Goodbye to Everything Reprise / Astral Body** — Album opener; establishes the concept's tone immediately. Richardson's dynamic restraint in the opening section demonstrates the compositional intelligence that defines the album.
- **Telos** — The most-studied drum performance on the record. Multiple metric modulations, varying kick density following the narrative arc, fills that mark structural transitions. Richardson's compositional approach at its most developed.
- **Lay Your Ghosts to Rest** — Compositional double-bass deployment at its most refined. Richardson varies kick density measure by measure to follow the track's emotional temperature. The fill architecture marks the song's sections as clearly as any melodic instrument.
- **Bloom** — *The Parallax II*'s most restrained track; Richardson's jazz-influenced ghost note work is most audible here. The Dark Hi-Hats provide the complex shimmer that the track's quieter passages demand.
- **Parallax** — Closing track; the full BTBAM progressive metal vocabulary in one extended performance. Every gear choice — the three-tom fill architecture, the Extra Dry Ride for clarity, the Dark crash for peak accents — demonstrates why *The Parallax II* earned its critical standing.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Blake Richardson use on The Parallax II: Future Sequence?**
Blake Richardson recorded *The Parallax II: Future Sequence* (2012) using a refined DW Collector's Series kit — the same core configuration (double 22" bass drums, 10"/12"/13" rack toms, 16" floor tom) evolved through seven years of BTBAM touring. He used Meinl Byzance Dark and Extra Dry cymbals and a DW 9002 double bass pedal. Recorded at Basement Studios, Winston-Salem, NC with producer Jamie King. Profile: [Blake Richardson at MetalForge](/drummer/blake-richardson).

**Q: What makes Telos the most studied track on The Parallax II?**
"Telos" features multiple metric modulations across its arrangement, varying kick density from dense double-bass passages to restrained single-foot patterns, and fill architecture that marks narrative transitions in the album's science fiction concept. Richardson's ability to execute complex rhythmic elements while serving the track's emotional arc — rather than displaying technique for its own sake — makes it the definitive study in his approach. **Q: Why did The Parallax II earn Grammy consideration?**
*The Parallax II: Future Sequence* earned Grammy consideration as a progressive metal record of genuine compositional ambition — 74 minutes of concept-driven music demonstrating BTBAM's place in serious progressive rock tradition while maintaining extreme metal credibility. *Prog Magazine*'s Album of the Decade designation reflected the critical consensus. Richardson's drumming across nine extended tracks demonstrates musical intelligence rare in any genre: metric modulation, jazz-influenced ghost note work, and compositional double-bass deployment in service of a science fiction narrative concept.

**Q: How do the Dark and Extra Dry Meinl Byzance series differ on The Parallax II?**
The Byzance Dark series features warmer, more complex tones with more overtone richness and slightly longer decay — suited to jazz-influenced and restrained passages where cymbal complexity adds harmonic texture. The Extra Dry series delivers drier, focused sounds with faster decay and less wash — suited to complex rhythmic passages where clarity of articulation is essential. Richardson used Dark Hi-Hats, Dark Crash, and Dark Splash for warmer musical contexts; Extra Dry Crash and Extra Dry Ride for more precise rhythmic demands.

**Q: How does The Parallax II connect to Alaska and Coma Ecliptic?**
*The Parallax II* (2012) is the peak of the arc that begins with [Alaska (2005)](/articles/alaska-drum-setup) — the breakthrough statement — and continues to [Coma Ecliptic (2015)](/articles/coma-ecliptic-drum-setup), where Richardson's mature restraint serves a more accessible narrative format. The Parallax II is the album where every element of Richardson's technique reaches its maximum compositional deployment, making it the essential center of the BTBAM progressive metal canon.
