## Overview

Released on October 30, 2009, on Nuclear Blast Records, Nile's sixth studio album "Those Whom the Gods Detest" is the band's best-charting and most commercially successful record. It entered the German charts at No. 54, the Swiss charts, and the US Billboard Heatseekers chart — remarkable reach for uncompromising technical death metal. Allmusic awarded it 4 out of 5 stars; Metal Music Archives rates it 9/10.

For George Kollias, "Those Whom the Gods Detest" marks a pivotal gear transition: by this album, he had moved from his earlier Tama/DW configuration to a Pearl Reference Maple kit paired with Pearl Demon Drive pedals — the platform that would define his career-peak performances. The shift to Pearl was not simply a sponsorship move; the Reference Maple's construction and the Demon Drive's engineering suited the specific demands of Kollias's heel-toe technique at 240–280 BPM in ways his prior gear could not fully match.

Tracks like "Those Whom the Gods Detest," "Kafir!," "The Eye of Ra," and "Permitting the Noble Dead to Descend to the Underworld" showcase the full range of what Kollias can do — from the opening barrage of gravity blasts to measured, melodically aware passages where the kit's tonal character comes forward. The Meinl Byzance cymbal setup, now well into its transition-era configuration, provided the articulation and musical complexity the album's Egyptian-themed compositions demanded.

This article examines every piece of gear George Kollias used on "Those Whom the Gods Detest," the techniques that made the album's drum performances exceptional, and why this record anchors the Nile discography for a new generation of extreme metal drummers.

### George's 2009 Kit: Pearl Reference Maple

By the "Those Whom the Gods Detest" sessions in 2009, George Kollias had transitioned to the Pearl Reference Maple — a significant upgrade from his earlier Tama Starclassic configuration. The Pearl Reference Maple uses Pearl's SST (Superior Shell Technology) construction: a multi-ply maple shell with alternating grain angles that maximizes resonance efficiency while maintaining the focused attack required for death metal at extreme tempos.

The Reference Maple's character sits between the warmth of a pure-maple kit and the projection of harder-shell alternatives. For Kollias's playing — where the kit must deliver both controlled, clear blast beat articulation and musically resonant tom passages — the Reference Maple's tonal balance is near-ideal. The shell responds quickly enough to articulate individual strokes at 240+ BPM while retaining enough body to sound authoritative in Nile's dense guitar environment.

The double 22" kick drum setup remained non-negotiable. Two independent bass drums give Kollias the per-foot control and independent resonance that no double pedal on a single drum can replicate. At 270+ BPM sustained kick runs — which appear on "Kafir!" and "Those Whom the Gods Detest" — the physical independence of two drums prevents the resonance bleed that can collapse rhythmic definition on a single-bass-drum double-pedal configuration.

Pearl's OptiMount tom suspension system allowed the rack toms to resonate freely, preserving the sustain and pitch definition that Nile's Egyptian-tinged melodic passages demand. The compact configuration — 10", 12", 14" rack toms plus 16" floor tom — keeps travel distances manageable at maximum playing speed while providing enough range for the kit's expressive demands.

### The Snare: Pearl Free-Floating Shell System

George Kollias used a Pearl Free-Floating 14" x 6.5" snare during the "Those Whom the Gods Detest" era. The Free-Floating system is Pearl's patented design where the shell is suspended independently from the throw-off and lug hardware — the shell resonates without interference from mechanical dampening that conventional snare construction introduces.

The practical effect for Kollias is a snare that responds faster and more evenly across the entire head. At 240+ BPM blast beats, where each stroke must produce a consistent, immediate crack, a Free-Floating shell eliminates the inconsistency that hardware-contact damping introduces into conventional snares. Every stroke sounds the same — fast, clear, and defined.

The 14" x 6.5" dimensions produce the combination of crack and body that Kollias's blast beat approach requires: deep enough to cut through Nile's guitar density with authority, compact enough to decay quickly between strokes. Nile producer (and guitar deity) Karl Sanders's approach to drum placement in the mix rewards exactly this character — a snare that speaks instantly and moves on without smearing the rhythmic space it occupies.

### The Meinl Byzance Arsenal: Transition Era

By 2009, George Kollias's relationship with Meinl Cymbals was fully established — the Byzance series had been his consistent platform through the Nile years, and "Those Whom the Gods Detest" represents a mature, well-calibrated Byzance configuration at the peak of his Meinl era.

The Byzance series is cast from B20 bronze — 80% copper, 20% tin — and hammered and lathed to the Meinl house spec for complex, musical tone. The Byzance character sits between the bright, fast-decay Paiste aesthetic and the darker, drier Istanbul/K Custom sound: it provides articulation at extreme speeds while retaining enough tonal complexity to serve Nile's melodically sophisticated passages.

The hi-hat selection — 14" Byzance Medium weights — gives Kollias precise, defined chick sounds for blast beat riding without becoming sloppy or washy at maximum tempo. A hi-hat that smears at 240+ BPM collapses the rhythmic definition Nile's music requires; the Byzance medium weights maintain articulation across Kollias's full tempo range.

The crash spread (17", 18", 19") provides dynamic contrast across Nile's compositional landscape. "Those Whom the Gods Detest" and "Kafir!" require crashes that accent with authority during climactic moments — the 19" Byzance Medium Heavy delivers that authority. More delicate accent work in transitional passages reaches for the 17". The 18" covers middle ground throughout.

The 18" Byzance China punctuates Nile's most aggressive transitions with its characteristic trashy, controlled decay — an essential texture in the band's sonic vocabulary.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drum kit does George Kollias use on Those Whom the Gods Detest?**

A: On Nile's 'Those Whom the Gods Detest' (2009, Nuclear Blast), George Kollias played a Pearl Reference Maple kit — a significant shift from the Tama Starclassic Maple he used on 'Annihilation of the Wicked' (2005). The Reference Maple configuration featured a double 22" kick drum setup, 10/12/14" rack toms, and a 16" floor tom. The kit is paired with a Pearl Free-Floating 14" x 6.5" snare. For full kit history and current setup, see: [George Kollias complete gear breakdown](/articles/whats-in-george-kollias-kit). For the earlier Nile album comparison: [Annihilation of the Wicked drum setup](/articles/george-kollias-nile-annihilation-drum-setup).

**Q: How fast is George Kollias on Those Whom the Gods Detest?**

A: George Kollias sustains blast beats at 240–270 BPM on 'Those Whom the Gods Detest,' with 'Kafir!' representing one of the album's most demanding sustained-tempo passages at approximately 260–270 BPM. Kollias is Guinness World Record-certified for sustaining 280 BPM single strokes — by the 2009 album his technique and equipment (Pearl Demon Drive direct-drive pedals) had matured to deliver more consistent, even-dynamics kick performance at these extremes than his earlier DW 9002 era. What distinguishes his speed is not peak BPM but sustained clarity across entire tracks — see [George Kollias at MetalForge](/drummer/george-kollias) for full technique breakdown.

**Q: What cymbals does George Kollias use on Those Whom the Gods Detest?**

A: George Kollias plays Meinl Byzance series cymbals on 'Those Whom the Gods Detest' (2009). The setup includes 14" Byzance Medium hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" Byzance crashes, a 20" Byzance Medium ride, and an 18" Byzance China. The Byzance series — cast from B20 bronze and handcrafted — provides the articulation at 240+ BPM that Nile's rhythmically complex compositions require while retaining enough tonal complexity for the album's melodically aware passages. Kollias has been a Meinl endorser through his entire Nile tenure; the Byzance series appears on all his major Nile recordings.

**Q: What pedals does George Kollias use on Those Whom the Gods Detest?**

A: By the 'Those Whom the Gods Detest' (2009) sessions, George Kollias was performing on Pearl Demon Drive double bass drum pedals — transitioning from the DW 9002 he used on 'Annihilation of the Wicked' (2005). The Demon Drive's direct-drive mechanism eliminates the mechanical slack of chain-drive systems, providing instant foot-to-beater response at 260–270 BPM. This engineering directly informed Kollias's later Pearl Demon XR co-design collaboration. The Demon Drive era represents the platform on which his technique fully matured.

**Q: Why is Those Whom the Gods Detest significant for death metal drumming?**

For drumming specifically, it captures George Kollias four years into his Nile tenure with mature technique, optimized Pearl Reference Maple gear, and Demon Drive pedal precision. The album demonstrates that extreme-speed drumming can coexist with dynamic awareness, polyrhythmic complexity, and compositional intelligence — not merely raw BPM achievement. Allmusic: 4/5; Metal Music Archives: 9/10. It anchors the Nile discography between the debut 'Annihilation of the Wicked' (2005) and later records, making it the natural starting point for studying Kollias's middle-career peak.
