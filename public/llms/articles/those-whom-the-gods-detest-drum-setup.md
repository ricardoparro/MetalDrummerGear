# George Kollias Drum Setup: Nile's Those Whom the Gods Detest (2009) Gear Breakdown

> Discover the Pearl Reference Maple kit, Meinl Byzance cymbals, and Pearl Demon Drive pedals George Kollias used to record Nile's best-selling album 'Those Whom the Gods Detest' (2009) — the record that consolidated his status as the world's fastest death metal drummer.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [George Kollias](/llms/drummers/george-kollias.md)
**Band / Album:** Nile — *Those Whom the Gods Detest* (2009)
**Genre:** Technical Death Metal / Brutal Death Metal

## Overview

Released on November 6, 2009, on Nuclear Blast Records, Nile's sixth studio album "Those Whom the Gods Detest" arrived four years after "Annihilation of the Wicked" (2005) had introduced George Kollias to the world as the most technically formidable drummer in extreme metal. Where Annihilation was the announcement, Those Whom the Gods Detest was the consolidation — a record that confirmed everything Kollias had promised, delivered with greater compositional depth and a new gear foundation.

The album became Nile's best-charting and best-selling record worldwide, earning critical scores that placed it among the finest death metal albums of its era: Allmusic 4/5, Metal Music Archives 9/10. Tracks like "Those Whom the Gods Detest," "Kafir!," "The Eye of Ra," and "Permitting the Noble Dead to Descend to the Underworld" became immediate reference points for extreme metal drumming — studied by players worldwide as benchmarks for blast beat endurance, technical precision, and the integration of Egyptian compositional motifs into brutal death metal structures.

Crucially, the album marked a significant gear transition for Kollias. He had recorded Annihilation of the Wicked on a Tama Starclassic Maple kit with DW 9002 double pedals. By 2009, he had moved to a Pearl Reference Maple setup and was performing on Pearl Demon Drive pedals — the beginning of the endorsement era that would culminate in the co-designed Pearl Demon XR. Produced once again by Neil Kernon at Sonic Ranch in Tornillo, Texas, the sessions captured Kollias at a gear and technique peak that remains the benchmark for extreme metal drumming.

### George's TWTGD Kit: Pearl Reference Maple

By 2009, George Kollias had transitioned from his Tama Starclassic Maple setup to the Pearl Reference Maple — Pearl's flagship professional line engineered for maximum acoustic performance and long-term durability under extreme playing demands. The Reference Maple's 6-ply maple shell construction delivered the warmth, projection, and attack that Kollias required for Nile's compositionally dense death metal.

Two 22" bass drums remained non-negotiable. Kollias's heel-toe double bass technique requires independent resonance from two separate kick shells. The Pearl 22" kicked with authority — enough low-frequency mass to be felt physically on the record while remaining tight enough to define individual notes at extreme tempos. On "Those Whom the Gods Detest" and "Kafir!," the kick drum definition is audible even in the densest blast sections.

Pearl's ADP (Articulation Defined Performance) tom mounting system allowed the toms to vibrate freely without shell contact, preserving natural sustain. On Those Whom the Gods Detest, where Nile's compositional architecture creates genuine melodic movement between blast sections, the toms needed to sing — and the Reference Maple delivered.

### The Snare That Drives the Blast

For Those Whom the Gods Detest, George Kollias used a Pearl Free-Floating snare — a drum whose engineering philosophy directly suited his demands at the session. Pearl's Free-Floating design mechanically decouples the shell from the mounting lugs entirely, allowing the shell to resonate as a pure acoustic body without interference from lug contact points.

At the extreme dynamics Kollias generates — full-force blast beat strokes at 240+ BPM sustained across complete takes — lug contact creates microphonic inconsistencies that accumulate into audible tonal variation between individual strokes. The Free-Floating design eliminates this. Every snare stroke on "Kafir!" and "The Eye of Ra" registers with the same fundamental character: a bright, cutting crack with fast decay and no tonal variation caused by mechanical interference.

### Meinl Byzance: The Full Endorsement Era

By 2009, George Kollias's Meinl Byzance endorsement was fully established. The Byzance series is cast from B20 bronze (80% copper, 20% tin) — the same alloy used in the finest Turkish cymbal-making traditions. What distinguishes Byzance from cheaper alloys is harmonic complexity: B20 produces cymbals with rich, layered overtones rather than the thin, one-dimensional crash of lesser materials.

The 14" Byzance Medium Hi-Hats were central to Kollias's blast beat hand vocabulary. At the extreme speeds of "Those Whom the Gods Detest" and "Kafir!," the hi-hat must close cleanly on each stroke, open and close in measured patterns, and remain articulate when the foot is working independent patterns below. The three-crash setup (17", 18", 19") provided dynamic gradation across the album's compositionally varied material — the 17" for quick accents, the 18" for structural transitions, the 19" for the album's most massive arrivals.

### Pearl Demon Drive: Engineering for 280 BPM

The Pearl Demon Drive represented a significant leap in Kollias's pedal setup compared to the DW 9002 he used on Annihilation of the Wicked (2005). Pearl's direct-drive system eliminated the beater lag inherent in chain-drive designs — at 280 BPM, the mechanical advantage of a direct-drive linkage translates into more precise beater positioning and faster stroke recovery.

Those Whom the Gods Detest represented Kollias's studio debut with Pearl pedals, and the transition is audible. The kick drum on TWTGD has a sharper, faster attack character than the Annihilation recordings — a direct consequence of the direct-drive mechanism's reduced beater lag. On "Kafir!" and "Permitting the Noble Dead," the kick drum attacks are more percussive and defined, sitting higher in the mix.
