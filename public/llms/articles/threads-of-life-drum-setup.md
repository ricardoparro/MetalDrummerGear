# Jason Bittner's Drum Setup on Shadows Fall's Threads of Life (2007)

> Complete breakdown of the drum kit, snare, cymbals, and hardware Jason Bittner used to record Shadows Fall's Atlantic Records debut Threads of Life (2007). Tama Starclassic Performer B/B, Sabian HHX cymbals, DW 9002 double bass pedal, and the Grammy-nominated "Redemption" drum performance.

## Overview

Released on April 3, 2007 through Atlantic Records, *Threads of Life* is Shadows Fall's major-label debut — the album that followed the Century Media run of *The Art of Balance* (2002) and *The War Within* (2004) with the band's first release on a major. It debuted at #46 on the Billboard 200, and its lead single "Redemption" earned the band a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance at the 50th Grammy Awards in February 2008 — their second Grammy nod in four years, following *The War Within*'s "What Drives the Weak."

Shadows Fall recorded *Threads of Life* with producer Nick Raskulinecz — known for his work with Foo Fighters, Rush, and Velvet Revolver — at Studio 606, Dave Grohl's studio complex in Northridge, California. It was the only Shadows Fall album Raskulinecz produced, and his approach brought a heavier, more modern low end to the band's sound than the productions that preceded it.

For Bittner, *Threads of Life* represents a technical maturation of the drumming vocabulary he established on *The Art of Balance* — polyrhythmic double-bass, dynamic control, precision under pressure — now deployed on the band's first major-label record.

### Threads of Life Kit: Tama Starclassic Performer B/B

Jason Bittner's drum kit on *Threads of Life* remained the Tama Starclassic Performer B/B — the birch/bubinga hybrid shell kit that had carried him through *The Art of Balance* and *The War Within*. Nick Raskulinecz's production favors a heavier, more modern low end than the band's earlier records, a shift that suited the Starclassic Performer's bubinga-driven warmth.

The double 22" x 18" bass drum configuration anchors the dynamic build that "Redemption" is built around — the track escalates from a restrained verse into a driving, double-kick-powered chorus. The four-tom configuration (10", 12", 14", 16") supports the fill vocabulary across the album's more dynamically varied songwriting.

### Snare: Steel Precision for the Grammy-Nominated Performance

Bittner's 14" x 6.5" steel snare remained his choice throughout *Threads of Life*. On "Redemption," the snare's role is central to the track's dynamic build: it starts restrained in the verse, then drives forward with increasing intensity as the track escalates toward its Grammy-nominated chorus.

### Sabian HHX: Definition for the Major-Label Debut

Bittner's Sabian HHX and HH cymbal setup continued the configuration that had served him across the entire Bittner-era Shadows Fall catalog. On "Redemption," the cymbal arrangement tracks the song's dynamic build directly: restrained hi-hat work in the verse gives way to fuller crash and ride engagement as the track escalates, with the 18" HHX crash marking the chorus's arrival.

### DW 9002: Driving the Grammy-Nominated Build

The DW 9002 double bass pedal remained Bittner's hardware foundation, and its role on "Redemption" is central to the track's Grammy-nominated impact. The song builds from a restrained verse into a driving, double-kick-powered chorus, and that escalation depends on the pedal delivering consistent, matched beater action across both feet as the intensity rises.

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Jason Bittner use on Threads of Life?**
A: On *Threads of Life* (2007), Jason Bittner played the same Tama Starclassic Performer B/B kit he had used since *The Art of Balance* (2002) — birch/bubinga hybrid shells in a double bass drum configuration (two 22" x 18" kicks), with 10" and 12" rack toms and 14" and 16" floor toms. See [Jason Bittner at MetalForge](/drummer/jason-bittner) for full career gear context.

**Q: What cymbals did Jason Bittner use on Threads of Life?**
A: Jason Bittner played Sabian HHX and HH series cymbals — HHX 14" hi-hats, HHX 16" and 18" crash cymbals, an HH 20" ride, and an HHX china. The same configuration he used throughout his Shadows Fall tenure.

**Q: Who produced Threads of Life and how did it chart?**
A: *Threads of Life* was produced by Nick Raskulinecz — known for his work with Foo Fighters, Rush, and Velvet Revolver — at Studio 606, Dave Grohl's studio complex in Northridge, California. It was Shadows Fall's major-label debut through Atlantic Records, released April 3, 2007, and debuted at #46 on the Billboard 200.

**Q: What makes "Redemption" a Grammy-nominated drum performance?**
A: "Redemption" earned Shadows Fall a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance at the 50th Grammy Awards in February 2008 — the band's second Grammy nod, following *The War Within*'s "What Drives the Weak." Jason Bittner builds from a restrained, articulate verse into a driving, double-kick-powered chorus, using the DW 9002's consistent dual-chain action to make the escalation feel earned rather than abrupt.
