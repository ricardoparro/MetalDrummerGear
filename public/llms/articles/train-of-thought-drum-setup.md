# Train of Thought Drum Setup — Mike Portnoy's Heaviest Dream Theater Kit (2003)

> Complete breakdown of Mike Portnoy's drum gear on Dream Theater's Train of Thought (2003). Discover the expanded Tama Starclassic Maple kit, Sabian AAX cymbals, Pearl Eliminator pedals, and the Metallica/Pantera-influenced technique behind DT's heaviest album.

## Overview

Released on November 3, 2003, *Train of Thought* is Dream Theater's most deliberately heavy record — a full-throttle plunge into Metallica and Pantera territory that marked the band's first release on Roadrunner Records. Before entering the studio, the band immersed themselves in *Master of Puppets* and *Vulgar Display of Power*, absorbing the directness and aggression that had defined heavy metal's mainstream peak in the 1990s.

For Mike Portnoy, *Train of Thought* represented the largest and heaviest kit configuration of his Dream Theater career to that point. The expanded Tama Starclassic Maple setup — stretched to approximately 13 drums — gave him a broader sonic palette for relentless double-bass patterns and tom-driven riffs. The switch to Sabian AAX cymbals (brighter and more aggressively voiced than the Artisan series of the *Scenes from a Memory* era) matched the album's harder edge. The Pearl Eliminator double pedals, with their multi-cam adjustability, brought new mechanical authority to Portnoy's two-foot vocabulary.

Dream Theater self-produced the album with John Petrucci at Avatar Studios (formerly The Power Station) in New York — one of the most storied large-format tracking rooms in American music. The room's live acoustic character contributed to the album's raw, physically dense drum sound.

### The Expanded Tama Starclassic Maple Kit

By 2003, Mike Portnoy had been playing Tama Starclassic Maple kits for several years, but the *Train of Thought* cycle pushed the configuration to its largest expression. The setup expanded to approximately 13 drums — five rack toms, three floor toms (including an 18"), and double 22"x18" bass drums. This was the most complete expression of Portnoy's maximal kit philosophy.

The Starclassic Maple's warm, resonant character provided mass and momentum rather than snap — heavy the way a freight train is heavy. The expanded tom array enabled the sweeping multi-drum cascades across "Endless Sacrifice" and "In the Name of God."

### The Sabian AAX Cymbal Setup

The shift from Sabian Artisan to Sabian AAX was one of the most consequential gear changes of this period. The Artisan series — dark, hand-hammered, warm — had served the meditative emotional depth of *Metropolis Pt. 2* perfectly. But *Train of Thought* needed brightness, projection, and aggressive response. The AAX Stage Crashes (17", 18", 19") explode on contact and decay quickly enough not to blur the dense riffing underneath. The AAX 21" Raw Bell Ride's defined bell cut through the album's low-midrange density during extended instrumental passages.

### Pearl Eliminator Pedals

For *Train of Thought*, Portnoy transitioned from DW 5000 double pedals (used on *Scenes from a Memory*) to the Pearl Eliminator — his primary endorsement this era. The Eliminator's interchangeable multi-cam system allowed adjustment of response curve and power-to-stroke ratio, critical for sustaining the high-velocity double-bass patterns across "This Dying Soul" and the 24-minute "In the Name of God."

## Recording: Avatar Studios (The Power Station)

Dream Theater self-produced *Train of Thought* with engineer Doug Oberkircher at Avatar Studios, New York. The production philosophy prioritized maximum heaviness: drier, tighter drum sound compared to the room-aware SFaM sessions, with close-mics on each of the 13 drums and minimal bleed. The deliberate Metallica/Pantera influence — Lars Ulrich's machine consistency and Vinnie Paul's physical authority — was absorbed into Portnoy's approach before the sessions began.

## Key Facts

- Dream Theater's first Roadrunner Records album — deliberate metal-label context
- Recorded at Avatar Studios (The Power Station), New York City, self-produced
- Expanded Tama Starclassic Maple to ~13 drums — largest DT era kit to this point
- Sabian AAX replaces Sabian Artisan — brighter and more aggressively voiced
- Pearl Eliminator double pedals replace DW 5000 of the Scenes from a Memory era
- Deliberate Metallica (*Master of Puppets*) and Pantera (*Vulgar Display of Power*) influence
- "In the Name of God" runs over 24 minutes — longest DT studio track at time of release
- Album runtime: ~75 minutes of unrelenting progressive heaviness
- Fills the arc: Scenes from a Memory (1999) → Six Degrees (2002) → **Train of Thought** (2003) → Octavarium (2005)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drum kit did Mike Portnoy use on Train of Thought?**
A: Mike Portnoy recorded Train of Thought (2003) on an expanded Tama Starclassic Maple kit — approximately 13 drums, including double 22"x18" bass drums, five rack toms (8", 10", 12", 13", 14"), and three floor toms (14", 16", 18"). This was the largest kit configuration of his Dream Theater career to that point, built for the album's maximally heavy approach. For more on his gear history, see the [Mike Portnoy drummer page](/drummer/mike-portnoy).

**Q: What cymbals did Mike Portnoy use on Train of Thought?**
A: Portnoy used Sabian AAX and AA cymbals for Train of Thought — a major shift from the darker Sabian Artisan series used on Metropolis Pt. 2: Scenes from a Memory. His setup included Sabian AAX 14" hi-hats, AAX Stage Crashes at 17", 18", and 19", a Sabian AA 16" crash, an AAX 21" Raw Bell Ride, and an AAX 18" Chinese cymbal. The AAX series' bright, aggressive voice matched the Metallica/Pantera-influenced production direction.

**Q: Why is Train of Thought Dream Theater's heaviest album?**
A: Train of Thought is DT's heaviest album by deliberate design. Before recording, the band immersed themselves in Metallica's *Master of Puppets* and Pantera's *Vulgar Display of Power* to absorb that level of heaviness. It was also their first Roadrunner Records album — a label synonymous with extreme metal — and they wanted to match that aesthetic. Portnoy's expanded 13-drum kit, Pearl Eliminator pedals, and Sabian AAX cymbals all supported the heavier playing style.

**Q: What pedals did Mike Portnoy use on Train of Thought?**
A: Pearl Eliminator double pedals — his primary endorsement this era, replacing the DW 5000 of the Scenes from a Memory period. The Eliminator's multi-cam system allows adjustment of the response curve for different power-to-stroke ratios, critical for sustaining the high-velocity double-bass patterns that characterize tracks like "This Dying Soul" and "In the Name of God."

**Q: How does the Train of Thought drum setup differ from Scenes from a Memory?**
A: Key differences: (1) Kit size — Train of Thought expanded to ~13 drums vs. Scenes from a Memory's 8-drum configuration; (2) Cymbals — Sabian AAX (bright, aggressive) vs. Sabian Artisan (dark, hand-hammered); (3) Pedals — Pearl Eliminator (multi-cam, power-focused) vs. DW 5000 (chain-drive, smooth); (4) Playing philosophy — Metallica/Pantera-influenced maximum heaviness vs. narrative-serving concept-album dynamics. See the [Scenes from a Memory drum setup article](/articles/scenes-from-a-memory-drum-setup) for the full comparison.
