## Overview

Travis Orbin (born September 3, 1988, in Raleigh, North Carolina) was the drummer for Periphery during the band's initial studio years, recording the self-titled debut Periphery (2010) and Periphery II: This Time It's Personal (2012) — two of the most influential albums in the djent genre's emergence as a commercial and critical force.

What Orbin brought to Periphery was a technical execution rarely seen in the new generation of progressive metal drummers. Periphery's music — built on the polyrhythmic guitar approach pioneered by Misha Mansoor — demands a drummer capable of sustaining complex polymetric patterns, odd-time signatures, and sudden dynamic shifts across long songs with multiple contrasting sections. Orbin handled all of this with a clinical precision that served the music's dense compositional logic.

His use of Roland SPD-SX electronic pads integrated into an acoustic kit was early adoption of electronic hybrid drumming in progressive metal — Orbin built pad-triggered textures and samples into performances that were otherwise demanding enough acoustically. This hybrid approach is now common in progressive and djent metal but was ahead of its time when Orbin pioneered it within Periphery's context.

His kit centers on DW Collector's Series drums — DW's flagship, fully custom-specified series — with Zildjian K Custom and A Custom cymbals and trigger-augmented bass drums.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Travis Orbin use with Periphery?**
A: Travis Orbin played DW Collector's Series drums with Periphery — DW's fully custom flagship line. The Collector's Series allows shell material, depth, hardware finish, and all specifications to be individually chosen, giving Orbin a kit optimized precisely for djent's poly rhythmic demands.

**Q: What cymbals did Travis Orbin use?**
A: Travis Orbin played a mix of Zildjian K Custom and A Custom cymbals. The combination of K Custom's darker, more complex overtone structure with A Custom's brighter, faster-responding character gave him tonal variety across Periphery's dynamically contrasting song sections.

**Q: What is a Roland SPD-SX and how did Travis Orbin use it?**
A: The Roland SPD-SX is an eight-pad electronic sampling pad. Orbin integrated it into his acoustic kit to trigger samples, electronic textures, and programmed sounds during live performance. This hybrid approach — layering electronic elements onto an acoustic performance — was particularly suited to Periphery's studio-produced sound and allowed Orbin to recreate programmed elements from the records without backing tracks.

**Q: Why did Travis Orbin leave Periphery?**
A: Travis Orbin departed Periphery in 2012, shortly after completing Periphery II. He was replaced by Matt Halpern, who has remained the band's drummer since. Orbin has continued as an independent musician, clinician, and YouTube educator documenting advanced technical drumming.

**Q: Does Travis Orbin use bass drum triggers?**
A: Yes. Travis Orbin uses bass drum triggers in live performance — acoustic drum triggers (typically Roland RT-30 or equivalent) that blend a sampled bass drum attack with the acoustic sound. This ensures consistent, defined kick attack regardless of playing dynamics or venue acoustic conditions.
