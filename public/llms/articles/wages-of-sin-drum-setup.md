## Overview

Released on March 26, 2002, *Wages of Sin* is the fourth studio album by Arch Enemy and the most consequential release in the band's catalog. It was the first album to feature Angela Gossow as vocalist — replacing original singer Johan Liiva — and the combination of Gossow's aggressive delivery and Arch Enemy's refined melodic death metal approach catapulted the band from Gothenburg underground recognition to international prominence. *Wages of Sin* charted across Europe and established Arch Enemy as a genuine global metal act.

For Daniel Erlandsson, the album represents the performance that defined his career. His drumming across the record demonstrates every quality that makes him one of melodic death metal's most respected practitioners: blast beats deployed with structural precision, double-kick patterns that follow Michael Amott's melodic guitar contours, fills that function as compositional events, and the dynamic awareness to make every transition between the album's atmospheric and full-intensity sections land with force.

The album was recorded at The Abyss Studio in Pärnäs, Sweden — producer Peter Tägtgren's facility and one of Scandinavia's most important metal recording environments in the early 2000s. Tägtgren's production captured the Tama Starclassic Maple drum sound with the clarity and punch that the album's double-bass-heavy tracks demand.

A common point of confusion worth addressing: Daniel Erlandsson and Adrian Erlandsson are brothers, both prominent Swedish melodic death metal drummers from the same Gothenburg scene — but they play for different bands. Daniel is the Arch Enemy drummer. Adrian is the At the Gates drummer. Adrian recorded Slaughter of the Soul (1995) for At the Gates while Daniel was building Arch Enemy's catalog. The two brothers developed parallel melodic death metal careers simultaneously in the same creative community. ## Drum Kit

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple
- **Configuration:** Double bass drums (2x 22" x 18"), Rack Toms: 10" x 9", 12" x 10"; Floor Toms: 14" x 14", 16" x 16"
- **Shell Material:** Pure maple — Starclassic Maple construction for warmth, clarity, and projection
- **Finish:** Gloss black
- **Estimated Value:** $2,800–4,500 (Tama Starclassic Maple shell pack, 2002 pricing)

The Tama Starclassic Maple was Erlandsson's recording and touring instrument during Arch Enemy's early Gossow era. The all-maple shell construction delivers the warm, resonant character that suits melodic death metal's harmonic density — the shells support Amott's guitar melody rather than competing with it in the frequency domain. Natural sustain in the toms carries melodic fill phrasing; controlled low-frequency weight in the kick drums provides the sub-bass foundation that *Wages of Sin*'s double-bass-heavy tracks require.

The double bass configuration — two 22" x 18" kick drums — generates the mass and punch that tracks like "Enemy Within" and "The First Deadly Sin" demand. The 22" diameter provides sub-bass body; the 18" depth adds beater attack that cuts through Amott's layered guitar production even in the densest passages. The compact tom configuration (10", 12", 14", 16") follows the Gothenburg approach: pitch variety sufficient for melodic fill phrasing without the sprawling setups that prioritize visual impact over playing speed.

### Snare

The 6.5" depth provides volume and body while maintaining quick response across the album's varied dynamic range. Tuning: medium to medium-high for brightness and attack through melodic death metal guitar frequencies. Estimated value: $300–500.

Paiste's professional series cymbals deliver a distinctly European tonal character — bell-forward, with defined attack and controlled wash — that suited Tägtgren's Abyss Studio production style. The 14" hi-hats establish Erlandsson's rhythmic pulse with fast, focused decay; each stroke registers distinctly in the production even in guitar-dense frequency environments. The crash pairing (16" + 18") provides accent variety: the smaller for precision punctuation, the larger for structural section boundaries. The China adds the aggressive, hard-edged accent that marks *Wages of Sin*'s most intense passages.

## Hardware & Pedals

**Bass Drum Pedals:** Axis A Longboard Double Bass Pedal

The Axis A Longboard is the most distinctive gear choice on *Wages of Sin* and the piece most critical to Erlandsson's double-kick performance on the album. The pedal's defining characteristic is its linear cam geometry — a direct-drive design producing consistent mechanical response across the full stroke range. Unlike the non-linear acceleration of cam-based competitors (DW, Pearl), the Axis produces the same feel at every point in the stroke. For Erlandsson's heel-up double-kick technique at melodic death metal tempos, this linearity means consistent velocity across the album's longest double-kick passages — the 15th stroke of a 16th-note pattern at 200 BPM produces the same result as the first.

**Sticks:** ProMark 5B — mass and durability for high-impact melodic death metal performance. The 5B's weight and diameter provide driving impact in aggressive passages while remaining manageable for faster melodic fill articulation.

**Drumheads:**

- Bass Drum Batter: Remo Powerstroke 3 — focused attack with controlled low-end for double-kick definition
- Bass Drum Resonant: Remo Powerstroke 3
- Toms Batter: Remo Emperor Coated — two-ply durability and controlled sustain
- Toms Resonant: Remo Ambassador Clear
- Snare Batter: Remo Ambassador Coated
- Snare Resonant: Remo Ambassador Snare Side

## Key Tracks and Drumming Highlights

**Enemy Within** (~200 BPM): Album opener — blast beat introduction followed by sustained double-kick patterns across the main riff sequences. Defines the *Wages of Sin* drum template immediately. The Axis A Longboard's linearity is most audible in the blast/double-kick transitions.

**Burning Angel** (~180 BPM): One of the album's most accessible tracks — mid-tempo groove-oriented foundation with melodic fill integration. Erlandsson's tom fill phrasing follows Amott's guitar melody in the pre-chorus. The Tama Starclassic Maple tom warmth carries the melodic phrase work.

**Heart of Darkness** (~175 BPM): Full double-kick foundation across verse sequences. Descending tom fill pattern in the pre-chorus is a signature Erlandsson phrase — pitch differentiation preserved clearly by Tägtgren's production and the Starclassic Maple's resonance.

**Shadows and Dust**: Album closer — sustained grooves at a slightly lower tempo, emphasizing weight over velocity. Demonstrates control and endurance across a longer-form arrangement.

## Recording Context: The Abyss Studio

The Abyss Studio in Pärnäs, Sweden — Tägtgren's own facility — was one of Scandinavia's most important metal recording environments in the early 2000s. Tägtgren's approach emphasized close-mic clarity combined with enough room character to give the kit physical presence. The Tama Starclassic Maple's natural resonance worked with the studio's acoustic environment: the shells' warmth complemented the controlled ambience Tägtgren preferred in his drum productions.

Key production characteristics: kick drum recorded with punch and definition for double-kick legibility; steel snare captured with brightness and authority; tom fills treated as melodic phrases with preserved pitch differentiation; Paiste crash wash controlled for clarity.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Daniel Erlandsson use on Wages of Sin?**
A: Daniel Erlandsson recorded Wages of Sin (2002) on a Tama Starclassic Maple kit — two 22" x 18" kick drums, rack toms at 10" and 12", and floor toms at 14" and 16". He later transitioned to Pearl Reference Pure drums.

**Q: What cymbals did Daniel Erlandsson use on Wages of Sin?**
A: Paiste professional series cymbals — 14" hi-hats, 16" and 18" crashes, 20" ride, and a China cymbal. He later moved to Sabian AAX and HHX.

**Q: What pedals did Daniel Erlandsson use on Wages of Sin?**
A: Axis A Longboard double bass pedal — a linear-cam design producing consistent mechanical response for sustained double-kick passages at melodic death metal tempos.

**Q: Who played drums on Arch Enemy's Wages of Sin?**
A: Daniel Erlandsson, Arch Enemy's co-founding drummer. His brother Adrian Erlandsson is the At the Gates drummer — a common point of confusion. Adrian recorded Slaughter of the Soul (1995) for At the Gates; Daniel recorded Wages of Sin (2002) for Arch Enemy.

**Q: Why is Wages of Sin Daniel Erlandsson's most important album?**
A: Wages of Sin was Arch Enemy's international breakthrough — Angela Gossow's debut as vocalist transformed the band's global profile, and the album charted across Europe. The drum performance on tracks like "Enemy Within" and "Burning Angel" became the reference standard for Erlandsson's melodic death metal approach.
