## Overview

Released June 9, 2014 on Century Media Records, War Eternal is the arc-opening album of Arch Enemy's Alissa White-Gluz era — the first Arch Enemy record with White-Gluz as vocalist, following Angela Gossow's retirement from performing after the Khaos Legions world tour. It is Arch Enemy's most-streamed album on Spotify, anchored by the title track 'War Eternal' — the band's most-played song on the platform globally.

Behind the kit, Daniel Erlandsson — Arch Enemy co-founder and drummer since the band's 1995 founding — delivered a performance that maintained the precision of the Gossow era within a new production context. War Eternal was produced by Jens Bogren at Fascination Street Studio in Örebro, Sweden: a significant departure from the Andy Sneap / Studio Fredman environment that had defined the Gossow-era albums. Bogren's production philosophy — warmer, more ambient, more dynamic — gave the album a drum sound with more space and tonal richness than any previous Arch Enemy record.

- **Sticks:** ProMark 5B

## Key Facts

- Released June 9, 2014 — Arch Enemy's ninth studio album
- First album with vocalist Alissa White-Gluz — arc-opening White-Gluz era record
- 'War Eternal' title track is Arch Enemy's most-streamed Spotify song
- Produced by Jens Bogren at Fascination Street Studio — first Arch Enemy Bogren collaboration
- Pearl Reference Pure kit — consistent Erlandsson configuration since Doomsday Machine (2005)
- Meinl Byzance multi-series cymbals — continued from Khaos Legions (2011)
- Pearl Demon Drive double bass pedal — introduced on Khaos Legions, continued through White-Gluz era
- Follows Khaos Legions (2011); precedes Will to Power (2017) in the Erlandsson discography
