## Overview

Released on April 27, 2018, *When Legends Rise* is Godsmack's seventh studio album and their most commercially polished record. It debuted at #4 on the Billboard 200 and hit #1 on Billboard's Top Rock Albums chart. The title track "When Legends Rise" spent seven consecutive weeks at #1 on the Mainstream Rock chart — the band's biggest radio performance in nearly a decade. "Under Your Scars" reached #1 on Adult Alternative Songs. The album was co-produced with Erik Ron, Godsmack's first outside production collaboration, resulting in their cleanest and most arena-ready studio record.

Shannon Larkin arrived at the *When Legends Rise* sessions with fifteen years of Godsmack recording experience behind him — *Faceless* (2003), *IV* (2006), *The Oracle* (2010), and *1000hp* (2014) — and a gear configuration refined for maximum studio and live impact. His primary kit was the Pearl Reference Pure: Pearl's most resonant professional series, featuring open-character maple/birch shells that responded well to the album's polished production approach. His Sabian cymbal setup evolved from the pure HH/HHX configuration of the *IV* era to incorporate Sabian AAX Stage Hi-Hats alongside the HHX crashes and ride that had been central to his sound since 2006. His double bass pedal returned to DW 9000 hardware — closing a circle from the *Faceless* era.

### Pearl Reference Pure: Open Resonance for a Polished Production

The Pearl Reference Pure represents a distinct evolution from the original Pearl Reference Series that Larkin used on *IV* (2006). Where the original Reference's multi-ply hybrid construction emphasized attack definition and projection — suited to *IV*'s aggressive, heavier direction — the Reference Pure's single-species maple/birch shell approach prioritizes resonance and natural sustain. For *When Legends Rise*'s polished arena-rock production, the Reference Pure's more open character provided a drum sound that responded naturally to studio shaping.

Larkin's double 22" bass drum configuration — a constant across every Godsmack album since *Faceless* — takes on a slightly different character through the Reference Pure shells. The more resonant low end produces kicks with greater sustain and body, well-suited to the album's cleaner, more produced mix. The four-tom spread (10", 12", 14", 16") remains unchanged from his *Faceless* setup — a deliberate, consistent constraint that keeps his approach focused on groove and power.

### Sabian HHX/AAX: The Evolved Hybrid Cymbal Setup

The notable cymbal update from previous Godsmack albums is the shift from Sabian HH Medium Hi-Hats to Sabian AAX 14" Stage Hi-Hats. The AAX Stage Hi-Hats are machine-hammered for a brighter, more immediately responsive voice than the hand-hammered HH series — better suited to *When Legends Rise*'s arena-scale, more commercial production aesthetic. The brighter AAX character provides energy and presence in the busier mix.

The HHX Evolution Crashes (17" and 19") carry forward from the *IV* era: fast-speaking with complex dark overtones, they remain the accent crash choice across the Godsmack catalog. The HHX 21" Raw Bell Dry Ride — present in Larkin's setup since *IV* — continues as his time-keeping centerpiece: its dry body wash and articulate bell define his ride sound through the entire Godsmack arc.

### DW 9000 Double Pedal: Return to DW Hardware

Shannon Larkin's return to the DW 9000 for the *When Legends Rise* era completed a full circle from the *Faceless* (2003) sessions, where the 9000 was also his pedal of choice. Between these albums, he had used the Pearl Eliminator on *IV* (2006). The DW 9000's dual-chain drive mechanism delivers consistent, smooth feel across extended live performance — the road-proven reliability essential for the double-kick patterns central to Godsmack's hard rock arrangements and the demanding touring cycle behind a chart-topping album.

## FAQ

Pearl Reference Pure — double 22" bass drums, 10" and 12" rack toms, 14" and 16" floor toms, with a Pearl Free-Floating 14"x6.5" brass snare. The Reference Pure's open, resonant shell character distinguishes it from the original Reference Series used on IV (2006).
