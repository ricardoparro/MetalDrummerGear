# Daniel Erlandsson's Drum Setup on Arch Enemy's Will to Power (2017)

> Arch Enemy's chart-topping White-Gluz era album — Top 5 in Germany (#3), Sweden (#3), and Austria (#4). Produced by Jens Bogren at Fascination Street Studio, Örebro, Sweden.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** Daniel Erlandsson
**Band / Album:** Arch Enemy — *Will to Power* (2017)
**Genre:** Melodic Death Metal
**Label:** Century Media Records
**Studio:** Fascination Street Studio, Örebro, Sweden
**Producer:** Jens Bogren, Michael Amott

## Overview

Released September 8, 2017 on Century Media Records, Will to Power is Arch Enemy's commercial peak of the Alissa White-Gluz era and one of the highest-charting melodic death metal albums of the 2010s. It reached #3 in Germany and Sweden, #4 in Austria, and charted across more than twenty countries. It was the second Arch Enemy album produced by Jens Bogren at Fascination Street Studio — the deepened production partnership produced the band's most tonally polished studio recording to that point.

Drummer Daniel Erlandsson turned in a performance that matched the album's compositional ambition. Will to Power features some of Arch Enemy's most melodically varied material — including 'The Eagle Flies Alone' (the lead single that broke into European radio) and 'The World Is Yours' — alongside the signature intensity peaks that defined the band's sound through the entire White-Gluz era. Erlandsson's drumming navigates that dynamic range with the precision and compositional awareness accumulated across more than two decades of Arch Enemy recording.

## Key Facts

- Released September 8, 2017 — Arch Enemy's tenth studio album
- Top 5 in Germany (#3), Sweden (#3), Austria (#4) — the band's highest chart positions in the White-Gluz era
- 'The Eagle Flies Alone' lead single charted in European rock and metal radio
- Second Jens Bogren / Fascination Street production — most polished Arch Enemy drum sound to that point
- Pearl Reference Pure kit — Erlandsson's consistent configuration now in a settled, deeply familiar form
- Meinl Byzance multi-series — tonal complexity well-served by Bogren's warm production palette
- Pearl Demon Drive continued — years of touring familiarity behind every double-kick passage
- Follows War Eternal (2014); precedes Deceivers (2022) in the White-Gluz era arc
