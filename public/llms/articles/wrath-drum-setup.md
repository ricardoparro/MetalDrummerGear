# Lamb of God 'Wrath' Drum Setup — Chris Adler's 2009 Grammy-Nominated Kit

> Explore Chris Adler's drum setup on Lamb of God's Grammy-nominated Wrath (2009) — Pearl Reference Pure kit, Zildjian A Custom cymbals, and the groove metal peak that debuted at #3 on the Billboard 200.

**Type:** Album Drum Setup
**Drummer(s):** [Chris Adler](/llms/drummers/chris-adler.md)
**Band / Album:** Lamb of God — *Wrath* (2009)
**Genre:** Groove Metal / New Wave of American Heavy Metal

## Overview

Released on February 24, 2009, Lamb of God's "Wrath" arrived as the band's most commercially triumphant moment — debuting at #3 on the Billboard 200 and earning a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance for "Set to Fail" at the 52nd Grammy Awards in 2010. For Chris Adler, Wrath represented the culmination of everything he had built across Ashes of the Wake and Sacrament — a performance that was simultaneously his most technical and his most musical.

Recorded at Audiohammer Studios in Sanford, Florida, with producer Machine (Mark Lewis), Wrath marked Adler's full transition to the Pearl Reference Pure kit — a significant shift from the DW Collector's Series of the Sacrament era. Paired with Zildjian A Custom cymbals in their upgraded 2009 hardware generation, the Wrath setup represents the most polished and precisely realized iteration of the Lamb of God drum sound.

Where Sacrament (2006) was about breaking into arena audiences, Wrath was about mastery. The grooves are tighter, the fills more purposeful, and the dynamic range more controlled than anything in the prior catalog. "Set to Fail," "Contractor," and "In Your Words" are among the most studied patterns in modern groove metal — not for their complexity, but for their inevitability.

## Gear Breakdown

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure (6-ply maple SST shells, Lacquer Natural Burst finish)
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference Maple Snare, 14" x 6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian — Zildjian A Custom (2009 upgraded hardware generation)
- **Hardware / Pedals:** Pearl Demon Drive Single Pedal (two units, one per bass drum); Pearl H-2050 Eliminator Hi-Hat Stand; Roc-N-Soc Nitro Throne; Promark Chris Adler Signature TX517W
- **Heads:** Remo Controlled Sound Coated (batter), Remo Ambassador Snare Side (resonant)
- **Snare tuning:** Medium-high tension for ghost note clarity and sharp rimshot definition

### The Pearl Reference Pure: Adler's 2009 Precision Machine

For Wrath, Chris Adler transitioned from DW Collector's Series to the Pearl Reference Pure — Pearl's professional-grade maple kit built around 6-ply SST (Superior Shell Technology) shells. Where the DW VLT shells delivered warm, resonant tones suited to Capitol Studios' natural reverb (Sacrament), the Pearl Reference Pure's construction delivers brighter attack and faster decay — perfectly matched to Audiohammer's more controlled acoustic environment.

The dual 22" × 18" bass drums remained non-negotiable. Pearl's OptiMount suspension system — floating toms via rubber isolators rather than clamp mounts — allowed the shells to resonate freely without hardware dampening. Machine's recording philosophy at Audiohammer relied on natural shell resonance, and the OptiMount system provided exactly that.

The transition from DW to Pearl is audible: Wrath's kick drums punch harder and decay faster; Sacrament's breathe with more room warmth. Both approaches served their respective albums perfectly — different tools, different environments, same player.

### The Wrath Snare

The Pearl Reference Maple snare (14" × 6.5") maintained the deeper-shell preference Adler had developed on Sacrament's DW setup. The Pearl's character is slightly brighter and more articulate, with a faster decay that suits Audiohammer's drier environment. Die-cast hoops ensured rimshot consistency across Wrath's demanding patterns — every hit lands at the same defined pitch, whether it's a ghost note or a full power rimshot.

### The Zildjian A Custom Evolution

Wrath continued the Zildjian A Custom setup from Sacrament but with upgraded hardware generation. The 2009 A Custom configuration offered faster hi-hat response and marginally brighter high-end shimmer. The core setup: 13" A Custom hi-hats, 16"/17"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China, 10" splash.

The 18" A Custom China remains Adler's most recognizable sonic signature — placed with surgical precision to punctuate riff transitions throughout Wrath's most aggressive tracks.
