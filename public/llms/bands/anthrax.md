## Drummer(s)

**Charlie Benante** — 1983–present
Charlie Benante joined Anthrax shortly after the band's 1981 formation and has been their drummer ever since, making him one of the longest-tenured drummers in thrash metal history. He is widely credited as one of the inventors of the "thrash beat" — a fast, syncopated pattern combining rapid 16th-note bass drum runs with accented snare work. Benante is also a skilled guitarist and songwriter. Beyond Anthrax, he has served as the tribute drummer for the Pantera reunion tour (2022–present).

## Signature Sound

Anthrax's drumming is built on Charlie Benante's aggressive yet groove-conscious approach to thrash metal. On albums like *Spreading the Disease* (1985) and *Among the Living* (1987), he helped define the thrash metal rhythmic vocabulary — fast double bass, syncopated snare accents, and rapid tom fills that propel Scott Ian's rhythm guitar. Anthrax's crossover with hip-hop on "I'm the Man" (1987) showed Benante's rhythmic flexibility. Compared to the raw aggression of Slayer or the technicality of Megadeth, Benante's approach is notably groove-oriented with a strong sense of swing even at high speeds.
