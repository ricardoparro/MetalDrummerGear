## Drummer(s)

**Inferno** — 1997–present (real name: Zbigniew Promiński)
Inferno joined Behemoth in 1997 during the band's transition from black metal to blackened death metal. He has been the band's drummer ever since, playing on every major album from *Satanica* (1999) onward. Known for his power, precision, and blast beat endurance, Inferno is one of the most respected extreme metal drummers in Europe. He uses an unusual double pedal combination — the **Czarcie Kopyto (Devil's Hoof)** — a handcrafted Polish boutique double pedal, which has become part of his signature sound. He also holds the distinction of playing with Pearl hardware outside Poland.

Inferno plays **Pearl Masterworks** shells paired with **Paiste RUDE Series** cymbals. His distinctive **Czarcie Kopyto (Devil's Hoof)** double pedal is handcrafted in Poland by a small boutique manufacturer.

## Signature Sound

Behemoth's drumming is defined by Inferno's ability to play with overwhelming power while maintaining melodic and compositional awareness within Nergal's complex blackened death metal arrangements. Albums like *Demigod* (2004), *The Apostasy* (2007), and *The Satanist* (2014) showcase his combination of hyper-fast blast beats with structured, varied patterns that support the atmospheric and symphonic elements of the music. The transition from Behemoth's early black metal to their current blackened death metal sound is reflected in Inferno's increasing technical sophistication. *The Satanist* is considered one of the greatest blackened death metal albums ever recorded.
