## Drummer(s)

**Sean Reinert** — 1987–1994, 2006–2017
Sean Reinert co-founded Cynic in Miami, Florida in 1987 with guitarist/vocalist Paul Masvidal, before also joining Death in 1990 and drumming on their landmark 1991 album Human. He returned to Cynic to record their own landmark album, Focus, in 1993. Reinert's jazz-trained musical vocabulary — ghost notes, dynamic control, and polymetric phrasing — brought a genuinely musical dimension to extreme metal drumming at a time when the genre was defined almost entirely by blast-beat velocity. He rejoined Cynic for their 2006 reunion, contributing to 2008's Traced in Air, before passing away on January 24, 2020, one day after his 49th birthday.

## Kit Overview

Sean Reinert was an early DW endorsee, playing a DW Collector's Series maple shell kit throughout his tenure in Cynic. The warm, open maple tone gave his ghost-note-driven, dynamically expansive playing a resonance rarely heard in early-1990s death metal production. On Focus he switched from Zildjian A to the darker, more complex Zildjian K Series and tuned his snare higher and more open to suit the album's jazz-fusion textures. For Cynic's 2008 reunion album, Traced in Air, he upgraded to a DW Collector's maple shell pack paired with Zildjian K Custom cymbals.

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series maple, 22" kick, 10"/12"/13"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** DW Collector's 14"x5.5" maple, die-cast hoops
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Series (Focus era), later Zildjian K Custom (Traced in Air)
- **Pedals:** DW 5000 single-chain double pedal
- **Sticks/Heads:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A; Remo Ambassador (clear toms, coated snare)

## Signature Sound

Cynic's drumming, as shaped by Sean Reinert, fuses technical death metal aggression with a genuinely jazz-fusion sensibility. Reinert maintained a fluid, fusion-informed double-bass technique rather than the brute-force approach common among his death metal peers, favoring ghost notes and polymetric phrasing over raw velocity. This musical vocabulary, first proven on Death's Human, found its fullest expression on Cynic's own Focus (1993), a landmark album that fused technical death metal with jazz fusion. The darker, more complex Zildjian K cymbals he adopted for that record underline the more textured, less abrasive tone Cynic pursued compared to straightforward extreme metal.
