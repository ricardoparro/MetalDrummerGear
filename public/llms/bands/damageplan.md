## Drummer(s)

**Vinnie Paul** — 2003–2004
Vinnie Paul co-founded Damageplan with his younger brother "Dimebag" Darrell Abbott in 2003, after the breakup of Pantera, the groove metal band the pair had co-founded and helped define through albums like Cowboys from Hell, Vulgar Display of Power, and Far Beyond Driven. Vinnie Paul was widely ranked among the greatest metal drummers of all time, known for a powerful, groove-oriented style that helped establish groove metal as a distinct subgenre. Following Dimebag's murder onstage in 2004, Damageplan disbanded; Vinnie Paul went on to form Hellyeah in 2006.

## Kit Overview

By the Damageplan era, Vinnie Paul had moved on from the Tama, Remo, and Pearl kits of his Pantera years to a ddrum setup, the brand he endorsed from 2008 until his death — though his ddrum association is documented as spanning his later career including this period. He consistently endorsed Sabian cymbals and Vic Firth drumsticks throughout his career.

- **Drums:** ddrum
- **Snare:** ddrum custom snares
- **Cymbals:** Sabian (AA, AAX Series)
- **Pedals:** ddrum double pedal
- **Sticks/Heads:** Vic Firth; Evans drumheads

## Signature Sound

Damageplan carried forward the thunderous, groove-driven drumming that Vinnie Paul had established with Pantera, formed directly in the wake of that band's breakup by the two brothers at its core, Vinnie Paul and Dimebag Darrell. His playing remained powerful and groove-oriented, the same approach several outlets credit with helping establish groove metal as its own subgenre. Damageplan's single album, New Found Power (2004), stands as the final studio statement of that partnership before Dimebag's murder onstage brought the band to an abrupt end.
