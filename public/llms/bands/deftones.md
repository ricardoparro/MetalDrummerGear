## Drummer(s)

**Abe Cunningham** — 1988–present (Co-founder)
Abe Cunningham is a founding member of Deftones and the only drummer in the band's history. Formed in Sacramento in 1988, Cunningham's drumming reflects the band's genre-blending approach — bridging alternative metal aggression with shoegaze dreaminess and post-hardcore intensity. He is known for his dynamic range, playing softly to support atmospheric passages and exploding into heavy sections with controlled fury. His ability to lock into the low, heavy grooves while simultaneously sustaining textural, atmospheric feels makes him one of alternative metal's most distinctive drummers.

## Signature Sound

Deftones' drumming is defined by Cunningham's dynamic sensitivity and the contrast between softness and brutality. Unlike many heavy metal drummers who maintain consistent intensity, Cunningham masters the art of restraint — on songs like "Change (In the House of Flies)" he plays delicate hi-hat patterns under Chino Moreno's atmospheric vocals, only to crash into heavy grooves when the song demands it. On heavier tracks like "My Own Summer (Shove It)" and "Back to School," his driving double bass and snare work can equal anything in nu-metal. The Deftones' shoegaze influence — heard on albums like *White Pony* (2000) and *Diamond Eyes* (2010) — requires a drummer who can sustain musical tension as much as rhythmic power.
