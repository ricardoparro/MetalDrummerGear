## Drummer(s)

**Vinnie Paul** — 2006–2018
Vinnie Paul founded Hellyeah in 2006, following the breakup of Pantera and the end of Damageplan after his brother Dimebag Darrell's murder onstage in 2004. Best known as the drummer and co-founder of Pantera, where he helped define 1990s groove metal on albums like Cowboys from Hell, Vulgar Display of Power, and Far Beyond Driven, Vinnie Paul brought that same powerful, groove-oriented style to Hellyeah as his final full-time band. He has been ranked by several outlets among the greatest metal drummers of all time, and drummed for Hellyeah until his death in 2018.

## Kit Overview

Vinnie Paul's Hellyeah-era setup was built around ddrum, the brand he endorsed from 2008 until his death, following earlier stints with Tama, Remo, and Pearl. He consistently endorsed Sabian cymbals and Vic Firth drumsticks, notably playing his sticks backwards, holding the tapered end.

- **Drums:** ddrum Vinnie Paul Signature Series
- **Snare:** ddrum custom snares
- **Cymbals:** Sabian (AAX, HHX, and signature models)
- **Hardware:** ddrum and Danmar
- **Sticks/Heads:** Vic Firth (played backwards, tapered end); Evans drumheads
- **Electronics:** ddrum and Roland triggers

## Signature Sound

Hellyeah was built as a groove metal supergroup around Vinnie Paul's drumming, carrying forward the powerful, groove-oriented approach he had established at Pantera and continued through Damageplan. It became his final full-time band, formed after the losses of Pantera's breakup and his brother Dimebag Darrell's murder while in Damageplan. His ddrum Signature Series kit and Sabian cymbal setup, refined across the Hellyeah era, powered the thunderous double kick and heavy grooves that had already helped establish groove metal as a distinct subgenre.
