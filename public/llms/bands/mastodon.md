## Drummer(s)

**Brann Dailor** — 2000–present
Brann Dailor, born March 19, 1975 in Rochester, New York, is best known as the drummer and co-vocalist for Grammy-winning progressive sludge metal band Mastodon. His drumming style combines jazz-influenced fills with progressive rock complexity, creating a distinctive, flowing approach that has influenced a generation of metal drummers. Dailor's playing is characterized by constant motion — intricate fills that weave around the riffs rather than simply keeping time — and his "lead drums" approach treats the drums as a melodic instrument. He co-founded Mastodon in 2000 with guitarist Bill Kelliher, his longtime collaborator from their days in Lethargy and Today Is the Day.

## Kit Overview

Brann Dailor is a longtime DW Drums endorser, playing their Collector's Series drums with a custom blue finish. He favors a large setup to accommodate his expansive, melodic tom work.

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series Maple (custom blue finish)

- **Throne:** DW 9000 Series Throne

His kit is built around multiple rack toms for melodic patterns, extensive use of China cymbals for accents, quick-response cymbals for fast transitions, and angled toms for fluid motion between drums.

## Signature Sound

Mastodon's drumming identity is inseparable from Brann Dailor's "lead drums" philosophy — treating the kit as a melodic voice that weaves constantly around the riffs rather than simply anchoring the beat. His jazz-influenced fills and progressive rock complexity have defined the band's sound since its 2000 formation, from the blistering debut *Remission* (2002) through the Moby Dick concept album *Leviathan* (2004) and the prog masterpiece *Crack the Skye* (2009), all the way to the Grammy-winning *Emperor of Sand* (2017) and the epic double album *Hushed and Grim* (2021). His large DW kit, with its extensive tom array and China cymbal accents, gives him the melodic range his flowing style demands, while his increasingly prominent co-lead vocals have added another dimension to Mastodon's evolving sound.
