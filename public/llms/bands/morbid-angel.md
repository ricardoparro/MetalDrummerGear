## Drummer(s)

**Pete Sandoval** — 1988–2013
Pedro Rigoberto "Pete" Sandoval, born May 21, 1964 in Santa Ana, El Salvador, is a Salvadoran-American drummer widely regarded as one of the most influential figures in extreme metal. Known as "Commando" and "Pete the Feet," his work with Morbid Angel and Terrorizer helped define death metal and grindcore drumming. AllMusic's Jason Birchmeier wrote that Sandoval "challenges one's perception of how fast a drummer can possibly drum," and his pioneering blast beat technique on *Altars of Madness* (1989) set the template countless extreme metal drummers have since followed.

**Tim Yeung** — 2004–2015
Tim Yeung, born November 27, 1978 in Pennsylvania, USA, is an American extreme metal drummer whose speed, precision, and endurance have made him one of the most sought-after drummers in death metal. His resume includes stints with Morbid Angel, Vital Remains, Hate Eternal, Divine Heresy, and Nile. Yeung's drumming combines machine-like consistency with the brutal intensity demanded by the most extreme forms of death metal, and his ability to hold clarity and power at sustained high tempos has earned him a reputation as one of the genre's elite players.

## Signature Sound

Morbid Angel's drumming legacy centers on Pete Sandoval's pioneering blast beat and gravity blast techniques, which he developed through relentless practice after joining the band with no prior double bass experience. His playing on *Altars of Madness* (1989) remains a foundational benchmark for death metal drumming, and he continued to anchor the band's most celebrated records through *Heretic* (2003). Tim Yeung, who had already built a reputation across Vital Remains, Hate Eternal, and Divine Heresy for machine-like consistency at extreme tempos, stepped in for the band's later, more controversial period, including *Illud Divinum Insanus* (2011). Together, their tenures reflect Morbid Angel's dual identity as both death metal originators and a band willing to push into new sonic territory.

## FAQ

**Q: Who plays drums in Morbid Angel?**
A: Pete Sandoval drummed for Morbid Angel from 1988–2013, and Tim Yeung served as session/touring drummer alongside him from 2004–2015, including on *Illud Divinum Insanus* (2011).

**Q: What is Pete Sandoval's gravity blast technique?**
A: Pete Sandoval pioneered the gravity blast, a one-handed blast beat technique that uses the rebound of the stick off the drumhead to generate extreme speed — a signature approach that set the template for death metal drumming after his work on *Altars of Madness* (1989).

**Q: What drum kit does Pete Sandoval use?**
A: During his classic Morbid Angel era, Pete Sandoval played Pearl Export/Masters series drums with a Pearl 14×6.5" Steel or Brass snare, Sabian AA/AAX cymbals, and a Pearl double pedal.
