## Drummer(s)

**Joey Jordison** — 2002–2011
Joey Jordison co-founded Murderdolls in 2002 in Hollywood, California, alongside Wednesday 13, but played guitar rather than drums for the project. He was best known as the original drummer of Slipknot from 1995 to 2013, widely regarded as one of the greatest and most influential metal drummers of all time for his blistering speed, technical precision, and theatrical stage presence, including performing on a rotating, inverted drum platform. Beyond Slipknot and Murderdolls, he also played guitar for Rob Zombie's touring band and drummed for projects including Sinsaenum, Vimic, and briefly Metallica in 2004. Jordison passed away on July 26, 2021.

## Kit Overview

Joey Jordison's Murderdolls role was on guitar rather than drums, so the band's recordings do not feature his signature drum setup. His drumming gear, developed as a longtime Pearl Drums endorser during his Slipknot tenure, was designed for speed, power, and durability: a Pearl Reference Series kit in custom purple/black finishes, his signature Pearl Joey Jordison 13"x6.5" snare (a smaller diameter for faster response), and Paiste RUDE and 2002 series cymbals known for their durability and cut, driven by a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal.

- **Cymbals:** Paiste RUDE and 2002 series

## Signature Sound

Murderdolls' 2002 debut, Beyond the Valley of the Murderdolls, is a horror punk project co-founded by Wednesday 13 and Slipknot's Joey Jordison — but on this record Jordison contributed guitar, not drums, a departure from the extreme-speed, technically precise drumming vocabulary he was building simultaneously in Slipknot. That parallel body of work, defined by blistering speed and theatrical showmanship on a rotating, inverted drum platform, made him one of the most influential metal drummers of his generation, even as Murderdolls showcased a different, horror-punk-oriented side of his musicianship.
