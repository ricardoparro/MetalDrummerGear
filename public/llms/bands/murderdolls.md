## Drummer(s)

**Joey Jordison** — 2002–2011 (Co-founder)
Murderdolls was co-founded in 2002 by Wednesday 13 and Slipknot's Joey Jordison (1975–2021) — but notably, Jordison played guitar in this project rather than his usual instrument. Better known as Slipknot's founding drummer from 1995 to 2013, Jordison's extreme speed and technical precision behind the kit made him one of the most influential metal drummers of his era, but Murderdolls gave him an outlet to explore songwriting and guitar work outside that role. The band's 2002 debut, *Beyond the Valley of the Murderdolls*, credits Jordison on guitar.

## Signature Sound

Murderdolls is a horror-punk project built around Wednesday 13's theatrical songwriting, distinct from the extreme-speed metal drumming Joey Jordison was famous for with Slipknot. Rather than anchoring the band behind the kit, Jordison contributed as a guitarist and co-writer on *Beyond the Valley of the Murderdolls* (2002), applying his musicianship to horror-punk's simpler, more direct rock-and-roll structures — a notable departure from his day job driving Slipknot's blast-beat-heavy nu-metal assault.
