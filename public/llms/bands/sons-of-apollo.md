## Drummer(s)

**Mike Mangini** — 2017–present
Mike Mangini, Dream Theater's drummer since 2010, joined progressive metal supergroup Sons of Apollo in 2017 alongside members of Journey, Mr. Big, and Guns N' Roses. A former Berklee College of Music professor and multiple Guinness World Record holder for drumming speed, Mangini brings his academic, mathematically-informed technique — built on Moeller technique mastery and ambidextrous playing — to the supergroup's complex compositions.

## Kit Overview

Mike Mangini plays a **Pearl Reference Series** kit built around two signature-spec Pearl Reference brass snares, integrated with a Roland TD-50 module for electronic triggers and samples.

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series (Masterworks Maple custom configuration)
- **Snares:** Pearl Reference Brass, 14×5" and 14×6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX and AAX Series (14" HHX Evolution Hi-Hats, 17"/18"/19" HHX Evolution Crashes, 21" HHX Raw Bell Dry Ride)
- **Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator Redline / Demon Drive Double Pedal
- **Electronics:** Roland TD-50 module

## Signature Sound

Sons of Apollo brought together some of progressive rock and metal's most technically accomplished musicians, and Mike Mangini's drumming reflects that pedigree — precise, mathematically constructed patterns built from polyrhythmic foundations, informed by his academic study of biomechanics and rhythm. His work on the band's debut *Psychotic Symphony* (2017) showcases the same ambidextrous, complex-meter-navigating style that has defined his tenure with Dream Theater since 2010.
