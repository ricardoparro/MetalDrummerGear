## Drummer(s)

**Jon Dette** — 1997–1999, 2001–2012
Jon Dette is best known as thrash metal's most reliable fill-in drummer, having also toured with Slayer (1996–1997) and Anthrax (2010). During his two stints with Testament he recorded *The Gathering* (1999), *The Formation of Damnation* (2008), and *Dark Roots of Earth* (2012).

**Paul Bostaph** — 1999–2001, 2024–present
Paul Bostaph, Slayer's longest-serving drummer (1992–2001, 2013–2019), joined Testament during a hiatus from Slayer and returned to the band's lineup in 2024. His machine-like double-bass consistency and Bay Area thrash roots made him a natural fit alongside Testament's guitar-driven riffing.

[Paul Bostaph full profile →](https://metalforge.io/drummer/paul-bostaph) · [LLM markdown](https://metalforge.io/llms/drummers/paul-bostaph.md)

**Gene Hoglan** — 2007–present
Gene Hoglan, nicknamed "The Atomic Clock" for his metronomic precision, has been Testament's primary drummer since 2007, recording *The Formation of Damnation* (2008) and *Titans of Creation* (2020). His technical, jazz-informed approach to extreme metal drumming — honed across Dark Angel, Death, and Dethklok — anchors Testament's modern era.

## Signature Sound

Testament's drumming spans two distinct eras: Jon Dette and Paul Bostaph's classic Bay Area thrash attack of the late '90s and early 2000s, and Gene Hoglan's technical, "Atomic Clock" precision that has defined the band's sound since 2007. Hoglan's ability to shift seamlessly between blast beats and groove-driven passages gave *The Formation of Damnation* and *Titans of Creation* a technical death metal edge layered onto Testament's Bay Area thrash foundation, standing alongside peers Slayer, Megadeth, and Anthrax.

## FAQ

**Q: Who plays drums in Testament?**
A: Gene Hoglan has been Testament's primary drummer since 2007. Paul Bostaph rejoined the band's lineup in 2024, and Jon Dette drummed across two earlier stints (1997–1999, 2001–2012).

**Q: What drum kit does Gene Hoglan use?**
A: Gene Hoglan plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit with a Pearl Reference 14×6.5" brass snare, Sabian AAX cymbals, and a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal.

**Q: Why is Gene Hoglan called "The Atomic Clock"?**
A: Gene Hoglan earned the nickname for his metronomic timing precision, a hallmark of his work across Dark Angel, Death, Testament, and Dethklok.

**Q: What drum kit did Paul Bostaph use with Testament?**
A: Paul Bostaph's setup during his Slayer and Testament years was a Pearl Masters Maple Complete (MCX) shell pack with a 14×6.5" Pearl Masters steel snare and Sabian AAX Series cymbals.

**Q: Is Testament part of the Bay Area thrash scene?**
A: Yes. Formed in 1983 in Berkeley, California, Testament is one of the most enduring bands of the Bay Area thrash metal scene alongside acts like Exodus and Death Angel.
