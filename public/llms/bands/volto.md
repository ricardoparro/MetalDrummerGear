## Drummer(s)

**Danny Carey** — 2000–present
Danny Carey (born May 10, 1961, in Lawrence, Kansas) is an American musician best known as the drummer for progressive metal band Tool since their formation in 1990. Widely regarded as one of the greatest rock drummers of all time, Carey is known for incorporating complex polyrhythms, unusual time signatures, and a jazz-influenced approach into heavy music. Standing at 6'5" (196 cm), Carey brings that same jazz-fusion sensibility to Volto!, the instrumental progressive rock/jazz-fusion project he has led since 2000, drawing on influences including jazz, world music, and his interest in sacred geometry and metaphysical concepts.

## Signature Sound

Volto! gives Danny Carey room to fully explore the jazz-fusion and progressive rock influences that inform but rarely take center stage in Tool's music. His massive Sonor SQ2 Heavy Beech kit, signature bronze snare, and Mandala electronic pads support the same complex polyrhythms and unusual time signatures that define his playing, but in Volto!'s instrumental jazz-fusion context those elements are pushed further toward improvisation and textural experimentation. As a side project led by one of rock's most technically accomplished drummers, Volto!'s sound is inseparable from Carey's genre-spanning musical vocabulary.
