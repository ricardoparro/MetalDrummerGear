# MetalForge Drummer Battles

MetalForge Drummer Battles lets users vote on who would win in a head-to-head drum battle between legendary metal drummers.

## How It Works
- Browse active matchups (e.g. "Dave Lombardo vs Lars Ulrich")
- Vote for which drummer you think would win
- See community-voted results and drum statistics

## Sample Battles
- Joey Jordison vs Lars Ulrich — Slipknot blast speed vs Metallica groove
- George Kollias vs Pete Sandoval — Nile death metal vs Morbid Angel speed
- Gene Hoglan vs Tomas Haake — Precision vs polyrhythm mastery

## Notable Battle Categories

### Speed
The fastest drummers in metal go head-to-head: George Kollias, Pete Sandoval, and Derek Roddy regularly appear in speed-focused battles. Kollias holds the record for fastest double bass at verified BPMs above 250. Sandoval pioneered the blast beat in death metal with Morbid Angel. Roddy brings extreme double bass precision from Hate Eternal and Nile.

### Groove
Groove battles pit masters of feel against each other: Vinnie Paul (Pantera), Dave Lombardo (Slayer), and Lars Ulrich (Metallica). Vinnie Paul's half-time feel defined groove metal on albums like *Vulgar Display of Power*. Lombardo's thrash shuffle on *Reign in Blood* remains one of metal's most studied performances. Ulrich's syncopated hi-hat patterns on *Master of Puppets* and *…And Justice for All* are iconic to thrash.

### Innovation
Progressive and technical drummers compete in innovation matchups: Tomas Haake (Meshuggah), Danny Carey (Tool), and Mike Portnoy (Dream Theater). Haake's polymetric patterns on *obZen* redefined what a drum machine could sound like played by a human. Carey incorporates modular synthesis and odd time signatures across Tool's catalog. Portnoy's encyclopedic fill vocabulary spans jazz, prog, and heavy metal.

### Death Metal Legends
Classic death metal battles feature Gene Hoglan (Death/Testament), Paul Mazurkiewicz (Cannibal Corpse), and Flo Mounier (Cryptopsy). Hoglan earned the nickname "The Atomic Clock" for his metronomic precision. Mazurkiewicz has been with Cannibal Corpse since 1988, one of the most consistent careers in extreme metal. Mounier's *None So Vile* performance is considered one of the most technically demanding in the genre.

## How Votes Work
Each battle accumulates community votes. Results update in real time and show percentage breakdowns. Users can view each drummer's gear stats, technique summaries, and career highlights alongside the matchup — so votes can be cast based on knowledge, not just name recognition.

## Stats Shown Per Battle
- BPM records (where documented)
- Primary kit brand and cymbal setup
- Career highlights and landmark albums
- Genre specialty and technique rating
