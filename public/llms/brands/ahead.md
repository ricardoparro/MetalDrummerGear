# Ahead Drumsticks — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Ahead pioneered aluminum-core drumsticks in 1992, founded by Rick Grossman and distributed by Big Bang Distribution. Lars Ulrich's signature model made Ahead a metal staple built for durability on marathon world tours.

## Metal Drummers Who Use Ahead

- Lars Ulrich (Metallica) — Ahead Lars Ulrich Signature Drumsticks
- Pete Sandoval (Morbid Angel, Terrorizer) — Ahead Lars Ulrich Signature (aircraft-grade aluminum)
- Joey Jordison's (Slipknot) former signature model lives on in the catalog as the Speed Metal stick

## Key Ahead Product Lines for Metal

## FAQ

Q: What Ahead sticks do metal drummers use?
A: Lars Ulrich (Metallica) and Pete Sandoval (Morbid Angel, Terrorizer) both play the Ahead Lars Ulrich Signature aluminum stick. Joey Jordison's (Slipknot) former signature model lives on in the catalog as the Speed Metal stick.

Q: Why do metal drummers use aluminum drumsticks?
A: Ahead's aircraft-grade aluminum core essentially can't break the way wood can, and the outer polyurethane sleeve is replaceable rather than the whole stick. That durability makes them a practical choice for touring drummers who wear through wood sticks fast.

Q: Is Ahead good for metal?
A: Yes. Ahead's aluminum construction was built specifically for durability under hard, aggressive playing, which is why Lars Ulrich adopted it early in his career and it remains a metal-touring staple today.

## History

Founded **1992** in California, USA by Rick Grossman.

Ahead was founded in 1992 by Rick Grossman, a drummer who had been working at Easton Sports, a manufacturer of aluminum sporting goods like baseball bats and hockey sticks. Grossman recognized that the same lightweight alloy technology could be applied to drumsticks, and built the first Ahead sticks around an aluminum core.

The design paired an aircraft-grade aluminum shaft with a replaceable polyurethane outer sleeve and threaded tip — when the sleeve wore out, a player replaced the sleeve rather than the whole stick, and the aluminum core essentially couldn't break the way a wood shaft could.

In 1997, Bob Kasha purchased the Ahead brand from Easton Sports. Today Ahead is distributed by Big Bang Distribution, a percussion distributor based in Northridge, California that was itself founded in 1992 as Ahead's exclusive distributor before expanding into a full-service percussion company.

Ahead's durability pitch — sticks that last many times longer than wood — built a strong following among touring hard-rock and metal drummers who wear through conventional sticks quickly.

**Milestones:**
- **1992** — Rick Grossman founds Ahead, applying Easton Sports' aluminum sporting-goods technology to drumsticks.
- **1992** — Big Bang Distribution is founded in Northridge, California as Ahead's exclusive distributor.
- **1997** — Bob Kasha purchases the Ahead brand from Easton Sports.

**Metal era:** Lars Ulrich (Metallica) has played his own Ahead signature aluminum sticks for decades, adopting them early in his career for their durability across Metallica's marathon world tours. Pete Sandoval (Morbid Angel, Terrorizer) also plays Ahead's Lars Ulrich Signature model, relying on the aluminum core to survive his relentless blast-beat attack, and Joey Jordison's (Slipknot) former signature model lives on as the brand's Speed Metal stick.
