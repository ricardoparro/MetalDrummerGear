# Axis Percussion Pedals — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Axis Percussion is an American pedal maker founded in 1990 in California, credited with pioneering the direct-drive bass drum pedal. Now operating as AXiS Pedal & Drum Co., it's the extreme-metal standard for drummers who need maximum speed and precision.

## FAQ

Q: What Axis pedals do metal drummers use?
A: Axis A Longboard double pedals are the brand's flagship model, played by Joey Jordison (Slipknot), Alex Bent (Trivium), and other extreme metal drummers for fast, precise double-bass response.

Q: Why do metal drummers choose Axis over other pedal brands?
A: Axis pioneered the direct-drive mechanism, connecting the footboard straight to the beater with a rigid linkage instead of a chain or strap. That eliminates the slippage that costs speed and consistency during extreme double-bass and blast-beat playing.

Q: Is Axis good for extreme metal?
A: Yes. Axis's direct-drive pedals are built specifically for speed, power, and precision, making them a standard choice among extreme metal drummers like Hellhammer (Mayhem) and Martin Lopez (Opeth, Soen) who need fast, reliable double-bass response.

## History

Founded **1990** in Southern California, USA by Darrell Johnston.

Axis Percussion started as Engineered Percussion, the project of drummer and machinist Darrell Johnston, who set out in the late 1980s to build a bass drum pedal with more speed, power, and control than the chain- and strap-drive designs dominating the market.

Johnston's direct-drive mechanism replaced the slack-prone chain or strap of a conventional pedal with a rigid linkage running straight from footboard to beater, eliminating the slippage that costs a hard-hitting drummer speed and consistency.

After a run of prototypes, Johnston's pedal debuted around 1990 as Axis Percussion, based in Southern California. Positive coverage in Modern Drummer and a strong reception at the 1991 NAMM show established the brand's reputation for durable, high-performance engineering.

Axis has since relocated its headquarters to Northern California, in the San Francisco Bay Area, with manufacturing facilities there and in Wisconsin, and now operates under the name AXiS Pedal & Drum Co. while keeping the same direct-drive philosophy that built its reputation.

**Milestones:**
- **late 1980s** — Darrell Johnston begins developing a direct-drive bass drum pedal as Engineered Percussion.
- **1990** — The pedal launches as Axis Percussion in Southern California.
- **1991** — Axis pedals gain wider recognition after coverage in Modern Drummer and a strong showing at the NAMM show.

**Metal era:** Joey Jordison (Slipknot) powered his extreme double-kick blast beats on Axis A Longboard pedals throughout Slipknot's most brutal touring era, and the brand's direct-drive precision has made it a standard choice in extreme metal since: Hellhammer (Mayhem), Alex Bent (Trivium), and Martin Lopez (Opeth, Soen) have all built their double-bass work around Axis pedals.
