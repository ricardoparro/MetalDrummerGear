# DW (Drum Workshop) — Metal Drummer Endorsers

DW (Drum Workshop) is an American drum manufacturer founded in 1972 in Oxnard, California. Renowned for handcrafted shells and the industry-standard 9000 series double pedal, DW is the premium choice for drummers who prioritize American-made craftsmanship. The DW 9000 double pedal is one of the most widely used pedals in metal across all subgenres.

## Key DW Product Lines for Metal

- Collectors Series — handcrafted shells in virtually any configuration; customizable ply, bearing edge, and finish; DW's flagship drum kit
- Performance Series — production-grade maple shells; more accessible price point while retaining DW quality
- 9000 Series Double Pedal — spring-loaded chain drive; direct and responsive feel; the most commonly spotted DW product in metal touring rigs
- 9002 Double Pedal — longboard variant; favored for extended footboard leverage at high BPM
- 9100AL Throne — air-lift height adjustment; standard touring throne used alongside DW kits and non-DW rigs alike

## FAQ

Q: What DW pedal do metal drummers use?
A: The DW 9000 Series Double Pedal is the most widely used DW product among MetalForge's roster. Ray Luzier uses the DW 9002 variant with its longboard design. Both feature DW's chain-drive mechanism, which delivers direct response for double bass patterns at speed.

Q: What drum kit does Art Cruz use?
A: Art Cruz plays a Ludwig Drums kit (Black Beauty snare) with Trick Pro 1-V double pedals and Gibraltar hardware. He previously used DW kits earlier in his career with Prong.

Q: What is the DW Collectors Series?
A: The DW Collectors Series is DW's hand-built custom drum line, available in exotic and standard wood configurations. Hannes Grossmann and Navene Koperweis use the Collectors Series for its precision manufacturing and tonal customization.

Q: Are DW drums used in death metal?
A: Yes. George Kollias (Nile) and Hannes Grossmann (Obscura) are among the technical death metal drummers who use DW Collectors Series kits. DW's 9000 pedal series is equally prevalent across death metal, thrash, and progressive metal touring rigs.
