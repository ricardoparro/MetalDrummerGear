# Evans — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Evans is an American drumhead manufacturer founded in 1957, best known for pioneering the first synthetic (Mylar) drumhead. Now part of D'Addario, Evans remains a staple in metal drumming through lines like the EMAD adjustable-muffling bass head, the oil-filled Hydraulic series, and the durable UV1 coated heads — engineered to hold tuning and tone through aggressive, high-volume playing.

## Key Evans Product Lines for Metal

- EMAD2 — adjustable muffling bass drumhead with two interchangeable control rings for a tight, focused low end; a go-to batter head for blast-beat drummers who need consistent attack without extra tape or pillows
- Hydraulic Series — oil-filled two-ply construction that kills overtones for a dry, punchy sound on snares and toms
- UV1 Coated — UV-cured coating built for extra durability under heavy, aggressive hitting
- Black Chrome — dual-ply coated head with a metallic finish for added durability and a warm, controlled tone; Jay Weinberg's batter head of choice

## FAQ

Q: What Evans drumheads do metal drummers use?
A: Metal drummers commonly use Evans EMAD2 on bass drums for its built-in muffling, and Hydraulic series heads on snares and toms for a dry, controlled attack. Jay Weinberg uses Evans Black Chrome heads for extra durability under heavy playing.

Q: Are Evans drumheads good for metal?
A: Yes, Evans is a top choice for metal drumming. Their heads are engineered for durability and tone control, letting drummers get a focused, punchy sound that cuts through heavily distorted guitars — used by drummers like Joey Jordison, Gene Hoglan, and George Kollias.

Q: What is the Evans EMAD?
A: The EMAD (Externally-Mountable Adjustable Damping) is Evans' flagship bass drumhead system, using removable foam control rings to dial in muffling without needing to open the drum. It's widely used across extreme metal for its tight, click-friendly low end.

Q: Evans EMAD vs Hydraulic for metal?
A: EMAD is designed primarily for bass drums, offering adjustable muffling for a tight low end. Hydraulic heads are oil-filled and work well on snares and toms, killing overtones for a dry, punchy sound. Many metal drummers use both together across a kit.

## History

Founded **1957** in the USA by Chick (Robert) Evans.

Evans was founded in 1957 to manufacture and sell the first synthetic (Mylar) drumhead, which Chick Evans had invented a year earlier in 1956 — replacing calfskin heads and changing drumming forever with a consistent, weather-resistant alternative.

The company built its reputation through the following decades on that original synthetic-head innovation, and in 1995 was acquired by J. D'Addario & Co., which still owns the brand today and has continued to expand its metal-focused product lines like EMAD and Hydraulic.

**Milestones:**
- **1956** — Chick Evans invents the first synthetic drumhead, replacing calfskin.
- **1957** — The company is founded to manufacture and sell the new synthetic heads.
- **1995** — Evans is acquired by J. D'Addario & Co., which still owns the brand today.

**Metal era:** Evans' official artist roster includes Gene Hoglan (Death, Testament, Dethklok) and Chris Adler (Lamb of God), and its heads appear across the touring and studio kits of extreme metal drummers including Joey Jordison, George Kollias, and Eloy Casagrande.
