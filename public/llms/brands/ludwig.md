# Ludwig Drums — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Ludwig is an American drum manufacturer founded in 1909 in Chicago, Illinois, one of the oldest and most historically significant drum companies in the world. Known for the Supraphonic snare (the best-selling snare drum in history) and Classic Maple shells, Ludwig's role in heavy metal traces back to the founding riffs of the genre itself — Bill Ward's Ludwig kit powered Black Sabbath's earliest recordings.

## History

**Founded:** 1909 in Chicago, Illinois, USA · **Founders:** William F. Ludwig Sr. and Theobald Ludwig

Ludwig was founded on December 11, 1909, in Chicago by brothers William F. Ludwig Sr. and Theobald Ludwig, sons of a German immigrant. William, a working drummer, started the company to build a sturdier bass drum pedal than what was commercially available, and the young company quickly expanded into snare drums and timpani, introducing the brass-shell Black Beauty snare by the 1920s.

Ownership shifted in the late 1920s when Ludwig & Ludwig was sold to the C.G. Conn instrument company; William F. Ludwig Sr. responded in 1937 by founding a new, competing company, WFL Drum Co., which introduced the Speed King bass drum pedal in 1938 — a design still used today. In 1955, William and his son William F. Ludwig II repurchased the Ludwig name from Conn.

The single biggest turning point in Ludwig's history came on February 9, 1964, when Ringo Starr appeared on The Ed Sullivan Show with The Beatles playing a Ludwig Black Oyster Pearl kit, seen by an estimated 73 million viewers. Demand for Ludwig kits exploded through the rest of the decade, cementing the company's reputation as "The Most Famous Name in Drums." Corporate ownership continued to change in later decades — Selmer acquired Ludwig in 1981, and production moved to Monroe, North Carolina in 1984 — but Ludwig has kept historic lines like the Black Beauty and Supraphonic in continuous or revived production.

**Timeline:**
- 1909 — William F. Ludwig Sr. and Theobald Ludwig found the company in Chicago and produce the first Ludwig bass drum pedal.
- 1937 — William F. Ludwig Sr. founds the WFL Drum Company in Chicago after the original company was sold to C.G. Conn.
- 1938 — WFL introduces the first Speed King bass drum pedal.
- 1955 — William F. Ludwig Sr. and William F. Ludwig II repurchase the Ludwig name from Conn.
- 1964 — Ringo Starr appears with his Ludwig kit on The Beatles' Ed Sullivan Show, triggering a surge in the brand's popularity.
- 1981 — The Selmer Company acquires Ludwig.
- 1984 — Ludwig production relocates from Chicago to Monroe, North Carolina.

**Ludwig in metal:** Ludwig's association with hard rock and metal runs through the drummers who came up in the wake of the Ringo Starr boom and pushed the kit into heavier territory. John Bonham of Led Zeppelin played Ludwig kits throughout the 1970s, and Bill Ward of Black Sabbath powered the genre's founding recordings on a Ludwig kit. Ludwig's snares — especially the Black Beauty — remain studio staples across metal recording today.

## Metal Drummers Who Use Ludwig

- Bill Ward (Black Sabbath) — Ludwig Classic Maple; Ludwig Supraphonic 14x6.5" LM402 snare; Ludwig Atlas Pro Double Pedal
- Art Cruz (Lamb of God) — Ludwig Drums; Ludwig 14x6.5" Black Beauty snare; Ludwig Atlas Throne

## Key Ludwig Product Lines for Metal

- Classic Maple — all-maple shells in classic configurations; warm, round tone with controlled sustain; Bill Ward's choice for Black Sabbath recordings
- Black Beauty Snare — solid brass shell; warm, full crack with plenty of overtones; Art Cruz's signature snare for Lamb of God live performances
- Supraphonic LM402 — chrome over brass shell; the most recorded snare drum in history; bright, cutting tone ideal for live metal environments
- Atlas Pro Double Pedal — heavy-duty direct-drive mechanism; Ludwig's flagship speed pedal used by Bill Ward on later Sabbath tours
- Legacy Maple — modern reissue of classic maple construction; used in studio and touring contexts

## FAQ

Q: What drums does Bill Ward play?
A: Bill Ward plays a Ludwig Classic Maple drum kit with a Ludwig Supraphonic 14x6.5" LM402 snare drum and Paiste 2002 & Giant Beat cymbals.

Q: What snare does Art Cruz use?
A: Art Cruz uses a Ludwig 14x6.5" Black Beauty snare drum, which produces a warm, full crack that cuts through Lamb of God's heavy guitar tones.

Q: What is the Ludwig Supraphonic snare?
A: The Ludwig Supraphonic LM402 is a chrome-over-brass 14x6.5" snare drum. It is the best-selling snare drum in history, appearing on countless classic rock and metal recordings. Its bright, cutting tone makes it well-suited to high-volume metal environments.

Q: Did Black Sabbath use Ludwig drums?
A: Yes. Bill Ward played Ludwig drum kits on Black Sabbath's foundational albums, including Paranoid (1970) and Master of Reality (1971). His jazz-influenced approach on Ludwig Classic Maple shells helped define the proto-metal drum sound.

Q: What drum brand does Lamb of God's drummer use?
A: Art Cruz, the current Lamb of God drummer, plays Ludwig Drums with a Ludwig Black Beauty snare. His predecessor Chris Adler used a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab.
