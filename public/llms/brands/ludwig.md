# Ludwig Drums — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Ludwig is an American drum manufacturer founded in 1909 in Chicago, Illinois, one of the oldest and most historically significant drum companies in the world. Known for the Supraphonic snare (the best-selling snare drum in history) and Classic Maple shells, Ludwig's role in heavy metal traces back to the founding riffs of the genre itself — Bill Ward's Ludwig kit powered Black Sabbath's earliest recordings.

## Metal Drummers Who Use Ludwig

- Bill Ward (Black Sabbath) — Ludwig Classic Maple; Ludwig Supraphonic 14x6.5" LM402 snare; Ludwig Atlas Pro Double Pedal
- Art Cruz (Lamb of God) — Ludwig Drums; Ludwig 14x6.5" Black Beauty snare; Ludwig Atlas Throne

## Key Ludwig Product Lines for Metal

- Classic Maple — all-maple shells in classic configurations; warm, round tone with controlled sustain; Bill Ward's choice for Black Sabbath recordings
- Black Beauty Snare — solid brass shell; warm, full crack with plenty of overtones; Art Cruz's signature snare for Lamb of God live performances
- Supraphonic LM402 — chrome over brass shell; the most recorded snare drum in history; bright, cutting tone ideal for live metal environments
- Atlas Pro Double Pedal — heavy-duty direct-drive mechanism; Ludwig's flagship speed pedal used by Bill Ward on later Sabbath tours
- Legacy Maple — modern reissue of classic maple construction; used in studio and touring contexts

## FAQ

Q: What drums does Bill Ward play?
A: Bill Ward plays a Ludwig Classic Maple drum kit with a Ludwig Supraphonic 14x6.5" LM402 snare drum and Paiste 2002 & Giant Beat cymbals.

Q: What snare does Art Cruz use?
A: Art Cruz uses a Ludwig 14x6.5" Black Beauty snare drum, which produces a warm, full crack that cuts through Lamb of God's heavy guitar tones.

Q: What is the Ludwig Supraphonic snare?
A: The Ludwig Supraphonic LM402 is a chrome-over-brass 14x6.5" snare drum. It is the best-selling snare drum in history, appearing on countless classic rock and metal recordings. Its bright, cutting tone makes it well-suited to high-volume metal environments.

Q: Did Black Sabbath use Ludwig drums?
A: Yes. Bill Ward played Ludwig drum kits on Black Sabbath's foundational albums, including Paranoid (1970) and Master of Reality (1971). His jazz-influenced approach on Ludwig Classic Maple shells helped define the proto-metal drum sound.

Q: What drum brand does Lamb of God's drummer use?
A: Art Cruz, the current Lamb of God drummer, plays Ludwig Drums with a Ludwig Black Beauty snare. His predecessor Chris Adler used a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab.

## History

Founded **1909** in Chicago, Illinois, USA, by William F. Ludwig and Theobald Ludwig.

Ludwig & Ludwig was founded in Chicago in 1909 by brothers William F. Ludwig Sr. and Theobald Ludwig. Their first product was a practical bass drum pedal, letting a single drummer control the bass drum by foot instead of relying on a second player — a foundational innovation for the modern drum kit.

The company grew quickly into one of America's dominant drum manufacturers, and in the 1920s introduced the Black Beauty, a brass-shell snare drum that remains one of the most recorded snares in music history.

The brand's defining moment arrived on February 9, 1964, when Ringo Starr appeared with his Ludwig kit on The Ed Sullivan Show during the Beatles' American television debut, watched by an estimated 73 million viewers. Demand for Ludwig drums outstripped supply for years afterward in what became known as the "Ludwig boom."

Ludwig relocated its manufacturing from Chicago to Monroe, North Carolina in 1984, and the company continues to build on its rock and metal legacy today.

**Milestones:**
- **1909** — William F. and Theobald Ludwig found the company in Chicago and introduce the first practical bass drum pedal.
- **1920s** — The Black Beauty brass snare drum is introduced and becomes one of the most recorded snares in history.
- **1938** — William F. Ludwig Jr. joins the family company.
- **1964** — Ringo Starr's Ludwig kit appears on The Ed Sullivan Show, sparking the "Ludwig boom" in rock drumming.
- **1984** — Ludwig relocates its manufacturing from Chicago to Monroe, North Carolina.

**Metal era:** John Bonham's powerful, bombastic style on a Ludwig kit throughout Led Zeppelin's catalog helped define the drumming vocabulary metal would inherit, and Bill Ward played Ludwig on Black Sabbath's earliest, genre-defining records. Decades later, the Black Beauty and Supraphonic snares remain studio staples for metal drummers seeking a cutting, present attack.
