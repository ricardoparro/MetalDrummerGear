# Mapex Drums — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Mapex is a Taiwanese drum manufacturer founded in 1989, widely respected for their Saturn and Black Panther series. Mapex drums have become a prominent choice in American groove metal and modern thrash, offering powerful maple shells and their own Falcon double pedal platform. The brand gained significant metal credibility through Chris Adler's decade-plus endorsement during Lamb of God's commercial peak.

## History

**Founded:** 1989 in Taipei, Taiwan · **Founder:** KHS Musical Instruments (KHS Group)

Mapex was founded in 1989 in Taiwan by KHS Musical Instruments, a company with decades of prior experience in wood processing and instrument manufacturing that had previously built drums for other brands rather than under its own name. The name Mapex is an acronym for Music And Percussion EXcellence, reflecting the founders' intent to move beyond contract manufacturing and build a brand of its own.

The early 1990s established Mapex as a serious player rather than a budget alternative. In 1992, the company introduced its premium Orion Custom Maple and Orion Studio Birch series, and drummer Billy Cobham became one of Mapex's best-known artist endorsers. In 1993, Mapex released the Mars Pro series, the first mid-range lacquer-finished drum set on the market. The company expanded manufacturing and distribution through the 1990s, establishing Mapex USA in Tennessee in 1996 and launching the Black Panther snare series and ITS (Isolated Tom System) mounting technology in 1997.

In the 2010s, Mapex leaned further into the Black Panther platform as its flagship snare line, launching the Black Panther Design Lab in 2017 as an incubator for experimental, custom-configured snare and kit designs — the platform Chris Adler used throughout his run with Lamb of God.

**Timeline:**
- 1989 — KHS Musical Instruments launches the Mapex brand in Taipei, Taiwan.
- 1992 — Mapex introduces its premium Orion Custom Maple and Orion Studio Birch series; Billy Cobham becomes a key endorser.
- 1993 — Mapex releases the Mars Pro series, the first mid-range lacquer-finished drum set on the market.
- 1996 — Mapex USA is established in La Vergne, Tennessee.
- 1997 — The Black Panther snare drum series and ITS (Isolated Tom System) technology launch.
- 2000 — Mapex expands manufacturing with a new facility in Tianjin, China.
- 2017 — Mapex launches the Black Panther Design Lab as a dedicated snare-and-kit design incubator.

**Mapex in metal:** Mapex's clearest connection to metal runs through the Black Panther snare line, launched in 1997, which built a reputation for aggressive, cutting metal-shell snares. That platform found its highest-profile metal use in Chris Adler's decade-plus run with Lamb of God, where his Black Panther Design Lab kit and signature Walnut/Maple snare powered albums including Ashes of the Wake, Sacrament, and Wrath.

- Arin Ilejay (ex-Avenged Sevenfold) — Mapex Saturn Series; Mapex Falcon Double Pedal

## Key Mapex Product Lines for Metal

- Saturn V — maple/walnut hybrid shells; warm, punchy tone with enhanced sustain; Jason Bittner's touring kit with Overkill
- Black Panther Design Lab — custom-configured shells in non-standard configurations; used by Chris Adler for Lamb of God's touring and studio work
- Black Panther Snare Series — wide range of shell materials including brass, steel, and wood; Mapex Black Panther 14x6.5" Brass used by Jason Bittner
- Falcon Double Pedal — Mapex's flagship speed pedal; strap-drive mechanism; used by Chris Adler and Jason Bittner as the primary hardware choice

## FAQ

Q: What drums does Chris Adler play?
A: Chris Adler plays a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab drum kit with a Mapex Chris Adler Signature 14x5.5" Walnut/Maple snare, Meinl Byzance cymbals, and Mapex Falcon Double Pedal.

Q: What drum kit does Jason Bittner use?
A: Jason Bittner plays a Mapex Saturn V with a Mapex Black Panther 14x6.5" Brass snare and Mapex Falcon Double Pedal.

Q: What cymbals does Chris Adler use?
A: Chris Adler uses Meinl Byzance Series cymbals including 14" Dark Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Dark Crashes, 21" Transition Ride, and 18" Extra Dry China.

Q: What is the Mapex Falcon pedal?
A: The Mapex Falcon is Mapex's premium double bass pedal, using a strap-drive mechanism for a lighter, more responsive feel. It was Chris Adler's primary pedal choice throughout his career with Lamb of God.

Q: Did Lamb of God use Mapex drums?
A: Yes. Chris Adler, Lamb of God's original drummer (1994–2019), was a long-time Mapex endorser and used the Black Panther Design Lab for the band's commercial peak albums including Ashes of the Wake and Sacrament.
