# Pro-Mark Drumsticks — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Pro-Mark is an American drumstick maker founded in 1957 in Houston, Texas by Herb Brochstein. Owned by D'Addario since 2011, Pro-Mark remains a workhorse stick brand across thrash, death, and industrial metal.

## Key Pro-Mark Product Lines for Metal

- TX5AXW Chris Adler Signature — a slimmer, technical 5A-class stick with an acorn wood tip, built for groove-driven precision
- 5A/5B Hickory — the everyday workhorse sizes played by Gene Hoglan, Dave Lombardo, and Mario Duplantier
- Model 2B — extra-heavy weight and extra-wide diameter, built for the hardest hitters
- Oak Nylon Tip — Japanese Shira Kashi white oak sticks with a durable nylon tip

## History

Founded **1957** in Houston, Texas, USA by Herb Brochstein.

Pro-Mark began in 1957 when Herb Brochstein, a Houston drum teacher and shop owner, was sold six pairs of Japanese-made drumsticks by a passing salesman — sticks that turned out to be noticeably better made than anything available domestically at the time.

Brochstein tracked down the Japanese factory with the help of Tat Kosaka and began importing sticks built to his own specifications, founding Pro-Mark to sell them in the United States. The name came from the phrase "The Mark Of A Professional."

When Brochstein finally visited the factory in person, he found it wasn't a factory at all — just two workers shaping each stick by hand with hand tools. Those hand-finished sticks turned out to be more consistent than the machine-made sticks most American drum companies were producing at the time, and that quality reputation carried Pro-Mark's early growth.

Brochstein's son Maury joined the family business and helped expand Pro-Mark's product line and international reach, and the company brought Japanese Shira Kashi white oak to the US market for the first time.

D'Addario acquired Pro-Mark in 2011, folding it into the same corporate family as Evans drumheads, but the company continues to operate out of Houston with the Brochstein family still involved in the business.

**Milestones:**
- **1957** — Herb Brochstein founds Pro-Mark in Houston, Texas, importing Japanese-made drumsticks.
- **1960s** — Pro-Mark becomes the first US stick brand to bring Japanese Shira Kashi white oak to market.
- **2011** — D'Addario acquires Pro-Mark, which continues operating out of Houston.
