# Pro-Mark Drumsticks — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Pro-Mark is a Houston, Texas drumstick maker founded in 1957 by Herb Brochstein, and the first US stick brand to bring Japanese Shira Kashi white oak to market. Now owned by D'Addario (since 2011), Pro-Mark runs one of the largest stick catalogs in the industry, with a signature roster spanning thrash, groove, death, and progressive metal.

## Key Pro-Mark Product Lines for Metal

- Model 2B — extra-heavy weight and extra-wide diameter, built for the hardest of hitters
- Shira Kashi White Oak Series — dense Japanese white oak sticks, an alternative to hickory for extra durability
- Signature Series — artist-specific models spanning thrash, groove, death, and progressive metal
- TX/Autograph lines — the core signature-model numbering system covering most of Pro-Mark's metal roster

## History

Founded **1957** in Houston, Texas, USA, by Herb Brochstein.

Pro-Mark traces back to a chance encounter in 1950s Houston: drummer and shop owner Herb Brochstein was sold six pairs of Japanese-made drumsticks by a passing salesman, and found them better made than the domestic sticks he'd been selling.

Brochstein tracked down the Japanese factory through contact Tatsuo Kosaka, and in 1957 founded Pro-Mark — its name derived from "The Mark of a Professional" — to import and sell the sticks in the United States. Pro-Mark became the first stick maker to bring Japanese Shira Kashi white oak to the US market.

The company remained a family business for over five decades, passing from Herb Brochstein to his son Maury Brochstein, until D'Addario & Company purchased Pro-Mark in 2011.

**Milestones:**
- **1957** — Herb Brochstein founds Pro-Mark in Houston, Texas, to import Japanese-made drumsticks.
- **2011** — D'Addario & Company purchases Pro-Mark from the Brochstein family.
- **2013** — Founder Herb Brochstein passes away at age 85, having led Pro-Mark for over five decades.

**Metal era:** Pro-Mark's broad, durable catalog has made it a common choice for hard-hitting metal drummers across every subgenre, with signature models for Dave Lombardo (Slayer), Joey Jordison (Slipknot), Chris Adler (Lamb of God), Gene Hoglan (Death, Testament), and Jason Bittner (Overkill).
