# Remo — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Remo is the world's largest drumhead manufacturer, founded by Remo Belli in North Hollywood, California, in 1957. Alongside Evans, Remo helped pioneer the modern synthetic drumhead, and its product line — from the muffled Powerstroke 3 bass head to the durable two-ply Emperor series — spans nearly every style of drumming, including metal.

## Key Remo Product Lines for Metal

- Powerstroke 3 — built-in muffling ring for a punchy, controlled bass drum sound; used by Mario Duplantier on bass drums and one of the most widely used metal batter heads
- Emperor Series — two-ply construction for durability and a full, controlled tone; used by Mario Duplantier on toms and by Nicko McBrain across his kit
- Ambassador Series — classic single-ply heads for a bright, open sound
- Pinstripe Series — two-ply design with a built-in overtone-control ring for extra durability under aggressive playing

## FAQ

Q: What Remo drumheads do metal drummers use?
A: Metal drummers frequently use the Remo Powerstroke 3 on bass drums for its built-in muffling ring, and Emperor heads on snares and toms for their durability and full, controlled tone. Mario Duplantier uses Remo Emperor on toms and Powerstroke 3 on bass drums.

Q: Are Remo drumheads good for metal?
A: Yes, Remo is one of the most widely used drumhead brands in metal. Their Powerstroke and Emperor lines are built to handle aggressive, high-volume playing while staying in tune through long sets — used by drummers like Lars Ulrich, Tomas Haake, and Nicko McBrain.

Q: What drumheads does Lars Ulrich use?
A: Lars Ulrich uses Remo drumheads across his Tama Starclassic Maple kit, paired with his Tama LU1465 Lars Ulrich Signature 14x6.5" snare.

Q: Remo Emperor vs Ambassador for metal?
A: Emperor heads are two-ply, offering more durability and a fuller, more controlled tone that suits heavier playing. Ambassador heads are single-ply, giving a brighter and more open sound. Many metal drummers, including Nicko McBrain, prefer Emperor for its durability under aggressive hitting.

## History

Founded **1957** in North Hollywood, California, by Remo Belli, alongside partners Roy Harte and chemist Sam Muchnick.

Remo was founded on June 1, 1957, when the WeatherKing synthetic drumhead debuted at NAMM, giving drummers a weather-resistant alternative to calfskin. Belli and Muchnick were awarded a US patent for the synthetic drumhead in 1960, cementing Remo's place alongside Evans as a pioneer of the modern drumhead.

The company's manufacturing consistency and broad product lineup grew Remo into the world's largest drumhead manufacturer, with the Powerstroke 3 — introduced in 1993 — becoming one of the most widely used bass drumheads in metal.

**Milestones:**
- **1957** — Remo Belli founds the company; the WeatherKing synthetic drumhead debuts at NAMM.
- **1960** — Belli and Muchnick are awarded a US patent for the synthetic drumhead.
- **1993** — The Powerstroke 3 bass drumhead is introduced and becomes a widely used metal bass drum batter head.

**Metal era:** Lars Ulrich (Metallica) uses a Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear bass drumhead, per Remo's official artist profile, and Remo heads appear across the touring kits of metal drummers including Tomas Haake (Meshuggah), Mario Duplantier (Gojira), and Nicko McBrain (Iron Maiden).
