# Sabian Cymbals — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Sabian is a Canadian cymbal manufacturer founded in 1981, born from the legendary Zildjian factory. Based in Meductic, New Brunswick, Sabian produces handcrafted cymbals across a wide range of alloys and finishes, making them a staple in heavy metal, death metal, and thrash drumming worldwide.

## History

**Founded:** 1981 in Meductic, New Brunswick, Canada · **Founder:** Robert Zildjian

Sabian was born out of a family rupture. Robert Zildjian, a member of the Zildjian cymbal dynasty tracing back to 1623 Constantinople, had helped run the Zildjian company's Canadian operations for years. After his brother Armand became president of the family firm in 1977, a conflict developed between the siblings following their father's death in 1979, and in 1981 Robert left to found a new company at the former Azco factory Zildjian itself had built in Meductic, New Brunswick in 1968. He named the company Sabian by combining the first letters of his three children's names: Sally, Billy, and Andy.

The new company moved quickly, turning out roughly 45,000 cymbals in its first full year of production and launching the AA and HH series that remain cornerstones of the brand today. Through the 1990s and 2000s, Sabian expanded aggressively, working directly with artists like Larrie Londin, Terry Bozzio, and Neil Peart to develop signature product lines including AAX, Radia, and Paragon, and grew beyond cymbals with the 2010 acquisition of Latin percussion maker Gon Bops.

Now in its fifth decade, Sabian remains headquartered at the same Meductic, New Brunswick site where it began, having grown from a splinter of the centuries-old Zildjian dynasty into one of the world's major cymbal manufacturers in its own right.

**Timeline:**
- 1968 — Zildjian builds the Azco plant in Meductic, New Brunswick — the facility that later becomes Sabian's headquarters.
- 1981 — Robert Zildjian leaves the family company and founds Sabian at the former Azco plant in Meductic, New Brunswick.
- 1982 — Sabian's first full year of production yields roughly 45,000 cymbals, including the debut AA and HH series.
- 1993 — The AA Rocktagon crash and a Larrie Londin signature ride lead toward the AAX line.
- 2001 — The HHX series launches, applying extensive hand-hammering for a darker, more complex tone.
- 2004 — Neil Peart collaborates with Sabian's R&D team to develop the Paragon series.
- 2010 — Sabian acquires Latin percussion maker Gon Bops.

**Sabian in metal:** Sabian's AAX and HHX lines are widely used across rock and metal for their bright, cutting bronze tone that projects through dense, distorted mixes — comparable to Zildjian's A Custom and K series — and the brand has built credibility through artist partnerships and steady product expansion since splitting from Zildjian in 1981.

## Key Sabian Product Lines for Metal

- HHX Series — hand-hammered, brilliant or natural finish; complex, musical tone with fast response; popular in progressive and melodic metal
- AAX Series — bright, cutting attack with quick decay; ideal for live metal performance where projection matters
- AA Series — classic alloy; warm wash with clear definition; used across rock and heavier applications
- B8X Series — entry-level; bronze alloy with consistent sound; widely used in practice and budget metal rigs
- Vault Series — bespoke, made-to-order cymbals; maximum customization for professional endorsers
- HH Series — hand-hammered; dark, dry, and controlled; suits metal drummers seeking vintage projection

## FAQ

Q: What cymbals does Nicko McBrain use?
A: Nicko McBrain plays Sabian HHX and HH Series cymbals, with a preference for HH Medium Ride and HHX China cymbals. His setup pairs large, commanding rides with quick-cutting crash cymbals to match Iron Maiden's galloping rhythmic style.

Q: Are Sabian cymbals good for metal?
A: Yes. The Sabian AAX and HHX Series are widely used in metal for their cutting attack and fast response. AAX Explosions are a popular crash choice for high-volume metal stages, while HHX rides deliver the complex wash needed for progressive and technical metal drummers.

Q: What is the difference between Sabian HH and HHX?
A: Sabian HH cymbals are fully hand-hammered with a traditional, dark, and controlled character. HHX cymbals extend that process with additional hammering and specialized lathing for a more complex, musical tone with greater overtone depth. HHX is generally preferred in modern metal contexts.

Q: Does Sabian make hi-hats for metal drumming?
A: Yes. The Sabian HHX Evolution Hi-Hats (used by Alex Bent) and AAX Stage Hi-Hats are popular choices in metal. Both offer a crisp, tight chick and fast response suited to double-bass-heavy playing and technical patterns.
