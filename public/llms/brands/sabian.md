# Sabian Cymbals — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Sabian is a Canadian cymbal manufacturer founded in 1981, born from the legendary Zildjian factory. Based in Meductic, New Brunswick, Sabian produces handcrafted cymbals across a wide range of alloys and finishes, making them a staple in heavy metal, death metal, and thrash drumming worldwide.

## Metal Drummers Who Use Sabian

- Nicko McBrain (Iron Maiden) — Sabian HHX Evolution and HH Series; primarily HH Medium Ride and HHX China
- Neil Peart (Rush) — Sabian HH and HHX Series; signature Paragon cymbals developed in close collaboration with Sabian
- Chris Adler (Lamb of God) — Sabian AAX Series; 20" AAX Stage Ride and 18" AAX Stage Crash
- Shannon Larkin (Godsmack) — Sabian HHX and AAX; 22" HHX Legacy Ride
- Alex Bent (Trivium) — Sabian HHX Series; 22" HHX Manhattan Ride and Evolution Hi-Hats
- Aquiles Priester (Hangar 18, ex-Angra) — Sabian HHX and AA Series; custom prototype rides and crashes
- Paul Bostaph (Slayer, Testament) — Sabian AAX and Artisan Series; crash-heavy setup favoring AAX Explosions

## Key Sabian Product Lines for Metal

- HHX Series — hand-hammered, brilliant or natural finish; complex, musical tone with fast response; popular in progressive and melodic metal
- AAX Series — bright, cutting attack with quick decay; ideal for live metal performance where projection matters
- AA Series — classic alloy; warm wash with clear definition; used across rock and heavier applications
- B8X Series — entry-level; bronze alloy with consistent sound; widely used in practice and budget metal rigs
- Vault Series — bespoke, made-to-order cymbals; maximum customization for professional endorsers
- HH Series — hand-hammered; dark, dry, and controlled; suits metal drummers seeking vintage projection

## FAQ

Q: What cymbals does Nicko McBrain use?
A: Nicko McBrain plays Sabian HHX and HH Series cymbals, with a preference for HH Medium Ride and HHX China cymbals. His setup pairs large, commanding rides with quick-cutting crash cymbals to match Iron Maiden's galloping rhythmic style.

Q: Are Sabian cymbals good for metal?
A: Yes. The Sabian AAX and HHX Series are widely used in metal for their cutting attack and fast response. AAX Explosions are a popular crash choice for high-volume metal stages, while HHX rides deliver the complex wash needed for progressive and technical metal drummers.

Q: What is the difference between Sabian HH and HHX?
A: Sabian HH cymbals are fully hand-hammered with a traditional, dark, and controlled character. HHX cymbals extend that process with additional hammering and specialized lathing for a more complex, musical tone with greater overtone depth. HHX is generally preferred in modern metal contexts.

Q: Does Sabian make hi-hats for metal drumming?
A: Yes. The Sabian HHX Evolution Hi-Hats (used by Alex Bent) and AAX Stage Hi-Hats are popular choices in metal. Both offer a crisp, tight chick and fast response suited to double-bass-heavy playing and technical patterns.

## History

Founded **1981** in Meductic, New Brunswick, Canada, by Robert Zildjian.

Sabian exists because of a rift inside the centuries-old Zildjian cymbal dynasty. By the late 1970s, brothers Robert and Armand Zildjian could not agree on the direction of the family's Avedis Zildjian Company, and in 1979 the business split between the two branches of the family.

In 1981, Robert Zildjian took his share of the family's cymbal-making expertise across the border and founded a new company in Meductic, a small town in rural New Brunswick, Canada.

Rather than position the new company as a discount alternative to Zildjian, Sabian invested in its own foundry and alloy research from the outset. Its first full year of production in 1982 turned out roughly 45,000 cymbals, including the debut of the AA and HH series that would become the foundation of its catalog.

Over the following decades Sabian built out AAX — a bright, fast-responding line — and HHX, a hand-hammered, darker counterpart introduced in 2001, giving the company two flagship series that speak directly to metal drummers who need either cutting projection or complex, dark overtones.

**Milestones:**
- **1979** — The Zildjian family cymbal business splits between Robert and Armand Zildjian's branches of the family.
- **1981** — Robert Zildjian founds Sabian in Meductic, New Brunswick, Canada.
- **1982** — Sabian's first full production year turns out roughly 45,000 cymbals, including the debut AA and HH series.
- **2001** — The HHX series launches, giving Sabian a hand-hammered, darker flagship line alongside AAX.
- **2010** — Sabian acquires hand-percussion maker Gon Bops.

**Metal era:** Sabian's AAX and HHX series became mainstays of modern metal within a generation of the company's founding. Ray Luzier (Korn) and Tomas Haake (Meshuggah) are official Sabian artists, both known for AAX and HHX cymbal setups.
