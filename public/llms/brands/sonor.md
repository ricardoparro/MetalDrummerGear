# Sonor Drums — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Sonor is a German drum manufacturer founded in 1875, renowned for its fully customizable SQ2 shell system and artist signature snares spanning brass, bronze, and heavy beech. The brand has become a first-call choice for progressive and technical metal drummers who need German-engineered precision and power.

## History

Founded **1875** in Weißenfels, Germany by Johannes Link.

Sonor traces its roots to 1875, when Johannes Link, a trained wood turner and tanner, opened a small workshop in Weißenfels, Germany, producing drumheads and simple military drums.

The company expanded steadily through the following decades: Link acquired his own sawmill in 1902, and in 1907 the SONOR trademark — Latin for "sonorous" or "resounding" — was registered with the Imperial Patent Office in Berlin. By 1925, the growing firm employed 145 people, making it one of the largest drum manufacturers of its kind in Germany.

Johannes Link's son Otto took over management of the company, and after the devastation of World War II, Otto and his son Horst rebuilt the business from a former barracks building in Aue, restarting production in 1946.

Sonor pushed drum design forward through the second half of the 20th century, introducing the first drum kit with concert toms in 1952 and, around its 100th anniversary in 1975, a 45-degree bearing edge design that became a hallmark of the brand's tone.

That engineering focus carried into Sonor's modern era with the 2006 launch of the SQ2 custom drum system — a fully configurable shell platform that gives Sonor the flexible, German-precision foundation that has made it a favorite among progressive and technical metal drummers.

**Milestones:**
- **1875** — Johannes Link opens a drumhead and military-drum workshop in Weißenfels, Germany.
- **1907** — The SONOR trademark is registered with the Imperial Patent Office in Berlin.
- **1946** — Otto and Horst Link rebuild the company in Aue after World War II.
- **1987** — The HLD 590 cast bronze snare drum debuts, becoming a Sonor signature model.
- **2006** — The fully customizable SQ2 drum system launches.

**Metal era:** Sonor's SQ2 system and artist-signature snare program have made it a first-call brand for progressive and technical metal: Tomas Haake (Meshuggah) has played Sonor SQ2 Heavy Beech kits and his own signature snare since helping found the djent sound, Danny Carey (Tool) built his sprawling kit around SQ2 Heavy Beech shells and a signature bronze snare, and Nicko McBrain (Iron Maiden) has powered Iron Maiden's galloping rhythms on an SQ2 kit with his own signature snare for decades. Hellhammer (Mayhem) has also relied on Sonor SQ2 Heavy Beech shells for the dense low end extreme black metal demands.
