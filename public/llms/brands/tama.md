# Tama Drums — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Tama is a Japanese drum brand launched in 1974 by Hoshino Gakki, widely regarded as the industry standard for metal drumming. Known for their Starclassic series shells and Iron Cobra hardware, Tama kits offer exceptional projection, durability, and punch required for high-volume metal performance.

## History

**Founded:** 1974 (TAMA brand launch) · **Parent company:** Hoshino Gakki, founded 1908 in Nagoya, Japan

Tama's roots trace back to Hoshino Gakki, a Japanese company founded in 1908 in Nagoya as a bookstore trading in sheet music before it grew into a full musical instrument importer and manufacturer. In the early 1960s Hoshino built its own instrument factory, nicknamed "Tama Seisakusho" after Tama Hoshino, the wife of the company's first president. Drum production initially ran under the "Star" name from 1966.

In 1974, Hoshino Gakki consolidated its drum ambitions into a single global brand, TAMA, launching with the mahogany-shelled Imperialstar series. Through the late 1970s and 1980s, TAMA built its reputation on engineering: the Superstar line drew players like Stewart Copeland and Billy Cobham, the Omnisphere ball tom mount arrived in 1980, and the Iron Cobra bass drum pedal, introduced in the mid-1980s, became one of the most widely copied double-pedal designs in the industry.

TAMA's highest-profile artist relationship began in 1984 when Metallica's Lars Ulrich became a company endorser — a partnership that has now run for four decades and produced multiple signature snares and, in 2024, a limited 40th Anniversary Signature Drum Kit.

**Timeline:**
- 1908 — Hoshino Gakki is founded in Nagoya, Japan, initially as a sheet-music and instrument retailer.
- 1962 — Hoshino Gakki opens the "Tama Seisakusho" manufacturing factory, named for Tama Hoshino.
- 1966 — Drum production begins under the "Star" brand name.
- 1974 — Hoshino Gakki launches the TAMA brand globally, debuting the Imperialstar series.
- 1980 — TAMA introduces the Omnisphere ball tom holder.
- 1983 — TAMA launches the Artstar line with thin 6mm shells.
- 1984 — Lars Ulrich of Metallica begins his endorsement relationship with TAMA.
- 2024 — TAMA releases the Lars Ulrich 40th Anniversary Signature Drum Kit.

**Tama in metal:** TAMA is one of the most metal-associated drum brands in the world, anchored by Lars Ulrich's endorsement, which began in 1984 and remains active four decades later. Beyond Ulrich, TAMA's Starclassic shells and Iron Cobra pedal series are staples across thrash, death, and technical metal, prized for the durability and precision needed for extreme playing.

## Key Tama Product Lines for Metal

- Starclassic Maple — 100% maple shells; punchy, warm attack; the most popular Tama model in metal
- Starclassic Bubinga — exotic bubinga shells; dense tone with extended low-end; used by Mario Duplantier and Eloy Casagrande
- Starclassic Walnut/Birch — warm walnut with birch inner ply; modern hybrid sound
- Starclassic Performer B/B — birch/bubinga blend; articulate and focused; used by Brann Dailor
- S.L.P. Snare Series — G-Maple, Bell Brass, Big Black Steel; preferred snares for studio and live metal use
- Iron Cobra 900 Double Pedal — cam-driven mechanism; Power Glide and Rolling Glide options; de facto standard for thrash and death metal
- Speed Cobra Double Pedal — lighter feel; spring-assist tension; preferred by progressively-minded drummers

## FAQ

Q: What drums does Lars Ulrich play?
A: Lars Ulrich plays a Tama Starclassic Maple drum kit, paired with his signature Tama LU1465 snare (14x6.5") and Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide double pedals.

Q: What Tama kit does Mario Duplantier use?
A: Mario Duplantier plays a Tama Starclassic Bubinga with dual 22"x18" bass drums, 12"x9" and 13"x10" rack toms, and a 16"x16" floor tom. He uses a Tama S.L.P. 14x6.5" G-Maple snare.

Q: What Tama double pedal is best for metal?
A: The Tama Iron Cobra 900 Double Pedal is the most widely used double pedal in metal. The Power Glide cam suits players who want a consistent feel across the stroke range, while the Rolling Glide delivers a more linear response.

Q: What Tama drum kit is used in thrash metal?
A: The Tama Starclassic Maple is the dominant thrash metal choice, used by Lars Ulrich, Dave Lombardo, and Charlie Benante. Its maple shells offer the cutting attack and projection needed to punch through heavy guitar riffs.

Q: Does Tama make a snare drum for metal?
A: Yes. The Tama S.L.P. G-Maple (used by Dave Lombardo and Mario Duplantier), Tama Bell Brass (used by Eloy Casagrande), and the Lars Ulrich Signature LU1465 are all Tama snares built for high-volume metal environments.
