# Vater Drumsticks — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Vater is a family-owned Massachusetts stick maker with roots in a 1950s Boston drum shop, launched as its own branded company in 1991. Built on 100%-USA manufacturing and batch-to-batch consistency, Vater has become a favorite of hard-hitting rock and metal drummers.

## Metal Drummers Who Use Vater

- Jay Weinberg (Slipknot, Suicidal Tendencies) — 908 signature model (blends 5A/5B proportions)
- Mike Mangini (ex-Dream Theater) — VHMMWP Wicked Piston signature model
- Derek Roddy (Hate Eternal, Nile) — VHDRW Player's Design Derek Roddy Model
- Brann Dailor (Mastodon) — 5B Wood Tip (confirmed endorser of the stock model, no custom signature)

## Key Vater Product Lines for Metal

- Power series — extra-thick, extra-durable models aimed at hard-hitting players
- Signature Series — artist-specific models built to the exact specs of Jay Weinberg, Mike Mangini, and Derek Roddy
- 5A/5B Wood — Vater's core hickory lineup, endorsed by drummers like Brann Dailor in stock form

## FAQ

Q: What drumsticks does Jay Weinberg use?
A: Jay Weinberg (Slipknot, Suicidal Tendencies) plays the Vater 908, a signature model blending 5A and 5B proportions.

Q: What sticks does Mike Mangini use?
A: Mike Mangini plays the Vater Wicked Piston (VHMMWP), a hickory signature model with a long taper and wood tip.

Q: Is Vater good for metal?
A: Yes. Vater's 100%-USA manufacturing is built around tight batch-to-batch consistency — every pair playing identically — which has made it a favorite of hard-hitting metal drummers, including Jay Weinberg, Mike Mangini, and Derek Roddy.

Q: Where are Vater drumsticks made?
A: Vater drumsticks are manufactured entirely in the USA, out of the company's factory in Holbrook, Massachusetts, which the Vater family opened in 1988.

Q: What sticks does Derek Roddy use?
A: Derek Roddy (Hate Eternal, Nile) plays the Vater Player's Design VHDRW, a hickory model between 5A and 5B sizing with a small acorn wood tip.

## History

Launched as **Vater Percussion** in **1991**, with roots to a 1956 Boston-area drum shop; based in Holbrook, Massachusetts, USA. Founded by Alan Vater and Ron Vater.

Vater's roots reach back to the 1950s: Boston drummer Jack Adams ran Jack's Drum Shop, and in 1956 stick-turner Fred Maichle began hand-crafting drumsticks for the shop, occasionally supplying custom sticks to touring musicians like Philly Joe Jones, Louie Bellson, and Buddy Rich.

Adams's son-in-law Clarence "Clarry" Vater established the C. Vater Music Center in Norwood, Massachusetts, and Clarence's sons Alan and Ron Vater grew up working the shop alongside their father and grandfather. The brothers began manufacturing drumsticks under contract for larger brands — first Vic Firth, then from 1986 Zildjian's own stick line.

The Vaters opened a dedicated factory in Holbrook, Massachusetts on October 22, 1988, and when the contract-manufacturing relationships with Zildjian and Vic Firth wound down soon after, they launched their own branded company, Vater Percussion, in 1991. Vater grew into one of the top five American drumstick manufacturers by the mid-1990s.

**Milestones:**
- **1956** — Fred Maichle begins hand-turning drumsticks for Jack Adams's Jack's Drum Shop in the Boston area.
- **1986** — Alan and Ron Vater begin manufacturing Zildjian's drumstick line under contract.
- **1988** — The Vater family opens a dedicated factory in Holbrook, Massachusetts.
- **1991** — Alan and Ron Vater launch Vater Percussion as its own branded company.

**Metal era:** Vater's consistency-first manufacturing has built a metal following anchored by signature models: Jay Weinberg (Slipknot) plays the 908, Mike Mangini (ex-Dream Theater) the Wicked Piston, and Derek Roddy (Hate Eternal, Nile) the Player's Design VHDRW. Brann Dailor (Mastodon) is a confirmed endorser of Vater's stock 5B Wood Tip.
