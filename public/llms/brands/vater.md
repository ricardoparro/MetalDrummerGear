# Vater Drumsticks — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Vater is an independent, family-owned American drumstick maker headquartered in Holbrook, Massachusetts, with roots tracing to a 1950s Boston drum shop. The Vater brand itself launched in 1990 and remains a go-to choice for hard-hitting metal drummers.

## FAQ

Q: What Vater sticks do metal drummers use?
A: Jay Weinberg (Slipknot) plays his own Vater 908 Signature. Mike Mangini (Dream Theater) plays the Vater Wicked Piston, Derek Roddy plays a Vater Player's Design signature, and Paul Bostaph (Slayer) and Brann Dailor (Mastodon) both play Vater's Power and 5-series sticks.

Q: Is Vater good for metal?
A: Yes. Vater's 100%-USA manufacturing and pair-to-pair consistency have made it a favorite of hard-hitting metal drummers, from Jay Weinberg's high-impact signature model to Derek Roddy's blast-beat-focused Player's Design stick.

Q: Is Vater independent or owned by a larger company?
A: Vater remains independent and family-owned, run by Ron and Alan Vater out of Holbrook, Massachusetts — unlike Vic Firth (Zildjian) or Pro-Mark (D'Addario), it isn't part of a larger drum or cymbal conglomerate.

## History

Founded **1990** in Holbrook, Massachusetts, USA by Ron and Alan Vater.

Vater's history begins in the 1940s at Jack's Drum Shop in Boston, opened by Jack Adams. In 1956, a stick turner named Fred Maichle began hand-turning drumsticks for the shop, sometimes custom-building sticks for touring musicians like Buddy Rich and Philly Joe Jones.

Adams's operation eventually moved into the C. Vater Music Center in Norwood, Massachusetts, owned by his son-in-law Clarence Vater. Clarence's sons, Alan and Ron Vater, grew up working the store alongside their father and grandfather.

When a labor dispute disrupted supply from an outside stick manufacturer in the late 1970s, the Vater family started renting lathe time to produce their own sticks — an out-of-necessity move that planted the seed for an independent stick brand.

The company relocated to Holbrook, Massachusetts in October 1988, and Vater-branded drumsticks officially launched in 1990. By the mid-1990s, Vater had grown into one of the top five drumstick manufacturers in the United States.
