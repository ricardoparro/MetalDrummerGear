Vic Firth is the world's most widely used drumstick brand, founded in 1963 by Boston Symphony Orchestra principal timpanist Vic Firth. Vic Firth merged with cymbal maker Zildjian in December 2010. ## History

Founded **1963** in Massachusetts, USA by Everett "Vic" Firth.

Vic Firth was founded in 1963 by Everett "Vic" Firth, principal timpanist of the Boston Symphony Orchestra from 1956 to 2002. After more than a decade with the BSO, Firth grew dissatisfied with the drumsticks available to him and hand-whittled his own prototypes, sending them to a wood turner in Montreal to be produced.

Those first two designs became the SD1 and SD2 — sticks Firth originally made for his own use. They caught on with his students, then with percussionists at other orchestras, and Firth's side project grew into a full manufacturing business.

As the company expanded beyond its orchestral roots into the drum-set market, it built a reputation for manufacturing consistency that became its core selling point: Vic Firth pairs are cut and weighed to play alike from box to box.

By the early 2000s, Vic Firth's factory in Maine was producing roughly 100,000 pairs of drumsticks a week, and the company had become the best-selling stick brand in the world, with distribution in more than 120 countries.

In December 2010, Vic Firth merged with Zildjian — the two family businesses had both been run out of the greater Boston area for generations — joining two of percussion's most trusted names under one roof while Vic Firth continued operating under its own brand.

**Milestones:**
- **1963** — Vic Firth hand-designs his first stick prototypes and founds the company in Massachusetts.
- **1960s** — The SD1 and SD2 become Vic Firth's first commercially available drumstick models.
- **early 2000s** — Vic Firth's Maine factory produces roughly 100,000 pairs of drumsticks per week.
- **2010** — Vic Firth merges with the Zildjian Company in December, joining two family-run percussion companies under one roof.
