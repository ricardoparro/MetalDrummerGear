# Wincent Drumsticks — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Wincent is a Swedish boutique drumstick maker founded in 1992 by Dan Nylén, known for proprietary centerless-grinding manufacturing and weight-matched pairs. It's the choice of Tomas Haake (Meshuggah) and Mikkey Dee (Motörhead, Scorpions).

## History

Founded **1992** in Robertsfors, Sweden (now headquartered in Gränna) by Dan Nylén.

Wincent started in 1992 when Dan Nylén began experimenting with making drumsticks in his parents' garage in Robertsfors, a small village in the far north of Sweden, working alongside his father Bo.

Nylén later moved south to Alingsås, where he released Wincent's first steel wire brushes — becoming the first company to offer brushes in different wire thicknesses (light, medium, and heavy).

The company eventually settled in Gränna, in southern Sweden, where Wincent remains headquartered today. There, Nylén built the brand's first dedicated drumstick machine around a proprietary one-step, centerless-grinding technique used exclusively by Wincent.

That precision-manufacturing focus — including adjustable O-ring rods that let drummers tune the timbre of their attack — has kept Wincent a boutique alternative to the industry's mass-market stick makers.

**Metal era:** Tomas Haake (Meshuggah) plays a Wincent signature stick — a 0.638" model with a front-heavy "Haake taper" built specifically for the mechanical precision his polymetric, djent-founding compositions demand. Mikkey Dee, who powered Motörhead for over two decades and later joined Scorpions, plays a heavier Wincent signature (W-MDS) built on a 2B-class .622" diameter for his hard-hitting, high-volume style.
