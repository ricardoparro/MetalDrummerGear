# Zildjian Cymbals — Metal Drummer Endorsers

Zildjian is a Turkish-American cymbal manufacturer founded in 1623 in Constantinople, making it the oldest continuously operating musical instrument company in the world. In metal, Zildjian dominates through the A Custom, K, and K Custom series — bright, cutting cymbals that project over high-gain guitar stacks. Zildjian is the most-endorsed cymbal brand among MetalForge's 63-drummer roster, with 20 roster endorsers.

## Key Zildjian Product Lines for Metal

- A Custom Series — machine-lathed, brilliant finish; bright, cutting tone; the most prevalent Zildjian choice in live metal touring; the A Custom Mastersound Hi-Hat is an industry standard
- K Series — hand-hammered, dark finish; complex, warm tone with controlled overtones; George Kollias and Martin Lopez favor K cymbals for their musical character
- K Custom Dark — darker voicing of the K series; less wash than standard K; used by George Kollias and Mario Duplantier
- K Sweet Series — medium-weight K variant with slightly more complexity; Mario Duplantier's hi-hat and crash choice
- Z Custom — heavy-weight raw bronze; extremely loud and aggressive; Lars Ulrich's Z Custom China is a defining part of Metallica's live sound
- A Zildjian Mega Bell Ride — large bell with strong definition; used by Art Cruz and Mario Duplantier for ride clarity over heavy guitars

## FAQ

Q: What cymbals does Lars Ulrich use?
A: Lars Ulrich uses Zildjian A Custom Series cymbals: 14" Dyno Beat Hi-Hats, 16", 17", and 18" Rock Crashes, a 20" Z Custom China, and a 22" ride.

Q: What cymbals does Mario Duplantier use?
A: Mario Duplantier uses Zildjian K Sweet 14" Hi-Hats, A Custom 14" Hi-Hats, K Custom Hybrid 18" Crash, A Custom 19" Crash, Z Custom 21" Mega Bell Ride, and 18" & 20" Chinas.

Q: What Zildjian hi-hat is most popular in metal?
A: The Zildjian A Custom Mastersound Hi-Hat (14") is the most commonly cited hi-hat among MetalForge's roster. Its crisp, cutting chick sound is well-suited to tight double bass patterns and thrash metal velocity.

Q: What Zildjian cymbals are best for thrash metal?
A: For thrash metal, the Zildjian A Custom series is the dominant choice — bright, cutting, and loud enough to project over high-gain Marshall and Mesa stacks. Lars Ulrich's A Custom setup is the reference rig for thrash. For heavier, more controlled overtones, the K Custom Dark series is an alternative favored in death and progressive metal.

Q: How many metal drummers use Zildjian?
A: Zildjian is the most-endorsed cymbal brand on MetalForge's roster with 20 out of 63 catalogued drummers using Zildjian cymbals as their primary choice, spanning thrash, death, progressive, groove, and extreme metal subgenres.
