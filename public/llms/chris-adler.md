# Chris Adler — Drum Kit, Gear & Technique

Chris Adler is the co-founding drummer of Lamb of God (1994–2019), one of the most influential figures in the New Wave of American Heavy Metal. His groove-driven approach — combining death metal intensity with syncopated, riff-locking patterns — defined the sound of a generation of American metal. In 2016 he won a Grammy for Best Metal Performance as the studio drummer on Megadeth's Dystopia.

## Kit Overview

Chris Adler plays a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab drum kit — the precision-engineered configuration that powered Lamb of God's rise from underground metal to one of the most successful American metal bands of the 21st century. The Mapex Black Panther's dry, focused tone delivers the cutting attack central to Adler's groove-driven patterns on Ashes of the Wake, Sacrament, and Wrath — albums that defined the New Wave of American Heavy Metal.

The cornerstone of the Chris Adler drum set is his Mapex Chris Adler Signature 14×5.5" Walnut/Maple snare — a shallow, articulate shell that punches through dense guitar walls with the precise crack essential for Lamb of God's syncopated riff-locking grooves. Meinl Byzance Series cymbals complete the drum kit: 14" Dark Hi-Hats for dry, controlled articulation; 18" and 19" Dark Crashes for explosive accent bursts; a 21" Transition Ride for clear rhythmic articulation; and an 18" Extra Dry China for raw, cutting punctuation. A Mapex Falcon double pedal and Promark TX5AXW Chris Adler Signature sticks complete this drum set defined by precision groove and Grammy-winning power.

## Technique Highlights

- **Galloping kick patterns:** Signature double bass "gallop" rhythms that lock directly into Lamb of God's down-tuned guitar riffs — central to "Laid to Rest," "Redneck," and "Walk with Me in Hell"
- **China cymbal placement:** Positioned multiple China cymbals for instant, instinctive off-beat crashes — a defining sonic signature of Lamb of God's groove metal sound
- **Ghost note hi-hat work:** Complex hi-hat patterns with ghost notes that add groove and texture beneath heavy riff sections without cluttering the mix
- **Pivot foot technique:** Adler's hi-hat foot "pivot" technique adds nuance to grooves, a widely studied approach among groove metal drummers

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drum kit does Chris Adler use?**
A: Chris Adler uses a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab drum kit with a Mapex Chris Adler Signature 14×5.5" Walnut/Maple snare, Meinl Byzance Series cymbals (14" Dark Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Dark Crashes, 21" Transition Ride, 18" Extra Dry China), and a Mapex Falcon double pedal.

**Q: What drum set does Chris Adler use?**
A: Chris Adler's drum set is a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab — a collaboration between Adler and Mapex to engineer a kit specifically suited to his power-groove style. The Chris Adler drum set is built around a shallow Walnut/Maple signature snare and Meinl Byzance Dark cymbals, producing the dry, punishing sound heard on every Lamb of God album from As the Palaces Burn (2003) through VII: Sturm und Drang (2015).

**Q: Why did Chris Adler leave Lamb of God?**
A: Chris Adler departed Lamb of God in 2019 after 25 years with the band. His departure was described as amicable; he cited a desire to focus on family and new creative projects, subsequently launching Firstborne with vocalist Girish Pradhan in 2021.

**Q: Did Chris Adler win a Grammy?**
A: Yes. Chris Adler won the Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance in 2017 for his work as studio drummer on Megadeth's Dystopia (2016) — the only Grammy win of his career, achieved while he was still a member of Lamb of God.
