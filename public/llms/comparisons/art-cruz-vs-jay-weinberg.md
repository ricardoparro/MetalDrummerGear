> Structured Q&A: Art Cruz (Lamb of God, 2019–present) vs Jay Weinberg (Slipknot, 2014–2023 / Suicidal Tendencies). Two modern American metal drummers who each replaced a founding member in a legacy band.

**Q: Who is Art Cruz?**
A: Art Cruz (born May 27, 1988, in Downey, California) is an American drummer who has been Lamb of God's drummer since 2019, when he replaced founding drummer Chris Adler. He first filled in for Adler on Lamb of God's 2018 North American tour supporting Slayer's farewell run, and the role became permanent in 2019. Before Lamb of God, he played in Winds of Plague (2010–2012) and Prong (2012–2019). He has recorded "Lamb of God" (2020) and "Omens" (2022) with the band.

**Q: Who is Jay Weinberg?**
A: Jay Weinberg (born 1990), son of E Street Band drummer Max Weinberg, is an American drummer who was Slipknot's drummer from 2014 to 2023, replacing founding drummer Joey Jordison. He recorded ".5: The Gray Chapter" (2014), "We Are Not Your Kind" (2019), and "The End, So Far" (2022) with Slipknot. Before Slipknot, he played with Against Me! and Madball. In 2023 he parted ways with Slipknot and joined Suicidal Tendencies, touring with them on Metallica's M72 World Stadium Tour.

**Q: Who replaced Chris Adler in Lamb of God?**
A: Art Cruz replaced Chris Adler in Lamb of God in 2019. Cruz first filled in for Adler on the band's 2018 tour supporting Slayer's farewell run, and the fill-in role became permanent, with Cruz recording "Lamb of God" (2020) and "Omens" (2022) as the band's full-time drummer.

**Q: Who replaced Jay Weinberg in Slipknot?**
A: Eloy Casagrande, formerly of Sepultura, replaced Jay Weinberg in Slipknot after Weinberg parted ways with the band in 2023. Weinberg himself had joined Slipknot in 2014, replacing founding drummer Joey Jordison.

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Art Cruz and Jay Weinberg?**
A: Art Cruz favors a groove-first, pocket-forward approach that prioritizes serving Lamb of God's syncopated riffs over technical display — his fills are functional rather than flashy. Jay Weinberg is defined by explosive power and blast-capable speed, delivering machine-like, theatrically intense performances that honored Joey Jordison's legacy while adding his own aggressive stamp to Slipknot's arena-scale productions.

**Q: What drums does Art Cruz play?**
A: Art Cruz plays Ludwig Classic Maple drums with a Ludwig 14"x6.5" Black Beauty snare.

**Q: What drums does Jay Weinberg play?**
A: Jay Weinberg plays SJC Custom Drums with his signature SJC "The Crucible" 14"x6.5" 48-ply brass snare.

**Q: What cymbals do Art Cruz and Jay Weinberg use?**
A: Art Cruz uses Zildjian A Custom cymbals (14" Hi-Hats, 17"-19" Crashes, 21" A Custom 20th Anniversary Ride, 18" A Custom China). Jay Weinberg uses Zildjian cymbals as well (14" A New Beat Hi-Hats, 18"-19" A Custom Crashes, 21" K Custom Ride, 19" K China) — both drummers share a Zildjian endorsement despite very different playing styles.

**Q: What bass drum pedals and sticks do they use?**
A: Art Cruz uses a Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot double pedal with Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. Jay Weinberg uses a DW 9000 Series double pedal with Vater Jay Weinberg 908 Signature sticks.

**Q: What albums did Art Cruz record with Lamb of God?**
A: Art Cruz recorded "Lamb of God" (2020) and "Omens" (2022) with the band, in addition to years of touring on the band's back catalog including "Ashes of the Wake" material.

**Q: What albums did Jay Weinberg record with Slipknot?**
A: Jay Weinberg recorded ".5: The Gray Chapter" (2014), "We Are Not Your Kind" (2019), and "The End, So Far" (2022) with Slipknot across his nine-year tenure.

**Q: What is the verdict — Art Cruz vs Jay Weinberg?**
A: Art Cruz and Jay Weinberg both inherited massive legacy-band drum seats and answered with different philosophies. Cruz brings groove-first restraint to Lamb of God, serving the song over spectacle. Weinberg brought powerhouse precision and theatrical intensity to Slipknot for nearly a decade before moving on to Suicidal Tendencies. Both are the modern blueprint for replacing an irreplaceable drummer in American metal.
