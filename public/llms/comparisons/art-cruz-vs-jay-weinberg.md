# Art Cruz vs Jay Weinberg — Q&A Comparison | MetalForge

> Structured Q&A: Art Cruz (Lamb of God) vs Jay Weinberg (ex-Slipknot, now Suicidal Tendencies). Two drummers who inherited legendary metal drum chairs — groove-first power vs powerhouse precision.

**Category:** Groove Metal / Nu Metal · **URL:** https://metalforge.io/vs/art-cruz-vs-jay-weinberg

---

**Q: Who replaced Chris Adler in Lamb of God?**
A: Art Cruz replaced Chris Adler in Lamb of God. Cruz had been filling in for Adler live as far back as 2018 before being named the band's permanent drummer in 2019, following Adler's departure. Cruz made his studio debut on the band's self-titled 2020 album, then continued on "Omens" (2022) and "Illegals in Heaven" (2024).

**Q: Who replaced Jay Weinberg in Slipknot?**
A: Jay Weinberg left Slipknot in November 2023 and the band continued with a new drummer. Weinberg himself had joined Slipknot in 2014 as the replacement for founding member Joey Jordison, recording four studio albums before his departure. After leaving Slipknot, Weinberg joined Suicidal Tendencies.

**Q: Who is Art Cruz?**
A: Art Cruz is the drummer for Lamb of God, having joined in 2019. He came up through the deathcore scene with Winds of Plague, and that blast-beat-driven background gave him a raw, physical foundation that he brought to Lamb of God's groove metal vocabulary when he inherited one of modern metal's most technically demanding drum chairs.

**Q: Who is Jay Weinberg?**
A: Jay Weinberg is the son of E Street Band drummer Max Weinberg. He joined Slipknot in 2014, becoming the band's drummer after founding member Joey Jordison's departure. His powerful, precise style honored Slipknot's legacy across ".5: The Gray Chapter" (2014), "We Are Not Your Kind" (2019), and "The End, So Far" (2022) while adding his own intensity. In 2023, he joined Suicidal Tendencies.

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Art Cruz and Jay Weinberg?**
A: Art Cruz favors overt, front-of-the-beat attack and raw power — a blast-beat foundation from his deathcore background layered onto Lamb of God's syncopated riff-locking grooves. Jay Weinberg's technique centers on relentless independence and endurance, navigating Slipknot's chaotic multi-percussionist live arrangement while channeling the aggression Jordison established without losing surgical timing.

**Q: What drums does Art Cruz play?**
A: Art Cruz plays a Ludwig drum kit with a Ludwig 14x6.5" Black Beauty snare — a punchy, articulate setup driven by a Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot Double Pedal for fast, ergonomic footwork across Lamb of God's demanding catalog.

**Q: What drums did Jay Weinberg play with Slipknot?**
A: Jay Weinberg plays a Pearl Masterworks Custom kit with a Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Steel snare, driven by a Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal — a setup built for the theatrical, high-intensity demands of Slipknot's live production.

**Q: What cymbals do Art Cruz and Jay Weinberg use?**
A: Both drummers share a Zildjian A Custom & K Series cymbal foundation despite playing different drum shell brands. Art Cruz runs 14" A Custom Mastersound Hi-Hats, A Custom crashes, a Zildjian Mega Bell Ride, and a K China. Jay Weinberg runs 14" A New Beat Hi-Hats, A Custom crashes, a K Custom Ride, and a K China.

**Q: What sticks do Art Cruz and Jay Weinberg use?**
A: Art Cruz plays Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. Jay Weinberg plays Promark ActiveGrip 5B sticks — both similarly weighted 5B models suited to their respective bands' aggressive, high-volume live shows.

**Q: Why is this comparison a "replacement drummer" story?**
A: Both Cruz and Weinberg inherited scrutinized legacy drum chairs in bands built around a founding member's identity. Cruz stepped in for Chris Adler in Lamb of God (2019); Weinberg stepped in for Joey Jordison in Slipknot (2014). Their careers answer the same question from opposite sides of American metal: can a new drummer honor a band's established sound while making the chair their own?

**Q: What is the verdict — Art Cruz vs Jay Weinberg?**
A: Art Cruz and Jay Weinberg both proved that a legacy drum seat can be honored and reinvented at the same time. Cruz brought deathcore-forged physicality to Lamb of God's groove metal template across three studio albums. Weinberg brought explosive precision to Slipknot's chaos across four albums before moving on to Suicidal Tendencies. The comparison is raw power vs relentless precision — two different answers to the same succession problem.
