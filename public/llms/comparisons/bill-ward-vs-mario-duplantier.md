**Q: Who is Bill Ward?**
A: Bill Ward (born May 5, 1948, in Birmingham, England) co-founded Black Sabbath in 1968 and, alongside Ozzy Osbourne, Tony Iommi, and Geezer Butler, helped invent heavy metal's rhythmic vocabulary across the band's classic Ozzy-era albums, including "Paranoid" (1970), "Master of Reality" (1971), and "Sabotage" (1975). His jazz-influenced style, shaped by heroes Gene Krupa and Buddy Rich, prioritized groove and dynamics over speed.

**Q: Who is Mario Duplantier?**
A: Mario Duplantier (born 1981 in Bayonne, France) co-founded progressive death metal band Gojira with his brother Joe Duplantier in 1996. He built his reputation on crushing double bass patterns layered with tribal, almost ceremonial rhythmic structures across albums like "From Mars to Sirius" (2005), "L'Enfant Sauvage" (2012), and "Magma" (2016).

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Bill Ward and Mario Duplantier?**
A: Ward's technique is rooted in jazz — a swinging, open-handed feel inherited from Gene Krupa and Buddy Rich that prioritized groove and dynamics over speed, giving early Black Sabbath riffs like "Iron Man" and "War Pigs" their loose, hard-swinging heaviness. Duplantier's technique blends raw power with polyrhythmic complexity and an unmistakable tribal sensibility, often locking into Gojira's tuned, resonant guitar tones with explosive fills and odd-meter grooves that feel organic rather than mechanical.

**Q: What drums does Bill Ward play?**
A: Bill Ward played a Ludwig Classic Maple kit with a Ludwig Supraphonic 14"x6.5" LM402 snare, driven by a Ludwig Atlas Pro double pedal, Vic Firth American Classic 2B sticks, and Remo heads.

**Q: What drums does Mario Duplantier play?**
A: Mario Duplantier plays a Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit with dual 22"x18" bass drums — forgoing a double pedal for a more organic feel — and a Tama S.L.P. 14"x6.5" G-Maple snare, powered by a Tama Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide double pedal and Tama Mario Duplantier Signature sticks.

**Q: What cymbals do Bill Ward and Mario Duplantier use?**
A: Bill Ward used Paiste 2002 & Giant Beat Series cymbals (15" Giant Beat Hi-Hats, 18" & 20" 2002 Crashes, a 24" 2002 Ride, 18" 2002 China). Mario Duplantier uses a sprawling Zildjian array (14" K Sweet & 14" A Custom Hi-Hats, 18" K Custom Hybrid Crash, 19" A Custom Crash, 20" K Sweet Crash, a 21" Z Custom Mega Bell Ride, and 18"/20" Chinas).

**Q: What bands are Bill Ward and Mario Duplantier known for?**
A: Bill Ward co-founded and drummed for Black Sabbath from 1968 to 1983, and again from 1997 to 2006. Mario Duplantier co-founded Gojira with his brother Joe Duplantier in 1996 and remains the band's drummer.

**Q: Why does Mario Duplantier use dual bass drums instead of a double pedal?**
A: Mario Duplantier prefers dual 22"x18" bass drums over a double pedal because it gives Gojira's tribal, progressive death metal rhythms a more organic feel, letting him layer polyrhythms with a different physical touch than a single-kick double-pedal setup allows.

**Q: What is Bill Ward's legacy in heavy metal drumming?**
A: Ward's jazz-informed swing and dynamic sensibility created the rhythmic vocabulary an entire genre was built on, and his playing on Black Sabbath's first six albums remains the foundational reference point for heavy metal drumming.

**Q: What is the verdict — Bill Ward vs Mario Duplantier?**
A: Bill Ward and Mario Duplantier occupy opposite ends of heavy metal's evolution while sharing a common thread — both prioritize feel and groove over pure technical display. Ward invented the genre's foundational swing more than five decades ago, giving Black Sabbath's earliest riffs their loose, hard-hitting heaviness. Duplantier represents progressive death metal's tribal, polyrhythmic modern era, driving Gojira's tuned-percussion attack with the same instinct for groove that made Ward's playing timeless.
