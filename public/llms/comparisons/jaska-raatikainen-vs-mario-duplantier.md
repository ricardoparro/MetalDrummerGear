**Q: Who is Jaska Raatikainen?**
A: Jaska Raatikainen (born July 18, 1979) co-founded Finnish melodic death metal band Children of Bodom in 1993 and, alongside the late Alexi Laiho, anchored the band's signature blend of neoclassical melodies and aggressive death metal for 26 years, from their 1997 debut "Something Wild" through their 2019 farewell "Hexed."

**Q: Who is Mario Duplantier?**
A: Mario Duplantier (born 1981 in Bayonne, France) co-founded progressive death metal band Gojira with his brother Joe Duplantier in 1996, building crushing double bass patterns layered with tribal, polyrhythmic structures across albums like "From Mars to Sirius" (2005), "L'Enfant Sauvage" (2012), and "Magma" (2016).

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Jaska Raatikainen and Mario Duplantier?**
A: Raatikainen's technique centers on precise, fluid double bass volleys and driving blast beats that lock tightly beneath Alexi Laiho's dense guitar and keyboard arrangements, a foundational element of melodic death metal's rhythmic template. Duplantier's technique blends raw power with polyrhythmic complexity and an unmistakable tribal, ceremonial quality, often locking into Gojira's tuned, resonant guitar tones with explosive fills and odd-meter grooves.
