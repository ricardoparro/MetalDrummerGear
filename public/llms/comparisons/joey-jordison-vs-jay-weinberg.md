**Q: Who is Joey Jordison?**
A: Joey Jordison (1975–2021) was an American musician and founding member of Slipknot, joining in 1995 and serving as their drummer through 2013. His blistering speed, technical precision, and theatrical stage presence — including drumming atop a rotating riser — made him one of the most influential metal drummers of his generation. He recorded "Slipknot" (1999), "Iowa" (2001), "Vol. 3: The Subliminal Verses" (2004), and "All Hope Is Gone" (2008) with the band, and also played guitar for Murderdolls and drums for Scar the Martyr and Sinsaenum.

**Q: Who is Jay Weinberg?**
A: Jay Weinberg (born September 8, 1990) is an American drummer and the son of E Street Band drummer Max Weinberg. He joined Slipknot in December 2013, becoming drummer #1 after Joey Jordison's departure, and remained with the band through November 2023. He recorded ".5: The Gray Chapter" (2014), "We Are Not Your Kind" (2019), and "The End, So Far" (2022) with Slipknot. Before Slipknot, he briefly played with Against Me! and Madball. In 2023, he joined Suicidal Tendencies.

**Q: Who replaced Joey Jordison in Slipknot?**
A: Jay Weinberg replaced Joey Jordison as Slipknot's drummer, joining in December 2013 and making his studio debut on ".5: The Gray Chapter" (2014). He remained Slipknot's drummer for nine years, until November 2023.

**Q: Why did Joey Jordison leave Slipknot?**
A: Joey Jordison's December 2013 departure was initially described as mutual, but he later revealed he had been fired while battling transverse myelitis, an autoimmune condition that temporarily left him unable to walk and significantly affected his drumming.

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Joey Jordison and Jay Weinberg?**
A: Joey Jordison defined Slipknot's chaotic, extreme drumming style with blast beats, death-metal-influenced speed, and theatrical intensity (including his rotating drum platform). Jay Weinberg brought a more groove-oriented, precision-first approach while still honoring the foundation Jordison built — powerful, explosive playing with strong dynamic control.

**Q: What drums does Joey Jordison play?**
A: Joey Jordison played Pearl Masters Premium Maple drums, paired with his signature Pearl Joey Jordison 13"×6.5" snare — a compact shell chosen for faster response at tempos exceeding 280 BPM.

**Q: What drums does Jay Weinberg play?**
A: Jay Weinberg played Pearl Masterworks Custom drums with a Pearl Reference 14"×6.5" Steel snare during his Slipknot tenure.

**Q: What cymbals do Joey Jordison and Jay Weinberg use?**
A: Joey Jordison used Paiste RUDE Series cymbals (14" Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Crashes, 20" Chinas, 22" Power Ride) for extreme durability and aggressive cut. Jay Weinberg used Zildjian A Custom & K Custom Series cymbals (14" A New Beat Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" A Custom Crashes, 21" K Custom Ride, 19" K China).

**Q: What bass drum pedals did they use?**
A: Both drummers used Pearl Demon Drive double pedals — Joey Jordison to power Slipknot's relentless double-kick patterns, and Jay Weinberg to maintain that same standard across his nine-year tenure.

**Q: What drumsticks do they use?**
A: Joey Jordison used Promark Joey Jordison Signature TX515W sticks. Jay Weinberg used Promark ActiveGrip 5B sticks.

**Q: What albums did each drummer record with Slipknot?**
A: Joey Jordison recorded "Slipknot" (1999), "Iowa" (2001), "Vol. 3: The Subliminal Verses" (2004), and "All Hope Is Gone" (2008). Jay Weinberg recorded ".5: The Gray Chapter" (2014), "We Are Not Your Kind" (2019), and "The End, So Far" (2022).

**Q: Why did Jay Weinberg leave Slipknot?**
A: Jay Weinberg departed Slipknot in November 2023. No detailed official reason was given by either party. He subsequently joined Suicidal Tendencies.

**Q: What is the verdict — Joey Jordison vs Jay Weinberg?**
A: The Slipknot succession represents two eras of metal drumming excellence. Joey Jordison was the irreplaceable original who built the rotating-riser, blast-beat-driven template that made Slipknot's drum chair legendary. Jay Weinberg proved worthy of carrying the torch for nearly a decade, honoring Jordison's legacy while delivering his own explosive precision. Both embody what it means to be a Slipknot drummer.
