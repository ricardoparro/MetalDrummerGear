**Q: Who is Mario Duplantier?**
A: Mario Duplantier (born 1981 in Bayonne, France) co-founded progressive death metal band Gojira with his brother Joe Duplantier in 1996, building a tribal style that combines crushing double bass with complex rhythmic structures across albums like "From Mars to Sirius" (2005) and "Magma" (2016).

**Q: Who is Hellhammer?**
A: Hellhammer (Jan Axel Blomberg, born 1969) is a Norwegian drummer best known for joining black metal pioneers Mayhem in 1988, delivering the genre-defining blast beats on "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" (1994) — the recording against which every subsequent black metal drum performance is measured. Beyond Mayhem, he has also played with Arcturus since 1991, and previously with Dimmu Borgir (2005–2010) and Shining.

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Mario Duplantier and Hellhammer?**
A: Duplantier forgoes a double pedal in favor of dual bass drums for a more organic feel, layering tribal, almost ritualistic polyrhythms beneath Gojira's environmentally-themed death metal. Hellhammer's blast beats are built on cold, relentless one-foot precision, and he famously plays barefoot for the direct tactile feedback he says is essential to holding tempo accuracy at extreme speeds.

**Q: What drums does Mario Duplantier play?**
A: Mario Duplantier plays a Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit with dual 22"x18" bass drums and a Tama S.L.P. 14"x6.5" G-Maple snare, powered by a Tama Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide double pedal and Tama Mario Duplantier Signature sticks.

**Q: What drums does Hellhammer play?**
A: Hellhammer plays a Sonor SQ2 Heavy Beech kit with a Sonor SQ2 14"x5.5" Maple snare, driven by an Axis double pedal and Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks.

**Q: What cymbals do Mario Duplantier and Hellhammer use?**
A: Mario Duplantier uses a sprawling Zildjian array (14" K Sweet & A Custom Hi-Hats, 18" K Custom Hybrid Crash, 19" A Custom Crash, 20" K Sweet Crash, a 21" Z Custom Mega Bell Ride, and 18"/20" Chinas). Hellhammer uses Paiste RUDE Series cymbals (14" RUDE Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" RUDE Crashes, 20" RUDE Ride, 18" RUDE China).

**Q: What bands are Mario Duplantier and Hellhammer known for?**
A: Mario Duplantier co-founded Gojira with his brother Joe Duplantier in 1996 and remains the band's drummer. Hellhammer has drummed for Mayhem since 1988 and has also recorded with Arcturus, Dimmu Borgir, and Shining.

**Q: Why does Mario Duplantier use dual bass drums instead of a double pedal?**
A: Mario Duplantier prefers dual 22"x18" bass drums over a double pedal because it gives Gojira's tribal, progressive death metal rhythms a more organic feel, letting him layer polyrhythms with a different physical touch than a single-kick double-pedal setup allows.

**Q: Does Hellhammer play barefoot?**
A: Yes — Hellhammer famously plays barefoot, saying the direct tactile feedback is essential to holding his icy, relentless blast-beat tempo accuracy at extreme speeds with Mayhem.

**Q: What is the verdict — Mario Duplantier vs Hellhammer?**
A: Mario Duplantier and Hellhammer represent two entirely different philosophies of extreme metal rhythm. Duplantier builds Gojira's progressive death metal around organic, dual-bass-drum polyrhythms that feel ritualistic rather than mechanical, prioritizing tribal groove over raw velocity. Hellhammer built his reputation on cold, icy blast-beat precision that turned speed itself into black metal's defining texture on "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas." Both are foundational figures in extreme metal's more demanding corners — one through organic, tribal complexity, the other through mechanical, atmosphere-first speed.
