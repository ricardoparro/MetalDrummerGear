**Q: Who is Ray Luzier?**
A: Ray Luzier (born 1970) is an American drummer who has been Korn's drummer since 2007, when he replaced founding member David Silveria. Before Korn, he was a sought-after session and touring musician, working with David Lee Roth and Army of Anyone. He has recorded "Korn III: Remember Who You Are" (2010), "The Path of Totality" (2011), and "The Paradigm Shift" (2013) with Korn, and also plays in the band KXM.

**Q: Who is Scott Travis?**
A: Scott Travis (born 1961) is an American drummer who has been Judas Priest's drummer since 1989, when he replaced Dave Holland for the landmark "Painkiller" album. Before Priest, he played with Racer X alongside guitarist Paul Gilbert. Travis has recorded "Painkiller" (1990), "Jugulator" (1997), "Demolition" (2001), "Angel of Retribution" (2005), "Nostradamus" (2008), "Redeemer of Souls" (2014), and "Firepower" (2018) with the band.

**Q: Who is the current drummer for Korn?**
A: Ray Luzier has been Korn's drummer since 2007, when he replaced founding member David Silveria. He has now served longer than Silveria did, recording "Korn III: Remember Who You Are" (2010), "The Path of Totality" (2011), and "The Paradigm Shift" (2013) with the band.

**Q: Who has been in both Korn and Judas Priest?**
A: No single drummer has played in both bands — Ray Luzier (Korn, 2007–present) and Scott Travis (Judas Priest, 1989–present) are separate drummers, but they share a similar career arc: both replaced a founding member and became the definitive long-term drummer for a legacy metal band.

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Ray Luzier and Scott Travis?**
A: Ray Luzier combines session-musician versatility with the syncopated, pocket-forward playing Korn's dense, detuned riffs demand, locking tightly with bassist Fieldy to hold down the band's signature groove. Scott Travis is defined by relentless double bass precision and speed — his performance on "Painkiller"'s title track remains one of heavy metal's most celebrated drumming feats, establishing the modern American power-drumming template within a classic British metal band.

A: Ray Luzier recorded "Korn III: Remember Who You Are" (2010), "The Path of Totality" (2011), "The Paradigm Shift" (2013), and subsequent Korn albums after joining in 2007.

**Q: What albums did Scott Travis record with Judas Priest?**

**Q: What is the verdict — Ray Luzier vs Scott Travis?**
A: Ray Luzier and Scott Travis are both technically accomplished replacement drummers who didn't just fill a seat — they outlasted the era they were hired to bridge. Luzier brought session-musician versatility and syncopated groove to Korn, becoming the band's longest-tenured drummer. Travis brought thrash-influenced double bass power to Judas Priest, delivering one of heavy metal's most celebrated performances on "Painkiller" and remaining behind the kit for over three decades.
