**Q: Who is the current drummer for Korn?**
A: Ray Luzier is Korn's current drummer, having joined the band in 2007. He replaced founding drummer David Silveria and has since recorded five studio albums with Korn, including "Korn III: Remember Who You Are" (2010), "The Path of Totality" (2011), "The Paradigm Shift" (2013), "The Serenity of Suffering" (2016), and "Requiem" (2022).

**Q: Who has been in both Korn and Judas Priest?**
A: No single drummer has played in both bands — Ray Luzier (Korn) and Scott Travis (Judas Priest) are two separate musicians. However, both are frequently compared as the definitive examples of a "replacement drummer" who became inseparable from their band's modern identity, across nu-metal and classic heavy metal respectively.

**Q: Who is Ray Luzier?**
A: Ray Luzier, born in 1970, is an American drummer who joined Korn in 2007. Before Korn, he was a sought-after session musician, working with artists like David Lee Roth and Army of Anyone. His drumming combines rock-solid grooves with technical flair, bringing a fresh energy to Korn's signature sound while respecting their nu-metal roots.

**Q: Who is Scott Travis?**
A: Scott Travis, born in 1961 in Norfolk, Virginia, is an American drummer best known as the drummer for Judas Priest since 1989. He made his debut on the landmark album "Painkiller," which reinvigorated the band with its aggressive, speed metal approach. Travis was the first non-British member of Judas Priest and brought a new level of technical proficiency to the band. Before Priest, he played with Racer X. He was inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame as a member of Judas Priest in 2022.

**Q: How long has Ray Luzier been Korn's drummer?**
A: Ray Luzier has held Korn's drum chair since 2007 — nearly two decades — longer than founding drummer David Silveria's own most productive stretch with the band, recording five studio albums across that span.

**Q: How long has Scott Travis been Judas Priest's drummer?**
A: Scott Travis has been Judas Priest's drummer since 1989, debuting on "Painkiller." He is the band's longest-tenured drummer and its first non-British member, recording every studio album from "Painkiller" through "Invincible Shield" (2024) — over three decades with the band.

**Q: What drums does Ray Luzier play?**
A: Ray Luzier plays Pearl Masters Maple Complete drums with a Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Brass snare and Sabian AAX Series cymbals (14" Stage Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" X-Plosion Crashes, 21" Stage Ride, 18" AAXtreme China), driven by a Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal and Promark Ray Luzier Signature TX420X sticks.

**Q: What drums does Scott Travis play?**
A: Scott Travis plays a DW Collector's Series Maple kit with a DW Collector's 14x6.5" Bronze snare and Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series cymbals (14" Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Crashes, 22" Power Ride, 20" China), driven by a DW 9000 Series Double Pedal and Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks.

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Ray Luzier and Scott Travis?**
A: Ray Luzier blends deep groove instincts with technical precision, giving Korn's detuned, atmospheric arrangements a live-band muscularity. Scott Travis brought double-bass speed and technical sophistication that Judas Priest had never featured before "Painkiller," reinvigorating a classic British heavy metal band with an aggressive, virtuosic approach.

**Q: What is the verdict — Ray Luzier vs Scott Travis?**
A: Ray Luzier and Scott Travis are metal's two great examples of a replacement drummer becoming a defining, tenure-outlasting presence. Luzier stepped into Korn's nu-metal chair in 2007 and has anchored the band for nearly 20 years across five studio albums. Travis stepped into Judas Priest's classic heavy metal chair in 1989 and has anchored the band for over 30 years, culminating in a 2022 Rock and Roll Hall of Fame induction. Different genres, different eras, same result: both turned a "replacement" role into one of metal's great longevity stories.
