**Q: Who is Scott Travis?**
A: Scott Travis (born 1961) is an American drummer who has been Judas Priest's drummer since 1989, replacing Dave Holland for the landmark "Painkiller" album (1990). He has since recorded "Jugulator" (1997), "Demolition" (2001), "Angel of Retribution" (2005), "Nostradamus" (2008), "Redeemer of Souls" (2014), and "Firepower" (2018). Before Priest, he played with Racer X alongside Paul Gilbert.

**Q: Who is Nicko McBrain?**
A: Nicko McBrain (born Michael Henry McBrain, 1952) is an English drummer who has been Iron Maiden's drummer since 1982. He defined the band's galloping sound across "Piece of Mind" (1983), "Powerslave" (1984), "Somewhere in Time" (1986), and "Seventh Son of a Seventh Son" (1988), and remains Maiden's drummer over four decades later. Before Maiden, he played with Trust and the Pat Travers Band.

**Q: Who is Judas Priest's drummer?**
A: Scott Travis has been Judas Priest's drummer since 1989. He is best known for his performance on "Painkiller" (1990), considered one of heavy metal's most technically demanding drum recordings.

**Q: Who is Iron Maiden's drummer?**
A: Nicko McBrain has been Iron Maiden's drummer since 1982, making him one of the longest-serving drummers in heavy metal. He has played on every Iron Maiden studio album since "Piece of Mind" (1983).

**Q: What is the main stylistic difference between Scott Travis and Nicko McBrain?**
A: Scott Travis brought modern American double-bass power to Judas Priest's classic British heavy metal sound, delivering machine-like speed and consistency. Nicko McBrain achieves Iron Maiden's galloping triplet patterns using only a single bass drum pedal — no double bass — relying on extraordinary single-foot technique rather than a second kick drum.

**Q: Does Nicko McBrain use a double bass pedal?**
A: No. Nicko McBrain has never used a double bass pedal throughout his entire Iron Maiden career, achieving the band's galloping rhythms with a single Sonor pedal and exceptional foot speed — a defining stylistic choice that sets him apart from most metal drummers, including Scott Travis.

**Q: What drums does Scott Travis play?**
A: Scott Travis plays a ddrum Dominion Series kit with a ddrum Scott Travis Signature 14"x6.5" snare.

**Q: What drums does Nicko McBrain play?**
A: Nicko McBrain plays a Sonor SQ2 Series kit with a Sonor Nicko McBrain Signature 14"x6.5" snare.

**Q: What cymbals do Scott Travis and Nicko McBrain use?**
A: Scott Travis uses Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series cymbals (14" RUDE Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" RUDE Crashes, 22" RUDE Power Ride, 18" RUDE China). Nicko McBrain uses Paiste 2002 & Signature Series cymbals (14" Sound Edge Hi-Hats, 16" & 18" Power Crashes, 22" Power Ride, 20" China).

**Q: What bass drum pedals and sticks do they use?**
A: Scott Travis uses a DW 9000 Series double pedal with Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. Nicko McBrain uses a single Sonor bass drum pedal with Vic Firth Nicko McBrain Signature sticks.

**Q: What albums did Scott Travis record with Judas Priest?**
A: Scott Travis recorded "Painkiller" (1990), "Jugulator" (1997), "Demolition" (2001), "Angel of Retribution" (2005), "Nostradamus" (2008), "Redeemer of Souls" (2014), and "Firepower" (2018).

**Q: What albums did Nicko McBrain record with Iron Maiden?**
A: Nicko McBrain recorded "Piece of Mind" (1983), "Powerslave" (1984), "Somewhere in Time" (1986), "Seventh Son of a Seventh Son" (1988), and every Iron Maiden studio album since.

**Q: What is the verdict — Scott Travis vs Nicko McBrain?**
A: Scott Travis and Nicko McBrain represent two pillars of classic British-rooted heavy metal drumming from the same generation. Travis modernized Judas Priest's sound with one of metal's most celebrated double-bass performances on "Painkiller." McBrain is Iron Maiden's irreplaceable heartbeat, proving a single pedal and impeccable technique can out-gallop drummers running double bass. Both have anchored their respective bands for over three decades.
