## Biography

John Longstreth (born February 26, 1976, in Burlington, Wisconsin) is an American extreme metal drummer widely regarded as one of the fastest and most technically proficient drummers in death metal, credited with pioneering extreme double-bass and one-handed drum roll techniques that have since spread through the genre. He joined Angelcorpse in 1996, recording Hammer of Gods (1996) and Exterminate (1998) before the band's split, then rejoined for its 2006–2007 reunion. In February 1999 he joined Kansas City technical death metal pioneers Origin, appearing on their self-titled debut (2000) and Informis Infinitas Inhumanitas (2002); after resigning in 2003 he rejoined in April 2006 and has remained Origin's drummer since, recording Echoes of Decimation (2005) through Chaosmos (2022). In January 2003 he joined Skinless to record From Sacrifice to Survival and tour behind it. He also drummed for Gorguts from 2009 to 2014, and filled in for Hate Eternal on their November 2018 North American tour with Cannibal Corpse. Longstreth now teaches drumming at The Collective in New York City.
