## Biography

John Longstreth (born February 26, 1976, in Burlington, Wisconsin) is an American extreme metal drummer widely regarded as one of the fastest drummers in death metal, credited with popularizing the one-handed roll as a blast-beat technique. He was the original drummer for blackened death metal band Angelcorpse from 1995 to 1998, recording Hammer of Gods (1996), the Nuclear Hell EP (1997), and Exterminate (1998), and rejoined the band for its 2006–2007 reunion, recording Of Lucifer and Lightning (2007). Longstreth joined technical death metal band Origin in February 1999, recording Origin (2000) and Informis Infinitas Inhumanitas (2002) before departing in January 2003. He rejoined Origin in 2006 and has remained its drummer since, recording Antithesis (2008), Entity (2011), Omnipresent (2014), Unparalleled Universe (2017), and Chaosmos (2022). During a gap between Origin stints, he recorded Skinless's From Sacrifice to Survival (2003, Relapse Records). He has also done session and touring work with Gorguts, Hate Eternal, Dying Fetus, Exhumed, and The Red Chord, among others, and leads the project Crator.
