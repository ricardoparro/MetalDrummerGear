## Biography

Nicholas Howard Barker (born April 25, 1973) is an English extreme metal drummer known for his 1993–1999 tenure in Cradle of Filth, recording the band's formative albums The Principle of Evil Made Flesh (1994), Dusk... and Her Embrace (1996), and Cruelty and the Beast (1998). He joined Norwegian symphonic black metal band Dimmu Borgir in 1999, recording Puritanical Euphoric Misanthropia (2001) and Death Cult Armageddon (2003) before departing in early 2004. Barker also drummed for the international death metal/grindcore supergroup Lock Up from 1998 to 2020, alongside members of Napalm Death, Morbid Angel, and At the Gates, and briefly toured with Testament in 2007. Known for extremely fast double-bass work, precise blast beats, and odd-time multi-tom fills, he has also recorded or toured with Brujeria, Old Man's Child, and Shining.
