# Adrian Erlandsson Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Adrian Erlandsson co-founded At the Gates in 1990 and drove the D-beat-and-blast-beat foundation of Slaughter of the Soul (1995) — one of the most influential albums in extreme metal history and the blueprint for the Gothenburg melodic death metal sound. Brother of Arch Enemy's Daniel Erlandsson, Adrian's career took a distinct path from Sweden through Britain's gothic and extreme metal scenes: founding The Haunted in 1996, joining Cradle of Filth from 1999-2006, then Paradise Lost from 2009-2016, all while returning to At the Gates for its full-time 2008 reformation. This timeline documents his complete gear evolution from the budget Pearl-era kit of At the Gates' underground years, through a career-long Tama Starclassic relationship built across The Haunted and Cradle of Filth, into the Tama Starclassic Bubinga / Sabian / Czarcie Kopyto rig that defines his current work.

## At the Gates Founding & Slaughter of the Soul (1990-1996)

**Albums:** At the Gates – Gardens of Grief (1991), The Red in the Sky Is Ours (1992), With Fear I Kiss the Burning Darkness (1993), Terminal Spirit Disease (1994), Slaughter of the Soul (1995)
**Tours:** At the Gates European Touring 1991-1996

Erlandsson co-founded At the Gates in Gothenburg in 1990, drumming on four albums before the band's defining statement, Slaughter of the Soul (1995) — recorded at Studio Fredman with producer Fredrik Nordström. The album's dry, punchy drum sound, built on a modest Pearl Export-era kit typical of underground European death metal budgets, became a production reference point that shaped melodic death metal and, later, metalcore for a decade. At the Gates disbanded in 1996 shortly after the album's release.

- **Drums:** Pearl Export / Studio House Kit — birch or birch-poplar composite shells, double 22" bass drums, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** 14"x6" Steel or Birch Studio Snare, high tension, tight snare wires
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A-Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Hardware:** Tama / Pearl standard double bass pedal setup — two separate kick drums with independent pedals
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth / Pro-Mark, standard hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$1,400

**Key developments:**
- Co-founded At the Gates in Gothenburg in 1990
- Recorded four albums building to Slaughter of the Soul (1995) — one of extreme metal's most influential records
- Recorded at Studio Fredman with producer Fredrik Nordström, establishing a dry, punchy drum sound that became a genre reference point
- At the Gates disbanded in 1996 shortly after Slaughter of the Soul's release

> "We didn't have money for fancy gear. We had a kit that worked and a studio that knew how to make it sound dangerous." — *Metal-Rules.com Interview, 2014*

## The Haunted & Cradle of Filth Years (1996-2006)

**Albums:** The Haunted – The Haunted (1998); Cradle of Filth – Midian (2000), Damnation and a Day (2003), Nymphetamine (2004), Thornography (2006)
**Tours:** The Haunted Touring 1996-1999, Cradle of Filth World Touring 1999-2006

The day after At the Gates' first breakup, Erlandsson formed The Haunted with guitarist Patrik Jensen, recording the band's self-titled 1998 debut before departing in 1999. He then joined British extreme/gothic metal act Cradle of Filth, recording Midian (2000), Damnation and a Day (2003), Nymphetamine (2004), and Thornography (2006) — a stylistic pivot from Gothenburg melodic death metal into symphonic extreme metal's more theatrical, orchestrated productions. This period marked his transition to a career-long Tama Starclassic relationship, replacing the Pearl-era kit of his At the Gates years.

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic — maple/birch shells, double 22" bass drums *(switch from Pearl)*
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic 14"x6" Steel/Bubinga, bright, cutting tuning *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A-Series / Sabian — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20"/21" ride, 18" China
- **Hardware:** Tama double bass pedal, chain-drive *(switch)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Rock, nylon tip, hickory *(switch)*
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated / Ambassador
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$3,200

**Key developments:**
- Formed The Haunted in 1996 with guitarist Patrik Jensen, recording the band's self-titled 1998 debut
- Left The Haunted in 1999 to join British extreme/gothic metal act Cradle of Filth
- Recorded four Cradle of Filth albums (2000-2006), including Midian and Nymphetamine
- Switched from Pearl to Tama Starclassic, beginning a brand relationship that continues today

> "Cradle of Filth needed something bigger and more theatrical than what I'd played before. It pushed my sound in a completely different direction." — *Rhythm Magazine Interview, 2003*

## Paradise Lost Era (2009-2016)

**Albums:** Paradise Lost – Draconian Times MMXI (2011), Tragic Idol (2012), The Plague Within (2015)
**Tours:** Paradise Lost World Touring 2009-2016

On Jeff Walker's recommendation, Erlandsson joined British gothic metal pioneers Paradise Lost in 2009, recording Draconian Times MMXI (2011, a 20th-anniversary re-recording), Tragic Idol (2012), and The Plague Within (2015). During this run he was simultaneously juggling At the Gates' full-time 2008 reformation and The Haunted's 2013 rejoin — three active bands at once, a workload that reflects the same reliable, in-demand professionalism defining his entire career. His gear matured into the Tama Starclassic Bubinga configuration, paired with Sabian AAX/HHX cymbals, that remains his definitive setup.

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga — bubinga shells, double 22" bass drums, four-tom configuration *(upgrade)*
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga 14"x6.5" *(upgrade)*
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX / HHX Series — 15" HHX Xcelerator hi-hats, 20" AAX Iso crash, 19" Paragon China *(switch from Zildjian)*
- **Hardware:** Monolit Czarcie Kopyto Pedal, boutique Polish double pedal *(switch)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Rock Nylon Tip
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD Onyx (kicks), EC Reverse Dot (snare) *(switch from Remo)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$5,800

**Key developments:**
- Joined Paradise Lost in 2009 on Jeff Walker's recommendation
- Recorded Draconian Times MMXI (2011), Tragic Idol (2012), and The Plague Within (2015)
- Juggled Paradise Lost alongside At the Gates' 2008 reformation and The Haunted's 2013 rejoin — three active bands at once
- Upgraded to Tama Starclassic Bubinga and switched to Sabian cymbals, Evans heads, and a Czarcie Kopyto pedal

> "Playing in three bands at once sounds exhausting on paper, but each one wanted something different from me, and that variety kept me sharp." — *Metal-Rules.com Interview, 2014*

## At the Gates & The Haunted: Present Day (2013-Present)

**Albums:** At the Gates – At War with Reality (2014), To Drink from the Night Itself (2018), The Nightmare of Being (2021); The Haunted – Exit Wounds (2014), Strength in Numbers (2017)
**Tours:** At the Gates World Touring 2014-Present, The Haunted Touring 2013-Present

With At the Gates' 2008 reformation now a full-time commitment, Erlandsson recorded At War with Reality (2014), To Drink from the Night Itself (2018), and The Nightmare of Being (2021) — three albums that carried the Slaughter of the Soul legacy into the band's second act. He also rejoined The Haunted in 2013, recording Exit Wounds (2014) and Strength in Numbers (2017). His gear has held steady on the Tama Starclassic Bubinga rig established during his Paradise Lost years, refined with an official Vic Firth signature stick model bearing the At the Gates name.

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga — unchanged from his Paradise Lost years
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX / HHX Series — 15" HHX Xcelerator hi-hats, 20" AAX Iso crash, 19" Paragon China
- **Hardware:** Monolit Czarcie Kopyto Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Rock Nylon Tip (At the Gates Signature) *(signature model)*
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD Onyx (kicks), EC Reverse Dot (snare)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$5,800

**Key developments:**
- At the Gates' 2008 reformation became a full-time commitment with At War with Reality (2014)
- Recorded To Drink from the Night Itself (2018) and The Nightmare of Being (2021) with At the Gates
- Rejoined The Haunted in 2013, recording Exit Wounds (2014) and Strength in Numbers (2017)
- Vic Firth stick relationship matured into an official At the Gates signature model

> "Coming back to At the Gates as a full-time band again, we had to prove Slaughter of the Soul wasn't a fluke we could never follow up. I think we did." — *Blunt Magazine Interview, 2020*

## FAQ

**Q: What bands has Adrian Erlandsson played drums for?**
A: Adrian Erlandsson co-founded At the Gates (1990-present), founded The Haunted (1996-1999, rejoined 2013-present), played in Cradle of Filth (1999-2006), and drummed for Paradise Lost (2009-2016) — often juggling multiple active bands at once.

**Q: Is Adrian Erlandsson related to Daniel Erlandsson?**
A: Yes. Adrian Erlandsson (At the Gates, The Haunted, Paradise Lost) and Daniel Erlandsson (Arch Enemy) are brothers, both from Sweden, and both prominent drummers in the melodic/extreme metal scene, though they built their careers with different bands.

**Q: What drum kit did Adrian Erlandsson use on Slaughter of the Soul?**
A: The exact kit used on At the Gates' Slaughter of the Soul (1995) is not definitively documented, but is consistent with a Pearl Export or Studio Fredman house kit of the era — birch/birch-poplar shells producing the dry, punchy drum sound that became a genre reference point.

**Q: What drum kit does Adrian Erlandsson use now?**
A: Adrian Erlandsson currently plays a Tama Starclassic Bubinga kit with Sabian AAX/HHX cymbals, a Monolit Czarcie Kopyto double pedal, Evans drumheads, and his own Vic Firth Rock Nylon Tip At the Gates signature sticks — a setup established during his 2009-2016 Paradise Lost years and carried into At the Gates' current lineup.

**Q: Did Adrian Erlandsson play in Cradle of Filth?**
A: Yes — Adrian Erlandsson was Cradle of Filth's drummer from 1999 to 2006, recording Midian (2000), Damnation and a Day (2003), Nymphetamine (2004), and Thornography (2006), a stylistic pivot from Gothenburg melodic death metal into symphonic extreme metal.
