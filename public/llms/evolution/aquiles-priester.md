# Aquiles Priester Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Aquiles Priester is a Brazilian power metal drummer renowned for extreme double-bass endurance and showmanship. He founded his own band Hangar in 1996, joined Angra in 2000 replacing Ricardo Confessori, and anchored the band's most celebrated era — Rebirth, Temple of Shadows, Aurora Consurgens, and Aqua — before a 2012 departure, a long W.A.S.P. tenure, and a 2023 return to Angra. This timeline documents his complete gear evolution from a Mapex Saturn kit built for double-bass speed to the current Trick Drums Custom and Ufip cymbal setup powering Angra's reunion-era live show.

## Founding Hangar & Building Double-Bass Speed (1996–2000)

**Albums:** Hangar — early recordings and formative years (1996–2000)
**Tours:** Hangar Brazilian club circuit

Aquiles Priester founded Brazilian power metal band Hangar in 1996, using the group's early recordings and club circuit to develop the extreme double-bass endurance and technical precision that would become his signature. His Mapex Saturn kit and Sabian HH/HHX cymbals were paired with an Axis A double pedal — known for its extremely light, fast action — ideal for building the kind of sustained high-speed footwork Priester would need once he stepped onto Angra's stage.

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn Series
- **Snare:** Standard steel snare 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HH & HHX Series
- **Hardware:** Axis A Double Pedal (extremely light, fast action)
- **Sticks:** Vater Fusion 55A (the same stick model used throughout his career)
- **Heads:** Standard Remo heads
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$2,800

**Key developments:**
- Founded Hangar in 1996, his own Brazilian power metal band
- Developed extreme double-bass endurance using an Axis A pedal's light, fast action
- Built the technical foundation that led to joining Angra in 2000

> "Hangar was where I learned to push myself. Nobody was watching yet, so I could just chase speed and endurance without pressure." — *Drum Magazine Brazil, 1999*

## Angra Classic Era (2000–2012)

**Albums:** Rebirth (2001), Temple of Shadows (2004), Aurora Consurgens (2006), Aqua (2010)
**Tours:** Rebirth World Tour 2001–2002, Temple of Shadows Tour 2004–2005, Aurora Consurgens Tour 2006–2007, Aqua Tour 2010–2011

Priester joined Angra in 2000, replacing Ricardo Confessori, and anchored the band's most critically celebrated run. He began this era on his Mapex Saturn kit before moving to a Pearl Reference Series shell pack, upgraded to Sabian's HHX Series, and switched between Axis and DW double pedals to sustain the blazing kick patterns that made tracks like "Spread Your Fire" and "Heroes of Sand" genre landmarks. He also began a long-running parallel commitment to W.A.S.P. in 2006, joining as their touring and studio drummer while remaining Angra's drummer.

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn Series (later Pearl Reference) *(switch — more consistent platform for demanding studio arrangements)*
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference Series Snare 14"x6.5" *(upgrade)*
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX Series *(upgrade — brighter cut through dense orchestral-metal arrangements)*
- **Hardware:** Axis & DW Double Pedals *(switch — rotated between brands for extreme kick speed)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Fusion 55A (unchanged)
- **Heads:** Standard Remo heads (unchanged)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$6,500

**Key developments:**
- Joined Angra in 2000, replacing Ricardo Confessori
- Recorded Rebirth (2001), Temple of Shadows (2004), Aurora Consurgens (2006), and Aqua (2010)
- Switched from Mapex Saturn to Pearl Reference Series shells and upgraded to a full Sabian HHX cymbal setup
- Joined W.A.S.P. as touring and studio drummer in 2006, running parallel to his Angra tenure
- Left Angra in 2012, continuing with W.A.S.P. and Hangar

> "The double bass has to feel like it belongs to the song. The moment it feels like a trick, you've lost the groove." — *Drum Magazine Brazil, 2005*

## W.A.S.P. / Hangar Focus (2012–2022)

**Albums:** Babylon (2009, W.A.S.P.), Golgotha (2015, W.A.S.P.)
**Tours:** W.A.S.P. touring circuit 2012–2022, Hangar Brazilian shows

After leaving Angra in 2012, Priester focused on W.A.S.P. and his own band Hangar. He settled into a Pearl Reference Series kit paired with Meinl Byzance cymbals and a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal — a heavier, more straightforward configuration suited to W.A.S.P.'s classic heavy metal sound rather than Angra's shifting progressive-power-metal arrangements.

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series (continued)
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference Series Snare 14"x6.5" (unchanged)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series — 14" hi-hats, 18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China *(switch — heavier, more straightforward sound)*
- **Hardware:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal *(switch)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Fusion 55A (unchanged)
- **Heads:** Evans (standard configuration) *(switch from Remo)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$8,500

**Key developments:**
- Left Angra in 2012, focusing on W.A.S.P. and his own band Hangar
- Recorded W.A.S.P.'s Babylon (2009) and Golgotha (2015)
- Switched from Sabian to Meinl Byzance cymbals and from Axis/DW to Pearl Demon Drive pedals
- Adopted a heavier, more straightforward configuration suited to W.A.S.P.'s classic heavy metal sound

> "W.A.S.P. asks for something different than Angra — less shifting, more weight. I had to rebuild my setup around hitting hard and staying consistent, not chasing every time-signature change." — *Modern Drummer Interview, 2015*

## Angra Return: Trick Drums & Ufip (2023–Present)

**Albums:** Angra — current album cycle (2023–present)
**Tours:** Angra reunion touring 2023–present

Priester returned to Angra in 2023 for the band's current album cycle, once again anchoring their ferocious live show. His current touring and recording setup is built around a Trick Drums Custom configuration — US-made custom maple shells chosen for the low-end warmth and focused attack power metal demands — paired with a full Ufip cymbal array and a DW 9000 double pedal, tuned to sustain his double-bass patterns at 180–220+ BPM across Angra's South American touring circuit.

- **Drums:** Trick Drums Custom — 22"x18" bass drum(s), 10"/12"/13"/16"/18" toms *(switch)*
- **Snare:** Trick Drums Custom Snare 14"x6.5" *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Ufip Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/20" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China *(switch — complex, warm tone and exceptional festival-stage projection)*
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Double Pedal *(switch — ball-bearing cam system for consistent, tunable response)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Fusion 55A (unchanged — the same stick model used throughout his entire career)
- **Heads:** Remo Coated Ambassador (batter), Remo Ambassador Snare Side *(switch back to Remo)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$13,500

**Key developments:**
- Returned to Angra in 2023 for the band's current album cycle
- Switched to a Trick Drums Custom configuration, replacing his Pearl Reference W.A.S.P.-era kit
- Moved to a full Ufip cymbal array, replacing Meinl Byzance
- Adopted a DW 9000 double pedal for sustained 180–220+ BPM double-bass endurance

> "Playing Angra live is like conducting an orchestra with your whole body. The gear has to be there for you at 100% from the first note to the last." — *Metal Hammer Brazil, 2023*

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the drummer of Angra?**
A: Aquiles Priester is Angra's current drummer, having returned to the band in 2023 after an earlier tenure from 2000 to 2012 that produced Rebirth, Temple of Shadows, Aurora Consurgens, and Aqua. He replaced Ricardo Confessori when he first joined in 2000.

**Q: What drum kit does Aquiles Priester play?**
A: Aquiles Priester's current setup is a Trick Drums Custom configuration paired with a full Ufip cymbal array and a DW 9000 double pedal. Earlier in his career he played Mapex Saturn and Pearl Reference Series kits with Sabian HHX and Meinl Byzance cymbals.

**Q: What other bands has Aquiles Priester played in?**
A: Beyond Angra, Aquiles Priester founded his own Brazilian power metal band Hangar in 1996 and remains a member. He also served as W.A.S.P.'s touring and studio drummer, recording Babylon (2009) and Golgotha (2015), before returning to Angra in 2023.

**Q: How fast is Aquiles Priester's double bass technique?**
A: Aquiles Priester is known for sustaining even double-bass patterns at 180–220+ BPM across full live sets, a technique he credits to full-body relaxation rather than force. He built this endurance from his earliest years with Hangar using an Axis A pedal's light, fast action.
