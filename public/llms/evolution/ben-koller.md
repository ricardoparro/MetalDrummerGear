# Ben Koller Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Ben Koller is the drummer of Converge and one of the most influential figures in modern hardcore and metallic hardcore drumming. Co-founding Converge in Salem, Massachusetts in 1990, he has driven the band's rhythmic chaos from raw high-school hardcore through Jane Doe (2001) — widely considered one of the defining albums of the genre — and into the layered complexity of Axe to Fall (2009) and beyond. His gear progression from DIY secondhand kits to a refined Tama Starclassic setup tracks how raw hardcore energy becomes controlled precision over three decades. This timeline documents every major equipment era across his career.

## Early Hardcore / DIY Formation Era (1990–2000)

**Albums:** Petitioning the Empty Sky (1996), When Forever Comes Crashing (1998), The Poacher Diaries (1998)
**Tours:** New England hardcore circuit, DIY Northeast US tours

Ben Koller co-founded Converge in Salem, Massachusetts in 1990 as a high-school hardcore band. Through the 1990s the band released a series of raw, self-produced records on independent labels that established Converge's reputation for chaotic intensity. Koller played on whatever kit was available — secondhand Pearl and Tama kits acquired through trades and local music stores, held together by determination and urgency. The sound was appropriately brutal.

- **Drums:** Various secondhand Pearl / Tama kits — 20"–22" kick, standard tom configuration (DIY-era setups; Koller used whatever was available, emphasising durability over tone)
- **Snare:** Pearl Steel 14"×5" (steel shell for maximum punch, cutting through dense hardcore guitar walls in small venues)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian ZBT / Sabian B8 Series — 13"–14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride (entry-level brass cymbals; loud and affordable for a band touring on no budget)
- **Hardware:** Pearl P-100 Single / Basic Double Pedal (budget chain-drive double pedal built for punishment)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B hickory (heavier sticks for the power-forward demands of hardcore drumming)
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador single-ply (replaced frequently due to the physicality of hardcore performance)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$1,500

**Key developments:**
- Converge formed in Salem, Massachusetts (1990)
- DIY recording approach on independent labels
- Petitioning the Empty Sky (1996) and When Forever Comes Crashing (1998) establish Converge in the underground
- Raw, urgency-driven playing style developed under real-world constraint

> "We were always just trying to get louder and faster. Whatever kit I could get my hands on, that was the kit." — *Revolver Magazine Interview, 2012*

## Jane Doe Breakthrough Era (2001–2008)

**Albums:** Jane Doe (2001), You Fail Me (2004), No Heroes (2006)
**Tours:** Hydra Head Records touring circuit, metallic hardcore underground tours, various US and European hardcore dates

Jane Doe (2001, Hydra Head Records) was the record that redefined what hardcore could be — and Ben Koller's drumming was central to its impact. The album's chaotic, jazz-influenced rhythmic structures demanded a step up in both setup and technique. Koller moved to a more stable Tama Rockstar / Imperialstar configuration, and the increased production quality of Jane Doe and its follow-ups amplified the power and precision of his upgraded setup.

- **Drums:** Tama Imperialstar / Rockstar Series — maple/poplar shells: 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms *(upgrade — first consistent platform, punchy mid-forward tone suited to Kurt Ballou's dense production)*
- **Snare:** Tama Steel 14"×5.5" *(upgrade — increased shell depth improved projection and body for wider studio distribution)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" A New Beat hi-hats, 16"/17" crashes, 20" ride *(upgrade — brighter, more defined response suited to Jane Doe's precise rhythmic structures)*
- **Hardware:** Tama Iron Cobra Double Pedal — Power Glide cam, chain drive *(switch — smooth response gave Koller improved control at extreme tempos)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B (continued heavier hickory choice)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor / Powerstroke 3 — Emperor on toms, Powerstroke 3 on kick *(upgrade — durability for You Fail Me and No Heroes' live performances)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$4,500

**Key developments:**
- Jane Doe (2001) — considered one of the defining albums of metallic hardcore
- Tama Imperialstar adopted as first consistent platform
- Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide double pedal for improved extreme-tempo control
- Zildjian A Series replaces budget brass
- You Fail Me (2004) and No Heroes (2006) — stripped, direct production reflects deliberate restraint

> "Jane Doe was us figuring out how to be as extreme as possible while still making it music. The drumming had to hold that chaos together." — *Decibel Magazine, 2011*

## Axe to Fall / Modern Complexity Era (2009–Present)

**Albums:** Axe to Fall (2009), All We Love We Leave Behind (2012), The Dusk in Us (2017), Bloodmoon: I (2021)
**Tours:** Epitaph Records touring circuit, Converge headline and festival tours worldwide, Bloodmoon touring cycle

Axe to Fall (2009, Epitaph) marked Converge's most ambitious production to date — a record featuring guest performances from members of Neurosis, Genghis Tron, Disfear, and Nails. The musical complexity demanded a corresponding step up in Koller's setup. His current Tama configuration — refined across All We Love We Leave Behind (2012), The Dusk in Us (2017), and Bloodmoon: I (2021) — balances the raw aggression of the early years with the precision his increasing compositional sophistication requires. Ben Koller is now widely recognised as one of the most influential drummers in modern heavy music.

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple — maple shells: 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms *(upgrade — consistent modern platform; premium maple shells deliver warmth and attack for touring and studio)*
- **Snare:** Tama Lars Ulrich Signature Snare / Custom Steel 14"×6.5" *(upgrade — deeper shell for maximum punch and projection central to Converge's live sound)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom / K Custom Series — 14" A Custom hi-hats, 16"/17"/18" crashes, 21" ride *(upgrade — combines A Custom brightness with K Custom warmth for a wider dynamic range)*
- **Hardware:** Tama Iron Cobra 900 Double Pedal — Roller Glide cam *(upgrade — improved spring tension adjustability and more consistent response)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B / Custom (maintained heavier hickory preference across his career)
- **Heads:** Evans G2 / EMAD — G2 on toms, EMAD on kick *(switch — warm attack on toms, focused punchy low-end for dense metallic hardcore mixes)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$12,000

**Key developments:**
- Tama Starclassic Maple adopted — most refined platform of career
- Evans G2/EMAD heads replace Remo
- Tama Iron Cobra 900 upgrade for improved live consistency
- Axe to Fall (2009) — Converge's most ambitious record; Epitaph debut
- All We Love We Leave Behind (2012) and The Dusk in Us (2017) cement Koller's status as modern hardcore's definitive drummer
- Bloodmoon: I (2021) — collaborative project with Chelsea Wolfe

> "Every album we make, I try to bring something new to the drums. Not just faster or harder — smarter. Axe to Fall taught me that complexity and rawness can coexist." — *Modern Drummer, 2013*

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Ben Koller use on Jane Doe?**
A: Ben Koller recorded Jane Doe (2001) — widely considered one of the defining albums of metallic hardcore — with a Tama Imperialstar / Rockstar Series kit featuring maple/poplar shells. His snare was a Tama Steel 14"×5.5", and he had recently upgraded to Zildjian A Series cymbals and a Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide double pedal.

**Q: What drum kit does Ben Koller use today?**
A: Ben Koller currently plays a Tama Starclassic Maple kit — an all-maple configuration with a 22" kick and 10"/12"/14"/16" toms. His cymbal setup combines Zildjian A Custom and K Custom series for a wider tonal range, and he uses the Tama Iron Cobra 900 double pedal.

**Q: What drumsticks does Ben Koller use?**
A: Ben Koller has played Vic Firth 5B hickory sticks (and custom variants) throughout his career — a heavier spec chosen to handle the physical demands of hardcore drumming without sacrificing control.

**Q: Why did Ben Koller switch from Tama to Evans drumheads?**
A: Koller moved from Remo to Evans G2 and EMAD heads during the Axe to Fall era (2009–present). The G2 provides warm attack with a defined transient on toms, while EMAD delivers focused, punchy low-end suited to Converge's dense metallic hardcore mixes.

**Q: Is Ben Koller still active with Converge?**
A: Yes — Ben Koller remains Converge's drummer and continues recording and touring with the band. Bloodmoon: I (2021), a collaborative project with Chelsea Wolfe, is his most recent major release, and Converge continues to headline festivals worldwide as of 2026.
