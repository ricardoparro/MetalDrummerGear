# Charlie Benante Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Charlie Benante joined Anthrax in 1983 and became one of thrash metal's defining drummers — credited with pioneering sustained double bass drumming and popularizing the blast beat. His gear evolution traces four decades of Anthrax's own arc: from an early Tama kit, through the Tama Artstar II that defined Among the Living, to a mid-90s DW detour during the band's groove-metal pivot, and back to the Tama Starclassic rig he has used since Anthrax's 2011 reunion — the same setup he now plays as Pantera's touring drummer.

See also: [Charlie Benante drummer profile](/drummer/charlie-benante)

## Early Anthrax Era (1983–1986)

**Albums:** Fistful of Metal (1984), Spreading the Disease (1985)
**Tours:** Fistful of Metal club tour 1984, Spreading the Disease tour 1985–1986

Benante joined Anthrax in 1983, just ahead of the Fistful of Metal sessions. On these early records he was already developing the sustained double bass patterns that would become his signature, playing a straightforward Tama kit paired with Paiste 2002 cymbals — the start of a cymbal partnership that has lasted his entire career.

- **Drums:** Tama Artstar — birch shells: 22" kick, 10"/12"/13"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Artstar 14"×6.5" steel
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 Series — 14" Sound Edge hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Hardware:** Tama HP35 Camco chain-drive pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador on toms, Pinstripe on kick
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$3,000

**Key developments:**
- Joined Anthrax in 1983, becoming the band's drummer and eventual primary composer
- Began developing sustained double bass patterns that would define thrash drumming
- Started a Paiste cymbal partnership that has lasted his entire career

> "I was trying to push the double bass further than anyone else was doing it at the time. That was the whole idea — relentless." — *Modern Drummer Interview, 1986*

## Among the Living Era (1987–1990)

**Albums:** Among the Living (1987), State of Euphoria (1988), Persistence of Time (1990)
**Tours:** Among the Living tour 1987–1988, Clash of the Titans tour 1990–1991

Among the Living is Charlie Benante's definitive thrash statement — sustained double bass, popularized blast beats, and a groove sensibility that helped set the template for the genre. Recorded with producer Eddie Kramer on a Tama Artstar II kit, the album anchored the Big Four alongside Metallica, Slayer, and Megadeth.

- **Drums:** Tama Artstar II — birch shells: dual 22" kicks, 10"/12"/13"/16" toms *(upgrade)*
- **Snare:** Tama Artstar II 14"×6.5" steel, high tension tuning *(upgrade)*
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 Series — 14" Sound Edge hi-hats, 16"/18"/20" crashes, 18" China *(upgrade)*
- **Hardware:** Tama HP35 Camco pedals — dual chain-drive for the twin-kick setup
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A — continued
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador on snare, Emperor on toms and kicks *(upgrade)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$6,500

**Key developments:**
- Among the Living (1987) becomes Anthrax's definitive thrash statement
- Upgraded to Tama Artstar II — the kit most associated with Benante's classic era
- Popularized the blast beat in metal, expanding on the technique from Spreading the Disease
- Big Four status cemented alongside Metallica, Slayer, and Megadeth

> "Among the Living was where everything I'd been working on came together — the speed, the groove, the arrangements." — *DRUM! Magazine Interview, 1988*

## Sound of White Noise Era (1993–1995)

**Albums:** Sound of White Noise (1993), Stomp 442 (1995)
**Tours:** Sound of White Noise tour 1993–1994, Stomp 442 tour 1995

Sound of White Noise marked Anthrax's pivot toward a heavier, groove-driven sound with new singer John Bush. Producer Dave Jerden's thick, mid-range-heavy aesthetic called for a different tonal foundation, and Benante switched from Tama to a DW Collector's Series maple kit — his only major drum brand departure before returning to Tama the following decade.

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series Maple — dual 22"×18" kicks, 10"/12"/13" toms, 16" floor tom *(switch from Tama)*
- **Snare:** DW Edge Series 14"×6.5" — maple shell with brass edge rings *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride (continued)
- **Hardware:** DW 5000 chain-drive double pedal *(switch from Tama Camco)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A — continued
- **Heads:** Remo Pinstripe on toms and kicks *(switch)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$13,000

**Key developments:**
- Switched from Tama to DW Collector's Series — the only major drum brand change of his career
- Sound of White Noise (1993) debuts at #7 on Billboard 200, Anthrax's highest chart position
- Adjusted playing approach for the album's slower, groove-oriented arrangements

> "DW gave me a kit I could really dig into. Every drum responded exactly how I wanted for where we were going musically." — *Rhythm Magazine Interview, 1993*

## Worship Music Reunion / Current Era (2011–Present)

**Albums:** Worship Music (2011), For All Kings (2016)
**Tours:** Worship Music reunion tour 2011–2012, Big Four tours 2010–2011, Pantera reunion tour 2022–present

Benante returned to Tama for Anthrax's classic-lineup reunion, settling on the Starclassic Maple series that remains his setup today. In 2010 he also retired the Tama HP35 Camco pedal he had used since 1984, switching to the modern Speed Cobra. Since 2022, Benante has used this same rig as touring drummer for Pantera's reunion, filling in for his late friend Vinnie Paul.

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple — dual 22" kicks, 10"/12"/13" toms, 16" floor tom *(switch back to Tama)*
- **Snare:** Tama Charlie Benante Signature 14"×6.5" *(signature model)*
- **Cymbals:** Paiste RUDE & 2002 / Signature Series — 14" hi-hats, 18"/19" crashes, 20" power ride, 18" China *(upgrade)*
- **Hardware:** Tama Speed Cobra double pedal *(switch from HP35 Camco)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Charlie Benante Signature *(signature model)*
- **Heads:** Evans *(switch)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$15,000

**Key developments:**
- Returned to Tama with the Starclassic Maple series, ending the DW era
- Retired the Tama HP35 Camco pedal in 2010 after 26 years for the Speed Cobra
- Earned signature models with both Tama (snare) and Vic Firth (sticks)
- Worship Music (2011) reunites the classic Joey Belladonna-era lineup
- Joined Pantera's 2022 reunion tour on the same rig, honoring the late Vinnie Paul

> "Forty years in, the Starclassic and the Paiste cymbals are just an extension of me at this point. It's about feel, not gear — but the gear has to keep up." — *Rhythm Magazine Interview, 2016*

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Charlie Benante use?**
A: Charlie Benante currently plays a Tama Starclassic Maple kit with a Tama Charlie Benante Signature 14"×6.5" snare, Tama Speed Cobra double pedal, and Paiste RUDE, 2002, and Signature series cymbals. This followed a run on DW Collector's Series drums during the Sound of White Noise era (1993–1995) and Tama Artstar/Artstar II kits through the classic thrash years.

**Q: What drums did Charlie Benante use on Among the Living?**
A: For Among the Living (1987), Charlie Benante played a Tama Artstar II kit with dual 22" bass drums, Paiste 2002 series cymbals, and a Tama HP35 Camco chain-drive pedal. The album was produced by Eddie Kramer and is considered his definitive thrash drumming performance.

**Q: Why did Charlie Benante switch from Tama to DW?**
A: Benante switched to a DW Collector's Series maple kit for Sound of White Noise (1993) to match producer Dave Jerden's thicker, mid-range-heavy production aesthetic as Anthrax pivoted to a heavier, groove-driven sound with new singer John Bush. It remains the only major drum brand change of his career — he returned to Tama for the band's 2011 Worship Music reunion.

**Q: What pedal did Charlie Benante use before switching to Speed Cobra?**
A: Benante used the vintage Tama HP35 Camco chain-drive double pedal from 1984 until 2010, when Tama discontinued it and he switched to the modern Tama Speed Cobra direct-drive pedal — his first pedal change in 26 years.

**Q: What cymbals does Charlie Benante use?**
A: Charlie Benante has been a Paiste artist for his entire career, starting with the Paiste 2002 series on Anthrax's earliest albums and expanding to the Paiste RUDE and Signature series for his current setup, built around 14" hi-hats, 18"/19" crashes, a 20" power ride, and an 18" China cymbal.
