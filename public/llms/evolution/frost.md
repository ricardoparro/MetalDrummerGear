# Frost Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Frost (Kjetil-Vidar Haraldstad) has been Satyricon's drummer since 1993 and a co-founder of 1349 since 2001 — one of Norwegian black metal's longest-running and most technically demanding dual-band careers. His drumming evolved across four distinct phases: the raw, corpse-painted blast beats of the early 1990s, the industrial-tinged technical expansion of Rebel Extravaganza, the architecturally sophisticated playing of Satyricon's commercial peak, and the leaner, more experimental approach of his current Sonor-era setup. This timeline documents every major gear era, era by era.

See also: [Frost drummer profile](/llms/drummers/frost.md)

---

## Raw Black Metal Genesis (1993–1996)

**Albums:** Dark Medieval Times (1993), Nemesis Divina (1996)
**Tours:** Early Norwegian black metal scene dates, Nemesis Divina European tour

Frost joined Satyricon in 1993, entering Norway's corpse-painted black metal underground at its rawest. His early playing favored unrelenting single-foot blast beats over technical polish, matching the genre's cold, monochrome atmosphere with budget, pre-endorsement gear.

- **Drums:** Pearl Export Series — birch/mahogany shells: 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Export 14"×5.5" steel
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Hardware:** Pearl standard hardware, single bass drum pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$1,800

**Key developments:**
- Joined Satyricon (1993), entering Norway's black metal underground
- Dark Medieval Times and Nemesis Divina establish his raw blast-beat foundation
- Single-foot blast beats become his early technical signature
- No endorsements yet — budget Pearl Export kit

> "In the beginning it wasn't about the gear at all. It was about how much darkness and speed you could put into a blast beat without breaking." — *Terrorizer Magazine Interview, 1997*

## Industrial Black Metal Evolution (1996–2002)

**Albums:** Rebel Extravaganza (1999), Volcano (2002)
**Tours:** Rebel Extravaganza European tour, Volcano release touring

Satyricon's sound shifted toward industrial textures, and Frost's gear evolved alongside it: a proper Pearl Custom kit, Roland electronic triggers for consistent attack at extreme tempos, and a double pedal that expanded his foot vocabulary beyond single-foot blasting. In 2001 he co-founded 1349, a project built entirely around maximum-velocity black metal.

- **Drums:** Pearl Custom — maple shells: 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms *(upgrade)*
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"×6.5" *(upgrade)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — 14" hi-hats, 16"/17"/18" crashes, 20" ride *(upgrade)*
- **Hardware:** Pearl Eliminator double pedal *(new — double bass added)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 2B hickory *(upgrade)*
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor *(upgrade)*
- **Electronics:** Roland trigger module on bass drum *(new)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$4,500

**Key developments:**
- Pearl Custom upgrade — a clear step up from budget Export-era gear
- Roland electronic triggers introduced — a defining live-sound tool ever since
- Double bass pedal added, expanding foot-technique vocabulary
- Co-founded 1349 (2001), dedicated to maximum-velocity extreme metal

> "1349 let me chase pure speed without compromise. Satyricon was starting to ask different questions of me — both bands needed reliable gear that wouldn't fail at 240 BPM." — *Metal Maniacs Interview, 2003*

## Sophisticated Progressive Black Metal (2002–2013)

**Albums:** Now, Diabolical (2006), The Age of Nero (2008), Satyricon (2013)
**Tours:** Now, Diabolical world tour, Wacken Open Air headline sets, Satyricon (2013) album tour

As Satyricon moved toward a leaner, more rock-structured sound, Frost's playing grew architectural — blast beats with internal dynamics and seamless transitions into mid-tempo grooves. His gear matured to match: a flagship Pearl Reference kit, a warmer brass snare, and a refined Zildjian mix blending bright A Customs with darker K Custom textures.

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference — maple shells, custom finish: 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms *(upgrade)*
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass 14"×6.5" *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom / K Custom Dark mix — 14" hi-hats, 16"/17"/18" crashes, 22" K Custom Dark ride, 18" K China *(upgrade)*
- **Hardware:** Pearl Eliminator double pedal (continued)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic Extreme 5B *(switch)*
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor / Ambassador combo
- **Electronics:** Roland trigger module, refined for festival use *(upgrade)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$7,000

**Key developments:**
- Now, Diabolical (2006) brings Satyricon mainstream metal success
- Pearl Reference becomes his flagship kit for the band's biggest touring era
- K Custom Dark ride added for darker, rhythmically sophisticated passages
- Playing matures from pure-aggression blasting into architecturally structured drumming

> "I stopped thinking about blast beats as a wall of noise. Every stroke has to mean something now, even at 240 BPM." — *Rhythm Magazine Interview, 2008*

## Experimental Modern Era (2013–Present)

**Albums:** Deep calleth upon Deep (2017), 1349 – The Infernal Pathway (2019)
**Tours:** Deep calleth upon Deep world tour, 1349 festival circuit

Frost's most recent era finds him switching to Sonor's premium SQ2 platform and pushing into more textured, exploratory territory without losing the technical foundation built across thirty years — a leaner setup for a drummer balancing two demanding bands at once.

- **Drums:** Sonor SQ2 Series — custom finishes, compact configuration *(switch from Pearl)*
- **Snare:** Sonor SQ2 14"×6" maple *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom & K Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/17"/18" crashes, 22" K Custom Dark ride, 18" K China *(upgrade)*
- **Hardware:** Sonor Perfect Balance Pedal *(switch)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic Extreme 5B (continued)
- **Heads:** Remo
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$9,000

**Key developments:**
- Switched from Pearl to Sonor SQ2 — his current and most enduring kit relationship
- Deep calleth upon Deep (2017) pushes Satyricon into more atmospheric textures
- 1349's The Infernal Pathway (2019) maintains maximum-velocity extreme metal in parallel
- Three decades in, balances two demanding bands with one streamlined setup

> "Black metal drumming is about channeling darkness and aggression through precision." — *Decibel Magazine, 2010*

## FAQ

**Q: How has Frost's drumming style evolved across Satyricon albums?**
A: Frost's playing has moved through four phases: the raw, primitive blast beats of Dark Medieval Times and Nemesis Divina (1993–1996); the industrial-tinged technical expansion of Rebel Extravaganza and Volcano (1996–2002), where Roland triggers and double bass entered his vocabulary; the architecturally sophisticated, dynamically controlled drumming of Now, Diabolical and the self-titled 2013 album (2002–2013); and the leaner, more experimental approach of Deep calleth upon Deep and 1349's recent work (2013–present).

**Q: What drum kit does Frost play now?**
A: Frost currently plays a Sonor SQ2 Series kit, having switched from Pearl drums around the Deep calleth upon Deep era. His setup includes a Sonor SQ2 14"×6" maple snare, Zildjian A Custom and K Custom Dark cymbals, a Sonor Perfect Balance pedal, and Vic Firth American Classic Extreme 5B sticks.

**Q: Why did Frost switch from Pearl to Sonor drums?**
A: After two decades on Pearl, Frost moved to Sonor's SQ2 custom-build platform for its focused, articulate attack at extreme blast-beat tempos and a more compact footprint — well suited to a drummer splitting his time between Satyricon's increasingly textured material and 1349's maximum-velocity extreme metal.

**Q: Is Frost still active in both Satyricon and 1349?**
A: Yes. Frost has drummed for Satyricon since 1993 and co-founded 1349 in 2001, maintaining both demanding roles simultaneously for over two decades — one of the longest dual-band tenures in black metal.

**Q: When did Frost add electronic triggers to his setup?**
A: Roland electronic triggers entered Frost's live rig by at least the Rebel Extravaganza era (1999), ensuring consistent bass drum attack at extreme tempos regardless of room acoustics — a tool that has remained part of his setup ever since.
