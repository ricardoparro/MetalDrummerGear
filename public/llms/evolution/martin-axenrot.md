# Martin Axenrot Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Martin Axenrot has been Opeth's drummer since 2006, when he replaced Martin Lopez mid-tour during the "Ghost Reveries" cycle after Lopez departed due to health issues. A veteran of Sweden's extreme metal underground — Witchery, Nifelheim, and death metal supergroup Bloodbath — Axenrot introduced a twin-kick Sonor SQ2 and Meinl Byzance setup on his studio debut, "Watershed" (2008), and has carried that same rig, largely unchanged, through Opeth's dramatic pivot to 1970s-influenced progressive rock. This timeline documents his complete gear evolution from the Swedish extreme metal underground to nearly two decades as Opeth's drummer.

## Witchery, Nifelheim & the Extreme Metal Underground (1997–2006)

**Albums:** Witchery — session and touring work (1997–2006); Nifelheim — session and touring work
**Tours:** Witchery touring circuit (late 1990s–2000s)

Before Opeth, Martin Axenrot spent nearly a decade in Sweden's extreme metal underground — joining thrash/black metal act Witchery in 1997 and playing with black metal band Nifelheim. There was no fixed drum or cymbal endorsement during this stretch; Axenrot played whatever touring-grade gear was available, building the faster, heavier attack that would later distinguish his approach to Opeth's drum chair from Martin Lopez's more musical, progressive style.

- **Drums:** Various touring-grade kits (no fixed endorsement)
- **Snare:** Standard steel snares, varied by session
- **Cymbals:** Standard extreme metal cymbal setup (brand undocumented)
- **Hardware:** Standard touring hardware, pedal setup varied by gig
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B (established here, carried into his Opeth career)
- **Heads:** Standard session heads
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$2,200

**Key developments:**
- Joined Witchery in 1997, building a reputation in the Swedish extreme metal underground
- Played with black metal act Nifelheim
- Developed a heavier, more aggressive attack than the progressive death metal style he would inherit from Martin Lopez

> "I came up playing fast, aggressive extreme metal — Witchery, Nifelheim. Nobody was thinking about Opeth back then. But that background is exactly what I brought to the kit when the call came." — *Close-Up Magazine Interview, 2008*

## Joining Opeth & the Watershed Debut (2006–2008)

**Albums:** Watershed (2008)
**Tours:** Ghost Reveries touring cycle (joined mid-tour, 2006), Watershed World Tour 2008

Martin Lopez's health issues forced him to step away mid-tour during Opeth's "Ghost Reveries" cycle in 2006, and Axenrot stepped into the drum chair. His studio debut, "Watershed" (2008), recorded with producer Jens Bogren at Fascination Street Studios, gave Opeth its highest US chart placement to that point — #23 on the Billboard 200. Axenrot introduced a twin 22"x18" bass drum Sonor Designer/SQ2 configuration, a deliberate departure from Lopez's single-kick rig, paired with Meinl Byzance cymbals for the dark, hand-hammered character Opeth's progressive death metal vocabulary required.

- **Drums:** Sonor Designer/SQ2 Series — twin 22"x18" bass drums, 10"x8"/12"x9" rack toms, 14"x14"/16"x16" floor toms *(new — signaled the drum chair had changed hands)*
- **Snare:** Sonor Designer Maple Snare 14"x5.75" *(new — warm fundamental with body for heavy backbeats)*
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/19" crashes, 22" ride, 18" China *(new — dark, hand-hammered character)*
- **Hardware:** Sonor Giant Step Twin Pedals + Sonor 600 Series Hi-Hat Stand *(new — direct-drive for faster, more precise double-bass response)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B (unchanged)
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 / Emperor / Ambassador *(new — standard package for the Watershed sessions)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$13,378

**Key developments:**
- Replaced Martin Lopez mid-tour during the Ghost Reveries cycle in 2006
- Studio debut on Watershed (2008) — Opeth's highest US chart placement to that point at #23 Billboard 200
- Introduced the twin-kick Sonor SQ2 platform and the Meinl Byzance cymbal relationship that continued through his entire Opeth tenure

> "Martin Lopez played incredible, musical drums. I never tried to copy that — I brought what I had from Bloodbath and Witchery instead, and Watershed is the sound of that." — *Rhythm Magazine Interview, 2008*

## Heritage to In Cauda Venenum: The Steady Sonor Era (2011–Present)

**Albums:** Heritage (2011), Pale Communion (2014), Sorceress (2016), In Cauda Venenum (2019)
**Tours:** Heritage World Tour 2011–2012, Pale Communion Tour 2014–2015, Sorceress Tour 2016–2017, In Cauda Venenum Tour 2019–2020

Opeth's "Heritage" (2011) marked a dramatic stylistic pivot toward 1970s progressive rock, abandoning death growls entirely — yet Axenrot carried the same twin-kick Sonor SQ2 platform and Meinl Byzance cymbals through the transition unchanged. That gear consistency continued across "Pale Communion" (2014), which reached #21 on the Billboard 200, then "Sorceress" (2016) and "In Cauda Venenum" (2019). Running in parallel, Axenrot became a permanent member of death metal supergroup Bloodbath in 2008, a role he has held alongside his Opeth tenure ever since.

- **Drums:** Sonor Designer/SQ2 Series (unchanged — same twin-kick platform carried from Watershed)
- **Snare:** Sonor Designer Maple Snare 14"x5.75" (unchanged)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series (unchanged — same dark, hand-hammered setup)
- **Hardware:** Sonor Giant Step Direct Drive Twin Pedals *(upgrade — refined pedal generation)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B (unchanged)
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 / Emperor / Ambassador (unchanged)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$14,600 (~$20,356 inflation-adjusted, 2026)

**Key developments:**
- Heritage (2011) marked a dramatic stylistic pivot toward 1970s progressive rock, away from death growls entirely
- Pale Communion (2014) reached #21 on the Billboard 200, Opeth's second-highest US chart placement
- Sorceress (2016) and In Cauda Venenum (2019) continued the band's clean-vocal progressive direction
- Maintained an unbroken Sonor SQ2 twin-kick and Meinl Byzance setup across four consecutive studio albums
- Became a permanent member of death metal supergroup Bloodbath in 2008

> "The music changed completely on Heritage — no more growls, totally different palette. My kit didn't need to change, though. Sonor and Meinl give me everything from a blast beat to a Mellotron ballad." — *DRUM! Magazine Interview, 2014*

## FAQ

**Q: Who replaced Martin Lopez in Opeth?**
A: Martin Axenrot replaced Martin Lopez as Opeth's drummer in 2006, stepping in mid-tour during the "Ghost Reveries" cycle after Lopez departed due to health issues. Axenrot made his studio debut on "Watershed" (2008) and has remained Opeth's drummer ever since — longer than Lopez's original tenure.

**Q: What drum kit does Martin Axenrot play?**
A: Martin Axenrot plays a Sonor Designer/SQ2 Series kit built around twin 22"x18" bass drums, paired with Meinl Byzance cymbals — a setup he introduced on Watershed (2008) and has carried unchanged through Heritage, Pale Communion, Sorceress, and In Cauda Venenum.

**Q: When did Martin Axenrot join Opeth?**
A: Martin Axenrot joined Opeth in 2006, replacing Martin Lopez mid-tour during the Ghost Reveries cycle. He made his studio debut on Watershed (2008), which gave Opeth its highest US chart placement to that point at #23 on the Billboard 200.

**Q: What other bands does Martin Axenrot play in?**
A: Beyond Opeth, Martin Axenrot has been a core member of death metal supergroup Bloodbath since 2008. Earlier in his career he played in Witchery (1997–2010) and black metal band Nifelheim, both part of the Swedish extreme metal underground that shaped his heavier approach to Opeth's drum chair.
