# Martin Lopez Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Martin Lopez brought jazz-classical training to Opeth's progressive death metal in 1997, building a drumming voice defined by dynamic range and melodic phrasing across five albums before health struggles forced his departure in 2006 — a moment that opened the door for successor Martin Axenrot. He resurfaced in 2010 with Soen, returning to the warmer, jazz-informed textures that had always been closer to his musical instincts. This timeline documents every major gear era, era by era.

## Jazz Foundations / Opeth Debut (1996–1998)

**Albums:** My Arms, Your Hearse (1998)
**Tours:** Early Opeth club dates

Martin Lopez began his professional career with Amon Amarth in 1996 before joining Opeth in 1997, replacing original drummer Anders Nordin mid-album on what would become My Arms, Your Hearse. Working with budget pre-endorsement gear, Lopez delivered an uncompromising, technically precise death metal performance that established the four-tom melodic layout he would carry through every subsequent Opeth record.

- **Drums:** Pearl Forum / Sonor Force-class (mixed budget kit) — 22" kick, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Generic steel 14"×5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian B8 Pro — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" china
- **Hardware:** DW 5000 Double Pedal, Pearl H-900 hi-hat stand
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$2,000

**Key developments:**
- Joined Opeth in 1997, replacing original drummer Anders Nordin mid-album
- My Arms, Your Hearse (1998) establishes the four-tom melodic layout he'd use for his entire Opeth tenure
- Pre-endorsement budget gear — technique already well ahead of equipment
- Jazz-trained vocabulary still held largely in reserve by the album's heavier, no-clean-vocals mandate

> "I came from a jazz background into a death metal band mid-record. I just tried to play what the songs needed and figure the rest out later." — *Terrorizer Magazine Interview, 1999*

## Still Life to Blackwater Park: Dynamic Breakthrough (1999–2001)

**Albums:** Still Life (1999), Blackwater Park (2001)
**Tours:** Still Life European tour, Blackwater Park world tour

With Still Life and Blackwater Park, Lopez's full dynamic vocabulary emerged. He upgraded from a budget kit to Pearl Export and then Sonor's Designer Series, switched from entry-level Sabian B8 Pro to the dark, complex Sabian HH line, and — working with producer Steven Wilson on Blackwater Park — captured the seamless transitions between extreme-metal brutality and delicate acoustic passages that define Opeth's signature sound.

- **Drums:** Pearl Export → Sonor Designer Series (Maple) — 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms *(switch)*
- **Snare:** Pearl Sensitone Steel → Sonor Designer 14"×5.5" *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HH (Hand Hammered) — 14" hi-hats, 20" ride, 22" Raw Bell Dry Ride, 16"/18" AAX crashes *(switch)*
- **Hardware:** DW 5000 Double Pedal (continued)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A (continued)
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador (continued)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$4,500

**Key developments:**
- Sabian HH cymbals adopted — the most consequential single gear change of his career
- Sonor Designer Series brought in for Blackwater Park's open, dynamic production
- Steven Wilson's production on Blackwater Park (2001) unlocks his full dynamic vocabulary
- Ghost-note architecture and brush-adjacent sensitivity fully emerge for the first time

> "Blackwater Park is where I finally sounded like myself on record. Everything before that was getting ready for this." — *Drummer World Interview, 2002*

## Deliverance to Ghost Reveries: Progressive Death Metal Peak (2002–2005)

**Albums:** Deliverance (2002), Damnation (2003), Ghost Reveries (2005)
**Tours:** Deliverance/Damnation co-headline tour, Ghost Reveries world tour

Lopez's most extreme recorded performance (Deliverance) and his first all-acoustic, brush-driven work (Damnation) bookend this era, before Ghost Reveries — his final album with Opeth — closed the arc with the most polished expression of his progressive death metal vocabulary.

- **Drums:** Sonor Designer Series (Maple, expanded) — 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Sonor Designer 14"×5.5" + secondary snare *(new — added for Damnation's brush work)*
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HH / AAX expanded mix *(upgrade)*
- **Hardware:** DW 5000 Double Pedal (continued)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A + wire brushes *(new — added for Damnation)*
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador (continued)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$6,000

**Key developments:**
- Deliverance (2002) — his most extreme, technically demanding Opeth performance
- Damnation (2003) — Opeth's all-acoustic album introduces brush technique and a secondary snare
- Ghost Reveries (2005) — final album with Opeth, closing the progressive death metal arc
- Departed Opeth in 2006 due to health issues (panic disorder)

> "Damnation forced me to find a completely different vocabulary. No blast beats to hide behind — just touch and dynamics." — *Modern Drummer Interview, 2003*

## Departure & Soen: Jazz-Classical Return (2006–Present)

**Albums:** Soen – Cognitive (2012), Lykaia (2017), Imperial (2021), Memorial (2024)
**Tours:** Soen world tours, European festival circuit

Lopez left Opeth in 2006 due to health issues including panic disorder — a departure that opened the drum seat for successor Martin Axenrot. After several quiet years, Lopez co-founded Soen in 2010, returning to warmer, more jazz-classically informed textures with a boutique Noble & Cooley kit and darker Zildjian K cymbals.

- **Drums:** Noble & Cooley Walnut — boutique American walnut shells *(switch)*
- **Snare:** Noble & Cooley Solid Shell 14"×6" Maple *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Dark Series — 14" K Dark Thin hi-hats, 18"/20" K Dark Medium Thin crashes, 22" K Dark Light ride, 18" K China *(switch)*
- **Hardware:** Axis Percussion Double Pedal, DW 9100 Throne *(switch)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A (continued lifelong choice)
- **Heads:** Remo (continued)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$8,000

**Key developments:**
- Left Opeth in 2006 due to panic disorder — successor Martin Axenrot took over the drum seat
- Co-founded Soen in 2010 with bassist Martin Mendez, returning to active recording
- Switched to boutique Noble & Cooley kit and Zildjian K Dark cymbals — warmer, jazz-informed tone
- Continued releasing acclaimed Soen albums through Lykaia (2017), Imperial (2021), and Memorial (2024)

> "Soen let me go back to what I actually hear in my head — more space, more warmth, less about extremity for its own sake." — *Sonic Perspectives Interview, 2017*

## FAQ

**Q: Was Martin Lopez Opeth's original drummer?**
A: No — Anders Nordin was Opeth's original drummer, playing on the band's first two albums. Martin Lopez joined in 1997, replacing Nordin mid-recording of My Arms, Your Hearse (1998), and went on to play on five Opeth albums through Ghost Reveries (2005), becoming the drummer most closely associated with the band's progressive death metal peak.

**Q: How did Martin Lopez influence Opeth's progressive sound?**
A: Lopez brought jazz-classical training into Opeth's progressive death metal framework, introducing dynamic range, melodic tom phrasing, ghost-note architecture, and — on Damnation (2003) — brush technique that no extreme metal drummer of the era was using. His four-tom layout and architectural fill approach became a structural template the band built on through Blackwater Park and Ghost Reveries.

**Q: Why did Martin Lopez leave Opeth?**
A: Lopez departed Opeth in 2006 due to health issues, including panic disorder, that made continued touring untenable. His departure marked a clear inflection point in Opeth's history, with Martin Axenrot taking over the drum seat for subsequent albums.

**Q: Who replaced Martin Lopez in Opeth?**
A: Martin Axenrot replaced Martin Lopez as Opeth's drummer following Lopez's 2006 departure and has remained the band's drummer ever since, while Lopez resurfaced in 2010 with the progressive metal band Soen.

**Q: What cymbals does Martin Lopez use with Soen?**
A: Lopez plays the Zildjian K Dark Series with Soen — 14" K Dark Thin hi-hats, 18" and 20" K Dark Medium Thin crashes, a 22" K Dark Light ride, and an 18" K China — a darker, warmer voice than the Sabian HH cymbals he used throughout his Opeth career.
