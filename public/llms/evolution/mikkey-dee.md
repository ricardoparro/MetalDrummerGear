# Mikkey Dee Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Mikkey Dee holds one of the longest unbroken tenures in metal drumming history: 23 years as Motörhead's drummer, from Bastards in 1993 to Lemmy Kilmister's death in December 2015. His career spans a King Diamond apprenticeship, the full arc of Motörhead's later career as a relentless touring and recording machine, and a post-Motörhead chapter with Scorpions that continues today. His Pearl and Premier kit progression tracks nearly four decades of non-stop professional metal drumming. This timeline documents every major equipment era across his career.

## King Diamond Era (1988–1992)

**Albums:** Them (1988), Conspiracy (1989), The Eye (1990)
**Tours:** King Diamond world tours 1988–1991, North American and European touring

Mikkey Dee joined King Diamond's band in 1988, appearing on four albums — Them (1988), Conspiracy (1989), The Eye (1990), and In Concert 1987: Abigail (live, 1991). The King Diamond years were his professional apprenticeship: technically demanding melodic metal with theatrical staging requirements, teaching Dee the discipline of consistent performance within complex arrangements. His Pearl setup during this period was the foundation for everything that followed.

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Professional — maple shells: 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms (standard for professional touring drummers of the era; warm, powerful tone suited to King Diamond's melodic metal)
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating 14"×5.5" (steel shell, crisp cutting attack across theatrically varied arrangements)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride (bright, cutting, durable — the professional choice for late-1980s hard rock and heavy metal touring)
- **Hardware:** Pearl P-2002C Eliminator Double Pedal (cam-driven; a step up from basic chain pedals for demanding technical arrangements)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B (heavier sticks for the physical demands of consistent touring and recording)
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador (standard single-ply batter heads)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$5,000

**Key developments:**
- Joined King Diamond's band in 1988
- Pearl Masters Professional endorsement established
- Them (1988) and Conspiracy (1989) — professional debut recordings
- Learned discipline of consistent performance within complex theatrical arrangements
- Foundation laid for the non-stop touring career that would define the following three decades

> "King Diamond taught me how to be a professional. The arrangements were demanding, the shows were theatrical — you had to be precise every night. That discipline stuck with me." — *Rhythm Magazine, 2010*

## Early Motörhead Era (1993–2004)

**Albums:** Bastards (1993), Sacrifice (1995), Overnight Sensation (1996), Snake Bite Love (1998), We Are Motörhead (2000)
**Tours:** Motörhead world tours 1993–2004, extensive North American and European touring, Lemmy's 50th birthday tour (1995)

Mikkey Dee joined Motörhead in 1992, replacing Phil Taylor ahead of Bastards (1993, ZYX Music) — the biggest and most demanding role of his career: the sonic avatar of Motörhead's thundering, unrelenting rock 'n' roll. His transition to a Premier kit aligned with Lemmy's British-rooted aesthetic, and the combination of Premier's punchy character and the band's massive touring schedule drove constant refinement of his live setup.

- **Drums:** Premier Signia / Artist Series — birch/maple shells: 22"×18" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms *(switch — British punchy birch character complemented Motörhead's raw, driven rock sound)*
- **Snare:** Premier Signia Steel 14"×6.5" *(switch — deeper steel snare for the explosive, walloping crack that became Dee's Motörhead signature)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A / A Custom Series — 14" A New Beat hi-hats, 16"/18" A crashes, 20" A ride (maintained Zildjian relationship; bright, cutting character suited Motörhead's raw rock production)
- **Hardware:** Premier / DW 5000 Double Pedal — dual-chain drive *(switch — dependable touring hardware that could survive hundreds of shows per year)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B (consistent heavier hickory through the early Motörhead years)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor / Powerstroke 3 — Emperor on toms, Powerstroke 3 on kick *(upgrade — focused, punchy attack cutting through Motörhead's enormous stage volume)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$7,000

**Key developments:**
- Joined Motörhead — replaced Phil Taylor ahead of Bastards (1993)
- Switched to Premier Signia — British drum character suits Motörhead's sound
- Bastards (1993) debut with band; Overnight Sensation (1996) reaches new commercial peak
- Established one of the most consistent drummer/band combinations in metal history
- Motörhead touring schedule among the most relentless in rock history — up to 200 shows per year

> "With Motörhead, you don't have to think — you just play. Lemmy sets the tempo and the room follows. My job was to be the engine. We played every night like it was the last night." — *Modern Drummer, 2002*

## Late Motörhead / Bad Magic Era (2004–2015)

**Albums:** Kiss of Death (2006), Motörizer (2008), The Wörld Is Yours (2010), Aftershock (2013), Bad Magic (2015)
**Tours:** Motörhead world tours 2004–2015, Download/Wacken/Hellfest headline slots, final Motörhead touring cycle 2015

The final and most refined chapter of Mikkey Dee's Motörhead tenure. Dee's setup reached its most polished form during this period: Pearl Reference drums replacing Premier as his primary endorsement, a full Evans head partnership, and an expanded cymbal configuration needed to fill increasingly large arenas and festival stages. Bad Magic won a Grammy for Best Metal Performance ("Thunder & Lightning") — Motörhead's only Grammy, awarded weeks before Lemmy's death on December 28, 2015.

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series — maple/birch hybrid: 22"×18" kick, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms *(switch — fuller, more resonant tone for the arena and festival stages Motörhead now regularly headlined)*
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"×6.5" *(switch — returned to Pearl Free-Floating; explosive steel crack remained his defining sound)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom / K Custom Dark Series — 14" A Custom hi-hats, 17"/18" crashes, 21" K Custom ride, 19" China *(upgrade — K Custom Dark ride for warmer wash, China added for the biggest production contexts)*
- **Hardware:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal *(switch — direct drive for precise, consistent response across hundreds of festival and arena shows per year)*
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature *(signature — custom hickory for the power and projection demands of Motörhead's final era)*
- **Heads:** Evans G2 / EMAD — G2 on toms, EMAD on kick *(switch — warm resonant attack on toms, focused projecting low-end at arena volume)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$18,000

**Key developments:**
- Pearl Reference replaces Premier as primary kit
- Evans G2/EMAD heads adopted
- Pearl Demon Drive direct drive switch
- Vic Firth Mikkey Dee signature sticks launched
- Expanded cymbal setup for arena and festival stages
- Bad Magic (2015) — Grammy winner ("Thunder & Lightning") — Motörhead's only Grammy
- Lemmy Kilmister dies December 28, 2015 — Motörhead disbands; 23-year tenure ends

> "The last show Motörhead ever played was in Germany. We didn't know it was the last show. That's the way Lemmy would have wanted it — just another night of rock 'n' roll. Everything you had, every night." — *Classic Rock Magazine, 2016*

## Scorpions Era (2016–Present)

**Albums:** Return to Forever (2015, contributed), Rock Believer (2022)
**Tours:** Scorpions world tours 2016–present, farewell tour appearances, stadium and arena headline slots globally

Following Lemmy's death and Motörhead's dissolution, Mikkey Dee joined Scorpions in 2016 — a natural fit given the German band's similar commitment to hard rock fundamentals and relentless touring. His current setup continues the Pearl Reference / Evans configuration developed in Motörhead's final years, now adapted for Scorpions' more melodic hard rock context — slightly warmer cymbal choices and a more dynamic snare approach.

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series (refined) — maple/birch hybrid: 22"×18" kick, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms (continued Pearl Reference partnership, now optimised for a wider dynamic range than Motörhead's all-out attack demanded)
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"×6.5" / Maple 14"×6.5" *(upgrade — alternates between steel and maple depending on setlist demands, a more nuanced approach than the consistent steel choice of the Motörhead years)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom / K Hybrid Series — 14" K Custom Dark hi-hats, 17"/18" crashes, 21" K Custom ride, 19" China *(upgrade — darker, more complex choices suited to Scorpions' broader dynamic range from power ballads to hard rock anthems)*
- **Hardware:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal (continued Demon Drive partnership; no reason to change a reliable decade-plus platform)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature (refined signature model serving both live performance and educational content)
- **Heads:** Evans G2 / EMAD2 — G2 on toms, EMAD2 on kick *(upgrade — tighter, more refined kick tone suited to Scorpions' polished arena production)*
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$22,000

**Key developments:**
- Joined Scorpions (2016) — first major band post-Motörhead
- Rock Believer (2022) — first full Scorpions studio album recorded with Dee from the start
- Cymbal evolution toward darker K Custom / K Hybrid choices
- Evans EMAD2 replaces EMAD for refined low-end
- Dual snare configuration (steel and maple) for broader dynamic range
- Continues one of the longest active careers in major metal and hard rock drumming

> "Scorpions is a different world from Motörhead — the dynamics, the ballads, the production values. But the commitment to the show is the same. You give everything every night. That part never changes." — *Rhythm Magazine, 2022*

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Mikkey Dee use in Motörhead?**
A: Mikkey Dee played a Premier Signia / Artist Series kit through the early Motörhead era (1993–2004), then switched to a Pearl Reference Series maple/birch hybrid kit for the late Motörhead / Bad Magic era (2004–2015) — the setup that carried through the band's final tours and their 2015 Grammy win.

**Q: What drum kit does Mikkey Dee use today?**
A: Mikkey Dee currently plays a Pearl Reference Series kit with Scorpions — a maple/birch hybrid configuration with a 22"×18" kick and 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms, alongside Zildjian K Custom / K Hybrid cymbals and the Pearl Demon Drive double pedal.

**Q: What drumsticks does Mikkey Dee use?**
A: Mikkey Dee plays the Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature model, launched during Motörhead's later era to reflect his status as one of the most recognised drummers in hard rock and metal. Before his signature model, he played standard Vic Firth 5B sticks.

**Q: Why did Mikkey Dee join Scorpions?**
A: Mikkey Dee joined Scorpions in 2016 following Lemmy Kilmister's death in December 2015 and Motörhead's subsequent dissolution. Scorpions' commitment to hard rock fundamentals and relentless touring made it a natural fit for Dee's playing style.

**Q: Did Mikkey Dee win a Grammy with Motörhead?**
A: Yes — Bad Magic (2015) won the Grammy for Best Metal Performance ("Thunder & Lightning"), Motörhead's only Grammy award, given just weeks before Lemmy Kilmister's death on December 28, 2015, which ended the band's run.

**Q: How long was Mikkey Dee Motörhead's drummer?**
A: Mikkey Dee was Motörhead's drummer for 23 years, from Bastards (1993) — his recording debut with the band, after joining in 1992 to replace Phil Taylor — until the band's dissolution following Lemmy Kilmister's death in December 2015.
