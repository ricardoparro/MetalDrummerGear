# Nicko McBrain Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Nicko McBrain has been Iron Maiden's drummer since 1982, one of the longest tenures of any metal drummer with a single band. His gear evolution runs from the clear-acrylic Ludwig Vistalite kit that powered "Piece of Mind" and "Powerslave," through the Premier Resonator era that carried Iron Maiden from "Somewhere in Time" to "The Final Frontier," to his current Sonor SQ1/SQ2 signature setup on "Senjutsu" (2021) — all built around one unbroken constant: a single bass drum pedal, no double kick, across more than four decades. This timeline documents that 40+ year journey.

## Piece of Mind / Powerslave Era (1982–1984)

**Albums:** Piece of Mind (1983); Powerslave (1984)
**Tours:** World Slavery Tour 1984–1985 — over 300 shows in 13 months

McBrain joined Iron Maiden in 1982, replacing Clive Burr. His debut album, "Piece of Mind" (1983), was recorded at Compass Point Studios on a clear-acrylic Ludwig Vistalite kit — establishing from his very first Maiden recording the single-bass-drum philosophy that would define his entire career. "Powerslave" (1984), featuring the 13-minute epic "Rime of the Ancient Mariner," pushed the setup through the punishing World Slavery Tour.

- **Drums:** Ludwig Vistalite — clear acrylic shells, single 22"×14" bass drum
- **Snare:** Ludwig Supraphonic LM400, 14"×5", seamless aluminum "Ludalloy" shell
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 — 14" Medium Hi-Hats, crashes, ride, china
- **Hardware:** Ludwig Speed King single pedal — no double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Pro-Mark 5B — balanced weight for technical playing
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador Coated
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$2,500

**Key developments:**
- Joined Iron Maiden in 1982, replacing Clive Burr
- "Piece of Mind" (1983) — first album, established the single-bass-drum philosophy from day one
- "Powerslave" (1984) — featured "Rime of the Ancient Mariner"
- World Slavery Tour 1984–1985 — over 300 shows in 13 months

> "I've never needed a double bass pedal. One foot is enough if you practice hard." — *Modern Drummer, 2010*

## Somewhere in Time to The Final Frontier — Premier Resonator Era (1986–2010)

**Albums:** Somewhere in Time (1986); Seventh Son of a Seventh Son (1988); Brave New World (2000); The Final Frontier (2010)
**Tours:** Reunion touring following Bruce Dickinson and Adrian Smith's 1999 return; Iron Maiden world tours 1986–2010

McBrain moved to a Premier Resonator birch kit for "Somewhere in Time" (1986), the first Iron Maiden album to feature an electronic component — Roland pads integrated for kick reinforcement in the album's synth-heavy production. "Seventh Son of a Seventh Son" (1988) was the band's commercial peak of the era. Through the 2000 Bruce Dickinson reunion on "Brave New World," and on through "The Final Frontier" (2010), the Premier Resonator platform and single-pedal philosophy remained the constant.

- **Drums:** Premier Resonator — birch shells, single 22"×18" bass drum
- **Snare:** Premier 2000 Snare, 14"×6.5", steel shell, tuned low and dry
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 — continued setup, unchanged across the entire era
- **Hardware:** Premier 252 pedal + Roland electronic pads — single pedal, first electronic component
- **Sticks:** Pro-Mark 5B — continued configuration
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador Coated
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$3,200

**Key developments:**
- "Somewhere in Time" (1986) — first Premier Resonator kit and first use of electronic pads
- "Seventh Son of a Seventh Son" (1988) — commercial peak of the era
- Bruce Dickinson and Adrian Smith rejoined in 1999, leading into "Brave New World" (2000)
- Premier Resonator and single-pedal philosophy carried through to "The Final Frontier" (2010)

> "Whatever the production called for, the drums had to serve it. On Somewhere in Time that meant tuning things drier than I ever had before." — *Rhythm Magazine, 1986*

## Sonor SQ1/SQ2 Signature Era (2015–Present)

**Albums:** The Book of Souls (2015); Senjutsu (2021)
**Tours:** Legacy of the Beast Tour; The Future Past Tour 2023

McBrain's mature setup moved to Sonor — German-engineered SQ1/SQ2 kits with North American maple shells — spanning "The Book of Souls" (2015) through "Senjutsu" (2021), which debuted at UK #1. Recorded with producer Kevin Shirley at Guillaume Tell Studio in Paris, Senjutsu required full single-take performances of unprecedented length, including the 12:40 "The Parchment" — driven, as ever, by a single bass drum pedal.

- **Drums:** Sonor SQ1/SQ2 — North American maple shells, three-rack-tom configuration, single 22"×17" bass drum
- **Snare:** Sonor Nicko McBrain Signature Snare, 14"×6.5", aluminum/brass alloy
- **Cymbals:** Paiste Signature series — expanded from the long-standing 2002 setup
- **Hardware:** Sonor single bass drum pedal — unchanged since 1983
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Nicko McBrain Signature (SNM) — larger diameter and length
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick), Remo Ambassador Coated (toms/snare)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$5,500

**Key developments:**
- "The Book of Souls" (2015) — Iron Maiden's longest studio album, first on the Sonor platform
- "Senjutsu" (2021) — debuted at UK #1, recorded with Kevin Shirley at Guillaume Tell Studio, Paris
- Sonor Nicko McBrain Signature snare and Vic Firth Nicko McBrain Signature sticks — both custom-built to his specifications
- Single bass drum pedal unchanged since "Piece of Mind" (1983) — over four decades on one foot

> "Senjutsu was made with one foot, same as Piece of Mind was. Some things you just don't need to change." — *Rhythm Magazine, 2021*

- **Ludwig (drums)** — 1982–1984 (Vistalite acrylic kit, Piece of Mind and Powerslave)
- **Premier (drums)** — 1986–2010 (Resonator birch kit, Somewhere in Time through The Final Frontier)
- **Sonor (drums)** — 2015–present (SQ1/SQ2 maple kit, The Book of Souls and Senjutsu)
- **Paiste (cymbals)** — 1983–present (2002 series early career → Signature series in the modern era, unbroken across four decades)

## FAQ

**Q: Who has been Iron Maiden's drummer?**
A: Nicko McBrain has been Iron Maiden's drummer since 1982, when he replaced Clive Burr. He has recorded every Iron Maiden studio album from "Piece of Mind" (1983) through "Senjutsu" (2021), making his tenure one of the longest of any drummer with a single major metal band.

**Q: How long has Nicko McBrain played with Iron Maiden?**
A: Nicko McBrain has played with Iron Maiden continuously since 1982 — more than 40 years as of "Senjutsu" (2021) and the subsequent "Future Past" tour. Before joining Maiden he played with Pat Travers Band (1977–1981) and the French rock band Trust (1981–1982).

**Q: Does Nicko McBrain use a double bass pedal?**
A: No. Nicko McBrain has never used a double bass pedal in his Iron Maiden career, from "Piece of Mind" (1983) to "Senjutsu" (2021). He achieves Iron Maiden's signature galloping rhythms entirely with a single bass drum and a single pedal, a deliberate technical choice maintained across more than four decades.

**Q: What drum kit does Nicko McBrain play?**
A: Nicko McBrain currently plays a Sonor SQ1/SQ2 kit with North American maple shells, paired with a Sonor Nicko McBrain Signature snare and Paiste Signature cymbals. Earlier in his career he played a Ludwig Vistalite acrylic kit ("Piece of Mind," 1983; "Powerslave," 1984) and a Premier Resonator birch kit spanning "Somewhere in Time" (1986) through "The Final Frontier" (2010).

**Q: What was the first Iron Maiden album with Nicko McBrain?**
A: "Piece of Mind" (1983) was Nicko McBrain's first album as Iron Maiden's drummer, recorded at Compass Point Studios on a Ludwig Vistalite acrylic kit. He had joined the band in 1982, replacing Clive Burr, who had drummed on "The Number of the Beast" (1982).
