# Paul Bostaph Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Paul Bostaph is Slayer's longest-serving drummer overall, filling the seat Dave Lombardo vacated across two separate tenures — 1992-2001 and 2013-2019 — and sitting behind the kit for the band's final show in 2019. This timeline documents his complete gear evolution from the DW Collector's Series and Zildjian A Custom setup of Divine Intervention (1994) and Diabolus in Musica (1998), through a switch to Tama Starclassic Maple and Paiste for God Hates Us All (2001), into a Pacific Drums LX chapter for Testament's comeback album The Formation of Damnation (2008), and finally a Pearl Masters Maple Complete rig for Repentless (2015) and Slayer's farewell tour.

## First Slayer Era — DW to Tama (1992–2001)

**Albums:** Divine Intervention (1994), Undisputed Attitude (1996), Diabolus in Musica (1998), God Hates Us All (2001)
**Tours:** Divine Intervention Tour 1994–1995, Ozzfest 1996, 1998

Paul Bostaph joined Slayer in 1992, replacing founding drummer Dave Lombardo, and made his studio debut on Divine Intervention (1994), followed by the punk covers album Undisputed Attitude (1996) and the drop-tuned, groove-oriented Diabolus in Musica (1998). Across this stretch he played a DW Collector's Series Maple kit — American-made all-maple shells with DW's True Pitch tuning system — paired with Zildjian A Custom cymbals, a brighter, cleaner setup than the Paiste-driven sound of the Lombardo era. For God Hates Us All (2001), his final album of this first tenure, Bostaph switched to a Tama Starclassic Maple kit and Paiste 2002/Signature cymbals, reflecting both Slayer's long-standing Tama endorsement and producer Matt Hyde's preference for a warmer, more resonant low end.

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series Maple (1994–1998) → Tama Starclassic Maple (2001) — double 22"x18" kicks throughout
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Steel 14"x6.5" → Tama Bell Brass / Steel 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom (1994–1998) → Paiste 2002 / Signature (2001) — 14" Hi-Hats, 16"/18"/19" Crashes, 20"–22" Ride, 18" China *(switch)*
- **Hardware:** DW 5002 double pedal → Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide *(switch — matched the new drum endorsement)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Power 5B
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$4,800

**Key developments:**
- Joined Slayer in 1992, replacing Dave Lombardo
- Divine Intervention (1994) — studio debut on DW Collector's Series Maple
- Diabolus in Musica (1998) — Zildjian A Custom cymbals replace the Paiste sound of the Lombardo era
- God Hates Us All (2001) — switch to Tama Starclassic Maple and Paiste 2002/Signature
- Left Slayer in 2001 when Dave Lombardo rejoined

> "Coming into Slayer after Dave was never about copying him note for note. It was about finding my own pocket while keeping the songs exactly as heavy as they needed to be." — *Rhythm Magazine Interview, 1997*

## Exodus / Testament Interim Era (2005–2008)

**Albums:** Shovel Headed Kill Machine (2005), The Formation of Damnation (2008)
**Tours:** The Formation of Damnation touring cycle, 2008–2009

After leaving Slayer in 2001, Bostaph diversified into the wider Bay Area thrash scene he came up in, joining Exodus for Shovel Headed Kill Machine (2005) before landing at Testament for The Formation of Damnation (2008) — the album that reunited Alex Skolnick and Greg Christian with Chuck Billy and Eric Peterson for the first time in over a decade. Bostaph tracked Formation on a Pacific Drums & Percussion (PDP) LX Series maple kit — DW's sister brand, and a natural landing point after he moved into the broader DW family around 2007 — paired with a shallower 14"x5" DW Edge snare and his long-running Paiste cymbal relationship.

- **Drums:** Pacific Drums & Percussion (PDP) LX Series Maple — 6-ply all-maple shells, double 22"x18" kicks, 10"/12"/14" rack toms, 16"/18" floor toms *(switch)*
- **Snare:** DW Edge Snare 14"x5" — shallower than his Slayer-era snares *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 / RUDE Series — 14" Sound Edge Hi-Hats, 16"/18" Crashes, 20" RUDE Ride, 18" China
- **Hardware:** DW 5000 Series double bass drum pedal *(switch)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Power 5B
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$4,000

**Key developments:**
- Joined Exodus for Shovel Headed Kill Machine (2005)
- Stepped into Testament for The Formation of Damnation (2008) after drummer Nick Barker's visa issues
- Formation of Damnation won Metal Hammer's Golden Gods award for Best Album (2008)
- A distinct PDP/DW gear chapter separate from any of his Slayer-era setups

> "Chuck asked me to play the record the way I approached Exodus — restrained, in service of the songs, without overplaying. That stuck with me." — *Metal Hammer Interview, 2008*

## Second Slayer Era — Pearl / Sabian (2013–Present)

**Albums:** Repentless (2015)
**Tours:** Final World Tour 2018–2019

Bostaph rejoined Slayer in 2013 following Dave Lombardo's second departure, recording Repentless (2015) — the band's first album without founding guitarist Jeff Hanneman — on a Pearl Masters Maple Complete (MCX) kit paired with Sabian AAX cymbals, a full brand switch from both the DW/Zildjian and Tama/Paiste setups of his first Slayer tenure. He sat behind that Pearl/Sabian rig through Slayer's Final World Tour, playing the band's last-ever show at the Forum in Los Angeles on November 30, 2019. In 2024 he returned to Testament, the band that gave him his post-Slayer gear chapter back in 2008.

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Maple Complete (MCX) — all-maple shells, double 22"x18" kicks *(switch — his third distinct drum brand across the Slayer career)*
- **Snare:** Pearl Masters Steel 14"x6.5" *(switch)*
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series — 14" AAX Stage Hi-Hats, 17"/19" AAX X-Plosion Crashes, 21" AAX Stage Ride, 18" AAXtreme China *(switch — first cymbal brand change away from Paiste/Zildjian in over two decades)*
- **Hardware:** Pearl Eliminator Redline double pedal, Pearl Eliminator hi-hat stand, Pearl D-2000 Roadster Throne *(switch)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Power 5B — unchanged since Forbidden
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$5,800

**Key developments:**
- Rejoined Slayer in 2013 following Dave Lombardo's second departure
- Repentless (2015) — Slayer's first album without Jeff Hanneman, full switch to Pearl/Sabian
- Played Slayer's final-ever show at the Forum in Los Angeles, November 30, 2019
- Returned to Testament in 2024

> "Repentless needed a different kind of weight than anything I'd played on before. The Pearl kit and Sabian cymbals gave me that modern, controlled power." — *DRUM! Magazine Interview, 2015*

## FAQ

**Q: Who replaced Dave Lombardo in Slayer?**
A: Paul Bostaph replaced Dave Lombardo in Slayer in 1992 and became the band's longest-serving drummer overall, playing on four studio albums across two tenures (1992–2001 and 2013–2019) and performing Slayer's final-ever show in 2019.

**Q: Did Slayer have other drummers besides Dave Lombardo?**
A: Yes — Paul Bostaph is the only drummer other than Dave Lombardo to record multiple Slayer studio albums, appearing on Divine Intervention (1994), Undisputed Attitude (1996), Diabolus in Musica (1998), and Repentless (2015) across two separate tenures.

**Q: What drum kit does Paul Bostaph use?**
A: Paul Bostaph played a Pearl Masters Maple Complete kit with Sabian AAX cymbals for Slayer's Repentless (2015) and the band's Final World Tour. Earlier in his career he used DW Collector's Series and Tama Starclassic kits during his first Slayer tenure, and a Pacific Drums (PDP) LX Series kit for Testament's The Formation of Damnation (2008).

**Q: Why did Paul Bostaph switch drum brands so often?**
A: Bostaph's gear changed with each new chapter of his career: DW Collector's Series and Zildjian A Custom for Divine Intervention and Diabolus in Musica (1994–1998), Tama Starclassic and Paiste for God Hates Us All (2001), Pacific Drums (PDP) and DW for Testament's Formation of Damnation (2008), and finally Pearl and Sabian for Repentless (2015) and Slayer's farewell tour.

**Q: How many albums did Paul Bostaph record with Slayer?**
A: Paul Bostaph recorded four studio albums with Slayer: Divine Intervention (1994), Undisputed Attitude (1996), Diabolus in Musica (1998), and Repentless (2015) — more than any Slayer drummer besides founding member Dave Lombardo.
