# Paul Bostaph Drum Kit Evolution — Complete Timeline

Paul Bostaph is Slayer's longest-serving drummer by total years — twelve combined across two tenures (1992–2001, 2013–2019) — succeeding Dave Lombardo not once but twice and playing the band's final-ever show. His gear evolution runs from Bay Area thrash roots with Forbidden, through a first Slayer tenure that shifted from DW/Zildjian on "Diabolus in Musica" (1998) to Tama/Paiste on "God Hates Us All" (2001), to the DW Collector's Series rig that carried Slayer through "Repentless" (2015) and the Final World Tour. This timeline documents that 32-year journey.

## Forbidden / Bay Area Thrash Roots (1987–1991)

**Albums:** Twisted Into Form (Forbidden, 1990)
**Tours:** Forbidden Bay Area club and regional touring 1987–1991

Bostaph came up through the same San Francisco Bay Area thrash scene that produced Metallica, Exodus, and Testament, joining Forbidden (then Forbidden Evil) in 1987. He recorded the thrash classic "Twisted Into Form" (1990) on Combat Records, establishing the powerful, precise drumming style he would later bring to Slayer.

- **Drums:** Pearl kit (early career) — standard production shells
- **Snare:** Pearl steel snare, bright tuning for cut
- **Cymbals:** Paiste developing relationship — standard crash/ride/hi-hat setup
- **Hardware:** Standard double bass pedal — entry-level configuration
- **Sticks:** Vater sticks — standard configuration
- **Heads:** Remo standard heads, coated/clear configuration
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$1,800

**Key developments:**
- Joined Forbidden (then Forbidden Evil) in 1987
- "Twisted Into Form" (1990) — Bay Area thrash classic
- Built the technical foundation that led to his 1992 Slayer recruitment

> "The Bay Area scene was brutal in the best way. Everybody was trying to out-play everybody else at the next show." — *Thrash Metal Chronicles Interview, 1990*

## First Slayer Tenure — Divine Intervention to Diabolus in Musica (1992–1998)

**Albums:** Divine Intervention (1994); Undisputed Attitude (1996); Diabolus in Musica (1998)
**Tours:** Divine Intervention Tour 1994–1995; Ozzfest 1996

Bostaph joined Slayer in 1992, filling Dave Lombardo's chair for the first time. "Divine Intervention" (1994) was his first Slayer studio album, followed by the punk-covers record "Undisputed Attitude" (1996). "Diabolus in Musica" (1998) dropped the guitars to D-flat tuning and sparked "selling out" accusations — recorded with Toby Wright at The Hook in Richmond, CA, on a DW Collector's Series Maple kit paired with Zildjian A Custom cymbals, a marked departure from the Paiste-centric sound of the classic Lombardo-era records.

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series Maple *(upgrade — all-maple shells, double 22"×18" kicks)*
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Steel Snare *(upgrade — 14"×6.5", steel shell)*
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom *(switch — hi-hats, crashes, ride, china)*
- **Hardware:** DW 5002 Double Bass Drum Pedal *(new — smooth cam action)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Power 5B *(new — heavier profile for sustained thrash power)*
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick), Remo Emperor Clear (toms)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$4,200

**Key developments:**
- Joined Slayer in 1992, succeeding Dave Lombardo for the first time
- "Divine Intervention" (1994) — Bostaph's first Slayer studio album
- "Diabolus in Musica" (1998) — D-flat tuning and DW/Zildjian gear shift sparked fan controversy, later reassessed as one of Slayer's most underrated records
- Switched from Paiste to Zildjian A Custom cymbals for the Diabolus sessions

> "People wanted Reign in Blood again. We gave them something heavier in a different way, and it took years for that to click for some fans." — *Rhythm Magazine Interview, 1998*

## God Hates Us All Era (1999–2001)

**Albums:** God Hates Us All (2001)
**Tours:** God Hates Us All promotional touring 2001

While on hiatus from Slayer, Bostaph joined Testament (1999–2001). He returned to the Slayer kit for "God Hates Us All," released the morning of September 11, 2001 — the last Slayer studio album before Dave Lombardo's eventual return on "Christ Illusion." Produced by Matt Hyde at The Warehouse Studio in Vancouver, the record moved to a Tama Starclassic Maple kit and Paiste 2002/Signature cymbals.

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple *(switch — all-maple shells with reinforcement rings, double 22"×18" kicks)*
- **Snare:** Tama Bell Brass / Steel Snare *(switch — 14"×6.5", steel shell)*
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002/Signature *(switch back — 14" Sound Edge hi-hats, 18"/19" crashes, 22" Signature Power Ride, 18" china)*
- **Hardware:** Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide ×2 *(switch — twin single pedals on separate bass drums)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Power 5B — continued
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated (batter)
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$4,700

**Key developments:**
- Joined Testament (1999–2001) during a hiatus from Slayer
- "God Hates Us All" released the morning of September 11, 2001
- Switched from DW/Zildjian to Tama Starclassic Maple and Paiste 2002/Signature
- Left Slayer in 2001 when Dave Lombardo rejoined the band

> "We finished that record weeks before it came out. Nobody could have known what that release date would mean." — *Terrorizer Magazine Interview, 2001*

## Return to Slayer — Repentless & The Final World Tour (2013–2019)

**Albums:** Repentless (2015)
**Tours:** Slayer Final World Tour 2018–2019

Bostaph rejoined Slayer in 2013 following Dave Lombardo's second departure, and recorded "Repentless" (2015) — the band's first album without founding guitarist Jeff Hanneman, who died in 2013. He was behind the kit for Slayer's Final World Tour (2018–2019) and played the band's last-ever show at the Forum in Los Angeles on November 30, 2019.

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series Maple/Mahogany *(switch — hybrid shells, double 22"×18" kicks for live shows)*
- **Snare:** DW 14"×6.5" Collector's Maple — confirmed final-era snare
- **Cymbals:** Paiste RUDE series *(switch — raw, unlathed finish for maximum aggression)*
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Series double pedal *(switch — confirmed final-era hardware)*
- **Sticks:** Vater Power 5B — confirmed final-era, unchanged since the late 1990s
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor/Ambassador
- **Estimated kit cost (original):** ~$5,000

**Key developments:**
- Rejoined Slayer in 2013 after Dave Lombardo's second departure
- "Repentless" (2015) — first Slayer album without Jeff Hanneman
- Played Slayer's final-ever show at the Forum, Los Angeles, November 30, 2019
- Slayer's longest-serving drummer by total years — twelve across two tenures

> "Playing that last show, I wasn't thinking about being the guy who wasn't Dave. I was thinking about doing right by the songs one more time." — *Rolling Stone Interview, 2019*

## FAQ

**Q: Who replaced Dave Lombardo in Slayer?**
A: Paul Bostaph replaced Dave Lombardo in Slayer, joining in 1992 after Lombardo's first departure. Bostaph recorded four Slayer studio albums — "Divine Intervention" (1994), "Undisputed Attitude" (1996), "Diabolus in Musica" (1998), and "Repentless" (2015) — across two separate tenures (1992–2001, 2013–2019), making him Slayer's longest-serving drummer by total years.

**Q: Did Slayer have other drummers besides Dave Lombardo?**
A: Yes. Paul Bostaph is the primary answer — he drummed for Slayer across two tenures totaling twelve years and recorded four studio albums. Jon Dette also filled in on tour in 1996–1997, and Bostaph played Slayer's final-ever show at the Forum in Los Angeles on November 30, 2019, after Lombardo's second and final departure in 2013.

**Q: What drum kit did Paul Bostaph play on Diabolus in Musica?**
A: Paul Bostaph recorded "Diabolus in Musica" (1998) on a DW Collector's Series Maple kit paired with Zildjian A Custom cymbals — a departure from the Paiste-centric sound of the classic Lombardo-era Slayer records, tracked by producer Toby Wright at The Hook in Richmond, California.

**Q: What gear did Paul Bostaph use on God Hates Us All?**
A: For "God Hates Us All" (2001), Bostaph switched to a Tama Starclassic Maple kit with Paiste 2002/Signature cymbals and Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide double pedals, produced by Matt Hyde at The Warehouse Studio in Vancouver.

**Q: Is Paul Bostaph still playing drums?**
A: Yes. After Slayer's farewell in 2019, Paul Bostaph returned to Testament in 2024, the Bay Area thrash band he had previously played with in 1999–2001.
