## About the Gretsch USA Custom

The USA Custom series was originally introduced in 1990 as Gretsch's top-tier professional offering, built on the legacy of the Silver Series and the classic Gretsch sound. Key features that make it popular with metal drummers:

- **Shell construction:** 7-ply maple/gum blend; the gum inner ply adds low-end focus and warmth without muddiness
- **Bearing edges:** 30-degree cut with a slight round-over — responsive to ghost notes and dynamic playing
- **Hoops:** Die-cast on the bass drum, triple-flanged on toms — balances articulation with rimshot power
- **Finish options:** Deep Cherry Burst, Satin Gold Lacquer, Ebony, and many custom wrap options
- **Price range:** $3,500–$6,000 (shell kit) depending on configuration

The USA Custom thrives in progressive, technical, and post-metal contexts where drummers need articulate attack and natural warmth rather than pure raw volume. Its smaller toms (up to 14") are favored by players building fusion-influenced poly-rhythmic setups like Garstka's.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Which metal drummers use the Gretsch USA Custom?**  
A: Matt Garstka of Animals as Leaders is the most prominent metal drummer associated with the Gretsch USA Custom. He has used the kit since joining Animals as Leaders in 2012, pairing it with Meinl Byzance cymbals for his polyrhythmic progressive metal style.

**Q: Is the Gretsch USA Custom good for metal drumming?**  
A: Yes — the USA Custom excels in progressive, technical, and avant-garde metal where dynamics, articulation, and sensitivity matter as much as raw power. Its maple/gum shell construction delivers focused attack with natural warmth, and the bearing edges respond well to everything from ghost notes to heavy rimshots. For death metal or grindcore that demands maximum cutting power and decay, players may prefer birch or all-maple kits from other brands.

**Q: What configuration does Matt Garstka use on the Gretsch USA Custom?**  
A: Garstka uses a compact setup — 22"×18" bass drum, 10"×7" and 12"×8" rack toms, and a 14"×12" floor tom. Smaller toms give faster response for his intricate linear patterns, and fewer drums reduce clutter while maximising the musical range he needs. He pairs the kit with a Pearl Matt Garstka Signature snare and Meinl Byzance Dual Hi-Hats.

**Q: How does the Gretsch USA Custom compare to Pearl or Tama pro kits?**  
A: The USA Custom is distinctly warmer-voiced than either the Pearl Reference Pure (which is snappier and more focused) or the Tama STAR Maple (which has more low-mid punch). Gretsch's maple/gum blend and 30-degree bearing edges produce a vintage-influenced tone that cuts differently in a mix — less high-frequency attack, more musical sustain. For progressive and fusion-influenced metal, many players prefer that character.
