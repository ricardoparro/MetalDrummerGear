# Pearl Reference Pure Drummers — Who Plays This Kit?

> MetalForge's guide to professional metal drummers who use the Pearl Reference Pure drum kit.
> Last updated: 2026-06-25

The Pearl Reference Pure is Pearl's flagship all-birch drum kit, designed to deliver maximum acoustic resonance with a focused, punchy attack suited to high-volume environments. Building on the hybrid-shell Reference series, the Reference Pure uses a pure birch shell construction that produces a brighter, faster-decaying tone than maple alternatives — ideal for metal drummers who prioritize cut and projection over warmth. The Reference Pure features Pearl's proprietary OptiMount system, which suspends the shells in a free-floating cradle for unrestricted resonance. This page profiles the metal drummers in MetalForge's database who play the Pearl Reference Pure.

### Nick Augusto — Trivium

Nick Augusto is best known as the drummer for Trivium from 2009 to 2014, during which he recorded two studio albums: In Waves (2011) and Vengeance Falls (2013). Originally hired as a drum technician for Trivium, Augusto stepped into the drummer role and quickly demonstrated his aggressive, high-speed playing style, which complemented Trivium's blend of melodic metal and thrash. The Pearl Reference Pure was his primary kit during the Trivium era, paired with Pearl's Demon Drive double pedal, a Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Brass snare, and Sabian AAX cymbals. His touring history with Trivium included support slots for major metal festivals and headline European and North American tours.

### Igor Cavalera — Sepultura / Cavalera Conspiracy

## Why Pro Metal Drummers Choose the Pearl Reference Pure

The Pearl Reference Pure stands apart from hybrid-shell kits through its single-wood birch construction. Key reasons metal drummers choose it:

- **Pure birch shells**: deliver a fast, punchy attack and bright high-end projection that cuts through dense guitar and bass mixes in live metal environments
- **OptiMount system**: Pearl's shell-suspension hardware eliminates lug contact with the shell, allowing the birch to resonate fully on every stroke
- **MasterCast hoops**: Pearl's proprietary die-cast hoops provide consistent rimshot tone and tuning stability under heavy playing
- **Size-specific bearing edges**: each shell diameter uses a bearing edge engineered for that size, maximizing resonance and attack characteristics
- **Heritage engineering**: the Reference Pure borrows Pearl's most advanced shell technology, originally developed for the hybrid Reference series, and applies it to an all-birch build

## FAQ

**Q: Which pro metal drummers use the Pearl Reference Pure?**
A: In MetalForge's database, Nick Augusto (Trivium) and Igor Cavalera (Sepultura, Cavalera Conspiracy) are among the documented players of the Pearl Reference Pure. Nick Augusto used this kit throughout his 2009–2014 tenure with Trivium, recording two studio albums on it. Igor Cavalera has incorporated the Reference Pure into his setup as part of his post-Sepultura career with the Cavalera Conspiracy.

**Q: Is the Pearl Reference Pure good for metal drumming?**
A: Yes — the Pearl Reference Pure is a strong choice for metal drumming, particularly for players who prioritize attack, projection, and fast shell response over the warmer tone of maple. Its pure birch shells deliver the bright, punchy sound that metal drummers need to cut through high-volume guitar rigs. Nick Augusto's Trivium-era playing demonstrated the kit's ability to handle the double-bass-heavy, technically demanding metalcore style at the professional level.

**Q: What is the difference between the Pearl Reference and the Pearl Reference Pure?**
A: The Pearl Reference (original) uses a hybrid shell construction combining birch, maple, and mahogany in different layers, designed to balance warmth and attack. The Pearl Reference Pure uses a single-species all-birch construction, emphasizing faster attack, brighter projection, and a tighter, more focused sound. For metal drummers who prefer cut over warmth, the Reference Pure is generally the more appropriate choice.
