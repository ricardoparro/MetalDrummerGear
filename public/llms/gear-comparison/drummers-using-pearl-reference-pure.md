# Pearl Reference Pure Drummers — Who Plays This Kit?

> MetalForge's curated guide to metal drummers who use the Pearl Reference Pure kit.
> Last updated: 2026-06-25

The Pearl Reference Pure is Pearl's top-tier acoustic drum shell — a stripped-back evolution of the original Reference Series that removes inner reinforcement rings to let the shell vibrate completely freely. The result is an exceptionally resonant, naturally sustaining drum with a rich fundamental tone. Metal drummers choose the Reference Pure for its ability to cut through dense guitar mixes while retaining musical warmth across the full dynamic range, from ghost notes to full-force hits.

## Why Metal Drummers Choose the Pearl Reference Pure

The Reference Pure differs from the standard Reference Series by removing the inner reinforcement rings — a structural element that stabilizes the shell but also dampens resonance. Without these rings, the shell can vibrate as a single uninterrupted unit, producing a fuller, longer-sustaining tone with a stronger fundamental pitch. This design philosophy prioritizes acoustic transparency over structural reinforcement.

For metal drummers, the benefit is a kit that sounds inherently musical even under aggressive attack. Fast, hard playing doesn't cause the shell to "choke" — instead, each hit speaks clearly and sustains naturally until the next stroke. Pearl's 10-ply maple/birch blend (also available in all-maple) adds warmth to the inherently bright Reference Pure design, giving players a tonally rich kit that handles equally well in the studio and on stage.

The Reference Pure's OptiMount system suspends each tom from a single point of contact, further maximizing free shell resonance and giving the kit a noticeably open, "studio" sound even in live environments.

## FAQ

**Q: Which metal drummers use the Pearl Reference Pure?**
A: Two prominent metal drummers in our database play the Pearl Reference Pure: Nick Augusto (Trivium, 2010–2014) and Igor Cavalera (Sepultura, Cavalera Conspiracy). Both have used the kit for demanding live and studio work in thrash and extreme metal contexts.

**Q: What is the difference between the Pearl Reference and the Pearl Reference Pure?**
A: The Pearl Reference Pure removes the inner reinforcement rings found in the standard Reference Series. These rings stabilize the shell but restrict vibration. Without them, the Reference Pure shell vibrates as a single uninterrupted unit, producing a fuller, longer-sustaining tone. For drummers who prioritize acoustic transparency and natural resonance over maximum shell stability, the Reference Pure is the preferred choice.

**Q: Is the Pearl Reference Pure good for recording metal drums?**
A: Yes — the Pearl Reference Pure's free-vibrating shell design gives it a naturally musical tone that responds well to microphone placement. Studio engineers appreciate the consistent fundamental pitch and extended sustain, which provide a solid foundation for close-micing techniques. Nick Augusto's work on Trivium's "In Waves" and "Vengeance Falls" demonstrates the Reference Pure's effectiveness in modern metal production, capturing both the snappy attack metalcore requires and the warmth that makes drum tracks sit well in a dense mix.
