## Head-to-Head: Tone, Build, Price

**Tone & Sound:** Sabian HHX blends hand-hammered warmth with enough attack to cut through dense riffing, while Meinl Byzance leans darker and more complex, favoring wash and overtone depth over immediate cut. Both respond well to the compressed, high-gain mixes common in modern metal production.

**Build & Durability:** Both are professional-grade B20 bronze cymbals built for touring. Sabian's AAX line is especially resistant to heavy, sustained hitting, while Meinl's Byzance line holds its hand-hammered profile well provided it isn't over-crashed.

**Value:** Sabian offers a broader price range from entry AA to pro HHX, giving metal drummers more tiers to grow into. Meinl Byzance sits at a consistent premium across its whole line, so there's less room to start cheap and upgrade piece by piece.

**For Metal Drumming:** Sabian dominates djent and groove metal thanks to signature innovations like Compression Hi-Hats. Meinl Byzance is the go-to for progressive and modern metal drummers chasing dark, complex tone that still translates cleanly in the studio.

**Verdict:** Sabian HHX/AAX is the versatile, value-driven choice for groove and djent drummers who need a full range of tones across one series. Meinl Byzance is worth the premium for progressive metal players who prioritize dark, studio-ready complexity over raw cut.
