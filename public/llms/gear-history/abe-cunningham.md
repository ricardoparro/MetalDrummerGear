## Gear Timeline

### White Pony Era (2000)

- **Drums:** Pearl Custom Series — 22" bass drum, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms, maple shells
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Series — 14" K hi-hats, 16"/18" K crashes, 20" K ride
- **Pedal:** Pearl Eliminator double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Clear (toms), Remo Ambassador Coated (snare)
- **Original setup cost (2000):** ~$7,772
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$14,786
- **Notable:** Recorded on "White Pony," Deftones' critical breakthrough and first Grammy Award win (Best Metal Performance, "Elite," 2001). Cunningham switched from ddrum to Pearl Custom and from Zildjian A Custom to K Series specifically to chase the warmer, more atmospheric tone the album's dynamics demanded.

### Diamond Eyes Era (2010)

- **Notable:** Rebuilt his setup around a Gretsch USA Custom kit following the trauma of bassist Chi Cheng's 2008 accident. "Diamond Eyes" was written and recorded rapidly as a tribute to Chi, and Cunningham's groove-forward playing reached full maturity on this record.

### Gore / Ohms Era (2016–2020)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series — maple/birch hybrid shells
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom Dark Series
- **Notable:** Returned to Pearl with the flagship Reference Series for "Gore" (2016) and "Ohms" (2020), adding a Roland SPD-SX sampling pad for live atmospheric textures. Ohms is widely praised for its restraint and cinematic scope.

### Current Era

- **Drums:** SJC Custom Drums — bespoke 5-piece shell pack
- **Snare:** SJC Custom 14"x6.5" Brass
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom & A Custom Series (14" K Custom hi-hats, 18"/20" K Custom crashes, 22" K Custom ride, 19" A Custom China)
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Series Double Pedal, DW Hardware
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 2B
- **Heads:** Remo
- **Notable:** Cunningham's current verified rig is a fully custom SJC Custom shell pack paired with Zildjian K Custom/A Custom cymbals — a boutique, artist-specific step up from the catalog Pearl gear he played on White Pony.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1988:** Co-founds Deftones in Sacramento at age 15
- **2000:** Switches from ddrum to Pearl Custom and Zildjian A Custom to K Series for "White Pony"
- **2010:** Moves to Gretsch USA Custom for "Diamond Eyes," recorded in tribute to Chi Cheng
- **2012–2020:** Returns to Pearl with the flagship Reference Series for Koi No Yokan, Gore, and Ohms
- **Current:** Moves to a bespoke SJC Custom shell pack with Zildjian K Custom/A Custom cymbals

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Abe Cunningham's drum kit cost?**  
A: Abe Cunningham's original 2000 Pearl Custom/Zildjian K setup — the rig behind Deftones' Grammy-winning "White Pony" — cost approximately $7,772. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $14,786. His current bespoke SJC Custom Drums/Zildjian K Custom setup carries a comparable modern equivalent cost, reflecting the jump from catalog gear to a fully custom boutique kit.

**Q: What brand of drums does Abe Cunningham use?**  
A: Abe Cunningham currently plays a bespoke SJC Custom Drums shell pack with Zildjian K Custom and A Custom cymbals, Vic Firth American Classic 2B sticks, and Remo heads. Earlier in his career he played Pearl Custom (White Pony era), Gretsch USA Custom (Diamond Eyes era), and Pearl Reference (Koi No Yokan/Gore/Ohms era).

**Q: Is Abe Cunningham's drum setup affordable?**  
A: Cunningham's White Pony-era Pearl Custom setup (~$7,772 in 2000) was a mid-tier professional configuration, not a signature-artist rig — accessible to any working drummer with a semi-pro budget. His current bespoke SJC Custom kit costs meaningfully more, reflecting the premium of a fully custom, boutique-built shell pack.

**Q: What drums did Abe Cunningham use on White Pony?**  
A: Abe Cunningham played a Pearl Custom Series 5-piece maple kit with a Pearl Free-Floating brass snare, Zildjian K Series cymbals, a Pearl Eliminator double pedal, and Vic Firth American Classic 5A sticks on Deftones' "White Pony" (2000).
