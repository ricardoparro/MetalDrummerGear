## Gear Timeline

### Founding / Early Deftones Era (1988–1994)

- **Drums:** ddrum Dios Series — maple shells, 22" kick, 12"/13"/16" toms
- **Snare:** ddrum Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Hardware:** Mixed budget stands, single bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador (clear toms, coated snare)
- **Notable:** Cunningham co-founded Deftones in Sacramento in 1988 with Chino Moreno, Stephen Carpenter, and Chi Cheng while still in high school. No major endorsement deal existed yet — the ddrum kit was durable, punchy, and affordable for a teenage band playing every weekend around Sacramento.

### Adrenaline / Around the Fur Era (1995–1999)

- **Drums:** ddrum Dios Series (expanded) — 22" kick, 10"/12"/13"/16" toms
- **Snare:** ddrum Steel 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 21" ride
- **Hardware:** DW 5000 double pedal added
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor
- **Notable:** Adrenaline (1995) marked Deftones' major label debut, and Around the Fur (1997) broke the band into the mainstream during the nu-metal boom. Cunningham added a double kick pedal and upgraded to brighter A Custom cymbals for the heavier material, while Ozzfest 1998 exposed the band to massive new audiences.

### White Pony Era (2000–2003)

- **Drums:** Pearl Custom Series — maple shells, 22"x18" kick, 10"x8"/12"x10"/14"x12" rack toms, 16"x16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Hardware:** Pearl Eliminator double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Coated Ambassador (snare)
- **Original setup cost (2000):** ~$7,898
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$15,025
- **Notable:** White Pony (2000) is widely regarded as Deftones' artistic masterpiece, winning the Grammy Award for Best Metal Performance ("Elite") in 2001. Cunningham switched from ddrum to Pearl Custom and from the brighter Zildjian A Custom to the darker K Series — a deliberate shift toward warmth and dynamic range that let tracks like "Digital Bath" and "Teenager" breathe. This is the most searched and most historically cited setup of his career.

### Saturday Night Wrist / Diamond Eyes Era (2003–2012)

- **Drums:** Gretsch USA Custom — maple shells, 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Gretsch USA Custom 14"x6.5" maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 21" ride
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Powerstroke 3 (kick)
- **Notable:** Following the grief of Chi Cheng's November 2008 accident, Cunningham rebuilt his rig around a warmer, more organic-sounding Gretsch USA Custom kit. Diamond Eyes (2010) — written and recorded rapidly as a tribute to Chi — showcased Cunningham at his most focused and groove-driven.

### Koi No Yokan / Gore / Ohms Era (2012–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series — maple/birch hybrid, 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom Dark Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), EMAD (kick)
- **Electronics:** Roland SPD-SX sampling pad
- **Notable:** Cunningham returned to Pearl with the flagship Reference Series for Koi No Yokan (2012), Gore (2016), and Ohms (2020) — the band's most compositionally sophisticated modern work. His K Custom Dark cymbals are the darkest, most complex tonal palette of his career, and the Roland SPD-SX lets him replicate Deftones' layered studio textures live.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1988:** Deftones founded in Sacramento; ddrum becomes Cunningham's early signature brand
- **2000:** Switched from ddrum to Pearl Custom Series and from Zildjian A Custom to K Series for White Pony
- **2003:** Moved to Gretsch USA Custom for a warmer, more organic tone during the Saturday Night Wrist and Diamond Eyes years
- **2012:** Returned to Pearl with the flagship Reference Series for Koi No Yokan, carried through Gore and Ohms
- **Cymbal arc:** Zildjian A → A Custom → K → K Custom → K Custom Dark, tracking a decades-long pursuit of darker, more complex tones

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Abe Cunningham use?**  
A: Abe Cunningham currently plays a Pearl Reference Series kit — a maple/birch hybrid configured with a 22" kick and 10", 12", 14", and 16" toms — paired with Zildjian K Custom Dark cymbals. He returned to Pearl for the Koi No Yokan era after using a Gretsch USA Custom during the Diamond Eyes years and Pearl Custom during White Pony.

**Q: What drums did Abe Cunningham use on White Pony?**  
A: On White Pony (2000), Cunningham played a Pearl Custom Series maple shell pack with a Pearl Free-Floating Brass snare and Zildjian K Series cymbals — switching from the ddrum kit and brighter Zildjian A Custom cymbals he'd used on Around the Fur. The complete setup cost approximately $7,898 in 2000, equivalent to roughly $15,025 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: How much does Abe Cunningham's drum kit cost?**  
A: Cunningham's White Pony-era rig cost approximately $7,898 in 2000 dollars. Adjusted for inflation to 2026 dollars, that's roughly $15,025 — reflecting both general CPI inflation and the growing collector demand for vintage Pearl Custom shells from alternative metal's turn-of-the-millennium peak.

**Q: What cymbals does Abe Cunningham use?**  
A: Cunningham plays Zildjian K Custom Dark cymbals — the darkest series in the Zildjian range. His progression ran from Zildjian A during the founding years, through A Custom for Around the Fur, K Series for White Pony, K Custom for Diamond Eyes, and finally K Custom Dark for the modern Koi No Yokan/Gore/Ohms era.

**Q: When did Abe Cunningham join Deftones?**  
A: Abe Cunningham is a founding member of Deftones — he co-founded the band in Sacramento, California in 1988 with Chino Moreno, Stephen Carpenter, and the late Chi Cheng. He has appeared on every Deftones studio album from Adrenaline (1995) through Ohms (2020).
