## Gear Timeline

### Winds of Plague / Prong Era (2008–2018)

- **Drums:** Entry-level maple/birch kits — various developing-years setups, 22" kick
- **Snare:** Steel shell 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Various brands (pre-Zildjian) — no fixed endorsement yet
- **Pedal:** Standard double pedal, mixed stands
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B hickory — the one constant across his entire career
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador / Pinstripe
- **Estimated kit cost:** ~$2,200
- **Notable:** Cruz spent a decade in the deathcore and industrial-metal trenches — Winds of Plague (The Great Stone War, Against the World) and, in parallel, Tommy Victor's Prong (Zero Days) — building the touring stamina and double-bass endurance that later defined his groove metal approach.

### Lamb of God Fill-In Era (2018–2019)

- **Drums:** Transitional Ludwig setup — early configuration ahead of permanent endorsement
- **Snare:** Ludwig 14"x6.5" (early) — maple or brass shell
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — hi-hats, crashes, ride established during fill-in dates
- **Pedal:** Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot (early adoption) — direct-drive double pedal
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Coated / EMAD2
- **Notable:** When Chris Adler stepped back from touring in 2018, Cruz filled in on Lamb of God's North American support dates for Slayer's farewell run, learning Adler's intricate catalog in weeks. The fill-in role became permanent in 2019 — and his Zildjian and Trick endorsements both began during this transition.

### Lamb of God (Self-Titled) Era (2019–Present)

- **Drums:** Ludwig Classic Maple — 22"x18" kick, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Ludwig 14"x6.5" (maple or brass), medium-high tuning
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom Series — 14" hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China, 10" splash
- **Pedal:** Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot Double Pedal, Gibraltar stands and throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B / Power 5B
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Coated / EMAD2
- **Original setup cost (2020):** ~$7,500
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$9,494
- **Notable:** Cruz's settled rig as Lamb of God's full-time drummer, anchoring the band's self-titled 2020 album and Omens (2022). Unlike predecessor Chris Adler's double-kick configuration, Cruz drives his patterns through a single bass drum with a direct-drive Trick pedal — a deliberate technical choice, not a compromise.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2008–2018:** No fixed gear endorsements — developing preferences across Winds of Plague and Prong
- **~2018:** No endorsement → Zildjian A Custom + Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot — established during his Lamb of God fill-in dates on the Slayer farewell tour
- **~2019:** Transitional Ludwig setup → full Ludwig Classic Maple — settled kit once he became a permanent Lamb of God member
- **Career-long:** Vic Firth 5B sticks remained a constant from Winds of Plague through Lamb of God

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Art Cruz's drum kit cost?**  
A: Cruz's 2020 Lamb of God setup — Ludwig Classic Maple kit, matching snare, Zildjian A Custom cymbals, Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot pedal, sticks, and heads — cost approximately $7,500. Adjusted for inflation to 2026, that's roughly $9,494.

**Q: What gear does Art Cruz use?**  
A: Art Cruz plays a Ludwig Classic Maple kit, a full Zildjian A Custom Series cymbal setup (hi-hats, multiple crashes, ride, China, and splash), a Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot direct-drive double pedal, Vic Firth 5B sticks, and Evans G2/EMAD2 heads.

**Q: What drum kit does Art Cruz play with Lamb of God?**  
A: Cruz's Lamb of God kit is a Ludwig Classic Maple — a 22"x18" bass drum with 10"/12" rack toms and 14"/16" floor toms — chosen for its warm, punchy tone that anchors the band's down-tuned groove metal riffs.

**Q: Who replaced Chris Adler in Lamb of God, and did the gear change?**  
A: Art Cruz replaced Chris Adler, first filling in on the 2018 Slayer farewell tour before becoming permanent in 2019. Unlike Adler's double-kick setup, Cruz plays a single bass drum driven by a direct-drive Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot pedal — a deliberate technical difference, not an equipment downgrade.

**Q: What bands did Art Cruz play in before Lamb of God?**  
A: Before Lamb of God, Cruz spent roughly a decade in the deathcore and industrial-metal underground — drumming for Winds of Plague (The Great Stone War, Against the World) and Tommy Victor's Prong (Zero Days) — without a fixed gear endorsement.
