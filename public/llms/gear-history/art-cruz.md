## Gear Timeline

### Winds of Plague / Prong Era (2008–2018)

- **Drums:** Entry-level maple/birch kits, no fixed endorsement
- **Snare:** Steel shell 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Various brands, pre-Zildjian
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador / Pinstripe
- **Notable:** Before Lamb of God, Cruz spent a decade in the deathcore and industrial-metal underground — drumming for Winds of Plague ("The Great Stone War," "Against the World") and Tommy Victor's Prong ("Songs from the Black Hole," "Zero Days"), building the touring stamina and double-bass endurance that would later define his groove metal approach.

### Lamb of God Fill-In Era (2018–2019)

- **Drums:** Transitional Ludwig setup
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — endorsement begins
- **Pedal:** Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot — early adoption
- **Notable:** When Chris Adler stepped back from touring in 2018, Cruz filled in on Lamb of God's North American tour supporting Slayer's farewell run, learning Adler's intricate catalog in weeks. The fill-in role became permanent in 2019.

### Lamb of God Debut Era (2019–present)

- **Drums:** Ludwig Classic Maple — 22"x18" bass drum, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Ludwig 14"x6.5" (maple/brass)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom Series — 14" hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China, 10" splash
- **Pedal:** Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot double pedal, direct-drive, single bass drum
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B / Power 5B
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Coated / EMAD2
- **Original setup cost (2020):** ~$7,500
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$9,494
- **Notable:** Cruz's studio debut as Lamb of God's full-time drummer was the self-titled "Lamb of God" (2020), followed by "Omens" (2022). His settled Ludwig/Zildjian/Trick rig — direct-drive pedal on a single bass drum rather than Adler's double-kick setup — has become his signature setup across both albums.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2018:** Fills in for Chris Adler on Lamb of God's Slayer farewell support dates; Zildjian and Trick endorsements begin
- **2019:** Fill-in role becomes permanent Lamb of God position
- **2020:** Records self-titled "Lamb of God" debut on Ludwig Classic Maple / Zildjian A Custom rig
- **2022:** "Omens" cements the Ludwig/Zildjian/Trick setup as his signature rig
- **Career constant:** Vic Firth 5B stick preference carried from his Winds of Plague/Prong years through to today

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Art Cruz's drum kit cost?**  
A: Art Cruz's 2020 Ludwig Classic Maple and Zildjian A Custom setup — the rig behind his debut self-titled "Lamb of God" album — cost approximately $7,500. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $9,494. He has carried the same setup through "Omens" (2022) as his signature rig.

**Q: What gear does Art Cruz use?**  
A: Art Cruz plays a Ludwig Classic Maple kit with a Ludwig 14"x6.5" snare, a full Zildjian A Custom cymbal set, a Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot direct-drive double pedal on a single bass drum, and Vic Firth 5B/Power 5B sticks.

**Q: Who did Art Cruz replace in Lamb of God?**  
A: Art Cruz replaced Chris Adler as Lamb of God's drummer, first filling in on the band's 2018 Slayer farewell support tour before the role became permanent in 2019.

**Q: What bands was Art Cruz in before Lamb of God?**  
A: Before Lamb of God, Art Cruz spent roughly a decade drumming for deathcore band Winds of Plague and for Tommy Victor's industrial-metal band Prong.
