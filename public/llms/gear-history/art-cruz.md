## Gear Timeline

### Lamb of God (Self-Titled) Era (2019–present, Lamb of God)

- **Drums:** Ludwig Classic Maple — 22"x18" bass drum, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Ludwig 14"x6.5" (maple or brass), medium-high tuning
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom Series — 14" hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China, 10" splash
- **Pedals:** Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot double pedal (single kick), Gibraltar stands, Roc-N-Soc Nitro Original throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B / Power 5B
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Coated (toms), Evans EMAD2 (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2020):** ~$9,250
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$11,709
- **Notable:** Cruz became Lamb of God's full-time drummer in 2019 after filling in on the band's 2018 Slayer farewell support dates, learning Chris Adler's intricate catalog on short notice. His studio debut, the self-titled Lamb of God (2020), established the Ludwig/Zildjian/Trick rig that remains his signature setup — the Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot's direct-drive action driving a single kick drum rather than Adler's double-kick configuration.

### Winds of Plague / Prong Era (2008–2018)

- **Drums:** Entry-level maple/birch kits — various developing-years setups, 22" kick
- **Snare:** Steel shell 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Various brands (pre-Zildjian)
- **Pedals:** Standard double pedal, mixed stands
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador / Pinstripe
- **Notable:** Before Lamb of God, Cruz spent roughly a decade in the deathcore and industrial-metal underground, powering Winds of Plague's symphonic deathcore assault and running a parallel stint in Tommy Victor's Prong. No fixed gear endorsements yet — this period built the touring stamina and double-bass endurance that defined his later groove metal approach.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Art Cruz use?**  
A: Art Cruz plays a Ludwig Classic Maple kit — a 22"x18" bass drum with 10"/12" rack toms and 14"/16" floor toms — paired with a Ludwig 14"x6.5" snare, a full Zildjian A Custom cymbal setup, and a Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot direct-drive double pedal on a single bass drum.

**Q: How much does Art Cruz's kit cost?**  
A: The Ludwig/Zildjian/Trick rig Cruz established for Lamb of God's self-titled 2020 album cost approximately $9,250 at the time, equivalent to roughly $11,709 in inflation-adjusted 2026 dollars. That same setup remains his current rig.

**Q: Who replaced Chris Adler in Lamb of God?**  
A: Art Cruz replaced Chris Adler as Lamb of God's drummer. Cruz first filled in for Adler on the band's 2018 North American tour supporting Slayer's farewell run, and the role became permanent in 2019. His studio debut with the band was the self-titled Lamb of God (2020).

**Q: How does Art Cruz's setup differ from Chris Adler's?**  
A: Cruz drives his double-bass patterns through a Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot direct-drive pedal on a single kick drum, rather than the double-kick configuration his predecessor Chris Adler used. This deliberate departure let him bring his own footwork identity to the seat while learning Adler's legacy catalog with respect.
