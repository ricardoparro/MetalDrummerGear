## Gear Timeline

### Early Hardcore / DIY Era (1990–2000)

- **Drums:** Secondhand Pearl/Tama kits — 20"–22" kick, standard tom configuration
- **Snare:** Pearl Steel 14"x5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian ZBT / Sabian B8 Series — entry-level brass
- **Pedal:** Pearl P-100 basic double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Original setup cost (1990s):** ~$1,500
- **Notable:** Koller co-founded Converge in Salem, Massachusetts in 1990 as a high-school hardcore band, playing whatever secondhand gear was available through Petitioning the Empty Sky (1996) and When Forever Comes Crashing (1998).

### Jane Doe Era (2001–2003)

- **Drums:** Tama Imperialstar / Rockstar Series — 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/17" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra double pedal
- **Original setup cost (2001):** ~$4,500
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$8,324
- **Notable:** Jane Doe (2001, Hydra Head Records) is one of the defining albums of metallic hardcore. Koller's first consistent endorsement-adjacent platform, the Tama Imperialstar, gave the durable, mid-forward tone Kurt Ballou's dense production demanded.

### You Fail Me Era (2004–2008)

- **Drums:** Tama Imperialstar / Rockstar Series (continued)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Powerstroke 3 (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2004):** ~$5,200
- **Notable:** The kit barely changed for You Fail Me (2004) and No Heroes (2006) — both records' deliberately stripped, direct production reflected restraint in gear as well as composition.

### Axe to Fall Era (2009–2016)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple — 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Custom Steel/Aluminium 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom / K Custom mix — 14" hi-hats, 16"/17"/18" crashes, 21" ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra 900 double pedal
- **Original setup cost (2009):** ~$12,000
- **Notable:** Axe to Fall (2009) — Converge's Epitaph debut, featuring guest performances from members of Neurosis, Genghis Tron, Disfear, and Nails — was their most ambitious production to date, demanding a refined, more versatile setup.

### The Dusk in Us Era (2017–present)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple (matured platform)
- **Heads:** Evans G2 (toms), EMAD (kick)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B / Custom — heavier hickory preference, maintained career-long
- **Original setup cost (2017):** ~$14,000
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$18,718
- **Notable:** The Dusk in Us (2017) and Bloodmoon: I (2021, with Chelsea Wolfe) cement the Tama Starclassic Maple as Koller's most refined modern platform — balancing the raw aggression of his early years with the compositional sophistication of latter-day Converge.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2001:** First consistent endorsement-adjacent kit — Tama Imperialstar/Rockstar replaces secondhand DIY gear for Jane Doe
- **2009:** Upgrade to Tama Starclassic Maple for Axe to Fall's increased complexity
- **2017:** Expanded Zildjian A Custom/K Custom cymbal setup; Evans heads replace Remo
- **Career constant:** Heavier Vic Firth 5B stick preference, maintained from his earliest DIY-era kits through his current Tama Starclassic rig

## FAQ

**Q: What is Ben Koller's drum setup worth?**  
A: Ben Koller's original 2001 Tama Imperialstar setup — the kit behind Converge's Jane Doe — cost approximately $4,500. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $8,324. His current Tama Starclassic Maple rig, assembled around The Dusk in Us (2017), cost approximately $14,000, equivalent to roughly $18,718 today.

**Q: What drums does Ben Koller use?**  
A: Ben Koller has been a Tama player since Converge's Jane Doe (2001) breakthrough, progressing from the Tama Imperialstar/Rockstar Series through You Fail Me and Axe to Fall, to the Tama Starclassic Maple platform he has used since around 2009, refined through The Dusk in Us (2017) and Bloodmoon: I (2021).

**Q: What cymbals does Ben Koller use?**  
A: Koller moved from budget Zildjian ZBT/Sabian B8 cymbals in Converge's earliest DIY years to Zildjian A Series for Jane Doe (2001), then to an expanded Zildjian A Custom/K Custom mix for his modern setup — chosen for a wider tonal range across Converge's increasingly dynamic compositions.

**Q: How long has Ben Koller played in Converge?**  
A: Ben Koller co-founded Converge in Salem, Massachusetts in 1990 and has remained the band's drummer for over three decades, playing on every album from Petitioning the Empty Sky (1996) through Bloodmoon: I (2021) and beyond.
