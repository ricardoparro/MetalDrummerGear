## Gear Timeline

### Self-Titled / Silent Circus Era (1999–2006)

- **Drums:** Tama Rockstar Series — poplar shells, entry-level; 22" bass drum, 12"/13"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian B8 / AAX Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador
- **Notable:** Between the Buried and Me formed in Winston-Salem, NC in 1999. Richardson's early setup was assembled on a limited budget during the band's self-booked DIY touring years, building the technical foundation behind the self-titled debut (2002) and The Silent Circus (2003).

### Colors Era (2007–2009)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch — hybrid shells; 22" bass drum, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Maple 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" Stage crashes, 21" ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor
- **Original setup cost (2007):** ~$7,000
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$11,062
- **Notable:** "Colors" (2007) is BTBAM's defining album — a single 65-minute progressive metal suite widely considered one of the genre's greatest achievements. Richardson upgraded to the Starclassic Walnut/Birch for the warm, complex tone the album's genre-fluid arrangements demanded, swinging from jazz passages to full metal brutality within the same track.

### The Great Misdirect / Parallax Era (2009–2014)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch (expanded), added 8" tom
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series — switched from Sabian for a darker, more complex Turkish B20 alloy tone
- **Notable:** The Great Misdirect (2009) and the Parallax concept albums (2011–2012) pushed BTBAM's progressive ambitions further. "The Parallax II: Future Sequence" (2012) is considered the band's most technically demanding release to date.

### Current Era — Tama Star Walnut

- **Drums:** Tama Star Walnut — all-walnut shells, premium flagship kit; 22" bass drum, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Star Walnut Snare 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Jazz / Dark Series
- **Pedal:** Tama Speed Cobra HP910 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Blake Richardson Signature
- **Electronics:** Roland SPD-SX Sampling Pad
- **Notable:** Richardson's current setup, powering Coma Ecliptic (2015), Automata I & II (2018), and Colors II (2021), is the most refined of his career — the all-walnut Star Walnut shells deliver the warmest, most musically complex tone in the Tama catalog.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2007:** Upgrades to Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch for "Colors" — BTBAM's defining progressive metal album
- **2009–2014:** Switches from Sabian to Meinl Byzance cymbals during the Parallax era
- **2015–present:** Upgrades to Tama Star Walnut flagship kit; launches Vic Firth signature stick model
- **Career constant:** Genre-fluid, dynamically extreme playing — spanning metal, jazz, and prog — across every era
