## Gear Timeline

### Early Career / Alaska Era (1999–2006)

- **Drums:** Tama Rockstar Series — poplar shells; 22" kick, 12"/13"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian B8 / AAX Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A Hickory (off-the-shelf, pre-endorsement)
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador (clear toms, coated snare batter)
- **Estimated kit cost (1999–2006):** ~$3,000
- **Notable:** Richardson co-founded Between the Buried and Me in Winston-Salem, NC in 1999. His entry-level Tama/Sabian rig was chosen for reliability across relentless DIY touring, not tone — but Alaska (2005) already drew serious critical attention despite the modest gear.

### Colors Era (2007–2009)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch — walnut/birch hybrid shells; 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Maple 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series (expanded) — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" Stage crashes, 21" ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B (upgrade for touring projection)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor double-ply
- **Original setup cost (2007):** ~$7,000
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$11,062
- **Notable:** Colors (2007) is widely considered BTBAM's masterpiece — a single 65-minute continuous track moving between extreme metal, jazz, progressive rock, and acoustic passages. Richardson's Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch upgrade gave him the warmer, more resonant tone the album's extraordinary dynamic range demanded.

### The Great Misdirect / Parallax Era (2009–2014)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch (expanded) — added an 8" tom for higher tonal range
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Maple 14"x6.5" (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 Double Pedal (continued)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor on toms, Powerstroke 3 on kick
- **Notable:** The most significant cymbal transition of Richardson's career — switching from Sabian to Meinl Byzance around The Great Misdirect (2009). The darker, more complex Turkish B20 alloy suited the increasingly sophisticated Parallax arrangements and remains his cymbal identity today.

### Coma Ecliptic / Automata / Colors II Era (2015–Present)

- **Drums:** Tama Star Walnut — all-walnut flagship shells; 22" kick, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Star Walnut 14"x6.5" (matching walnut snare)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Jazz / Dark — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 22" ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Speed Cobra HP910 Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Blake Richardson Signature
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor on toms, EMAD on kick
- **Electronics:** Roland SPD-SX sampling pad
- **Notable:** The most refined version of his setup across a 25-year career. Tama's premium all-walnut Star Walnut kit, paired with Meinl Byzance Jazz/Dark cymbals and a Vic Firth signature stick, powers Coma Ecliptic (2015), Automata I & II (2018), and Colors II (2021).

## Key Gear Changes

- **1999–2007:** Tama Rockstar entry-level kit — the budget rig that carried BTBAM through relentless DIY touring and Alaska (2005)
- **~2007:** Tama Rockstar → Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch — warmer, more resonant tone for Colors's extraordinary dynamic range
- **~2009:** Sabian AAX → Meinl Byzance — the defining cymbal switch of his career, adopted for The Great Misdirect / Parallax era
- **~2015:** Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch → Tama Star Walnut — premium all-walnut flagship for the modern BTBAM era
- **Career-long:** Vic Firth sticks remained a constant throughout, progressing from off-the-shelf 5A/5B models to a signature stick

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Blake Richardson's drum kit cost?**  
A: Richardson's 2007 Colors-era setup — Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch kit, matching snare, Sabian AAX cymbals, Iron Cobra pedal, sticks, and heads — cost approximately $7,000. Adjusted for inflation to 2026, that's roughly $11,062. His current Tama Star Walnut / Meinl Byzance rig, being a premium flagship configuration, costs meaningfully more new — around $22,000 by recent estimates.

**Q: What gear does Blake Richardson use?**  
A: Blake Richardson currently plays a Tama Star Walnut kit — Tama's premium all-walnut flagship series — paired with Meinl Byzance Jazz and Dark cymbals, a Tama Speed Cobra HP910 double pedal, and his own Vic Firth Blake Richardson Signature sticks.

**Q: What drum kit did Blake Richardson use on Colors?**  
A: On Colors (2007), Richardson played a Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch — a walnut/birch hybrid shell kit with a 22" kick and 10"/12"/14"/16" toms — paired with an expanded Sabian AAX cymbal setup. This upgrade from his earlier Tama Rockstar gave him the warm, resonant tone the album's genre-spanning arrangements required.

**Q: Why did Blake Richardson switch from Sabian to Meinl Byzance cymbals?**  
A: Richardson switched to Meinl Byzance around The Great Misdirect (2009) for the darker, more complex tone of the Turkish B20 alloy, which suited BTBAM's increasingly sophisticated Parallax-era arrangements better than Sabian's brighter AAX voice. He has remained a Meinl artist ever since.

**Q: Is Blake Richardson a founding member of Between the Buried and Me?**  
A: Yes — Richardson co-founded BTBAM in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, in 1999 and has appeared on every studio album from the self-titled debut (2002) through Colors II (2021).
