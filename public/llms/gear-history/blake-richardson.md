## Gear Timeline

### Colors Era (2005–2009, Between the Buried and Me)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series — North American Hard Rock Maple; 22"x18" bass drum, 10"/12"/13" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series Maple Snare 14"x6.5", medium tension tuning
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Extra Dry Series — 14" Extra Dry hi-hats, 17"/18" Extra Dry thin crashes, 20" Extra Dry ride, 18" China
- **Pedals:** DW 9002 double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick), Remo Ambassador Coated (toms/snare)
- **Original setup cost (2007):** ~$8,650
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$13,670
- **Notable:** Richardson's DW/Meinl foundation, established two years earlier on Alaska (2005), reached full maturity on Colors — widely considered progressive metal's gold standard. 'Prequel to the Sequel,' the album's 7/8-groove centerpiece, remains one of the most-studied drum performances in the genre; the Meinl Byzance Extra Dry cymbals' minimal wash kept its complex metric modulation clearly articulated through a 65-minute single-arc composition.

### Current Setup (Pearl Reference Pure era)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series Maple Snare
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Dark/Extra Dry dual configuration — 14" Dark hi-hats, 17" Extra Dry thin crash, 18" Dark crash, 20" Extra Dry ride, 10" Dark splash, 18" China
- **Pedals:** DW 9002 double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated / Ambassador Coated
- **Notable:** Richardson's core DW/Meinl foundation from the Colors era carried through The Great Misdirect (2009) and The Parallax II: Future Sequence (2012) largely unchanged. His current rig expands the cymbal voice into a Dark/Extra Dry dual configuration for maximum tonal complexity while keeping the same DW 9002 pedal and Vic Firth 5B sticks that drove his breakout performances.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1999:** Between the Buried and Me founded in Winston-Salem, NC
- **2005:** Alaska recorded — DW Collector's Series and Meinl Byzance Extra Dry rig established as BTBAM's breakthrough foundation
- **2007:** Colors recorded — DW/Meinl rig refined into progressive metal's most-studied performance ('Prequel to the Sequel')
- **2009:** The Great Misdirect — DW/Meinl foundation carries into an even more ambitious concept record
- **2012:** The Parallax II: Future Sequence — band's most technically demanding release
- **Career-long constant:** DW Collector's Series shells and Meinl Byzance cymbals as the tonal foundation across two decades

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Blake Richardson use?**  
A: Blake Richardson currently plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit with a DW Collector's Series maple snare, a Meinl Byzance Dark/Extra Dry cymbal setup, and a DW 9002 double bass pedal. On Between the Buried and Me's 2007 landmark Colors, he played a DW Collector's Series kit with Meinl Byzance Extra Dry cymbals — that original setup cost approximately $8,650 in 2007, equivalent to about $13,670 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: How much does Blake Richardson's kit cost?**  
A: Richardson's Colors-era (2007) DW Collector's Series/Meinl Byzance Extra Dry rig cost approximately $8,650 at the time, or roughly $13,670 in inflation-adjusted 2026 dollars. His current Pearl Reference Pure/Meinl Byzance Dark-Extra Dry setup is valued at approximately $8,960.

**Q: What cymbals did Blake Richardson use on Colors?**  
A: Richardson used a full Meinl Byzance Extra Dry setup on Colors — 14" Extra Dry hi-hats, 17"/18" Extra Dry thin crashes, a 20" Extra Dry ride, and an 18" China. The Extra Dry series' minimal wash and controlled decay kept his complex metric modulation clearly articulated through the album's densest arrangements, particularly the 7/8 ride pattern on "Prequel to the Sequel."

**Q: What double bass pedal does Blake Richardson use?**  
A: Richardson has used the DW 9002 double bass pedal since at least the Alaska (2005) and Colors (2007) sessions and continues to use it today. Its adjustable cam lets him vary kick density within the same groove — from sparse single-strokes to dense double-bass runs — without changing feel, central to his compositional approach to double kick.
