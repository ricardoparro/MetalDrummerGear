## Gear Timeline

### Leviathan Era (2004)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga — six-ply bubinga shells, double 22"x18" kicks, 10"/12" racks, 14"/16" floors
- **Snare:** Tama Bell Brass 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX Series — 14" Groove Hi-Hats, 17"/18" Crashes, 21" Ride, 18" China, 10" Splash
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 3A Wood Tip
- **Heads:** Evans Genera G2 Clear (toms), EMAD Clear (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2004):** ~$7,600
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$13,200
- **Notable:** Dailor signed with Tama during 2002's Remission sessions, and the full Starclassic Bubinga rig was in place for Leviathan — the Moby-Dick concept album that earned a Grammy nomination for "Blood and Thunder" and turned Mastodon from Atlanta sludge upstarts into one of metal's most critically celebrated bands.

### Crack the Skye Era (2006–2010)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga (continued, refined configuration)
- **Snare:** Tama Bell Brass 14"x6.5" (deeper shell for added body)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl cymbals (switched from Sabian HHX for a darker, more atmospheric tone)
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide double pedal (continued)
- **Sticks:** Vater 3A / transitioning toward 5B
- **Estimated cost (2009):** ~$9,500
- **Notable:** Crack the Skye (2009) is widely regarded as Dailor's technical and compositional peak with Mastodon — the album where his cymbal transition to Meinl's darker Byzance-family voice matched the record's more progressive, atmospheric songwriting.

### Emperor of Sand / Hushed and Grim Era (2011–present)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic (refined configuration, current)
- **Snare:** Tama "Brann's Bronze" Signature Snare 14"x6.5" (BD1465) — released 2024
- **Cymbals:** Meinl MB20/MB8 Series, including his own Meinl Classics Custom 21" Signature Ghost Ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Speed Cobra double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 5B
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Clear / EMAD2
- **Estimated cost (current):** ~$7,900
- **Notable:** Dailor won a Grammy for Best Metal Performance with Emperor of Sand (2017) and continued the Tama/Meinl core through Hushed and Grim (2021). His setup is now built around his own signature Tama snare and Meinl ride, cementing two decades-long endorsement relationships.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2002:** Signs with Tama during Mastodon's Remission sessions
- **2004:** Full Tama Starclassic Bubinga + Sabian HHX rig in place for Leviathan
- **~2009:** Switches from Sabian HHX to Meinl cymbals for Crack the Skye's darker, more atmospheric tone
- **2017:** Grammy Award win for Best Metal Performance (Emperor of Sand)
- **2024:** Tama releases the "Brann's Bronze" BD1465 signature snare
- **Career constants:** Tama drums since 2002; Meinl cymbals since the Crack the Skye era

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Brann Dailor's drum kit cost?**
A: Brann Dailor's original 2004 Tama Starclassic Bubinga setup — the rig behind Mastodon's Leviathan — cost approximately $7,600. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $13,200. His current Tama/Meinl setup with his signature snare and ride is valued at approximately $7,900 at current retail.

**Q: What drums does Brann Dailor play?**
A: Brann Dailor has played Tama drums since signing during Mastodon's Remission sessions in 2002 — starting with the Starclassic Bubinga shell pack on Leviathan (2004), and continuing with refined Starclassic configurations through his current setup, now paired with his own Tama "Brann's Bronze" signature snare.

**Q: What cymbals does Brann Dailor use?**
A: Dailor used Sabian HHX cymbals through the early-to-mid 2000s, including on Leviathan (2004), before switching to Meinl for a darker, more atmospheric tone around Crack the Skye (2009). His current setup uses Meinl MB20/MB8 cymbals plus his own Meinl Classics Custom 21" Signature Ghost Ride.

**Q: What snare drum does Brann Dailor use?**
A: Brann Dailor used a 14"x5.5" Tama Bell Brass snare on Leviathan, later moving to a deeper 6.5" shell. In 2024, Tama released his signature "Brann's Bronze" 14"x6.5" snare (model BD1465), which anchors his current setup.
