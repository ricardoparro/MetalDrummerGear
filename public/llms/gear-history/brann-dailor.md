## Gear Timeline

### The Hunter Era (2011, Mastodon)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple — custom wrap finish; 22" bass drum (single kick), 10"/12"/13" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Tama Starphonic / Artstar Snare, 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series — 14" hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China
- **Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra single pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Brann Dailor Signature
- **Heads:** Remo Coated Ambassador
- **Original setup cost (2011):** ~$7,025
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$10,233
- **Notable:** The Hunter marked Dailor's pivot from the double-kick complexity of Crack the Skye to a groove-forward, single-kick approach — the Starclassic Maple's warm tone was chosen specifically to let his melodic tom work read as musical content. The album earned Mastodon their first Grammy, with "Black Tongue" winning Best Metal Performance.

### Current Setup (Hushed and Grim era, 2021–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure — 20" bass drum, 10"/12"/13" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Tama S.L.P. 14"x6.5" G-Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom / Z Custom Series
- **Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra Lever Glide hi-hat stand, Tama Speed Cobra double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Coated Ambassador / Fiberskyn
- **Notable:** Dailor's most recent recorded setups show a shift away from the Tama Starclassic platform toward Pearl Reference Pure shells and a brighter Zildjian cymbal palette, while the Meinl Byzance relationship and jazz-influenced melodic playing style remain career-long constants.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1999:** Co-founds Mastodon; early setup built around a budget Mapex kit with a DW 5000 double pedal
- **2006:** Blood Mountain — switches to Gretsch USA Custom shells
- **2009:** Crack the Skye — expanded 6-tom configuration; widely considered Dailor's finest performance
- **2011:** The Hunter recorded — Tama Starclassic Maple + Meinl Byzance groove-era rig established; first Grammy win
- **2017:** Emperor of Sand — Tama Starclassic Maple rig continues; second Grammy win for Best Metal Performance
- **2021:** Hushed and Grim — switches to Pearl Reference Pure and Zildjian A Custom/Z Custom cymbals
- **Career-long constant:** Melodic, jazz-informed "lead drums" approach that treats the kit as a compositional instrument rather than a timekeeper

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Brann Dailor's kit cost?**  
A: Dailor's 2011 The Hunter-era Tama Starclassic Maple setup — including Meinl Byzance cymbals and a Tama Iron Cobra single pedal — cost approximately $7,025 in 2011 dollars, equivalent to roughly $10,233 adjusted for 2026 inflation. His gear has evolved considerably since, moving through Emperor of Sand's Tama Starclassic rig to a Pearl Reference Pure kit on Hushed and Grim (2021).

**Q: What drum kit does Brann Dailor use?**  
A: On Mastodon's 2011 breakthrough The Hunter, Dailor played a Tama Starclassic Maple kit with a 22" single bass drum, 10"/12"/13" rack toms, and a 16" floor tom, paired with Meinl Byzance cymbals. His setup has continued to evolve on subsequent Mastodon albums, most recently toward Pearl Reference Pure shells.

**Q: Why did Brann Dailor switch to a single bass drum on The Hunter?**  
A: The Hunter marked a deliberate compositional pivot away from the sprawling double-kick architecture of Crack the Skye (2009) toward shorter, more direct song structures. Committing to a single 22" kick redirected Dailor's creative energy toward groove, feel, and melodic tom work rather than technical bass drum velocity.

**Q: What cymbals does Brann Dailor use?**  
A: On The Hunter, Dailor used Meinl Byzance Series cymbals — 14" hi-hats, 17", 18", and 19" crashes, a 21" ride, and an 18" China — chosen for their dark, blooming tone and used with deliberate restraint rather than frequent crashing.
