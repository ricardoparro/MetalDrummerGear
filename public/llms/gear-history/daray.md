## Gear Timeline

### Vader Debut Era (2006–2008)

- **Drums:** Pearl Export Series — birch/poplar shells, 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Export 14"x5.5" steel
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Single chain-drive pedal, standard stands
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador
- **Estimated cost (2006–2008):** ~$3,200
- **Notable:** Daray joined Vader, Poland's most internationally recognized death metal band, stepping into a lineup with a demanding technical and touring pedigree.

### Dimmu Borgir Concurrent Role (2008–2011)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure — maple/birch hybrid shells, 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"x5" Brass
- **Cymbals:** Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series — 14" RUDE Hi-Hats, 16"/18" RUDE/2002 Crashes, 22" RUDE Power Ride
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic Extreme 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick), Ambassador (toms)
- **Estimated cost (2008–2011):** ~$4,500
- **Notable:** Joined Dimmu Borgir in 2008, replacing Hellhammer, while continuing his Vader tenure — a dual role requiring gear that performed identically against a four-piece death metal band and a full symphony orchestra and choir.

### Welcome to the Morbid Reich Era (2011–2016)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure Series — custom black finish, 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"x5" Brass, bright cutting crack
- **Cymbals:** Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series — 14" RUDE Hi-Hats, 17"/18" RUDE Crashes, 22" RUDE Power Ride, 18" 2002 China
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic Extreme 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick), Ambassador (toms)
- **Original setup cost (2011):** ~$6,723
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$9,793
- **Notable:** Welcome to the Morbid Reich (2011) captured Daray's signature Pearl Reference Pure / Paiste RUDE & 2002 rig — engineered for consistent trigger response and extreme durability across both Vader's straightforward aggression and Dimmu Borgir's orchestral productions.

### Dimmu Borgir Focus / Current Setup (2016–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure Series (custom black finish, refined), 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"x5" Brass, maintained
- **Cymbals:** Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series, mature configuration
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal, trigger-assisted setup
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic Extreme 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick), Ambassador (toms)
- **Estimated cost (current):** ~$7,500
- **Notable:** Left Vader in 2016 to focus exclusively on Dimmu Borgir, recording Eonian (2018) on the same Pearl/Paiste platform established in 2011.

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Daray's drum kit cost?**
A: Daray's 2011 Welcome to the Morbid Reich-era Pearl Reference Pure setup cost approximately $6,723. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $9,793. His current Dimmu Borgir-era setup is valued at approximately $7,500 at current retail.

**Q: What drums does Daray play?**
A: Daray has played a Pearl Reference Pure custom black finish kit since 2008 — his signature setup for both Vader and Dimmu Borgir. Earlier in his career he used a Pearl Export kit (2006–2008) before upgrading to the Reference Pure platform.

**Q: What cymbals does Daray use?**
A: Daray's cymbal setup has centered on Paiste since the start of his career, evolving from the 2002 Series (2006–2008) to a mixed Paiste RUDE & 2002 configuration from 2008 onward — the RUDE series' thick, warp-resistant construction is built for extreme volume and durability across punishing multi-night touring runs.

**Q: What band is Daray the drummer for?**
A: Daray is the drummer for Norwegian symphonic black metal band Dimmu Borgir (since 2008) and was the drummer for Polish death metal band Vader from 2006 to 2016. He continues to hold both extreme-speed technical drumming duties and theatrical live-production demands across his Dimmu Borgir role.
