## Gear Timeline

### King of All Kings Era (2002, Hate Eternal)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple — 100% maple shells; 22"x18" bass drum (x2, double bass), 10"/12" rack toms, 16"/18" floor toms
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Maple 14"x6.5", medium-bright tuning
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002/Rude Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Pedals:** Tama Speed Cobra double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater custom specification
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick), Remo Ambassador Coated (toms/snare)
- **Original setup cost (2002):** ~$7,100
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$12,929
- **Notable:** Roddy's gravity blast technique — a one-handed rolling motion derived from marching percussion rudiments — sustained 220–280 BPM blast beats on King of All Kings, recorded with Erik Rutan. The 100%-maple Starclassic shells were chosen specifically for their fast attack and controlled decay, preserving individual stroke definition where slower-decaying shells would blur into an undifferentiated wash at extreme tempo.

### Current Setup (DW / Educational Era, 2015–present)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series Maple
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series Maple or Brass
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX & AAX hybrid setup
- **Pedals:** Axis A Longboard double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 5B or custom model
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD/EQ4 (kick), Evans EC2 Clear (toms), Evans Genera HD Dry / Remo Emperor Coated (snare)
- **Notable:** Following 2007's "The Evolution of Blast Beats" Hudson Music DVD — a foundational resource for extreme metal drumming technique — Roddy moved to a DW Collector's Series kit and a Sabian HHX/AAX hybrid cymbal setup. The Axis A Longboard's direct-drive action reflects gear chosen for consistency at the tempos his global drum clinic circuit and educational work demand.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1994:** Builds technical foundation in the death metal underground; develops the gravity blast technique
- **2002:** King of All Kings recorded — Tama Starclassic Maple/Paiste rig captures one of death metal's most extreme performances
- **2007:** Releases "The Evolution of Blast Beats" instructional DVD, becoming a foundational extreme-metal drumming resource
- **2015:** Switches to DW Collector's Series and Sabian HHX/AAX; expands worldwide drum clinic circuit
- **Career-long constant:** Gear chosen for fast attack and controlled decay in service of technique, not raw power

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Derek Roddy use?**  
A: Derek Roddy currently plays a DW Collector's Series Maple kit with a Sabian HHX/AAX hybrid cymbal setup and an Axis A Longboard double pedal. On Hate Eternal's 2002 King of All Kings, he played a Tama Starclassic Maple kit with Paiste 2002/Rude cymbals and a Tama Speed Cobra double pedal — that original setup cost approximately $7,100 in 2002, equivalent to about $12,929 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: How much does Derek Roddy's kit cost?**  
A: Roddy's King of All Kings-era (2002) Tama Starclassic Maple/Paiste rig cost approximately $7,100 at the time, or roughly $12,929 in inflation-adjusted 2026 dollars. His current DW Collector's Series/Sabian HHX-AAX setup is valued at approximately $8,000-plus.

**Q: What is the gravity blast technique?**  
A: The gravity blast is a one-handed rolling stick technique Roddy pioneered, derived from rudimental marching percussion mechanics. It uses the stick's natural rebound against gravity to generate blast-beat-speed patterns without alternating hands, allowing him to sustain 220–280 BPM tempos with less fatigue than bilateral-hand approaches.

**Q: What instructional content has Derek Roddy produced?**  
A: Roddy's "The Evolution of Blast Beats" (Hudson Music, 2007) is widely considered a foundational resource for extreme metal drumming technique, breaking down blast beat styles and efficient technique for developing speed and endurance. He continues to conduct drum clinics worldwide.
