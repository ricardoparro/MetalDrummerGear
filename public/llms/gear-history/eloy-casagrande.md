## Gear Timeline

### Sepultura Debut Era (2011–2013)

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn V — 5-piece; 22"x18" kick, 10"x8" rack, 12"x9" rack, 14"x14" floor, 16"x16" floor
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther 14"x6.5" steel
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series — 14" hi-hats, 17" crash, 18" crash, 21" ride
- **Pedal:** DW 9000XF double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B Hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Powerstroke 3 (kick)
- **Estimated setup cost (2011):** ~$4,500
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$6,450
- **Notable:** Casagrande joined Sepultura at just 19 years old in 2011, immediately deploying a professional-grade Mapex Saturn V that belied his youth. The Saturn V's hybrid maple/walnut shells delivered the punch-plus-warmth profile that suited Sepultura's transition into modern groove-inflected metal. His debut on Kairos (2011) announced one of metal's most exciting new drumming talents — an instinctive player combining Brazilian rhythmic feel with European technical precision.

### Sepultura The Mediator Era (2013–2017)

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn V — same shell configuration, updated hardware and finish
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther Blade 14"x6.5" steel, die-cast hoops
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series — 14" AAX Stage hi-hats, 17" AAX X-Plosion crash, 18" AAX crash, 21" AAX Raw Bell Dry ride
- **Pedal:** DW 9000XF double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B American Classic
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Clear (toms), Coated Emperor (snare), Powerstroke 3 (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2014):** ~$4,662
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$6,452
- **Notable:** The Mediator Between Head and Hands Must Be the Heart (2013) represents Casagrande's most fully realised Sepultura-era work. His drumming combined thrash metal velocity with progressive time-feel — tracks like 'The Vatican' and 'Grief' showcased a player already thinking beyond standard metal drumming templates. The Mapex Black Panther Blade snare's deep steel shell gave his backbeat a powerful, penetrating crack that anchored Sepultura's dense modern production.

### Transition / Session Era (2017–2022)

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn Evolution — updated version of Saturn shell platform
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther Blaster 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX and HHX mixed setup
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Notable:** Following his departure from Sepultura, Casagrande continued developing his craft through session work and live touring. This transitional period saw him refine his approach across multiple projects, maintaining the Mapex platform while exploring different tonal combinations. His drumming became increasingly recognised in wider metal circles, setting the stage for a high-profile endorsement transition.

### Slipknot Era (2023–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference — Slipknot-configuration shell pack, custom finish
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX and HHX — expanded live touring setup
- **Pedal:** DW 9000XF double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B American Classic
- **Heads:** Evans G2 (toms), EMAD2 (kick), EC Reverse Dot (snare)
- **Estimated current setup cost:** ~$9,000–$12,000 (full touring rig)
- **Notable:** Casagrande's transition to Pearl Reference with Slipknot marked one of metal's most-watched drummer succession stories. Replacing Jay Weinberg in 2023, he inherited one of the biggest stages in metal with Slipknot's 44 million monthly Spotify listeners. The Pearl Reference's multi-wood shell construction — combining maple, birch, and mahogany — gave his Slipknot setup a fuller, more produced tone suited to arena-scale live performance. The transition from Mapex's warmth to Pearl Reference's projection mirrors the scale shift from Sepultura-level touring to Slipknot's global stadium circuit.
