## Gear Timeline

### Early Cryptopsy Era (1993–2000)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Custom — professional maple shell pack, 6-piece
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series — 14" hi-hats, 16" crash, 18" crash, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B Hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador (toms), Emperor (kick)
- **Estimated setup cost (1996):** ~$3,200
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$6,800
- **Notable:** Mounier's early work on None So Vile (1996) and Whisper Supremacy (1998) established him as technical death metal's preeminent drummer. The Pearl Masters Custom provided the clarity and projection needed for Cryptopsy's complex, layered compositions. Mounier's Meinl Byzance endorsement — which would continue throughout his career — gave his cymbal work a dark, complex character that never pierced harshly at extreme tempos. None So Vile remains widely cited as the greatest technical death metal album ever recorded.

### None More Black / And Then You'll Beg Era (2003–2008)

- **Drums:** Sonor Force 3007 — professional German-made maple shell pack
- **Snare:** Sonor Dvessel Series Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Dark — 14" hi-hats, 16" crash, 18" crash, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B Extreme
- **Heads:** Evans G2 (toms), EMAD2 (kick)
- **Notable:** Mounier transitioned to Sonor during this period, beginning a deep endorsement relationship with the German manufacturer. The Sonor Force 3007's maple shells delivered a warmer, more complex tone than his Pearl setup, better suited to Cryptopsy's increasingly dense and dissonant compositional approach. The upgrade to DW 9000 pedals gave his double-kick work — already approaching 260 BPM in blast sections — greater mechanical consistency.

### Sonor SQ2 Era (2008–2015)

- **Drums:** Sonor SQ2 Thin Beech — custom-order 6-piece; 22"x18" kick, 10"x8" rack, 12"x9" rack, 14"x14" and 16"x16" floors
- **Snare:** Sonor Custom Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Dark — 14" hi-hats, 16" crash, 18" crash, 20" ride (expanded setup)
- **Pedal:** DW 9000XF extended footboard double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B Extreme
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Clear (toms), EC2 Coated (snare), EMAD2 (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2012):** ~$12,112
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$17,284
- **Notable:** The Sonor SQ2 represented the pinnacle of Mounier's gear journey — one of the most expensive production drum kits available, each built to custom specifications in Germany. Mounier's thin beech configuration delivered maximum resonance with a bright, cutting attack ideal for technical death metal's dense production. The SQ2's bespoke manufacturing guaranteed consistency that standard production kits cannot achieve, critical for Mounier's playing at extreme tempos. This era produced the Cryptopsy self-titled album (2012) and The Book of Suffering — Tome I (2015).

### Current Setup (2015–present)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Bubinga — professional bubinga shell pack, 6-piece
- **Snare:** Tama Starphonic Steel 14"x6"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Dark and Extra Dry mixed setup
- **Pedal:** DW 9000XF double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B Extreme
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Clear (toms), EMAD2 Frosted (kick)
- **Notable:** Mounier transitioned to Tama Starclassic Bubinga for his most recent setup — bubinga's exceptionally dense, focused tone giving his kit a punchy low-end clarity that complements Cryptopsy's current sound. The move from Sonor to Tama represented a shift in tonal philosophy: where the SQ2 maximised resonance, the Starclassic Bubinga prioritises focused attack and sustain control. His Meinl Byzance endorsement continues unbroken, providing continuity across all eras.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1993 → 2003:** Pearl Masters Custom to Sonor Force 3007 — transition to German engineering as Mounier deepened his technical approach
- **2003 → 2008:** Sonor Force 3007 to Sonor SQ2 — upgrade to Sonor's flagship bespoke custom kit
- **2015 onward:** Sonor SQ2 to Tama Starclassic Bubinga — shift in tonal philosophy from maximum resonance to focused attack
- **Throughout career:** Meinl Byzance endorsement maintained without interruption — one of technical death metal's longest continuous cymbal relationships
- **Pedal progression:** DW 5000 (1990s) → DW 9000 (2000s) → DW 9000XF (current) — progressive upgrade within DW family for extended footboard reach

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Flo Mounier's drum kit cost?**  
A: Flo Mounier's current Tama Starclassic Bubinga setup costs approximately $8,000–$12,000 for the full rig including Meinl Byzance cymbals and DW hardware. His iconic Sonor SQ2 setup from around 2012 cost approximately $12,112, equivalent to ~$17,284 adjusted for 2026 inflation — one of the most expensive setups in the technical death metal world.

**Q: What brand does Flo Mounier endorse?**  
A: Flo Mounier currently endorses Tama drums (Starclassic Bubinga), Meinl cymbals (Byzance Dark and Extra Dry series), DW pedals (9000XF double pedal), and Vic Firth sticks (5B Extreme). He previously endorsed Sonor drums for over a decade, during which he used the flagship SQ2 custom kit.

**Q: What is Flo Mounier's most expensive piece of gear?**  
A: Flo Mounier's most expensive piece of gear was historically his Sonor SQ2 Thin Beech custom shell pack, which cost approximately $8,500–$11,000 depending on configuration — making it one of the most expensive drum kits ever featured on MetalForge. His current Tama Starclassic Bubinga is similarly positioned at the premium tier, retailing for approximately $5,000–$7,000 for the shell pack alone.

**Q: What drum kit did Flo Mounier use on None So Vile?**  
A: On None So Vile (1996), Flo Mounier used a Pearl Masters Custom kit with Meinl Byzance cymbals and a DW 5000 double pedal. The setup cost approximately $3,200 at the time, equivalent to ~$6,800 adjusted for 2026 inflation. None So Vile's production showcases Mounier's Pearl-era sound at its most explosive — the kit's brightness amplifying the album's relentless technical assault.

**Q: What double bass pedal does Flo Mounier use?**  
A: Flo Mounier uses the DW 9000XF double pedal — the extended footboard version of DW's flagship 9000 series. The extended footboard accommodates Mounier's wide foot technique at blast-beat tempos approaching 260 BPM. He transitioned from the DW 5000 (1990s) to the DW 9000 (2000s) to the current 9000XF as his technical demands evolved.
