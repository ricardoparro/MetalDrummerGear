## Gear Timeline

### Raw Black Metal / Corpse Paint Era (1993–1999)

- **Drums:** Pearl Export Series — 5-piece, birch/poplar shells, 22"x16" kick, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Export 14"x5.5" Steel
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" thin crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Basic single chain-drive pedal — no double bass yet
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B hickory
- **Original setup cost (1996):** ~$1,841
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$3,844
- **Notable:** Frost joined Satyricon in 1993, entering Norway's second-wave black metal scene. Nemesis Divina (1996) established the primitive, hyperspeed single-foot blast beat foundation that would define his career, recorded on a deliberately unglamorous Pearl Export kit typical of a scene where budgets favored corpse paint over gear endorsements.

### Industrial Black Metal / Founding 1349 (1999–2006)

- **Drums:** Sonor Designer Series — beech shells, 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Sonor Designer 14"x6" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Double pedal with electronic kick trigger
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic Extreme 5B
- **Estimated cost (1999):** ~$4,500
- **Notable:** Rebel Extravaganza (1999) pushed Satyricon into industrial territory, and Frost co-founded 1349 in 2001 as a pure-extremity outlet. He switched from Pearl to Sonor during this period — a brand relationship that continues today — and added trigger augmentation to guarantee consistent kick attack on Liberation (2003) and Hellfire (2005).

### Progressive Black Metal / Mainstream Breakthrough (2006–2013)

- **Drums:** Sonor SQ2 Series — custom maple shells, 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Sonor SQ2 14"x6" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom & K Series — 14" A Custom hi-hats, 16"/17"/18" A Custom crashes, 22" K Custom Dark ride, 18" K China
- **Pedal:** Sonor Perfect Balance Pedal
- **Estimated cost (2006):** ~$7,500
- **Notable:** Now, Diabolical (2006) and The Age of Nero (2008) brought Satyricon to commercial peak, and Frost upgraded to Sonor's premium custom-build SQ2 platform — his enduring signature kit. By the self-titled Satyricon (2013), his playing had matured into a study in restraint and dynamics.

### Experimental Maturity / Dual-Band Mastery (2013–present)

- **Drums:** Sonor SQ2 Series (refined custom finish)
- **Snare:** Sonor SQ2 14"x6" Maple (unchanged signature snare)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom & K Custom Dark — 14" A Custom hi-hats, 16"/17"/18" A Custom crashes, 22" K Custom Dark ride, 18" K China
- **Pedal:** Sonor Perfect Balance Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic Extreme 5B
- **Current value (2017 era):** ~$8,500
- **Notable:** Frost now balances Satyricon's atmospheric direction (Deep calleth upon Deep, 2017) with 1349's relentless extremity (The Infernal Pathway, 2019), on a fully refined Sonor SQ2 setup that carries three decades of architectural development in his blast beat technique.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1999:** Switched from Pearl Export to Sonor Designer — beginning a decades-long brand partnership, coinciding with 1349's founding
- **~2001:** Electronic kick trigger added to guarantee consistent attack at extreme tempo
- **2006:** Upgraded to Sonor's custom SQ2 platform — his signature kit ever since
- **2006:** Expanded cymbal setup from pure Zildjian A to a mixed A Custom / K Series voicing
- **Cost growth:** From a budget ~$1,841 Pearl Export rig in 1996 to an ~$8,500 custom Sonor SQ2 setup by 2017 — reflecting three decades of rising professional stature

## FAQ

**Q: How much did Frost's drum kit cost on Nemesis Divina?**
A: Frost's 1996 Pearl Export/Paiste 2002 setup behind Satyricon's Nemesis Divina cost approximately $1,841, equivalent to roughly $3,844 today after inflation — a deliberately modest, professional-but-unpretentious rig typical of the mid-1990s Norwegian black metal underground.

**Q: What drum kit does Frost play now?**
A: Frost plays a custom Sonor SQ2 Series kit — his signature setup since the mid-2000s — paired with a Sonor SQ2 14"x6" maple snare, a mixed Zildjian A Custom/K Custom Dark cymbal configuration, and a Sonor Perfect Balance pedal.

**Q: Why did Frost play without a double pedal early in his career?**
A: On Nemesis Divina (1996), Frost's blast beats were played entirely with a single-foot technique on one kick drum — no double pedal. That single-foot hyperspeed technique, developed during his earliest Satyricon years, became one of his signature calling cards.

**Q: What cymbals does Frost use?**
A: Frost used Paiste 2002 in the early Satyricon years, switched to Zildjian A Series around 1999, and settled into a mixed Zildjian A Custom and K Custom Dark configuration from the mid-2000s onward — a voicing that balances bright cutting accents with darker atmospheric wash.

**Q: How has Frost's gear evolved across his career?**
A: Frost's gear moved from a budget Pearl Export kit (1993–1999) to Sonor Designer (1999–2006) and finally to his signature custom Sonor SQ2 platform (2006–present), with cymbals evolving from Paiste 2002 to Zildjian A and eventually a mixed A Custom/K Custom Dark setup — tracking his rise from Norwegian underground drummer to one of black metal's most respected technicians.
