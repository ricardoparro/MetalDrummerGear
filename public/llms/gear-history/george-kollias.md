## Gear Timeline

### Those Whom the Gods Detest Era (2009, Nile)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure — all-maple shell pack; 22" bass drum, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Paiste Signature Series — 14" hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China
- **Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 double bass pedal
- **Electronics:** ddrum kick triggers
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth George Kollias Signature (first signature model)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Powerstroke 3 (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2009):** ~$7,549
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$11,529
- **Notable:** Widely regarded as Kollias's technical and artistic peak — Those Whom the Gods Detest and its companion tour cemented his reputation as death metal's most precise blast beat drummer. This era also introduced his first Vic Firth signature drumstick, engineered specifically for durability at sustained extreme tempos.

### Current Setup (Vile Nilotic Rites era, 2015–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure — custom finish, refined over 10+ years on the same platform
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x6.5" (decade-plus snare of choice)
- **Cymbals:** Paiste Signature Series (complete configuration)
- **Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 double bass pedal (unchanged since the beginning of his career)
- **Electronics:** ddrum Acoustic Pro Triggers on kick and snare
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth George Kollias Signature (updated model, heavier rear end)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor / Powerstroke 3
- **Notable:** Kollias has never switched pedal brands — his blast beat technique, Guinness World Record-certified at 280+ BPM, was built around the Tama Iron Cobra's specific feel since his pre-Nile days in the Greek underground.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1995:** Builds technical foundation in the Athens underground on a budget Tama/Paiste rig with Sickening Horror and Nightfall
- **2004:** Joins Nile; Pearl endorsement begins
- **2005:** Annihilation of the Wicked — debut recording sets a new technical benchmark for death metal
- **2009:** Those Whom the Gods Detest recorded — Pearl Reference Pure and first Vic Firth GK signature sticks established
- **2015:** What Should Not Be Unearthed — ddrum triggers upgraded to a full hybrid Acoustic Pro system
- **2019:** Vile Nilotic Rites — most refined and mature Nile drumming to date
- **Career-long constant:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 double pedal, unchanged across his entire professional career

## FAQ

**Q: How much does George Kollias's drum kit cost?**  
A: Kollias's 2009 Those Whom the Gods Detest-era rig — a Pearl Reference Pure kit with Paiste Signature cymbals and a Tama Iron Cobra HP900 double pedal — cost approximately $7,549 in 2009 dollars, equivalent to roughly $11,529 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: What drum kit does George Kollias use?**  
A: George Kollias plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit — all-maple shells in a 22" kick, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" tom configuration. He has been a Pearl artist since joining Nile in 2004, upgrading from the Pearl Reference Series to the all-maple Reference Pure by 2009.

**Q: What pedal does George Kollias use?**  
A: Kollias plays the Tama Iron Cobra HP900 double bass pedal — the same pedal he has used throughout his entire career, dating back to before he joined Nile. His blast beat technique, verified at 280+ BPM by Guinness World Records, was built around its specific feel and response.

**Q: How much does it cost to build a rig like George Kollias's?**  
A: A Pearl Reference Pure shell pack alone runs approximately $4,200–5,200, with Paiste Signature Series cymbals adding another ~$2,400–2,900. The full documented Those Whom the Gods Detest-era rig, including hardware, triggers, sticks, and heads, totaled roughly $7,549 in 2009 dollars — about $11,529 today.
