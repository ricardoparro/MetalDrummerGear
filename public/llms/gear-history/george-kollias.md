## Gear Timeline

### Greek Underground Era (1995–2004)

- **Drums:** Tama Rockstar/Starclassic — basic 5-piece; 22" kick, 12"/13"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Tama Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B Hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador
- **Original setup cost (~2002):** ~$2,500
- **Notable:** Before joining Nile, Kollias developed his extreme technique in the Greek metal underground with bands including Sickening Horror and Nightfall. The Tama Iron Cobra double pedal he used on this budget kit became the foundation of his record-breaking speed — a pedal he never switched away from, even after every other piece of gear changed.

### Annihilation of the Wicked Era (2005–2008)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series — maple/birch hybrid; 22"x18" bass drum, 10"x9"/12"x10" rack toms, 14"x12"/16"x14" floor toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Paiste Signature Series — 14" hi-hats, 17"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 Power Glide (continued)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Powerstroke 3 (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2005):** ~$9,000
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$15,100
- **Notable:** Kollias joined Nile in 2004, replacing Tony Laureano, and his first Pearl and Paiste endorsements arrived alongside his debut recording. Annihilation of the Wicked (2005) set a new benchmark for sustained extreme-metal blast beats — Kollias would later be Guinness World Record-certified for 280 BPM single strokes, a feat rooted in the technique he demonstrated on this album.

### Those Whom the Gods Detest Era (2009–2015)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure — all-maple; 22" kick, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x6.5" (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Paiste Signature Series (expanded) — 14" hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 (continued)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth George Kollias Signature (first signature stick)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor / Powerstroke 3
- **Electronics:** ddrum triggers on kick drums (new)
- **Estimated setup cost (2009):** ~$14,000
- **Notable:** Nile's technical and artistic peak. Those Whom the Gods Detest (2009) and At the Gate of Sethu (2012) cemented Kollias's reputation as death metal's most precise drummer, and his solo debut Invictus (2009) showed compositional range beyond pure speed. ddrum kick triggers were added to keep the double bass audible at Nile's extreme live volumes.

### Vile Nilotic Rites Era (2015–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure (refined custom specs)
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x6.5" (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Paiste Signature Series (continued) — full configuration unchanged for over a decade
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 (continued — over 20 years with the same pedal design)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth George Kollias Signature (updated taper)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor / Powerstroke 3
- **Electronics:** ddrum Acoustic Pro Triggers
- **Estimated setup cost (current):** ~$18,000
- **Notable:** What Should Not Be Unearthed (2015) and Vile Nilotic Rites (2019) represent Kollias's most mature work with Nile, alongside a second solo album, Labyrinth of Chaos (2018). Worldwide drum clinics and instructional DVDs ("Intense Metal Drumming" I and II) have made him one of extreme metal's most influential educators.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1995–2004:** Tama Rockstar/Starclassic + Paiste 2002 — the pre-endorsement Greek underground rig, built around the Tama Iron Cobra pedal that never left his setup
- **2004–2005:** Joined Nile; first Pearl Reference Series and Paiste Signature endorsements
- **2009:** Upgraded to Pearl Reference Pure all-maple shells; first Vic Firth George Kollias signature sticks; ddrum triggers added
- **2015–present:** Refined Pearl Reference Pure + Paiste Signature configuration, the same Tama Iron Cobra pedal design carried through his entire Nile career

## FAQ

**Q: How fast does George Kollias play?**  
A: George Kollias is Guinness World Record-certified for sustaining 280 BPM single strokes on the bass drum — the fastest verified speed in metal drumming history. On Nile's Annihilation of the Wicked (2005), he sustains blast beats consistently above 240 BPM across entire tracks with full clarity, a distinction that separates him from drummers who only reach high tempos briefly.

**Q: What drum kit does George Kollias use?**  
A: George Kollias currently plays a Pearl Reference Pure all-maple kit, the result of a partnership that began with the Pearl Reference Series on Nile's 2005 debut Annihilation of the Wicked. Before joining Nile, he played a budget Tama Rockstar/Starclassic kit in the Greek metal underground.

**Q: What drums did George Kollias use before joining Nile?**  
A: Before Nile, Kollias played Tama Rockstar/Starclassic drums with Paiste 2002 cymbals — a modest, self-funded setup he used while developing his technique with Greek bands Sickening Horror and Nightfall. The complete pre-endorsement rig cost roughly $2,500.

**Q: What cymbals does George Kollias use?**  
A: George Kollias has played Paiste cymbals throughout his career with Nile — starting with Paiste 2002 in the Greek underground, upgrading to Paiste Signature Series with his first Nile endorsement in 2005, and continuing with a full Paiste Signature configuration today.

**Q: How much does George Kollias's drum kit cost?**  
A: George Kollias's original 2005 Pearl Reference Series setup for Annihilation of the Wicked cost approximately $9,000, equivalent to roughly $15,100 today after inflation. His current Pearl Reference Pure configuration is estimated at around $18,000.
