## Gear Timeline

### Co-founds Obscura (2008)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series — developing endorsement
- **Snare:** DW Collector's 14"x5.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series
- **Pedal:** DW double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B

### Cosmogenesis Era (2009)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series — all-maple 5-piece shell pack, 22"x18" bass drum, 10"x8" rack, 12"x9" rack, 14"x14" floor, 16"x16" floor
- **Snare:** DW Collector's 14"x5.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series — 14" Byzance Traditional Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Byzance Brilliant Crashes, 21" Byzance Traditional Ride
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 Series double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Evans G2 Clear (toms), EMAD (kick), Genera HD Dry (snare)
- **Original setup cost (2009):** ~$8,992
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$13,733
- **Notable:** Cosmogenesis established Obscura as heir apparent to Death's progressive death metal legacy; the all-maple DW Collector's shells gave Grossmann the warmth and articulation needed for the album's sweeping, multi-tempo compositions.

### Omnivium Era (2011)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series (same rig)
- **Snare:** DW Collector's 14"x5.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series (same configuration)
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 Series double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Evans
- **Estimated cost (2011):** ~$9,400
- **Notable:** Carried the same DW/Meinl configuration into Obscura's second album before departing the band in 2014.

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Hannes Grossmann's drum kit cost?**
A: Hannes Grossmann's 2009 Cosmogenesis-era DW Collector's Series setup cost approximately $8,992. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $13,733.

**Q: What drums did Hannes Grossmann use on Cosmogenesis?**
A: Grossmann used an all-maple DW Collector's Series 5-piece shell pack — a 22"x18" bass drum, 10"x8" and 12"x9" rack toms, and 14"x14"/16"x16" floor toms — chosen for warmth and articulation across Obscura's sweeping compositions.

**Q: What cymbals did Hannes Grossmann use with Obscura?**
A: Grossmann used a Meinl Byzance setup — 14" Byzance Traditional Hi-Hats, 18" and 19" Byzance Brilliant Crashes, and a 21" Byzance Traditional Ride — for dark, complex overtones without piercing harshness at blast-beat tempos.

**Q: Did Hannes Grossmann's gear change after leaving Obscura?**
A: Grossmann's DW Collector's / Meinl Byzance configuration remained his defining setup through his 2008–2014 Obscura tenure. After departing, he continued with Alkaloid, the progressive death metal band he co-founded in 2013.
