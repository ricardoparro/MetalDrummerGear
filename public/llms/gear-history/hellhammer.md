## Gear Timeline

### Early Mayhem Era (1988–1993)

- **Drums:** Pearl Export Series — budget configuration
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — entry-level, budget-conscious setup
- **Pedal:** Budget chain-drive double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Original setup cost (1988):** ~$2,200
- **Notable:** Jan Axel Blomberg joined Mayhem in 1988, replacing original drummer Manheim — the year after the band's raw, foundational Deathcrush (1987) EP. His earliest Mayhem-era gear was minimalist by necessity, built for speed and durability rather than tone.

### De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas Era (1994)

- **Drums:** Pearl MLX Maple — 22"x16" kick, 10"/12"/14" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Pearl Free Floating Steel 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Pearl P-2000C Eliminator double pedal
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Ambassador (snare)
- **Original setup cost (1994):** ~$3,800
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$8,400
- **Notable:** De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas (1994) is widely considered black metal's defining album, completed in the shadow of guitarist Euronymous's murder. Hellhammer's upgraded Pearl MLX maple kit and Zildjian A Custom cymbals gave the focused attack heard throughout the record.

### Ordo Ad Chao Era (2007)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters / Reference Series upgrade
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom added to rotation
- **Original setup cost (2007):** ~$6,500
- **Notable:** As Hellhammer's workload expanded across Mayhem, Dimmu Borgir, and Arcturus through the 2000s, his gear was upgraded to the more refined Pearl Masters/Reference platform he continues to use today.

### Daemon Era (2019–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom / Z Custom / K Custom mix — 14" hi-hats, A Custom crashes, Z Custom ride
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor/Ambassador
- **Original setup cost (2019):** ~$8,200
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$10,506
- **Notable:** Daemon (2019) marked Mayhem's return to raw roots. Hellhammer's current Pearl Reference/Zildjian setup is built for durability and consistent speed across his demanding multi-band schedule — he has remained a Pearl Drums and Zildjian Cymbals endorser for over three decades.

## FAQ

**Q: What is Hellhammer's drum setup worth?**  
A: Hellhammer's Pearl MLX/Zildjian A Custom setup from the De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas era (1994) cost approximately $3,800. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $8,400. His current verified Pearl Reference Series kit, with a mixed Zildjian A Custom/Z Custom/K Custom cymbal setup and Pearl Demon Drive double pedal, cost approximately $8,200 when assembled around the Daemon (2019) era — equivalent to roughly $10,506 today.

**Q: What drums does Hellhammer use?**  
A: Hellhammer has been a Pearl Drums endorser since the mid-1990s. He progressed from a budget Pearl Export kit at his 1988 Mayhem debut to a Pearl MLX maple kit for De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas (1994), then to the Pearl Masters/Reference platform he continues to use across Mayhem, Dimmu Borgir, and Arcturus today.

**Q: Did Hellhammer play on Mayhem's Deathcrush?**  
A: No — Deathcrush (1987) was recorded by Mayhem's original drummer, Kjetil "Manheim" Rasmussen. Hellhammer (Jan Axel Blomberg) joined Mayhem in 1988, the year after Deathcrush's release, and went on to record all of the band's full-length albums starting with De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas (1994).

**Q: What cymbals does Hellhammer use?**  
A: Hellhammer has been a Zildjian artist since the mid-1990s, starting with the A Custom series on De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas. His current setup mixes Zildjian A Custom, Z Custom, and K Custom models — chosen for brightness and cut in Mayhem's dense, high-gain mix.
