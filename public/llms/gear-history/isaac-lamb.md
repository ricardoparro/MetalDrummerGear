## Gear Timeline

### Kublai Khan TX Founding (2009)

- **Drums:** Entry-level kit
- **Estimated cost (2009):** ~$1,500
- **Notable:** Co-founded Kublai Khan in Sherman, Texas with vocalist Matt Honeycutt, establishing the beatdown hardcore sound that has defined the band ever since.

### Balancing Survival and Happiness Era (2014)

- **Drums:** Developing SJC Custom endorsement
- **Estimated cost (2014):** ~$4,500
- **Notable:** Signed to Artery Recordings; the SJC Custom Drums relationship began developing ahead of the band's growing profile.

### Nomad Era (2017)

- **Drums:** SJC Custom Drums — 22" bass drum, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** SJC Custom 14"x6.5" American maple
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Classics Custom Dark — 14" hi-hats, 18"/20" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 Series double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 2B
- **Heads:** Evans Hydraulic across kick, toms, and snare
- **Original setup cost (2017):** ~$6,334
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$8,466
- **Notable:** Nomad, Kublai Khan TX's Rise Records debut, carried the band to its largest audience yet and established the definitive SJC Custom / Meinl Classics Custom Dark configuration. The SJC shells' tight bearing edges gave Lamb's kick-snare relationship the focused attack to punch through heavily downtuned 7-string guitars.

### Absolute Era (2019–present)

- **Drums:** SJC Custom Drums (primary endorser, custom configurations)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Classics Custom Dark Series (complete setup)
- **Estimated cost (current):** ~$9,385
- **Notable:** Absolute, widely cited as Kublai Khan TX's defining statement, showcases Lamb's most refined power-through-restraint performance on the same SJC / Meinl / DW core established on Nomad.

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Isaac Lamb's drum kit cost?**
A: Isaac Lamb's 2017 Nomad-era SJC Custom Drums setup cost approximately $6,334. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $8,466.

**Q: What drums does Isaac Lamb play?**
A: Lamb plays a boutique SJC Custom Drums kit in American maple — a 22" bass drum driven by a DW 9000 double pedal, 10"/12" rack toms, and 14"/16" floor toms — paired with an SJC Custom 14"x6.5" maple snare, chosen for the focused attack and fast decay beatdown hardcore's dense guitar context demands.

**Q: What cymbals does Isaac Lamb use?**
A: Lamb uses a complete Meinl Classics Custom Dark setup — 14" hi-hats, 18"/20" crashes, a 21" ride, and an 18" China — chosen for controlled decay that prevents cymbal wash from ringing into the crushing breakdowns that follow closely in beatdown hardcore arrangements.

**Q: Why is Isaac Lamb's drumming considered notable?**
A: Lamb has been the only drummer in Kublai Khan TX's history since its 2009 founding, building a "power through restraint" groove philosophy where deliberate space in verses and choruses makes the band's devastating breakdowns register with maximum physical impact.

**Q: Has Isaac Lamb's gear changed since Nomad?**
A: No — the SJC Custom Drums / Meinl Classics Custom Dark / DW 9000 configuration established on Nomad (2017) carried directly into Absolute (2019) and remains Lamb's core setup today.
