## Gear Timeline

### Something Wild / Hatebreeder Era (1997–1999)

- **Drums:** Pearl Export Series — pre-endorsement configuration; 22"x18" dual kick, 10"x9"/12"x10" rack toms, 14"x14"/16"x16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Pearl 14"x5.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** Pearl double bass pedal (early configuration)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated (toms), Powerstroke 3 (kick)
- **Original setup cost (1999):** ~$2,756
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$5,420
- **Notable:** Raatikainen co-founded what became Children of Bodom (originally Inearthed) with Alexi Laiho in 1993. Something Wild (1997) was the band's raw, self-funded debut — no sponsored kit, no cymbal contract, just a Pearl Export configuration and Zildjian A Series cymbals a teenage drummer assembled himself. Hatebreeder (1999) brought international recognition on the same setup, with tracks like "Children of Bodom" and "Warheart" establishing the sustained double-bass template Raatikainen would refine for the next two decades.

### Follow the Reaper / Hate Crew Deathroll Era (2000–2003)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Premium Maple — flagship endorsement solidified; six-ply all-maple shells
- **Snare:** Pearl Masters 14"x5.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — hi-hats and crashes, developing K Custom ride relationship
- **Pedal:** Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal (adopted)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A (established)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor/Ambassador
- **Estimated setup cost (2003):** ~$4,500
- **Notable:** Follow the Reaper (2000) is widely considered Children of Bodom's defining statement, and Hate Crew Deathroll (2003) was the band's commercial peak. The Pearl Masters Premium Maple kit and Pearl Eliminator double pedal Raatikainen adopted here became his core setup for the rest of his career.

### Are You Dead Yet? / Blooddrunk Era (2005–2008)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Premium Maple (continued)
- **Snare:** Pearl Masters 14"x5.5" Maple (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom crashes, K Custom ride — full setup established
- **Pedal:** Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal (continued)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Notable:** Are You Dead Yet? (2005) achieved the widest mainstream reach of any Children of Bodom album, including an Ozzfest 2006 slot, while the full Zildjian A Custom/K Custom cymbal configuration reached its established form.

### I Worship Chaos / Hexed Era (2013–2019)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Premium Maple (updated custom finish)
- **Snare:** Pearl Masters 14"x5.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom 14" hi-hats, 17"/18" crashes, 20" K Custom ride — fully documented final configuration
- **Pedal:** Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Estimated setup cost (2015):** ~$5,000
- **Notable:** Halo of Blood (2013) and I Worship Chaos (2015) marked a mature, compositionally focused return to Children of Bodom's melodic roots. Hexed (2019) was the band's final album before their dissolution — Raatikainen's Pearl and Zildjian setup remained essentially unchanged at its core since 2000.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1997–1999:** Pearl Export + Zildjian A Series — the pre-endorsement rig used on Something Wild and Hatebreeder, assembled before any sponsorship deal
- **2000:** Upgraded to flagship Pearl Masters Premium Maple; adopted the Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal — both used through Hate Crew Deathroll's commercial peak
- **Mid-2000s:** Cymbal setup matured from Zildjian A Series to a full A Custom/K Custom configuration
- **2000–2019:** Core Pearl Masters/Zildjian/Eliminator setup remained essentially unchanged across every subsequent Children of Bodom album through Hexed (2019)

## FAQ

**Q: What drum set does Jaska Raatikainen use?**  
A: Jaska Raatikainen played a Pearl Masters Premium Maple kit — a six-ply all-maple shell pack — throughout most of Children of Bodom's career, adopted around Follow the Reaper (2000) and carried through to the band's final album, Hexed (2019). Before that, on Something Wild (1997) and Hatebreeder (1999), he played a pre-endorsement Pearl Export configuration.

**Q: What drums did Jaska Raatikainen use on Hatebreeder?**  
A: On Hatebreeder (1999), Raatikainen played a pre-endorsement Pearl Export configuration with Zildjian A Series cymbals and a Pearl double bass pedal. The complete setup cost approximately $2,756, equivalent to about $5,420 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: What cymbals does Jaska Raatikainen use?**  
A: Jaska Raatikainen plays Zildjian cymbals — starting with the A Series on Children of Bodom's earliest albums and upgrading to a full A Custom and K Custom configuration by the mid-2000s, which he kept through the band's final tour in 2019.

**Q: How much does Jaska Raatikainen's drum kit cost?**  
A: Raatikainen's original 1999 Pearl Export setup for Hatebreeder cost approximately $2,756, equivalent to roughly $5,420 today. His later flagship Pearl Masters Premium Maple configuration, adopted around 2000, is estimated at around $4,500 to $5,000.

**Q: What bass drum pedal does Jaska Raatikainen use?**  
A: Jaska Raatikainen used a standard Pearl double bass pedal on Children of Bodom's earliest albums, then adopted the Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal around Follow the Reaper (2000) — its interchangeable cam system supported the sustained double-kick patterns that define the band's discography through Hexed (2019).
