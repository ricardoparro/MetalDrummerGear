## Gear Timeline

### Follow the Reaper Era (2000, Children of Bodom)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Premium Maple — six-ply all-maple shell pack; 22" bass drum, 10"/12" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Pearl Masters 14"x5.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (kick/toms), Ambassador (snare)
- **Original setup cost (2000):** ~$3,846
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$7,317
- **Notable:** Follow the Reaper is widely considered Children of Bodom's defining album — this is where Raatikainen's developing Pearl endorsement solidified into the flagship Masters Premium Maple kit and Pearl Eliminator pedal that anchored the rest of his career. The Eliminator's interchangeable cam system gave him the identical, repeatable pedal response that "Needled 24/7" and "Sixpounder"'s sustained double-kick patterns demanded.

### Final Setup (Hexed era, 2019)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Premium Maple — updated custom finish, unchanged core configuration
- **Snare:** Pearl Masters 14"x5.5" Maple (confirmed final-era snare)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom 14" hi-hats, 17"/18" crashes, 20" K Custom ride
- **Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor / Ambassador
- **Notable:** Twenty-six years, the same band, mostly the same gear. Children of Bodom's final album before their 2019 dissolution used a setup barely changed from the one Raatikainen established on Follow the Reaper in 2000 — refinement rather than reinvention was the throughline of his entire career.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1993:** Co-founds Inearthed (later Children of Bodom) with Alexi Laiho
- **1997–1999:** Something Wild and Hatebreeder — developing Pearl and Zildjian A Series endorsements
- **2000:** Follow the Reaper recorded — Pearl Masters Premium Maple and Pearl Eliminator pedal established as core gear
- **2003:** Hate Crew Deathroll — the band's commercial peak, same rig carries through
- **2008:** Blooddrunk — full Zildjian A Custom/K Custom cymbal setup reaches its established configuration
- **2019:** Hexed — final Children of Bodom album; core rig unchanged since 2000
- **Career-long constant:** Pearl Masters Premium Maple kit and Pearl Eliminator double pedal, in place for the majority of the band's 26-year career

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Jaska Raatikainen's drum set cost?**  
A: Raatikainen's 2000 Follow the Reaper-era setup — a Pearl Masters Premium Maple kit with a Pearl Eliminator double pedal and Zildjian A Custom cymbals — cost approximately $3,846 in 2000 dollars, equivalent to roughly $7,317 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: What drum set does Jaska Raatikainen use?**  
A: Jaska Raatikainen played a Pearl Masters Premium Maple kit — a six-ply all-maple shell pack — throughout the majority of Children of Bodom's career, paired with a Pearl Masters 14"x5.5" maple snare and a Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal.

**Q: Did Jaska Raatikainen's gear change much across his career?**  
A: Remarkably little. He upgraded from a developing Pearl endorsement on Something Wild (1997) and Hatebreeder (1999) to the flagship Pearl Masters Premium Maple kit and Pearl Eliminator pedal on Follow the Reaper (2000), then carried that same core setup — with an expanded Zildjian A Custom/K Custom cymbal configuration — all the way through to Hexed (2019).

**Q: What pedal did Jaska Raatikainen use?**  
A: Raatikainen used the Pearl Eliminator double bass pedal from Follow the Reaper (2000) onward. Its interchangeable cam system let him adjust the pedal's acceleration curve to his technique, giving him the mechanical consistency his complex double-kick patterns required across two decades.
