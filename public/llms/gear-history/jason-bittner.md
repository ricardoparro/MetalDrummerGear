## Gear Timeline

### The War Within Era (2002–2015, Shadows Fall)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Performer B/B — birch/bubinga hybrid shells; 22"x18" bass drum, 10"x8"/12"x9" rack toms, 14"x14"/16"x16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Tama Starphonic Steel 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX/HH Series — 14" HH hi-hats, 16"/18" HHX crashes, 20" HH ride, HHX china
- **Pedals:** DW 9002 double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B Hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick), Coated Ambassador (snare)
- **Original setup cost (2004):** ~$4,773
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$8,279
- **Notable:** Bittner joined Shadows Fall in 2001 and built his reputation on this Tama/Sabian/DW rig through The Art of Balance (2002), The War Within (2004) — the band's Grammy-nominated NWOAHM breakthrough — and Threads of Life (2007). The birch/bubinga hybrid shells and steel Starphonic snare gave him the cutting attack needed to punch through Shadows Fall's dense, layered arrangements.

### Overkill / Category 7 Era (2017–present)

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn V — all-maple shell pack
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther 14"x6.5" Brass
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K & A Custom Series — 14" K hi-hats, 18"/19" A Custom crashes, 21" K Custom ride, 18" K China
- **Pedals:** Mapex Falcon double pedal, Mapex T865 throne
- **Sticks:** Promark Jason Bittner Signature 5BX
- **Heads:** Evans
- **Notable:** After Shadows Fall disbanded in 2015, Bittner briefly carried his Tama rig into Flotsam & Jetsam's Dreams of Death before assembling an entirely new setup — Mapex Saturn V shells, Zildjian K & A Custom cymbals, and a Mapex Falcon double pedal — for his 2017 move to Overkill. That same rig has carried through his work in thrash supergroup Category 7 since 2024.

- **2001:** Joined Shadows Fall, establishing the Tama Starclassic Performer B/B and Sabian HHX/HH rig

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Jason Bittner use on The War Within?**  
A: Jason Bittner played a Tama Starclassic Performer B/B kit on The War Within (2004), with birch/bubinga hybrid shells, a Tama Starphonic Steel 14"x6.5" snare, and Sabian HHX/HH cymbals. The complete setup cost approximately $4,773 in 2004 dollars — equivalent to about $8,279 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: What drum kit does Jason Bittner play now?**  
A: Jason Bittner currently plays a Mapex Saturn V all-maple kit with Zildjian K & A Custom cymbals and a Mapex Black Panther 14"x6.5" Brass snare. He switched to this Mapex/Zildjian rig in 2017 when he joined Overkill, moving away from the Tama/Sabian setup he'd used throughout his Shadows Fall career, and has carried it through his work in Category 7 since 2024.

**Q: How much would Jason Bittner's Shadows Fall-era drum kit cost today?**  
A: The original 2004 Tama Starclassic Performer B/B setup cost approximately $4,773. Adjusted for inflation to 2026 dollars, that's roughly $8,279 — reflecting the working touring drummer's rig Bittner used across Shadows Fall's The Art of Balance (2002), The War Within (2004), and Threads of Life (2007).

**Q: Did Jason Bittner always play Mapex drums?**  
A: No — Bittner played Tama Starclassic Performer B/B kits throughout his Shadows Fall career (2001–2015) and briefly into Flotsam & Jetsam. He switched to Mapex Saturn V shells only in 2017, when he joined Overkill, alongside a matching switch from Sabian to Zildjian cymbals.
