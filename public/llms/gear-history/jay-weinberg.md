## Gear Timeline

### Hardcore & Punk Roots (2009–2013)

- **Drums:** Various touring-grade kits — no fixed endorsement
- **Snare:** Stock steel snare 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series
- **Hardware:** Basic touring hardware, single bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador
- **Notable:** Before Slipknot, Weinberg filled in for his father Max Weinberg on the 2009 Bruce Springsteen "Working on a Dream" World Tour dates, then built his chops touring with hardcore/punk acts Against Me! and Madball. No fixed drum or cymbal endorsement existed during this stretch — he played whatever road gear was available, developing the physical, no-frills intensity that put him in the room for Slipknot's secret 2013 audition process.

### .5: The Gray Chapter Era (2013–2017)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure — natural finish, maple/birch hybrid; double 22" bass drums, 8"/10"/12"/14" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference Pure 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18"/19" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Series double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Jay Weinberg Signature (debut)
- **Heads:** Evans EC2 / EMAD
- **Notable:** Weinberg joined Slipknot in 2013, replacing founding drummer Joey Jordison at age 22, and recorded .5: The Gray Chapter (2014) at Shangri-La Studios with producer Greg Fidelman. The album debuted at #5 on the Billboard 200 — Slipknot's highest US chart position at the time — and Vic Firth built his first signature stick model during this stretch.

### We Are Not Your Kind Era (2018–2020)

- **Drums:** SJC Custom Drums — custom Slipknot-themed finish, maple shells; double 22"x18" bass drums, four rack toms, two floor toms
- **Snare:** Tama SLP (Sound Lab Project) 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom Series — 14" hi-hats, 17"/19" dark crashes, 20" ride, 18" China, 10" splash
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Series double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 5A
- **Heads:** Evans Hybrid / G2 Coated
- **Original setup cost (2019):** ~$6,592
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$8,446
- **Notable:** By We Are Not Your Kind (2019) — Slipknot's chart-topping, critically acclaimed fifth album — Weinberg had moved to a boutique SJC Custom Drums shell pack finished to match the band's stage aesthetic, switched to a Tama SLP snare for a more focused crack, and traded the brighter Zildjian A series for the darker K Custom series. This is the setup most associated with his nine-year Slipknot tenure and the most searched entry point for "jay weinberg slipknot drum kit" queries.

### The End, So Far Era (2021–2023)

- **Drums:** SJC Custom Drums (unchanged platform)
- **Snare:** Tama SLP 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom Series (fully established arsenal)
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Series double bass pedal, Roland trigger system added
- **Sticks:** Vater 5A
- **Heads:** Evans Hybrid / G2 Coated
- **Notable:** The End, So Far (2022) — Slipknot's most genre-expanding studio record — found Weinberg's SJC/K Custom setup fully mature, integrated with Roland triggers to keep pace with the band's hybrid acoustic/electronic live show. In December 2023, after nearly a decade behind mask #1, Slipknot announced Weinberg's departure; Eric Morotti was later confirmed as his replacement.

### Suicidal Tendencies Era (2023–present)

- **Drums:** SJC Custom Drums (OBEY x ST Collaboration Kit) — maple shells, double 22"x18" bass drums
- **Snare:** SJC Jay Weinberg "The Crucible" 14"x6.5" — 48-ply brass, signature model
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A New Beat hi-hats, A Custom crashes, K Custom ride, K China, FX Break Bell
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Series double pedal, DW 9100 Throne
- **Sticks:** Vater Jay Weinberg 908 Signature
- **Heads:** Evans Black Chrome
- **Notable:** Weinberg landed on his feet almost immediately, joining crossover thrash pioneers Suicidal Tendencies in 2023 and touring stadiums as support on Metallica's M72 World Stadium Tour. He carried his SJC platform into the new gig, now built around his first full signature snare and an expanded Zildjian cymbal setup blending his brighter A-series roots with the K Custom darkness of the Slipknot years.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2013:** Joined Slipknot, replacing founding drummer Joey Jordison; debuted on Pearl Reference Pure
- **2018:** Switched from production Pearl shells to boutique SJC Custom Drums; cymbals shifted from Zildjian A to K Custom for We Are Not Your Kind
- **2019:** Moved from Vic Firth to Vater sticks
- **2021:** Roland trigger system added for The End, So Far's hybrid live show
- **2023:** Fired from Slipknot in December; joined Suicidal Tendencies within months, debuting his first full signature snare (SJC "The Crucible") and signature stick (Vater 908)

## FAQ

**Q: What kit did Jay Weinberg use in Slipknot?**  
A: Weinberg debuted on a Pearl Reference Pure kit for .5: The Gray Chapter (2014), then switched to a boutique SJC Custom Drums shell pack finished in Slipknot's visual identity for We Are Not Your Kind (2019) and The End, So Far (2022). His cymbals shifted from Zildjian A series to the darker K Custom series alongside the SJC switch, paired with a Tama SLP snare and DW 9000 double pedal.

**Q: What drum kit does Jay Weinberg play now?**  
A: With Suicidal Tendencies, Jay Weinberg plays an SJC Custom Drums kit finished in an OBEY x Suicidal Tendencies collaboration wrap, centered on his signature SJC "The Crucible" 14"x6.5" 48-ply brass snare, an expanded Zildjian cymbal setup, DW 9000 hardware, and his own Vater 908 Signature sticks.

**Q: How much does Jay Weinberg's drum kit cost?**  
A: His We Are Not Your Kind-era rig cost approximately $6,592 in 2019 dollars. Adjusted for inflation to 2026 dollars, that's roughly $8,446 — a boutique SJC Custom shell pack plus a full Zildjian K Custom cymbal arsenal.

**Q: Who is Jay Weinberg's father?**  
A: Jay Weinberg is the son of Max Weinberg, longtime drummer for Bruce Springsteen's E Street Band. Jay filled in on drums for his father during Springsteen's 2009 "Working on a Dream" World Tour dates before building his own career through the hardcore and punk touring circuit and eventually winning Slipknot's 2013 audition.

**Q: Why did Jay Weinberg leave Slipknot?**  
A: Slipknot announced Jay Weinberg's departure in December 2023 after nine years and three studio albums (.5: The Gray Chapter, We Are Not Your Kind, and The End, So Far). Eric Morotti was later confirmed as his replacement behind mask #1. Weinberg joined Suicidal Tendencies within months of the split.
