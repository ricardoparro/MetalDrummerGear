## Gear Timeline

### Slayer Touring Era (1996-1997)

- **Drums:** Ludwig Classic Maple — 22"x16" bass drums (x2), 10"x8"/12"x9" rack toms, 14"x14"/16"x16" floor toms
- **Snare:** 14"x6.5" steel or maple shell, medium-bright tuning
- **Cymbals:** Sabian / Zildjian professional series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" china
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 or Tama Iron Cobra double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Promark or Vater 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick), Emperor Coated (toms), Coated Ambassador (snare)
- **Original setup cost (1996):** ~$3,906
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$8,156
- **Notable:** Filled in for an unavailable Paul Bostaph on short notice, learning Slayer's full Reign in Blood / South of Heaven / Seasons in the Abyss catalogue and delivering it live across the 1996-1997 touring cycle. His Ludwig Classic Maple kit gave the material a warmer, more open midrange than Dave Lombardo's Tama setup — a distinct sonic identity on Slayer's most scrutinized drum chair.

### The Gathering Era (1997-1999)

- **Drums:** Professional maple kit, optimized for Testament's studio environment
- **Cymbals:** Sabian or Zildjian professional setup
- **Estimated cost (1999):** ~$4,262
- **Notable:** Joined Testament for The Gathering (1999) — widely considered one of the band's finest albums, featuring guitar work from both Alex Skolnick and James Murphy, and the record most associated with Dette's career.

### Formation of Damnation / Dark Roots of Earth Era (2001-2012)

- **Drums:** Evolved professional kit configuration, DW 9000/Iron Cobra pedal maintained
- **Snare:** 14"x6.5" — consistent professional standard
- **Estimated cost (2008-2012):** ~$5,200-$5,800
- **Notable:** The Formation of Damnation (2008) marked Testament's Nuclear Blast-era comeback; Dark Roots of Earth (2012) continued the resurgence. In 2010, Dette also filled in for Charlie Benante on Anthrax tour dates — making him one of the few drummers to have performed with three of thrash metal's Big Four acts.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1996:** Ludwig Classic Maple kit established during Slayer touring fill-in — distinct from Lombardo's Tama and Bostaph's later DW setups
- **1999:** Testament's The Gathering — brighter, more focused tuning for Billy Sherwood's production
- **2008:** Formation of Damnation — Testament's Nuclear Blast comeback record
- **2010:** Anthrax fill-in for Charlie Benante
- **2012:** Dark Roots of Earth continues Testament's resurgence
- **Career constant:** DW 9000 or Tama Iron Cobra double bass pedal maintained across nearly two decades of touring

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Jon Dette's drum kit cost?**
A: Jon Dette's 1996 Slayer-era Ludwig Classic Maple setup cost approximately $3,906. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $8,156.

**Q: What drums did Jon Dette use with Slayer?**
A: Dette played a Ludwig Classic Maple kit during his 1996-1997 Slayer touring period — six-ply maple shells in a double 22"x16" bass drum configuration, paired with a 14"x6.5" steel or maple snare. This distinguished him from Dave Lombardo (Tama throughout his tenure) and Paul Bostaph (DW during the Final World Tour era), giving Slayer's catalogue a warmer, more open midrange character.

**Q: What is Jon Dette's drum kit setup?**
A: Dette's core setup centers on a Ludwig Classic Maple kit with Sabian or Zildjian cymbals and a DW 9000 or Tama Iron Cobra double bass pedal, playing Promark or Vater 5B sticks. His snare is a 14"x6.5" steel or maple shell tuned medium-bright for cut in thrash metal mixes.

**Q: Why is Jon Dette considered notable?**
A: Dette is one of the few drummers in thrash metal history to have performed with three of the genre's Big Four-adjacent acts: Slayer (1996-1997), Testament (1997-1999, 2001-2012), and Anthrax (2010, filling in for Charlie Benante). His ability to learn complex, demanding setlists on short notice and deliver them reliably made him the go-to fill-in specialist for thrash metal's biggest touring acts.

**Q: What Testament albums did Jon Dette record?**
A: Dette recorded three studio albums with Testament: The Gathering (1999), The Formation of Damnation (2008), and Dark Roots of Earth (2012). The Gathering is widely considered one of Testament's finest records and is the album most associated with Dette's career.
