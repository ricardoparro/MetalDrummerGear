## Gear Timeline

### Terra Incognita Era (1996–2004)

- **Drums:** Pearl Export Series — poplar shells, dual 22"x16" kicks, 10"/12"/13" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Pearl Sensitone Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** Pearl P-2002C Eliminator double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Original setup cost (2001):** ~$3,000
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$5,550
- **Notable:** Gojira (then Godzilla) built their sound in Bayonne, France on a budget Pearl Export kit. The dual-22" kick configuration was established from day one and became Duplantier's signature foundation, heard throughout Terra Incognita (2001) and The Link (2003).

### From Mars to Sirius Era (2005–2008)

- **Drums:** Gretsch USA Custom — maple shells, dual 22"x18" kicks, 10"/12"/13"/16"/18" toms
- **Snare:** Gretsch USA Custom 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Paiste 2002 — 14" Heavy hi-hats, 18"/19" crashes, 22" ride, 20" China
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 Turbo double pedal
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor
- **Original setup cost (2005):** ~$9,000
- **Notable:** Duplantier's first major endorsement. From Mars to Sirius (2005) and The Way of All Flesh (2008, Grammy-nominated) put Gojira on the global extreme metal map, and the Gretsch maple shells gave the warm, complex resonance behind tom showcases like "Flying Whales."

### L'Enfant Sauvage Era (2009–2015)

- **Drums:** Gretsch USA Custom (expanded) — dual 22"x18" kicks, 8"/10"/12"/13"/16"/18" toms
- **Snare:** Gretsch USA Custom 14"x6.5" (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance — 14" Traditional hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 double pedal
- **Heads:** Evans G2 (toms), EMAD (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2012):** ~$12,000
- **Notable:** Switched from Paiste 2002 to Meinl Byzance for darker, more complex tonal character suited to L'Enfant Sauvage's (2012) blend of brutality and atmosphere — widely considered Gojira's finest album.

### Fortitude Era (2016–present)

- **Drums:** Sonor SQ2 — fully custom maple/beech shells, dual 22"x18" kicks, 10"/12"/13"/16"/18" toms
- **Snare:** Sonor SQ2 14"x6.5" custom maple
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance — 14" Traditional hi-hats, 17"/18"/19" crashes, 21" Vintage ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide double pedal
- **Sticks:** Tama Mario Duplantier Signature (first signature stick deal)
- **Heads:** Ahead Drumheads
- **Original setup cost (2021):** ~$18,000
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$21,762
- **Notable:** Gojira's modern era — headlining Download Festival, supporting Metallica at stadiums, and performing at the Paris Olympics 2024 opening ceremony. The custom Sonor SQ2, specified per shell, is Duplantier's most personal and refined kit to date.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2001:** Terra Incognita recorded on a budget Pearl Export kit — dual-22" kick configuration established
- **2005:** First major endorsement — switch to Gretsch USA Custom for From Mars to Sirius breakthrough
- **2012:** Cymbal switch from Paiste 2002 to Meinl Byzance for L'Enfant Sauvage's dynamic range
- **2016:** Switch to fully custom Sonor SQ2 kit
- **2021:** First Tama signature stick model launched alongside Fortitude
- **Career constant:** Dual 22" kick configuration, maintained from his earliest budget kit through his current custom Sonor rig

## FAQ

**Q: What is Mario Duplantier's drum setup worth?**  
A: Mario Duplantier's original 2001 Pearl Export setup — the budget kit behind Gojira's Terra Incognita — cost approximately $3,000. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $5,550. His current custom Sonor SQ2 rig, paired with Meinl Byzance cymbals and Tama signature sticks, cost approximately $18,000 when assembled around 2021, equivalent to roughly $21,762 today.

**Q: What drums did Mario Duplantier use in Gojira?**  
A: Across nearly three decades with Gojira, Duplantier progressed from a budget Pearl Export kit (Terra Incognita, 2001) to a Gretsch USA Custom maple kit (From Mars to Sirius and L'Enfant Sauvage, 2005–2015) to his current fully custom Sonor SQ2 setup (Magma and Fortitude, 2016–present). The dual-22" kick configuration has remained constant throughout.

**Q: What cymbals does Mario Duplantier use?**  
A: Duplantier used Paiste 2002 cymbals through Gojira's early and breakthrough years before switching to Meinl Byzance around L'Enfant Sauvage (2012) for a darker, more complex tonal character — a relationship he has maintained through Fortitude and into Gojira's current stadium-headlining era.

**Q: Does Mario Duplantier have a signature drumstick?**  
A: Yes — Tama launched the Mario Duplantier Signature stick model around 2016–2017, designed around his aggressive attack and the tribal tom-pattern vocabulary that defines his playing.
