# Matt Halpern Drum Kit Gear History — Periphery

## Gear Timeline

### Self-Titled Debut Era (2010)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series — maple shells with HVX bearing edges; 22"×18" single bass drum, 10"×8" and 12"×9" racks, 14"×14" and 16"×16" floors
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series Maple Snare 14"×6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX series — 14" Groove Hi-Hats, HHX Evolution crashes (17"/18"/19"), 21" Evolution Ride, 18" HHX Chinese
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive / Tama Speed Cobra (rotated)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Matt Halpern Signature (established by 2010)
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD2 (kick), G2 Coated (toms), UV1 (snare)
- **Notable:** Released April 20, 2010 on Sumerian Records, Periphery's self-titled debut codified djent as a genre. Halpern's single-kick discipline on a 22"×18" DW bass drum forced rhythmically intricate kick writing rather than relying on double bass to fill space.

### Periphery II Transitional Era (2012)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series (continued)
- **Snare:** DW Collector's Series Maple Snare (evolved configuration)
- **Cymbals:** Transition from Sabian HHX to Meinl Byzance begins
- **Pedal:** DW hardware (continued)
- **Notable:** "Periphery II: This Time It's Personal" (2012) marked the start of Halpern's shift away from Sabian toward the dark, complex Meinl Byzance voice that would define his sound from this point forward.

### Select Difficulty Era (2016) — Grammy-Nominated

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn Series — maple/walnut hybrid shells with SONIClear bearing edges; 22"×18" kick, 10"×7" and 12"×8" racks, 14"×14" and 16"×16" floors
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther Design Lab 14"×6" (cherry/brass/maple options)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series (Extra Dry, Dual, Traditional, Brilliant) — full setup established
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 Double Pedal, Mapex Falcon hi-hat stand, Mapex Saddle Throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Matt Halpern Signature
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD2 (kick), G2 Coated (toms), UV1 (snare)
- **Original setup cost (2016):** ~$10,398
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$14,200
- **Notable:** "Periphery III: Select Difficulty" earned a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance for "The Price Is Wrong" at the 59th Grammy Awards — the first Grammy nomination for any djent-identified act. This is the album where Halpern's full Mapex Saturn + Meinl Byzance setup reached peak integration.

### Hail Stan / Aliens Era (2019–2023)

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn V MH Exotic — succeeded the standard Saturn configuration
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther Design Lab (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance (mature, complete arsenal)
- **Pedal:** DW 9000, Mapex Falcon hardware (consistent)
- **Notable:** "Periphery IV: Hail Stan" (2019) and "Periphery V: Aliens" (2023) showcase Halpern's most mature Mapex Saturn V MH Exotic configuration — a flagship-tier kit reflecting his fully developed signature endorsement relationship.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2010:** DW Collector's Series + Sabian HHX on the genre-defining self-titled debut
- **2012:** Transition begins from Sabian HHX to Meinl Byzance on "Periphery II"
- **~2014:** Full Mapex endorsement begins, succeeding DW
- **2016:** Mapex Saturn + Mapex Black Panther Design Lab signature snare fully established — Grammy-nominated "Select Difficulty" era
- **2019–2023:** Upgrade to Mapex Saturn V MH Exotic for "Hail Stan" and "Aliens"

## FAQ

**Q: What gear does Matt Halpern use?**  
A: Matt Halpern currently plays a Mapex Saturn V MH Exotic drum kit with a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab signature snare, a comprehensive Meinl Byzance cymbal setup, DW 9000 double pedals, and Vic Firth Matt Halpern Signature sticks. His gear evolved from a DW Collector's Series + Sabian HHX setup on Periphery's 2010 debut through a full Mapex/Meinl transition completed by 2016.

**Q: What is Matt Halpern's signature snare?**  
A: Matt Halpern's primary recording snare since 2016 has been the Mapex Black Panther Design Lab — a 14"×6" snare offered in cherry, brass, steel, and maple shell options, giving him flexibility to match snare character to musical context. It succeeded a DW Collector's Series maple snare used on Periphery's earlier albums.

**Q: How much does Matt Halpern's drum kit cost?**  
A: Matt Halpern's 2016 "Select Difficulty" Mapex Saturn setup cost approximately $10,398, equivalent to roughly $14,200 in 2026 after inflation. The Mapex Saturn shell pack alone runs $4,500–$7,000, the Black Panther Design Lab snare adds $450–$800, and the full Meinl Byzance cymbal setup runs $2,500–$3,500.

**Q: Was Periphery III: Select Difficulty nominated for a Grammy?**  
A: Yes. "Periphery III: Select Difficulty" (2016) earned a Grammy nomination for Best Metal Performance at the 59th Grammy Awards in 2017 for the track "The Price Is Wrong" — the first Grammy nomination for any djent-identified act.

**Q: What cymbals does Matt Halpern use?**  
A: Matt Halpern uses Meinl Byzance cymbals spanning the Extra Dry, Dual, Traditional, and Brilliant lines, including 14" Dual Hi-Hats, Extra Dry and Traditional crashes from 18" to 20", a 22" Extra Dry Medium Ride, and an 18" Brilliant China. The handcrafted B20 bronze Byzance line has been his cymbal voice since the Periphery II transitional era around 2012.
