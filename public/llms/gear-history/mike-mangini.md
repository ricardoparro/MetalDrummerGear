## Gear Timeline

### A Dramatic Turn of Events Era (2010–2016)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masterworks Custom — 6-piece custom shell pack; 22"x18" bass drum, 10"x8"/12"x9" rack toms, 14"x14"/16"x16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass Snare 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Traditional — 14" hi-hats, 16" thin crash, 18"/20" medium crashes, 22" medium ride, 18" china, 10" splash
- **Pedals:** DW 9002 double bass pedal, DW 9000 Series hardware, DW 9120M throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Mike Mangini Signature
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick), Emperor Clear (toms), Coated Ambassador (snare)
- **Original setup cost (2011):** ~$12,356
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$17,999
- **Notable:** Mangini won a highly publicized audition to replace founding drummer Mike Portnoy in 2010 and debuted on A Dramatic Turn of Events (2011) — an album built around his own technical vocabulary. He briefly swapped in Ludwig Keystone X Maple shells for the band's 2013 self-titled album before returning to Pearl for The Astonishing (2016), keeping the Meinl/DW hardware combination throughout.

### Distance Over Time / A View from the Top of the World Era (2019–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure — hybrid maple shell pack
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference Brass Snare (14"x5" and 14"x6.5" options)
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX/AAX Series — 14" HHX Evolution Hi-Hats, 17"/18"/19" Evolution Crashes, 21" HHX Raw Bell Dry Ride, 18" AAX Chinese, 10" AAX Splash
- **Pedals:** Pearl Eliminator Redline double pedal, Pearl H-2050 hi-hat stand, Pearl BC-830 boom stands, Pearl Roadster D-790 throne
- **Electronics:** Roland SPD-SX sampling pad for hybrid passages
- **Notable:** Starting with Distance Over Time (2019), Mangini made a wholesale switch away from the Meinl/DW combination he'd used since joining the band — moving to Pearl Reference Pure shells, Sabian HHX/AAX cymbals, and Pearl's own Eliminator Redline pedal. The same rig carried through A View from the Top of the World (2021), with a Roland SPD-SX added for hybrid acoustic/electronic work.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2010:** Joined Dream Theater, replacing founding drummer Mike Portnoy; assembled Pearl Masterworks/Meinl Byzance/DW rig
- **2013:** Temporary switch to Ludwig Keystone X Maple shells for Dream Theater's self-titled album
- **2016:** Returned to Pearl (Masterworks Maple) for The Astonishing; Meinl/DW hardware unchanged
- **2019:** Major switch to Pearl Reference Pure shells, Sabian HHX/AAX cymbals, and Pearl Eliminator Redline pedal — retiring the DW hardware entirely
- **2021:** Added Roland SPD-SX sampling pad for A View from the Top of the World's hybrid passages
- **Career-long constant:** Vic Firth Mike Mangini Signature sticks across every era

## FAQ

**Q: What drums did Mike Mangini use on A Dramatic Turn of Events?**  
A: Mike Mangini played a custom Pearl Masterworks kit on A Dramatic Turn of Events (2011), paired with Meinl Byzance Traditional cymbals, a Pearl Free-Floating Brass Snare, and DW 9002 double pedal and hardware. The complete setup cost approximately $12,356 in 2011 dollars — equivalent to about $17,999 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: What drum kit does Mike Mangini play now?**  
A: Mike Mangini currently plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit with Sabian HHX/AAX cymbals and a Pearl Reference Brass snare. He switched to this setup starting with Distance Over Time (2019), moving away from the Meinl Byzance/DW combination he'd used since joining Dream Theater in 2010, and has kept it through A View from the Top of the World (2021).

**Q: How much would Mike Mangini's 2011 Dream Theater drum kit cost today?**  
A: The original 2011 Pearl Masterworks Custom setup cost approximately $12,356. Adjusted for inflation to 2026 dollars, that's roughly $17,999 — reflecting general CPI inflation on the fully custom Masterworks shell pack, Meinl Byzance cymbal set, and DW 9000-series hardware Mangini used for his Dream Theater debut.

**Q: Did Mike Mangini always use Pearl drums with Dream Theater?**  
A: Almost — he used Pearl Masterworks kits from his 2011 debut through 2016, with one exception: a temporary switch to Ludwig Keystone X Maple shells for the band's 2013 self-titled album. Since 2019's Distance Over Time, he has played Pearl Reference Pure shells alongside Sabian cymbals instead of his earlier Meinl Byzance setup.
