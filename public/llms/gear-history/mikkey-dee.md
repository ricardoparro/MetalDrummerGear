## Gear Timeline

### King Diamond Era (1988–1992)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Professional — maple shells, 22" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating 14"x5.5" steel
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Pearl P-2002C Eliminator Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Estimated cost (1988):** ~$5,000
- **Notable:** Dee joined King Diamond's band in 1988, appearing on Them (1988), Conspiracy (1989), and The Eye (1990). This was his professional apprenticeship — technically demanding melodic metal with theatrical staging that taught him the discipline of consistent nightly performance.

### Early Motörhead Era (1993–2004)

- **Drums:** Premier Signia — birch/maple shells, 22"x18" kick, 10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Premier Signia Steel 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A / A New Beat Series
- **Pedal:** Premier / DW 5000 Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Original setup cost (1993):** ~$4,118
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$9,336
- **Notable:** Dee joined Motörhead in 1992, replacing Phil Taylor ahead of Bastards (1993). He switched from Pearl to Premier — a British-built rig whose punchy character matched Lemmy's raw rock 'n' roll aesthetic — carrying the setup through Sacrifice (1995), Overnight Sensation (1996), Snake Bite Love (1998), and We Are Motörhead (2000).

### Late Motörhead / Bad Magic Era (2004–2015)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series — maple/birch hybrid, 22"x18" kick, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom / K Custom Dark Series
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature
- **Heads:** Evans G2 / EMAD
- **Estimated cost (2015):** ~$18,000
- **Notable:** Dee's final and most refined Motörhead chapter — Kiss of Death (2006), Motörizer (2008), The Wörld Is Yours (2010), Aftershock (2013), and Bad Magic (2015), which won Motörhead's only Grammy ("Thunder & Lightning") weeks before Lemmy Kilmister's death on December 28, 2015.

### Scorpions Era (2016–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series (refined) — maple/birch hybrid, 22"x18" kick, 8"/10"/12"/14"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel/Maple 14"x6.5" (alternates by material)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K Custom / K Hybrid Series
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature (refined)
- **Heads:** Evans G2 / EMAD2
- **Estimated cost (2022):** ~$22,000
- **Notable:** Following Lemmy's death and Motörhead's dissolution, Dee joined Scorpions in 2016, appearing on Rock Believer (2022) — continuing the Pearl Reference/Evans platform now adapted for Scorpions' broader dynamic range, with darker K Custom/K Hybrid cymbal voicing.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1993:** Switched from Pearl Masters Professional to Premier Signia when joining Motörhead
- **~2004:** Switched back to Pearl, adopting the Pearl Reference Series and Evans heads for Motörhead's final decade
- **Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature** sticks launched during the late Motörhead era, reflecting his stature as one of hard rock's most recognized drummers
- **2016:** Joined Scorpions post-Motörhead, continuing the Pearl Reference platform with darker Zildjian K Custom/K Hybrid cymbals
- **Cost growth:** From a ~$4,118 Premier Signia debut rig in 1993 to a ~$22,000 Pearl Reference setup by the Scorpions era — one of the longest, most consistent gear evolutions in metal drumming

## FAQ

**Q: How much did Mikkey Dee's drum kit cost on Bastards?**
A: Mikkey Dee's 1993 Premier Signia setup behind Motörhead's Bastards — his debut album with the band — cost approximately $4,118, equivalent to roughly $9,336 today after inflation.

**Q: What drum kit does Mikkey Dee play now?**
A: With Scorpions, Mikkey Dee plays a Pearl Reference Series kit with a Pearl Free-Floating steel/maple snare, Zildjian K Custom/K Hybrid cymbals, a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal, and Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature sticks — a refined continuation of the setup he developed in Motörhead's final decade.

**Q: What drums did Mikkey Dee use before Motörhead?**
A: Before joining Motörhead in 1992, Dee spent four years with King Diamond's band (1988–1992) playing a Pearl Masters Professional kit with a Pearl Free-Floating snare and Zildjian A Series cymbals.

**Q: Why did Mikkey Dee switch drum brands during his Motörhead tenure?**
A: Dee switched from Pearl to Premier Signia when he joined Motörhead in 1993 for a punchier, more driven British-built tone matching Lemmy Kilmister's rock 'n' roll aesthetic, then switched back to Pearl Reference around 2004 for a fuller, more resonant sound suited to Motörhead's growing arena and festival stages.

**Q: How long was Mikkey Dee Motörhead's drummer?**
A: Mikkey Dee played drums for Motörhead for 23 years, from Bastards (1993) until the band's dissolution following Lemmy Kilmister's death on December 28, 2015 — one of the longest unbroken tenures of any drummer with a single major metal band.
