## Gear Timeline

### Early Death Metal Era (1988–1993)

- **Drums:** Pearl Export — 5-piece; 22"x16" kick, 12"x10" rack, 13"x11" rack, 16"x16" floor
- **Snare:** Pearl Sensitone Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16" crash, 18" crash, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B Hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Ambassador (toms), Coated Ambassador (snare), Powerstroke P3 (kick)
- **Original setup cost (1992):** ~$1,748
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$4,082
- **Notable:** The Pearl Export was the workhorse of choice for early death metal drummers — affordable, durable, and with a bright poplar tone that cut through dense, downtuned guitar arrangements. Mazurkiewicz's unrelenting blast-beat precision on Tomb of the Mutilated (1992) and Butchered at Birth (1991) used this straightforward setup to devastating effect. The DW 5000 double pedal was industry-standard for extreme metal at the time.

### The Bleeding / Vile Era (1994–1996)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Custom — upgraded maple shell pack
- **Snare:** Pearl Sensitone Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series (expanded — added second crash)
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Notable:** As Cannibal Corpse's profile grew through the mid-1990s, Mazurkiewicz upgraded within the Pearl family to the Masters Custom line. The shift from poplar Export shells to maple Masters shells brought a warmer, fuller tone without sacrificing the brightness that defined early Cannibal Corpse production. The Bleeding (1994) and Vile (1996) represent a transitional sound as the band refined its approach to extreme death metal.

### Gallery of Suicide / Bloodthirst Era (1998–2002)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Custom — professional multi-wood shell pack
- **Snare:** Pearl Sensitone Elite Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A and A Custom mixed setup
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 double pedal (upgraded to heavier spring)
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Notable:** The Pearl Reference Custom represented a significant upgrade — its multi-wood construction combined maple, birch, and mahogany layers for a tonal complexity previously unavailable. Mazurkiewicz's drumming on Bloodthirst (1999) and Gore Obsessed (2002) benefited from the Reference Custom's enhanced projection and low-end definition, critical for Cannibal Corpse's increasingly produced studio sound.

### Kill / Evisceration Plague Era (2006–2010)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters MCX — maple and birch hybrid shell pack
- **Snare:** Pearl Masters Steel Free-Floating 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom Series — 14" hi-hats, 17" crash, 19" crash, 21" ride
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B American Classic
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Powerstroke 3 (kick), Controlled Sound (snare)
- **Notable:** By the Kill era (2006), Mazurkiewicz had settled into the Pearl Masters MCX — a professional-grade kit balancing warmth and projection. The upgrade to DW 9000 pedals gave his double-kick work greater consistency on extended touring schedules. Kill and Evisceration Plague marked the peak of Cannibal Corpse's commercial period with Century Media, placing increased demands on Mazurkiewicz's live rig.

### Current Setup (2015–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Premium — flagship Pearl maple shell pack
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom — 14" hi-hats, 17" crash, 19" crash, 22" medium ride
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B American Classic
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor (toms), Powerstroke Pro (kick), Controlled Sound Black Dot (snare)
- **Notable:** Mazurkiewicz's current rig represents the full arc of Pearl endorsement — from entry-level Export to the Masters Premium flagship. After nearly 35 years with Cannibal Corpse, his setup prioritises reliability and consistency for marathon tours supporting albums like Violence Unimagined (2021) and Chaos Horrific (2023).

## Key Gear Changes

- **1988 → 1994:** Pearl Export to Pearl Masters Custom — first upgrade within Pearl family as Cannibal Corpse grew from underground to major death metal force
- **1994 → 1998:** Pearl Masters Custom to Pearl Reference Custom — continued endorsement deepening as the band signed with Metal Blade
- **2006 → 2010:** DW 5000 to DW 9000 double pedal — upgraded within DW family for extended touring demands
- **2010s:** Pearl Masters MCX to Pearl Masters Premium — moved to Pearl's flagship line as endorsement deepened
- **Cymbal consistency:** Zildjian A series maintained throughout career — one of death metal's longest continuous cymbal endorsements

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Paul Mazurkiewicz's drum kit cost?**  
A: Paul Mazurkiewicz's current Pearl Masters Premium setup costs approximately $6,000–$8,000 for the full rig including cymbals and hardware. His iconic 1992 Tomb of the Mutilated-era Pearl Export setup cost ~$1,748 at the time, equivalent to ~$4,082 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: What brand does Paul Mazurkiewicz endorse?**  
A: Paul Mazurkiewicz is a long-standing Pearl Drums endorser, having used Pearl kits throughout his entire career with Cannibal Corpse from 1988 to present. He also endorses Zildjian cymbals, DW pedals, Vic Firth sticks, and Remo drumheads.

**Q: What is Paul Mazurkiewicz's most expensive piece of gear?**  
A: Paul Mazurkiewicz's most expensive piece of gear is his Pearl Masters Premium shell pack, which retails for approximately $4,500–$5,500 depending on configuration and finish. The Pearl Free-Floating Steel snare (approximately $600–$800) and Zildjian A Custom cymbal set (approximately $1,200–$1,800) represent the other major investments in his current rig.

**Q: What drum kit did Paul Mazurkiewicz use on Tomb of the Mutilated?**

**Q: When did Paul Mazurkiewicz switch from Pearl Export to Pearl Masters?**  
A: Mazurkiewicz transitioned from the Pearl Export to the Pearl Masters Custom around 1993–1994, during the sessions leading to The Bleeding. He then progressed through Pearl Reference Custom, Masters MCX, and eventually the Masters Premium as his endorsement deepened.
