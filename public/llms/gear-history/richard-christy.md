## Gear Timeline

### Death / The Sound of Perseverance Era (1996–1998)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Custom — maple shells, 22"x18" kick, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Maple 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AA Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" AA Rock Crash, 20" AA Ride
- **Pedal:** Pearl double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Heads:** Remo Pinstripe / coated Ambassador
- **Original setup cost (1998):** ~$3,202
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$6,435
- **Notable:** Chuck Schuldiner handpicked Christy after an audition in 1996, entrusting him with Death's most technically demanding album, The Sound of Perseverance (1998). His Pearl Masters Custom maple kit and lighter 5A sticks were chosen for warmth and articulation under Morrisound Recording's dry, exacting production — priorities that reflected Schuldiner's preference for musicianship over gear spectacle.

### Control Denied / The Fragile Art of Existence Era (1999)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Custom (consistent configuration)
- **Snare:** Pearl Maple 14"x6.5" (unchanged)
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AA Series (unchanged)
- **Pedal:** Pearl double pedal (unchanged)
- **Estimated cost (1999):** ~$3,400
- **Notable:** Christy carried the same Pearl/Sabian setup into Control Denied's The Fragile Art of Existence, released posthumously after Chuck Schuldiner's death in 2001 — a progressive metal showcase that further demonstrated his technical range beyond pure death metal.

### Iced Earth Era (2003–2007)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Custom-derived setup, adapted for touring
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AA (carried over)
- **Pedal:** Pearl double pedal (carried over)
- **Estimated cost (2004):** ~$4,200
- **Notable:** Christy joined Iced Earth in 2003, adapting his technical death metal foundation to Jon Schaffer's more structured power/thrash arrangements on The Glorious Burden (2004) and the Overture of the Wicked EP (2006), before leaving in 2007 to focus on his entertainment career with The Howard Stern Show.

## FAQ

**Q: How much did Richard Christy's drum kit cost on The Sound of Perseverance?**
A: Richard Christy's 1998 Pearl Masters Custom setup behind Death's The Sound of Perseverance cost approximately $3,202, equivalent to roughly $6,435 today after inflation.

**Q: What drums did Richard Christy use with Death?**
A: Richard Christy played a Pearl Masters Custom maple kit with a Pearl 14"x6.5" maple snare, Sabian AA cymbals, a Pearl double pedal, and Vic Firth American Classic 5A sticks on Death's The Sound of Perseverance (1998).

**Q: Why did Chuck Schuldiner pick Richard Christy for Death?**
A: Chuck Schuldiner handpicked Christy after an audition in 1996, drawn to his technical precision and musicality. Schuldiner later called him one of the best drummers he ever worked with, entrusting him with Death's most technically demanding material on The Sound of Perseverance.

**Q: What gear did Richard Christy use after Death?**
A: After Death disbanded in 2001, Christy carried a similar Pearl Masters Custom/Sabian AA setup into Control Denied's The Fragile Art of Existence (1999, recorded prior) and then into Iced Earth starting in 2003, adapting his technical death metal foundation to Jon Schaffer's power/thrash arrangements.

**Q: Does Richard Christy still play drums professionally?**
A: Christy left Iced Earth in 2007 to focus on his entertainment career, becoming a prominent member of The Howard Stern Show. His metal drumming legacy remains centered on Death's The Sound of Perseverance and Control Denied's The Fragile Art of Existence.
