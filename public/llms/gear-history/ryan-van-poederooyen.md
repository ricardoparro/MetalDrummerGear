## Gear Timeline

### Early Devin Townsend Band Era (1999–2008)

- **Drums:** Single-kick maple/mahogany shell pack, 22" kick, 10"/12" rack toms, 16"/18" floor toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating steel snare, 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride
- **Pedal:** Single chain-drive pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo coated Ambassador
- **Estimated cost (1999–2008):** ~$2,200
- **Notable:** Joined Devin Townsend's band in 1999, playing a modest single-kick setup during the Physicist (2000) and Terria (2001) touring era.

### Devin Townsend Project Launch (2009–2011)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series — double-bass maple/mahogany hybrid shells, two 22"x18" kicks, 10"/12" rack toms, 16"/18" floor toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"x6.5" Brass
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX & HHX Series — 14" AAX Hi-Hats, 16" AAX Crash, 18" HHX Crash, 20" HHX Ride, 19" AAXtreme China
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal, Pearl D-3000 Throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo coated Ambassador (batter), Diplomat (snare side)
- **Estimated cost (2009–2011):** ~$5,200
- **Notable:** DTP's Ki/Addicted/Deconstruction/Ghost cycle began — Van Poederooyen upgraded to a true double-bass Pearl Reference configuration to handle the project's dramatically expanded dynamic range.

### Deconstruction Era (2011–2019)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series, double-bass configuration
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"x6.5" Brass, free-floating
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX & HHX Series — mixed bright/dark configuration
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal, Pearl D-3000 Throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo coated Ambassador, Diplomat
- **Original setup cost (2011):** ~$7,848
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$11,432
- **Notable:** Deconstruction (2011) — released the same year as the ambient companion record Ghost — is widely cited among prog-metal drummers as one of the genre's most demanding drum performances, recorded on the definitive Pearl Reference / Sabian AAX & HHX rig.

### Empath / Current Setup (2019–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series, mature double-bass configuration
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"x6.5" Brass, maintained
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX & HHX Series
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal, Pearl D-3000 Throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo coated Ambassador, Diplomat
- **Estimated cost (current):** ~$9,600
- **Notable:** Setup matured for Empath (2019), Townsend's most genre-spanning record spanning opera, jazz, folk, and extreme metal elements — Van Poederooyen's versatility remains central to the collaboration's 25-plus-year run.

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Ryan Van Poederooyen's drum kit cost?**
A: Ryan Van Poederooyen's 2011 Deconstruction-era Pearl Reference setup cost approximately $7,848. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $11,432. His current setup is valued at approximately $9,600 at current retail.

**Q: What drums does Ryan Van Poederooyen play?**
A: Ryan Van Poederooyen plays a Pearl Reference Series double-bass kit, using a maple/mahogany hybrid shell construction that delivers immediate attack for fast double-kick articulation alongside the warmth needed across Devin Townsend's extreme dynamic range.

**Q: What cymbals does Ryan Van Poederooyen use?**
A: Ryan Van Poederooyen plays a mixed Sabian AAX & HHX Series setup — the bright, focused AAX line for dense articulation and the darker, more complex HHX series for Townsend's ambient and progressive material, all on the same kit.

**Q: What band is Ryan Van Poederooyen the drummer for?**
A: Ryan Van Poederooyen has been Devin Townsend's drummer since 1999, spanning the Devin Townsend Band, the Devin Townsend Project (2009–2018), and Townsend's current solo output. Deconstruction (2011) is widely cited as one of the most demanding drum performances in progressive metal.
