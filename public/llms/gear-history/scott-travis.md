## Gear Timeline

### Painkiller Era (1990)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters/Custom Series — twin 22"x18" bass drums, 10"/12" rack toms, 14"/16" floor toms, maple shells
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Brass 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" A hi-hats, 16"/18" A crashes, 20" A ride, 18" china
- **Pedal:** Pearl P-2002 chain-drive twin pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Clear (toms), Remo Ambassador Coated (snare)
- **Original setup cost (1990):** ~$5,976
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$14,979
- **Notable:** Travis's first full studio album as Judas Priest's drummer. "Painkiller" is widely ranked among the greatest metal drum performances ever recorded, built on relentless sixteenth-note double-bass patterns that his twin-kick Pearl setup was built to deliver.

### Angel of Retribution Era (2005)

- **Notable:** Rob Halford's reunion with Judas Priest after the Ripper Owens years. Travis continued on his Pearl/Zildjian platform through this period.

### Firepower / Invincible Shield Era (2018–2024)

- **Drums:** ddrum Dominion Series
- **Snare:** ddrum Scott Travis Signature 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series (14" RUDE hi-hats, 18"/19" RUDE crashes, 22" RUDE Power Ride, 18" RUDE china)
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Series Double Pedal, Gibraltar Rack System
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Remo
- **Notable:** Travis's current verified setup — a ddrum Dominion Series kit built around his own ddrum Scott Travis Signature snare, with Paiste RUDE/2002 cymbals replacing his earlier Zildjian A Series and a DW 9000 double pedal driving the double bass. This is the rig heard on "Firepower" (2018) and "Invincible Shield" (2024), Judas Priest's two highest-charting albums.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1989:** Joins Judas Priest, replacing Dave Holland
- **1990:** Records "Painkiller" on a Pearl Masters/Custom twin-kick maple kit with Zildjian A Series cymbals
- **2018–2024:** Switches to ddrum Dominion Series with his own ddrum Scott Travis Signature snare, Paiste RUDE/2002 cymbals, and a DW 9000 double pedal
- **Career constant:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks used from Painkiller through today

## FAQ

**Q: How much does Scott Travis's drum kit cost?**  
A: Scott Travis's original 1990 Pearl/Zildjian setup — the rig behind Judas Priest's landmark "Painkiller" — cost approximately $5,976. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $14,979. His current ddrum Dominion Series/Paiste rig, built around his own signature snare, carries a comparable modern equivalent cost.

**Q: What brand of drums does Scott Travis use?**  
A: Scott Travis currently plays a ddrum Dominion Series kit with his own ddrum Scott Travis Signature snare, Paiste RUDE and 2002 Series cymbals, a DW 9000 Series double pedal, and Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. Early in his Judas Priest tenure, on 1990's "Painkiller," he played a Pearl Masters/Custom kit with Zildjian A Series cymbals.

**Q: Is Scott Travis's drum setup affordable?**  
A: Travis's Painkiller-era Pearl setup (~$5,976 in 1990) was a solidly professional but not extravagant configuration. His current ddrum Dominion Series kit, built around his own signature snare model, costs more — reflecting a signature-artist premium rather than an entry-level or budget rig.

**Q: What drums did Scott Travis use on Painkiller?**  
A: Scott Travis played a Pearl Masters/Custom Series twin-kick maple kit with a Pearl Free-Floating brass snare, Zildjian A Series cymbals, and a Pearl P-2002 chain-drive twin pedal on Judas Priest's "Painkiller" (1990).
