## Gear Timeline

### Pre-Godsmack Era — Wrathchild America / Souls at Zero / Amen (1985–2001)

- **Drums:** Tama Rockstar/Swingstar Series — 22" kick, 12"/13"/16" toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Steel Snare 14"x5.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AA Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** Tama hardware with budget double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 5B
- **Notable:** Larkin's long pre-Godsmack career spanned Maryland's Wrathchild America, alternative metal acts Souls at Zero and Amen, and a stint in Ugly Kid Joe. His Sabian and Vater relationships — both of which carried through the rest of his career — began during this period.

### Faceless Era (2002–2007)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Performer MX — maple/birch hybrid; 22"x18" kick, 10"/12"/14" rack toms, 16" floor tom
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Performer MX 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX Series — 14" hi-hats, 16"/18" crashes, 21" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** Tama Iron Cobra HP900 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater Shannon Larkin Signature (first signature model)
- **Heads:** Evans G2 (toms), EMAD (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2002):** ~$8,250
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$15,023
- **Notable:** Larkin's debut with Godsmack. Faceless (2003) established him as the backbone of one of rock radio's most powerful bands, and the Tama Starclassic Performer MX became his signature sound — punchy, focused, and built for arenas.

### The Oracle / 1000hp Era (2009–2015)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple — pure maple shells, custom finish
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX/AAX mix
- **Notable:** Godsmack's commercial peak. Larkin upgraded from Performer MX to pure Starclassic Maple — a warmer, more resonant shell suited to the band's evolving, more melodic direction on The Oracle (2010) and 1000hp (2014).

### When Legends Rise Era (2016–2024)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch — hybrid shells combining walnut warmth with birch attack
- **Pedal:** Tama Speed Cobra HP910 double pedal
- **Cymbals:** Fully HHX-based Sabian setup
- **Electronics:** Roland SPD-SX sampling pad added for arena-quality live consistency
- **Notable:** Larkin's most refined Godsmack-era setup, carried through When Legends Rise (2018) and Lighting Up the Sky (2023). He exited Godsmack in 2024 after a 22-year tenure — the longest of any Godsmack drummer.

### Current Era — ddrum Dios Series

- **Drums:** ddrum Dios Series
- **Snare:** ddrum Dios 14"x6.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX & HHX Series — 14" AAX Stage Hi-Hats, 18"/19" AAX X-Plosion Crashes, 21" HHX Raw Bell Dry Ride, 10" AAX Splash, 18" AAX China
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 Series double pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B
- **Heads:** Evans
- **Notable:** Larkin's verified current setup is a ddrum Dios Series kit — a different chapter from the Tama/Vater rig that defined his Godsmack breakthrough, but built on the same hard-hitting, groove-first foundation. His Sabian relationship, now decades long, remains the one constant across his entire career.

## Key Gear Changes

- **2002:** Joined Godsmack — Tama Starclassic Performer MX endorsement and first Vater signature stick launched
- **~2010:** Tama Starclassic Performer MX → Tama Starclassic Maple — warmer tone for The Oracle's melodic direction
- **~2018:** Tama Starclassic Maple → Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch — most praised sound of his Godsmack career
- **2024:** Exits Godsmack after a 22-year tenure
- **Current:** ddrum Dios Series with Sabian AAX/HHX cymbals and Vic Firth sticks
- **Career constant:** Sabian cymbal endorsement, maintained from his pre-Godsmack years through today

## FAQ

**Q: What is Shannon Larkin's drum setup worth?**  
A: Shannon Larkin's original 2002 Tama Starclassic Performer MX setup — the rig behind Godsmack's Faceless and IV — cost approximately $8,250. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that's equivalent to roughly $15,023. His current verified ddrum Dios Series kit, with Sabian AAX/HHX cymbals and a DW 9000 double pedal, represents a modern equivalent setup worth approximately $6,000–$7,000 at current retail prices.

**Q: What drums did Shannon Larkin use in Godsmack?**  
A: Across his 22-year Godsmack tenure (2002–2024), Shannon Larkin played a progression of Tama Starclassic kits: the Performer MX (maple/birch hybrid) on Faceless and IV, Starclassic Maple on The Oracle and 1000hp, and Starclassic Walnut/Birch on When Legends Rise and Lighting Up the Sky. He paired all three with Sabian cymbals throughout.

**Q: What drums does Shannon Larkin play now?**  
A: Shannon Larkin's current verified kit is a ddrum Dios Series setup with a ddrum Dios 14"x6.5" maple snare, Sabian AAX & HHX cymbals, a DW 9000 Series double pedal, and Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks — his setup following his 2024 departure from Godsmack.

**Q: Did Shannon Larkin leave Godsmack?**  
A: Yes — Shannon Larkin departed Godsmack in 2024 after holding the drummer position since 2002, making him the band's longest-serving drummer at 22 years. Before Godsmack he played with Wrathchild America, Souls at Zero, Ugly Kid Joe, and Amen.

**Q: What cymbals does Shannon Larkin use?**  
A: Shannon Larkin has been a Sabian artist for decades, starting with the AA series in his pre-Godsmack bands and moving through HHX during his main Godsmack tenure. His current setup mixes Sabian AAX and HHX models, including AAX Stage Hi-Hats, AAX X-Plosion Crashes, and an HHX Raw Bell Dry Ride.
