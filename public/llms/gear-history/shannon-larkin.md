## Gear Timeline

### Faceless Era (2002–2005)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple — 5-piece double-kick; 22"x18" kicks (x2), 10"x8" and 12"x9" rack toms, 14"x14" and 16"x16" floor toms, maple shells
- **Snare:** Tama Starclassic Steel 14"x6.5" — steel shell
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series — 14" Stage Hi-Hats, 18" and 19" X-Plosion Crashes, 21" ride
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 double bass pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater Standard 5A/5B Hickory
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor Coated (toms), Remo Ambassador (snare side), Remo Powerstroke (kick)
- **Original setup cost (2003):** ~$4,988
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$8,882
- **Notable:** Larkin joined Godsmack in 2002, replacing founding drummer Tommy Stewart, and recorded Faceless (2003) — the album that debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 and went 4x Platinum — on a road-tested Tama Starclassic Maple kit. The lead single "I Stand Alone," written for The Chronicles of Riddick, became one of the most-played rock songs of the 2000s. Larkin arrived with over a decade of professional experience from Wrathchild America (thrash metal) and Ugly Kid Joe ("Everything About You," 1992).

### IV Era (2006–2009)

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX (consistent)
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 (unchanged)
- **Notable:** Godsmack IV (2006) continued the band's commercial run. Larkin's Tama-based setup remained largely unchanged through this period as Godsmack solidified its position as one of hard rock's top touring acts.

### The Oracle Era (2010–2013)

- **Drums:** Transition to Ddrum Reflex Series — Larkin became a Ddrum signature artist
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX/HHX mixed configuration
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 (unchanged)
- **Notable:** Around The Oracle (2010), Larkin moved from Tama to a Ddrum Reflex Series endorsement, beginning a long-term signature-artist relationship that would expand to include a signature snare and signature sticks.

### 1000HP Era (2014–2017)

- **Drums:** Ddrum Reflex Series with Ddrum Shannon Larkin Signature snare introduced
- **Snare:** Ddrum Shannon Larkin Signature 14"x6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX & HHX Series (consistent)
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 (unchanged)
- **Notable:** 1000HP (2014) saw the debut of Larkin's Ddrum Shannon Larkin Signature snare — a dedicated signature model reflecting his growing stature as one of hard rock's most recognizable drummers.

### When Legends Rise / Lighting Up the Sky Era (2018–present)

- **Drums:** Ddrum Reflex Series — full touring configuration
- **Snare:** Ddrum Shannon Larkin Signature 14"x6.5" Maple
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX & HHX Series — 14" AAX Stage Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" AAX X-Plosion Crashes, 21" HHX Raw Bell Dry Ride, 10" AAX Splash, 18" AAX Chinese
- **Hardware:** DW 9000 Series Double Pedal, Ddrum Throne
- **Sticks:** Vater Shannon Larkin Signature
- **Heads:** Evans (current heads)
- **Notable:** Larkin's current setup represents the full maturation of his Ddrum endorsement, with a complete signature line spanning his snare and sticks. He remained Godsmack's longest-serving drummer, holding the chair from 2002 to 2024 across When Legends Rise (2018), Godsmack's 1000HP-era catalog, and Lighting Up the Sky (2023).

## Key Gear Changes

- **2002–2009:** Tama Starclassic Maple — Larkin's Faceless-era professional standard kit
- **~2010:** Tama → Ddrum Reflex Series — long-term signature-artist endorsement begins
- **~2014:** Tama Starclassic Steel snare → Ddrum Shannon Larkin Signature snare — dedicated signature model introduced
- **~2020s:** Vater Standard sticks → Vater Shannon Larkin Signature — full signature stick line
- **Sabian consistency:** Larkin has remained a Sabian cymbal artist across his entire Godsmack career, expanding from AAX-only to a mixed AAX/HHX setup
- **DW 9000 consistency:** Larkin's double pedal choice has remained unchanged since joining Godsmack in 2002

## FAQ

**Q: What is Shannon Larkin's drum setup worth?**  
A: Shannon Larkin's 2003 Faceless-era Tama Starclassic Maple setup cost approximately $4,988, equivalent to about $8,882 adjusted for 2026 inflation. His current Ddrum Reflex signature setup — including his Ddrum Shannon Larkin Signature snare, Sabian AAX/HHX cymbals, and DW 9000 hardware — runs approximately $6,800-$7,000 in modern equivalent pricing.

**Q: What drum kit does Shannon Larkin use?**  
A: Shannon Larkin currently plays a Ddrum Reflex Series drum kit with a Ddrum Shannon Larkin Signature snare. He used a Tama Starclassic Maple kit during Godsmack's commercially biggest early years, including the 4x Platinum Faceless (2003), before transitioning to his long-term Ddrum signature-artist relationship around 2010.

**Q: What cymbals does Shannon Larkin use?**  
A: Shannon Larkin uses Sabian AAX and HHX series cymbals — a relationship spanning his entire Godsmack tenure since 2002. His current setup includes 14" AAX Stage Hi-Hats, 18" and 19" AAX X-Plosion Crashes, a 21" HHX Raw Bell Dry Ride, a 10" AAX Splash, and an 18" AAX Chinese cymbal.

**Q: How much does Shannon Larkin's snare drum cost?**  
A: Shannon Larkin's current Ddrum Shannon Larkin Signature snare (14"x6.5" maple) retails for approximately $350. His earlier Tama Starclassic Steel snare, used on Faceless (2003), cost approximately $320 at the time and would be a $280 collector item today.

**Q: Did Shannon Larkin always play Ddrum?**  
A: No — Shannon Larkin played a Tama Starclassic Maple kit when he joined Godsmack in 2002 and through the band's commercial peak with Faceless (2003). He transitioned to a Ddrum Reflex Series signature-artist relationship around The Oracle era (2010), which expanded over the following years to include a signature snare and signature Vater sticks.
