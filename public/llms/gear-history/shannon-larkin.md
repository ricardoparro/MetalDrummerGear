# Shannon Larkin Drum Kit Gear History — Godsmack

**Drummer:** Shannon Larkin

## Gear Timeline

### Faceless Era (2002–2004)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series — 6-piece; 22"×18" kick (×2), 10"×8" and 12"×9" rack toms, 14"×14" and 16"×16" floor toms
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"×6.5" Brass — brass shell, 8-lug
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series — 14" Stage Hi-Hats, 16" and 18" X-Plosion Crash, 21" Raw Bell Dry Ride, 18" AAX China
- **Pedal:** Pearl Eliminator double pedal
- **Sticks:** Promark American Hickory 5B, wood tip
- **Heads:** Evans G2 (toms), EMAD (kick), Genera (snare)
- **Original setup cost (2003):** ~$4,277
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$7,614
- **Notable:** Larkin joined Godsmack in 2002, replacing Tommy Stewart, and recorded Faceless (2003) as his first full studio album with the band. His powerful, groove-driven Pearl Reference Series setup anchored hard rock radio hits like "Straight Out of Line" and "Serenity," establishing the Pearl/Sabian pairing he has maintained ever since.

### IV Era (2005–2007)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX (consistent configuration)
- **Pedal:** Pearl Eliminator (unchanged)
- **Notable:** IV (2006) deepened Godsmack's commercial success and Larkin's Pearl/Sabian endorsement relationship, with his setup remaining functionally identical to the Faceless era — a deliberate "if it isn't broken" approach to gear.

### The Oracle / 1000hp Era (2010–2014)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX (consistent)
- **Sticks:** Transition to Promark Shannon Larkin Signature model
- **Notable:** Around this period Promark launched a Shannon Larkin signature stick — a heavier, hard-rock-oriented model reflecting his powerful, full-arm playing technique. The Oracle (2010) and 1000hp (2014) continued Godsmack's run of platinum and gold-certified releases.

### When Legends Rise Era (2018)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure — upgraded flagship Pearl shell pack
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"×6.5" Brass (continued)
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX (unchanged across his entire career)
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive double pedal — upgrade from the Eliminator
- **Notable:** When Legends Rise (2018) marked a stylistic shift toward more melodic, radio-friendly material, with Larkin upgrading to the Pearl Reference Pure kit for a more refined, controlled tone befitting the album's more polished production.

### Current / Anniversary Touring Era (2023–present)

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure (continued)
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14"×6.5" Brass
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series — 14" Stage Hi-Hats, 18" and 19" X-Plosion Crash, 21" Raw Bell Dry Ride, 18" AAX China
- **Pedal:** Pearl Demon Drive double pedal + Pearl D-2000 Roadster Throne
- **Sticks:** Promark Shannon Larkin Signature
- **Notable:** Larkin's current touring rig represents over two decades of consistency with Pearl and Sabian — one of the longest unbroken endorsement relationships in modern hard rock, spanning six Godsmack studio albums.

## FAQ

**Q: What is Shannon Larkin's drum setup worth?**  
A: Shannon Larkin's current Pearl Reference Pure touring setup costs approximately $7,000–$9,000 for the shell pack alone, with Sabian AAX cymbals adding $1,600–$2,000 and Pearl hardware/pedals around $750–$900. A complete modern equivalent setup runs roughly $9,500–$12,000. His original 2003 Pearl Reference Series setup cost approximately $4,277 — equivalent to about $7,614 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: What drum kit does Shannon Larkin use?**  
A: Shannon Larkin currently plays a Pearl Reference Pure drum kit with Sabian AAX cymbals, a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal, and Promark Shannon Larkin Signature sticks. He has been a Pearl and Sabian endorser throughout his entire run as Godsmack's drummer, dating back to 2002.

**Q: What brand does Shannon Larkin endorse?**  
A: Shannon Larkin endorses Pearl drums, Sabian cymbals, and Promark sticks — a pairing he has maintained consistently since joining Godsmack in 2002. He has his own Promark Shannon Larkin Signature stick model.

**Q: What snare drum does Shannon Larkin use?**  
A: Shannon Larkin uses a Pearl Reference 14"×6.5" Brass snare. The brass shell delivers the powerful, cutting backbeat that drives Godsmack's groove-oriented hard rock sound, and he has used this same snare configuration consistently since his 2003 debut album with the band, Faceless.

**Q: When did Shannon Larkin join Godsmack?**  
A: Shannon Larkin joined Godsmack in 2002, replacing original drummer Tommy Stewart. His first full studio album with the band was Faceless (2003), which established the Pearl Reference Series and Sabian AAX setup he has built on for over two decades.
