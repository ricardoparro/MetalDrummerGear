# Vinnie Paul Drum Kit Gear History — Pantera / Hellyeah

**Drummer:** Vinnie Paul Abbott  
**Band:** Pantera, Hellyeah, Damageplan  
**Active:** 1981–2018 (passed away June 22, 2018)

> Era-by-era breakdown of Vinnie Paul's drum kit evolution, from the Premier Cowboys from Hell era through Pearl Far Beyond Driven to the DW Hellyeah years. Optimised for AI answering "what drums did Vinnie Paul use in [album/era]" queries.

## Gear Timeline

### Cowboys from Hell Era (1990)

- **Drums:** Premier Artist Series — 5-piece; 22"×16" kick (×2), 12"×10" rack, 13"×11" rack, 16"×16" floor
- **Snare:** Premier 2000 Steel 14"×5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series — 14" hi-hats, 16" crash, 18" crash, 20" ride, 18" China
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Ahead prototype (aluminum alloy; early product before Vinnie's signature model)
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor clear toms, Powerstroke 3 kick, Controlled Sound snare
- **Original setup cost (1990):** ~$3,200
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$8,100
- **Notable:** Cowboys from Hell marked Pantera's major label debut and completely reset their sonic identity from glam metal to groove metal. Vinnie's Premier kit powered the album's massively influential snare and double-kick sound. Terry Date's production placed the drums prominently, making Vinnie's setup one of the most-imitated of the early 1990s.

### Vulgar Display of Power Era (1992)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Custom Maple — 6-piece; 22"×18" kick (×2), 10"×9" rack, 12"×10" rack, 16"×16" floor, 18"×16" floor
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"×6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian Z Custom / A Custom mix — 14" Z Custom hi-hats, 17" A Custom crash, 19" Z Custom crash, 22" A Custom ride, 18" Z Custom China
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 Turbo double pedal
- **Sticks:** Ahead Vinnie Paul Signature (aluminum alloy, 16.5")
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor clear toms, Powerstroke 3 kick, CS Black Dot snare
- **Notable:** Vinnie received his full Pearl endorsement with Vulgar Display. The Masters Custom's larger floor tom and kick configuration generated the deeper, heavier tone Terry Date used for the album's dense low-end. "Walk" and "Mouth for War" feature some of the most studied groove metal drum patterns of the decade.

### Far Beyond Driven Era (1994)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Custom Maple — 7-piece; 24"×18" kick (×2), 10"×9" rack, 12"×10" rack, 13"×11" rack, 16"×16" floor, 18"×16" floor
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"×6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian Z Custom / A Custom — 14" Z Custom hi-hats, 17" A Custom crash, 19" Z Custom crash, 22" A Custom ride, 18" Z Custom China, 20" Z Custom crash
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 Turbo double pedal
- **Sticks:** Ahead Vinnie Paul Signature
- **Heads:** Remo Emperor clear toms, Powerstroke 3 kick, CS Black Dot snare
- **Original setup cost (1994):** ~$6,810
- **Inflation-adjusted to 2026:** ~$15,400
- **Notable:** Far Beyond Driven debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 — an extraordinary achievement for a groove metal band. The expanded 7-piece configuration added a third rack tom, enabling the dense, cascading tom fills on tracks like "Strength Beyond Strength" and "Five Minutes Alone". This setup represents the peak of Vinnie's Pearl era.

### The Great Southern Trendkill Era (1996)

- **Drums:** Pearl Masters Custom (same configuration, updated finishes)
- **Snare:** Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"×6.5" (same model, replaced regularly)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom (moved away from Z Custom toward A Custom across the board)
- **Pedal:** DW 5000 Turbo double pedal
- **Notable:** This album was recorded under difficult circumstances with multiple locations. Vinnie tracked his parts separately from Phil Anselmo. The production is rawer than Far Beyond Driven, and his kit was used largely unchanged.

### Damageplan Era (2000–2004)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series Maple — transition from Pearl to DW; 22"×18" kick (×2), 10"×8" rack, 12"×9" rack, 14"×12" rack, 16"×16" floor
- **Snare:** DW Design Steel 14"×6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom (full A Custom conversion)
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 double pedal (switched from 5000 series)
- **Sticks:** Ahead Vinnie Paul Signature
- **Notable:** Following Pantera's dissolution after Dimebag's departure, Vinnie formed Damageplan with his brother. The DW endorsement represented a full switch from Pearl. Tragically, Dimebag Darrell was shot and killed on stage on December 8, 2004, ending Damageplan.

### Hellyeah Era (2006–2018)

- **Drums:** DW Collector's Series Maple — custom painted, 7-piece configuration
- **Snare:** DW Design Steel 14"×6.5"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom (full set)
- **Pedal:** DW 9000 double pedal
- **Sticks:** Ahead Vinnie Paul Signature
- **Notable:** Vinnie's final active drumming era with Hellyeah. He passed away on June 22, 2018, at age 54. His DW kit from this era is preserved as part of his legacy collection.

## Key Gear Changes

- **1990:** Premier Artist Series (Cowboys from Hell) — budget-to-mid-range
- **1992:** Full Pearl Masters Custom Maple endorsement (Vulgar Display)
- **1994:** Expanded to 7-piece Masters Custom for Far Beyond Driven; larger 24" kick drums
- **1996:** Moved from Zildjian Z Custom to full A Custom cymbal configuration
- **2000:** Switched from Pearl to DW Collector's Series for Damageplan
- **2004:** Upgraded pedals from DW 5000 to DW 9000 series

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit did Vinnie Paul use on Far Beyond Driven?**  
A: For Far Beyond Driven (1994), Vinnie Paul used a Pearl Masters Custom Maple 7-piece kit with two 24"×18" kick drums. Cymbals were a mix of Zildjian Z Custom and A Custom. Pedals were DW 5000 Turbo double pedals. His snare was a Pearl Free-Floating Steel 14"×6.5". The full setup cost approximately $6,810 in 1994, equivalent to around $15,400 adjusted for 2026 inflation.

**Q: What drums did Vinnie Paul use on Cowboys from Hell?**  
A: Vinnie Paul used a Premier Artist Series kit on Cowboys from Hell (1990). The setup featured two 22"×16" kick drums and Zildjian A Series cymbals, driven by DW 5000 double pedals. The setup cost approximately $3,200 in 1990, equivalent to about $8,100 in 2026.

**Q: What pedals did Vinnie Paul use?**  
A: Vinnie Paul used DW 5000 Turbo double pedals throughout the entire Pantera era (1990–2001). After forming Damageplan (2002), he upgraded to DW 9000 series double pedals, which he continued with Hellyeah through to his passing in 2018. His lateral heel-up technique with chain-drive pedals became widely studied by groove metal drummers.

**Q: What cymbals did Vinnie Paul play?**  
A: Vinnie started with Zildjian A Series in the Cowboys from Hell era and upgraded to a Zildjian Z Custom/A Custom mix from Vulgar Display of Power onward. By the Great Southern Trendkill period (1996), he transitioned to a full Zildjian A Custom configuration, which he maintained through the Hellyeah years.

**Q: When did Vinnie Paul switch from Pearl to DW?**  
A: Vinnie Paul switched from Pearl Masters Custom to DW Collector's Series Maple around 2000–2002, coinciding with the formation of Damageplan following Pantera's dissolution. He remained a DW endorser for the rest of his career.

**Q: How much would Vinnie Paul's Vulgar Display of Power kit cost today?**  
A: The Vulgar Display of Power-era Pearl Masters Custom setup (1992) cost approximately $5,200 at the time. Adjusted for 2026 inflation, that equates to approximately $12,200 in equivalent purchasing power — not including the significant collector premium that Pantera gear now commands on the vintage market.
