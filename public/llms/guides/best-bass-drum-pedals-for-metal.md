Tomas Haake of Meshuggah drives impossibly precise polyrhythmic double bass patterns with his Tama Iron Cobra — chosen for its reliable chain drive action and the Power Glide cam that makes even the most complex patterns feel controllable. George Kollias of Nile uses Pearl Demon Drive pedals to achieve speeds exceeding 280 BPM, exploiting the direct drive's linear response for maximum speed. Lars Ulrich has built Metallica's thunderous double bass foundation on DW pedals, valuing their adjustability and traditional chain drive feel. Joey Jordison's frantic patterns across Slipknot's catalog relied on pedals that could handle physical punishment without mechanical failure.

## What Makes a Great Metal Bass Drum Pedal

**Drive System:** Direct drive (rod connection) = linear response, maximum speed potential. Chain drive = traditional "swing" feel, preferred by many groove-oriented metal drummers. Both work for metal — it's a feel preference, not a performance requirement.

**Bearings:** High-quality sealed bearings (Pearl NiNjA, DW dual bearing) maintain consistent action under sustained heavy use. Cheap bushings wear out and create inconsistent response over time.

**Cam Shape:** Round/linear cams (Tama Power Glide) provide consistent response throughout the stroke — ideal for sustained double bass patterns. Offset cams add power at the end of the stroke, preferred by some groove drummers.

**Footboard:** Extended longboards enable heel-toe technique for extreme speeds. Standard boards suit ankle and leg technique. The Iron Cobra and Speed Cobra feature extended footboards; standard DW boards suit traditional technique.

## Chain Drive vs Direct Drive for Metal

**Chain Drive (Iron Cobra, DW 9000):**
- Traditional feel with natural "swing" in the stroke
- Tomas Haake's choice for Meshuggah's precision — proves chain drive can handle anything
- Lars Ulrich's choice for Metallica's classic thunder
- More accessible price point at mid-range levels
- Best for: Most metal styles; especially groove-oriented, thrash, and power metal

**Direct Drive (Demon Drive, Trick Bigfoot):**
- Linear, immediate response — no mechanical slack
- George Kollias's tool for 280+ BPM
- Preferred by death metal speed specialists
- Higher price at pro level
- Best for: Maximum speed; blast beat specialists; technical death metal

**The truth:** Tomas Haake plays more complex patterns than most drummers ever will — on chain drive. Lars Ulrich built legendary recordings — on chain drive. George Kollias achieves insane speed — on direct drive. Your technique matters infinitely more than your drive type.

## FAQ

**What bass drum pedals do metal drummers use?**
The most common on MetalForge's roster: Tama Iron Cobra 900 (Tomas Haake, Joey Jordison), Pearl Demon Drive (George Kollias, Dave Lombardo), and DW 9000 (Lars Ulrich, Chris Adler). The Iron Cobra 900 is the most common metal choice overall for its balanced combination of feel, reliability, and adjustability.

**What pedal does Tomas Haake use?**
Tomas Haake of Meshuggah uses Tama Iron Cobra 900 double pedals with the Power Glide cam, chosen for its consistent, predictable chain drive response. The Iron Cobra's durability and reliability have made it his consistent choice across decades of touring and recording Meshuggah's impossibly complex polyrhythmic material.

**What pedal does George Kollias use?**
George Kollias of Nile uses Pearl Demon Drive double pedals. He achieves speeds exceeding 280 BPM — among the fastest recorded double bass in metal history. The Demon Drive's direct drive action via NiNjA bearings provides the frictionless, linear response that his extreme speed technique demands.

**Is direct drive or chain drive better for metal?**
Both work for metal. Direct drive (Pearl Demon Drive) offers maximum speed potential — preferred by speed specialists like George Kollias. Chain drive (Tama Iron Cobra, DW 9000) has more feel and swing — preferred by groove players like Tomas Haake and Lars Ulrich. Technique matters more than drive type. Start with whichever feel you prefer.

**Do I need a double bass pedal for metal?**
Yes — virtually all metal styles require double bass capability. Single pedal playing severely limits what you can play in metal contexts. A double pedal is considered essential equipment for any serious metal drummer.

**What's the best budget double bass pedal for metal?**
The Tama Iron Cobra 600 (around €250–€300) is the best budget option — same Power Glide cam geometry as the pro 900 series with solid die-cast aluminum construction. For mid-range, the Pearl Eliminator Redline (€350–€400) adds NiNjA bearings and interchangeable cams.
