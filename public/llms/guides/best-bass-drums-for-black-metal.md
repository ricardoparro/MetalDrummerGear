## What Bass Drum Setup Do Black Metal Drummers Actually Use?

Black metal's bass drum has a different job than death metal's. Where death metal wants a tight, punchy thud that separates cleanly from the mix, black metal usually wants the kick to blend into the wall of tremolo-picked guitars and blast beats — raw, resonant, and often left with minimal muffling so the shell can breathe and wash together with the rest of the kit, the way it does on genre-defining recordings.

Hellhammer of Mayhem recorded "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" on a Pearl Masters Premium kit, its birch/maple hybrid shell left open enough to ring rather than choke off, establishing the raw low-end template the genre still measures itself against. Inferno, who drums for both Behemoth and Gorgoroth, plays a Pearl Reference Pure bass drum — its thin 6-ply maple shell with no reinforcement ring gives a more resonant, articulate low end suited to Behemoth's more technical black/death hybrid attack. Frost of Satyricon and 1349 has evolved toward a fully custom Sonor SQ2 bass drum, precision-built for the hyperspeed blast beats that define his current playing.

This guide breaks down what actually makes a bass drum work for black metal — shell material, muffling philosophy, and head selection — and which specific shells the genre's most influential drummers play, from raw DIY setups to the professional rigs behind black metal's most important records.

**Key Points:**

- Black metal favors raw, resonant shells with minimal muffling — the opposite of death metal's tightly controlled thud
- Pearl Masters Premium (Hellhammer) established the genre's foundational, breathing low-end tone
- 22"x18" is standard, though some drummers use 20" for a tighter, more aggressive attack
- Thin, unreinforced shells (Pearl Reference Pure — Inferno) trade durability for extra resonance and openness

## What Makes a Great Black Metal Bass Drum?

### 🌊 Minimal Muffling Philosophy

Where death metal chokes the kick down for a tight, punchy attack, black metal typically leaves the bass drum far more open — little or no internal muffling — so it rings and blends into the wall of raw guitar tone rather than cutting cleanly through it.

**Recommendation:** Little to no internal muffling; let the shell resonate and wash into the mix

### 🪵 Shell Resonance

Birch/maple hybrid shells (Hellhammer's Pearl Masters Premium) balance punchy attack with enough body to anchor lo-fi, icy production. Thin, unreinforced maple (Inferno's Pearl Reference Pure) resonates even more freely for a more open, articulate low end.

**Recommendation:** Birch/maple hybrid for raw body; thin unreinforced maple for maximum resonance

### ⭕ Shell Diameter

22"x18" is the black metal standard, giving enough low-end weight for icy, atmospheric passages while staying light enough for sustained blast beat work. Some drummers favor 20" for a tighter, more aggressive punch that suits faster, more percussive black metal.

**Recommendation:** 22" for standard projection and openness; 20" for a tighter, more aggressive attack

### 🎯 Head Selection

Single-ply, lightly coated batter heads let the shell speak more freely than the heavily reinforced heads death metal favors — appropriate for a genre that prizes raw tone over maximum durability, though reinforced options remain a sensible upgrade for heavy touring use.

**Recommendation:** Single-ply coated batter for raw openness; reinforced options for touring durability

### 🛡️ Durability for DIY Touring

Black metal's DIY roots mean gear often travels in vans through harsh weather with minimal soundcheck time. Hardware needs to hold tuning and survive abuse without constant adjustment — a major reason Hellhammer and Inferno both built careers on Pearl's reputation for reliability.

**Recommendation:** Heavy-duty hoops and hardware that holds tuning through temperature swings

### 🥁 Single Kick vs Double Pedal

Black metal rarely uses dual bass drums the way some progressive death metal drummers do — a single 22" kick with a fast double pedal (or, historically, a single pedal for a rawer feel) is the standard across Hellhammer, Inferno, and Frost.

**Recommendation:** Single 22" bass drum with a reliable double pedal for sustained blast beat work

## Raw vs Technical Bass Drum Tone for Black Metal

Black metal drummers split their bass drum choice across two proven philosophies:

- Birch/maple hybrid shells left with minimal muffling

- The historically foundational black metal sound

## FAQ

**What bass drums do black metal drummers use?**
Hellhammer of Mayhem plays a Pearl Masters Premium, Inferno of Behemoth and Gorgoroth plays a Pearl Reference Pure, and Frost of Satyricon and 1349 plays a custom Sonor SQ2. All three run a single 22"x18" bass drum with a double pedal rather than dual kick drums.

**Why do black metal drummers use less muffling than death metal drummers?**
Black metal typically wants the bass drum to blend into the wall of raw, tremolo-picked guitar tone rather than cut cleanly through it the way a tightly muffled death metal kick does. Minimal muffling lets the shell resonate and wash into the mix, which is central to the genre's raw, atmospheric character.

**What size bass drum is best for black metal?**
22"x18" is the black metal standard, matching Hellhammer, Inferno, and Frost's setups — it balances enough low-end weight for icy, atmospheric passages with the speed needed for sustained blast beats. Some drummers use 20" for a tighter, more aggressive punch.

**Should I use a thin or thick shell for black metal?**
It depends on your style. Thin, unreinforced maple (Inferno's Pearl Reference Pure) maximizes resonance and articulation for technical black/death hybrid playing. Thicker birch/maple hybrid shells (Hellhammer's Pearl Masters Premium) give a bit more body and durability while still resonating far more openly than a heavily muffled death metal kick.

**What bass drum head is best for black metal?**
Single-ply, lightly coated batter heads are the black metal standard, letting the shell speak more freely than the reinforced heads death metal favors. Drummers who tour heavily sometimes step up to a reinforced head for durability, at a small cost to raw openness.

## Build the Raw, Resonant Low End Black Metal Demands

Black metal's bass drum requirements are the inverse of death metal's — instead of chasing a tight, punchy attack that cuts cleanly through the mix, the genre wants a shell that resonates and blends into the wall of raw guitar tone. Hellhammer's Pearl Masters Premium proves that a birch/maple hybrid shell left open with minimal muffling is still the foundational black metal sound, more than three decades after "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas."

If your playing leans more technical or blast-heavy, follow Inferno's example and step up to a thin, unreinforced maple shell like the Pearl Reference Pure for extra articulation without losing resonance. And if you want a bass drum tuned precisely to your own hyperspeed blast beat technique, Frost's custom Sonor SQ2 shows what's possible with a fully configurable shell program.

Start with a 22"x18" shell, keep the muffling light, and let the drum breathe — that's the proven foundation across black metal's most influential drummers.
