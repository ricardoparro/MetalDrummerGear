## What China Cymbal Does Vinnie Paul Use?

Groove metal trades blast-beat speed for pocket, weight, and swagger — but the china cymbal doesn't take a back seat. Groove metal's slower, more deliberate tempos give a china more room to breathe, turning it into a signature punctuation mark on breakdowns and groove-riff accents.

Vinnie Paul of Pantera ran an 18" Sabian China throughout the band's catalog, delivering the explosive punctuation heard on "Walk" and "Mouth for War." Chris Adler of Lamb of God favors an 18" Meinl Byzance Extra Dry China for a darker, drier trash. Art Cruz, also of Lamb of God, layers a 19" Zildjian A Ultra Hammered China alongside a 17" K China with EFX holes, while Igor Cavalera of Sepultura built his groove-and-thrash hybrid sound around an 18" Paiste 2002 China.

## FAQ

**What china cymbal size is best for groove metal?**
18" is the groove metal china standard, used by Vinnie Paul and Chris Adler. Art Cruz runs a 19" for extra low-end weight, layered with a smaller 17" china.

**Do groove metal drummers use china cymbals live?**
Yes. Vinnie Paul, Chris Adler, Art Cruz, and Igor Cavalera all run a china as a standard part of their live setups.

**China vs crash cymbal — what's the difference for groove metal?**
A crash rings out cleanly with a longer decay. A china produces a punchier, dirtier attack better suited to punctuating breakdowns and pocket-tight groove accents.

**What's the best budget china cymbal for groove metal?**
The Sabian XSR Fast China (€100–130) brings B20 bronze to a mid-range price with punchy, controlled response.
