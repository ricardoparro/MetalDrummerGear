## What China Cymbal Does Lars Ulrich Use?

Thrash metal moves fast, and a china cymbal earns its place by punctuating that speed with the same explosive punch a crash provides — but faster, dirtier, and more attention-grabbing. Thrash metal's china choices lean bright, aggressive, and built to cut through a wall of downpicked guitars at 200+ BPM.

Lars Ulrich has used a 20" Zildjian Z Custom China throughout Metallica's catalog since the 1996 Load sessions. Dave Lombardo's 18" Paiste China provides the brutal, iconic punctuation on Slayer's "Angel of Death." Charlie Benante of Anthrax runs an 18" Paiste China from the same RUDE/2002 family, and Paul Bostaph — who has held the kit for both Slayer and Testament — plays an 18" Sabian AAXtreme China.

## FAQ

**What china cymbal size is best for thrash metal?**
18" is the thrash metal china standard, used by Dave Lombardo, Charlie Benante, and Paul Bostaph. Lars Ulrich's 20" is the notable exception, chosen for extra low-end drama.

**Do thrash metal drummers use china cymbals live?**
Yes, extensively. Lars Ulrich, Dave Lombardo, Charlie Benante, and Paul Bostaph all run a china as a core part of their live and touring setups.

**China vs crash cymbal — what's the difference for thrash metal?**
A crash rings out cleanly with a longer decay. A china produces a faster, dirtier, more explosive attack better suited to punctuating gallop rhythms and rapid-fire transitions.

**What's the best budget china cymbal for thrash metal?**
The Zildjian ZBT China (€70–90) delivers a genuinely trashy B8 bronze sound at a true beginner price. The Sabian XSR Fast China (€100–130) brings B20 bronze to a mid-range price.
