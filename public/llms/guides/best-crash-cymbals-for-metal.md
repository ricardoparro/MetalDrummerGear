## The Crash Cymbal Every Metal Drummer Builds a Setup Around

Every fill lands on one, every verse-to-chorus transition needs one. The crash is the most-used accent cymbal in metal drumming — designed to explode instantly and ring out cleanly, unlike a china's darker, trashier decay.

Lars Ulrich has used Zildjian A Custom Rock Crashes (16", 17", 18") throughout Metallica's catalog since the 1996 Load sessions. Joey Jordison built Slipknot's Iowa-era cymbal setup around Paiste RUDE and 2002 Power Crashes (16"-19"), and Charlie Benante (Anthrax) has run the same Paiste RUDE/2002 Power Crash lineup (18"/19") for decades. Gene Hoglan rides 18" and 20" Sabian AAX Crashes across Death, Testament, and Dethklok, and Mario Duplantier (Gojira) layers 18" K Custom Hybrid, 19" A Custom, and 20" K Sweet Crashes for a graduated set of accent weights.

## Thin vs Medium/Heavy Crash for Metal

**Thin/Fast Crashes (Lars Ulrich's Rock Crashes, Gene Hoglan/Flo Mounier's X-Plosion Crashes):**
- Explode instantly with a shorter, more controlled decay
- Best for rapid-fire accents

**Medium/Heavy Crashes (Joey Jordison and Charlie Benante's Power Crashes):**
- Louder, more sustained decay with real low-end weight
- Best for sustained accents and heavier breakdowns

**Verdict:** Most pro metal drummers run both weights — which is exactly why Mario Duplantier layers three different crashes at three different weights.

## FAQ

**What crash cymbal does Lars Ulrich use?**
Lars Ulrich uses Zildjian A Custom Rock Crashes in 16", 17", and 18" sizes, part of his Zildjian A Custom setup since the 1996 Load sessions.

**What size crash cymbal is best for metal?**
16"-19" covers the metal crash range. Smaller crashes (16"-17") respond faster; larger crashes (18"-19") project louder and sustain longer. Most pro drummers, like Mario Duplantier, run multiple sizes.

**How many crash cymbals should a metal drummer have?**
Most pro metal drummers run 2-4 crashes at different sizes and weights. Mario Duplantier (Gojira) layers three — 18", 19", and 20" — each with a different tonal character.

**What's the best budget crash cymbal for metal?**
The Zildjian ZBT Crash (€60–80) delivers a genuinely explosive B8 bronze sound at a true beginner price. The Sabian XSR Fast Crash (€100–130) brings B20 bronze to a mid-range price.

**Crash vs china cymbal — what's the difference for metal?**
A crash rings out cleanly with a longer, more musical decay, making it the standard choice for fills and transitions. A china produces a faster, dirtier, more explosive attack with a shorter decay, better suited to accenting breakdowns and blast beat punctuation.
