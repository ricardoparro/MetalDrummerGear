## Why Black Metal Wants the Opposite of a Perfect Drum Tone

Every other genre of metal chases control and a punchy, engineered attack. Black metal, at its most trve, wants none of that — raw, thin, slightly chaotic drum tone, with toms that ring wild and untamed.

Hellhammer's foundational recordings with Mayhem and Frost's work across Satyricon and 1349 both rely on Remo's thinner, single-ply constructions rather than the double-ply, heavily muffled heads that dominate death metal and modern metalcore. Inferno of Behemoth and Daray of Dimmu Borgir represent black metal's more produced, symphonic wing, and lean on Evans for a still-aggressive but slightly more controlled tone.

## Recommended Drum Heads for Black Metal

### 1. Remo Coated Ambassador — Best Overall

- **Construction:** 1-ply coated (10mil)
- **Price range:** €20–€35 per head
- **Best for:** The genre's foundational raw, open attack

**Who uses it:**
- **Hellhammer** ([/drummer/hellhammer](https://metalforge.io/drummer/hellhammer)) — Remo endorser, foundational black metal drum tone
- **Frost** ([/drummer/frost](https://metalforge.io/drummer/frost)) — Remo drumheads across his Sonor SQ2 kit

Black metal's closest thing to a reference head. Both Hellhammer and Frost run Remo, and the Ambassador's warm, open, single-ply character is the foundation of the genre's raw, unfiltered attack — it rings and breathes exactly as black metal wants during a sustained blast beat passage.

### 2. Remo Diplomat Coated — Most Authentically Raw

- **Construction:** 1-ply coated (7.5mil — extra thin)
- **Price range:** €18–€30 per head
- **Best for:** DIY, underground black metal recordings

The thinnest, most raw-sounding mainstream coated head available. A specialist choice — the Diplomat wears quickly — but for raw, underground black metal recorded in DIY conditions, its thin, trashy character is arguably more authentic than anything else.

- **Construction:** 1-ply coated (10mil)
- **Price range:** €18–€30 per head
- **Best for:** Modern, produced black metal that needs touring reliability

Single-ply and unmuffled, maximizing the natural ring and wash that defines black metal's atmosphere during a blast beat passage, letting the whole kit bleed together into the genre's signature wall-of-sound quality.

### 5. Evans Genera G1 Clear — Brightest Option

A brighter alternative for drummers who want the harshest possible attack rather than the coated head's slight warmth — a good match for the genre's more atmospheric corners.

## Remo vs Evans for Black Metal

**Remo:** The genre's foundational choice — Hellhammer and Frost both use Remo. Ambassador and Diplomat deliver the rawest, most open tone.

**Evans:** Chosen by black metal's more produced, symphonic wing (Inferno, Daray). Level 360 collar gives more reliable tuning for global touring while staying single-ply and open.

**Verdict:** Remo Coated Ambassador is the genre's true reference tone. Evans G1 Coated is the right call if you need touring-grade reliability alongside black metal's raw character.

## FAQ

**What drum heads do black metal drummers use?**
Traditional black metal drummers favor thin, single-ply heads. Hellhammer and Frost both use Remo, primarily the Coated Ambassador. Inferno and Daray, representing the genre's more produced symphonic wing, both use Evans instead.

**Why does black metal use thinner drum heads than death metal?**
Black metal's aesthetic prizes raw, slightly out-of-control tone over precision and durability. Single-ply heads ring more and distort more easily at volume, producing the "blown-out" character central to the genre.

**Should I use coated or clear heads for black metal?**
Coated is the more common choice — Remo Coated Ambassador is the genre's reference tone. Clear heads like Evans Genera G1 Clear are used by some drummers chasing an even harsher, brighter attack.

**Do single-ply heads wear out faster in black metal?**
Yes, especially thinner films like the Remo Diplomat. Traditional and underground black metal drummers generally accept this trade-off for the tone; touring symphonic acts sometimes lean toward Evans' slightly more durable single-ply options.

**What resonant head pairs best with a raw black metal tone?**
Remo Ambassador Clear is the standard pairing — single-ply and unmuffled, maximizing the natural wash and resonance central to black metal's atmosphere.
