## Why Extreme Metal Destroys Ordinary Drum Heads

George Kollias sustains blast beats past 240 BPM for entire songs. Pete Sandoval pioneered the gravity blast, hammering a batter head at a rate few manufacturers ever designed for. Derek Roddy built his career on one-footed blast technique that pushes bass drum heads to their absolute limit. Ordinary double-ply heads that work fine for groove metal or thrash simply don't survive this treatment for long — they dent prematurely and lose tension mid-set.

### 2. Evans Power Center Reverse Dot — Best Durability Specialist

- **Construction:** 2-ply coated with underside reinforcement dot
- **Price range:** €28–€42 per head
- **Best for:** Drummers with highly concentrated striking technique

An underside reinforcement dot, out of the stick's path, adds durability without changing surface feel. For drummers pushing gravity blasts and sustained heel-up bass drum work, this reinforcement can mean the difference between a head surviving a full set or failing mid-song.

## Evans vs Aquarian vs Remo for Extreme Metal

**Evans:** The dominant choice across the extreme metal roster. Level 360 collar ensures consistent tuning across demanding tours. Preferred by George Kollias, Pete Sandoval, Derek Roddy, Gene Hoglan.

**Aquarian:** Heavier outer-ply construction built specifically for hard hitters. Excels at low tuning without sacrificing durability.

**Remo:** Emperor and Emperor X provide solid double-ply durability with a warmer voice and real reinforcement.

**Verdict:** Evans G2 Coated is the proven default, backed by three generations of the genre's fastest drummers. Aquarian Super-2 is the pick if you specifically need maximum toughness at low tuning.

## FAQ

**What drum heads do extreme metal drummers use?**
Evans dominates extreme metal's professional roster. George Kollias, Pete Sandoval, Derek Roddy, and Gene Hoglan all use Evans, primarily double-ply coated heads like the G2, with reinforced options like Power Center Reverse Dot for drummers whose technique concentrates the most force.

**Why do extreme metal drummers need more durable drum heads?**
Sustained blast beats — often at 200-240+ BPM for entire songs — put far more cumulative stress on a head than groove or thrash metal's more moderate tempos. Techniques like the gravity blast and one-footed blast concentrate enormous repeated force on small areas of the head.

**What's the best bass drum head for extreme metal double bass?**
Evans EQ4 Clear is purpose-built for this — its foam damping ring and reinforced double-ply construction survive sustained, high-velocity double bass work far better than a standard batter head repurposed for kick duty.

**Is Aquarian a good alternative to Evans for extreme metal?**
Yes, particularly for drummers who tune low and hit exceptionally hard. Aquarian Super-2's heavier outer ply is built specifically for brutal, down-tuned hitting.

**How often should extreme metal drummers change their heads?**
More frequently than most metal styles — often every 2-4 months of regular touring and blast beat playing, versus 3-6 months for less extreme styles.
