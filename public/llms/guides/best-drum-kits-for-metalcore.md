## What Drum Kit Should I Use for Metalcore?

Metalcore drum kits need to do two jobs that pull in opposite directions: deliver crushing weight on half-time breakdowns, and stay articulate and fast through technical, death-metal-influenced passages and blast beats. Few genres demand this much range from a single drum setup.

Nick Augusto powered Trivium's "In Waves" and "Vengeance Falls" on a Pearl Reference Pure setup, its thin 6-ply maple shells delivering sensitivity for fast, technical thrash-metalcore hybrid work. Art Cruz brought his aggressive, hard-hitting style to Lamb of God on a Ludwig kit anchored by a 14x6.5" Black Beauty snare, after cutting his teeth in metalcore bands Winds of Plague and Prong. Travis Orbin defined the djent-adjacent side of metalcore on Periphery's genre-shaping self-titled debut, playing SJC Custom Drums integrated with electronic triggers.

## FAQ

**What drum kit should I use for metalcore?**
Pearl Reference Pure is a strong all-around choice — Nick Augusto used its thin 6-ply maple shells across Trivium's "In Waves" and "Vengeance Falls." For heavier, more aggressive metalcore, Art Cruz's Ludwig Black Beauty setup delivers maximum breakdown crack; for hybrid, electronically augmented djent-metalcore, Travis Orbin's SJC Custom Drums with Roland SPD-SX integration is the standard.

**What drum kit does Nick Augusto use?**
Nick Augusto played a Pearl Reference Pure setup throughout his 2009–2014 tenure as Trivium's drummer, anchored by a Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Brass snare, Sabian AAX Series cymbals, and a Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal.

**How do metalcore drummers get such a heavy breakdown sound?**
Breakdown weight comes primarily from snare choice and tuning. Deep snares like Art Cruz's Ludwig 14x6.5" Black Beauty deliver a sharp, cutting crack tuned relatively low for maximum impact on the simplified, half-time patterns that define breakdowns.

**Do metalcore drummers need electronic triggers?**
Not always, but many modern metalcore and djent-adjacent drummers do. Travis Orbin integrates a Roland SPD-SX Sampling Pad directly into his acoustic SJC Custom kit. Traditional breakdown-and-blast-beat metalcore works fine with a purely acoustic setup like Pearl Reference Pure or Ludwig.

**Do I need an expensive kit to play metalcore?**
No — a budget Pearl Export or Tama Imperialstar will teach the technique needed to switch between crushing breakdowns and fast technical fills.

**What snare is best for metalcore breakdowns?**
A deep 14"x6.5" or larger snare in steel or brass delivers the sharpest, heaviest crack for breakdowns. Art Cruz's Ludwig 14x6.5" Black Beauty and Nick Augusto's Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Brass snare are both proven examples.
