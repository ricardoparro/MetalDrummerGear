## What Drum Kit Should I Use for Nu-Metal?

Nu-metal drum kits are built around groove and feel more than raw speed or technical extremity. The genre's defining sound — hip-hop-influenced syncopation, heavy floor-tom-driven patterns, and a punchy, cutting backbeat — demands a kit that locks into a pocket and stays there.

Joey Jordison built one of metal's most recognizable drum sounds on a Tama Starclassic Performer kit during Slipknot's Iowa era, paired with his own Pearl Joey Jordison Signature 13x6.5" snare and Paiste RUDE and 2002 Series cymbals. John Otto locked in Limp Bizkit's hip-hop-influenced groove on an OCDP Custom Type 5 Acrylic kit — the transparent acrylic shells becoming as visually iconic as Otto's funk-inspired playing. Ray Luzier has driven Korn's dense, syncopated grooves since 2007 on a Pearl Reference Series kit.

## Recommended Drum Kits for Nu-Metal

### 1. Tama Starclassic Performer — Best Overall

- **Shell material:** Birch/Bubinga Hybrid
- **Price range:** €2,000–€3,500 (shell pack)
- **Best for:** Aggressive, technically proficient nu-metal with double kick work

**Who uses it:**
- **Joey Jordison** ([/drummer/joey-jordison](https://metalforge.io/drummer/joey-jordison)) — Slipknot's Iowa-era setup (22" bass drum, 12"/13"/16" toms)

Paired with his signature Pearl 13x6.5" snare and Paiste RUDE and 2002 Series cymbals, the kit gave Jordison's playing the aggressive cut needed to stand out amid Slipknot's dual-percussionist arrangements. Axis A Longboard double pedals powered his extreme double kick technique on the band's rotating drum riser.

### 2. OCDP Custom Type 5 Acrylic — Best for Groove & Stage Presence

- **Shell material:** Acrylic
- **Price range:** €2,500–€4,500 (shell pack)
- **Best for:** Groove-locked, funk-influenced nu-metal with strong visual identity

**Who uses it:**
- **John Otto** ([/drummer/john-otto](https://metalforge.io/drummer/john-otto)) — Limp Bizkit, since 1994

Two signature OCDP snares (a 14x6.5" 40-ply vented shell and a 10x6" 20-ply piccolo) give Otto's hip-hop-influenced, funk-inspired grooves their punchy, cutting backbeat on tracks like "Rollin'" and "Break Stuff." Zildjian A Custom cymbals and a Gibraltar G Class single-pedal setup complete a rig built around groove-first playing.

## FAQ

**What drum kit should I use for nu-metal?**
The Tama Starclassic Performer is the historically definitive choice — Joey Jordison built Slipknot's aggressive Iowa-era sound on this kit. For groove-first, hip-hop-influenced playing, John Otto's OCDP Custom Type 5 Acrylic is the standard. For modern nu-metal, Ray Luzier's Pearl Reference Series has anchored Korn's syncopated grooves since 2007.

**What drum kit did Joey Jordison use?**
Joey Jordison of Slipknot played a Tama Starclassic Performer kit (22" bass drum, 12"/13"/16" toms) during the Iowa era, paired with his own Pearl Joey Jordison Signature 13x6.5" snare and Paiste RUDE and 2002 Series cymbals. He later moved to Pearl MasterWorks and Pearl Reference Series kits for subsequent Slipknot eras.

**What drum kit does Ray Luzier use?**
Ray Luzier of Korn plays a Pearl Reference Series kit with a Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Brass snare, Sabian AAX Series cymbals, and a DW 9002 double bass pedal — the setup he's maintained since joining Korn in 2007.

**Do I need an expensive kit to play nu-metal?**
No — nu-metal is about groove and feel more than gear. A budget Pearl Export or Tama Imperialstar will teach the syncopated, hip-hop-influenced technique the genre demands.

**What drum kit does John Otto use?**
John Otto of Limp Bizkit plays an OCDP Custom Type 5 Acrylic kit, with two signature OCDP snares (14x6.5" 40-ply vented and 10x6" 20-ply piccolo), Zildjian A Custom cymbals, and a Gibraltar G Class single bass drum pedal.

**Single pedal or double pedal for nu-metal?**
Either works — nu-metal rarely relies on sustained double bass. John Otto uses a single Gibraltar G Class pedal for Limp Bizkit's straight-ahead funk-rock grooves, while Joey Jordison's Axis A Longboard double pedal and Ray Luzier's DW 9002 double pedal are used mainly for accent bursts.
