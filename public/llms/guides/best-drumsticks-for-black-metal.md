## What Drumsticks Should I Use for Black Metal?

Black metal drumming is built on relentless blast beats sustained for entire songs at tempos that punish a stick as hard as anything in extreme metal. The genre's raw, icy production aesthetic means the stick also has to deliver a cutting tonal character without sounding overly processed.

Hellhammer of Mayhem, who recorded the genre-defining "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas," plays Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. Frost of Satyricon and 1349, one of extreme metal's fastest drummers, plays Vic Firth's American Classic Extreme 5B — a reinforced variant for hyperspeed blast beat endurance. Inferno of Behemoth, driving the band's technical black/death hybrid sound, plays a standard Vic Firth 5B.

## Standard 5B vs Extreme 5B for Black Metal

**Standard 5B (Hellhammer, Inferno):** Balanced mass and control for both raw power and versatile technical patterns.

**Extreme 5B (Frost):** Reinforced construction for sustained, full-song blast beat endurance without a break.

**Verdict:** Start with the standard Vic Firth 5B. Upgrade to the Extreme variant if you're routinely sustaining blast beats for entire songs without a break.

## FAQ

**What drumsticks does Hellhammer use?**
Hellhammer plays Vic Firth American Classic 5B drumsticks, the stick he recorded "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" on and has used since 1988.

**What drumsticks does Frost use?**
Frost plays Vic Firth's American Classic Extreme 5B — a reinforced variant built for sustained, hyperspeed blast beat endurance.

**What drumsticks does Inferno use?**
Inferno plays a standard Vic Firth 5B, matching the versatility his technical black/death hybrid drumming requires.

**What stick size is best for black metal blast beats?**
5B is the black metal standard — Hellhammer, Frost, and Inferno all play 5B-class sticks for enough power without rapid fatigue.

**What's the difference between Vic Firth 5B and American Classic Extreme 5B?**
The Extreme 5B is reinforced for greater durability under sustained abuse — Frost's choice for continuous, full-song blast beats. The standard 5B, played by Hellhammer and Inferno, is sufficient for most black metal playing.

**Do black metal drummers need special sticks for cold, DIY touring conditions?**
American hickory's density holds up better than lighter woods under the temperature swings and minimal-budget touring that defined black metal's DIY roots.
