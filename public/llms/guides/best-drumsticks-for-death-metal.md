## Why Death Metal Sticks Live and Die on Blast Beat Durability

Death metal puts more sustained physical stress on a drumstick than almost any other style of drumming. Blast beats at 200–280 BPM demand thousands of consecutive full-force strokes per song, and every one accelerates wear on the tip and shoulder.

George Kollias plays a Vic Firth signature stick engineered specifically for sustaining 280 BPM blast beats with Nile. Gene Hoglan — "The Atomic Clock" — plays Promark 5B sticks, matching his powerful, metronomic hitting style across Death, Testament, and Dark Angel. Flo Mounier of Cryptopsy plays Vic Firth's American Classic 5A, a lighter stick chosen for ghost-note sensitivity in his gravity-blast-driven playing.

## 5A vs 5B for Death Metal

**5B (Gene Hoglan, George Kollias):** Greater mass drives consistent, audible snare attacks across extended blast passages. Best for maximum blast beat power.

**5A (Flo Mounier):** Lighter mass rewards ghost-note articulation and gravity blast sensitivity. Best for technical death metal blending speed with dynamic range.

**Verdict:** Start with 5B (Promark or Vic Firth) for blast beat power and durability. Move to 5A if your playing prioritizes technical articulation and ghost-note sensitivity over raw mass.

## FAQ

**What drumsticks does George Kollias use?**
George Kollias plays a Vic Firth signature model (SGK) engineered to survive sustained blast beats at 240+ BPM without losing precision.

**What drumsticks does Gene Hoglan use?**
Gene Hoglan uses Promark 5B drumsticks, matching the power and metronomic precision that earned him the nickname "The Atomic Clock."

**What drumsticks does Flo Mounier use?**
Flo Mounier plays Vic Firth's American Classic 5A, chosen for the ghost-note sensitivity his technical death metal requires.

**Are 5A or 5B drumsticks better for death metal?**
5B is the standard for maximum blast beat power and durability. 5A suits technical death metal that prioritizes ghost-note articulation over raw mass.

**What stick size holds up best under blast beats?**
5B or heavier, like George Kollias's Vic Firth signature or Gene Hoglan's Promark 5B, survives thousands of consecutive full-force strokes per song without chipping.

**How often should death metal drummers replace their sticks?**
Replace wood sticks as soon as chipping or cracking affects feel — under blast-beat-heavy playing, sticks may last only a single set. Aluminum options like Ahead's Acroite last months instead.
