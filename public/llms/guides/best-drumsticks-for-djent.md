## Why Djent Demands a Different Kind of Stick

Djent asks something different of a stick than thrash or death metal does. Where those genres reward raw power and sustained mass, djent's polyrhythmic, palm-muted precision rewards control — the ability to land a triplet-based fill exactly where it needs to sit against a guitar riff shifting meter every few bars, then snap back into a locked, metronomic groove without losing articulation.

Tomas Haake of Meshuggah plays Wincent's Tomas Haake Signature model, built for the relentless consistency his polyrhythmic drumming demands. Matt Halpern of Periphery plays a Promark signature model balancing technical control with enough mass to cut through Periphery's dense guitar layers. Blake Richardson of Between the Buried and Me plays Vic Firth's American Classic 3A, a lighter stick suited to his dynamic, fill-heavy approach.

## 3A vs 5A vs 5B for Djent

**3A (Blake Richardson's choice):** Lightest option — maximum articulation for fast, fill-heavy patterns. Best for genre-hopping prog/djent.

**5A (standard):** Balanced control and power — the natural middle ground before committing to a signature model.

**5B-adjacent signature (Wincent Haake, Promark Halpern):** Built to specific tolerances for professional consistency, slightly more mass for cutting through denser mixes.

**Verdict:** Start with a standard 5A to build technique. Move to a signature model once you know whether your playing leans toward Haake's metronomic precision or Halpern's heavier, jazz-influenced control.

## FAQ

**What drumsticks does Tomas Haake use?**
Tomas Haake plays the Wincent Tomas Haake Signature, built to tight tolerances for the metronomic consistency Meshuggah's polyrhythmic compositions demand.

**What drumsticks does Matt Halpern use?**
Matt Halpern plays a Promark signature model balancing technical control with enough mass to cut through Periphery's dense guitar arrangements.

**What drumsticks does Blake Richardson use?**
Blake Richardson plays Vic Firth's American Classic 3A, chosen for the dynamic sensitivity BTBAM's fill-heavy compositions require.

**What stick diameter is best for djent?**
3A or 5A is the djent standard — thinner than the 5B used in thrash or death metal, rewarding articulation over raw power.

**Do I need special sticks for a triggered djent kit?**
Not strictly, but consistency matters more on a triggered rig. Aluminum sticks (Ahead Slim) maintain identical weight and balance across every take.

**Should I use a lighter stick for djent than other metal subgenres?**
Generally yes — djent's technical, fill-heavy demands reward the control a 3A or 5A provides over the raw power of a 5B or heavier stick.
