## Why Metalcore Sticks Need Strong Rebound for Breakdown-Heavy Dynamics

Metalcore's defining structural feature — the breakdown — puts a specific demand on a drumstick: seconds after a stripped-down, half-time breakdown groove, the same stick has to snap back into fast, technical, blast-beat-adjacent patterns.

Matt Greiner of August Burns Red plays Vic Firth's American Classic 5A, suited to his technical control and intricate fills. Ben Koller of Converge plays Vic Firth's American Classic 5B for the mass his aggressive, hardcore-rooted style demands. Nick Augusto, who powered Trivium's thrash-influenced metalcore, also plays Vic Firth's American Classic 5B.

## 5A vs 5B for Metalcore

**5A (Matt Greiner):** Lighter mass rewards intricate fills and dynamic control. Best for technical metalcore.

**5B (Ben Koller, Nick Augusto):** Greater mass makes breakdowns land with crushing weight, with strong rebound into fast technical passages.

**Verdict:** Start with 5B (Vic Firth or Promark) if your metalcore leans toward crushing breakdowns. Move to 5A if your material prioritizes technical fills and dynamic range.

## FAQ

**What drumsticks does Matt Greiner use?**
Matt Greiner plays Vic Firth's American Classic 5A, chosen for the technical control his Grammy-nominated drumming requires.

**What drumsticks does Ben Koller use?**
Ben Koller plays Vic Firth's American Classic 5B, matching Converge's hardcore-rooted metalcore sound since "Jane Doe" (2001).

**What drumsticks does Nick Augusto use?**
Nick Augusto plays Vic Firth's American Classic 5B, the same model as Ben Koller, throughout his 2009–2014 tenure powering Trivium's thrash-influenced metalcore.

**Are 5A or 5B drumsticks better for metalcore?**
5B is favored by Ben Koller and Nick Augusto for breakdown mass. 5A, Matt Greiner's choice, suits technical metalcore with intricate fills and dynamic shifts.

**What stick gives the best rebound for breakdown-to-technical transitions?**
American hickory sticks in the 5A–5B range, like the Vic Firth American Classic models played by all three drummers in this guide, balance mass and rebound for that transition.

**Do metalcore drummers need durable sticks for touring?**
Yes — metalcore's relentless touring schedule makes durability a real concern. Ahead's aluminum Acroite series doesn't chip or crack the way wood does over a long tour run.
