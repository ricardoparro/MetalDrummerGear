## What Hi-Hats Are Used in Groove Metal?

Groove metal's mid-tempo (100–140 BPM), riff-locked patterns put the hi-hat at the center of the pocket. Unlike thrash or death metal, where hi-hats are chosen to survive hundreds of rapid strokes per minute, groove metal hi-hats are voiced for tight, deliberate articulation on fewer, more purposeful hits — every chick needs to land exactly on the riff.

Vinnie Paul built groove metal's founding hi-hat template on 14" Sabian AA/AAX Hi-Hats, giving Pantera's grooves tight, cutting articulation across "Cowboys from Hell" and "Vulgar Display of Power." Chris Adler of Lamb of God took a drier, more controlled approach with 14" Meinl Byzance Dark Hi-Hats, giving Lamb of God's syncopated riff-locking grooves a cutting, low-wash voice on "Ashes of the Wake" and "Sacrament." Shannon Larkin of Godsmack runs 14" Sabian AAX Stage Hi-Hats for crisp articulation behind two decades of multi-platinum Godsmack albums. Igor Cavalera of Sepultura and Cavalera Conspiracy plays 14" Paiste RUDE Hi-Hats for the harsh, cutting aggression behind "Roots Bloody Roots" and Sepultura's tribal groove metal evolution.

This guide breaks down hi-hat choices for groove metal — comparing four drummers whose setups define the genre's tight, riff-locked hi-hat demands, with recommendations from budget to professional touring rigs.

**Key Points:**

- Vinnie Paul's Sabian AA/AAX hi-hats defined groove metal's founding, cutting articulation
- Chris Adler's Meinl Byzance Dark hi-hats deliver the drier, more controlled response Lamb of God's syncopated riffs need
- Shannon Larkin's Sabian AAX Stage Hi-Hats give Godsmack's radio-ready groove rock crisp precision
- Igor Cavalera's Paiste RUDE hi-hats bring harsh, cutting aggression to Sepultura's tribal groove metal attack

## What Makes a Great Groove Metal Hi-Hat?

### 🎩 Tight Articulation for Groove Locking

Groove metal's deliberate, riff-locked patterns need hi-hats that articulate cleanly at mid-tempo rather than smearing together. Chris Adler's Meinl Dark Hi-Hats stay dry and controlled for Lamb of God's syncopated grooves, while Vinnie Paul's Sabian 14" hi-hats keep Pantera's grooves tight and defined.

**Recommendation:** 14" hi-hats voiced for tight, controlled articulation at mid-tempo

### 🦶 Chick Weight and Response

Since groove metal relies on fewer, more deliberate hits rather than rapid strokes, hi-hats need a defined, purposeful chick that lands exactly on the riff's accent points. A mushy or unpredictable chick undermines the pocket-locked feel the genre demands.

**Recommendation:** Medium to medium-heavy weight for a precise, purposeful chick

### ⚖️ Dry vs Bright Voicing

Igor Cavalera's Paiste RUDE hi-hats favor harsh, cutting weight suited to Sepultura's tribal groove attack, while Chris Adler's Meinl Byzance Dark line favors dry, controlled response for Lamb of God's tighter syncopation. Both philosophies work — the choice depends on how much bite your riffs need.

**Recommendation:** Harsh, cutting RUDE-style hi-hats for tribal weight; dry Byzance Dark-style hi-hats for tight syncopation

### 📏 Diameter

14" is the groove metal hi-hat standard — Vinnie Paul, Chris Adler, Shannon Larkin, and Igor Cavalera all use 14" pairs. This size balances projection with the controlled articulation the genre's mid-tempo riffing demands.

**Recommendation:** 14" for standard groove metal projection and control

### 🛡️ Touring Durability

Groove metal's heavy, deliberate hits and constant touring schedule mean hi-hats need to survive sustained, high-impact use without cracking or losing their voicing prematurely.

**Recommendation:** Mid-to-heavy gauge B20 or CuSn8 bronze built for sustained, high-impact touring use

## FAQ

**What hi-hats are used in groove metal?**
Vinnie Paul of Pantera used 14" Sabian AA/AAX Hi-Hats for tight, cutting articulation. Chris Adler of Lamb of God uses 14" Meinl Byzance Dark Hi-Hats for dry, controlled precision. **What size hi-hats for groove metal?**

**Best hi-hats for riff-locked grooves?**
Hi-hats with a defined, purposeful chick handle groove metal's deliberate, accented patterns best. Groove metal hi-hats split into three proven traditions: the bright, cutting articulation that Vinnie Paul established as the genre's founding voice, the dry, controlled precision that Chris Adler favors for tightly syncopated riff-locking, and the harsh, raw aggression that Igor Cavalera brings to tribal groove metal.

Whatever you choose, prioritize a defined, purposeful chick over flashy features — groove metal's pocket-driven riffing depends on every hi-hat hit landing exactly where the riff demands it.
