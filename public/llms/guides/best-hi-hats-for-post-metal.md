## What Hi-Hats Are Used in Post-Metal?

Post-metal hi-hat choice hinges on one question no other metal subgenre asks quite as directly: can a single pair sound genuinely atmospheric at a whisper and still cut through a wall of downtuned guitars at full volume? The genre's long-form crescendos — quiet, textural builds erupting into crushing, cathartic peaks — demand a hi-hat with real dynamic range rather than a pair optimized purely for speed or purely for wash.

No roster drummer records under a literal "post-metal" tag, so this guide features the genre's closest working analogues by shared atmosphere and dynamic range. Brann Dailor of Mastodon plays 14" Meinl Byzance Dark Hi-Hats, delivering a dry, focused chick that anchors both Mastodon's crushing riffs and its jazz-influenced restraint. Danny Carey of Tool plays 15" Paiste Signature Sound Edge Hi-Hats, built for an open, washy feel across whisper-quiet, odd-time grooves. Mario Duplantier of Gojira pairs 14" Zildjian K Sweet and A Custom Hi-Hats for flexible textures across atmospheric, environmentally-themed passages, while Abe Cunningham of Deftones plays 14" Zildjian K Custom Hi-Hats chosen specifically to serve the band's stated blend of "heavy and ambient music."

This guide breaks down what makes a great post-metal hi-hat — wide dynamic range, atmospheric voicing, and controlled response at both extremes — and which specific models these four influential drummers rely on, from budget starter pairs to the professional setups behind post-metal's most dynamically ambitious records.

## What Makes Great Post-Metal Hi-Hats?

### 🎭 Wide Dynamic Range

Abe Cunningham's K Custom Hi-Hats give a dark, nuanced chick suited to Deftones' atmospheric passages, while the same hi-hats hold up cleanly once the band shifts to crushing heaviness. A post-metal pair needs to serve both ends convincingly.

**Recommendation:** Hi-hats voiced to respond musically at low volume without losing control at full weight

### 🌑 Warm, Dark-Leaning Voicing

Dailor's Meinl Byzance Dark Hi-Hats favor warm, controlled tone that adds atmosphere to slow builds rather than cutting harshly through them.

**Recommendation:** Dark or dry-voiced hi-hats for atmospheric, controlled response

### ✨ Open, Washy Texture for Quiet Passages

Danny Carey's 15" Sound Edge Hi-Hats give an open, washy feel on odd-time grooves — exactly the kind of textural voice that carries a slow-building post-metal passage before the crescendo hits.

**Recommendation:** A pair with an open, washy option for textural build-up sections

### 🌐 Flexible Textures for Compositional Ambition

Mario Duplantier's dual 14" K Sweet and A Custom Hi-Hats reflect post-metal's tendency toward long-form, texturally ambitious compositions that need more tonal colors than a single hi-hat voice can offer.

**Recommendation:** Two distinct hi-hat voices for drummers writing longer, texturally varied material

### 🛡️ Touring Durability

Post-metal's long sets and repetition-heavy songwriting mean hi-hats need to survive heavy, sustained crescendo impact night after night without cracking prematurely.

**Recommendation:** Mid-to-heavy gauge B20 bronze hi-hats built for repeated heavy impact

## Dark & Focused vs Open & Washy Hi-Hats for Post-Metal

Post-metal's hi-hat choices split into two complementary camps, defined by how each drummer approaches the genre's atmosphere-to-crescendo dynamic range:

**Dark & Focused (Meinl Byzance Dark — Brann Dailor's choice; Zildjian K Custom — Abe Cunningham's choice):**
- Dry, focused chick with complex, low overtones
- Suits restrained, atmospheric verses that need to hold together at low volume
- Shifts convincingly into crushing weight without losing control
- The controlled, close-voiced end of post-metal's hi-hat spectrum

**Open & Washy (Paiste Signature Sound Edge — Danny Carey's choice; Zildjian K Sweet/A Custom — Mario Duplantier's choice):**
- Open, shimmering texture built for slow, textural build-up sections
- Wider dynamic range from whisper to full crescendo
- Suits longer, compositionally ambitious material
- The atmospheric, texture-forward end of post-metal's hi-hat spectrum

**Post-Metal Verdict:** Choose dark and focused (Meinl Byzance Dark or Zildjian K Custom) if your material leans toward restrained, crushing-to-ambient shifts in the Mastodon or Deftones tradition. Choose open and washy (Paiste Sound Edge or Zildjian K Sweet/A Custom) if you write longer, texturally ambitious build-ups in the Tool or Gojira tradition.

## Find Your Post-Metal Hi-Hat Voice

Post-metal hi-hats split into two proven traditions: the dark, focused character that Brann Dailor and Abe Cunningham both build their setups around, and the open, washy sound that Danny Carey and Mario Duplantier favor for longer, texturally ambitious compositions.

If your material leans toward restrained, crushing-to-ambient shifts, start with a 14" Meinl Byzance Dark pair or the budget HCS Dark — both stay controlled and atmospheric at low volume while still carrying real weight at the crescendo. If you write longer, texturally ambitious build-ups, a 15" Paiste Sound Edge pair or dual Zildjian K Sweet/A Custom setup will give you the open, shimmering range your compositions demand.

Whatever you choose, prioritize dynamic range over raw speed — the hi-hat has to serve both the whisper-quiet verse and the crushing, cathartic peak, and it needs to move between them with total control.
