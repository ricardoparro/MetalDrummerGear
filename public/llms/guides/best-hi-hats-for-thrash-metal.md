## What Hi-Hats Does Lars Ulrich Use?

Hi-hats carry thrash metal's relentless rhythm guitar work more than any other cymbal in the kit. Sixteenth-note downpicking, rapid-fire gallop rhythms, and constant tempo surges all demand a hi-hat that responds instantly and stays articulate under the genre's dense, aggressive riffing — a sluggish or washy pair simply can't keep pace with 200+ BPM thrash.

Lars Ulrich of Metallica has used 14" Dyno Beat Hi-Hats from the Zildjian A Custom Series throughout the band's career, giving Metallica's dense guitar walls a tight, cutting chick sound that has defined the classic thrash metal hi-hat tone since the "Load" sessions. Dave Lombardo of Slayer relies on Paiste's RUDE and 2002 Series for a raw, explosive hi-hat character that punctuates Slayer's most violent passages. Charlie Benante of Anthrax also favors the same Paiste RUDE and 2002 combination, giving his thrash riffing the same powerful, full-bodied projection.

This guide covers exactly what makes a great thrash metal hi-hat — weight, finish, and response — and which specific models the genre's Big Four-era legends play, from budget starter pairs to the professional setups heard on classic and modern thrash records.

**Key Points:**

- Zildjian A Custom (14" Dyno Beat) defines Lars Ulrich's tight, cutting thrash metal hi-hat chick
- Paiste RUDE & 2002 gives Dave Lombardo and Charlie Benante raw, explosive hi-hat aggression
- 14" is the thrash metal hi-hat standard across all three featured drummers
- Fast response at 200+ BPM separates a great thrash hi-hat from one that washes out under pressure

## What Makes Great Thrash Metal Hi-Hats?

### ⚡ Cutting Power

Thrash metal's dense, downpicked rhythm guitar walls demand hi-hats with strong upper-midrange presence. Lars Ulrich's brilliant-finish Zildjian A Custom hi-hats emphasize cutting attack — without enough cut, the hi-hat chick disappears under the guitars at live volumes.

**Recommendation:** Brilliant or bright-finish B20 bronze for maximum cut in dense mixes

### ⚡ Fast Response at High Tempo

Thrash riffing moves fast — hi-hat patterns need to speak instantly and stay locked to rapid 16th-note rhythm guitar work. Medium weight hi-hats keep pace with thrash tempos better than heavy, slow-responding alternatives.

**Recommendation:** Medium weight for fast response at 180-220+ BPM

### 🔊 Projection at Live Volume

Dave Lombardo and Charlie Benante both favor Paiste's CuSn8 bronze RUDE and 2002 lines for their exceptional projection — important for cutting through arena and festival PA systems where stage volume is extreme.

**Recommendation:** CuSn8 bronze (Paiste 2002/RUDE) for maximum live projection

### 📏 Diameter

14" is the standard thrash metal hi-hat size — Lars Ulrich, Dave Lombardo, and Charlie Benante all use 14" pairs. This size balances projection with the fast response the genre's rapid-fire riffing demands.

**Recommendation:** 14" for standard thrash metal projection and response

### 🛡️ Durability Under Aggressive Playing

Thrash drumming is physically demanding — hard, fast strokes stress hi-hats more than moderate-tempo styles. B20 bronze handles repeated heavy hits better than budget B8 alloys, which crack faster under sustained aggressive use.

**Recommendation:** B20 bronze for serious thrash players; B8 acceptable for learning
