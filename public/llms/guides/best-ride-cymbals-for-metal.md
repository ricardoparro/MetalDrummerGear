## Why the Ride Cymbal Matters More Than You Think in Metal

The ride cymbal carries the pulse under blast beats, drives double-bass verses, and — via its bell — cuts through a wall of guitars when a fill or chorus needs extra punch. A ride that's too thin washes out under gain-drenched mixes; one that's too dark disappears entirely once the whole band kicks in.

George Kollias (Nile) rides a 21" Zildjian A Custom Mega Bell Ride for the piercing accents that cut through Nile's dense technical death metal arrangements past 240 BPM. Mario Duplantier (Gojira) plays a 21" Zildjian Z Custom Mega Bell Ride for the same reason. Mike Portnoy runs a 21" Sabian Raw Bell Dry Ride across Dream Theater and his other projects for dry, controlled stick definition, while Dave Lombardo (Slayer) rides a 22" Paiste Reign Power Ride for a heavier, more explosive wash. Gene Hoglan has played a 22" Sabian AAX Ride across Death, Testament, and Dethklok for over two decades.

## Mega Bell vs Standard Bell Ride for Metal

**Mega Bell Rides (George Kollias, Mario Duplantier):**
- Oversized bell for piercing, cutting accents through dense mixes
- Best when bell patterns are a core part of your playing

**Standard Bell Rides (Gene Hoglan, Dave Lombardo):**
- More balanced stick/bell response for all-around playing
- Best for drummers who want versatility without specializing for bell work

**Verdict:** Start with a 21"-22" standard-bell pro ride like the Sabian AAX. Upgrade to a Mega Bell design once you know how much bell work your playing actually demands.

## FAQ

**What ride cymbal do metal drummers use?**
George Kollias (Nile) rides a 21" Zildjian A Custom Mega Bell Ride, Mario Duplantier (Gojira) plays the darker 21" Z Custom Mega Bell Ride, and Mike Portnoy rides a 21" Sabian Raw Bell Dry Ride. Gene Hoglan has played a 22" Sabian AAX Ride across Death, Testament, and Dethklok for over two decades.

**What size ride cymbal is best for metal?**
21"-22" is the metal standard, balancing low-end weight and wash with enough speed for fast patterns. 20" rides respond faster but offer less body.

**Do I need a Mega Bell ride for metal drumming?**
Not necessarily. A Mega Bell design like the Zildjian A Custom Mega Bell (George Kollias's choice) is a genuine upgrade if bell accents are central to your playing. A standard-bell option like the Sabian AAX (Gene Hoglan's choice) is more versatile.

**What's the best budget ride cymbal for metal?**
The Zildjian ZBT Ride (€70–90) delivers a bright, cutting B8 bronze tone with a usable bell at a true beginner price. The Sabian XSR Monarch Ride (€130–160) brings B20 bronze to a mid-range price.

**Dry ride vs washy ride — which is better for metal?**
Dry, controlled rides like Sabian's Raw Bell Dry Ride (Mike Portnoy's choice) keep stick definition tight, ideal for technical and progressive metal. Washier rides like Paiste's Reign Power Ride (Dave Lombardo's choice) build a bigger wall of sound suited to thrash.
