# How to Sound Like Alex Bent — Trivium Drum Sound Guide

**Drummer:** Alex Bent  
**Band:** Trivium  
**Genre:** Modern Metal / Technical Death Metal  
**Guide URL:** https://metalforge.io/guides/how-to-sound-like-alex-bent

## Overview

Alex Bent served as Trivium's drummer from 2017 to 2025, stepping into the band's demanding drum chair at producer Mark Lewis's recommendation after building his reputation in the technical death metal underground with Brain Drill, Arkaik, and Battlecross. Over four studio albums — "The Sin and the Sentence" (2017), "What the Dead Men Say" (2020), "In the Court of the Dragon" (2021), and "Struck Dead" (2025) — Bent elevated Trivium's rhythmic complexity while keeping the band's melodic metal accessibility intact.

What separates Bent from a pure technical death metal drummer is restraint. He can execute blast beats and intricate fills with extreme metal clarity, but he never loses sight of the song — blending riff-locked precision with arena-ready groove.

## Kit Setup

Bent plays a **Pearl Reference Series** kit with a maple/birch hybrid shell in matte black:

- **Kick Drum:** 22" x 18" Bass Drums (double kick)
- **Snare:** 14" x 6.5" Pearl Reference Maple/Birch
- **Rack Toms:** 10" x 8", 12" x 9"
- **Floor Toms:** 16" x 16"
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Brilliant Series — 14" Medium Hi-Hats, 16"/18"/19" Medium Thin Crashes, 21" Medium Ride, 18" China
- **Pedals:** Axis Percussion Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B
- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke P3 Clear (kick), Remo Ambassador Coated (snare), Remo Emperor Clear (toms)

## Tuning & Setup

Bent tunes for clarity and articulation so every note in his fast, technical passages stays audible:

- **Kick:** Medium-tight tension with foam pad and port hole for a clicky, defined attack that stays articulate during blast beats.
- **Snare:** Medium-high tension, light muffling. The hybrid shell delivers a full, cutting crack without choking its natural resonance.
- **Toms:** Medium-high tension, one Moongel per tom, tuned roughly a third apart for quick, focused decay on fast fills.

## Technique Tips

Bent plays **matched grip** with a compact, controlled stroke honed in technical death metal. His blast beats stay distinctly articulate between kick, snare, and hi-hat even at extreme tempos, and his fills serve song transitions rather than showing off.

**Signature patterns:**

- **Clarity Blast Beat (180–220 BPM, Advanced):** Distinct articulation across kick, snare, and hi-hat at extreme speed. Practice at half tempo, focusing on even volume across every hit.
- **Riff-Locked Double Bass (160–200 BPM, Advanced):** Double bass patterns that mirror Trivium's guitar riffs note for note rather than defaulting to straight 16ths.
- **Song-Serving Fills (Variable, Intermediate-Advanced):** Compact fills that punctuate structural transitions and lead directly into the next section's downbeat.

## Practice Routine

1. **Blast Beat Clarity Builder (15 min daily):** At 100 BPM, play blast beats focusing on identical volume across kick, snare, and hi-hat. Increase 5 BPM only when every hit sounds identical.
2. **Riff Transcription Drill (20 min daily):** Transcribe a Trivium guitar riff by ear, then build a double bass pattern that mirrors its exact rhythm.
3. **Structural Fill Writing (10 min daily):** Write three short fills that lead directly into the next song section's downbeat, then compare which feels most natural.

**Common mistakes:** Sacrificing articulation for raw speed; playing fills that don't resolve into the next section; ignoring the guitar riff when building double bass patterns; over-muffling drums past natural resonance.
