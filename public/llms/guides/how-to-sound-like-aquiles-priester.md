# How to Sound Like Aquiles Priester — Angra Drum Sound Guide

**Drummer:** Aquiles Priester  
**Band:** Angra  
**Genre:** Power Metal / Neoclassical Metal  
**Guide URL:** https://metalforge.io/guides/how-to-sound-like-aquiles-priester

## Overview

Aquiles Priester (born 1971, São Paulo, Brazil) is the engine behind Angra and one of the most technically accomplished drummers in power metal history. Since joining Angra in 2000, he has set the standard for neoclassical power metal drumming — blazing double-bass patterns delivered with mechanical evenness, enormous dynamic range, and the musical intelligence to deploy extreme technique in service of melody rather than ego. His performance on Temple of Shadows (2004), Angra's masterpiece, is studied by power metal drummers worldwide as a benchmark for combining extreme speed with genuine musicality.

## Tuning & Setup

Aquiles tunes for attack, clarity, and projection. At 180+ BPM double-bass tempos, excess sustain blurs consecutive kick strokes into mud:

- **Kick:** Medium-firm tension with foam muffling touching the batter head. The Evans EQ3 built-in ring provides controlled attack without deadening the tone. Ported resonant head for mic placement.
- **Snare:** Medium-high tension on a brass shell. The brass rewards moderate-to-high tension with warm crack and body rather than thin brightness. Minimal muffling — one Moongel only if recording demands it.
- **Toms:** Medium tension with full sustain. Power metal fills need resonant, pitched toms. One small Moongel per tom maximum, or none at all.

## Technique Tips

Aquiles uses **matched grip** with a deeply relaxed technique built for endurance. His philosophy is that tension is the enemy of speed — at 180+ BPM, any grip or body tension rapidly degrades both pace and tone. He uses heel-up double-bass technique with ankle-drive for upper-range speed and hip/leg engagement for endurance on long passages.

**Signature patterns:**

- **Neoclassical Double-Bass Endurance Run (165–182 BPM, Expert):** Continuous 16th-note double-bass with absolute evenness between left and right feet. Pairs with a riding right hand and backbeat snare. Build from 120 BPM; raise by 5 BPM only when current tempo feels effortless.
- **Progressive Feel Transitions (Variable, Advanced):** On Temple of Shadows tracks, Aquiles moves between blast beats, double-bass grooves, lyrical 4/4 patterns, and orchestral sections within a single song. Each transition is immediate and musical. Practice each section independently before connecting transitions.
- **Power Metal Speed Groove (175–190 BPM, Expert):** Makes extreme speed feel like a groove through relaxed technique and phrasing. The hi-hat breathes, fills are musically placed. Target the feel, not just the notes — recording yourself and listening back is essential.

**Key songs to study:** *Spread Your Fire* (Temple of Shadows, 2004) · *Heroes of Sand* (Temple of Shadows, 2004) · *Rebirth* (Rebirth, 2001) · *The Temple of Hate* (Temple of Shadows, 2004) · *Carry On* (Temple of Shadows, 2004)

## Practice Routine

1. **Double-Bass Evenness Drill (20 min daily):** Play continuous 16th-note double-bass at 100 BPM. Record and listen back — any audible volume difference between feet means more slow practice. Raise by 5 BPM only when perfectly even on recording. Goal: even double-bass at 170+ BPM.
2. **Ride-Over-Double-Bass Coordination (15 min daily):** Play continuous double-bass at 140 BPM and add right-hand ride patterns progressively — quarters, 8th notes, then mixed. Do not let the feet change when the hands change. Goal: steady ride over unbroken double-bass at 160+ BPM.
3. **Temple of Shadows Song Study (30 min, 3x/week):** Learn "Spread Your Fire" or "Heroes of Sand" section by section using Aquiles' "Inside My Drums" breakdowns as reference. Slow to 70% speed first. Record full-speed and compare to original. Goal: note-accurate performance matching his dynamics and feel.

**Common mistakes:** Sacrificing evenness for speed (an uneven fast tempo sounds worse than an even slow one); tensing up during double-bass passages (tension creates fatigue and kills speed); ignoring the right-hand ride pattern (Aquiles' groove lives as much in the hands as the feet); not recording practice sessions.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Aquiles Priester use?**  
A: Aquiles Priester plays Pearl Reference Series drums — Pearl's top-tier hybrid-shell line combining maple, birch, and mahogany. He uses two 22" kick drums with a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal and a 14" x 6.5" Pearl Reference Brass snare. He has been a Pearl endorser throughout his Angra career.

**Q: What cymbals does Aquiles Priester use?**  
A: Aquiles plays Sabian HHX and AAX cymbals. His setup includes 14" HHX Evolution hi-hats, 18" and 19" HHX X-Plosion crash cymbals, a 21" HHX Groove ride, and an 18" AAX Chinese. The HHX line is chosen for its articulate, fast response that stays clear at 180+ BPM double-bass tempos.

**Q: How does Aquiles Priester play double bass so fast?**  
A: Aquiles attributes his double-bass speed to relaxation and building slowly from an even foundation — never sacrificing evenness for speed. He uses heel-up technique and has stated that tension is the primary limiter of double-bass speed. Drummers who chase tempo before mastering evenness plateau early.

**Q: What pedals does Aquiles Priester use?**  
A: Aquiles uses Pearl Demon Drive double pedals — Pearl's flagship direct-drive unit. The direct-drive linkage provides mechanical consistency and speed response needed for sustained 16th-note patterns at 180+ BPM across a full touring set.

**Q: What sticks does Aquiles Priester use?**  
A: Aquiles plays Vic Firth Aquiles Priester Signature sticks — a model he co-designed with Vic Firth. Earlier in his career he played Vater Fusion 55A sticks, which remain a comparable option for drummers seeking his pre-signature setup feel.

**Q: Is Aquiles Priester still with Angra?**  
A: Yes. Aquiles rejoined Angra in 2023 for their current album cycle after his original tenure from 2000 to 2012. He is currently active with the band, touring and recording.
