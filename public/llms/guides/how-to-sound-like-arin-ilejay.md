# How to Sound Like Arin Ilejay — Avenged Sevenfold Drum Sound Guide

## Overview

Arin Ilejay joined Avenged Sevenfold in 2011 under circumstances no drummer would envy — permanently replacing founding drummer Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan, who died in December 2009, after Dream Theater's Mike Portnoy filled in as a tribute performer on "Nightmare" (2010). Rather than attempt to replicate The Rev's technically labyrinthine style, Ilejay committed to power, consistency, and service to the song.

That approach crystallized on "Hail to the King" (2013), the only A7X studio album Ilejay recorded — a deliberate tribute to classic heavy metal influences including Metallica, Black Sabbath, and Guns N' Roses. The album debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 and topped the charts in the UK, Ireland, Canada, Brazil, and Finland, going on to sell multi-platinum worldwide.

## Tuning & Setup

Ilejay tuned for punch and authority, keeping A7X's classic-metal riffs driving and defined without sacrificing low-end weight:

- **Kick:** Medium-tight tension, moderate muffling. Punchy, attack-focused tuning keeps galloping double-bass patterns defined while retaining low-end weight.
- **Snare:** Medium tension, minimal muffling. The maple/mahogany blend balances crack and body so it sits forward in the mix without losing warmth.
- **Toms:** Medium tension, one Moongel per tom. Resonant heads tuned slightly lower than batter heads for a focused decay that cuts through a layered mix.

## Technique Tips

Ilejay plays **matched grip** with an authoritative, high-velocity attack built for arena projection. Rather than showcase technical complexity, his playing on "Hail to the King" emphasizes consistent power and a driving mid-tempo groove that services classic heavy metal songwriting.

**Signature patterns:**

- **Galloping Double Bass Groove (100–160 BPM, Intermediate):** Double bass locked to classic-metal galloping riffs — think Metallica's "Sad But True." Learn the guitar riff first, then match the pattern exactly to its rhythm.
- **Classic Metal Half-Time Verse (140–155 BPM, Beginner-Intermediate):** A weighted, deliberate half-time feel in verses before opening into a driving chorus. Practice locking the backbeat to a metronome, focusing on even spacing rather than speed.
- **China-Accented Riff Punctuation (Variable, Intermediate):** China cymbal hits marking riff transitions and structural moments. Map out the transitions first, then place accents only at those points.

## Practice Routine

1. **Galloping Riff-Lock Drill (15 min daily):** Pick a galloping thrash or classic-metal riff and practice a double bass pattern that matches its exact rhythm at full commitment, not just correct timing.
2. **Half-Time Backbeat Builder (10 min daily):** Play a half-time backbeat against a metronome at 140–155 BPM, focusing on identical volume and spacing on every snare hit for two minutes straight.
3. **Structural Accent Mapping (10 min daily):** Map out the exact riff transitions in a song, then place china cymbal accents only at those transition points.

**Common mistakes:** Adding technical complexity where the song calls for restraint and power; playing double bass without matching the riff's exact rhythmic feel; letting half-time grooves feel sparse instead of weighted; scattering china accents randomly instead of at structural transitions.

## FAQ

**Q: What makes Arin Ilejay's drumming style unique?**  
A: Ilejay deliberately traded The Rev's technically extravagant, jazz-influenced approach for straightforward power and groove on "Hail to the King" — a classic heavy metal tribute album. His skill lies in making simple, driving grooves feel authoritative and arena-ready rather than underpowered.

**Q: What gear do I need to sound like Arin Ilejay?**  
A: Ilejay played a DW Collector's Series kit with a 14" x 5.5" maple/mahogany snare, Zildjian A Custom cymbals, a DW 9000 double bass pedal, and Vic Firth American Classic 5A sticks. A DW Design Series kit with Zildjian Planet Z cymbals delivers a similar attack-focused sound at a fraction of the cost.

**Q: What songs should I learn first to sound like Arin Ilejay?**  
A: Start with "Shepherd of Fire" for its half-time verse groove, then "Hail to the King" for its galloping, Metallica-referencing verse pattern. "This Means War" is the best showcase of his driving double-bass work, and "Coming Home" demonstrates his dynamic restraint.

**Q: Did Arin Ilejay play on Avenged Sevenfold's Nightmare album?**  
A: No. "Nightmare" (2010) was recorded by Dream Theater's Mike Portnoy as a tribute performance following The Rev's death in 2009. Ilejay joined A7X in 2011 as the permanent replacement and recorded only one studio album with the band, "Hail to the King" (2013).

**Q: How do I get Arin Ilejay's drum sound?**  
A: Tune the kick for punch and attack, keep the snare at moderate tension for a direct crack with body, and lock every double bass pattern to the guitar riff's exact galloping rhythm. Commit fully to each stroke — Ilejay's arena-scale power comes from conviction and consistency, not flash.
