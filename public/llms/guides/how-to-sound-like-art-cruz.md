# How to Sound Like Art Cruz — Lamb of God Groove Metal Drum Sound Guide

**Drummer:** Art Cruz  
**Band:** Lamb of God  
**Genre:** Groove Metal / American Metal

## Overview

When Chris Adler departed Lamb of God in 2019, the band needed a drummer who could honor one of groove metal's most demanding drum legacies while bringing fresh energy of his own. Art Cruz (born May 27, 1988, in Downey, California) was that drummer — first filling in on Lamb of God's 2018 North American tour supporting Slayer's farewell run before the position became permanent in 2019.

Cruz brings a distinctive background to the role: deathcore intensity from his years with Winds of Plague, industrial groove sensibility from Prong, and the preparation to learn Adler's intricate back catalogue with respect rather than trying to replicate it note-for-note. His studio debut, the self-titled *Lamb of God* (2020), and the follow-up *Omens* (2022) showcase a band that evolved without losing its identity.

## Kit Setup

Cruz plays a **Ludwig Classic Maple** kit:

- **Kick Drum:** 22" x 18" Bass Drum (single, with double pedal)
- **Snare:** 14" x 6.5" Ludwig Snare (Maple or Brass)
- **Rack Toms:** 10", 12"
- **Floor Toms:** 14", 16"
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom / K Series — 14" A Custom hi-hats, 17"/18" A Custom crashes, A Custom ride, A Custom China
- **Pedals:** Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth 5B / Power 5B
- **Heads:** Evans EMAD2 (kick), Evans G2 Coated / HD Dry (snare), Evans G2 Coated (toms)

## Tuning & Setup

Cruz tunes for punch and presence that projects at arena volumes while staying tight enough to lock precisely with Lamb of God's syncopated riff patterns:

- **Kick:** Medium-firm tension with EMAD2 built-in dampening ring. Controlled, punchy attack keeps double-pedal strokes locked to the guitar's syncopation.
- **Snare:** Medium-high tension, minimal muffling. Punchy, present crack punctuates syncopated riffs without overwhelming the guitar work.
- **Toms:** Medium tension, light muffling. Stays articulate and resonant for functional, service-first fills.

## Technique Tips

Cruz's playing serves Lamb of God's syncopated, riff-driven songwriting rather than showcasing technique for its own sake. His single-kick-plus-double-pedal footwork locks tightly to the guitar syncopation, and his fills stay functional — built to support the riff rather than interrupt it.

**Signature patterns:**

- **Syncopated Groove-Metal Pocket (100–140 BPM, Intermediate):** Locks kick and snare precisely to Lamb of God's syncopated, off-grid guitar riffs. Isolate a riff and build a kick pattern that hits exactly on its accents.
- **Trick Pedal Double-Kick Runs (150–190 BPM, Advanced):** Achieves footwork from a single bass drum with a double pedal rather than twin kicks, favoring compact, consistent runs.
- **Service-First Fills (Variable, Intermediate):** Short, functional transitions that set up the next riff rather than technical showcases.

**Key songs to study:** *Memento Mori* (Lamb of God, 2020) · *Nevermore* (Omens, 2022) · *Grayscale* (Omens, 2022)

## Practice Routine

1. **Riff-Lock Groove Drill (15 min daily):** Isolate a syncopated riff, build a kick pattern that lands exactly on its accents, then layer snare and hi-hat around it.
2. **Double-Pedal Consistency Drill (15 min daily):** Play sustained single strokes on a double pedal at moderate tempo, focusing on even spacing before increasing tempo.
3. **Service-First Fill Writing (10 min daily):** Write a short 1-2 beat fill that leads directly into the next riff's downbeat.

**Common mistakes:** Playing a generic groove under a syncopated riff instead of locking the kick to its accents; chasing double-kick speed before consistency; extending fills into technical showcases; over-muffling the kick drum.
