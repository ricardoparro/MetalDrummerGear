# How to Sound Like Charlie Benante — Anthrax Thrash Drum Sound Guide

**Drummer:** Charlie Benante  
**Band:** Anthrax  
**Genre:** Thrash Metal  
**Guide URL:** https://metalforge.io/guides/how-to-sound-like-charlie-benante

## Overview

Charlie Benante (born 1962, The Bronx, New York) is one of the most historically significant drummers in heavy metal. As the driving force behind Anthrax since 1983, Benante is widely credited with popularizing the blast beat — the rapid alternating snare-kick barrage that became the defining technical marker of extreme metal drumming. His work on Among the Living (1987) and Spreading the Disease (1985) established the Big Four thrash drumming aesthetic, and his playing on "Caught in a Mosh," "Madhouse," and "Indians" remains essential study material for any serious metal drummer.

What separates Benante from other Big Four drummers is his combination of extreme technical capability with groove and musicality. He plays thrash at 175+ BPM without losing the infectious energy that made Anthrax the most street-level and fun of the Big Four bands.

## Tuning & Setup

Benante tunes for maximum attack and punch:

- **Kick:** Medium-tight tension with foam touching the batter head and a ported resonant head. Short, punchy attack — sustain is an enemy when double-bass passages need clarity.
- **Snare:** High tension on both heads. Steel shell at high tension delivers the bright, cutting crack that cuts through 170+ BPM thrash riffs. Minimal muffling.
- **Toms:** Medium-tight with one Moongel per drum. Punch and definition over sustain.

## Technique Tips

Benante plays **matched grip** with a powerful, forward-leaning attack. His technique prioritizes speed and impact equally — blast beats at extreme tempos while maintaining clarity on every hit.

**Signature patterns:**

- **The Anthrax Thrash Blast (170–200 BPM, Advanced):** The pattern Benante popularized — snare-kick alternation in rapid unison. Build from 120 BPM with even volume between all limbs before increasing speed.
- **Caught in a Mosh Groove (170–180 BPM, Intermediate-Advanced):** Riff-locking kick placements at 175 BPM where the kick maps to guitar accent points. Learn the riff first; the kick pattern follows.
- **Double-Bass Thrash Gallop (150–180 BPM, Advanced):** Continuous 16th-note double-bass motion driving forward momentum beneath a driving snare. Endurance matters as much as speed.
- **Indians Mid-Tempo Groove (140–155 BPM, Intermediate):** Melodic tom fills and a more open feel — the underappreciated side of Benante's dynamic range.

**Key songs to study:** *Caught in a Mosh* (Among the Living, 1987) · *Madhouse* (Spreading the Disease, 1985) · *Indians* (Among the Living, 1987) · *Antisocial* (State of Euphoria, 1988) · *Be All, End All* (State of Euphoria, 1988)

## Practice Routine

1. **Blast Beat Foundation Builder (20 min daily):** Start at 120 BPM with alternating single strokes on snare and right foot kick on every L stroke. Increase 5 BPM per session. Play 2 clean minutes at each tempo before moving up. Even volume between hands and kick is the goal.
2. **Riff-Locking Kick Drill (20 min per session):** Listen to "Caught in a Mosh" and map where the kick lands on guitar accent points. Practice that riff-locking concept across multiple Anthrax songs. The skill is hearing riff accents and mapping kick placements to them.
3. **Double-Bass Endurance (15 min daily):** Alternating 16th notes at 120 BPM for 2 minutes. Increase 5 BPM when clean. Equal volume from both feet throughout.

**Common mistakes:** Over-muffling the snare (it should crack and project); neglecting mid-tempo material (Benante is more than blast beats); uneven double-bass volume (left foot must match right); rushing blast beats before building clean foundation at slower tempos.

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Charlie Benante use?**  
A: Charlie Benante plays Tama Starclassic Maple drums. His setup includes two 22" x 18" kick drums, a 14" x 6.5" Tama Charlie Benante Signature snare, 10" and 12" rack toms, and 16" and 18" floor toms.

**Q: Did Charlie Benante invent the blast beat?**  
A: Benante is widely credited with popularizing the blast beat in thrash metal. While proto-blast techniques existed in hardcore punk, Benante's use on Anthrax recordings in the mid-1980s introduced it to the thrash and death metal communities that followed. He is consistently cited as the key figure who brought blast beats into metal drumming consciousness.

**Q: What cymbals does Charlie Benante use?**  
A: Charlie Benante uses Paiste cymbals — primarily the RUDE and 2002 series. His setup includes 14" RUDE hi-hats, 18" and 19" RUDE Crash Rides, a 20" 2002 Power Ride, and an 18" 2002 China.

**Q: What double pedal does Charlie Benante use?**  
A: Charlie uses the Tama Speed Cobra double pedal — Tama's spring-loaded cam-drive design known for smooth, fast response and excellent rebound, suited to the rapid double-bass gallop patterns that drive Anthrax's thrash sound.

**Q: What sticks does Charlie Benante use?**  
A: Charlie Benante plays his own Vic Firth Charlie Benante Signature sticks, designed for both powerful accent strokes and sustained blast beat sequences.
