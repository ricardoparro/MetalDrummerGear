# How to Sound Like Hellhammer — Mayhem / Dimmu Borgir Drum Sound Guide

**Drummer:** Hellhammer (Jan Axel Blomberg)  
**Band:** Mayhem / Dimmu Borgir  
**Genre:** Black Metal / Symphonic Black Metal  
**Guide URL:** https://metalforge.io/guides/how-to-sound-like-hellhammer

## Overview

Hellhammer (born Jan Axel Blomberg on August 2, 1969, Oslo, Norway) is the most consequential drummer in Norwegian black metal history. Since joining Mayhem in 1988 as a teenager, he has defined the sonic parameters of extreme black metal drumming — combining inhuman blast beat velocity with the razor precision that separates a musical performance from mere noise. His performance on "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" (1994) set a standard for blast beat drumming that remains the benchmark thirty years later.

Beginning in 1999, Hellhammer took on simultaneous membership in Dimmu Borgir, one of symphonic black metal's most commercially successful bands. His ability to adapt from Mayhem's raw, atmospheric extremity to Dimmu Borgir's orchestrated grandeur demonstrates a musical range that eludes most extreme metal drummers. He plays barefoot — a deliberate technique providing direct tactile feedback through the bass drum pedal essential for maintaining tempo precision at extreme speeds.

- **Kick Drums:** 22" x 18" (x2 — double kick configuration) with Pearl Demon Drive Double Bass Pedal

- **Hi-Hat Stand:** Pearl H-2050 Eliminator
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B (wood tip)
- **Heads:** Remo Coated Ambassador (snare), Remo Emperor Coated (toms), Remo Powerstroke 3 (kick)
- **Notable:** Plays barefoot for direct tactile pedal feedback

## Tuning & Setup

Pearl Reference shells deliver focused attack with natural warmth — tune to reinforce that quality:

- **Kick:** Medium tension with Remo Powerstroke 3. Target defined attack transient with controlled low-end — not excessive boom or sustain. The attack transient must cut through black metal's dense guitar wall. The Powerstroke's internal dampening ring handles sustain control without eliminating the fundamental.
- **Snare:** Medium-bright batter tension on the Reference shell — high enough to project through dense guitars without EQ, low enough to retain body. One Moongel at the edge for recording. Tune snare wires tight for fast blast beat response.
- **Toms:** Medium tension, minimal muffling. Maple/mahogany shells provide natural sustain control — let toms ring more openly than expected. Even response across all five toms is essential for dramatic fills to flow dynamically.

## Technique Tips

Hellhammer uses **matched grip** with a controlled, powerful delivery — each hit deliberate rather than wild, which gives blast beats at 200+ BPM their structured, architectural quality.

**Signature patterns:**

- **Black Metal Blast Beat (180–220+ BPM, Advanced):** Alternating kick and snare at extreme tempo with hi-hat on every 8th note. Consistency of force is everything — every kick stroke must match the previous one in volume and timing. Build from 100 BPM with matching volumes before increasing speed. Blur means uneven dynamics, not just high tempo.
- **Barefoot Pedal Coordination (Variable, Intermediate-Advanced):** Left-foot hi-hat independence is fundamental. Practice hi-hat foot patterns completely separately from hand patterns before combining at speed. At blast beat tempos, the left foot must be as reliable as the right.
- **Dynamic Contrast (Variable, Intermediate):** Move from blast beat devastation to pp atmospheric playing within one track — no transition time. Mayhem's compositional approach uses this contrast as a structural tool. Controlled quiet is as important as controlled blast.
- **Dramatic Tom Fill (160–200 BPM passages, Intermediate):** Five-tom descending runs between blast sections. Single strokes at even volume across all toms before adding speed. The fill functions as a transitional phrase — it must flow smoothly.

**Key songs to study:** *Freezing Moon* (De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas, 1994) · *Buried by Time and Dust* (De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas, 1994) · *A Bloodsword and a Colder Sun* (Ordo Ad Chao, 2007) · *Puritania* (Puritanical Euphoric Misanthropia, 2001) · *Procreating Satan* (De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas, 1994)

## Production Notes: Raw vs Polished

**Raw Black Metal (De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas approach):** Grieghallen Studio with producer Pytten gave the 1994 album its cold, dry character — minimal room reverb, minimal processing, natural Pearl attack preserved. For home recording: close-mic snare and toms, two overheads wide and close to the shells rather than high above the kit, avoid heavy compression. The kit's natural attack character dominates. Zildjian's brightness cuts through dense guitars without EQ intervention.

**Symphonic Black Metal (Dimmu Borgir approach):** Orchestrated productions require a more polished, defined drum sound that sits cleanly under orchestral elements — more room sound, gentle parallel compression, EQ for clarity across a wide spectrum. The Pearl Reference shells' consistency serves both contexts without fundamental setup changes.

## Practice Routine

1. **Even-Force Blast Beat Builder (20 min/session):** Blast beats from 100 BPM. Record yourself. Are kick and snare equal in volume? Increase by 5 BPM per session only when recording sounds even. Target: 180+ BPM clean, even-force blasts.
2. **Left Foot Independence (15 min daily):** Groove pattern with hands and right foot while left foot plays hi-hat on every beat, then every 8th note. Record: any rushing or dragging shows up immediately. Left foot must be as reliable as right.
3. **Dynamic Contrast Drill (10 min daily):** 8 bars of blast beats at maximum intensity, then immediately 8 bars of ppp soft playing. No transition time. Controlled quiet = as important as controlled blast.
4. **Song Study — De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas (30 min, 3x/week):** Learn "Freezing Moon" measure-by-measure. Focus on where blasts start and end, atmospheric sections between them, hi-hat dynamics within the blast. Compare recording against original.

**Common mistakes:** Chasing blast speed before even dynamics; ignoring dynamic range (the quiet sections are essential, not optional); skipping left-foot development; over-muffling the kit (black metal favors natural shell attack); treating blast beats as just speed rather than structured rhythmic architecture.

## FAQ

**Q: What drums does Hellhammer use?**  
A: Hellhammer plays Pearl Reference drums — Pearl's flagship production shells with maple/African mahogany hybrid construction. Double 22" x 18" kick drums, three rack toms (10", 12", 14"), two floor toms (16", 18"). The Reference series is his consistent choice across both Mayhem and Dimmu Borgir.

**Q: What cymbals does Hellhammer play?**  
A: Hellhammer plays Zildjian — primarily A Custom series. Setup: 14" A Custom Hi-Hats, 17" and 19" A Custom Crashes, Z Custom 20" Dark Ride, A 18" China Boy. Zildjian's cold, bright character cuts through black metal's dense guitar frequencies — this brightness is central to the Norwegian black metal sound.

**Q: How does Hellhammer achieve his blast beat speed?**  
A: Three factors: Pearl Demon Drive direct-drive pedal (eliminates chain-drive delay for immediate beater response), barefoot playing (direct tactile feedback for precise foot coordination at extreme tempos), and decades of technique built on even-force fundamentals. His blasts are structurally precise — each stroke in its exact rhythmic position with consistent force — not just fast.

**Q: Why does Hellhammer play barefoot?**  
A: Playing barefoot provides direct tactile feedback through the bass drum pedal and hi-hat stand. At 200+ BPM blast beats, shoe soles dampen the proprioceptive information needed for precise left-foot/right-foot coordination. Barefoot eliminates that buffer, giving immediate feedback about pedal position, spring tension, and footboard return — critical for maintaining blast beat precision across a full performance.

**Q: What is the best Hellhammer song to learn first?**  
A: Start with "Freezing Moon" from De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas (1994). It showcases the core blast beat architecture with clear transitions between blasts and atmospheric sections — ideal for understanding how Hellhammer uses extreme tempo as a compositional tool. "Buried by Time and Dust" from the same album demonstrates dynamic range within extreme playing.

**Q: How do I get the De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas drum sound?**  
A: Close-mic the snare and toms, two overheads wide and close to the shells, avoid heavy compression or reverb. The cold, dry Grieghallen quality comes from minimal room sound in the mix — the kit's natural attack character dominates. Pearl shells and Zildjian's bright cymbals in a dry room capture the essential character.
