# How to Sound Like Hellhammer — Mayhem / Dimmu Borgir Drum Sound Guide

**Drummer:** Hellhammer (Jan Axel Blomberg)  
**Band:** Mayhem / Dimmu Borgir  
**Genre:** Black Metal / Symphonic Black Metal  
**Guide URL:** https://metalforge.io/guides/how-to-sound-like-hellhammer

## Overview

Hellhammer (born Jan Axel Blomberg on August 2, 1969, in Oslo, Norway) is the defining drummer of Norwegian black metal. Since joining Mayhem in 1988, he has shaped what extreme metal drumming sounds like — mechanically precise blast beats with tonal clarity, double-bass endurance across hour-long sets, and the ability to adapt between raw black metal rawness and polished symphonic production. His drumming on Mayhem's "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" (1994) established the sonic template that generations of black metal drummers have studied, while his Dimmu Borgir work demonstrates how those same skills translate to orchestral contexts. No other drummer has operated at the intersection of raw and refined extreme metal as effectively.

- **Heads:** Remo Powerstroke 3 Clear (kick), Remo Emperor Coated (snare), Remo Emperor Clear (toms)

## Tuning & Setup

Hellhammer tunes for definition and clarity at extreme tempos:

- **Kick:** Medium tension with internal foam touching batter head. Ported resonant head. The kick must be punchy enough that each hit registers at 220 BPM without sustaining into the next note.
- **Snare:** Medium-high to high tension. Bright crack to cut through dense guitar frequencies. Minimal muffling (one Moongel or small tape strip for studio; none live). Snare wire tension kept tight for clean response at blast beat tempos.
- **Toms:** Medium tension with one small Moongel per drum. Controlled sustain — toms must speak and stop cleanly in fast fill passages. Tune each tom to a distinct musical pitch for separation in dense mixes.

## Technique Tips

Hellhammer uses **matched grip** with a deeply relaxed hand technique. Speed comes from rebound, not muscle. His hands generate minimal force and allow the stick to return naturally — the same motion at 120 BPM and 220 BPM.

**Signature patterns:**

- **Norwegian Black Metal Blast Beat (180–220 BPM, Advanced):** Snare and kick alternate in single-stroke pattern while hi-hat or ride provides secondary layer. Every note must be equal volume and distinct — not a noise wall. Build from 120 BPM; increase 5 BPM only when clean.
- **Open Hi-Hat Raw Blast (160–200 BPM, Advanced):** Hi-hat slightly open during blasts, creating the sizzling wash of De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas. Atmospheric and raw rather than controlled and tight. The sound of Norwegian black metal's first decade.
- **Symphonic Double-Bass Lock (120–160 BPM, Intermediate-Advanced):** Extended 16th-note double-bass patterns locked precisely to click track for Dimmu Borgir orchestral arrangements. No rhythmic drift permitted when playing against a full orchestra. Left and right foot must be equal in volume and timing.
- **Economy Fill (Variable, Intermediate):** Fills of two to four notes maximum. Every fill serves the next riff section rather than displaying technique. Count your fill notes — if you have five, remove two.

## Practice Routine

1. **Blast Beat Clarity Builder (20 min daily):** Single-stroke snare alternating with kick at 120 BPM. Every note equal volume. Increase 5 BPM only when clean. Goal: defined blasts at 200+ BPM.
2. **Double Bass Endurance Drill (15 min daily):** Continuous 16th-note double bass for 2 minutes at 120 BPM. Rest 1 minute. Repeat 4 rounds. Increase tempo weekly. Goal: even feet at 160+ BPM for 2 minutes.
3. **Raw vs. Polished Adaptation (15 min, 3x/week):** Alternate between open hi-hat blast (raw Mayhem feel) and closed hi-hat precision blast (Dimmu Borgir symphonic feel). Two bars of each without stopping. Goal: immediate stylistic switching.

**Common mistakes:** Gripping sticks too tight (kills rebound and speed); muddy blast beats where notes wash together (clarity is Hellhammer's signature); weak left foot double bass (must match right foot at 180+ BPM); over-filling (maximum impact with minimum notes).

## Recording/Mixing Tips

**Raw black metal (De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas style):**
- Room microphone prominent in the mix — ambient sound is part of the aesthetic
- Minimal compression on snare — transient crack should be sharp and uncontrolled
- Kick drum not sample-replaced; the natural Remo Powerstroke thud defines the era
- Overall drum mix sitting behind guitars in the frequency spectrum — guitars dominate, drums serve the atmosphere

**Polished symphonic (Dimmu Borgir / Death Cult Armageddon style):**
- Click track mandatory — orchestral arrangements require metronomic precision
- Snare sample-reinforced or replaced for brightness in orchestral mixes
- Kick drum tight and punchy with high-pass filter removing low-end mud
- Drums sit on top of orchestral strings rather than beneath guitar — they drive the arrangement

## FAQ

**Q: What drum kit does Hellhammer use?**  
A: Hellhammer plays Pearl Reference Series drums — Pearl's flagship maple/birch hybrid shell kit. His setup includes 22" bass drums (double), a 14" x 5.5" or 6.5" Pearl Reference snare, and standard tom configurations. He has been a long-term Pearl endorser and uses their Demon Drive double pedal.

**Q: How does Hellhammer play blast beats so fast and clearly?**  
A: His blast beats are defined by relaxed hands and natural stick rebound rather than muscular force. By allowing the stick to spring back from the drumhead, he achieves both speed and clarity — each note registers before the next arrives. The Pearl Demon Drive direct-drive pedal contributes its immediate response to the kick's precision. Decades of daily practice built the muscle memory.

**Q: What cymbals does Hellhammer use?**  
A: Hellhammer uses Zildjian cymbals — A Custom hi-hats and crashes, Z Custom ride, and an Oriental China for harsh accents. Zildjian A Custom cymbals are bright and fast-decaying, cutting through dense guitar frequencies without washing out at blast beat tempos.

**Q: What is the difference between his Mayhem and Dimmu Borgir styles?**  
A: With Mayhem, Hellhammer plays for raw black metal atmosphere — open hi-hats, looser rhythmic feel, fills that serve darkness over technicality. With Dimmu Borgir, he locks to click tracks for orchestral arrangements where any drift creates audible chaos in the brass and string mix. The blast beats are structurally similar; the intent and precision demands are completely different.

**Q: Can a beginner learn Hellhammer's blast beats?**  
A: Not immediately — blast beats at his tempo and clarity level require advanced technique, typically 2-3 years of dedicated practice. Intermediate drummers can learn the pattern structure at 120-140 BPM and build from there. Focus on clarity first: a defined 140 BPM blast is more valuable than a sloppy 200 BPM blur.
