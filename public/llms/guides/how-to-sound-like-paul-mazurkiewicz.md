# How to Sound Like Paul Mazurkiewicz — Cannibal Corpse Drum Sound Guide

## Overview

Paul Mazurkiewicz is one of death metal's most consistent and influential drummers. As Cannibal Corpse's original drummer since their 1988 formation in Buffalo, New York, Paul has appeared on every Cannibal Corpse album — across more than three decades of the best-selling death metal catalog in history. From "Eaten Back to Life" (1990) through "Violence Unimagined" (2021), Paul's drumming has been the rhythmic foundation of the genre's flagship band. His signature technique is the two-kick shuffle groove — a double bass pattern where a slight dynamic imbalance between feet creates a propulsive, rolling feel within brutal death metal context. Unlike blast-speed-focused drummers, Paul approaches death metal as a groove challenge: his kick patterns follow guitar riff attack points precisely, his snare placement is deliberate, and his fills serve the song's structural logic. Pearl Reference drums and the Pearl Demon Drive double pedal define his recorded sound across 15+ studio albums.

## Tuning & Setup

Paul tunes for attack and definition. Cannibal Corpse's production is dense — guitars, bass, and drums all occupy significant sonic space. His drums need to cut through without competing:

- **Kick:** Medium-tight tension, moderate muffling (half-pillow touching both heads). Port hole in resonant head (3-4") helps with mic placement and controls natural sustain. Each double bass note must be individually audible in the two-kick shuffle.
- **Snare:** High tension, minimal muffling (one Moongel strip). The Free-Floating design provides maximum resonance — the Moongel controls excess ring without killing the live crack.
- **Toms:** Medium-high tension with one Moongel each. Quick attack for fast cascade fills, tuned in clear intervals so the fill sounds melodically intentional.

## Technique Tips

Paul uses **matched grip** with a heel-up double bass technique. His approach prioritizes riff-synchronization — the kick follows guitar riff attack points rather than running independent patterns.

**Paul's Core Techniques:**

- **The Two-Kick Shuffle Groove (140–170 BPM, Intermediate-Advanced):** Paul's signature double bass pattern with a slight dynamic imbalance between feet — right foot slightly louder, left foot slightly softer — creating a rolling shuffle feel within death metal grooves. Slow to 80 BPM and feel the asymmetry before adding speed. Don't over-exaggerate it; the shuffle is subtle.
- **Riff-Locked Double Bass (150–185 BPM, Advanced):** Paul's kick follows guitar riff rhythm precisely. Learn the guitar riff by ear first. Play the riff rhythm on kick alone. Add snare on 2 and 4. Build the full groove outward from the riff's internal logic. 'Hammer Smashed Face' and 'Evisceration Plague' are essential study tracks.
- **Death Metal Tom Cascade (variable, Intermediate):** Descending single-stroke fills from snare to high tom to low tom to floor tom, ending on a kick-snare unison crash. These fills mark structural boundaries. Practice at consistent volume across all toms — no accidental soft hits.
- **Cannibal Corpse Groove Pocket:** Paul locks tightly with guitarists Pat O'Brien and Rob Barrett. His kick follows their attack points so precisely that guitar and drums move as a unified mass. Developing this riff-awareness is more important than adding blast beat speed.

**Key songs to study:** *Hammer Smashed Face* (Tomb of the Mutilated, 1992) · *Stripped, Raped and Strangled* (The Bleeding, 1994) · *Evisceration Plague* (Evisceration Plague, 2009) · *Frantic Disembowelment* (The Wretched Spawn, 2004) · *Covered with Sores* (Gallery of Suicide, 1998)

## Practice Routine

1. **Two-Kick Shuffle Builder (15 min daily):** Play a basic death metal groove at 80 BPM. Introduce a slight dynamic difference between feet — right slightly louder. Increase to 140 BPM over weeks. The asymmetry should feel natural, not forced.
2. **Guitar Riff Kick Tracking (20 min daily):** Choose a Cannibal Corpse track. Listen to the guitar riff rhythm. Play that rhythm on kick alone with a metronome. Add snare on 2 and 4. Add hi-hat. Build layers from the riff outward. Goal: riff-locked kick on 5 Cannibal Corpse tracks.
3. **Tom Cascade Control (10 min daily):** Single-stroke descending cascade from high tom to floor tom at consistent volume. Every hit equal power. Practice at 60 BPM before adding to grooves.

**Common mistakes:** Playing the shuffle too heavily (subtle asymmetry, not a limp); kick patterns running independent of guitar riffs (lock in with what the guitars play); rushing fills (Paul's fills are deliberate and powerful, not hurried); neglecting endurance training (Cannibal Corpse plays 90-minute sets at these tempos).

## FAQ

**Q: What is the 'two-kick shuffle' and why is it associated with Paul Mazurkiewicz?**  
A: The two-kick shuffle is Paul's signature double bass technique where the two feet play with a slight dynamic imbalance — the stronger foot slightly louder — creating a rhythmic shuffle feel within fast death metal double bass patterns. Combined with riff-locking kicks, this gives Cannibal Corpse songs a propulsive, groove-oriented feel that distinguishes them from pure blast-focused bands.

**Q: How long has Paul been with Cannibal Corpse?**  
A: Paul is an original Cannibal Corpse member, co-founding the band in Buffalo, NY in 1988. He has appeared on every Cannibal Corpse studio album and is one of the longest-tenured original members in death metal history.

**Q: What Pearl pedal does Paul Mazurkiewicz use?**  
A: Paul uses the Pearl Demon Drive double pedal. The direct-drive mechanism provides zero-lag response, critical for tracking guitar riff rhythms precisely at death metal tempos.

**Q: How do I start learning Cannibal Corpse drumming as a beginner?**  
A: Start with mid-tempo tracks like 'Stripped, Raped and Strangled' or 'Covered with Sores' before attempting the fastest material. Learn the guitar riffs first — Paul's kick patterns are extensions of those riffs, making the drum patterns logical rather than arbitrary.
