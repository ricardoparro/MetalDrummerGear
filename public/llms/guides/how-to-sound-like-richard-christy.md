# How to Sound Like Richard Christy — Death / Iced Earth Drum Sound Guide

**Drummer:** Richard Christy  
**Band:** Death / Iced Earth  
**Genre:** Death Metal / Power Metal  
**Guide URL:** https://metalforge.io/guides/how-to-sound-like-richard-christy

## Overview

Richard Christy holds a rare position in metal drumming: he successfully performed in two stylistically distant metal subgenres with authority — technical death metal (Death, live 1999-2000, captured on "Live in L.A.") and American power metal (Iced Earth, studio albums "Horror Show" (2001) and "The Glorious Burden" (2004)). Death's complex Chuck Schuldiner compositions demanded technical death metal precision and syncopated kick placement. Iced Earth's galloping power metal required double bass endurance, dramatic fills, and the ability to drive large-scale epic arrangements. Richard delivered both convincingly. Later, frequent appearances on The Howard Stern Show made him one of the most publicly visible extreme metal drummers — introducing his drumming to a mainstream audience that had never encountered the genre. His Tama Iron Cobra 900 Rolling Glide double pedal is central to his approach in both styles: smooth, consistent response suited to both extended power metal gallops and technical death metal precision.

## Tuning & Setup

Richard tunes for projection that works across power metal and death metal contexts. His drums must cut through loud guitar arrangements in both genres:

- **Kick:** Medium-tight tension, moderate muffling (half-pillow touching both heads). Port hole in resonant head (3-4"). Aim for punchy attack with controlled sustain — each foot must speak individually in gallop passages.
- **Snare:** High tension, minimal muffling (one Moongel or bare). Steel shell adds brightness — cuts through both power metal arrangements and death metal density.
- **Toms:** Medium-high tension with one Moongel each. Tuned in musical intervals for melodic cascade fills in Iced Earth material; fast attack for brief technical fills in Death material.

## Technique Tips

Richard uses **matched grip** with a heel-up double bass technique adaptable to multiple metal contexts. His style emphasizes energy and musicality over pure technical display.

**Richard's Core Techniques:**

- **Power Metal Double Bass Gallop (160–190 BPM, Intermediate-Advanced):** Tama Iron Cobra 900 Rolling Glide drives triplet or 16th note double bass runs under half-time snare. Essential for Iced Earth material. Practice at 120 BPM — focus on perfectly even spacing between feet before adding speed. The gallop must groove, not just be fast.
- **Technical Death Metal Kick Tracking (150–185 BPM, Advanced):** Richard's Death-influenced playing features syncopated kick patterns following Chuck Schuldiner's complex riff logic. Transcribe the guitar riff. Identify attack points. Build the kick pattern from the riff outward. 'Spirit Crusher' and 'Scavenger of Human Sorrow' demonstrate how sophisticated kick placement can be.
- **Epic Metal Fill (variable, Intermediate):** Large, dramatic fills marking section boundaries in Iced Earth arrangements. Snare roll building to a tom cascade, landing on kick-snare unison crash. The drama is in the crescendo — practice the buildup-and-release shape specifically.
- **Genre-Switch Adaptability:** Richard's key skill is adapting feel to musical context. Power metal requires theatrical energy and steady half-time snare feel. Death metal requires precision and syncopation. Practice switching between these contexts without stopping.

**Key songs to study:** *Spirit Crusher* (Death - The Sound of Perseverance, 1998 — live version) · *Travel in Stygian* (Iced Earth - Horror Show, 2001) · *Burning Times* (Iced Earth - The Glorious Burden, 2004) · *Dracula* (Iced Earth - Horror Show, 2001) · *Declaration Day* (Iced Earth - The Glorious Burden, 2004)

## Practice Routine

1. **Power Metal Gallop Builder (15 min daily):** Steady 8th notes on hi-hat with right hand. Snare on beats 2 and 4. Double bass triplet gallop underneath. Start at 100 BPM. Increase 10 BPM weekly. Goal: solid gallop at 180+ BPM.
2. **Technical Death Metal Kick Tracking (20 min daily):** Choose a Death track. Identify guitar riff attack points. Play riff rhythm on kick only with metronome. Add minimal hand pattern. Build to full groove. Goal: kick follows riff attack points naturally.
3. **Genre-Switch Drill (10 min daily):** Play 8 bars of power metal gallop groove. Switch to a death metal technical groove for 8 bars without stopping. Return to power metal. Repeat. Goal: fluid transitions between stylistic contexts.
4. **Epic Fill Development (10 min daily):** Snare roll crescendo → tom cascade → kick-snare unison crash. Practice the full arc slowly until the buildup-and-release feels instinctive.

**Common mistakes:** Playing death metal and power metal with identical feel (each requires different energy); chasing blast speed before developing gallop endurance (power metal double bass is its own discipline); neglecting fill drama in power metal context (Iced Earth calls for theatrical fills, not just fast ones); over-complicating Death-style patterns (Richard's approach is surgical, not busy).

## FAQ

**Q: What bands has Richard Christy played with?**  
A: Richard Christy performed with Death (live, 1999-2000, documented on "Live in L.A.") and Iced Earth (studio albums "Horror Show" and "The Glorious Burden," live touring 2001-2006). He later became known to mainstream audiences through The Howard Stern Show.

**Q: How does Richard's Iced Earth drumming differ from his Death work?**  
A: Iced Earth is theatrical power metal — gallop double bass, dramatic fills at section boundaries, half-time snare feel during verses. Death is surgical technical death metal — syncopated kick patterns following Schuldiner's complex riff logic, less predictable snare placement. Both require different energy and approach.

**Q: What double pedal does Richard Christy use?**  
A: Richard uses the Tama Iron Cobra 900 Rolling Glide double pedal. The Rolling Glide cam provides smooth, consistent response — more forgiving than the Power Glide cam, adapting well to both power metal groove feel and death metal precision.

**Q: How did Richard Christy become known outside metal?**  
A: Richard became a recurring personality on The Howard Stern Show, where his metal drumming became a feature segment. His appearances introduced Stern's mainstream audience to death metal and power metal drumming.

**Q: What is the best starting point for learning Richard Christy's style?**  
A: Start with Iced Earth's "Horror Show" (2001). The power metal context is more approachable than Death's technical material, and Richard's gallop double bass on tracks like 'Damien' and 'Jack' are excellent endurance training. Once comfortable with power metal gallops, move to Death's "Live in L.A." for the technical death metal vocabulary.
