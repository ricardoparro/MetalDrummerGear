# Igor Cavalera — Drum Kit, Gear & Technique

Igor Cavalera is the co-founding drummer of Sepultura (1984–2006) and Cavalera Conspiracy (2007–present), widely regarded as one of the most innovative drummers in metal history. His fusion of thrash metal intensity with Brazilian tribal percussion — particularly on Chaos A.D. (1993) and Roots (1996) — created a template that influenced groove metal, nu-metal, and a generation of heavy music.

## Kit Overview

Igor Cavalera plays a Tama Starclassic Maple drum kit — the powerful, resonant configuration that anchored Sepultura's transformation from thrash metal aggression to tribal groove metal across landmark albums like Chaos A.D. (1993) and Roots (1996). The Starclassic Maple's warm, focused tone supports both the punishing metal foundation and the tribal percussion vocabulary — atabaques, caixas, surdos — that made Roots a landmark fusion of metal and Brazilian percussion.

The cornerstone of the Igor Cavalera drum set is his Tama S.L.P. 14×6.5" G-Maple snare, delivering the punishing crack that drives groove metal riffs on "Refuse/Resist" and "Roots Bloody Roots." Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series cymbals define the sonic aggression of the Igor Cavalera drum kit: 14" RUDE Hi-Hats for harsh, cutting articulation; 18" and 19" RUDE Crashes for explosive, slow-decay accents; a 22" RUDE Power Ride for heavyweight rhythmic definition; and an 18" 2002 China for tribal punctuation. A Tama Iron Cobra double pedal powers his double bass drive, while Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks complete this drum set forged across two decades with Sepultura and Cavalera Conspiracy.

## Technique Highlights

- **Tribal percussion fusion:** Integrated atabaques, surdos, and caixas into metal compositions — pioneered on Roots (1996) with Xavante tribe collaboration
- **Groove metal foundation:** Shifted from thrash blast beats to heavy, mid-tempo groove patterns on Chaos A.D., influencing Korn, Soulfly, and the nu-metal generation
- **Polyrhythmic layering:** Combines standard metal kit with Brazilian Afro-rhythmic patterns for a syncopated, world-music-inflected feel
- **Dynamic range:** Moves from sparse tribal sections to full-force blast beats within single songs — "Territory" and "Attitude" exemplify this range

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What drum kit does Igor Cavalera use?**
A: Igor Cavalera uses a Tama Starclassic Maple drum kit with a Tama S.L.P. 14×6.5" G-Maple snare, Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series cymbals (14" RUDE Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" RUDE Crashes, 22" RUDE Power Ride, 18" 2002 China), and a Tama Iron Cobra double pedal.

**Q: What drum set does Igor Cavalera use?**
A: Igor Cavalera's drum set is a Tama Starclassic Maple — the platform he developed during Sepultura's most influential period. The Igor Cavalera drum set is notable for extending beyond a standard metal kit to include tribal percussion instruments (atabaques, surdos, caixas) alongside the core Tama configuration, reflecting the Roots-era fusion that defined his legacy.

**Q: What drums did Igor Cavalera play on Roots?**
A: On Roots (1996), Cavalera's drum set centered on a Tama Starclassic with Paiste cymbals, augmented by a full array of Brazilian tribal percussion — atabaques, surdos, repiniques, and caixas — recorded partly on location with the Xavante tribe in Brazil. The album's percussion texture was created in collaboration with Carlinhos Brown.

**Q: Why did Igor Cavalera leave Sepultura?**
A: Igor Cavalera departed Sepultura in 2006 following a management dispute (his wife and manager Dana Wells was let go by the band). He subsequently reunited with his brother Max to form Cavalera Conspiracy in 2007.
