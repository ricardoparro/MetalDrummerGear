## Angel of Death Opening

**Song:** Angel of Death | **Album:** Reign in Blood (1986) | **BPM:** 210 BPM | **Technique:** intro chaos | **Difficulty:** expert

The opening that changed thrash metal forever. Dave's explosive entrance after Tom Araya's iconic scream set the standard for extreme metal drumming. Pure controlled chaos.

### How to Play

- Immediate blast beat entry after the scream
- Rapid-fire snare and bass drum combination
- Cymbal accents drive the intensity
- Fills link each phrase with precision

### Key Elements

- This is advanced - build up to it gradually
- Practice the blast beat pattern at 150 BPM first
- Focus on the transition from opening into groove
- Watch Dave's live performances for technique insights

## Raining Blood Double Bass

**Song:** Raining Blood | **Album:** Reign in Blood (1986) | **BPM:** 190 BPM | **Technique:** main groove | **Difficulty:** advanced

After the thunderstorm intro comes one of metal's most recognizable double bass patterns. Dave's relentless attack creates the perfect backdrop for the song's apocalyptic imagery.

### How to Play

- Continuous 16th notes on double bass
- Snare provides the backbeat anchor
- Hi-hat controls dynamics throughout
- Crash cymbal accents mark transitions

### Key Elements

- Practice double bass 16ths at 120 BPM first
- Add snare once feet are consistent
- Work on stamina - this pattern is relentless
- Use a practice pad for bass drum technique

**Core Techniques:** [Double Bass](https://metalforge.io/technique/double-bass), [Groove](https://metalforge.io/technique/groove), [Dynamics](https://metalforge.io/technique/dynamics)

## Seasons in the Abyss Groove

**Song:** Seasons in the Abyss | **Album:** Seasons in the Abyss (1990) | **BPM:** 174 BPM | **Technique:** main groove | **Difficulty:** intermediate

A more groove-oriented approach from Dave, showing his versatility beyond pure speed. This mid-tempo thrasher demonstrates how restraint can be just as powerful as chaos.

### How to Play

- Driving hi-hat pattern anchors the groove
- Punchy snare on the backbeats
- Selective double bass for emphasis
- Dynamic control between sections

### Key Elements

- Focus on the groove feel, not just the notes
- Lock in with the guitar riff
- Use dynamics to build tension
- Great song for developing pocket and feel
