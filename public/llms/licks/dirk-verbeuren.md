## We'll Be Back / Tipping Point Thrash Drive

**Song:** Tipping Point | **Album:** The Sick, the Dying... and the Dead! (2022) | **BPM:** ~200 BPM | **Technique:** signature pattern | **Difficulty:** expert

Dirk Verbeuren brought a new level of extreme-metal precision to Megadeth when he joined in 2016, and his playing on 2022's The Sick, the Dying... and the Dead! — including "Tipping Point" — shows how he fuses thrash drive with death-metal technicality. A Belgian drummer who made his name in Soilwork and a long list of extreme bands, Verbeuren is renowned for blistering double bass, clean blast beats, and the inventive coordination that produced his signature "Dirk Blast." On a modern Megadeth track like this he channels all of that into relentless thrash propulsion: fast, even double bass driving the riffs, tight transitions between blasting and grooving, and fills that are technical without ever cluttering the song. His signature is control at speed — everything stays articulate and locked to the riff no matter how fast it gets. In his official drum playthrough you can see exactly how it's executed: the foot technique, the hand-foot coordination, and the way he shifts gears cleanly. For drummers, this is a study in modern thrash and extreme-metal drumming: it develops fast, even double bass, blast-beat control, and the coordination to move between feels at high tempo. The approach is to build the double bass slowly and evenly, lock it tightly to the riff rather than playing generically, and practise the transitions in isolation before stringing them together. "Tipping Point" shows why Verbeuren is regarded as one of the most technically complete drummers in modern metal.

- Drive the riffs with fast, even double bass locked tightly to the guitars
- Shift cleanly between blasting and grooving at high tempo
- Keep fills technical but uncluttered — serve the song
- Stay articulate at speed rather than smearing the notes
- Build the double bass slowly and evenly before chasing tempo

Verbeuren came up through the European extreme-metal scene, where evenness and endurance at high speed are everything, and he brings that discipline to a thrash burner like "Mechanix." ## King of the Threshold Blast Masterclass

**Song:** King of the Threshold | **Album:** Stabbing the Drama (2005) | **BPM:** ~210 BPM | **Technique:** signature pattern | **Difficulty:** expert

Before Megadeth, Dirk Verbeuren spent years as the drummer for Swedish melodic death metal greats Soilwork, and tracks like "King of the Threshold" from 2005's Stabbing the Drama are where he forged the extreme technique he's famous for. Soilwork's music demands a drummer who can blast, blend, and groove at high speed while keeping melodic-death music dynamic, and Verbeuren is one of the best to ever do it. His signature is the blast beat — he's so associated with the technique that he literally has one named after him, the "Dirk Blast," a hybrid blast that combines elements of conventional and gravity blasting for extra speed and texture. On "King of the Threshold" he weaves blasts, fast double bass, and tight grooves through the song, always keeping it clean and musical rather than a wall of noise. For drummers, this is a deep study in melodic-death and extreme-metal drumming: it develops blast-beat control, fast and even double bass, and the ability to transition between blasting and grooving while keeping the music dynamic. The approach is to build the blast beat slowly and evenly, develop the gravity-blast rebound that powers his hybrid technique, and practise locking the blasts and double bass to the riffs. "King of the Threshold" is a showcase of the technical foundation that made Verbeuren one of extreme metal's most respected and influential drummers.

### How to Play

- Weave blasts, fast double bass and tight grooves through the song
- Develop the gravity-blast rebound behind the hybrid "Dirk Blast"
- Keep the blasting clean and musical, not a wall of noise
- Lock the blasts and double bass tightly to the riffs
- Transition between blasting and grooving while keeping it dynamic

### Key Elements

- Build the blast beat slowly until it is even and controlled
- Develop the gravity-blast rebound behind the hybrid blast
- Lock the blasts and double bass to the riffs
- Keep the blasting clean — aim for musical, not noisy
