## Ace of Spades Driving Power

**Song:** Ace of Spades | **Album:** Ace of Spades (2008 re-recording) | **BPM:** ~280 BPM | **Technique:** signature pattern | **Difficulty:** advanced

"Ace of Spades" is the song that defines Motörhead, and although Phil Taylor played on the 1980 original, Mikkey Dee — Motörhead's drummer from 1992 until Lemmy's passing in 2015 — re-recorded it in 2008 and powered it on stage night after night for over two decades. Mikkey's version is a clinic in relentless, high-octane rock-metal drumming: the tempo is brutal, but he drives it with a punishing, locked-in groove, heavy backbeat, and the explosive energy that made Motörhead a force of nature. His signature is raw power married to rock-solid time — he plays fast and hard without ever rushing, and his fills are aggressive bursts that propel the song rather than decorate it. Mikkey is also a celebrated double-bass and technical player, but on "Ace of Spades" he channels all of that ability into pure drive, proving that taste and intensity matter as much as chops. In the isolated drum track you can hear how disciplined the part is beneath the chaos: the groove never wavers, the backbeat is enormous, and every fill lands clean. For drummers, "Ace of Spades" is a study in high-tempo rock-metal stamina and feel: it develops fast, steady time, a powerful backbeat that holds up at speed, and the endurance to sustain full intensity for a whole song. The approach is to lock the groove to a metronome and build the tempo gradually, keep the body relaxed so the speed is sustainable, and play the fills as controlled bursts. It's a definitive example of how Mikkey Dee turned breakneck speed into an unstoppable groove.

- Drive the song with aggressive fill bursts, not decoration

## Overkill Double-Bass Freight Train

**Song:** Overkill | **Album:** Overkill (2008 re-recording) | **BPM:** ~195 BPM | **Technique:** signature pattern | **Difficulty:** advanced

"Overkill" is famous for one of the most influential drum parts in heavy metal — the relentless double-bass pattern that rumbles like a freight train and is widely credited as a foundational moment for double-bass drumming in the genre. Phil Taylor created it on the 1979 original, but Mikkey Dee re-recorded the song in 2008 and made its thundering double bass a highlight of Motörhead's live shows, including the famous false endings where the band stops and the drums roar back to life. Mikkey's interpretation is a powerhouse: he drives the iconic double-bass roll with even, controlled feet and a huge backbeat, then unleashes the dramatic stop-start dynamics that make "Overkill" so thrilling live. His signature here is the combination of stamina and showmanship — sustaining that pounding double bass while milking the song's theatrical structure. In the isolated drum track you can hear how steady the double bass stays under the chaos and how precisely he hits the song's dynamic shifts. For drummers, "Overkill" is a classic study in double-bass drumming: it develops even, sustained double-bass technique, the endurance to keep it going, and the control to play dramatic stop-start dynamics cleanly. The approach is to build the double-bass roll slowly until both feet are even, develop the stamina to sustain it, and practise the false-ending hits so they land tight. It's a cornerstone of metal drumming history and a perfect vehicle for Mikkey Dee's power.

### How to Play

- Drive the iconic double-bass roll with even, controlled feet
- Anchor a huge backbeat over the rumbling double bass
- Play the dramatic stop-start dynamics and false endings cleanly
- Sustain the double bass with real endurance through the song
- Build the roll slowly until both feet are perfectly even

### Key Elements

- Develop stamina to sustain the double bass through the song

## Killed by Death Groove & Stamina

**Song:** Killed by Death | **Album:** Killed by Death (2008 re-recording) | **BPM:** ~165 BPM | **Technique:** signature pattern | **Difficulty:** advanced

"Killed by Death" is one of Motörhead's most beloved anthems and a permanent fixture of their live show, where it traditionally closed the set. Phil Taylor played the 1984 original, but Mikkey Dee re-recorded it in 2008 and powered it live for over two decades, bringing his trademark drive and intensity to its swaggering, mid-fast groove. The song is a perfect showcase of Mikkey's ability to make a powerful rock-metal groove feel enormous: the backbeat hits like a hammer, the time is rock solid, and he punctuates the arrangement with aggressive, well-placed fills that lift each section. His signature is exactly this blend of power, consistency, and showmanship — he serves the song with a huge, driving groove while injecting the energy that made Motörhead's live shows legendary. In the isolated drum track you can hear how heavy and steady the groove is and how cleanly his fills snap back into time. For drummers, "Killed by Death" is an accessible but rewarding study in powerful groove and stamina: it develops a strong, in-the-pocket backbeat at a driving tempo, the consistency to hold it for a whole song, and tasteful fills that mark transitions. The approach is to lock the groove to a metronome, focus on making the backbeat hit hard and steady, and place the fills where the song lifts. It's a great example of how Mikkey Dee carried Motörhead's classics with power and authority.

### How to Play

- Anchor a hammering, in-the-pocket backbeat at a driving tempo
