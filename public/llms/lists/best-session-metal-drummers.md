# Top 10 Best Session Metal Drummers — Complete Ranked Guide

> **Last updated:** 2026-07-10 · **Source:** [MetalForge.io](https://metalforge.io) · [View full list →](https://metalforge.io/lists/best-session-metal-drummers)

## Overview

Touring with one band is hard enough. These ten drummers built careers instead on being the player other artists call — hired guns and in-demand studio specialists whose credits span dozens of bands rather than a single flagship act. Session drumming in metal demands a different skill set from a full-time band member: the ability to walk into someone else's songs, learn a foreign vocabulary of riffs and tempos in days rather than years, and deliver a performance that serves someone else's vision without a full album cycle to settle in.

The best session and hired-gun drummers in metal, ranked. Gene Hoglan, Tim Yeung, Derek Roddy, Kevin Talley and more — the drummers other bands call when they need a studio specialist.

---

## Rankings

Ranked by documented performance records, genre-defining influence, and technical contribution. Top entries: Gene Hoglan, Tim Yeung, Derek Roddy, Kevin Talley, Navene Koperweis, and more.

### 1. Gene Hoglan

**Band:** Death / Testament / Dethklok
**Highlight:** Metal's ultimate hired gun, "The Atomic Clock"
**Why ranked here:** Gene Hoglan has drummed across more than 20 bands over four decades — Dark Angel, Death, Testament, Dethklok, Strapping Young Lad, and Fear Factory among them — with a metronomic precision so reliable it earned him his nickname. Few drummers in any genre have been trusted by so many different bandleaders to walk in and deliver a studio-ready performance. Hoglan earns rank #1 for the widest, longest-running session and guest-drumming résumé in metal.

### 2. Tim Yeung

**Band:** Morbid Angel / Hate Eternal / Vital Remains

**Why ranked here:** Tim Yeung has recorded and toured across Hate Eternal, Morbid Angel, Vital Remains, Divine Heresy, and Nile, building a reputation as one of extreme metal's most reliable session players for bands needing blast-beat speed and stamina on a deadline. His ability to slot into wildly different death metal projects without losing the technical ceiling each one demands makes him the genre's go-to call. ### 3. Derek Roddy

That reputation for showing up ready to deliver record-setting speed on short notice is exactly what makes a drummer valuable as a session player rather than just a full-time member. Roddy earns rank #3 for proving hired-gun reliability at the absolute technical extreme.

### 7. Travis Orbin

**Band:** Darkest Hour / ex-Periphery
**Highlight:** A prolific session musician with his own studio-work channel
**Why ranked here:** Travis Orbin built his reputation as Periphery's original studio drummer on their genre-defining 2010 self-titled debut, and has continued as a prolific session musician since, posting studio playthroughs on his own "Travis Orbin Drum" YouTube channel. That combination — a landmark studio credit followed by an ongoing, publicly documented session career — is a rare level of transparency about what session drumming in metal actually looks like day to day. Orbin earns rank #7 for turning session work itself into a visible, ongoing body of work.

### 8. Arin Ilejay

**Band:** ex-Avenged Sevenfold

**Why ranked here:** Arin Ilejay recorded Avenged Sevenfold's "Hail to the King" (2013), an album that debuted at number one on the Billboard 200, before continuing as an active session and solo drummer after his departure. That path — from a chart-topping studio credit into ongoing session work — shows how a single high-visibility recording can establish a drummer's value as a hired specialist for years afterward. Ilejay earns rank #8 for parlaying one of metal's biggest studio credits into a lasting session career.

### 9. Ryan Van Poederooyen

**Band:** Devin Townsend Project
**Highlight:** Session work and teaching alongside a decade with Devin Townsend
**Why ranked here:** Ryan Van Poederooyen anchored Devin Townsend's genre-defying compositions across "Accelerated Evolution," "Synchestra," and "Deconstruction," a body of work that demanded constant stylistic pivoting between ambient passages and crushing metal sections — exactly the adaptability that session work requires. He has also worked directly as a session drummer and drum teacher in the Montreal area, formalizing that versatility into a second career. Van Poederooyen earns rank #9 for adaptability built across one of metal's most unpredictable songwriters and carried into direct session work.
