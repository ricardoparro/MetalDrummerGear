## Overview

The percussion architects of metal's slowest, most mournful extreme subgenre. Funeral doom drumming operates at the opposite end of metal's tempo spectrum — not 200+ BPM blast beats but deliberate, crushing 20-60 BPM tempos that transform the drum kit into an instrument of pure weight and atmospheric density. Where extreme metal maximizes speed, funeral doom maximizes darkness: each strike a slow tolling, each fill a measured descent into grief. From Shape of Despair's Finnish atmospheric desolation to Bell Witch's American bass-and-drums minimalism, these drummers define the art of playing so slowly that each beat becomes an event.

The greatest funeral doom metal drummers of all time. Janne Parviainen, Jesse Shreibman, Shaun Taylor-Steels, Brann Dailor, Martin Lopez and more — the definitive ranking of funeral doom and death-doom metal's most atmospheric and devastating percussionists.
