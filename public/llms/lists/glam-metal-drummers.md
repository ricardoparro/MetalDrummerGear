# Top 10 Glam Metal Drummers — Complete Ranked Guide

> **Last updated:** 2026-07-01 · **Source:** [MetalForge.io](https://metalforge.io) · [View full list →](https://metalforge.io/lists/glam-metal-drummers)

## Overview

Glam metal (also called hair metal) fused hard rock's arena-scale hooks with heavy metal's power and MTV-era spectacle — big choruses, groove-first drumming built for stadium singalongs rather than technical showmanship, and a Sunset Strip scene that dominated rock radio and record sales throughout the mid-to-late 1980s.

No band built purely around glam metal's classic Sunset Strip template — Mötley Crüe, Poison, Ratt, Cinderella — currently has a drummer in MetalForge's database. These ten drummers are instead drawn from the closest working analogues: Pantera's own glam-era catalog before their groove metal reinvention, W.A.S.P.'s continuing lineup, and the broader 80s arena hard rock and heavy metal scene that shared MTV airtime, stadium bills, and commercial ambition with the Sunset Strip bands.

## Rankings

Ranked by directness of connection to glam metal and closeness to its groove-first, arena-ready drumming philosophy.

### 1. Vinnie Paul

**Band:** Pantera
**Highlight:** Pantera's own glam metal origins
**Why ranked here:** Pantera spent the entire 1980s as a working glam metal band before reinventing themselves

Before Vinnie Paul and Pantera reinvented themselves as groove metal pioneers with 1990's "Cowboys from Hell," the band spent the entire 1980s playing in the straight-ahead glam metal style typical of the era across "Metal Magic" (1983), "Projects in the Jungle" (1984), "I Am the Night" (1985), and even an album literally titled "Power Metal" (1988).

Full drummer profile: [Vinnie Paul on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/vinnie-paul)

### 2. Aquiles Priester

**Band:** W.A.S.P.
**Highlight:** W.A.S.P.'s continuing shock-glam lineage
**Why ranked here:** Direct membership in one of the Sunset Strip era's most theatrical shock-glam acts

Aquiles Priester has drummed for W.A.S.P. since 2006 — one of the Sunset Strip era's most theatrical shock-glam acts, led by Blackie Lawless — bringing his double bass speed and showmanship to a band whose flamboyant stagecraft and image-driven sound helped define glam metal's more extreme, horror-tinged wing.

Full drummer profile: [Aquiles Priester on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/aquiles-priester)

### 3. Mikkey Dee

**Band:** Motörhead / Scorpions
**Highlight:** Sunset Strip roots before Motörhead's rock and roll fury
**Why ranked here:** His official bio credits an early association with Dokken's Don Dokken

According to his official biography, Mikkey Dee's pre-Motörhead credits include an early association with Don Dokken, placing him adjacent to the same Sunset Strip glam scene before he found fame with Motörhead's relentless hard rock and, later, Scorpions' arena-scale classic rock.

Full drummer profile: [Mikkey Dee on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/mikkey-dee)

### 4. Shannon Larkin

**Band:** Godsmack

Shannon Larkin's powerful, groove-oriented drumming with Godsmack since 2002 carries forward glam metal's core commercial formula — big, accessible hooks and arena-ready grooves prioritized over technical complexity — into a new generation of platinum-selling hard rock.

Full drummer profile: [Shannon Larkin on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/shannon-larkin)

### 5. Bill Ward

**Band:** Black Sabbath

Bill Ward's jazz-influenced swing and showmanship with Black Sabbath in the early 1970s helped establish the theatrical, larger-than-life hard rock stage presence that glam metal's Sunset Strip generation inherited and amplified into full-blown spectacle over a decade later.

Full drummer profile: [Bill Ward on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/bill-ward)

### 6. Nicko McBrain

**Band:** Iron Maiden

Nicko McBrain's tenure with Iron Maiden through the mid-1980s put him on the same MTV-era arena stages and magazine covers as the Sunset Strip glam bands, Maiden's elaborate stage shows and mascot Eddie reflecting heavy metal's broader turn toward spectacle that glam metal pushed to its commercial extreme.

Full drummer profile: [Nicko McBrain on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/nicko-mcbrain)

### 7. Charlie Benante

**Band:** Anthrax

Charlie Benante and Anthrax brought a sense of humor, colorful image, and MTV-friendly presentation to 1980s thrash that set them apart from their more austere Bay Area peers — a showmanship instinct that, while musically distinct from glam metal, shared the same MTV-driven commercial playbook.

Full drummer profile: [Charlie Benante on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/charlie-benante)

### 8. Scott Travis

**Band:** Judas Priest

Scott Travis joined Judas Priest in 1989, just three years after the band's own glam-adjacent pop-metal experiment "Turbo" (1986) courted the same MTV audience the Sunset Strip bands dominated, inheriting a leather-clad arena metal image that predates and parallels glam metal's visual language.

Full drummer profile: [Scott Travis on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/scott-travis)

### 9. Arin Ilejay

**Band:** Avenged Sevenfold

Arin Ilejay's sole studio album with Avenged Sevenfold, "Hail to the King" (2013), was widely noted by critics for its deliberate throwback to classic 1980s arena metal songwriting — simplified, hook-driven riffs and anthemic choruses that echo glam metal's commercial formula for a modern audience.

Full drummer profile: [Arin Ilejay on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/arin-ilejay)

### 10. Igor Cavalera

**Band:** Sepultura

Igor Cavalera co-founded Sepultura in 1984, part of the same worldwide 1980s heavy metal explosion that saw glam metal dominate American charts while thrash and extreme metal scenes built a harder-edged, image-averse alternative on the opposite end of the decade's commercial spectrum.

Full drummer profile: [Igor Cavalera on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/igor-cavalera)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who is the best glam metal drummer?**
A: No band built purely around glam metal's classic Sunset Strip template — Mötley Crüe, Poison, Ratt, Cinderella — currently has a drummer in MetalForge's database. Vinnie Paul is the closest working analogue: Pantera spent the entire 1980s as a glam metal band, releasing four albums in that style before reinventing themselves as groove metal pioneers in 1990. Aquiles Priester follows closely for his ongoing work with W.A.S.P., one of the era's most theatrical shock-glam acts. Mötley Crüe's Tommy Lee, Poison's Rikki Rockett, Ratt's Bobby Blotzer, and Cinderella's Fred Coury remain glam metal's most direct historical touchstones outside this database.

**Q: What makes glam metal drumming unique?**
A: Glam metal drumming prioritizes big, accessible grooves and arena-ready hooks over the technical showmanship found in thrash, progressive, or extreme metal. Where those genres often treat the drummer as a co-lead instrument driving relentless speed or complexity, glam metal drumming stays in service of the song — simple, powerful backbeats, tasteful fills, and a groove built for stadium singalongs and MTV-friendly choruses rather than displays of raw technique.

**Q: What bands define glam metal?**
A: Mötley Crüe's "Shout at the Devil" (1983) and "Dr. Feelgood" (1989) bookend glam metal's commercial rise on the Sunset Strip. Poison, Ratt, Cinderella, Warrant, and W.A.S.P. built the genre's classic MTV-era catalog through the mid-to-late 1980s, blending hard rock riffs with big hair, spandex, and makeup-driven visual spectacle. Glam metal dominated American rock radio and record sales until grunge and alternative rock displaced it commercially in the early 1990s.

**Q: What gear do glam metal's closest analogues use?**
A: Vinnie Paul's ddrum Vinnie Paul Signature kit with Sabian AA and AAX cymbals delivered the powerful backbeat behind Pantera's glam-era catalog before the band's later groove metal reinvention. Aquiles Priester's Pearl Reference kit with Sabian HHX and AAX cymbals brings speed and showmanship to W.A.S.P.'s theatrical live show. Mikkey Dee's Yamaha Recording Custom kit with Zildjian A Custom and K Series cymbals powers the straightforward, hard-hitting rock and roll drumming that carried him from the Sunset Strip scene to Motörhead and Scorpions. Across the genre, big, punchy snares and bright, cutting cymbals built for simple, powerful backbeats define the sound.
