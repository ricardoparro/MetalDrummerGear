## Overview

The percussionists behind metal's pre-Christian, mythology-steeped fusion of black metal ferocity and folk-melodic identity. Pagan metal grew out of Scandinavian and Eastern European black and melodic death metal in the 1990s, trading pure aggression for folk instrumentation, mid-tempo anthemic grooves, and lyrical themes drawn from Norse, Slavic, and Baltic pre-Christian mythology and pagan history. No band built purely around the pagan metal template — Enslaved, Moonsorrow, Arkona, Månegarm — currently has a drummer in MetalForge's database, so these ten drummers are drawn from the closely related Viking metal, Scandinavian black metal, and melodic death metal lineages that share pagan metal's mythological themes and folk-melodic instincts: Amon Amarth's Jocke Wallgren chief among them. They represent the closest working analogues to pagan metal's epic, mythology-driven rhythmic foundation.

The greatest pagan metal drummers and their closely related Viking and Scandinavian black metal lineage. Jocke Wallgren, Hellhammer, Inferno, Daray and more — the definitive ranking of pagan metal's most mythology-driven percussionists.
