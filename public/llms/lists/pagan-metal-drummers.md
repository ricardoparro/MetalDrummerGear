## Overview

The percussionists behind heavy metal's most mythologically-charged, folk-infused extreme metal current. Pagan metal fuses black and death metal's aggression with pre-Christian European folklore, tribal war-drum patterns, and epic mid-tempo battle rhythms that black metal's blast beats alone can't deliver.

Where the genre's foundational acts — Enslaved, Moonsorrow, Arkona, and Månegarm — built pagan metal's Nordic and Slavic heathen identity, the drumming vocabulary that defines the movement (hypnotic ritual patterns, tribal rhythmic emphasis, and mythological thematic scope) runs directly through extreme metal's Scandinavian and folk-adjacent lineage. This ranking spotlights the drummers whose Nordic black metal roots, tribal rhythmic sensibility, and mythologically-themed compositions most directly parallel and influenced pagan metal's rise.

## Rankings

Ranked by thematic and rhythmic proximity to pagan metal's heathen, folk-infused identity. Top entries: Jocke Wallgren, Frost, Daniel Erlandsson, Jaska Raatikainen, Igor Cavalera, and more.

### 1. Jocke Wallgren

**Band:** Amon Amarth
**Highlight:** Viking metal's epic rhythmic foundation
**Why ranked here:** Closest rhythmic neighbor to pagan metal in MetalForge's drummer database

Jocke Wallgren (Amon Amarth) earns rank #1 as the closest rhythmic neighbor pagan metal has within MetalForge's drummer database. His vast, oceanic mid-tempo grooves and epic build-ups are built to evoke Norse mythology and Viking-age imagery — the same heathen thematic territory pagan metal bands like Enslaved and Moonsorrow occupy. His ability to make double bass patterns feel monumental rather than merely fast defines Viking-adjacent pagan metal drumming.

### 2. Frost

**Band:** Satyricon / 1349

Frost (Satyricon) earns rank #2 for channeling the same Norwegian black metal lineage that birthed pagan metal — Enslaved, one of pagan metal's founding acts, emerged from the identical early-1990s Norwegian scene. His hypnotic, ritualistic blast beat patterns and mid-tempo atmospheric sections established a template for pairing extreme metal's aggression with pre-Christian, mythologically-themed songwriting.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who is the best pagan metal drummer?**
A: MetalForge's drummer database doesn't yet carry dedicated profiles for pagan metal's own founding-band drummers — Iver Sandøy (and predecessor Cato Bekkevold) of Enslaved, or Marko Tarvonen of Moonsorrow — so this ranking highlights extreme metal drummers whose Nordic black metal roots, tribal rhythmic approach, and mythologically-themed compositions most directly parallel and influenced the genre. Jocke Wallgren of Amon Amarth is the closest rhythmic neighbor, delivering the same epic, mythology-driven mid-tempo grooves pagan metal is built around, while Frost of Satyricon represents the Norwegian black metal scene that directly produced Enslaved.

**Q: What makes pagan metal drumming unique?**
A: Pagan metal fuses black and death metal's aggression with pre-Christian European folklore and mythology — Nordic (Enslaved, Månegarm), Slavic (Arkona), and Finnish (Moonsorrow) heathen traditions each bring regional folk instrumentation and rhythmic sensibility. Distinct pagan metal drumming favors epic, mid-tempo tribal patterns and hypnotic battle-march rhythms alongside black metal's blast beats, prioritizing atmosphere and mythological narrative weight over pure extreme metal speed.

**Q: What bands play pagan metal?**
A: Pagan metal's foundational and best-known acts include Enslaved (Norway, the genre's most influential pioneer), Moonsorrow (Finland, epic Finnish heathen metal), Arkona (Russia, Slavic pagan metal), Månegarm (Sweden, Nordic pagan/Viking metal), Turisas (Finland, orchestral Viking metal), Korpiklaani and Finntroll (Finland, folk/pagan crossover), and Ensiferum (Finland, folk-power-pagan hybrid). The genre overlaps heavily with Viking metal (Amon Amarth, Bathory's later era) and folk metal.

**Q: What gear connects pagan metal's percussive approach to extreme metal drumming?**
A: Pagan metal drummers typically favor large, resonant kits capable of both black metal blast-beat speed and huge, room-filling tribal tom work — Amon Amarth's Jocke Wallgren uses kits configured for exactly this dual demand. Frost of Satyricon relies on Sonor SQ2 with Zildjian cymbals calibrated for Norwegian black metal's raw atmospheric character. Tribal-influenced percussion often adds extra floor toms and larger-diameter drums to create the deep, war-drum resonance pagan metal's mythological themes call for.
