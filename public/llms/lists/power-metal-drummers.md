## Overview

The double-bass architects of metal's most triumphant, melody-first subgenre. Power metal fuses NWOBHM's galloping rhythms and Judas Priest's twin-guitar bombast with neoclassical melody and blazing, sustained speed, prioritizing anthemic hooks and technical showmanship over raw brutality. Scott Travis's arrival on Judas Priest's "Painkiller" (1990) stands as the genre's foundational drumming document — a relentless double bass assault that redefined what power metal drumming could sound like — while Aquiles Priester's work with Brazilian power metal legends Angra brought South American neoclassical fire to the genre's European template.

Helloween, Blind Guardian, HammerFall, and DragonForce (whose Dave Mackintosh made hyper-speed drumming a viral clickbait staple) do not currently have dedicated drummer profiles in MetalForge's database, so these ten drummers are drawn from power metal's own ranks and the closely related heavy metal, prog-power, and melodic death metal lineages that share the genre's melodic ambition and blazing technical demands.

They represent the closest working analogues to power metal's triumphant, technically demanding rhythmic philosophy.

The greatest power metal drummers ranked. Aquiles Priester, Scott Travis, Jaska Raatikainen, Mike Portnoy and more — the definitive ranking of power metal's most triumphant, technically ferocious percussionists.

## Rankings

Ranked by proximity to power metal's melody-first, technically demanding rhythmic philosophy and direct historical influence on the genre's European and American lineages.

### 1. Aquiles Priester

**Band:** Angra / W.A.S.P.
**Highlight:** Angra and W.A.S.P. — power metal's explicit genre standard-bearer
**Why ranked here:** Aquiles Priester is tagged directly under the power metal genre in MetalForge's database, and his work with Brazilian power metal pioneers Angra from 2000 to 2012 brought neoclassical fire and blistering double bass technique that helped define South America's contribution to the genre's global sound.

Aquiles Priester earns rank #1 as the database's only explicitly power-metal-tagged drummer, with W.A.S.P. adding a Sunset Strip shock-glam credential alongside his neoclassical Angra pedigree.

### 2. Scott Travis

**Band:** Judas Priest
**Highlight:** Judas Priest's Painkiller — power metal drumming's foundational document
**Why ranked here:** Scott Travis joined Judas Priest in 1989 and immediately delivered "Painkiller" (1990), an album whose relentless double bass assault and thrash-informed speed is widely cited as one of power metal drumming's foundational performances.

Scott Travis earns rank #2 for revitalizing Priest's sound and influencing a generation of speed-and-power-focused drummers across the genre's European scene.

Full drummer profile: [Scott Travis on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/scott-travis)

### 3. Jaska Raatikainen

**Band:** Children of Bodom
**Highlight:** Children of Bodom — neoclassical melodic death metal's power metal crossover
**Why ranked here:** Jaska Raatikainen's 26-year tenure with Children of Bodom, tagged in MetalForge's database under both melodic death metal and power metal, combined neoclassical keyboard-driven melody with blistering double bass technique.

Jaska Raatikainen earns rank #3 for a genre-blurring approach that made Children of Bodom a gateway between melodic death metal and power metal fandoms.

Full drummer profile: [Jaska Raatikainen on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/jaska-raatikainen)

### 4. Richard Christy

**Band:** Death / Iced Earth
**Highlight:** Iced Earth's US power metal foundation
**Why ranked here:** Richard Christy joined American power metal institution Iced Earth in 2003, bringing the technical precision he built on Death's "The Sound of Perseverance" to Jon Schaffer's thrash-tinged power metal songwriting.

Richard Christy earns rank #4 as a direct link between technical death metal drumming and US power metal's riff-driven sound.

Full drummer profile: [Richard Christy on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/richard-christy)

### 5. Nicko McBrain

**Band:** Iron Maiden
**Highlight:** Iron Maiden's galloping NWOBHM foundation
**Why ranked here:** Nicko McBrain's galloping, technically precise drumming across Iron Maiden's "Powerslave" (1984) and "Somewhere in Time" (1986) established the NWOBHM rhythmic template that Helloween, Blind Guardian, and virtually every European power metal band built upon.

Nicko McBrain earns rank #5 since power metal's entire rhythmic vocabulary descends from Maiden's gallop.

Full drummer profile: [Nicko McBrain on MetalForge](https://metalforge.io/drummer/nicko-mcbrain)

### 6. Mikkey Dee

**Band:** Scorpions / Motörhead
**Highlight:** King Diamond's theatrical, power-metal-adjacent extremity
**Why ranked here:** Before three decades powering Motörhead and Scorpions, Mikkey Dee drummed for King Diamond from 1985 to 1989, an operatic, horror-themed heavy metal sound that shares power metal's taste for melodrama and neoclassical flourishes.

Mikkey Dee earns rank #6 for bringing genuine technical ambition to theatrical heavy metal's most power-metal-adjacent corner.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Who is the best power metal drummer?**
A: Aquiles Priester is the most direct answer — the only drummer tagged under the power metal genre in MetalForge's database, having brought neoclassical fire and blistering double bass technique to Brazilian power metal legends Angra from 2000 to 2012. Scott Travis of Judas Priest earns the historical argument, since "Painkiller" (1990) is widely cited as power metal drumming's foundational document. Helloween's drummers, Blind Guardian's Frederik Ehmke, and DragonForce's Dave Mackintosh remain the genre's most direct European touchstones outside this database.

**Q: What is power metal?**
A: Power metal fuses NWOBHM's galloping rhythms and Judas Priest's twin-guitar bombast with neoclassical melody, fantasy and mythology-themed lyrics, and blazing, sustained speed. The genre crystallized in the late 1980s and early 1990s through German bands Helloween and Blind Guardian and Swedish/Finnish acts, later spreading globally through Angra (Brazil), Stratovarius (Finland), and Sonata Arctica, with DragonForce pushing the genre's speed and technical extremity to its commercial peak in the 2000s.

**Q: What makes power metal drumming unique?**
A: Power metal drumming demands sustained double bass speed similar to thrash and death metal, but deployed in service of triumphant, major-key melody rather than aggression alone — galloping rhythms, precisely articulated fills, and tight synchronization with keyboard and twin-guitar harmonies define the style. Drumming must stay clean, articulate, and anthemic even at 200+ BPM, since the genre's vocal hooks and orchestral or keyboard arrangements need a rhythmic foundation that supports melody rather than overwhelming it.

**Q: What bands define power metal?**
A: Helloween is widely credited as power metal's foundational band, with "Keeper of the Seven Keys" (1987–88) establishing the genre's template. Blind Guardian pushed the genre's fantasy-themed songwriting and vocal harmonies further, while Angra brought Brazilian and neoclassical influences to the sound. Stratovarius and Sonata Arctica defined the Finnish school's keyboard-heavy approach, and DragonForce took power metal's speed and technicality to its most extreme, viral-clip-friendly conclusion in the 2000s.
