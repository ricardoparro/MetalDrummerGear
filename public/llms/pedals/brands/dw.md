## Notable DW Pedal Lines

- **5000 Series** — Chain drive, DW's original 1979 pedal design and an industry benchmark that the whole 9000 lineage descends from.
- **9000 Series** — Chain drive, DW's heavy-duty, fully adjustable flagship double pedal, the most common DW pedal on the verified roster.
- **MDD (Machined Direct Drive)** — Direct drive, DW's no-chain, no-belt option, giving the rigid, zero-slack feel extreme metal drummers chasing sustained speed look for.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is the DW 9000 chain or direct drive?**
A: Chain drive. DW's direct-drive option is the MDD (Machined Direct Drive).

**Q: What's the difference between the DW 5000 and 9000 Series?**
A: Both are chain-drive double pedals descended from the original 1979 DW 5000 design. The 9000 Series is DW's heavier-duty, fully adjustable flagship pedal and is the more common of the two on MetalForge's verified roster.

**Q: What metal drummers use the DW 9000 double pedal?**
A: 8 of the 11 verified DW pedal users on MetalForge play the 9000 Series, including Jay Weinberg, Scott Travis, and Abe Cunningham.
