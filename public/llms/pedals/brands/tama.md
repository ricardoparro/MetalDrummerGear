## Notable Tama Pedal Lines

- **Iron Cobra (900 Power Glide)** — Chain-drive double pedal, Tama's original 1993 design and still the metal-industry standard; the Power Glide cam gives a consistent feel across the stroke range.
- **Speed Cobra (910)** — A longboard, chain-drive double pedal; the extended footboard acts as a longer lever, needing less foot strength per stroke, which suits fast footwork and heel-toe technique.
- **Dyna-Sync** — A direct-drive Tama pedal, a rigid connection between footboard and beater with no chain or belt, for players who want zero-slack response.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: What Tama pedal do most metal drummers use?**
A: Across MetalForge's verified roster, the Iron Cobra (chain drive) and Speed Cobra (chain drive, longboard) are by far the most common Tama pedals, used by 15 drummers including Lars Ulrich, Dave Lombardo, and Dirk Verbeuren.

**Q: Is the Tama Iron Cobra chain or direct drive?**
A: Chain drive. The Speed Cobra is also chain drive; Tama's direct-drive pedal is the Dyna-Sync.

**Q: What's the difference between the Tama Iron Cobra and Speed Cobra?**
A: Both are chain-drive double pedals. The Speed Cobra uses a longer, longboard-style footboard that acts as a longer lever, needing less foot strength per stroke — suited to fast footwork and heel-toe technique — while the Iron Cobra uses a standard-length footboard.
