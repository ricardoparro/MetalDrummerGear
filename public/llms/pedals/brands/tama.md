# Tama Bass Drum Pedals: Metal-Relevant Lines & Which Drummers Use Them

**Founded:** 1974, Nagoya, Japan (Hoshino Gakki)
**Part of Hoshino Gakki, which also owns the Ibanez guitar brand**

---

## Overview

Tama is the single most common pedal brand across MetalForge's verified metal roster. Its 1993-launched Iron Cobra bass drum pedal became a metal-industry-standard chain-drive double pedal, and the newer Speed Cobra extends that chain-drive design with a longboard footboard for extra leverage. Lars Ulrich has been an official Tama artist since the mid-1980s, building his kits around Tama shells and, later, the Iron Cobra pedal.

## Metal-Relevant Lines

| Line | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Iron Cobra (incl. 900 / Power Glide) | Chain drive — the most common single pedal model across the verified metal roster, from Lars Ulrich to Dave Lombardo. |
| Speed Cobra (incl. 910) | Chain drive with a longboard footboard for extra leverage; used across the roster by drummers including Tomas Haake and Dirk Verbeuren. |
| Dyna-Sync | Tama's direct-drive pedal — not yet confirmed on the verified roster, but part of the brand's current lineup alongside the chain-drive Cobra pedals. |
