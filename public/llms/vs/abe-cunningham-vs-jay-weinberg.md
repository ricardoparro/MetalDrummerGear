## Playing Style

Abe Cunningham has been Deftones' drummer since the band's 1988 formation, anchoring the group's evolution from aggressive alt-metal into the atmospheric, dynamically layered sound that defined landmark albums "White Pony" (2000), "Diamond Eyes" (2010), and "Koi No Yokan" (2012). His playing is defined by the ability to shift between crushing heaviness and delicate, ambient restraint within a single song, making him one of alternative metal's most texturally sophisticated drummers. Jay Weinberg, son of E Street Band drummer Max Weinberg, joined Slipknot in 2014 after Joey Jordison's departure and spent nine years powering the band's arena-scale masked-metal productions across ".5: The Gray Chapter" (2014), "We Are Not Your Kind" (2019), and "The End, So Far" (2022), before parting ways with Slipknot in 2023 and joining Suicidal Tendencies, touring alongside Metallica on the M72 World Stadium Tour.

## Technique

Cunningham's technique centers on dynamic control — his ghost notes, tom textures, and hi-hat nuance let Deftones' quiet, ambient passages breathe before detonating into hard-hitting grooves, a dynamic range few alt-metal drummers can match. He prioritizes serving the song's atmosphere over displays of raw speed. Weinberg's technique is built for maximum power and consistency at arena scale — his explosive, high-impact playing had to honor Joey Jordison's established Slipknot vocabulary while adding his own aggressive intensity, requiring stamina and precision across Slipknot's famously physical, multi-percussionist live shows featuring nine members on stage.

## Influence & Legacy

Abe Cunningham has been the rhythmic backbone of one of alternative metal's most influential and enduring bands for over three decades, with "White Pony" widely regarded as a genre landmark that expanded what heavy music could sound like dynamically. Jay Weinberg's nine-year Slipknot tenure proved he could honor a beloved predecessor's legacy while carving out his own identity in one of metal's most scrutinized drum chairs, and his subsequent move to Suicidal Tendencies and the Metallica M72 Stadium Tour has kept him at the center of major modern metal touring.

## Verdict

Abe Cunningham and Jay Weinberg represent two contrasting pillars of modern heavy music drumming. Cunningham is alt-metal's dynamics master, using three-plus decades with Deftones to prove that atmosphere and restraint can hit as hard as raw power. Weinberg is arena metal's power specialist, delivering nine years of explosive, consistent performances that carried Slipknot's masked-performer spectacle on some of metal's biggest stages. Cunningham answers "what makes Deftones' drumming atmospheric," while Weinberg answers "what gives Slipknot its arena-scale intensity."
