## Drummer Profiles

### Abe Cunningham

Abraham "Abe" Cunningham (born July 27, 1973, in Sacramento, California) is an American drummer and founding member of Deftones since the band formed in 1988. Over more than three decades, Cunningham has developed a distinctive style characterized by unconventional patterns, creative use of space, and an intuitive feel for dynamics that serves Deftones' emotional arc rather than technical showmanship. His drumming on *White Pony* (2000, Grammy-winning "Elite") and *Diamond Eyes* (2010) helped shape alternative metal's atmospheric direction across nine studio albums.

### Shannon Larkin

Shannon Larkin (born April 24, 1967, in Baltimore, Maryland) is an American drummer best known as Godsmack's drummer since 2002, when he joined replacing Tommy Stewart. Before Godsmack, Larkin made his mark with Ugly Kid Joe — recording the hit "Everything About You" — and thrash band Wrathchild America. Known for his powerful, groove-oriented style and dynamic stage presence, Larkin helped drive Godsmack's run of platinum albums including *Faceless* (2003) and *IV* (2006).

## Playing Style

Abe Cunningham's playing prioritizes emotional arc, space, and dynamic texture over technical showmanship — his grooves shift effortlessly between delicate atmosphere and crushing heaviness within Deftones' shoegaze-influenced sound.

Shannon Larkin brings a powerful, groove-oriented hard rock approach honed across stints with Wrathchild America, Ugly Kid Joe, and Godsmack — his deep pocket and hard-hitting backbeat anchor riff-driven songwriting with enough musical sensitivity to keep it accessible.

---

## Technique

Cunningham's approach relies on unconventional snare placement, creative hi-hat and ride patterns, and pocket-focused playing that mirrors Deftones' emotional content — his dynamic control transitioning between crushing downbeats and delicate textures is one of alternative metal's most recognizable signatures.

Larkin favors a deep pocket and hard-hitting backbeat that serves Godsmack's riff-driven hard rock songwriting, combining showmanship and energetic live delivery with the musical sensitivity needed to anchor the band's heavier, more accessible material.

- **Atmosphere vs power:** Cunningham builds dynamic, atmospheric textures; Larkin drives forward with hard-hitting groove and power.
- **Drum brand:** Cunningham plays Tama Starclassic; Larkin plays Pearl Reference Pure.

## Influence & Legacy

Cunningham has been the defining rhythmic voice of Deftones across nine studio albums and 35+ years — his drumming on *White Pony* and *Diamond Eyes* helped shape alternative metal's atmospheric direction for a generation of drummers.

Larkin's 20-plus years driving Godsmack's multi-platinum catalog, alongside his earlier hit run with Ugly Kid Joe's "Everything About You," established him as one of hard rock's most recognizable and durable drummers across two distinct eras of the genre.

## Verdict

Abe Cunningham and Shannon Larkin represent two contrasting faces of nu-metal-adjacent alternative and hard rock drumming. Cunningham is the master of atmospheric restraint — his unconventional patterns and dynamic control are inseparable from Deftones' shoegaze-tinged identity. Larkin is groove-driven power personified — his deep pocket and hard-hitting backbeat built Godsmack's commercial rock sound into a multi-platinum institution. The Deftones vs Godsmack matchup is one of metal's most compelling commercial cross-genre contrasts.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the better drummer, Abe Cunningham or Shannon Larkin?**
A: Both are exceptional in different lanes. Cunningham excels at atmospheric dynamics and unconventional groove that define Deftones' emotional textures. Larkin excels at hard-hitting power and deep pocket that drove Godsmack's multi-platinum hard rock catalog.

**Q: What drum kit does Abe Cunningham use compared to Shannon Larkin?**
A: Abe Cunningham plays Tama Starclassic Maple/Bubinga drums with Zildjian cymbals and a Tama Iron Cobra double pedal. Shannon Larkin plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit with Sabian AAX cymbals and a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal.

**Q: How long has Abe Cunningham been with Deftones?**
A: Abe Cunningham is a founding member of Deftones, having been with the band since it formed in Sacramento, California in 1988 — over 35 years as the band's sole drummer. His Grammy came with "Elite" (*White Pony*, 2000, Best Metal Performance).

**Q: What bands did Shannon Larkin play in before Godsmack?**
A: Shannon Larkin played with thrash band Wrathchild America (1989–1991) and then Ugly Kid Joe (1991–1997), recording the hit "Everything About You," before joining Godsmack in 2002, replacing original drummer Tommy Stewart.
