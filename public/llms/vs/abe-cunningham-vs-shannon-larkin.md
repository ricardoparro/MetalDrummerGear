## Playing Style

Abe Cunningham co-founded Deftones in Sacramento in 1988 and has been the band's sole drummer ever since, shaping a style built on space, dynamic restraint, and emotional arc rather than technical showmanship — his playing across "White Pony" (2000), "Diamond Eyes" (2010), and "Ohms" (2020) helped define alternative metal's atmospheric, shoegaze-tinged direction. Shannon Larkin joined Godsmack in 2002, bringing veteran hard rock groove authority to the band's arena-scale sound across albums including "Faceless," "IV," and "The Oracle." Before Godsmack, Larkin played in Ugly Kid Joe, Wrathchild America, and Amen, giving him a broader hard rock pedigree than most of his nu-metal-era peers.

## Technique

Cunningham's technique centers on unconventional snare placement, creative hi-hat and ride patterns, and dynamic control that shifts between whisper-quiet shimmer and crushing heaviness within a single song — a style that prioritizes mood over conventional metal drumming vocabulary. Larkin's approach is muscular, groove-first hard rock drumming: powerful, hook-driven rhythms that lock tightly with Tony Rombola's guitar riffs, giving Godsmack's songs the pocket and weight that made them commercially dominant through the 2000s.

## Key Differences

- **Genre lane:** Cunningham serves Deftones' atmospheric alt-metal; Larkin serves Godsmack's arena hard rock.
- **Drum brand:** Cunningham plays Tama Starclassic; Larkin plays Pearl Reference Pure — different shell pedigrees built for different sonic goals.
- **Cymbals:** Cunningham plays Zildjian; Larkin plays Sabian AAX — distinct tonal signatures.
- **Approach:** Cunningham prioritizes space and dynamics; Larkin prioritizes weight and groove.
- **Career arc:** Cunningham is a 35-plus-year founding member of one band; Larkin arrived at Godsmack in 2002 after stints in Ugly Kid Joe, Wrathchild America, and Amen.

## Influence & Legacy

Cunningham has been Deftones' sole drummer across nine studio albums and 35-plus years — his drumming on "White Pony" (Grammy winner, Best Metal Performance for "Elite") and "Diamond Eyes" helped shape a generation of alternative metal drummers toward atmospheric, dynamics-first playing. Larkin's two-decade tenure with Godsmack helped sustain the band's commercial dominance through multiple platinum records, and his pre-Godsmack résumé made him one of the most well-traveled drummers to find mainstream success in 2000s hard rock.

## Verdict

Abe Cunningham and Shannon Larkin represent two contrasting approaches to commercially successful modern metal drumming. Cunningham is the master of atmospheric restraint — unconventional patterns and dynamic control that make Deftones' textures feel alive. Larkin is the master of arena-ready groove — muscular, hook-driven rhythms that gave Godsmack its rhythmic foundation. The Deftones vs Godsmack matchup pits alt-metal mood against hard rock power.
