## Gear Deep Dive

### Alex Bent Setup

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure Series
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian (14" K Custom Hybrid Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" K Custom Hybrid Crashes, 21" K Custom Hybrid Ride, 18" A Custom China)
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14x5" Brass
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Axis A Longboard double pedal; Pearl D-3000 throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B

Alex Bent's Pearl Reference Pure Series kit and brass snare deliver the cutting attack that carried his technical death metal precision into Trivium's more song-focused modern metalcore across "The Sin and the Sentence" (2017) and "In the Court of the Dragon" (2021). His Axis A Longboard double pedal, a favorite among extreme-metal specialists for speed, powered the blistering footwork he built during his years with Brain Drill and Arkaik.

### Jason Bittner Setup

- **Drums:** Mapex Saturn V (all-maple shell pack)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian K & A Custom Series (14" K Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" A Custom Crashes, 21" K Custom Ride, 18" K China)
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther 14x6.5" Brass
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Mapex Falcon double pedal; Mapex T865 throne
- **Sticks:** Promark Jason Bittner Signature 5BX

Jason Bittner's Mapex Saturn V kit has powered his sound since joining Overkill in 2017, after more than a decade on the same platform with Shadows Fall, including the Grammy-nominated "The War Within" (2004). His brass Black Panther snare delivers the steel-edged crack that cuts through both NWOAHM's layered metalcore arrangements and Overkill's dense thrash riffing, driven by the Mapex Falcon double pedal that sustains his double-kick passages across two active, stylistically distinct bands.

## Style & Technique Comparison

Jason Bittner has spent over two decades bridging thrash metal and NWOAHM, joining Shadows Fall in 1999 and driving the band's genre-defining metalcore output through "The Art of Balance" (2002) and "The War Within" (2004), before joining thrash legends Overkill in 2017 and recording "The Wings of War" (2019) and "Scorched" (2023). He also co-founded supergroup Category 7 in 2024, maintaining dual-band duties across thrash and NWOAHM simultaneously — his Berklee College of Music training shows in his technical precision and sustained double-kick stamina.

Alex Bent built his reputation in the technical death metal underground with Brain Drill, Arkaik, and Battlecross before joining Trivium in 2017, bringing that extreme-metal precision to the modern metalcore giants across "The Sin and the Sentence" (2017), "What the Dead Men Say" (2020), "In the Court of the Dragon" (2021), and "Struck Dead" (2025) until his October 2025 departure. Trivium's songwriting required him to fold blast-beat velocity and intricate fills into groove-focused, song-serving precision rather than raw technical display.

---

## Key Differences

The core divergence is band structure: Bittner has maintained duties across two simultaneously active bands for most of his career — Shadows Fall alongside Flotsam and Jetsam, and now Overkill alongside Category 7 — while Bent moved sequentially through underground technical death metal bands before landing his single, eight-year run with Trivium.

Gear reflects each drummer's dual demands. Bittner's Mapex Saturn V kit and Zildjian K & A Custom cymbals are voiced to cut through both metalcore and thrash riffing. Bent's Pearl Reference Pure kit and Zildjian K Custom Hybrid cymbals are tuned for the cutting clarity Trivium's guitar-forward modern metalcore requires.

---

## Influence & Legacy

Bittner earned multiple Modern Drummer "Best Metal Drummer" honors and has filled in live for Anthrax's Charlie Benante, cementing his reputation as one of thrash and NWOAHM's most versatile working drummers across two simultaneously active bands.

Bent proved that technical death metal underground chops could translate directly into mainstream modern metalcore success, elevating Trivium's rhythmic complexity across four albums and eight years before moving on in 2025.

---

## Verdict

Jason Bittner and Alex Bent represent two paths through the NWOAHM and modern metalcore landscape. Bittner built a 25-plus-year career maintaining technical double-kick versatility across metalcore and thrash simultaneously, first with Shadows Fall and Overkill and now alongside Category 7. Bent brought technical death metal underground precision to Trivium's more radio-accessible modern metalcore songwriting for eight years before his 2025 departure. Asking "who is the better NWOAHM drummer?" comes down to whether you value Bittner's sustained dual-genre stamina or Bent's extremity-to-accessibility translation.
