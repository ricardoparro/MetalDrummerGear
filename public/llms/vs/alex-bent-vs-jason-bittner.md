## Playing Style

Alex Bent joined Trivium in 2017, replacing Paul Wandtke, and recorded three albums with the band — "The Sin and the Sentence" (2017), "What the Dead Men Say" (2020), and "In the Court of the Dragon" (2021) — before departing in 2024. Before Trivium, Bent built his reputation in technical death metal with Arkaik and Brain Drill, filled in for Gene Hoglan on Testament tours, and continues to drum for Eric Peterson's (Testament) side project Dragonlord, giving him one of modern metal's most technically demanding résumés. Jason Bittner has spent over two decades at the intersection of thrash and the New Wave of American Heavy Metal (NWOAHM) — he joined Shadows Fall in 2001 and was central to the band's commercial breakthrough on "The Art of Balance" (2002) and the Grammy-nominated "The War Within" (2004), then joined thrash legends Overkill in 2017, and has also filled in live for Anthrax's Charlie Benante on multiple occasions.

## Technique

Bent brings death-metal-trained technicality to Trivium's thrash-metalcore hybrid — his background in Arkaik and Brain Drill gave him blast beat precision and extreme-metal endurance that he channels into Trivium's more song-oriented, riff-driven framework, resulting in playing that is faster and more technically dense than a typical metalcore drummer's. A Berklee College of Music alumnus, Bittner combines academic-grade technical precision with raw power — his sustained double-kick passages and tight, controlled fill vocabulary have earned him multiple "Best Metal Drummer" awards from Modern Drummer magazine, and his dual fluency in NWOAHM groove (Shadows Fall) and classic thrash aggression (Overkill) makes him one of the few drummers equally credible in both camps.

## Influence & Legacy

Alex Bent's technical death metal pedigree brought a new level of extreme-metal chops to Trivium during a run of three albums that pushed the band's rhythmic complexity further than any drummer before him, while his continued work with Dragonlord and his Testament touring history keep him closely tied to the thrash and technical death metal underground. Jason Bittner is a defining voice of NWOAHM drumming — his Modern Drummer "Best Metal Drummer" honors and his instructional DVDs and drum-publication writing have made him a respected educator, and his move from Shadows Fall to Overkill in 2017 connected two generations of American thrash and heavy metal drumming under one player.

## Verdict

Alex Bent and Jason Bittner both answer the question of who is the best NWOAHM/thrash-adjacent drummer from different technical backgrounds. Bent brings technical death metal extremity — honed in Arkaik and Brain Drill — into Trivium's metalcore-thrash hybrid, delivering speed and precision beyond genre norms. Bittner brings Berklee-trained, award-winning versatility spanning Shadows Fall's NWOAHM breakthrough and Overkill's classic thrash legacy. Both represent the modern American metal drummer's ability to move fluidly between adjacent subgenres without losing technical identity.
