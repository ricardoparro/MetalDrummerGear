Ex-Trivium's Alex Bent vs Overkill's Jason Bittner pits two drummers from opposite ends of the New Wave of American Heavy Metal spectrum against each other. Bent joined Trivium in 2017, bringing technical death metal precision from Brain Drill and Arkaik to modern metalcore. Bittner spent nearly a decade and a half defining Shadows Fall's NWOAHM sound before stepping into thrash royalty with Overkill in 2017 — the same year Bent joined Trivium. Alex Bent's Pearl Reference Series kit was engineered to translate his technical death metal precision to arena scale for Trivium, balancing maple's warmth with birch's cutting attack across "The Sin and the Sentence" (2017) and "In the Court of the Dragon" (2021) — full breakdown in [What's In Alex Bent's Kit](https://metalforge.io/articles/whats-in-alex-bents-kit). ### Jason Bittner Setup

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Performer B/B (birch/bubinga hybrid shells, dual 22"x18" bass drums)

Jason Bittner's Tama Starclassic Performer B/B kit is built for the dual demands of NWOAHM and thrash — the birch/bubinga hybrid shells provide the attack and warmth needed to cut through both Shadows Fall's dense, down-tuned arrangements and Overkill's classic thrash guitar wall. ## Style & Technique Comparison

Alex Bent joined Trivium in 2017 at age 24, bringing a technical death metal pedigree from Brain Drill, Arkaik, and Battlecross to the modern metal giants' catalog. His background gave him the tools to blend extreme blast beats and intricate fills with the melodic accessibility Trivium's songwriting demands, a versatility built through drum competitions and jazz band training before he ever joined a signed act — recording "The Sin and the Sentence" (2017), "What the Dead Men Say" (2020), "In the Court of the Dragon" (2021), and "Struck Dead" (2025) before departing in October 2025.

Jason Bittner built his reputation as the engine behind Shadows Fall's technical ambition within the NWOAHM scene, fusing thrash-tempo double-bass patterns with metalcore's groove and melody across landmark records including "The Art of Balance" (2002) and the Grammy-nominated "The War Within" (2004). His powerful, driving double bass and tight, punchy snare work — influenced by thrash pioneers Dave Lombardo, Nicko McBrain, and Vinnie Paul — translated directly into Overkill's classic thrash gallop when he joined the band in 2017, recording "The Wings of War" (2019) and "Scorched" (2023).

## Key Differences

The core divergence is origin story and role: Bent came up through the technical death metal underground before landing a scrutinized modern-metal drum chair at 24, while Bittner spent nearly fifteen years co-architecting an entire NWOAHM sound with Shadows Fall before proving his thrash pedigree could carry one of the genre's most demanding full-time chairs in Overkill.

Gear reflects each context. Bent's Pearl Reference kit and Meinl Byzance Brilliant cymbals are voiced for the cutting clarity Trivium's guitar-forward modern metal arrangements require. Bittner's Tama Starclassic Performer B/B kit and Sabian HHX/HH cymbals are tuned for durability and cutting brightness across both NWOAHM's dense guitar textures and thrash's high-tempo velocity.

---

## Influence & Legacy

Bent elevated Trivium's rhythmic complexity across four albums over eight years, proving a technical death metal background from underground bands like Brain Drill could translate directly into mainstream modern metal success before his departure in October 2025.

Bittner helped define early-2000s NWOAHM's rhythmic vocabulary during Shadows Fall's commercial peak before seamlessly stepping into thrash metal royalty with Overkill, becoming one of the rare drummers to anchor top-tier bands across two adjacent but distinct American heavy metal subgenres simultaneously.

---

## Verdict

Alex Bent and Jason Bittner represent NWOAHM-adjacent drumming from two different generations and angles. Bent brought outsider technical death metal precision to Trivium's increasingly ambitious modern metal songwriting across eight years and four albums. Bittner spent over two decades proving that groove-first, road-tested power translates seamlessly from Shadows Fall's metalcore breakthrough to Overkill's veteran thrash institution. Both answer "who is the better NWOAHM-era drummer?" from opposite ends of the technical-versus-pocket spectrum.

**Q: Who is the better NWOAHM drummer, Alex Bent or Jason Bittner?**
