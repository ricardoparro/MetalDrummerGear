- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch

- **Snare:** Tama S.L.P. 14x6" G-Maple

- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Matt Garstka Signature

## Playing Style

Alex Bent built his career in technical death metal with Arkaik and Brain Drill before joining Trivium in 2017, bringing death-metal-honed speed and precision to three albums: "The Sin and the Sentence" (2017), "What the Dead Men Say" (2020), and "In the Court of the Dragon" (2021). Matt Garstka joined instrumental progressive metal band Animals as Leaders in 2012, blending jazz fusion training with djent-influenced polyrhythms across "The Joy of Motion" (2014) and "The Madness of Many" (2016).

## Technique

Bent brings traditional technical death metal vocabulary — blast beats, precise double bass, and thrash-honed power — reshaped for Trivium's riff-driven metalcore-adjacent songwriting. Garstka favors linear independence, ghost-note density, and polymetric phrasing drawn from jazz fusion, giving Animals as Leaders' constantly shifting time signatures a fluid, almost improvisational precision rather than the direct aggression Bent's death metal background emphasizes.

## Key Differences

Alex Bent plays a Pearl Reference Pure Series kit with a Pearl Reference 14x5" Brass snare and Zildjian K Custom Hybrid cymbals (14" Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Crashes, 21" Ride, 18" A Custom China), driven by an Axis A Longboard double pedal and Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. Matt Garstka plays a Tama Starclassic Walnut/Birch kit with a Tama S.L.P. 14x6" G-Maple snare and Meinl Byzance Series cymbals (15" Dual Hi-Hats, 18" & 20" Extra Dry Medium Crashes, 22" Dual Ride), driven by a Tama Speed Cobra 910 double pedal and Vic Firth Matt Garstka Signature sticks. Bent brings traditional technical death metal vocabulary — blast beats, precise double bass, and thrash-honed power — reshaped for Trivium's riff-driven metalcore-adjacent songwriting. Garstka favors linear independence, ghost-note density, and polymetric phrasing drawn from jazz fusion, giving Animals as Leaders' constantly shifting time signatures a fluid, almost improvisational precision rather than the direct aggression Bent's death metal background emphasizes.

## Influence & Legacy

Bent's background spanning Arkaik, Brain Drill, and Trivium — plus filling in for Gene Hoglan on Testament tours — has made him a respected connector between technical death metal and mainstream metalcore audiences. Garstka has become one of the most-watched young drummers in progressive metal through his YouTube content and Animals as Leaders' globally influential instrumental catalog, representing modern djent's technical frontier.

## Verdict

Bent and Garstka represent two different technical lineages converging on the modern tech-metal audience. Alex Bent brings direct technical death metal power reshaped for Trivium's riff-driven songwriting — precision and thrash-honed aggression. Matt Garstka brings jazz-fusion-trained polymetric fluency to Animals as Leaders' instrumental djent — complexity that feels conversational rather than forced. Ask which lineage defines modern technical drumming and the honest answer is both, from opposite directions.
