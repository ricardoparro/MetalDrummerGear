## Style & Technique Comparison

Alex Bent joined Trivium in 2017, bringing a technical death metal pedigree from Arkaik, Brain Drill, and Battlecross to the New Wave of American Heavy Metal giants' catalog, recording "The Sin and the Sentence" (2017) (see [Sin and the Sentence drum setup](https://metalforge.io/articles/trivium-sin-and-the-sentence-drum-setup)), "What the Dead Men Say" (2020) (see [What the Dead Men Say drum setup](https://metalforge.io/articles/trivium-what-the-dead-men-say-drum-setup)), and "In the Court of the Dragon" (2021) (see [In the Court of the Dragon drum setup](https://metalforge.io/articles/trivium-in-the-court-of-the-dragon-drum-setup)) before departing in 2024.

Matt Garstka has been the drummer for instrumental progressive metal band Animals as Leaders since 2012, blending jazz fusion, electronic music, and polyrhythmic djent across "The Joy of Motion" (2014) and "The Madness of Many" (2016) — records that pushed technical metal drumming into new rhythmic territory.

---

## Key Differences

The core divergence is riff-driven technical death metal versus compositional progressive djent. Bent's technique blends technical death metal speed and precision — blast beats, intricate fills, and tight riff-locked double bass — with the melodic accessibility Trivium's modern metal songwriting demands. Garstka's technique is defined by complex polyrhythms, intricate ghost-note patterns, and innovative dynamic control, requiring genuine limb independence to execute Animals as Leaders' odd-meter, instrumental compositions — a more compositionally mathematical approach than Bent's riff-driven foundation.

Gear reflects that split. Bent's Pearl Reference kit and Zildjian K Custom Hybrid cymbals are voiced for cutting technical death metal attack. Garstka's Tama Starclassic kit and Meinl Byzance cymbals are tuned for the warm low end and nuanced articulation his jazz-fusion-informed djent requires.

---

## Influence & Legacy

Bent elevated Trivium's rhythmic complexity across three albums over seven years, proving a technical death metal background from underground bands like Brain Drill could translate directly into mainstream modern metal success.

Garstka's polyrhythmic, jazz-informed approach with Animals as Leaders helped define the djent movement's rhythmic vocabulary for a generation of progressive metal drummers, influencing the broader Periphery- and Meshuggah-adjacent technical drumming scene.

---

## Verdict

Alex Bent and Matt Garstka represent modern technical metal drumming from two different angles. Bent brought outsider technical death metal precision to Trivium's increasingly ambitious modern metal songwriting across seven years and three albums. Garstka has spent over a decade proving that jazz-fusion-informed polyrhythmic complexity can anchor an entire instrumental genre movement with Animals as Leaders. Both answer "who is the better modern technical drummer?" from opposite ends of the tech-metal spectrum.
