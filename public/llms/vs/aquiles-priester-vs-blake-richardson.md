## Playing Style

Aquiles Priester is a Brazilian drummer renowned for blazing speed, precision, and showmanship, best known for his tenure with neoclassical power metal band Angra (2000–2012, 2023–present) and W.A.S.P. (2014–2021), alongside his own project Hangar. His double bass abilities and ability to sustain extreme tempos through complex, melodic power metal arrangements have made him one of the most celebrated drummers in the genre worldwide. Blake Richardson has been the drummer for American progressive metal band Between the Buried and Me since 2005, replacing founding drummer Jason Roe and becoming central to the band's genre-defying evolution. His playing fuses death metal precision with jazz influences across landmark albums including "Colors" (2007), "The Great Misdirect" (2009), and "Colors II" (2021), earning Grammy nominations and widespread acclaim for one of progressive metal's most technically demanding bodies of work.

## Technique

Priester's technique is built around sustained, blazing double bass speed combined with complex melodic fills that serve power metal's neoclassical, symphonic song structures — his playing must be both relentlessly fast and musically supportive of soaring vocal melodies and shred guitar leads. His showmanship and ability to maintain top-tier tempos for entire sets have made him a fan favorite at clinics and festivals worldwide. Richardson's technique centers on extreme genre fluidity — moving seamlessly between blast beats, odd time signatures, jazz-influenced ghost notes, and fill-heavy arrangements often within the same song, reflecting BTBAM's habit of fusing death metal, prog rock, and experimental textures into single compositions. Where Priester's complexity serves melodic consistency at speed, Richardson's complexity serves constant stylistic transformation.

## Key Differences

Aquiles Priester plays Pearl Reference Series drums with a Pearl Reference 14x6.5" brass snare and Sabian HHX & AAX Series cymbals, powered by a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal and his own Vic Firth signature sticks — a setup built for cutting through power metal's dense, symphonic arrangements. ## Influence & Legacy

Aquiles Priester has won numerous awards from Brazilian and international music publications and is widely regarded as one of the most celebrated power metal drummers in the world, inspiring a generation of South American metal drummers pursuing speed and showmanship. Blake Richardson's work has influenced a generation of progressive metal drummers — inspired himself by Terry Bozzio, Dennis Chambers, and Tomas Haake — and his Grammy-nominated performances on BTBAM's "Colors" series are frequently cited as a benchmark for treating constant genre-shifting as a natural rhythmic language rather than a technical gimmick.

## Verdict

Aquiles Priester and Blake Richardson represent two of extreme-technical metal's most celebrated traditions. Priester is power metal's neoclassical speed king — blazing, melodic, and built for sustained showmanship across Angra's symphonic catalog. Richardson is progressive metal's genre-fluid architect — constantly transforming between death metal brutality and jazz sophistication within BTBAM's most ambitious records. Both serve the question of who is the best progressive/power metal drummer, just from opposite technical philosophies: melodic speed consistency vs constant stylistic reinvention.
