## Playing Style

Arin Ilejay joined Avenged Sevenfold in 2011, stepping into one of metal's most scrutinized drum chairs after founding drummer Jimmy "The Rev" Sullivan passed away in December 2009 and Dream Theater's Mike Portnoy completed a tribute performance on "Nightmare" (2010). Ilejay's only studio credit with the band, "Hail to the King" (2013), debuted at #1 on the Billboard 200 and in five other countries, built around a deliberate homage to classic heavy metal rather than an attempt to replicate The Rev's idiosyncratic style. Jay Weinberg faced a similar mandate in 2014, joining Slipknot after Joey Jordison's departure and spending nine years driving the band's masked arena productions across ".5: The Gray Chapter," "We Are Not Your Kind," and "The End, So Far" before joining Suicidal Tendencies in 2023, touring alongside Metallica's M72 World Stadium Tour.

## Technique

Ilejay's approach on "Hail to the King" prioritized power, consistency, and service to the song over the technical complexity his predecessor was known for — straightforward, arena-calibrated grooves built to project across amphitheaters rather than showcase virtuosity. Weinberg's technique channels the aggressive, hardcore-informed intensity that made him a natural fit for Slipknot's nine-member chaos, built on relentless double-kick stamina and the physical commitment needed to hold down a percussion section alongside Slipknot's additional drummer and sample triggers night after night on world tours.

## Influence & Legacy

Ilejay's A7X tenure remains a case study in disciplined restraint — proving a band could deliver a #1 album without replicating an irreplaceable predecessor's style, even as his four-year run ended in 2014 with Brooks Wackerman taking over. Weinberg's nine years in Slipknot established him as one of modern metal's most durable touring drummers, carrying the band's masked-performer legacy through three studio albums before a high-profile move to Suicidal Tendencies validated his standing as an in-demand hired gun for legacy acts.

## Verdict

Arin Ilejay and Jay Weinberg both faced the same daunting assignment — replacing a beloved, era-defining drummer in one of metal's biggest bands — and both chose disciplined power over imitation as the answer. Ilejay's "Hail to the King" era was a compact, commercially triumphant four years that proved restraint could still top the charts. Weinberg's nine-year Slipknot run was a marathon, and his move to Suicidal Tendencies shows a drummer whose reputation for reliability under pressure now travels well beyond the band that made him famous. The comparison is a study in two different timelines for the same challenge: stepping into impossible shoes and making the seat your own.
