Cruz joined Lamb of God in 2019 after filling in for Chris Adler, who had stepped away due to injury. Coming from a deathcore and thrash metal background with Winds of Plague, Cruz brings explosive attack and physical power to the Lamb of God seat. His Ludwig/Zildjian combination produces a punchy, articulate tone with the Trick double pedal delivering fast, even footwork across LoG's demanding catalog.

### Chris Adler Setup

- **Drums:** Mapex Black Panther Series
- **Cymbals:** Meinl (14" Soundcaster Fusion Hi-Hats, MB20 & Generation X crashes, Generation X chinas)
- **Snare:** Mapex Black Panther "Sledgehammer" 14"x6.5"
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Mapex Falcon Double Pedal, DW 9000 Series Throne
- **Sticks:** Promark TX5AXW Chris Adler Signature

Adler co-founded Lamb of God in 2000 as a member of founding group Burn the Priest and spent nearly two decades defining the groove metal drumming template. His Mapex Black Panther kit was specifically chosen for its tone in dense, downtuned metal contexts. The Meinl Generation X chinas — with their industrial, cutting attack — became a signature element of Lamb of God's sonic identity, and Adler's Promark signature stick was developed for the leverage his ride-bell–heavy technique demands.

## Style & Technique Comparison

Chris Adler defined Lamb of God's drumming approach from first principles. His signature technique centres on the ride bell: where most groove metal drummers anchor on the hi-hat, Adler rides the bell with his right hand, driving syncopated kick patterns underneath with tight Mapex Falcon double pedal action. This gives Lamb of God its mechanical, industrial momentum — a wall of riff locked to a churning, almost programmatic kick grid. His four-way independence is exceptional, enabling him to layer china crashes, ghost notes, and triplet fills simultaneously over a double-bass foundation that never drops. Adler's playing on *Ashes of the Wake* (2004) and *Sacrament* (2006) set a technical benchmark for groove metal drumming that remains a study reference worldwide.

Art Cruz brings a different physical vocabulary. His background in deathcore gave him a blast-beat foundation and raw aggression that Adler, as a purely groove metal architect, didn't need. Where Adler's groove is precise and controlled, Cruz plays with more overt power — his Ludwig/Zildjian setup emphasises attack on the front of the beat, reinforcing Lamb of God's more recent material, which trends heavier and more dynamic than the mid-2000s catalog. Cruz's Trick Bigfoot pedal delivers fast, ergonomic footwork, and his cymbal selection — with both A Custom and K china combinations — gives him tonal flexibility across the full dynamic range of a Lamb of God live set.

---

## Key Differences

Adler's Meinl setup, especially the Generation X china, produced a dark, grinding tone that became inseparable from Lamb of God's first decade of recordings. Cruz's Zildjian A Custom series produces a brighter, more cutting sound in the high frequencies — a different character, not inferior, but distinctly different in feel. Adler's ride-bell technique gives his groove a mechanical precision that Cruz, working from a more power-oriented approach, doesn't replicate exactly — though Cruz executes the parts faithfully in live performance. Stick choice reflects their backgrounds: Adler's Promark signature is optimised for ride-bell leverage; Cruz's Vic Firth 5B is a general-purpose workhorse suited to the power attack he favours.

## Influence & Legacy

Chris Adler's influence on groove metal drumming is foundational. His ride-bell approach, innovative kick patterning, and Mapex/Meinl endorsements shaped an entire generation of heavy music drummers. The interplay between his kick patterns and the guitar riffs on *As the Palaces Burn* and *Ashes of the Wake* is studied by drummers and producers for its sense of lock and rhythmic precision. His tenure with Megadeth produced a Grammy-winning album (*Dystopia*, 2016), extending his influence beyond groove metal.

Art Cruz inherited one of the most technically demanding seats in modern metal and rose to it — learning Adler's notoriously complex catalog while adding his own physicality. His work on *Omens* (2022) and *Illegals in Heaven* (2024) demonstrates that Lamb of God continues to operate at the highest level of groove metal execution. Cruz's deathcore background makes him, arguably, the more versatile technician in pure terms, even if Adler's innovative contribution to the genre remains the deeper legacy.

## Which Should You Study First?

**Study Chris Adler first if:** You want to master groove metal from the ground up — his ride-bell technique, syncopated kick patterns, and four-way independence are essential vocabulary for the genre. Start with "Laid to Rest" (2004) and "Walk with Me in Hell" (2006).

**Study Art Cruz first if:** You come from a heavier, more aggressive background and want to apply deathcore technique to groove metal contexts. His approach bridges the gap between extreme metal and groove metal more naturally.

## Verdict

This is not a competition — it is a generational handoff. Chris Adler built Lamb of God's drumming identity over twenty years and defined groove metal's modern technical standard. Art Cruz stepped into that chair, honored the legacy, and kept one of metal's hardest-working bands performing at the highest level. Both are elite drummers; what you value more — innovation or execution — determines which era resonates most.

## FAQ

**Q: Why did Art Cruz replace Chris Adler in Lamb of God?**
A: Chris Adler stepped away from Lamb of God in 2019 due to arm injury and other factors. Art Cruz had been filling in during Adler's absences and was officially announced as the permanent drummer. Cruz had to learn Lamb of God's entire technically demanding catalog in a short time.

**Q: What drums does Art Cruz play vs Chris Adler?**
A: Art Cruz plays Ludwig drums with Zildjian cymbals and Trick double pedals. Chris Adler played Mapex Black Panther drums with Meinl cymbals and Mapex Falcon double pedals during his Lamb of God tenure.

**Q: Who is the better Lamb of God drummer, Art Cruz or Chris Adler?**
A: Both are exceptional. Chris Adler created the groove metal drumming template that defines Lamb of God's classic sound. Art Cruz executes that template faithfully while bringing his own physicality. The consensus among fans is that Adler's contribution was more innovative; Cruz's execution is more powerful.
