## Playing Style

Art Cruz joined [Lamb of God](/articles/whats-in-art-cruzs-kit) in 2019, replacing founding drummer Chris Adler after first filling in on the band's 2018 tour supporting Slayer's farewell run. Cruz brought a groove-first, service-the-song philosophy to one of groove metal's most demanding drum chairs, learning Adler's intricate vocabulary with respect while adding his own power on "Lamb of God" (2020) and "Omens" (2022). Jay Weinberg, son of E Street Band drummer Max Weinberg, joined [Slipknot](/articles/jay-weinberg-complete-drum-setup) in 2014 following Joey Jordison's departure, delivering nine years of powerhouse precision across ".5: The Gray Chapter," "We Are Not Your Kind," and "The End, So Far" before parting ways with the band in 2023 and joining Suicidal Tendencies.

## Technique

Cruz's technique centers on disciplined double bass work and dynamic control — he prioritizes pocket and groove over flash, a philosophy suited to Lamb of God's syncopated riff-driven songwriting. His fills are functional, built to serve Mark Morton and Willie Adler's guitar work rather than showcase technicality for its own sake. Weinberg's technique is defined by explosive power and machine-like consistency — his blast-capable speed and theatrical intensity honored Joey Jordison's legacy while adding his own aggressive stamp, delivering note-perfect performances across Slipknot's masked, high-production arena shows night after night.

## Verdict

Art Cruz and Jay Weinberg both inherited enormous legacy-band drum seats and answered the challenge with distinct philosophies. Cruz brings groove-first restraint to Lamb of God, serving the song over spectacle. Weinberg brought powerhouse precision and theatrical intensity to Slipknot, honoring Joey Jordison's legacy while making the seat his own for nearly a decade. Both are the modern blueprint for how to replace an irreplaceable drummer in American metal.
