## Playing Style

Ben Koller has been Converge's drummer since late 1999, anchoring the mathcore and metalcore landmark "Jane Doe" (2001) and every studio album since — "You Fail Me" (2004), "Axe to Fall" (2009), "All We Love We Leave Behind" (2012), and "The Dusk in Us" (2017), the latter earning a Grammy nomination. His playing is chaos-driven and emotionally explosive, built for hardcore's unpredictable song structures and sudden tempo shifts. He also co-founded heavy rock band Mutoid Man with Stephen Brodsky and joined supergroup Killer Be Killed alongside members of Mastodon and The Dillinger Escape Plan. Blake Richardson has been Between the Buried and Me's drummer since early 2005, powering the band's genre-blending progressive death metal across "Colors" (2007), "The Great Misdirect" (2009), "The Parallax II: Future Sequence" (2012), "Automata I & II" (2018), and "Colors II" (2023). His playing is composed and architectural — precise metric modulation and jazz-informed complexity layered into songs that shift genre by the measure rather than the album.

## Technique

Koller's technique prioritizes raw power and organic dynamics over machine-like precision — his blast beats and hardcore-punk-rooted fills feel volatile and reactive, mirroring Converge's unpredictable song structures and Kurt Ballou's dense, chaotic guitar work. Richardson's technique is built around metric modulation, jazz-influenced ghost notes, and seamless transitions between death metal blast beats and delicate, almost fusion-like passages — a compositional approach that treats odd time signatures as a natural language rather than a technical showcase. Where Koller channels chaos into intensity, Richardson channels complexity into architecture.

## Influence & Legacy

Koller is widely regarded as one of the most important drummers in modern heavy music, having helped define mathcore and metalcore's rhythmic vocabulary through Converge's quarter-century run — his intensity and genre-crossing versatility across Mutoid Man and Killer Be Killed have made him an icon of underground extreme music. Richardson has become a touchstone for the modern progressive metal generation, frequently cited alongside Terry Bozzio, Dennis Chambers, and Tomas Haake as an influence, with his Grammy-nominated work on "Colors" and "The Dusk in Us"-era BTBAM records helping define the genre-blending sound of 2010s and 2020s progressive metal.

## Verdict

Ben Koller and Blake Richardson represent extreme progressive metal's two most technically demanding poles. Koller is mathcore's chaos-driven force — volatile, emotionally intense, and built for hardcore's sudden turns. Richardson is progressive metal's composed architect — precise metric modulation and jazz-informed complexity built into every measure. Both answer the question "who is more technically demanding?" from opposite directions: Koller through explosive unpredictability, Richardson through structured intricacy.
