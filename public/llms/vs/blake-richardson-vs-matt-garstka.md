## Playing Style

Blake Richardson has been Between the Buried and Me's drummer since early 2005, replacing Jason Roe, and has driven the band's genre-blending evolution across landmark albums including "Colors" (2007), "The Great Misdirect" (2009), and "Colors II" (2023), earning Grammy nominations for playing that fuses death metal precision with jazz influences. Matt Garstka has been the drummer for instrumental progressive metal band Animals as Leaders since 2012, bringing a virtuosic blend of jazz fusion, electronic music, and progressive metal to landmark records "The Joy of Motion" (2014) and "The Madness of Many" (2016), establishing himself as one of the defining voices of modern djent drumming.

## Technique

Richardson's technique is defined by fill-heavy, genre-spanning complexity — complex blast beats, constantly shifting odd time signatures, and death-metal-informed intensity that lets Between the Buried and Me pivot between brutal extremity and progressive, almost orchestral compositional passages within a single track. His influences (Terry Bozzio, Dennis Chambers, Tomas Haake) reflect this fusion-jazz-meets-extreme-metal approach. Garstka's technique centers on polyrhythmic precision and linear independence — intricate ghost note patterns, complex polyrhythms, and innovative dynamic control that serve Animals as Leaders' instrumental, guitar-forward compositions, requiring surgical limb independence rather than the fill-driven density of Richardson's style.

## Influence & Legacy

Blake Richardson's two-decade run with Between the Buried and Me, citing influences from Terry Bozzio to Tomas Haake, has made him one of the most creative and technically proficient drummers in modern progressive metal, with Grammy-nominated work that helped define genre-blending "progressive death metal" for a generation of technical drummers. Matt Garstka's decade-plus with Animals as Leaders has established him as a defining figure in modern djent and instrumental progressive metal, with his polyrhythmic vocabulary and jazz-fusion sensibility widely studied by drummers pursuing technical, guitar-forward metal.

## Verdict

Blake Richardson and Matt Garstka represent progressive metal's two most technically demanding modern traditions. Richardson brings fill-heavy, genre-blending complexity — death metal brutality fused with jazz-informed odd-meter intricacy — across two decades with Between the Buried and Me. Garstka brings polyrhythmic, linearly independent precision that defines Animals as Leaders' instrumental djent sound. Both answer the question of who is the best progressive metal drummer: Richardson through compositional density, Garstka through rhythmic mathematics.
