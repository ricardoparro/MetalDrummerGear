## Playing Style

Blake Richardson has drummed for Between the Buried and Me since 2005, fusing death metal blast beats with jazz fusion sophistication and progressive rock complexity across landmark albums including "Colors" (2007), "The Great Misdirect" (2009), and "Colors II" (2021). Mike Mangini joined Dream Theater in 2010 after winning a highly publicized audition process, bringing a scientific, biomechanically optimized approach to progressive metal built on world-record drumming speed and years as a Berklee College of Music professor.

## Technique

Richardson's technique defies categorization, requiring him to shift fluidly between brutal extremity and jazz-informed odd-meter intricacy within a single song, prioritizing serving each composition over technical display for its own sake. Mangini holds multiple Guinness World Records for drumming speed and applies rigorous biomechanical analysis to his playing, favoring hybrid acoustic/electronic setups and exhaustive technical preparation across Dream Theater's complex polyrhythmic material.

## Influence & Legacy

Richardson's two-decade run with Between the Buried and Me, citing influences like Danny Carey, Sean Reinert, and Bill Bruford, has made him one of the most creative and technically proficient drummers in modern progressive metal. Mangini's world-record speed and scientific approach to drumming influenced the technical and educational drumming communities well before he ever joined Dream Theater, and his tenure has kept the band's most demanding material in world-class hands.

## Verdict

Blake Richardson and Mike Mangini share a Sabian HHX Evolution cymbal foundation but arrive at progressive metal from opposite directions. Richardson is Between the Buried and Me's genre-blending compositional chameleon, treating extreme complexity as a vehicle for musicality. Mangini is Dream Theater's record-holding technician, treating drumming as a science of speed and efficiency. Both represent progressive metal's outer technical limits — one through creative unpredictability, the other through calculated precision.
