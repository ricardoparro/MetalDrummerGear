Casagrande carried Sepultura's thrash legacy into the modern era before landing the biggest drum chair in metal history: Metallica. ## Kit Comparison

### Brann Dailor Setup

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Performer B/B (Birch/Bubinga)
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series (14" Dark Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Heavy Hammered Crashes, 21" Ghost Ride, 18" Extra Dry China)
- **Snare:** 14x6.5" Birch/Mahogany (warm, musical tone)
- **Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vater 5B

Dailor's Tama Starclassic Performer B/B shell pack combines Birch clarity with Bubinga warmth — a sonic blend that suits Mastodon's dense, layered progressive sludge. His Meinl Byzance cymbals are known for warm, complex tones with long sustain — the ideal match for a drummer who uses cymbals as compositional textures rather than simple timekeeping tools. His Vater 5B sticks give him the weight to drive Mastodon's heavy riffs while maintaining the articulation his melodic fill work demands.

### Eloy Casagrande Setup

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom and K Series (14" A Custom Hi-Hats, 18" & 20" A Custom Crashes, 22" A Custom Ping Ride, 18" K Dark China)
- **Snare:** 14x6.5" Maple (punchy, cutting attack)
- **Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A

Casagrande's Tama Starclassic Maple delivers the bright, punchy projection needed to fill arenas with Metallica's legendary catalog. His Zildjian A Custom cymbals are chosen for their cutting attack and consistent response across the dynamic range of Metallica's material — from intimate ballads to full-throttle thrash. Both Dailor and Casagrande share the Tama Iron Cobra double pedal, making it the common gear ground between two very different playing philosophies.

## Style Comparison

Brann Dailor plays drums as if he's improvising jazz over a heavy metal band — except the improvisation is compositionally deliberate and the jazz vocabulary is expressed through sludge metal textures. His fills overflow bars, his toms carry melodic lines, and his kit is never just keeping time. On Mastodon albums like *Blood Mountain*, *Crack the Skye*, and *The Hunter*, Dailor's drumming is as distinctive as the guitar riffs — listeners often describe it as "the most recognizable drum part in the song." He is also Mastodon's vocalist, adding another dimension to his performance demands.

Eloy Casagrande represents a different kind of mastery: groove-first thrash power built for precision and adaptability. His years with Sepultura required mastering the tribal-thrash rhythmic vocabulary that Igor Cavalera defined — a demanding legacy. With Metallica, the requirement expanded further: covering *Battery*, *Master of Puppets*, *One*, *The Unforgiven*, and dozens of other catalog pieces that span 40 years of tempos, dynamics, and stylistic shifts. Casagrande's ability to serve all those songs while stamping his own authority defines his modern-era stature.

The contrast is organic-melodic vs groove-thrash — a veteran's jazz chaos vs a successor's technical power.

## Career Comparison

Brann Dailor co-founded Mastodon in Atlanta in 2000 alongside guitarist Bill Kelliher, bassist Troy Sanders, and guitarist Brent Hinds. The band's debut album *Remission* (2002) introduced Dailor's melodic fill approach to the metal world. Albums like *Leviathan* (2004), *Blood Mountain* (2006), and *Crack the Skye* (2009) built Mastodon into one of progressive metal's most critically acclaimed acts. Dailor has been with the band for 25 years — one of the most sustained creative partnerships in modern metal drumming.

Eloy Casagrande joined Sepultura in 2011 at age 16, replacing Jean Dolabella. He played on six Sepultura studio albums — *Kairos* (2011), *The Mediator Between Head and Hands Must Be the Heart* (2013), *Machine Messiah* (2017), *Quadra* (2020), and *SepulQuarta* (2021) — establishing himself as one of Brazil's elite young drummers. In 2023 he departed Sepultura. In 2024, Metallica announced Casagrande as their new drummer, making him the most-watched young drummer in metal at a single stroke.

## Influence & Legacy

Brann Dailor helped define progressive sludge metal as a genre. His approach — treating fills as melodic statements and the kit as a compositional partner — influenced a generation of rock and metal drummers. When modern drummers describe wanting to "make the drums part of the song rather than just the beat," they are often describing an influence that traces back to Dailor's work with Mastodon.

Eloy Casagrande's influence is growing rapidly. His Metallica appointment in 2024 generated immediate global interest and search traffic — "eloy casagrande drum kit" and "who is metallica's new drummer" became overnight queries. He represents the passing of the torch from one generation of thrash metal drumming to the next — and the scrutiny of that position means his every performance is watched, studied, and debated by the global metal drumming community.

## Verdict

Brann Dailor and Eloy Casagrande are two of modern metal's most compelling drummers — the veteran and the successor, the jazz-chaos melodist and the thrash authority.
