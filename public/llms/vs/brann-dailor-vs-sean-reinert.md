Brann Dailor's Tama Starclassic Performer B/B — a birch/bubinga shell configuration — delivers the warm, focused attack that complements Mastodon's dense, riff-driven arrangements. His Tama S.L.P. G-Maple snare provides cutting crack and warm tone across defining albums like *Leviathan*, *Blood Mountain*, and *Crack the Skye*. The Meinl Byzance cymbal set gives Dailor a dark, complex tonal palette suited to his constant-motion, jazz-influenced fill vocabulary, and a Tama Speed Cobra double pedal drives the fluid double bass patterns beneath Mastodon's polyrhythmic guitar layers.

### Sean Reinert Setup

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Series (14" A New Beat Hi-Hats, 16" & 18" A Medium Thin Crashes, 20" A Medium Ride)
- **Snare:** Pearl Sensitone 14x5.5" Steel/Brass
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Tama Iron Cobra Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A

Sean Reinert's Pearl Reference maple kit and Pearl Sensitone steel/brass snare formed a jazz-crossover setup built for dynamic range — essential for a player whose ghost-note vocabulary and ability to shift between whisper-quiet passages and full-force death metal intensity defined his sound. His Zildjian A Series cymbals provided the complex wash and definition that jazz-informed playing requires, distinguishing his kit from the stripped-down, speed-focused setups favored by many of his death metal contemporaries.

## Style & Technique Comparison

Brann Dailor drives Mastodon's progressive sludge metal with jazz-informed constant motion — his fills function as melody rather than punctuation, with his toms singing over the riffs on albums like *Leviathan* (2004), *Blood Mountain* (2006), and *Crack the Skye* (2009). Dailor treats the kit as a lead instrument, and his technique blurs the line between drumming and composition through flowing, jazz-vocabulary fills layered beneath his role as Mastodon's co-vocalist.

Sean Reinert (1971–2020) is technical death metal's founding father. His work on Death's *Human* (1991) and Cynic's *Focus* (1993) introduced jazz-fusion harmonic and rhythmic sophistication to a genre still consolidating its identity around raw brutality. Reinert's jazz training gave him ghost note vocabulary, odd-meter fluency, and dynamic sensitivity that let him navigate between brutal death metal intensity and jazz-informed delicacy within the same passage — a combination rarely, if ever, heard in extreme metal before him.

Both drummers proved that technical extremity and musical nuance were never mutually exclusive, but from different traditions: Dailor from progressive sludge metal's riff-driven architecture, Reinert from death metal's push toward jazz-fusion sophistication in the early 1990s.

## Key Differences

Dailor's Meinl Byzance cymbals and Tama Starclassic birch/bubinga shells favor a dark, warm tonal palette suited to Mastodon's dense, distorted guitar mix. Reinert's Zildjian A Series and Pearl maple kit favor brighter articulation and dynamic range, better suited to the complex, dynamically shifting arrangements of *Human* and *Focus*.

Their roles within their bands also diverge sharply: Dailor is both drummer and co-vocalist for Mastodon, integrating his melodic fills with his own singing. Reinert was purely an instrumentalist, whose jazz-fusion drumming became the compositional backbone that Death and Cynic built their most celebrated material around.

## Influence & Legacy

Brann Dailor helped define progressive sludge metal and influenced a generation of drummers who treat fills as melodic statements. His Mastodon work from *Remission* through *Emperor of Sand* represents one of the most compositionally distinctive drumming catalogs in modern metal, and his dual role as drummer and vocalist remains almost unique among metal's technically elite players.

Sean Reinert's work on *Human* and *Focus* essentially invented technical death metal's creative possibility space, proving jazz vocabulary and extreme metal intensity were not mutually exclusive — a foundation that Cynic, Atheist, and the broader progressive/technical death metal scene built upon for three decades. His death in January 2020 prompted an outpouring from the metal community that underscored how foundational his contribution was.

## Verdict

Brann Dailor and Sean Reinert represent two of metal's most musically creative drummers, each expanding what extreme and progressive metal drumming could sound like. Dailor is jazz chaos incarnate within Mastodon's riff-driven sludge — his drumming is inseparable from the band's melodic identity. Reinert is the founding architect of jazz-death fusion — his early-1990s work opened a creative direction that technical death metal still explores. Both prove that the most brutal and the most beautiful corners of metal were never as far apart as they seemed.
