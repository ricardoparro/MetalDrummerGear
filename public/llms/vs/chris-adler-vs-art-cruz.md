## Style & Technique Comparison

Chris Adler co-founded Lamb of God in 1994 and spent 25 years building groove metal's technical template around syncopated triplet grooves, a signature independent-hi-hat "pivot" technique, and razor-sharp double bass patterns on Grammy-nominated albums including "Ashes of the Wake" (2004), "Sacrament" (2006), and "Wrath" (2009), before departing the band in 2019.

Art Cruz stepped into one of modern metal's most closely watched drum chairs later that year, having first filled in on Lamb of God's 2018 tour supporting Slayer's farewell run. His explosive, dynamics-forward attack — shaped by years in Prong and Winds of Plague — brought aggressive fills and precise double bass work to the band's self-titled 2020 album (see [Lamb of God self-titled drum setup](https://metalforge.io/articles/lamb-of-god-self-titled-drum-setup)) and "Omens" (2022) (see [Omens drum setup](https://metalforge.io/articles/omens-drum-setup)), reenergizing the band's sound while honoring Adler's legacy.

---

## Key Differences

The core divergence is engineered precision versus explosive energy: Adler's technique is built on obsessive rhythmic precision — tight, syncopated triplet grooves and a double bass approach influenced as much by Latin and jazz rhythms as by metal. Cruz brings a more explosive, dynamics-forward attack with aggressive fills and a willingness to push harder into extremity than Adler's more restrained groove pocket.

Gear reflects that split. Adler's Mapex kit and darker Meinl Byzance cymbals were voiced for dry, focused cut. Cruz's Ludwig kit and brighter Zildjian A Custom cymbals lean into a more explosive, cutting attack.

---

## Influence & Legacy

Adler defined the New Wave of American Heavy Metal drumming template through Lamb of God's Grammy-nominated 2000s and 2010s catalog, proving groove metal could be simultaneously crushingly heavy and mathematically precise — a reference point every successor is measured against.

Cruz took on the hardest job in modern metal drumming — replacing a beloved founding member — and earned respect by delivering two well-received studio albums that honored the band's legacy while injecting fresh aggression, proving Lamb of God's groove metal foundation could evolve without losing its identity.

---

## Verdict

Chris Adler and Art Cruz represent metal's most closely watched drummer succession story. Adler is Lamb of God's founding architect — 25 years of razor-precise, syncopated groove metal drumming that helped define an entire genre movement. Cruz is the drummer who had to fill that chair — bringing explosive aggression and his own identity to two studio albums without erasing what came before. Both answer "who is the better Lamb of God drummer?" from opposite sides of the same succession.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is technically more skilled, Chris Adler or Art Cruz?**
A: Both are highly skilled, but in different ways. Chris Adler is renowned for obsessive rhythmic precision — tight, syncopated triplet grooves and a signature independent-hi-hat "pivot" technique built over 25 years with Lamb of God. Art Cruz brings a more explosive, dynamics-forward attack with aggressive fills and precise double bass work honed in Prong and Winds of Plague. Adler represents mathematically engineered groove; Cruz represents raw, explosive energy — there is no objectively "better," only different technical philosophies.

**Q: What is each drummer's signature technique?**
A: Chris Adler's signature is his "pivot" technique — using independent left-foot hi-hat work to add dynamic accents while both hands stay free for riff-locked patterns, plus tight triplet-based double bass grooves. Art Cruz's signature is his explosive dynamic range and aggressive fill work, built from years of high-energy touring with Winds of Plague and Prong before joining Lamb of God.

**Q: Why did Lamb of God change drummers?**
A: Chris Adler departed Lamb of God in 2019 after 25 years as the band's founding drummer. Art Cruz, who had already filled in for Adler on Lamb of God's 2018 tour supporting Slayer's farewell run, was announced as his permanent replacement later that year and recorded the band's self-titled 2020 album and "Omens" (2022).

**Q: What gear do Chris Adler and Art Cruz use?**
A: Chris Adler played a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab kit with a Mapex Chris Adler Signature snare and Meinl Byzance Series cymbals. Art Cruz plays a Ludwig Drums kit with a Ludwig Black Beauty snare and Zildjian A Custom Series cymbals — a notably brighter cymbal voicing than Adler's darker Meinl setup.
