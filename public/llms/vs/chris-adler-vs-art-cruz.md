## Gear Deep Dive

### Chris Adler Setup

- **Drums:** Mapex Black Panther Design Lab
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series (14" Dark Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Dark Crashes, 21" Transition Ride, 18" Extra Dry China)
- **Snare:** Mapex Chris Adler Signature 14x5.5" Walnut/Maple
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Mapex Falcon Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth Chris Adler Signature

Chris Adler's Mapex Black Panther Design Lab kit delivered the dry, focused tone central to his groove-driven patterns across "Ashes of the Wake" (2004) and [Sacrament](https://metalforge.io/articles/sacrament-drum-setup) (2006) — full breakdown in [What's In Chris Adler's Kit](https://metalforge.io/articles/whats-in-chris-adlers-kit). His shallow, articulate signature snare punched through dense guitar walls with the precise crack essential for Lamb of God's syncopated riff-locking grooves, while Meinl's Extra Dry China gave his fills a raw, cutting punctuation.

### Art Cruz Setup

- **Drums:** Ludwig Classic Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom & K Series (14" A Custom Mastersound Hi-Hats, A Custom crashes, 21" A Zildjian Mega Bell Ride, K China and FX cymbals)
- **Snare:** Ludwig 14x6.5" Black Beauty
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5B

Art Cruz's Ludwig/Zildjian rig settled into place for Lamb of God's 2020 self-titled album — his first studio recording as the band's full-time drummer — and carried into [Omens](https://metalforge.io/articles/omens-drum-setup) (2022), full breakdown in [What's In Art Cruz's Kit](https://metalforge.io/articles/whats-in-art-cruzs-kit). The Black Beauty snare's brass shell produces a brighter, more cutting crack than Adler's walnut/maple signature, and the Trick Bigfoot pedal delivers the fast, ergonomic footwork his deathcore background demands.

## Style & Technique Comparison

Chris Adler co-founded Lamb of God (as Burn the Priest) in 1994 and drove the band's rise from Richmond, Virginia underground act to New Wave of American Heavy Metal figureheads. His signature is riding the cymbal bell with his right hand while his left foot works independent "pivot" hi-hat accents — where most groove metal drummers anchor on the hi-hat, Adler's bell-driven approach gave Lamb of God its mechanical, industrial momentum, locking tight, syncopated kick patterns beneath a wall of riff. His four-way independence let him layer china crashes, ghost notes, and triplet fills over a double-bass foundation that never dropped, a technical benchmark studied by groove metal drummers worldwide.

Art Cruz filled in for Adler on Lamb of God's 2018 Slayer farewell tour before being named permanent drummer in 2019. Coming from the deathcore and industrial-groove underground with Winds of Plague and Prong, Cruz brings a blast-beat foundation and more overt physical power than Adler's controlled precision. His playing emphasizes attack on the front of the beat, reinforcing Lamb of God's heavier, more dynamic recent material, and his tonal flexibility across A Custom and K China cymbal combinations lets him cover the band's full dynamic range live.

## Key Differences

Adler's Meinl setup, especially the Extra Dry China, produced a dark, grinding tone inseparable from Lamb of God's first decade of recordings. Cruz's Zildjian A Custom series produces a brighter, more cutting sound — different, not inferior. Adler's ride-bell technique gives his groove a mechanical precision that Cruz, working from a more power-oriented approach, doesn't replicate exactly, though Cruz executes the parts faithfully in live performance. Stick and pedal choice reflect their backgrounds: Adler's Mapex Falcon and signature stick were tuned for ride-bell leverage; Cruz's Trick Bigfoot and Vic Firth 5B are built for speed and raw power.

## Influence & Legacy

Chris Adler's ride-bell approach and innovative kick patterning shaped an entire generation of groove metal drummers, and his 2016 guest stint on Megadeth's Grammy-winning "Dystopia" extended his influence beyond Lamb of God. His interplay between kick patterns and guitar riffs on "As the Palaces Burn" and "Ashes of the Wake" remains a study reference for drummers and producers.

Art Cruz inherited one of the most technically demanding drum chairs in modern metal and rose to it — learning Adler's notoriously complex catalog while adding his own physicality. His work on "Omens" (2022) demonstrates Lamb of God continuing to operate at groove metal's highest level, answering modern metal's most-asked "who replaced who" question in the process.

## Verdict

Chris Adler vs Art Cruz is a generational handoff, not a rivalry. Adler built Lamb of God's drumming identity over 25 years and defined groove metal's modern technical standard with his ride-bell innovation. Cruz stepped into that chair in 2019, honored the legacy, and kept one of metal's hardest-working bands performing at the highest level. Both are elite drummers; what you value more — innovation or execution — determines which era resonates most.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is technically more skilled, Chris Adler or Art Cruz?**
A: Both are elite technicians in different registers. Chris Adler's innovative ride-bell technique and four-way independence created Lamb of God's groove metal template over two decades, widely considered a technical benchmark for the genre. Art Cruz brings a blast-beat-capable deathcore background and faithfully executes Adler's demanding catalog live while adding his own explosive dynamics — the consensus is Adler's contribution was more innovative, while Cruz is arguably the more versatile all-around technician.

**Q: What is each drummer's signature technique?**
A: Chris Adler's signature is riding the cymbal bell with his right hand while his left foot works independent "pivot" hi-hat accents, driving Lamb of God's syncopated kick patterns underneath. Art Cruz's signature is explosive dynamic contrast — powerful, front-of-the-beat attack drawn from his deathcore roots in Winds of Plague, layered onto the groove-metal foundation Adler established.

**Q: Why did Lamb of God change drummers?**
A: Chris Adler stepped away from Lamb of God in 2019 after 25 years, and Art Cruz — who had already filled in during the band's 2018 Slayer farewell tour — was named the permanent replacement. Cruz had to learn Adler's entire technically demanding catalog in a short time before recording the band's 2020 self-titled album.

**Q: What gear do Chris Adler and Art Cruz use?**
A: Chris Adler played a Mapex Black Panther Design Lab kit with a Mapex Chris Adler Signature snare and Meinl Byzance Series cymbals, driven by a Mapex Falcon double pedal. Art Cruz plays a Ludwig Classic Maple kit with a Ludwig Black Beauty snare and Zildjian A Custom & K Series cymbals, driven by a Trick Pro 1-V Bigfoot double pedal.
