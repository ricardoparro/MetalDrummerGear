## Playing Style

Eloy Casagrande became Sepultura's drummer in 2011 at just 20 years old, spending 13 years driving the Brazilian thrash institution's modern era before joining Slipknot in 2024, where his explosive speed and technical prowess earned him Modern Drummer magazine's No. 1 metal drummer honor in their 2024 Readers' Poll. Mario Duplantier co-founded progressive death metal band Gojira with his brother Joe Duplantier in Bayonne, France, in 1996, and built his reputation on crushing double bass patterns layered with tribal, almost ceremonial rhythmic structures across albums like "From Mars to Sirius" (2005), "L'Enfant Sauvage" (2012), and "Magma" (2016).

## Technique

Casagrande's technique is built for modern extreme dynamics — explosive double bass speed, technical fill construction, and the stamina to move between Sepultura's thrash-groove foundation and Slipknot's maximalist nine-member live show without losing precision. Duplantier blends raw power with polyrhythmic complexity and an unmistakable tribal sensibility, often locking into Gojira's tuned, resonant guitar tones with explosive fills and odd-meter grooves that feel organic rather than mechanical.

## Influence & Legacy

Casagrande represents metal drumming's modern generation — Modern Drummer's 2024 Readers' Poll No. 1 metal drummer, whose transition from Sepultura to Slipknot at the top of both bands' drum chairs demonstrates the technical standard contemporary metal now demands. Duplantier has become one of progressive death metal's most influential modern voices, his tribal, polyrhythmic approach on Gojira records shaping a generation of drummers who blend technical death metal with atmospheric, almost meditative groove.

## Verdict

Eloy Casagrande and Mario Duplantier are two of the most-searched technical drummers in modern metal, each defining a different national school of extreme precision. Casagrande built a reputation on explosive speed and stamina across 13 years with Sepultura before stepping into Slipknot's drum chair in 2024, earning Modern Drummer's top metal drummer honor along the way. Duplantier has spent nearly three decades turning Gojira's tribal, tuned-percussion attack into one of progressive death metal's most influential templates. Brazilian power meets French tribal precision — both share Tama Starclassic kits and Iron Cobra pedals as the technical foundation for two very different sonic identities.
