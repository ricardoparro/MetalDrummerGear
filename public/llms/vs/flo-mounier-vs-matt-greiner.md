## Playing Style

Flo Mounier has been the only constant member of Canadian technical death metal pioneers Cryptopsy since joining in 1992, setting new standards for extreme metal drumming on landmark albums like "None So Vile" (1996) and "Whisper Supremacy" (1998) with sustained blast beats at 280+ BPM, complex polyrhythmic fills, and inhuman stamina. Matt Greiner co-founded metalcore band August Burns Red in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, in 2003, and has driven the band's Grammy-nominated catalog for over two decades with jazz-influenced dynamics and technical precision that helped define modern metalcore's compositional ambition.

## Technique

Mounier is widely regarded as one of the most technically proficient drummers in death metal, bringing jazz influences from Buddy Rich and Dave Weckl into extreme metal — his gravity-blast technique produces sustained speed with musical dynamics and ghost notes rather than pure brute force. Greiner's technique also draws on jazz-informed dynamics, but channels them into meticulous compositional control, favoring purposeful transitions between blast beats, breakdowns, and melodic sections that showcase August Burns Red's progressive songwriting rather than raw speed alone. Both drummers treat extreme metal's blast beat as a musical tool shaped by jazz phrasing rather than a brute-force gesture — Mounier in service of sustained velocity, Greiner in service of composed structure.

## Influence & Legacy

Mounier's "None So Vile" is regularly cited as one of the greatest extreme metal drum recordings of all time, and his teaching work directly influences thousands of death metal drummers worldwide through clinics conducted across three decades. Greiner has helped raise metalcore's compositional ceiling across August Burns Red's Grammy-nominated catalog, and his own signature Pearl snare and Vic Firth stick line reflect his standing as one of the genre's most respected technicians.

## Verdict

Flo Mounier and Matt Greiner both prove that extreme metal's most demanding subgenres reward jazz-informed musicality as much as raw speed. Mounier pushes technical death metal to its absolute physical limits with Cryptopsy, sustaining blast beats past 280 BPM while retaining the ghost notes and dynamics of his jazz influences — "None So Vile" remains a benchmark nearly 30 years later. Greiner channels a similar jazz sensibility into metalcore's compositional architecture with August Burns Red, prioritizing purposeful transitions over maximum velocity. Mounier is technical death metal's speed specialist; Greiner is metalcore's compositional technician — both represent their subgenres' most respected drum chairs.
