## Playing Style

Flo Mounier has been the only constant member of Canadian technical death metal pioneers Cryptopsy since joining in 1992, setting new standards for extreme metal drumming on landmark albums like "None So Vile" (1996) and "Whisper Supremacy" (1998) with sustained blast beats at 280+ BPM, complex polyrhythmic fills, and inhuman stamina. Matt Greiner co-founded metalcore band August Burns Red in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, in 2003, and has driven the band's Grammy-nominated catalog for over two decades with jazz-influenced dynamics and technical precision across albums including "Messengers" (2007) and "Guardians" (2020).

## Technique

Mounier is widely regarded as one of the most technically proficient drummers in death metal, bringing jazz influences from Buddy Rich and Dave Weckl into extreme metal — his gravity-blast technique produces sustained speed with musical dynamics and ghost notes rather than pure brute force. Greiner weaves jazz-influenced dynamics and odd time signatures seamlessly into August Burns Red's metalcore song structures, favoring integrated compositional complexity — blast beats deployed as one color among many — over overt technical display. Both drummers channel jazz vocabulary into extreme metal, but Mounier pushes it toward maximum sustained velocity while Greiner folds it into songwriting discipline.

## Influence & Legacy

Mounier's "None So Vile" is regularly cited as one of the greatest extreme metal drum recordings of all time, and his teaching work directly influences thousands of death metal drummers worldwide through clinics conducted across three decades. Greiner has earned two Grammy nominations for Best Metal Performance with August Burns Red and co-founded the Greiner & Kilmer drum company, helping establish metalcore as a genre capable of the same technical ambition long associated with death metal.

## Verdict

Flo Mounier and Matt Greiner represent two technical-precision peaks from different extreme metal subgenres and different eras. Mounier pushes death metal to its absolute physical limits with Cryptopsy — "None So Vile" remains a benchmark nearly 30 years later for blast beat velocity and jazz-informed complexity. Greiner turns that same jazz-informed technical instinct into Grammy-nominated metalcore songcraft with August Burns Red, prioritizing compositional discipline over raw speed. Mounier is technical death metal's benchmark for extremity; Greiner is metalcore's benchmark for integrated, purposeful technicality.
