## Gear Deep Dive

### Flo Mounier Setup

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX & HHX Series (14" AAX Stage Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" AAX X-Plosion Crashes, 22" AAX Stage Ride, 19" HHX Evolution O-Zone China)
- **Snare:** Tama S.L.P. 14x6.5" G-Maple
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Tama Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide Double Pedal, Tama 1st Chair Throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A

Mounier's Tama Starclassic Maple kit delivers warm, projecting tone even at the extreme speeds Cryptopsy demands. The maple shells maintain tonal clarity through rapid-fire technical passages — critical when ghost notes and complex fills must register in a dense death metal mix. His Tama Iron Cobra 900 pedals are trusted throughout the extreme metal world for consistent response under heavy playing. Mounier combines both Sabian AAX and HHX lines — AAX for controlled bright attack, HHX for broader tonal character — giving him a richer sonic palette than most extreme metal drummers employ.

### Pete Sandoval Setup

- **Drums:** ddrum Dios Series
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series (14" Stage Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" X-Plosion Crashes, 20" Stage Ride, 18" Chinese)
- **Snare:** ddrum Dios 14x6.5" Maple
- **Pedals/Hardware:** ddrum Mercury Double Pedal, ddrum Throne
- **Sticks:** Ahead Lars Ulrich Signature

Pete Sandoval's ddrum setup prioritizes function and durability. His stripped-back approach reflects a philosophy where the player's technique — not the equipment — defines the performance. The ddrum Mercury double pedal is built for the punishment extreme death metal touring inflicts night after night. Sandoval's Sabian AAX cymbals provide consistent, cutting attack across his performances. His association with ddrum came through sponsorship during Morbid Angel's peak touring years, and his setup remained reliably minimalist throughout.

## Style & Technique Comparison

Flo Mounier brought technical death metal blast beats to new heights of complexity with Cryptopsy. His performance on *None So Vile* (1996) is widely considered one of the most demanding drum recordings in metal history — a relentless combination of extreme blast beat velocity, jazz-influenced ghost notes, and compositionally complex fills that defied what listeners thought was possible. Mounier's gravity blast technique uses wrist tension variation to control stick rebound, enabling sustained speeds with tonal nuance and fluidity. This jazz crossover quality — the swing and timing underneath the brutality — distinguishes Mounier from pure speed players.

Pete Sandoval is a founding father of the gravity blast and Florida death metal drumming. His work on Morbid Angel's *Altars of Madness* (1989) and *Blessed Are the Sick* (1991) defined the raw, relentless power at the core of American death metal. Sandoval developed an ankle-driven technique for extreme double bass — using the natural spring rebound of the pedal mechanism to generate foot speeds beyond what muscular effort alone produces. This technique became standard vocabulary for extreme metal drummers globally and represents one of the most significant technical innovations in the history of the drum kit.

Both players helped establish the vocabulary of extreme metal drumming, but from distinct traditions: Sandoval from the Florida death metal scene that birthed Obituary, Cannibal Corpse, and Deicide; Mounier from the Canadian technical death metal movement that pushed complexity, precision, and jazz influence into the extreme.

## Key Differences

Mounier's combination of Sabian AAX and HHX cymbals reflects his technical approach — more tonal variety suited to Cryptopsy's compositionally demanding music. Sandoval's exclusive AAX use prioritizes consistent, cutting attack across all cymbal voices, reducing variables in a speed-focused playing style.

Their snare choices mirror this distinction: Sandoval's ddrum Dios maple snare is punchy and functional; Mounier's Tama S.L.P. G-Maple offers greater tonal complexity suited to the dynamic range that technical death metal requires.

The core technique difference is the approach to blast beats: Mounier's wrist tension variation enables swing and ghost-note integration within blast patterns. Sandoval's ankle-driven approach maximizes raw speed through momentum — two different engineering solutions to the same extreme velocity problem.

## Influence & Legacy

Flo Mounier's influence is especially strong in technical death metal circles. *None So Vile* remains a benchmark album studied by serious extreme metal drummers. Cryptopsy's ongoing influence on the technical death metal genre — brutal truth, slam, goregrind — runs through Mounier's drumming. His online presence and teaching work have helped pass advanced techniques directly to the next generation of death metal drummers worldwide.

Pete Sandoval's influence extends across all of extreme metal. He is credited alongside Dave Lombardo and Gene Hoglan as one of the key inventors of modern extreme metal drumming. His gravity blast and ankle-driven double bass technique became standard vocabulary for death, grindcore, and black metal drummers globally. Morbid Angel's position as one of the founding bands of death metal means Sandoval's contributions are built into the DNA of the entire genre.

## Verdict

Flo Mounier and Pete Sandoval are two pillars of extreme metal drumming who approached speed and brutality from different angles. Sandoval is the raw, primal force who helped invent the language — Florida death metal's percussion foundationstone. Mounier is the technical master who pushed that language to its outer limits — technical death metal's highest recorded standard. Both are irreplaceable. The debate between them is whether you value the invention of the vocabulary or the perfection of it.
