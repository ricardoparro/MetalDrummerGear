## Playing Style

Gavin Harrison, born in Harrow, London, in 1963, spent his early career as an in-demand session drummer before joining Porcupine Tree in 2002, a move that redefined progressive rock drumming on albums like "In Absentia" (2002), "Deadwing" (2005), and "Fear of a Blank Planet" (2007); he joined King Crimson's revolutionary three-drummer lineup in 2008 and also plays with The Pineapple Thief. Matt Greiner co-founded metalcore band August Burns Red in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, in 2003, and has driven the band's Grammy-nominated catalog for over two decades with jazz-influenced dynamics and technical precision that helped define modern metalcore's compositional ambition on records like "Found in Far Away Places" (2015) and "Guardians" (2020).

## Technique

Harrison plays with restrained sophistication, layering polyrhythms and ghost notes through odd time signatures while combining jazz vocabulary with metal intensity — an almost scientific approach to rhythm that lets 7/8 groove harder than straight 4/4. Greiner brings jazz-informed dynamics and odd-meter phrasing to metalcore songwriting, weaving technical complexity into riff-driven arrangements without sacrificing the genre's breakdown-heavy impact.

## Key Differences

Gavin Harrison plays a custom Sonor SQ2 kit (22" bass drum, 10" & 12" rack toms, 14" & 16" floor toms) with his own signature 14x5.25" brass snare and Zildjian K Custom Special Dry cymbals (14" Special Dry Hi-Hats, 16" & 18" Special Dry Crashes, 21" Special Dry Ride, 18" Trash China), driven by a Sonor Perfect Balance Pedal. Matt Greiner plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit in Piano Black with his own Pearl Matt Greiner Signature 14x6" steel snare and Meinl Byzance cymbals (15" Dual Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Extra Dry Medium Crashes, 21" Transition Ride, 18" Extra Dry China), driven by a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal and his own Vic Firth Matt Greiner Signature sticks. ## Influence & Legacy

Harrison has been recognized multiple times by Modern Drummer magazine as progressive rock's best drummer, and his instructional material on polyrhythms and odd-time playing is required study for serious drummers worldwide. Greiner's two-decade run with August Burns Red helped establish technical metalcore as Grammy-nominated, mainstream-viable music, and his jazz-informed approach to a genre built on breakdowns has influenced a generation of technically ambitious heavy drummers.

## Verdict

Gavin Harrison and Matt Greiner occupy the progressive end of two very different genres, each prized for bringing jazz sophistication to heavy music rather than raw speed. Harrison's restrained, polyrhythmic mastery redefined what progressive rock drumming could sound like across Porcupine Tree, King Crimson, and The Pineapple Thief. Greiner's jazz-informed technicality turned August Burns Red into a Grammy-nominated force within modern metalcore. Both prove that odd time signatures and ghost-note nuance can serve the song rather than just showcase chops — international technical-precision drumming from two entirely different corners of heavy music.
