## Gear Deep Dive

### Gene Hoglan Setup

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Pure (maple/mahogany hybrid shell)
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series (15" Hi-Hats, 18" & 20" Crashes, 22" Ride, 20" China)
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Brass
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal, Pearl D-2000 Throne
- **Sticks:** Promark 5B
- **Heads:** Evans

Nicknamed "The Atomic Clock" for his metronomic precision, Hoglan plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit chosen for its tonal clarity and projection at the extreme tempos he maintains across death metal, thrash, and progressive metal. His brass Pearl Reference snare delivers the precise, cutting crack his playing demands, and Sabian AAX cymbals give him crisp, articulate response across Death, Testament, Dark Angel, and Dethklok sessions. Pearl Demon Drive double pedals — engineered for single-chain precision — power the rapid double-bass passages that are a Hoglan trademark.

### Pete Sandoval Setup

- **Drums:** ddrum Dios Series
- **Cymbals:** Sabian AAX Series (14" Stage Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" X-Plosion Crashes, 20" Stage Ride, 18" Chinese)
- **Snare:** ddrum Dios 14x6.5" Maple
- **Pedals/Hardware:** ddrum Mercury Double Pedal, ddrum Throne
- **Sticks:** Ahead Lars Ulrich Signature
- **Heads:** Evans

Sandoval built his career on Morbid Angel's genre-defining death metal records, and his ddrum Mercury double pedal was central to executing the gravity blast technique he is credited with pioneering — using the rebound of the beater to sustain extreme velocity with less physical strain. His Sabian AAX cymbals provided bright, cutting attack, and the ddrum Dios maple snare delivered the sharp crack that anchored classics like *Altars of Madness* (1989) and *Covenant* (1993).

Notably, both drummers converge on Sabian AAX cymbals — a shared choice for bright, cutting attack at extreme tempos — despite building otherwise very different kits around opposite technical philosophies.

## Style & Technique Comparison

Gene Hoglan opened Dark Angel's *Darkness Descends* (1986) at a then-record 247 BPM at just 19 years old, and went on to redefine progressive death metal with Death's *Individual Thought Patterns* (1993) before anchoring Testament and the animated band Dethklok for decades. His technique blends raw speed with musicality and stamina — his blast-beat precursor patterns were architectural rather than pure noise, and he sustains complex, endurance-heavy sets without relying on a click track. Hoglan's genre-spanning career — thrash, death metal, industrial metal, progressive metal — makes him one of extreme metal's most versatile figures.

Pete Sandoval co-founded grindcore pioneers Terrorizer before joining Morbid Angel in 1988, where his gravity blast technique became the template for modern death metal drumming. AllMusic's Jason Birchmeier wrote that Sandoval "challenges one's perception of how fast a drummer can possibly drum." His playing on *Altars of Madness* (1989), *Blessed Are the Sick* (1991), and *Covenant* (1993) is pure, relentless top-end velocity rather than Hoglan's more varied, groove-inflected attack — a specialist's mastery of one extreme rather than a generalist's range.

## Key Differences

Hoglan's Pearl Reference Pure kit and brass signature snare are voiced for versatility across multiple metal subgenres, from Testament's thrash to Dethklok's animated death metal. Sandoval's ddrum Dios setup was tuned specifically for Morbid Angel's dense, Florida-style death metal attack. The pedal choice tells the real story: Hoglan's Pearl Demon Drive supports his broad technical vocabulary across bands, while Sandoval's ddrum Mercury was purpose-built for the gravity blast mechanics that made him extreme metal's speed benchmark.

## Influence & Legacy

Hoglan's combination of speed, power, and musicality across Dark Angel, Death, Testament, Strapping Young Lad, and Dethklok has influenced countless metal drummers and made him one of extreme metal's most respected all-around figures — a reference point well outside pure death metal. Sandoval's gravity blast became the foundational technique of modern death metal and grindcore drumming, setting a physical standard that every extreme-speed drummer since has been measured against.

## Verdict

Gene Hoglan and Pete Sandoval represent extreme metal's two poles of speed. ## FAQ

**Q: Who plays faster, Gene Hoglan or Pete Sandoval?**
A: Pete Sandoval is generally considered the faster pure blast-beat drummer — his gravity blast technique, which he is credited with pioneering, let him sustain velocities that pushed the physical limits of extreme metal drumming on records like *Altars of Madness* (1989). Gene Hoglan prioritizes precision, stamina, and musicality over sheer top-end speed, though his 247 BPM opening to Dark Angel's *Darkness Descends* (1986) was a genre speed record in its own right.

**Q: What is each drummer's signature technique?**
A: Gene Hoglan's signature is metronomic, click-track-free precision across marathon sets — earning him the nickname "The Atomic Clock." Pete Sandoval's signature is the gravity blast, a technique using the kick pedal beater's rebound to generate extra speed with less physical strain, which he is credited with pioneering and perfecting.

**Q: Who has influenced more drummers, Gene Hoglan or Pete Sandoval?**
A: Both are hugely influential in different ways. Sandoval's gravity blast became the technical foundation nearly every modern extreme-speed drummer studies. Hoglan's influence is broader across subgenres — his work spans thrash, death metal, industrial metal, and animated death metal, making him a reference point for drummers well outside pure death metal.

**Q: What gear do Gene Hoglan and Pete Sandoval use?**
A: Gene Hoglan plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit with a Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Brass snare and Sabian AAX cymbals. Pete Sandoval played a ddrum Dios Series kit with a ddrum Dios 14x6.5" Maple snare and Sabian AAX Series cymbals, driven by a ddrum Mercury double pedal.
