## Playing Style

Gene Hoglan, nicknamed "The Atomic Clock" for his metronomic timing, built a four-decade career across Dark Angel, Death, Testament, Strapping Young Lad, and Dethklok, blending thrash aggression with death metal complexity at tempos up to 220 BPM. Pete Sandoval, a founding member of Morbid Angel, is credited with pioneering and perfecting the gravity blast — a technique using the drum rim as a fulcrum to generate blast beats far faster than a conventional wrist-driven approach, heard across "Altars of Madness" (1989), "Blessed Are the Sick" (1991), and "Covenant" (1993).

## Technique

Hoglan's reputation rests on combining raw speed with musicality and stamina — he sustains extreme tempos across entire albums without sacrificing groove or dynamic phrasing, a rare balance in extreme metal. Sandoval's gravity blast technique prioritizes pure, sustained velocity, using leg and rim leverage to produce relentless blast beats that became the genre-defining sound of death metal's early Florida and Morbid Angel scene, though at the cost of the groove-oriented musicality Hoglan is known for.

## Influence & Legacy

Hoglan's precision-plus-stamina approach influenced thrash and death metal drummers seeking speed without sacrificing feel, and his work across Death's "Symbolic" and Testament's "Formation of Damnation" is studied for its combination of technicality and groove. Sandoval's gravity blast fundamentally changed what was considered possible on a drum kit, becoming standard vocabulary for death metal drummers worldwide seeking to push blast beat tempos beyond wrist-driven limits.

## Verdict

Hoglan and Sandoval represent extreme metal's two poles of speed. Gene Hoglan is precision plus stamina — sustained tempo and musicality across decades and multiple bands. Pete Sandoval is pure velocity — the gravity blast innovator who redefined how fast a death metal drummer could play. Ask "who plays faster" and the answer is Sandoval in short bursts; ask "who sustains it best across a whole career" and the answer is Hoglan.
