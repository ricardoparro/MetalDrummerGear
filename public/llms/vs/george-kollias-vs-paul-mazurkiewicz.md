## Playing Style

George Kollias joined Nile in 2004 and has anchored every album since "Annihilation of the Wicked" (2005), bringing Egyptian-themed technical death metal to life with sustained blast beats and relentless double bass across some of the genre's most demanding compositions, including "Ithyphallic" (2007), "Those Whom the Gods Detest" (2009), and "Vile Nilotic Rites" (2019). Paul Mazurkiewicz co-founded Cannibal Corpse in Buffalo, New York in 1988 and has anchored every studio album the world's best-selling death metal band has released since their 1990 debut "Eaten Back to Life" — a 35-plus-year run of thunderous, mid-tempo-focused brutality across landmark records like "Tomb of the Mutilated" (1992) and "The Bleeding" (1994).

## Technique

Kollias uses heel-toe bass drum technique to sustain extreme tempos with mechanical precision, co-designing the Pearl Demon XR double pedal specifically for the endurance Nile's marathon compositions demand. Mazurkiewicz uses a traditional alternating-leg double kick approach, keeping his parts locked to Cannibal Corpse's riffs rather than showcasing individual virtuosity — a song-serving philosophy that has kept the band's rhythmic foundation rock-solid across 16 studio albums.

## Influence & Legacy

Kollias raised the technical ceiling for death metal drumming worldwide — his instructional books and clinics have spread heel-toe blast beat technique to a global audience of extreme metal drummers. Mazurkiewicz has been the backbone of the best-selling death metal band of all time for over three and a half decades, proving that unwavering consistency and groove can define a genre just as powerfully as raw technical speed.

## Verdict

George Kollias and Paul Mazurkiewicz represent death metal drumming's two defining philosophies. Kollias is the speed king — Nile's technical, blast-beat-driven Egyptian death metal demands sustained tempos past 280 BPM, and Kollias delivers them with heel-toe precision that has made him a benchmark for extreme metal drummers everywhere. Mazurkiewicz is the consistency king — Cannibal Corpse's crushing, mid-tempo-focused brutality has never wavered across 16 studio albums and 35-plus years, anchored by a drummer who serves the riff over raw velocity. Both endorse Pearl drums, but their answers to "how fast should death metal be?" could not be more different.
