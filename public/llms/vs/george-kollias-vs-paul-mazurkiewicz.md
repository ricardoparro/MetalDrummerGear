## Playing Style

George Kollias, born in Korinth, Greece in 1977, joined Nile in 2004 (replacing Tony Laureano) and has spent two decades pushing technical death metal's speed ceiling on albums like "Annihilation of the Wicked" (2005), "Ithyphallic" (2007), "Those Whom the Gods Detest" (2009), and "Vile Nilotic Rites" (2019), sustaining blast beats at 280+ BPM across Nile's Egyptian-themed, historically dense compositions. Paul Mazurkiewicz co-founded Cannibal Corpse in Buffalo, New York in 1988 and has anchored every studio album the band has released since their 1990 debut "Eaten Back to Life" — a 35-plus-year tenure that makes him one of the most consistent drummers in death metal, driving landmark records like "Tomb of the Mutilated" (1992) and "The Bleeding" (1994) with thunderous, song-serving brutality.

## Technique

Kollias sustains extreme tempos with heel-toe double bass technique and co-designed the Pearl Demon XR pedal specifically for the speed his playing demands, treating extreme velocity as a controllable, teachable skill through his instructional books, DVDs, and clinics. Mazurkiewicz uses a traditional alternating-leg double kick approach, prioritizing consistency, groove, and relentless drive over technical flash — his parts serve Cannibal Corpse's riffs rather than showcasing individual virtuosity, keeping tempos punishing across marathon brutal death compositions.

## Key Differences

George Kollias plays a Pearl Masterworks Stadium Exotic kit with a Pearl George Kollias Signature 14x6.5" snare and Zildjian cymbals (14" K Mastersound Hi-Hats, 17" & 18" K Custom Dark Crashes, 21" A Custom Mega Bell Ride, 18" China), driven by his co-designed Pearl Demon XR double pedal and Vic Firth George Kollias Signature SGK sticks. Paul Mazurkiewicz plays a Pearl Reference Series kit with a Pearl Free-Floating 14x6.5" steel snare and Zildjian A Custom Series cymbals, driven by a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal and his own Vater signature sticks. Kollias sustains extreme tempos with heel-toe double bass technique built for maximum speed; Mazurkiewicz uses traditional alternating-leg double kick built for maximum consistency.

## Influence & Legacy

Kollias raised the technical ceiling for death metal speed and endurance, and as an educator has spread advanced blast beat and double bass technique to a global audience of extreme metal drummers through instructional books, DVDs, and clinics. Mazurkiewicz has been the backbone of the world's best-selling death metal band for over 35 years, anchoring every Cannibal Corpse record from "Eaten Back to Life" through "Violence Unimagined" (2021) and helping the band sell over 2 million albums worldwide without ever missing a release.

## Verdict

George Kollias and Paul Mazurkiewicz represent two different paths to death metal drumming greatness. Kollias is the technical speed specialist — his 280+ BPM sustained blast beats with Nile pushed the genre's velocity ceiling and turned extreme tempo into a teachable discipline. Mazurkiewicz is the model of unbroken consistency — over three decades anchoring the world's best-selling death metal band without a single lineup change at the drum chair. Both are frequently cited alongside Pete Sandoval in "fastest death metal drummer" discussions, and both answer that question from opposite directions: Kollias through technical mastery of extreme tempo, Mazurkiewicz through relentless, career-long reliability at the genre's punishing standard pace.
