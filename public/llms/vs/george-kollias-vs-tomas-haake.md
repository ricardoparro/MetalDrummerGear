## Gear Deep Dive

### George Kollias Setup

- **Drums:** Pearl Masterworks Stadium Exotic (Piano Black with Gold Hardware)
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian (14" K Mastersound Hi-Hats, 17" & 18" K Custom Dark Crashes, 21" A Custom Mega Bell Ride, 18" China)
- **Snare:** Pearl George Kollias Signature 14x6.5"
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Pearl Demon XR Double Pedal (Co-designed), Pearl D-3000 Throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth George Kollias Signature SGK

Kollias co-designed the Pearl Demon XR double pedal specifically for extreme speed and reliability in demanding death metal performance. His Pearl Masterworks kit offers superior build quality that withstands the punishment of Nile's intense touring schedule. The Zildjian K and A Custom cymbal combination delivers dark complexity alongside cutting attack — perfect for Nile's Egyptian-themed brutal death metal compositions.

### Tomas Haake Setup

- **Drums:** Sonor SQ2 Heavy Beech (24"x18" Bass, 10"x8", 12"x9", 13"x10", 16"x14", 18"x16" Toms)
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX & AAX Series (14" HHX Compression Hi-Hats, 15" Artisan Hi-Hats, 19" & 20" & 21" HHX Stage Crashes, 22" Legacy Ride, 19" AAXtreme China)
- **Snare:** Sonor Tomas Haake Signature 14x6.5" & Artist Series Bronze
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Tama Speed Cobra Single Pedals (x2), Porter & Davies BC2 Throne
- **Sticks:** Wincent Tomas Haake Signature

Haake's most distinctive gear choice is his use of two single bass drum pedals rather than a standard double pedal — a deliberate constraint that shapes his entire approach to kick drum patterning. His Sonor SQ2 Heavy Beech kit delivers exceptional resonance and projection suited to Meshuggah's polyrhythmic complexity and dense low-tuned guitar arrangements. The Sabian HHX series provides focused, cutting cymbal attack that penetrates the band's massive sonic wall.

## Style & Technique Comparison

George Kollias represents the apex of death metal speed drumming. Famous for his gravity blast beats and heel-toe double bass technique, Kollias plays at tempos that push the outer limits of human physical capability — sustained blast beats at 280+ BPM are his trademark. His drumming with Nile is brutal, precise, and relentlessly extreme, serving the band's ancient Egyptian thematic brutality with technical mastery and endurance.

Tomas Haake invented the djent drumming template with Meshuggah. His playing is defined by machine-like polyrhythmic precision — complex kick, snare, and cymbal patterns that cycle across extended odd time signatures independent of the guitar riff, creating hypnotic polyrhythms that feel mechanically inhuman. Where Kollias's technical identity is about kinetic velocity, Haake's is about rhythmic mathematics.

Both players are obsessive about precision, but they pursue it in fundamentally different ways. Haake locks into complex polyrhythmic patterns and holds them with robotic consistency across long compositions. Kollias channels technique into extreme speed, maintaining blast beat tempos that most drummers cannot sustain at all.

## Key Differences

- **Technical focus:** Kollias pursues extreme kinetic speed; Haake pursues polyrhythmic mathematical complexity.
- **Pedal setup:** Kollias uses a co-designed Pearl Demon XR double pedal for maximum speed; Haake uses two single pedals — a unique physical constraint that defines his rhythmic vocabulary.
- **Drums:** Kollias plays Pearl Masterworks for projection and durability; Haake uses Sonor SQ2 for warmth and resonance.
- **Cymbals:** Kollias uses Zildjian K and A Custom for dark complexity with cutting attack; Haake uses Sabian HHX for focused, compressed attack.
- **Sub-genre:** Kollias defines brutal/technical death metal; Haake defines djent and polyrhythmic progressive metal.

## Influence & Legacy

George Kollias raised the technical ceiling for death metal speed drumming through his work with Nile. His instructional videos and online presence spread advanced techniques like gravity blasts to a global audience of developing drummers, making him one of the most educationally influential extreme metal drummers of his generation.

Tomas Haake pioneered the djent movement and influenced an entire generation of modern progressive and extreme metal drummers. His work on Meshuggah albums like *Catch Thirtythree* (2005) and *ObZen* (2008) redefined what rhythmic complexity could mean in metal — inspiring Periphery, Animals as Leaders, and countless djent-influenced bands that followed.

## Verdict

George Kollias and Tomas Haake represent two different extremes of technical mastery in modern extreme metal. Kollias redefines what is physically achievable at high speed; Haake redefined what rhythmic complexity can mean. Both are essential figures in extreme metal drumming history, and any debate between them ultimately comes down to whether you value raw speed or mathematical polyrhythm.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is the better drummer, George Kollias or Tomas Haake?**
A: Both are elite-level drummers who dominate different technical dimensions. George Kollias is unmatched in death metal blast beat speed and extreme tempo endurance. Tomas Haake is unparalleled in polyrhythmic complexity and djent-style precision. The "better" drummer depends entirely on whether you value kinetic speed or mathematical rhythm.

**Q: What gear does George Kollias use vs Tomas Haake?**
A: George Kollias plays Pearl Masterworks Stadium Exotic drums with Zildjian K and A Custom cymbals and the Pearl Demon XR double pedal he co-designed. Tomas Haake plays Sonor SQ2 Heavy Beech drums with Sabian HHX & AAX cymbals and two Tama Speed Cobra single pedals.

**Q: How do George Kollias and Tomas Haake's pedal setups differ?**
A: Kollias uses a conventional double pedal — the Pearl Demon XR he co-designed for maximum speed performance. Haake uses two separate single pedals, an unconventional setup that forces precise independent control and shapes his polyrhythmic kick patterning in ways a double pedal would not.

**Q: What albums define George Kollias's and Tomas Haake's drumming legacies?**
A: Kollias's defining work includes Nile's *Annihilation of the Wicked* (2005) and *Those Whom the Gods Detest* (2009). Haake's defining recordings include Meshuggah's *Destroy Erase Improve* (1995), *Catch Thirtythree* (2005), and *ObZen* (2008).

**Q: What BPM does George Kollias blast beat at?**
A: George Kollias is known for sustaining blast beats at approximately 280 BPM — one of the highest documented sustained blast beat tempos in death metal drumming. His heel-toe technique and co-designed Pearl Demon XR double pedal are key tools enabling this extreme speed.
