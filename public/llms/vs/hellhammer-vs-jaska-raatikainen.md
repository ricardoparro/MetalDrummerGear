## Playing Style

Hellhammer (born Jan Axel Blomberg, 1969) has driven Norwegian black metal since joining Mayhem in 1988, replacing founding drummer Manheim, and his cold, blast-beat-driven performance on "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" (1994) remains the genre's defining recording; he has run a parallel career with Dimmu Borgir since 1999. Jaska Raatikainen co-founded Finnish melodic death metal band Children of Bodom in 1993 alongside the late Alexi Laiho and remained its sole drummer for the band's entire 26-year run, anchoring the neoclassical melodic death metal sound across albums including "Hatebreeder" (1999) and "Follow the Reaper" (2000) through the band's 2019 farewell "Hexed."

## Technique

Hellhammer's technique is built around cold, relentless blast beats delivered with icy one-foot precision that became the template for second-wave black metal drumming — speed in service of atmosphere and aggression rather than technical display. Raatikainen balances death metal velocity with melodic awareness — precise, fluid double bass volleys and blast beats that lock tightly beneath Children of Bodom's neoclassical riffing, with fills composed to support rather than overwhelm Laiho's guitar and Janne Wirman's keyboard interplay.

## Key Differences

Hellhammer's classic "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" rig was a developing-endorsement Pearl 5-piece shell pack (22"x16" kick, 10"x9" and 12"x10" racks, 16"x16" floor tom) with a Pearl 14x5.5" steel snare tuned bright for maximum crack, paired with Zildjian A Series cymbals; his modern rig has expanded to Pearl Reference and Pearl Masters Premium series kits for his Dimmu Borgir symphonic work. Jaska Raatikainen plays a Pearl Masters Premium Maple kit with a Pearl Masters 14x5.5" Maple snare and Zildjian A Custom & K Custom Series cymbals (14" A Custom Hi-Hats, 17" & 18" A Custom Crashes, 20" K Custom Ride, 18" China), powered by a Pearl Eliminator double pedal and Vic Firth American Classic 5A sticks. ## Influence & Legacy

Hellhammer defined the sound and speed vocabulary of second-wave black metal, and his three-decade-plus dual career with Mayhem and Dimmu Borgir has made him the genre's most in-demand extreme drummer for both raw and symphonic contexts. Raatikainen helped build Finland's melodic death metal scene into a global commercial force — Children of Bodom sold over two million albums worldwide, and his playing on "Hatebreeder" and "Follow the Reaper" influenced a generation of melodic death and metalcore drummers.

## Verdict

Hellhammer and Jaska Raatikainen are two of Nordic extreme metal's most influential drummers, yet they built their reputations on opposite philosophies. Hellhammer's cold, chaotic blast beats defined black metal's atmosphere-first template on "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas," a record that still anchors the genre three decades later. Raatikainen spent 26 years as Children of Bodom's sole drummer, marrying death metal velocity to neoclassical melody and helping turn Finnish melodic death metal into a global export. Both are foundational Nordic voices — Hellhammer built black metal's blast-beat blueprint, Raatikainen built melodic death metal's.
