## Playing Style

Hellhammer (born Jan Axel Blomberg, 1969) has anchored Norwegian black metal since joining Mayhem in 1988, delivering the cold, blast-beat-driven performance on "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" (1994) that remains second-wave black metal's defining recording, while also driving Dimmu Borgir's symphonic black metal since 1999. Jaska Raatikainen co-founded Finnish melodic death metal band Children of Bodom in 1993 alongside the late Alexi Laiho and remained its sole drummer for the band's entire 26-year run, anchoring the neoclassical melodic death metal sound across "Hatebreeder" (1999) and "Follow the Reaper" (2000) through the band's 2019 farewell "Hexed."

## Technique

Hellhammer's technique favors cold, relentless one-foot blast beats built for atmosphere and aggression over technical display — a chaotic, weather-system quality that defined black metal's drumming vocabulary. Raatikainen balances death metal velocity with melodic awareness — rapid double bass volleys and blast beats locked tightly to Children of Bodom's neoclassical riffing, with fills composed to support Laiho's guitar and Janne Wirman's keyboard interplay rather than overwhelm it. Both are Nordic extreme-metal icons, but where Hellhammer's speed serves cold aggression, Raatikainen's serves melodic precision.

## Key Differences

Hellhammer's classic "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" rig was a developing-endorsement Pearl 5-piece shell pack with a Pearl 14"x5.5" steel snare tuned bright for crack and Zildjian A Series cymbals, since expanded into Pearl Reference and Masters Premium series kits for his Dimmu Borgir work, driven by a Pearl Eliminator double pedal and Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. ## Influence & Legacy

Hellhammer defined the blast-beat vocabulary that second-wave black metal built its identity around, and his dual Mayhem/Dimmu Borgir career has made him one of extreme metal's most in-demand drummers for over three decades. Raatikainen helped build Finland's melodic death metal scene into a global commercial force — Children of Bodom sold over two million albums worldwide, and his playing on "Hatebreeder" and "Follow the Reaper" influenced a generation of melodic death and metalcore drummers.

## Verdict

Hellhammer and Jaska Raatikainen are two of Nordic extreme metal's most enduring icons, built on opposite philosophies of speed. Hellhammer's blast beats carry black metal's cold, chaotic atmosphere across a 35-plus-year, two-band career with Mayhem and Dimmu Borgir. Raatikainen spent 26 years turning Children of Bodom's neoclassical melodic death metal into one of Finland's biggest metal exports, with speed always in service of melody. Both share a Pearl Eliminator double pedal foundation, but the music it drives could hardly sound more different.
