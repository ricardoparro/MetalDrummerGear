## Playing Style

Hellhammer (born Jan Axel Blomberg, 1969) has driven Norwegian black metal since joining Mayhem in 1988, and his cold, blast-beat-driven performance on "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" (1994) remains second-wave black metal's defining recording; he has run a parallel career with Dimmu Borgir since 1999. Mikkey Dee joined Motörhead in 1992, following stints with King Diamond (1985-1989) and Don Dokken, and spent 23 years as Lemmy Kilmister's drummer — the longest tenure of any Motörhead drummer — anchoring the band's relentless rock 'n' roll assault across albums including "Bastards" (1993), "Overnight Sensation" (1996), and the band's final studio album "Bad Magic" (2015); he has drummed for Scorpions since 2016.

## Technique

Hellhammer's technique is built around cold, relentless blast beats delivered with icy one-foot precision that became the template for second-wave black metal drumming — speed in service of atmosphere and aggression rather than technical display. Dee's technique is built for stamina and raw power — hard-hitting snare work, thunderous double bass, and a no-frills aggressive attack that matched Lemmy's relentless, no-nonsense rock 'n' roll energy night after night across decades of nonstop touring. Where Hellhammer's blasts are a cold, chaotic weather system, Dee's power is a steady, driving engine built for endurance across marathon Motörhead tours.

## Key Differences

Hellhammer's classic "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" rig was a developing-endorsement Pearl 5-piece shell pack with a Pearl 14x5.5" steel snare tuned bright for maximum crack, paired with Zildjian A Series cymbals; his modern rig has expanded to Pearl Reference and Pearl Masters Premium series kits for his Dimmu Borgir symphonic work. Mikkey Dee plays a Yamaha Recording Custom kit with a Yamaha Mikkey Dee Signature 14x8" snare and Zildjian A Custom & K Series cymbals (14" A Custom Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" A Custom Crashes, 22" K Custom Ride, 20" Oriental China), driven by a Yamaha FP9 double pedal and Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature sticks. ## Influence & Legacy

Hellhammer defined the sound and speed vocabulary of second-wave black metal, and his three-decade-plus dual career with Mayhem and Dimmu Borgir has made him the genre's most in-demand extreme drummer for both raw and symphonic contexts. Dee is widely regarded as one of the most respected drummers in hard rock and metal, having anchored Motörhead's most celebrated live era for 23 years before stepping into Scorpions' drum chair in 2016 — one of the rare drummers to hold down two legendary bands' drum chairs across a single career.

## Verdict

Hellhammer and Mikkey Dee represent two different Scandinavian-rooted metal lineages built for two different kinds of extremity. Hellhammer's icy blast beats defined black metal's cold, atmosphere-driven vocabulary across three decades with Mayhem and Dimmu Borgir. Dee's thunderous, no-frills power drove Motörhead's relentless rock 'n' roll assault for 23 years under Lemmy Kilmister before he carried that same stamina into Scorpions. One built extremity through icy precision, the other through raw endurance — together they bridge Norwegian black metal and UK-rooted heavy rock across a shared Scandinavian pedigree.
