## Playing Style

Hellhammer (born Jan Axel Blomberg, 1969) has anchored Norwegian black metal since joining Mayhem in 1988, delivering the cold, blast-beat-driven performance on "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" (1994) that remains second-wave black metal's defining recording, while also driving Dimmu Borgir's symphonic black metal since 1999. Mikkey Dee joined Motörhead in 1992, following stints with King Diamond (1985-1989) and Don Dokken, and spent 23 years as Lemmy Kilmister's drummer — the longest tenure of any Motörhead drummer — anchoring the band's relentless rock 'n' roll assault across "Bastards" (1993), "Overnight Sensation" (1996), and the band's final album "Bad Magic" (2015) before Lemmy's death; he has drummed for Scorpions since 2016.

## Technique

Hellhammer's technique favors cold, relentless one-foot blast beats built for atmosphere and aggression rather than technical display — a chaotic, weather-system quality that defined black metal's drumming vocabulary. Dee's technique is built on raw stamina and hard-hitting, no-frills power rather than extreme speed for its own sake, driving Motörhead's rock 'n' roll engine night after night across decades of relentless touring. Where Hellhammer's speed reads as icy and calculated, Dee's power reads as physical and direct — two very different answers to what extreme drumming means across a generational and stylistic gap.

## Key Differences

Hellhammer's classic "De Mysteriis Dom Sathanas" rig was a developing-endorsement Pearl 5-piece shell pack with a Pearl 14"x5.5" steel snare tuned bright for crack and Zildjian A Series cymbals, since expanded into Pearl Reference and Masters Premium series kits for his Dimmu Borgir work, driven by a Pearl Eliminator double pedal and Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. Mikkey Dee plays a Yamaha Recording Custom kit with a Yamaha Mikkey Dee Signature 14"x8" snare and Zildjian A Custom & K Series cymbals (14" A Custom Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" A Custom Crashes, 22" K Custom Ride, 20" Oriental China), driven by twin Yamaha FP9 pedals and Vic Firth Mikkey Dee Signature sticks. ## Influence & Legacy

Hellhammer defined the blast-beat vocabulary that second-wave black metal built its identity around, and his dual Mayhem/Dimmu Borgir career has made him one of extreme metal's most in-demand drummers for over three decades. Dee's 23-year run as Lemmy Kilmister's drummer made him one of the most respected engines in heavy metal history, and his move to Scorpions in 2016 proved his stamina-driven power translates across bands and eras just as readily as Hellhammer's blast beats translated across Mayhem and Dimmu Borgir.

## Verdict

Hellhammer and Mikkey Dee are cross-generational icons from two very different corners of Scandinavian and UK-rooted metal. Hellhammer built black metal's blast-beat blueprint out of Norway's early-1990s scene, chasing cold, atmospheric extremity. Dee built his legend on raw stamina and power, anchoring Motörhead for 23 years before carrying that same drive into Scorpions. Neither approach replaces the other — one is extreme metal's coldest technical extreme, the other is heavy metal's most durable engine.
