## Drummer Profiles

### Inferno

Inferno (born Zbigniew Robert Promiński, January 5, 1978, in Gdańsk, Poland) is a Polish extreme metal drummer and a founding member of Behemoth. He has been the rhythmic backbone of one of black and death metal's most important bands through 20+ years and 12 studio albums, including *Demigod* (2004), *The Apostasy* (2007), *Evangelion* (2009), and *I Loved You at Your Darkest* (2018). Inferno is revered for his ability to sustain extreme blast beat tempos across full live sets night after night — his playing is defined by controlled brutality and exceptional endurance. He has performed with Behemoth on some of metal's largest stages worldwide, including Download Festival, Wacken Open Air, and multiple world tours.

### Flo Mounier

Flo Mounier (born Florent Mounier, March 9, 1976, in Saint-Mandé, France) is a French-Canadian drummer and a founding member of Cryptopsy. His performance on *None So Vile* (1996) is universally regarded as one of the greatest and most demanding extreme metal drum recordings ever captured — blast beats at world-record tempos combined with jazz-influenced rhythmic diversity that set the standard for technical death metal drumming. Mounier's gravity blast technique enabled the extreme velocities that made *None So Vile* a landmark recording, and his crossover interest in jazz drumming gives his playing a musical fluidity that distinguishes him from pure speed specialists. He is a multiple Modern Drummer award nominee and one of the most studied extreme metal drummers in the world.

## Playing Style

Inferno delivers controlled, enduring brutality — his black/death metal playing is defined by relentless blast beats, precise double bass, and machine-like consistency that holds up across Behemoth's marathon live sets. His genius is not peak velocity but sustained ferocity: playing at extreme tempos night after night, city after city, at the level Behemoth's increasingly large audiences demand.

Flo Mounier defined the technical death metal blast beat with Cryptopsy — his playing on *None So Vile* set a benchmark for sheer velocity and complexity that still stands nearly 30 years later. Mounier combines gravity blast technique for maximum tempo with jazz-influenced rhythmic diversity and groove sensibility, giving his playing a swing and musical intelligence that separates him from drummers who pursue speed alone.

---

## Technique

Inferno uses a technique optimized for endurance rather than peak tempo — his single-stroke blast beats maintain ferocious speeds across full sets through efficient mechanics rather than maximum force. His double bass work provides the rhythmic foundation for Behemoth's dense, layered compositions and his ability to lock in with guitarist/vocalist Nergal's riff cycles at extreme tempos defines the band's sound.

Flo Mounier employs a gravity blast (drop stroke) technique that enabled the record-setting blast tempos on *None So Vile*. His jazz background gives him an unusual rhythmic vocabulary for an extreme metal drummer — polyrhythmic awareness and dynamic range that appear between and within the blast sections, making his playing unpredictable and musically interesting even at maximum intensity.

## Key Differences

- **Approach:** Inferno prioritizes endurance and live consistency; Flo Mounier prioritizes peak velocity and technical complexity.
- **Technique:** Inferno uses single-stroke blast beats for sustained extreme tempos; Flo uses gravity blasts for maximum BPM.
- **Jazz influence:** Flo Mounier has significant jazz crossover in his vocabulary; Inferno's style is more purely extreme metal.
- **Cymbals:** Inferno uses Paiste Signature/2002 for warm, defined attack; Flo uses Sabian AAX/HHX for bright, aggressive cut.
- **Band context:** Inferno powers one of metal's biggest live touring acts (Behemoth); Flo defined technical death metal's studio benchmark (Cryptopsy's *None So Vile*).

## Influence & Legacy

Inferno has been Behemoth's rhythmic backbone across 20+ years and their rise from the Polish underground to one of extreme metal's most significant international acts. His playing on *Demigod*, *Evangelion*, and *I Loved You at Your Darkest* documents one of extreme metal's most important long-term drummer-band partnerships.

Flo Mounier's *None So Vile* performance is regularly cited as one of the greatest extreme metal drum recordings ever made. His gravity blast technique and the velocities it enabled on tracks like "Orgiastic Disembowelment" and "Phobophile" influenced an entire generation of death metal drummers worldwide. His educational presence — clinics, interviews, and instructional content — has spread his technical vocabulary globally.

## Verdict

Inferno and Flo Mounier are the blast-beat kings of their respective corners of extreme metal. Flo Mounier holds the edge in peak velocity and technical death metal complexity — *None So Vile* remains a benchmark nearly three decades later. Inferno holds the edge in controlled endurance and live consistency — Behemoth's punishing touring schedule demands it, and Inferno delivers at extreme intensities every night. The debate is peak performance vs sustained power: Flo's studio ceiling vs Inferno's live floor.

## FAQ

**Q: Who is faster: Inferno or Flo Mounier?**
A: Both push the limits of human drumming. Flo Mounier set records with the blast tempos on *None So Vile* using gravity blast technique. Inferno's genius is sustaining extreme speeds across full live sets night after night — his endurance at high tempos is legendary among extreme metal drummers.

**Q: What kits do Inferno and Flo Mounier use?**
A: Inferno plays a Pearl Reference Pure kit with Paiste Signature and 2002 cymbals. Flo Mounier uses a Tama Starclassic kit with Sabian AAX and HHX cymbals. Both use Pearl Eliminator double pedals.

**Q: Which drummer has more technical diversity?**
A: Flo Mounier has more jazz and groove crossover in his vocabulary — his background in jazz drumming gives his death metal playing rhythmic diversity beyond pure blast technique. Inferno excels in controlled brutality and live consistency across extreme tempos.

**Q: What is Flo Mounier's most famous recording?**

A: *Demigod* (2004) and *Evangelion* (2009) are widely considered Behemoth's strongest albums, with Inferno's drumming central to their ferocity. *I Loved You at Your Darkest* (2018) demonstrates his continued evolution as Behemoth expanded their sound.
