## Playing Style

Jaska Raatikainen co-founded Finnish melodic death metal band Children of Bodom in 1993 and remained its sole drummer for the band's entire 26-year run, anchoring the neoclassical melodic death metal sound across albums from "Hatebreeder" (1999) through the band's 2019 farewell "Hexed." Hannes Grossmann built his reputation in German technical death metal, drumming for Necrophagist from 2004 to 2010, joining Obscura in 2007, and co-founding polyrhythm-heavy Alkaloid in 2014, bringing classical composition training into extreme metal's most mathematically demanding subgenre.

## Technique

Raatikainen's blast beats and double bass volleys are built to serve melody, tracking the harmonic contours of Alexi Laiho's guitar and Janne Wirman's keyboard lines rather than showcasing raw velocity for its own sake. Grossmann plays with an open-handed setup and constructs polyrhythmic passages where his hands and feet occupy different meters simultaneously, delivering clean, articulate blast beats at extreme tempos that prioritize separation and clarity even inside Alkaloid's most convoluted arrangements.

## Influence & Legacy

Raatikainen helped build Finland's melodic death metal scene into a global commercial force, with Children of Bodom selling over two million albums worldwide and influencing a generation of melodic death and metalcore drummers. Grossmann has become one of technical death metal's most respected craftsmen, his work across Necrophagist, Obscura, and Alkaloid pushing the genre's polyrhythmic and compositional ceiling for drummers who prize precision over pure speed.

## Verdict

Jaska Raatikainen and Hannes Grossmann represent two disciplined extremes of death metal drumming. Raatikainen spent 26 years locking Children of Bodom's blast beats to Alexi Laiho's neoclassical guitar lines, proving melodic death metal could be both aggressive and tuneful. Grossmann built his career on technical death metal's outer edge with Necrophagist, Obscura, and Alkaloid, treating polyrhythm as compositional material rather than mere display. Raatikainen is melody's blast-beat guardian; Grossmann is technical death metal's polyrhythmic architect — both raised the bar for precision in extreme metal.
