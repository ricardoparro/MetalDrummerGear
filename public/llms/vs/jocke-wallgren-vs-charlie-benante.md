## Style & Technique Comparison

Charlie Benante joined Anthrax in 1983 and has appeared on all 11 of the band's studio albums, becoming one of thrash metal's founding architects and a pioneer of the blast beat technique on landmark records like "Among the Living" (1987) and "Spreading the Disease" (1985). His technique is built on fast, syncopated skank beats fused with hardcore punk energy, aggressive double bass, and a rhythmic vocabulary that helped define East Coast thrash and crossover thrash alongside bands like S.O.D.

Jocke Wallgren joined Amon Amarth in 2016, replacing longtime drummer Fredrik Andersson, and has anchored the band's anthemic Viking metal sound on "Jomsviking" (2016), "Berserker" (2019), and "The Great Heathen Army" (2022). His technique centers on locking a driving double bass pulse to Amon Amarth's riff-driven arrangements, delivering powerful snare backbeats and dynamic mid-section restraint that serve melodic death metal's anthemic, groove-first songwriting rather than thrash metal's stop-start aggression.

---

## Key Differences

The core divergence is genre vocabulary: Benante's playing is built around syncopated, stop-start thrash riffing and hardcore-influenced skank beats, while Wallgren's playing serves melodic death metal's more continuous, anthemic riff structures with sustained double bass grooves.

Career stage also separates them sharply. Benante is a four-decade veteran who helped invent thrash metal's drumming vocabulary in the 1980s. Wallgren is a modern-era specialist who joined an already-established band in 2016 and has focused on sustaining and evolving its sound across three studio albums.

Chart performance tells its own story: "Jomsviking," Wallgren's debut with Amon Amarth, cracked the U.S. Top 20, debuting at No. 19 on the Billboard 200 — a reminder that melodic death metal has found real commercial traction in the streaming era.

---

## Influence & Legacy

Charlie Benante helped define East Coast thrash and crossover thrash/hardcore fusion, and his blast beat pioneering influenced extreme metal drummers well beyond Anthrax — he even filled in for the late Vinnie Paul in the reunited Pantera in 2022.

Jocke Wallgren represents melodic death metal's modern generation, keeping Amon Amarth's Viking-themed sound commercially vital more than two decades into the band's career and proving the genre can still chart in the U.S. Top 20.

## Verdict

Jocke Wallgren and Charlie Benante embody two distinct heavy metal drumming traditions rather than a direct rivalry. Benante is a founding thrash metal architect whose blast beat pioneering and East Coast crossover energy shaped an entire genre across four decades with Anthrax. Wallgren is the modern melodic death metal groove specialist keeping Amon Amarth's Viking metal sound charting in the 2020s. Comparing them is comparing thrash metal's syncopated power to melodic death metal's anthemic drive — different traditions, both essential to heavy metal's drumming vocabulary.
