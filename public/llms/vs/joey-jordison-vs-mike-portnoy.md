# Joey Jordison vs Mike Portnoy — Drum Kit Comparison

## Joey Jordison Setup

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series
- **Cymbals:** Paiste RUDE & 2002 Series (14" Wild Hi-Hats, 16", 17", 18", 19" Power Crashes, 20" & 22" Wild Chinas, 22" Power Ride)
- **Snare:** Pearl Joey Jordison Signature 13x6.5"
- **Pedals:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Promark Joey Jordison Signature TX515W

## Mike Portnoy Setup

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple/Birch
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX Series (14" Evolution Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Evolution Crashes, 21" Raw Bell Dry Ride, 10" & 12" Evolution Splashes, 19" O-Zone Crash)
- **Snare:** Tama Mike Portnoy Signature Melody Master 14x5.5"
- **Pedals:** Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide Double Pedal
- **Sticks:** Promark Mike Portnoy Signature TX420N

## Key Differences

Joey played Pearl drums with Paiste cymbals. Mike uses Tama drums with Sabian cymbals for his iconic sound. Joey brought extreme metal intensity to mainstream nu-metal with raw aggression. Mike delivers theatrical prog metal with complex arrangements. Joey excels at blast beats, extreme speed, and theatrical showmanship. Mike masters rapid-fire transitions, theatrical fills, and complex time signatures.
