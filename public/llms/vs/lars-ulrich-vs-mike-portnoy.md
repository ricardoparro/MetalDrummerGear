## Gear Deep Dive

### Lars Ulrich Setup

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple
- **Cymbals:** Zildjian A Custom Series (14" Dyno Beat Hi-Hats, 16", 17" & 18" Rock Crashes, 20" Z Custom China, 22" Ride)
- **Snare:** Tama LU1465 Lars Ulrich Signature 14x6.5"
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Tama Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide Double Pedal, Tama 1st Chair Throne
- **Sticks:** Ahead Lars Ulrich Signature Drumsticks

Lars Ulrich's Tama Starclassic Maple kit delivers the punchy, cutting attack that drives Metallica's riffs, paired with his signature Tama LU1465 snare — a 14x6.5" powerhouse known for its sharp crack and exceptional projection in arena and stadium settings. His Zildjian A Custom Series cymbals provide bright, crisp articulation across hi-hats, crashes, china, and ride. Tama Iron Cobra 900 Power Glide double bass pedals deliver the fast, consistent double kick patterns that define Metallica's classic thrash rhythms, while Ahead aluminum sticks hold up across marathon world tours.

### Mike Portnoy Setup

- **Drums:** Tama Starclassic Maple/Birch
- **Cymbals:** Sabian HHX Series (14" Evolution Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Evolution Crashes, 21" Raw Bell Dry Ride, 10" & 12" Evolution Splashes, 19" O-Zone Crash)
- **Snare:** Tama Mike Portnoy Signature Melody Master 14x5.5"
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide Double Pedal, Tama 1st Chair Ergo-Rider Throne, Roland electronic pads
- **Sticks:** Promark Mike Portnoy Signature TX420N

Mike Portnoy's Tama Starclassic Maple/Birch hybrid kit delivers the blend of warmth and projection needed to cover Dream Theater's intricate arrangements and the diverse catalog he brings to The Winery Dogs, Sons of Apollo, and Liquid Tension Experiment. His signature Tama Melody Master snare is tuned for articulate presence across complex time signatures, while his enormous Sabian HHX cymbal array — including splashes and an O-Zone crash — gives him an expansive tonal palette. Tama Iron Cobra Power Glide double bass pedals handle his demanding double kick work, and Roland electronic pads extend the kit into electronic territory.

## Style & Technique Comparison

Lars Ulrich co-founded Metallica with James Hetfield in Los Angeles in 1981 and built thrash metal's mainstream foundation across "Kill 'Em All" (1983), "Master of Puppets" (1986), and "...And Justice for All" (1988). His drumming is feel-driven and groove-oriented, prioritizing powerful, punchy patterns and mid-tempo stomp that lock directly into Metallica's riffs rather than showcasing technical complexity for its own sake — his playing serves the song above all else.

Mike Portnoy co-founded Dream Theater in 1985 and spent 25 years as the band's drummer and lyricist, building progressive metal's technical vocabulary across "Images and Words" (1992) and "Metropolis Pt. 2: Scenes from a Memory" (1999). A 30-time Modern Drummer "Drummer of the Year" winner, Portnoy is a virtuoso in odd time signatures, polyrhythms, and rapid-fire fills, with theatrical, note-dense playing that is often the compositional centerpiece of Dream Theater's epic-length arrangements.

Both are titans of metal drumming, but they occupy opposite philosophies: Ulrich proves that groove and feel can define a genre and sell tens of millions of records, while Portnoy proves that technical virtuosity itself can be the main event.

---

## Key Differences

Both drummers build their kits on a Tama drum foundation, but their cymbal choices diverge sharply — Ulrich's Zildjian A Custom Series delivers bright, cutting attack suited to thrash's aggressive rhythm-guitar interplay, while Portnoy's Sabian HHX Series offers a much broader tonal palette with splashes and an O-Zone crash built for progressive metal's textural variety.

Ulrich's LU1465 snare is a punchy 14x6.5" model built for cutting through a wall of rhythm guitars, while Portnoy's Melody Master snare is a shallower 14x5.5" tuned for articulate presence across rapidly shifting time signatures.

Technique-wise, Ulrich favors iconic, repeatable patterns that anchor a song's identity, while Portnoy layers rapid technical transitions and odd-meter fills that make the drumming itself a focal point of the composition.

## Influence & Legacy

Lars Ulrich's influence is inseparable from Metallica's rise to become the best-selling metal band in history — his groove sensibility shaped how millions of fans and drummers understand metal drumming at its most commercially accessible, and he became the first Danish musician inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.

Mike Portnoy shaped an entire generation of progressive metal drummers through Dream Theater's technical ambition and his prolific instructional output, becoming one of the most decorated and widely imitated drummers in the genre's history, with his influence extending through Liquid Tension Experiment, The Winery Dogs, and Sons of Apollo.

## Verdict

Lars Ulrich and Mike Portnoy represent opposite poles of metal drumming philosophy. Ulrich is the feel-driven engine behind thrash metal's biggest commercial success story, prioritizing groove and song service over technical display across more than four decades with one band. Portnoy is progressive metal's definitive technical virtuoso, whose odd-time signatures and theatrical fills turned drumming itself into a compositional focal point. The debate pits accessible, riff-driven thrash power against relentless progressive-metal technicality.
