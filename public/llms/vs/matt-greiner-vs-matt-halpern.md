## Gear Deep Dive

### Matt Greiner Setup

- **Drums:** Meinl Drum Festival Kit / Greiner & Kilmer Custom
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series (15" Dual Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" Dual Crashes, 21" Transition Ride, 18" Extra Dry China, 10" & 12" Splashes)
- **Snare:** Greiner & Kilmer Custom 14x6.5" Maple
- **Pedals/Hardware:** DW 9000 Series Double Pedal, DW 9100 Throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth American Classic 5A
- **Heads:** Evans

Matt Greiner co-founded drum company Greiner & Kilmer with fellow drummer Kaleb Kilmer in 2012, giving him direct input into the design of his own snare. His Meinl Byzance cymbals deliver the warm, complex overtones that suit August Burns Red's melodically sophisticated metalcore — not the aggressive brightness of traditional metal cymbal choices. The DW 9000 double pedal is renowned for smooth, powerful response essential to Greiner's explosive double bass work.

### Matt Halpern Setup

- **Drums:** Pearl Reference Series
- **Cymbals:** Meinl Byzance Series (15" Dark Hi-Hats, 18" & 20" Extra Dry Medium Crashes, 22" Transition Ride, 18" Extra Dry China)
- **Snare:** Pearl Reference 14x6.5" Brass
- **Pedals/Hardware:** Pearl Demon Drive Double Pedal, Pearl D-2000 Throne
- **Sticks:** Promark Matt Halpern Signature

Matt Halpern's Pearl Reference Series kit is one of the most respected production drum kits at the high end of the market — premium North American maple construction with excellent tonal consistency. His Meinl Byzance cymbals provide dark, harmonically complex tones that complement Periphery's layered progressive sound. The Pearl Demon Drive pedal is widely praised for its smooth cam action and fast response — critical for the technical patterns at the heart of djent drumming.

## Style & Technique Comparison

Matt Greiner drives August Burns Red's metalcore with blazing double bass, explosive fills, and dynamic control that bridges technical precision with melodic songwriting. A founding member of the band since 2003, Greiner has developed a highly personal style that treats drums as a compositional element, not just rhythmic support. His work on albums like "Messengers," "Constellations," and "Phantom Anthem" earned Grammy nominations and established him as the defining voice of technical metalcore drumming.

Matt Halpern joined Periphery and became the architect of the band's djent drumming identity. A Berklee College of Music graduate, Halpern brings academic rigor to his playing while maintaining an organic, groove-focused feel that makes Periphery's polyrhythmic complexity feel musical rather than mechanical. His combination of technical excellence and deep pocket has made him a hero to a generation of progressive metal drummers worldwide.

Notably, both players share the Meinl Byzance cymbal line — a point of common ground despite their different musical contexts and genres.

---

## Key Differences

Both Greiner and Halpern are technical, modern players who use Meinl Byzance cymbals, but their drum kit and pedal choices diverge significantly. Greiner uses custom Greiner & Kilmer drums and DW 9000 double pedals for metalcore power and aggression. Halpern uses Pearl Reference Series and Pearl Demon Drive pedals for the groove-focused precision that defines djent.

Greiner's playing leans toward high-energy metalcore intensity — explosive double bass, aggressive fills, and dynamic contrast. Halpern's playing prioritizes musical communication within complex time signatures — deep groove, ghost notes, and feel over flash.

## Influence & Legacy

Matt Greiner co-founded August Burns Red at 18 and helped build one of metalcore's most enduring bands. His Grammy-nominated drum work across 10+ albums established the technical metalcore drumming template for the 2000s and 2010s. His 2012 co-founding of drum company Greiner & Kilmer reflects his investment in the broader drumming community beyond performing.

Matt Halpern shaped the global djent movement through multiple Periphery albums and built one of metal's most engaged drum education communities through clinics, online lessons, and platforms like Drumeo. His influence on modern progressive metal drumming is substantial — a generation of players learned to navigate polyrhythmic complexity through Halpern's teaching and playing.

## Verdict

Matt Greiner is the architect of modern metalcore drumming — technical, powerful, and dynamic in equal measure. Matt Halpern is the groove-grounded djent pioneer whose Berklee-trained musicality made polyrhythmic complexity feel approachable. Both Matts represent the modern era of metal drumming excellence, each dominating their genre through technical mastery and musical intelligence. Their shared Meinl Byzance cymbal choice hints at a common sensibility — both value harmonic depth and complex overtones over raw aggression — even as their genres pull them in different directions.
