## Playing Style

Nick Augusto joined Trivium as a drum technician before stepping in as the band's touring and studio drummer, ultimately recording two studio albums, "In Waves" (2011) and "Vengeance Falls" (2013), across a tenure from 2009 to 2014. His background in grindcore band Maruta and Metal Militia (alongside future Trivium bassist Paolo Gregoletto) gave his Trivium tenure an aggressive, high-speed foundation. Blake Richardson has anchored Between the Buried and Me since early 2005, replacing founding drummer Jason Roe and becoming central to the band's evolution into one of progressive metal's most acclaimed acts across landmark records like "Colors" (2007), "The Great Misdirect" (2009), and "Colors II" (2021), the latter earning a Grammy nomination.

## Technique

Augusto's playing favors powerful, speed-driven metalcore fundamentals — tight, aggressive double bass work and straightforward technicality built to serve Trivium's thrash-influenced songwriting rather than to showcase complexity for its own sake. Richardson fuses death metal precision with jazz influences, drawing on heroes like Terry Bozzio, Dennis Chambers, and Tomas Haake to build complex blast beats, constantly shifting odd time signatures, and fill-heavy arrangements that treat the drum kit as a compositional voice within BTBAM's genre-blending progressive death metal.

## Influence & Legacy

Augusto's five-year run helped carry Trivium through a pivotal stretch as the band pushed toward a more thrash-oriented, aggressive sound, and he later formed Corrosion and continued touring with acts including Light the Torch. Richardson's two-decade tenure with Between the Buried and Me has made him one of the most respected and technically proficient drummers in modern progressive metal, with Grammy-nominated work that continues to influence the current generation of technical death metal and djent-adjacent drummers.

## Verdict

Nick Augusto and Blake Richardson represent two different technical demands within modern heavy music. Augusto brought fast, aggressive metalcore fundamentals to Trivium during a five-year run that helped sharpen the band's thrash edge. Richardson has spent two decades building Between the Buried and Me's genre-crossing identity through jazz-informed complexity and compositional ambition few drummers attempt. The debate is metalcore speed and power versus progressive death metal's technical and rhythmic depth.
