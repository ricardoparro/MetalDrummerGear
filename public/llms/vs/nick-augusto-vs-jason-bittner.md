## Playing Style

Nick Augusto joined Trivium as a drum technician before stepping in as the band's touring and studio drummer, ultimately recording two studio albums, "In Waves" (2011) and "Vengeance Falls" (2013), across a tenure from 2009 to 2014. His background in grindcore band Maruta and Metal Militia (alongside future Trivium bassist Paolo Gregoletto) gave his Trivium tenure an aggressive, high-speed foundation. Jason Bittner rose to prominence with Shadows Fall during the early-2000s metalcore explosion, powering the Grammy-nominated "The War Within" (2004) and helping the band headline Ozzfest in 2005, before a 2013-2017 stint in Flotsam and Jetsam and, in 2017, joining thrash metal veterans Overkill — where he recorded "The Wings of War" (2019) and "Scorched" (2023), the first Overkill album fully written with him behind the kit.

## Technique

Augusto's playing favors powerful, speed-driven metalcore fundamentals — tight, aggressive double bass work and straightforward technicality built to serve Trivium's thrash-influenced songwriting rather than to showcase complexity for its own sake. Bittner combines thrash metal power with the groove and heaviness of modern metal, drawing on Dave Lombardo, Nicko McBrain, Vinnie Paul, and Charlie Benante to build a style defined by powerful, driving double bass patterns, tight and punchy snare work, and the versatility to move between Shadows Fall's metalcore attack and Overkill's classic thrash gallop without losing his identity.

## Key Differences

Nick Augusto played a Pearl Reference Pure kit with a Pearl Reference 14x6.5" brass snare and Sabian AAX Series cymbals (14" Stage Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" X-Plosion Crashes, 21" Stage Ride, 18" AAXtreme China), powered by a Pearl Demon Drive double pedal and Vic Firth American Classic 5B sticks. ## Influence & Legacy

Augusto's five-year run helped carry Trivium through a pivotal stretch as the band pushed toward a more thrash-oriented, aggressive sound, and he later formed Corrosion and continued touring with acts including Light the Torch. Bittner helped define 2000s metalcore's commercial breakthrough with Shadows Fall's Grammy-nominated "The War Within," then made one of modern metal's more unusual career pivots — trading metalcore for classic thrash when he joined Overkill in 2017, ultimately earning a co-writing credit on 2023's "Scorched" two decades into his professional career.

## Verdict

Nick Augusto and Jason Bittner both built their reputations powering thrash-adjacent metalcore, but on very different timelines. Augusto's five-year Trivium run was a compact, high-intensity chapter that sharpened the band's thrash edge before he moved on. Bittner's career spans over two decades and two distinct chairs — Shadows Fall's Grammy-nominated metalcore breakthrough and, later, a full pivot into Overkill's classic thrash lineage. The debate is a focused metalcore tenure versus a career-long thrash-metalcore journeyman's staying power.
