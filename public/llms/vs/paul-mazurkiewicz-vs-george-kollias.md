Mazurkiewicz's setup is warm and powerful, tuned to sit in the low end of Cannibal Corpse's down-tuned, riff-driven brutal death metal — a kit built for impact and groove rather than maximum attack.

### George Kollias Setup

- **Drums:** Pearl Masterworks Stadium Exotic (Piano Black with Gold Hardware)
- **Snare:** Pearl George Kollias Signature 14×6.5"

- **Pedals/Hardware:** Pearl Demon XR Double Pedal (co-designed with Pearl), Pearl D-3000 Throne
- **Sticks:** Vic Firth George Kollias Signature

Kollias's setup is engineered specifically for sustained extreme speed — his co-designed Pearl Demon XR pedal provides the linear, direct action needed for blast beats above 240 BPM, and his signature snare cuts through Nile's dense guitar and bass walls.

## Playing Style

Paul Mazurkiewicz has been Cannibal Corpse's only drummer since the band formed in Buffalo, New York in 1988, appearing on all seventeen studio albums from "Eaten Back to Life" (1990) through "Chaos Horrific" (2023). His style is brutality-first: powerful double bass, authoritative blast beats, and mid-tempo bulldoze passages that serve the riff rather than display technique. George Kollias has drummed for Nile since 2004, bringing Greek technical death metal precision to the band's Egyptology-themed, polyrhythmically dense catalog on albums including "Annihilation of the Wicked" (2005) and "Those Whom the Gods Detest" (2009).

## Technique

Mazurkiewicz's technical signature is relentless consistency — his blast beats lock tightly with Alex Webster's bass, prioritizing groove and impact over raw speed. His decades-long stamina across seventeen albums and constant touring is itself a feat few extreme metal drummers match. Kollias represents technical death metal's speed ceiling, sustaining blast beats above 240 BPM with surgical clarity rather than blur, and has codified his methods into widely used instructional material — openly teaching techniques most extreme drummers treat as trade secrets.

## Key Differences

- **Philosophy:** Mazurkiewicz serves the riff and song structure; Kollias pursues sustained technical extremity.
- **Speed:** Kollias is verified among the fastest extreme metal drummers alive, sustaining 240+ BPM blast beats; Mazurkiewicz prioritizes weight and groove over top-end speed.
- **Cymbals:** Mazurkiewicz plays Sabian AAX; Kollias plays Zildjian A Custom — different brands, both built for extreme metal cutting power.
- **Pedals:** Both use Pearl Demon-series double pedals, but Kollias co-designed his Demon XR specifically for blast-beat speed.
- **Education:** Kollias is one of death metal's most prolific instructors with DVDs, courses, and worldwide clinics; Mazurkiewicz's influence comes through performance and longevity rather than teaching.

## Influence & Legacy

Paul Mazurkiewicz's unbroken 35-plus-year tenure with Cannibal Corpse — the best-selling death metal band of all time — makes his rhythmic template one of the genre's most widely emulated. His drumming on "Tomb of the Mutilated" (1992) and "The Bleeding" (1994) helped define brutal death metal's rhythmic vocabulary. George Kollias has become one of extreme metal's premier educators alongside his Nile career, and his 2020 solo album "Invictus" proved his musical range extends well beyond pure velocity.

## Verdict

Paul Mazurkiewicz and George Kollias represent death metal drumming's two defining poles. Mazurkiewicz is the genre's great consistent force — seventeen Cannibal Corpse albums and 35-plus years of brutal, song-serving drumming. Kollias is technical death metal's speed authority — a co-designed pedal, a signature snare, and blast beats sustained above 240 BPM with surgical precision. Mazurkiewicz wins on legacy and longevity; Kollias wins on verified technical extremity.
