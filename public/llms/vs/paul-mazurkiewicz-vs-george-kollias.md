## Drummer Profiles

### Paul Mazurkiewicz

Paul Mazurkiewicz (born September 8, 1968, in Buffalo, New York) is an American drummer and co-founder of Cannibal Corpse, formed in 1988. He has performed on every Cannibal Corpse album since their 1990 debut *Eaten Back to Life*, including landmark releases like *Tomb of the Mutilated* (1992) and *Kill* (2006). His drumming style combines relentless blast beats, complex double bass patterns, and groove-oriented sections that helped define brutal death metal's rhythmic template. With Cannibal Corpse being the best-selling death metal band of all time, Mazurkiewicz's 35-plus years of consistent recording and touring make him one of the most enduring figures in extreme metal history.

### George Kollias

George Kollias (born August 30, 1977, in Korinth, Greece) is a Greek drummer best known as Nile's drummer since 2004, when he joined the band replacing Tony Laureano. He is widely regarded as one of the fastest and most technically proficient drummers in extreme metal, sustaining blast beats at 240+ BPM with clarity on albums like *Annihilation of the Wicked* (2005), *Ithyphallic* (2007), and *Those Whom the Gods Detest* (2009). Beyond Nile, Kollias is an accomplished educator who has released instructional DVDs and taught clinics worldwide, openly sharing the techniques behind his legendary speed.

## Playing Style

Paul Mazurkiewicz delivers unflinching brutality rooted in old-school death metal — his alternating blast beats and relentless mid-tempo bulldoze sections have become part of the genre's shared vocabulary, prioritizing power and groove that translate directly to Cannibal Corpse's pit-driving live shows.

George Kollias pushes the outer limits of sustained speed and endurance, building a career on technical precision at tempos that would reduce most drummers to imprecise noise. His Nile work showcases a drummer who treats extreme velocity as a controllable, musical tool rather than raw chaos.

---

## Technique

Mazurkiewicz operates in a brutality-first register — his double bass patterns and blast beats are built for consistency across three-and-a-half decades of touring rather than peak technical display, giving Cannibal Corpse's sound its bulldozing, unrelenting character.

Kollias co-designed the Pearl Demon XR double pedal specifically to handle the rapid-fire bass drum sequences his style demands, and his ability to maintain clarity and musicality at 240+ BPM sets a technical benchmark that few extreme metal drummers approach. His parallel career as an educator has formalized and spread his speed-and-endurance methodology.

---

## Key Differences

- **Speed vs groove:** Kollias is renowned for sustained extreme velocity; Mazurkiewicz prioritizes brutal, consistent groove over peak speed.
- **Pedal design:** Kollias co-designed his own Pearl Demon XR double pedal for extreme speed; Mazurkiewicz uses the standard Pearl Demon Drive.
- **Cymbals:** Mazurkiewicz plays Sabian AAX; Kollias plays Zildjian K and A Custom series.
- **Education:** Kollias has built a substantial instructional career (DVDs, clinics, online courses); Mazurkiewicz's influence is primarily through his recorded catalog.
- **Tenure:** Mazurkiewicz co-founded Cannibal Corpse in 1988 and has 35+ years with one band; Kollias joined Nile in 2004, a comparatively newer chair in an already-established band.

---

## Influence & Legacy

Paul Mazurkiewicz has been the rhythmic backbone of Cannibal Corpse, the best-selling death metal band of all time, for over 35 years — his consistency across fifteen-plus studio albums made the band's brutal sound recognizable worldwide and helped establish death metal's commercial viability.

George Kollias is widely cited as one of the fastest drummers in extreme metal, and his openly shared educational content has taught his speed and endurance techniques to thousands of drummers, extending his influence well beyond Nile's discography into the broader technical death metal community.

## Verdict

Paul Mazurkiewicz and George Kollias represent two distinct pillars of death metal drumming excellence. Mazurkiewicz is the genre's old-school architect — three and a half decades of unflinching brutality that built Cannibal Corpse into death metal's best-selling act. Kollias is the modern speed scientist — a co-designed signature pedal and 240+ BPM blast beats that pushed Nile's technical death metal into uncharted territory. The debate is brutal groove vs sustained extreme velocity.
