## Drummer Profiles

### Paul Mazurkiewicz

Paul Mazurkiewicz (born October 8, 1968, in Buffalo, New York) is the founding drummer of Cannibal Corpse and one of the longest-tenured drummers in death metal history. A founding member since 1988, Paul has recorded every Cannibal Corpse studio album from "Eaten Back to Life" (1990) through the modern era — an unbroken 35+ year tenure behind the kit of the world's best-selling death metal band. His alternating-leg double kick approach delivers the thunderous, punishing drive that defines Cannibal Corpse's brutality, while his straightforward, song-serving drum parts prioritize maximum impact over technical showboating. Paul represents the Tampa, Florida school of death metal: direct, brutal, and relentless.

### Pete Sandoval

Pete Sandoval (born March 15, 1964, in Guatemala City, Guatemala) is the drummer for Morbid Angel and the inventor of the gravity blast — a technique that revolutionized how drummers approach extreme speed. Pete joined Morbid Angel in time for their landmark debut "Altars of Madness" (1989) and drove their classic run through "Domination" (1995). His invention of the gravity blast — using gravity and drum stick rebound to achieve single-footed blast beat speeds that were physically impossible with conventional technique — spread throughout all of extreme metal. Pete's drumming defined the Miami/Tampa Florida death metal sound alongside Morbid Angel's occult themes, establishing the aggressive, technical template for death metal drumming worldwide.

## Playing Style

Paul anchors Cannibal Corpse's relentless brutality with thunderous alternating-leg double kick that drives some of death metal's most violent riffs. Pete defined Morbid Angel's occult intensity with single-footed hyperblasts and savage gravity blast technique that pushed the physical limits of human drumming.

## Technique

Paul uses a traditional alternating-leg double kick approach to sustain brutal tempos across marathon brutal death compositions — his power comes from consistency, drive, and staying locked in with Alex Webster's bass. Pete pioneered the gravity blast — letting gravity and rebound do the work — enabling inhuman single-footed blast beat speeds that redefined what was physically possible in extreme metal.

**Q: What is the gravity blast technique and who invented it?**
A: The gravity blast (also called the "Morrisound blast" or "rebound blast") is a drumming technique where the drummer uses gravity and natural rebound to generate extremely fast single-stroke rolls between the bass drum and snare. Pete Sandoval is credited with pioneering its widespread use in death metal. It enabled blast beat speeds that were physically impossible with conventional push-pull technique.
