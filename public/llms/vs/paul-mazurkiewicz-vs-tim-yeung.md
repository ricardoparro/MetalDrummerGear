## Playing Style

Paul delivers brutal death metal's most enduring consistency — powerful blasts, crushing double bass, and mid-tempo bulldoze passages that define Cannibal Corpse's identity. Tim brings extreme speed and technical cross-genre versatility — his blast tempos exceed 280 BPM and his resume spans Italian brutal death, technical death metal, and industrial death metal.

## Technique

Paul's strength is inexhaustible stamina and consistency across decades — his double bass locks perfectly with Alex Webster's bass lines, and his rhythmic framework prioritizes maximum aggression over technical complexity. Tim's technique centers on verified high-velocity single-stroke blasts and the ability to navigate between multiple death metal subgenres — from Hour of Penance's brutal precision to Decrepit Birth's polyrhythmic complexity.

## Key Differences

Both play Pearl drums with Sabian cymbals, but their approaches diverge sharply. Paul plays for brutal impact and endurance — seventeen albums, thirty-five years. Tim plays for maximum technical speed and cross-genre range. Paul defines brutal death metal's template; Tim represents its most technically accelerated modern evolution.

## Influence & Legacy

Paul Mazurkiewicz's 35-year tenure with Cannibal Corpse makes him one of the most recorded drummers in death metal history. His drumming on "Tomb of the Mutilated" (1992), "Bleeding" (1994), and "Kill" (2006) defined brutal death metal's rhythmic vocabulary. Tim Yeung's career as death metal's most prolific extreme session and touring drummer has inspired a generation of drummers pursuing maximum speed — his verified BPM records and diverse resume (Hour of Penance, Decrepit Birth, Divine Heresy, Morbid Angel touring) represent the upper limit of physical drumming extremity.

## Verdict

Paul Mazurkiewicz by longevity and legacy; Tim Yeung by verified technical extremity. Together they answer the question: who is the most brutal death metal drummer? Mazurkiewicz built the genre's most enduring rhythmic foundation; Yeung pushed its physical limits to the extreme.
