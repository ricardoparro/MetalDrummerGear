## Playing Style

Shannon Larkin, born in 1967 in Chicago, joined Godsmack in 2002 after stints with Wrathchild America, Ugly Kid Joe, and Amen, driving the band's thunderous hard rock machine across six studio albums including "Faceless" (2003), "IV" (2006), and "The Oracle" (2010) until his 2024 departure. Abe Cunningham co-founded Deftones in Sacramento, California in 1988 as a teenager and has remained the band's drummer for over three decades, developing an unconventional, atmospheric style that helped define alternative metal across "Around the Fur" (1997), "White Pony" (2000), and "Diamond Eyes" (2010).

## Technique

Larkin brings deep rock roots and veteran groove instincts to Godsmack, locking in with Tony Rombola's guitars through muscular, hook-driven rhythms and dynamic control built for arena rock. Cunningham favors creative, unconventional patterns that serve the song's emotional arc over technical showmanship, drawing on influences like John Bonham and Stewart Copeland to seamlessly shift between crushing heaviness and ethereal, shoegaze-influenced restraint.

## Influence & Legacy

Larkin helped maintain Godsmack's commercial momentum through multiple platinum records, becoming one of the most recognizable faces in modern hard rock drumming across more than two decades behind the kit. Cunningham is regularly cited by younger alternative metal drummers as a primary influence — his dynamic, feel-first approach on tracks like "Digital Bath" and "Change (In the House of Flies)" proved that restraint and texture could be as powerful as raw aggression.

## Verdict

Shannon Larkin and Abe Cunningham represent two paths through legacy-era alternative and hard rock drumming. Larkin is the groove authority — a veteran session-honed player whose powerful, hook-driven rhythms gave Godsmack an unshakeable arena rock foundation for over two decades. Cunningham is the texture specialist — a founding member whose unconventional, dynamics-first playing has been Deftones' heartbeat since the band formed in a Sacramento classroom in 1988. Comparing them is comparing "groove metal" muscle to "alt-metal" atmosphere — feel over speed, in two very different flavors.
