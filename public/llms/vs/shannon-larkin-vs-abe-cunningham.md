## Playing Style

Shannon Larkin drummed for Godsmack from 2002 to 2024 — one of modern hard rock's longest single-band tenures — after stints in Wrathchild America (1989–1991) and Ugly Kid Joe (1991–1997, including the hit "Everything About You"), bringing a seasoned, road-tested groove instinct to Godsmack's hard rock machine across platinum-selling albums including "Faceless" (2003), "IV" (2006), and "1000hp" (2014). Abe Cunningham co-founded Deftones in Sacramento in 1988 and has been the band's only drummer across more than three decades, shaping alternative metal's most atmospheric, shoegaze-influenced sound through landmark records like the Grammy-winning "White Pony" (2000), "Diamond Eyes" (2010), and "Ohms" (2020).

## Technique

Larkin locked in tight, muscular grooves with guitarist Tony Rombola, favoring hook-driven, hard-hitting patterns and dynamic control that made Godsmack's sound commercially unstoppable across the 2000s — his playing prioritizes pocket and power over technical display. Cunningham's technique is defined by unconventional patterns and creative use of space rather than technical showmanship — his unconventional snare placements and dynamic control let him move seamlessly between crushing heaviness and ethereal delicacy, mirroring Deftones' emotional arc rather than following typical metal drumming conventions.

## Key Differences

Shannon Larkin's Godsmack-era setup centered on ddrum Dios Series drums with a ddrum Dios 14x6.5" Maple snare and Sabian AAX & HHX Series cymbals (14" AAX Stage Hi-Hats, 18" & 19" AAX X-Plosion Crashes, 21" HHX Raw Bell Dry Ride) — a groove-oriented rig built for arena hard rock. Abe Cunningham has been a longtime Tama endorser, playing a Tama Starclassic Maple/Bubinga kit with a Tama S.L.P. Big Black Steel 14x8" snare and Zildjian cymbals (14" A New Beat Hi-Hats, A Custom Crashes, K Custom Ride), driven by a Tama Iron Cobra double pedal and Zildjian Abe Cunningham Artist Series sticks.

## Influence & Legacy

Larkin anchored Godsmack's rhythm section for 22 years, helping sustain their commercial momentum through multiple platinum records and becoming one of the most recognizable faces in modern hard rock drumming before his 2024 departure. Cunningham's influence runs deep in alternative and nu-metal-adjacent drumming — his groove-and-feel-first approach with Deftones, prioritizing pocket and atmosphere over technical complexity, shaped how a generation of alt-metal drummers think about serving a song's emotional arc rather than showcasing chops.

## Verdict

Shannon Larkin and Abe Cunningham represent two adjacent but distinct strands of modern hard rock and alternative metal drumming. Larkin built Godsmack's radio-dominant groove machine through hook-first power and veteran consistency across a 22-year tenure. Cunningham has spent over three decades shaping Deftones' atmospheric, feel-first sound as the band's only-ever drummer, never chasing technical flash. Both prove that pocket and song service matter more than raw speed in hard rock and alt-metal — the comparison is groove-metal power versus alt-metal restraint.
