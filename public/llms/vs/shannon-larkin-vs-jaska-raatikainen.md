## Playing Style

Shannon Larkin has anchored Godsmack since 2002 with a powerful, groove-oriented style built for the band's heavy, riff-driven hard rock, hitting hard while keeping tracks like "I Stand Alone" and "Awake" locked into an accessible pocket. Jaska Raatikainen co-founded Children of Bodom in 1993 and spent 26 years driving the Finnish band's signature blend of neoclassical melodies and melodic death metal, anchoring dense keyboard-and-guitar arrangements from "Something Wild" (1997) through the band's 2019 farewell.

## Technique

Larkin favors hard-hitting, straightforward power over technical flash, prioritizing feel and impact — the same approach that let him briefly fill in for Black Sabbath's touring lineup for one show in 1997. Raatikainen built his technique around clean, locked-in double bass and precise, driving blast beats, keeping Bodom's dense arrangements anchored with surgical consistency at extreme tempos.
